India Forex Reserves Fall $10.29 Billion to $688.06 Billion Amid West Asia Conflict

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's forex reserves drop $10.29 billion to $688.06 billion for week ended March 27, 2026
  • 02/Second consecutive weekly decline after $11.41 billion fall; reserves down $40.4 billion from February all-time high of $728.49 billion
  • 03/Foreign currency assets down $6.62 billion to $551.07 billion; gold reserves fall $3.67 billion to $113.52 billion

India's foreign exchange reserves declined sharply in the latest reporting week. The reserves fell by $10.29 billion to $688.06 billion for the week ended March 27.

This marks the second consecutive weekly drop. In the previous week, reserves had declined by $11.41 billion to $698.35 billion.

The reserves had touched an all-time high of $728.49 billion in late February. Since then, they have fallen by approximately $40.4 billion.

The rupee has come under pressure since the West Asia conflict began. The RBI has been intervening through dollar sales and policy measures to stabilise the currency.

Foreign currency assets, the largest component, decreased by $6.62 billion. They stood at $551.07 billion for the reporting week.

Gold reserves declined by $3.67 billion to $113.52 billion. Special Drawing Rights increased marginally by $17 million to $18.65 billion.

India's reserve position with the IMF was down $17 million to $4.82 billion. The declines reflect RBI's active intervention to manage currency volatility.
