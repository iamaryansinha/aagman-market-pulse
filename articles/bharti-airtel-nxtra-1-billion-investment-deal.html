Bharti Airtel Jumps 3% After $1 Billion Nxtra Data Investment Deal

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Bharti Airtel stock rises 3% to ₹1,950 following $1 billion investment announcement in Nxtra Data subsidiary
  • 02/Global investors Alpha Wave Global, Carlyle, and Anchorage Capital lead the funding round
  • 03/The transaction values Nxtra Data at $3 billion; deal marks major bet on India's data center growth

Bharti Airtel (NSE: BHARTIARTL) shares jumped nearly 3% to ₹1,950 on April 1, 2026. The rally follows the company's announcement of a $1 billion investment in its data center arm Nxtra Data.

Global investment firms Alpha Wave Global, Carlyle, and Anchorage Capital are leading the funding round. The transaction values Nxtra Data at approximately $3 billion post-money.

Nxtra Data operates India's second-largest data center network. The company manages 13 data centers across 12 cities with over 120 MW of IT load capacity.

The fresh capital will fund Nxtra's expansion plans. The company aims to add 150 MW of capacity by 2027 as AI and cloud computing demand surges.

India's data center market is projected to grow at 15% CAGR through 2030. Digital adoption and AI infrastructure needs are driving significant investment interest.

The deal follows similar moves by competitors. Jio and Adani have also announced data center expansion plans worth billions.

Bharti Airtel has gained 28% over the past year. The stock trades at a premium to sector peers amid strong subscriber growth and improving margins.
