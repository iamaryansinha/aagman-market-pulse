IndiGo Summer Schedule 2026: 2,000 Daily Domestic Flights Planned

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IndiGo plans to operate up to 2,000 daily domestic flights in the ongoing summer schedule starting March 29, 2026
  • 02/This marks recovery from 10% capacity cap imposed in December 2025 after operational disruptions
  • 03/Airline adds new international routes to Athens and Shanghai; challenges remain from fuel costs and airport constraints

IndiGo (NSE: INDIGO) will operate up to 2,000 daily domestic flights in the summer schedule. The ramp-up begins from March 29, 2026.

The airline was operating over 1,850 domestic flights until late March. This expansion follows a swift operational recovery.

A 10 per cent capacity cap was enforced on IndiGo in December 2025 after network disruptions. Regulatory authorities have now cleared the airline to restore full operations.

Abhijit Dasgupta, Senior Vice-President at IndiGo, confirmed all resources are in place. The summer schedule has been approved by relevant authorities.

The airline upgraded its operational dashboards for real-time monitoring. Crew and aircraft rostering systems have also been improved.

IndiGo recently inducted its first A321XLR aircraft. This enables direct services to Athens from Delhi and Mumbai.

New international routes include Shanghai, which commenced March 29. Reunion Island service begins April 29, 2026.

Domestic network expansion includes Bhavnagar and Jamnagar in Gujarat. Navi Mumbai operations scale to over 400 weekly departures.

The airline plans services from Noida International Airport in Jewar. Timeline depends on airport readiness.

Challenges persist including airspace restrictions and airport constraints. Rising fuel and insurance costs continue to impact operations.
