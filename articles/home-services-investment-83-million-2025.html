Home Services Sector Attracts $83 Million Investment In 2025: Report

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Online home services companies raised approximately $83 million in 2025, marking a 28% year-on-year increase from $64.7 million in 2024
  • 02/Sector funding has grown 2.7x since 2023 when investments stood at just $30.5 million according to Tracxn data
  • 03/Urban demand for instant service delivery and the "Insta-help" model continues to drive investor interest in the segment

Online home services companies raised approximately $83 million in 2025. The figure marks a sharp increase from previous years, according to Tracxn data.

Investments stood at $64.7 million in 2024. This represents a year-on-year growth of roughly 28%.

The sector has seen 2.7x growth since 2023. Funding was just $30.5 million two years ago.

The "Insta-help" model is driving this surge. Urban consumers increasingly demand instant, app-based home services.

Cleaning, repairs, and maintenance services are leading categories. Investors are betting on recurring revenue models.

The pandemic accelerated digital adoption for home services. Post-pandemic habits have stuck with urban households.

Companies offering quick turnaround times are winning market share. Same-day and next-day slots are now industry standard.

Funding growth signals strong investor confidence. The sector is consolidating after initial fragmentation.

Urban demand remains the primary growth engine. Tier-1 cities account for the majority of transaction volume.

The home services market is projected to expand further. Technology-enabled platforms are replacing traditional service discovery methods.
