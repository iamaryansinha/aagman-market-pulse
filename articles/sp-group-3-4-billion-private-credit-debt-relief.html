SP Group Secures $3.4 Billion Private Credit Deal to Refinance Debt

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Shapoorji Pallonji Group raises $3.4 billion through private credit, marking India's largest such transaction
  • 02/Three-year zero-coupon NCD carries 18.5-19.75% annual yield backed by 18.4% Tata Sons stake
  • 03/Deal refinances $3.8 billion maturing debt; investors eye eventual Tata Sons stake sale or IPO

Shapoorji Pallonji Group has secured $3.4 billion through a private credit deal led by Deutsche Bank. The transaction represents India's largest private credit fundraising to date.

The three-year zero-coupon non-convertible debenture (NCD) carries an annual yield of 18.5-19.75%. The loan-to-value ratio stands at 16%, with funds disbursed shortly after signing.

Key investors include Ares Management, Cerberus Capital, Davidson Kempner, Farallon Capital, and Synergy Capital. Synergy Capital alone invested ₹640 crore (~$75 million), marking their sixth collaboration with the group.

The proceeds will refinance existing debt facilities totaling $3.8 billion maturing between March 2025 and April 2026. This includes $2.2 billion raised from Ares SSG and Farallon Capital in 2021, and $1.6 billion from Cyrus Investments in June 2023.

Collateral comprises the Mistry family's 18.4% stake in Tata Sons, alongside energy and real estate assets. The Tata Sons shares are valued at approximately $20 billion based on recent estimates.

The SP Group, led by billionaire Shapoor Mistry, has faced liquidity challenges since the pandemic. Total consolidated debt stood at ₹55,000-60,000 crore, split evenly between promoter debt and operating businesses.

This marks the fourth rollover of debt facilities for the group. Previous refinancing rounds carried yields as high as 22%, reflecting the collateral transfer restrictions on Tata Sons shares.

The deal provides breathing room until March 2028. Investors are betting on an eventual settlement involving sale of the Tata Sons stake or a potential IPO of the unlisted holding company.

"This continued partnership underscores a foundation of confidence and shared vision," said Sudhir Maheshwari, Founder and Managing Partner of Synergy Capital.

Tata Trusts has historically opposed SP Group's attempts to monetize the Tata Sons stake. However, recent boardroom changes at Tata Trusts may pave the way for a future settlement.
