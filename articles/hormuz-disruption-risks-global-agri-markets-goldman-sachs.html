Hormuz Disruption Risks Trigger Global Agri Supply Shock, Goldman Sachs Warns

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Goldman Sachs warns Hormuz Strait closure would trigger 40-50% spike in grain shipping costs and disrupt 30% of global LNG trade
  • 02/Risk of fertilizer shortages could cut crop yields by 15-20% in key agricultural regions including Europe and South Asia
  • 03/Wheat and corn prices could surge 25-35% if shipping routes remain blocked for over 30 days

Goldman Sachs has issued a stark warning on global agricultural markets. A potential disruption at the Strait of Hormuz could trigger severe supply shocks.

The investment bank estimates grain shipping costs could spike 40-50% immediately. About 21% of global petroleum liquids pass through this chokepoint daily.

Fertilizer markets face acute vulnerability. Natural gas accounts for 60-80% of nitrogen fertilizer production costs globally.

A Hormuz closure would disrupt 30% of world LNG trade. This would cascade directly into fertilizer price inflation within weeks.

European and South Asian agricultural regions are most exposed. Crop yields could decline 15-20% without adequate fertilizer supply.

Wheat and corn prices face 25-35% upside risk. The analysis assumes a disruption lasting more than 30 days.

Energy-intensive agricultural inputs remain highly correlated to natural gas. Input cost inflation would compress farmer margins globally.

Goldman recommends hedging positions in soft commodities. Volatility in agri markets could exceed historical averages through 2026.
