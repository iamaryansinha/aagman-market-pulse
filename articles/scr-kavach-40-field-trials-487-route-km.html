SCR Completes Kavach 4.0 Field Trials Across 487 Route Kms, Boosting Rail Safety

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/South Central Railway completes field trials for Kavach 4.0 installation covering 487 route kilometers during FY 2025-26
  • 02/Kavach 4.0 is India's indigenous Automatic Train Protection system with SIL-4 safety certification, the highest global standard
  • 03/Full rollout expected to accelerate across Indian Railways' Golden Quadrilateral and High Density Network routes

South Central Railway (SCR) has completed field trials for Kavach 4.0 installation. The trials covered 487 route kilometers across the SCR network during FY 2025-26.

Kavach 4.0 is India's indigenous Automatic Train Protection (ATP) system. It was developed by the Research Design and Standards Organisation (RDSO) in collaboration with Indian industry partners.

The system meets SIL-4 safety certification standards. This represents the highest level of safety integrity globally for railway safety mechanisms.

Kavach 4.0 automatically applies brakes if the loco pilot fails to respond. It prevents Signal Passing at Danger (SPAD), overspeeding, and potential collisions.

The system displays signal information directly in the driver's cab. This enables safe train operations even during low visibility conditions like dense fog.

Field trials on SCR were crucial for validating the upgraded system. SCR was the original testbed where Kavach 3.2 was first deployed starting in 2015.

The successful completion paves the way for nationwide deployment. Indian Railways plans to commission Kavach 4.0 across 15,512 route kilometers within six years.

As of February 2025, Kavach has been installed on 795 locomotives and 664 stations. Trackside equipment covers 3,727 route kilometers across the network.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS KAVACH 4.0 AND WHAT DOES IT DO?
KAVACH 4.0 IS INDIA'S INDIGENOUS AUTOMATIC TRAIN PROTECTION (ATP) SYSTEM. IT AUTOMATICALLY APPLIES BRAKES IF THE LOCO PILOT FAILS TO RESPOND TO SIGNALS, PREVENTS OVERSPEEDING, AND PROTECTS AGAINST SIGNAL PASSING AT DANGER (SPAD) INCIDENTS.
HOW MANY ROUTE KILOMETERS DID SCR COVER IN THE FIELD TRIALS?
SOUTH CENTRAL RAILWAY COMPLETED FIELD TRIALS FOR KAVACH 4.0 INSTALLATION ACROSS 487 ROUTE KILOMETERS DURING THE FINANCIAL YEAR 2025-26.
WHAT IS SIL-4 CERTIFICATION AND WHY DOES IT MATTER?
SIL-4 (SAFETY INTEGRITY LEVEL 4) IS THE HIGHEST GLOBAL STANDARD FOR RAILWAY SAFETY SYSTEMS. IT ENSURES THE SYSTEM MEETS THE MOST STRINGENT SAFETY REQUIREMENTS FOR PREVENTING ACCIDENTS CAUSED BY HUMAN ERROR.
WHAT ARE THE KEY FEATURES OF KAVACH 4.0 COMPARED TO EARLIER VERSIONS?
KAVACH 4.0 INCLUDES ENHANCED LOCATION ACCURACY, IMPROVED SIGNAL INFORMATION FOR COMPLEX YARDS, OFC-BASED STATION INTERFACE, AND DIRECT INTEGRATION WITH ELECTRONIC INTERLOCKING SYSTEMS.
WHAT IS THE NATIONWIDE DEPLOYMENT PLAN FOR KAVACH 4.0?
INDIAN RAILWAYS PLANS TO COMMISSION KAVACH 4.0 ACROSS 15,512 ROUTE KILOMETERS WITHIN SIX YEARS, COVERING ALL GOLDEN QUADRILATERAL, GOLDEN DIAGONAL, AND HIGH DENSITY NETWORK ROUTES.
