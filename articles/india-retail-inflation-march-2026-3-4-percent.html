India Retail Inflation Rises To 3.4% In March 2026, Highest In 11 Months

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's retail inflation climbed to 3.4% in March 2026, up from 3.21% in February and 2.74% in January
  • 02/The uptick was driven by higher food prices, LPG costs, and geopolitical fuel supply pressures
  • 03/Inflation remains within RBI's 2%-6% comfort zone, though the MPC is expected to hold rates in April

India's retail inflation based on the Consumer Price Index rose to 3.4% year-on-year in March 2026. The Ministry of Statistics released the data on Monday, April 13.

The March print marks an 11-month high. It climbed from 3.21% in February and 2.74% in January under the revised 2024 base series.

Food and beverages remained the primary driver of the headline uptick. The segment contributed most of the increase, though food inflation is expected to stabilize near 3%.

Fuel costs added pressure following hikes in domestic LPG cylinder prices in early March. Global energy supply disruptions linked to the Iran-US conflict pushed transport and input costs higher.

Core inflation, which excludes food and fuel, stayed unchanged at 3.4%. This suggests broader price pressures across the economy remain contained.

Rural inflation stood at 3.37% in February, while urban inflation was at 3.02%. March state-level data will be available on the Ministry's eSankhyiki portal.

The Reserve Bank of India targets inflation at 4% with a tolerance band of 2% to 6%. The March reading sits comfortably within this range.

Analysts expect the Monetary Policy Committee to maintain a pause in its April 2026 meeting. Geopolitical tensions pose upside risks to the inflation trajectory in coming months.

HIGHER FOOD PRICES, INCREASED DOMESTIC LPG CYLINDER COSTS, AND RISING FUEL PRICES DUE TO THE IRAN-US CONFLICT WERE THE MAIN DRIVERS.

YES. THE 3.4% READING IS WELL WITHIN THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA'S TARGET BAND OF 2% TO 6%, WITH A MIDPOINT TARGET OF 4%.

CORE INFLATION EXCLUDES FOOD AND FUEL PRICES. IT REMAINED STABLE AT 3.4%, INDICATING THAT BROADER ECONOMIC PRICE PRESSURES ARE CONTAINED.

ANALYSTS EXPECT THE MONETARY POLICY COMMITTEE TO PAUSE RATE CHANGES IN APRIL 2026, GIVEN THAT INFLATION REMAINS WITHIN THE TARGET RANGE DESPITE GEOPOLITICAL RISKS.
