Sensex, Nifty Set For Worst Monthly Fall Since March 2020 Covid Crash

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex and Nifty 50 have declined 10.5% each in March 2026, the worst monthly drop since March 2020
  • 02/The Covid crash in March 2020 saw markets plummet over 23%, making this the second-worst month in six years
  • 03/Investors are watching global cues and domestic flows for signs of stabilization in April

Indian equity benchmarks are heading for their worst monthly performance in nearly six years. The Nifty 50 and BSE Sensex have each shed approximately 10.5% during March 2026.

The decline marks the steepest monthly fall since the Covid-induced market crash of March 2020. Back then, both indices had plummeted over 23% as the pandemic triggered a global selloff.

The current rout ranks as the second-worst monthly performance for Indian markets in the past six years. It reflects sustained foreign institutional investor (FII) selling and weak global sentiment.

Broader markets have fared no better. The Nifty Midcap 100 and Nifty Smallcap 100 indices have corrected sharply from their January peaks.

Global headwinds continue to weigh on emerging markets. Concerns over US tariffs, rate policy uncertainty, and slowing growth have dented risk appetite.

Domestic institutional investors have provided some cushion through consistent buying. However, their flows have not been sufficient to offset FII outflows.

Traders are now looking ahead to the April series for recovery signals. Key support levels for Nifty are placed at 21,800 and 21,500.

THE LAST TIME INDIAN MARKETS SAW A STEEPER MONTHLY DECLINE WAS IN MARCH 2020 DURING THE COVID-19 PANDEMIC CRASH, WHEN INDICES PLUNGED OVER 23%.
WHAT IS DRIVING THE CURRENT MARKET SELLOFF?
THE DECLINE IS DRIVEN BY SUSTAINED FOREIGN INSTITUTIONAL INVESTOR (FII) SELLING, GLOBAL UNCERTAINTY OVER US TARIFFS, RATE POLICY CONCERNS, AND SLOWING GROWTH SENTIMENT.
HOW HAVE BROADER MARKETS PERFORMED?
THE NIFTY MIDCAP 100 AND NIFTY SMALLCAP 100 INDICES HAVE CORRECTED SHARPLY FROM THEIR JANUARY 2026 PEAKS, UNDERPERFORMING THE LARGE-CAP BENCHMARKS.
