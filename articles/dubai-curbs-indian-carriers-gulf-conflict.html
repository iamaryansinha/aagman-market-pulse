Dubai Curbs On Indian Carriers Under Scrutiny Amid Gulf Conflict Flight Restrictions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Dubai International Airport restricts Indian carriers to one rotation per day between April 20 and May 31, 2026 for Northern Summer season
  • 02/UAE carriers Emirates and FlyDubai operate ~35 daily flights to India at pre-disruption levels without similar curbs
  • 03/FIA has urged MoCA to seek removal of restrictions and consider reciprocal measures on Dubai carriers

Dubai International Airport has mandated flight cancellations for Indian carriers for the Northern Summer 2026 season. The restrictions apply from April 20 to May 31, 2026.

Foreign carriers have been limited to only one rotation per day during this period. The curbs were flagged by the Federation of Indian Airlines (FIA) to the Ministry of Civil Aviation (MoCA).

UAE-based carriers Emirates and FlyDubai have resumed operations to India at pre-disruption levels. These airlines are not subject to similar restrictions.

Emirates and FlyDubai together operate around 35 daily flights between Dubai and multiple Indian cities. Indian carriers continue to have limited ability to scale up services.

The FIA noted that such restrictions are creating anti-competitive market conditions. They also cause operational inefficiencies and substantial revenue losses.

Industry stakeholders said the imbalance becomes more visible during disruptions. Indian carriers face longer routings to Europe and the US due to restricted airspace.

These longer routes add hours to flight times and increase fuel burn significantly. Higher fuel consumption is further straining airline economics.

The FIA has urged the government to engage with Dubai authorities to remove the restrictions. It suggested reciprocal measures could be considered.

Such measures would restrict Dubai carriers in line with cumulative seat capacity deployed by Indian airlines on the route. This would create a more balanced approach under bilateral arrangements.
