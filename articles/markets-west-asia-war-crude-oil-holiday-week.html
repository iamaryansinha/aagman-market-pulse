Markets Eye West Asia War, Crude Oil As Holiday-Shortened Week Begins

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nifty 50 closed at 22,819.60 on Friday, down 1.3% for the week as FPIs sold $2.73 billion in equities
  • 02/Brent crude trades at $105.55 per barrel, up 60% since West Asia conflict began in February 2026
  • 03/Markets face holiday-shortened week with subdued volumes; India VIX near 27 signals elevated volatility

Indian equity markets enter a holiday-shortened week with investors tracking West Asia war developments and volatile crude oil prices. The Nifty 50 ended Friday at 22,819.60, while the Sensex closed at 73,583.22.

Foreign Portfolio Investors have been net sellers for four consecutive weeks. The equity segment saw outflows of $2.73 billion last week, taking March withdrawals to $12.3 billion.

Brent crude futures climbed to $105.55 per barrel on Friday, rising 2.36% for the session. Prices have surged 60% since the conflict began in late February 2026.

U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude settled at $97.84 per barrel, up 3.56%. The 30-day realized volatility for Brent has peaked at 35%, the highest since Q4 2024.

Trading volumes are expected to remain subdued during the truncated week. Historical data shows holiday-shortened sessions often see heightened volatility due to lower liquidity.

The India VIX, which measures market volatility, is trading near 27. This indicates significant uncertainty among market participants regarding near-term direction.

Key technical levels will be critical this week. For the Nifty 50, immediate support is placed at 22,200-22,300, while resistance is seen at 23,500-24,000.

A decisive break below 22,200 could trigger further selling toward 21,700. Conversely, a sustained move above 24,000 would signal potential relief rally toward 26,000.

Bank Nifty faces crucial support at 50,500-50,800. The index closed at 52,274.60 on Friday, falling over 2% for the week.

The conflict has disrupted 18% of global air cargo, forcing logistics shifts that increase fuel consumption. Qatar's Ras Laffan LNG facility damage will require 3-5 years of repairs.

Global markets remain on edge as central banks monitor inflation pressures from energy prices. The Federal Reserve and RBI face difficult balancing acts between growth and price stability.

THE INDIA VIX IS A VOLATILITY INDEX THAT MEASURES MARKET EXPECTATIONS OF NEAR-TERM VOLATILITY. IT IS CURRENTLY TRADING NEAR 27, WHICH INDICATES SIGNIFICANT UNCERTAINTY AND EXPECTATION OF LARGE PRICE SWINGS IN THE NIFTY 50 INDEX OVER THE NEXT 30 DAYS.
