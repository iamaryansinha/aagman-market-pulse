Top 5 States Drive 65% of India's GDP: Maharashtra Leads Rubix Study

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Top 5 states (Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, UP, Karnataka, Gujarat) contribute over 65% of India's GDP
  • 02/These states account for nearly 75% of India's total exports, says Rubix Data Sciences study
  • 03/Maharashtra leads with ~13% GDP share; per capita income doubled to ₹3.09 lakh by FY2025

India's top five states now drive over 65 per cent of the country's GDP. They also account for nearly 75 per cent of total exports, according to a new Rubix Data Sciences report.

The study, "State of the States: Assessing State-Level Performance in India's Economic Transition," highlights Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, Karnataka and Gujarat as the key growth engines.

Maharashtra remains India's largest state economy with around 13 per cent contribution to national GDP. Its per capita income has more than doubled to ₹3.09 lakh by FY2025.

The state's growth is supported by massive infrastructure investments. Major projects include the Mumbai Trans Harbour Link and expanding metro networks.

Tamil Nadu ranks second, contributing nearly 9 per cent to GDP. It follows a balanced growth model driven by industrial capability and financial depth.

Uttar Pradesh has emerged as the third-largest contributor with a similar 9 per cent GDP share. Rapid infrastructure expansion and industrial hubs have driven sharp income growth.

Karnataka contributes about 8 per cent to GDP. Its innovation-led growth in technology and services has nearly tripled per capita income.

Gujarat ranks fifth but dominates India's export landscape with nearly 30 per cent share. It leads in capital formation and manufacturing.

"India's growth story is becoming more nuanced. States that combine investment with efficiency are emerging as long-term leaders," said Mohan Ramaswamy, Founder & CEO, Rubix Data Sciences.

The report describes India's growth as a "dual-speed" phenomenon. Leading states are pulling ahead while others expand gradually through infrastructure support.

WHAT IS THE PER CAPITA INCOME OF MAHARASHTRA IN FY2025?
MAHARASHTRA'S PER CAPITA INCOME HAS MORE THAN DOUBLED TO ₹3.09 LAKH BY FINANCIAL YEAR 2025.

THE REPORT DESCRIBES INDIA'S GROWTH AS A "DUAL-SPEED" PHENOMENON WHERE A FEW STATES LEAD ACROSS KEY INDICATORS WHILE OTHERS EXPAND GRADUALLY THROUGH INFRASTRUCTURE AND POLICY SUPPORT.
