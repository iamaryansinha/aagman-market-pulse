US Report Flags India's High Tariffs Up To 150% On Imports, Trade Barriers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/US Trade Representative's 2026 NTE report flags India's import duties as high as 150% on alcoholic beverages and 100% on coffee
  • 02/India's WTO bound tariff rates on agricultural products average 113.1%, ranging up to 300%, creating uncertainty for US exporters
  • 03/Report acknowledges India reduced tariffs on lifesaving medicines and EV components in 2026 budget

The United States Trade Representative has released its 2026 National Trade Estimate Report. It highlights significant trade barriers in India affecting US exports.

The report flags India's "high" import duties on agricultural products, pharmaceuticals, and alcoholic beverages. Specific duties mentioned include 45% on vegetable oils, 50% on apples and motorcycles, 60% on automobiles, 70% on natural rubber, 100% on coffee and walnuts, and 150% on alcoholic beverages.

India's WTO bound tariff rates on agricultural products average 113.1%. These rates range as high as 300%, among the highest globally.

The report notes India has considerable flexibility to change tariff rates. This is due to the large disparity between WTO bound and applied rates. Such changes create uncertainty for US workers, farmers, and exporters.

On non-tariff barriers, the report cites import bans, licensing requirements, and mandatory Quality Control Orders. US stakeholders report that obtaining import licenses for remanufactured goods is onerous. Applications require excessive details with long delays.

The report acknowledges positive developments in India's 2026 budget. Tariffs were reduced on lifesaving medicines, EV battery components, critical minerals, and electronic components.

However, concerns remain about BIS standards not aligning with international norms. Burdensome QCOs still affect US exports including medical devices, chemicals, electronics, and cosmetics.

The report also flags India's internet shutdowns. These restrict access to services and disrupt commercial operations, undermining digital trade.

WHAT DOES THE US TRADE REPRESENTATIVE'S 2026 NTE REPORT SAY ABOUT INDIA'S TARIFFS?
THE REPORT FLAGS INDIA'S IMPORT DUTIES AS HIGH, WITH SPECIFIC RATES INCLUDING 45% ON VEGETABLE OILS, 50% ON APPLES AND MOTORCYCLES, 60% ON AUTOMOBILES, 70% ON NATURAL RUBBER, 100% ON COFFEE AND WALNUTS, AND 150% ON ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES.
WHAT ARE INDIA'S WTO BOUND TARIFF RATES ON AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS?
INDIA'S WTO BOUND TARIFF RATES ON AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS AVERAGE 113.1% AND RANGE AS HIGH AS 300%, AMONG THE HIGHEST IN THE WORLD.
WHAT NON-TARIFF BARRIERS DOES THE REPORT HIGHLIGHT?
THE REPORT CITES IMPORT BANS, RESTRICTIVE LICENSING REQUIREMENTS, MANDATORY QUALITY CONTROL ORDERS, CUSTOMS BARRIERS, PRICE CONTROLS ON MEDICAL DEVICES, AND COMPLEX CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS FOR EQUIPMENT.
HAS INDIA MADE ANY RECENT TARIFF REDUCTIONS?
YES, INDIA'S 2026 BUDGET REDUCED APPLIED TARIFFS ON LIFESAVING MEDICINES, RAW MATERIALS FOR EV AND MOBILE PHONE BATTERIES, CRITICAL MINERALS, AND CERTAIN ELECTRONIC COMPONENTS.
WHAT OTHER TRADE CONCERNS DOES THE REPORT RAISE?
THE REPORT FLAGS RESTRICTIONS ON FOREIGN INVESTMENT IN FINANCIAL SERVICES AND RETAIL, BARRIERS TO DIGITAL TRADE, INTERNET SHUTDOWN PRACTICES, AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY POLICIES AS ONGOING CONCERNS.
