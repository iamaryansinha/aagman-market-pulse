Aviation Demand Resilient Despite Geopolitical Disruptions, Says Noida Airport CEO

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Noida International Airport CEO Christoph Schnellmann says India's aviation growth story remains intact despite West Asia crisis and geopolitical disruptions
  • 02/Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated the ₹30,000 crore greenfield airport on March 28, 2026; first phase capacity of 12 million passengers annually
  • 03/First domestic flights expected 30-45 days after Airport Security Programme approval; international flights to commence within months after domestic launch

India's aviation growth story remains resilient despite ongoing geopolitical disruptions, according to Noida International Airport CEO Christoph Schnellmann. He emphasised that the West Asia crisis represents only a temporary disruption that will not derail the sector's long-term trajectory.

Schnellmann spoke to businessline ahead of the airport's inauguration by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on March 28, 2026. The greenfield airport at Jewar in Greater Noida marks a significant milestone for India's aviation infrastructure expansion.

"We believe in India's aviation growth story," Schnellmann stated. The ₹30,000 crore investment reflects confidence in the sector's enduring potential despite recent global challenges.

The airport has secured critical regulatory clearances including security approval from the Bureau of Civil Aviation Security and an aerodrome licence from the Directorate General of Civil Aviation. These approvals pave the way for operational commencement.

First domestic flights are expected within 30-45 days following Airport Security Programme approval. International operations will launch a couple of months after domestic services begin.

IndiGo will serve as the launch carrier at Noida International Airport. Akasa Air plans to operate both domestic and international services, while Air India Express is also expected to commence operations from the facility.

The first phase features one runway and one terminal with annual capacity of 12 million passengers. Subsequent phases will scale capacity to 70 million passengers per year upon full completion.

Connectivity infrastructure includes an approved greenfield corridor linking the airport to the Delhi-Mumbai Expressway at a cost of ₹3,631 crore. Long-term plans include a Regional Rapid Transit System link and a proposed high-speed rail corridor between Delhi and Varanasi.
