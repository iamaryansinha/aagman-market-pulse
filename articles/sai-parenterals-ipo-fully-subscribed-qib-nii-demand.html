Sai Parenterals IPO Fully Subscribed On Final Day, QIBs And NIIs Drive Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sai Parenterals IPO gets fully subscribed on final day with QIBs and NIIs driving the bulk of demand
  • 02/₹408.79 crore issue comprises ₹285 crore fresh issue and ₹123.79 crore offer for sale at ₹372-392 per share
  • 03/Allotment to be finalised on March 30; listing on BSE and NSE scheduled for April 2, 2026

Sai Parenterals Limited's ₹408.79 crore IPO has been fully subscribed on its final day of bidding. The issue, which opened on March 24 and closed on March 27, saw strong participation from institutional investors.

Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs) and Non-Institutional Investors (NIIs) led the subscription drive. Retail participation remained relatively subdued throughout the three-day bidding period.

The Hyderabad-based pharmaceutical company priced its shares between ₹372 and ₹392 per share. The face value of each share is ₹5. Retail investors needed a minimum of ₹14,896 to apply for one lot of 38 shares.

The IPO comprised a fresh issue of ₹285 crore and an offer for sale of up to 31.57 lakh shares worth ₹123.79 crore. Selling shareholders included Vikasa India EIF I Fund, Devendra Chawla, and Ashish Maheshwari among others.

Ahead of the IPO, the company raised ₹122.64 crore from anchor investors. Morgan Stanley Asia Singapore, Kotak Mahindra Life Insurance, Kotak Life Sciences Fund, and Quant Mutual Fund participated in the anchor book.

Sai Parenterals operates five manufacturing facilities across Telangana and Andhra Pradesh. The company manufactures branded generic formulations and provides CDMO services across multiple therapeutic segments.

The company reported revenue of ₹163.74 crore in FY2025, up from ₹155.18 crore in FY2024. Profit after tax rose to ₹14.43 crore in FY2025 compared to ₹8.42 crore in the previous year.

IPO proceeds will fund capacity expansion (₹110.8 crore), a new R&D centre (₹18 crore), debt repayment (₹14.3 crore), working capital (₹33 crore), and acquisition of Noumed Pharmaceuticals in Australia (₹35.64 crore).

The allotment status will be finalised on March 30, 2026. Successful applicants will receive shares in their demat accounts by April 1, with listing scheduled for April 2 on both BSE and NSE.
