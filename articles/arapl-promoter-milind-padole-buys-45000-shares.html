ARAPL Promoter Milind Padole Buys 45,000 Shares, Signals Confidence In Growth Trajectory

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Promoter Milind Padole acquires 45,000 equity shares of ARAPL in a vote of confidence for the robotics firm's long-term prospects
  • 02/The purchase was disclosed under SEBI insider trading norms; ARAPL shares have declined 60% over the past year
  • 03/Investors should watch for further promoter buying and quarterly results as the company expands into US and European markets

Affordable Robotic & Automation Ltd (ARAPL) promoter Milind Padole has purchased 45,000 equity shares of the company. The disclosure was made under SEBI's insider trading regulations on March 30, 2026.

Padole serves as the Founder and Managing Director of ARAPL. The share acquisition signals strong promoter confidence in the company's growth trajectory despite recent stock underperformance.

ARAPL is India's first publicly listed robotics company. Founded in 2005 and headquartered in Pune, the firm has deployed over 7,500 industrial robots and 14,000 automated parking systems.

The company offers industrial robotics, multilevel car parking systems, smart warehousing solutions, and autonomous vehicles. Its subsidiary Humro specializes in autonomous mobile robots for warehouses and logistics hubs.

ARAPL recently secured its first US order for the Atlas AC2000 autonomous truck loading robot. The three-year lease deal is valued at ₹3.6 crore and could expand to 15 warehouses.

The stock has faced significant headwinds, falling approximately 60% over the past year. The company reported operating revenue of ₹160.47 crore for FY2025 with a market capitalization of around ₹244 crore.

"This investment is a reflection of my continued conviction in ARAPL's strategic direction and long-term value creation potential," said Milind Padole. "We are at a pivotal moment when global manufacturing and infrastructure sectors are rapidly transitioning to intelligent, automated systems."

The company operates from a 350,000 sq. ft. facility and holds nine patents. With increasing global demand for automation solutions, ARAPL is positioning itself to capitalize on opportunities in the US and European markets.
