India Imports 12 Million Barrels Venezuelan Crude as Iran War Disrupts Supply

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India set to import over 12 million barrels of Venezuelan crude in April, the highest volume in nearly six years
  • 02/Supply diversification follows Iran war disruptions to Strait of Hormuz, which transits nearly 40% of India's oil imports
  • 03/Reliance Industries receives first Venezuelan cargo in a year; sanctions eased after Maduro's capture in January

India is importing the most Venezuelan crude in almost six years. Over 12 million barrels are headed to India's west coast this month.

The last time India imported this much Venezuelan oil was February 2020. Data from intelligence firm Kpler confirms the volume.

The move comes as the Iran war disrupts Middle East supplies. The Strait of Hormuz normally transits nearly 40% of India's oil imports.

India depends on imports for about 90% of its crude consumption. Supply security has become critical amid West Asia tensions.

Venezuelan crude is similar to Middle East grades. Both are sulfur-rich blends suited to Indian refineries.

Trade was previously curbed by US sanctions on Venezuela. Washington eased those restrictions after President Nicolás Maduro's capture in January.

The Ottoman Sincerity tanker arrived at Reliance's Sikka port this week. It carried nearly 1 million barrels of Boscan crude loaded via ship-to-ship transfer.

This marks the first Venezuelan cargo to reach India in one year. Reliance holds a US license to purchase directly from state-owned PDVSA.

The company also loaded its first direct cargo on the VLCC Helios this week. Earlier purchases were routed through traders Vitol and Trafigura.

Heavy Venezuelan barrels yield more diesel and jet fuel. This is valuable as middle distillate markets remain structurally tight.

Most shipments are Venezuela's flagship Merey blend according to Kpler data. The grade is popular among Asian refiners.
