Jubilant FoodWorks Ends Dunkin' India Franchise Deal After 12 Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Jubilant FoodWorks announces it will not renew Dunkin' franchise agreement in India after 12-year partnership
  • 02/The company operates approximately 25 Dunkin' stores across India, a fraction of its 1,800+ Domino's outlets
  • 03/Jubilant will evaluate Dunkin' operations in a phased manner; shares declined 3% on the news

Jubilant FoodWorks Ltd (NSE: JUBLFOOD) has announced it will not renew its franchise agreement with Dunkin' in India. The partnership, which began in 2014, is set to conclude.

The company stated it will undertake a phased and orderly evaluation of its Dunkin' operations. This suggests a potential wind-down or restructuring of the brand's presence in India.

Jubilant currently operates approximately 25 Dunkin' stores across the country. This is a negligible footprint compared to its dominant 1,800+ Domino's Pizza outlets.

The Dunkin' brand has struggled to gain traction in India's competitive quick-service restaurant market. It faces stiff competition from local chains and rival international brands.

Jubilant FoodWorks shares fell approximately 3% following the announcement. Investors appear to view the exit as a strategic pruning of underperforming assets.

The move allows Jubilant to focus resources on its core Domino's Pizza business. The company is also expanding its Popeyes fried chicken brand in India.

Analysts suggest the Dunkin' exit was expected given its limited scale. The decision reflects management's disciplined approach to capital allocation.
