ICRA Revises Indian Aviation Outlook to Negative on Geopolitical, Cost Pressures

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ICRA revises Indian aviation industry outlook to negative from stable on March 27, 2026, citing West Asian geopolitical tensions and cost pressures
  • 02/Domestic air passenger traffic growth forecast slashed to 0-3% from 6-8%; international growth cut to 7-9% from 8-10%
  • 03/Industry net losses projected at ₹17,000-18,000 crore in FY26; FY27 estimates carry downward bias due to fuel costs and rupee depreciation

Ratings agency ICRA has revised its outlook on the Indian aviation industry to negative from stable. The downgrade was announced on March 27, 2026.

The revision follows disruptions in international airspace due to escalating geopolitical tensions in West Asia. Flight cancellations and airspace closures have disrupted operations.

ICRA has slashed its domestic passenger traffic growth forecast to 0-3% for FY27. This is down sharply from the earlier estimate of 6-8%.

International passenger traffic growth for Indian carriers is now projected at 7-9%. The previous estimate was 8-10%.

The ratings agency cited a sharp depreciation of the rupee against the US dollar as a key concern. Rising aviation turbine fuel (ATF) prices are adding further pressure.

Fuel accounts for 30-40% of airlines' operating expenses. Additionally, 35-50% of total costs, including lease payments and maintenance, are dollar-denominated.

This makes Indian airlines highly vulnerable to currency depreciation and global fuel price volatility.

ICRA now expects the industry to report net losses of ₹17,000-18,000 crore in FY26. Earlier projections for FY27 losses at ₹11,000-12,000 crore now carry a downward bias.

Higher airfares following fuel surcharge levies of 5-6% on ticket prices could dampen demand. Rerouting flights due to airspace closures increases fuel burn and operating costs.

The removal of airfare caps by DGCA, introduced in December 2025 after IndiGo disruptions, poses further downside risks to passenger traffic.
