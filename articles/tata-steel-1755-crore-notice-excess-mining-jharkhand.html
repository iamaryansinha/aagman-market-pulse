Tata Steel Gets ₹1,755 Crore Notice For Excess Mining In Jharkhand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Jharkhand DMO issues ₹1,755 crore demand notice to Tata Steel for alleged excess coal mining at West Bokaro Colliery between FY01-FY07
  • 02/Authorities claim 16.24 million tonnes extracted beyond permissible limits; notice cites Supreme Court's Common Cause case principles
  • 03/Company disputes demand as lacking justification; will challenge before judicial forums; stock at ₹193.50

Tata Steel Ltd has received a demand notice of ₹1,755.10 crore from the District Mining Office (DMO) in Ramgarh, Jharkhand. The company disclosed the notice in an exchange filing on April 4, 2026.

The notice alleges excess coal extraction of approximately 16.24 million metric tonnes from the company's West Bokaro Colliery. The period under scrutiny spans from financial year 2000-01 to 2006-07.

The DMO issued the demand based on grounds similar to those in the Supreme Court's Common Cause vs Union of India case. This landmark judgment established principles for penalizing illegal mining and excess production.

The authorities claim Tata Steel violated statutory mining limits during the seven-year period. The penalty reflects both the scale of alleged overproduction and associated financial recovery under applicable laws.

Tata Steel strongly disputes the demand. The company stated the notice lacks both justification and substantive basis in its regulatory filing.

The steelmaker plans to challenge the demand through appropriate legal channels. It will seek relief before competent judicial or quasi-judicial forums.

The notice dated March 30, 2026, was received by the company on April 3, 2026. The disclosure complies with SEBI Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements Regulations, 2015.

Tata Steel shares currently trade at ₹193.50. The stock has declined 0.74% over the past five days and 3.52% in the last month.

However, the stock has gained 13.78% over the past six months. Investors will watch how the legal challenge unfolds and any precedent it may set for similar cases.
