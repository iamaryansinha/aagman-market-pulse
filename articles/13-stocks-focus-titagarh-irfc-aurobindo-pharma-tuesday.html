13 Stocks In Focus: Titagarh, IRFC, Aurobindo Lead Tuesday's Movers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Titagarh receives In-Principle Approval for Falta shipyard expansion under Shipbuilding Development Scheme
  • 02/IRFC sanctions ₹1,000 Cr term loan to MAHAGENCO; Aurobindo's BP11 biosimilar meets all Phase 3 endpoints
  • 03/PDS signs ₹450 Cr sourcing contract with US retailer; Deep Industries bags ₹59 Cr ONGC award

Titagarh Naval Systems (TNSL) has secured In-Principle Approval from the Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways. The approval covers its Brownfield Expansion project at Falta, West Bengal, under the Shipbuilding Development Scheme (SbDS).

The SbDS scheme supports India's domestic shipbuilding ecosystem. Recent Union Budget priorities have reinforced this policy momentum, targeting defence and commercial vessel construction.

Indian Railway Finance Corporation (IRFC) has sanctioned a ₹1,000 crore term loan. The funds go to Maharashtra State Power Generation Company Limited (MAHAGENCO), with full disbursement already completed.

This follows IRFC's recent ₹12,842 crore loan agreement with Hindustan Urvarak & Rasayan Ltd. The Navratna CPSE continues expanding its non-railway lending portfolio.

Aurobindo Pharma's subsidiary CuraTeQ Biologics announced positive Phase 3 results. Its BP11 biosimilar to Xolair (omalizumab) met all primary endpoints in chronic spontaneous urticaria patients.

The 300 mg dose showed high comparability to the reference product. This marks progress toward commercialization in the global biosimilar market.

PDS Ltd has entered a Sourcing as a Service (SaaS) contract with a leading US value retailer. The engagement covers vendor management, compliance, and supply chain execution.

Expected sourcing volumes are approximately ₹450 crore ($50 million). The contract has potential for gradual scale-up, leveraging PDS's global network.

Deep Industries received a Letter of Award from ONGC. The contract covers Natural Gas Compressor and Gas Dehydration services at Malleswaram, Rajamundry Asset.

The three-year contract carries an estimated value of ₹59 crore. The award strengthens Deep Industries' position in the oil and gas services segment.

Diamond Power Infrastructure secured a letter of intent from Adani Power. The contract covers Power and Control Cables for Raipur Phase-II Thermal Project in Chhattisgarh.

The order value is ₹43.26 crore inclusive of GST. United Drilling Tools also received a ₹57.06 lakh order for downhole tools from Aadyashree Drilling International.

Globus Spirits launched TERAI Tequila, marking entry into the premium tequila category. BR Goyal Infrastructure secured a ₹23.37 crore work order for a Hockey and Athletic Stadium in Ujjain.

Sharika Enterprises appointed Sanjay Verma as CEO effective April 1, 2026. Indobell Insulations secured a ₹1.47 crore order from Goa Shipyard for Searox Slabs.
