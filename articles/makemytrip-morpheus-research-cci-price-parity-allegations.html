Morpheus Research Accuses MakeMyTrip of Defying CCI Order with Continued Price Parity Practices

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Activist short-seller Morpheus Research has released a scathing report accusing MakeMyTrip of openly defying a 2022 Competition Commission of India order that fined the company $26 million for anti-competitive practices
  • 02/The research firm, which disclosed a short position in the NASDAQ-listed travel platform, alleges that MakeMyTrip continues to enforce price parity arrangements with hotel partners through indirect mechanisms
  • 03/The report also raises concerns about accounting irregularities, exposure to insolvent airline GoAir, and the use of adjusted financial metrics

Morpheus Research dropped a bombshell report on Monday accusing MakeMyTrip of continuing anti-competitive practices despite being penalised by India's competition watchdog over two years ago.

The activist short-seller disclosed it holds a short position in MakeMyTrip before detailing what it claims is evidence that the company is "openly defying" the Competition Commission of India's October 2022 order. That order had slapped a $26 million fine on the travel platform for what the regulator called "anti-competitive" and "abusive" conduct.

The core issue is price parity. This is a contractual restriction that prevents hotels from offering lower room rates on competing platforms or their own websites. If a hotel wants to list on MakeMyTrip, price parity clauses traditionally force them to maintain the same rate across all channels. The CCI found this practice abusive and ordered it stopped.

But Morpheus Research claims the enforcement never really ended. Instead, it simply went underground.

According to the report, MakeMyTrip now uses indirect enforcement through what it calls a "price competitiveness score." Hotels receive daily scores based on their pricing across platforms. Fall out of parity, and your ranking drops. Lower rankings mean less visibility. Less visibility means fewer bookings.

Hotel executives and former MakeMyTrip employees confirmed to Morpheus that price parity remains widely enforced, the report states. The mechanism is different, but the outcome is identical. Hotels cannot offer better deals elsewhere without facing consequences on India's largest travel platform.

This indirect approach is particularly concerning because it may evade regulatory scrutiny while achieving the same market control. The CCI's 2022 order specifically identified such tactics as problematic.

The allegations could not have come at a more sensitive time for MakeMyTrip. The company recently announced it is evaluating a potential domestic listing of its Indian business after completing internal restructuring that merged RedBus India into its main operations. A regulatory cloud hanging over its core business practices complicates that narrative significantly.

Beyond the competition concerns, Morpheus Research raised red flags about MakeMyTrip's financial reporting. The firm flagged exposure to receivables from GoAir, the airline that went insolvent, noting that only part of the amount appears provisioned. The report also questioned the gap between adjusted profits and figures reported under international accounting standards, suggesting the company uses "accounting tricks" to pad profits.

Additionally, the report accused MakeMyTrip of misleading customers through "manipulative dark patterns" and allowing problematic hotels to operate on its platform.

MakeMyTrip did not respond to PTI's requests for comment before publication.
