Gold Futures Surge ₹1,997 to ₹1.41 Lakh/10g on Global Rebound

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold futures surge ₹1,997 per 10 grams to ₹1,41,490 on MCX, marking a 1.43% single-day gain on March 27
  • 02/Rebound follows previous day's sharp fall of ₹4,604 or 3.2% that dragged prices to ₹1,39,493 per 10g
  • 03/Rally driven by global gold price recovery; April delivery contract on Multi Commodity Exchange

Gold futures on the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) surged ₹1,997 per 10 grams on March 27, 2026. The April delivery contract closed at ₹1,41,490. This marks a sharp rebound from the previous session.

The 1.43% gain comes a day after gold futures crashed ₹4,604 or 3.2%. Prices had fallen to ₹1,39,493 per 10 grams on March 26.

The rally is driven by a rebound in global gold prices. International markets have recovered after recent volatility. This has lifted sentiment in domestic futures trading.

The MCX gold contract has seen volatile swings this week. Traders witnessed sharp price movements amid changing global cues. Safe-haven demand continues to influence bullion markets.

Gold remains a key hedge against inflation and currency fluctuations. Indian investors track both domestic and international price movements closely for trading decisions.

The April contract is the most active gold futures series on MCX. Trading volumes have remained elevated during this period of price volatility and recovery.

Investors should monitor global economic data releases. Federal Reserve policy signals continue to impact gold price direction. Dollar movement and US bond yields remain key watch factors for the coming sessions.
