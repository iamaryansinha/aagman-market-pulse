US Expands Iran Ship Crackdown Globally, Authorizes Worldwide Seizures

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/US military authorizes worldwide seizures of Iran-linked ships, expanding beyond regional waters to Pacific and global routes
  • 02/16 warships deployed including 1 aircraft carrier, 11 destroyers, with 10,000+ troops enforcing the blockade
  • 03/14 ships turned around in first 3 days; ceasefire expires soon as mediators press for truce extension

The US military has expanded its crackdown on Iran-linked shipping from regional waters to worldwide operations. Forces are now authorized to intercept, board, and seize any vessel suspected of supporting Tehran anywhere on the seas.

Gen Dan Caine, chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, announced the expanded scope. He specifically pointed to Pacific operations targeting vessels that left before this week's blockade began outside the Strait of Hormuz.

US forces worldwide "will actively pursue any Iranian-flagged vessel or any vessel attempting to provide material support to Iran," Caine stated at the Pentagon. The military published an expansive list of goods it now considers contraband.

The banned materials include weapons, ammunition, and military equipment classified as "absolute contraband." Oil, iron, steel, and aluminum are listed as "conditional contraband" for dual-use concerns.

Electronics, power generation equipment, and heavy machinery can also be seized if "circumstances indicate intended military end-use," according to the military notice published Thursday.

The blockade involves 16 US warships: 11 destroyers, 3 amphibious assault ships, 1 aircraft carrier, and 1 littoral combat ship. Over 10,000 American troops are supporting enforcement operations.

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed that less than 10% of America's naval power is committed to the operation. US Central Command reported 14 ships turned around rather than confront the blockade in its first three days.

Some Iran-linked vessels have halted movements, turned off radio transponders, or headed back toward Iran's coast, according to shipping data firms monitoring the region.

The expansion comes as a ceasefire between Israel, the US, and Iran is set to expire within days. Mediators are pressing for an extension to the truce that has paused nearly seven weeks of war.

"Any ship that would cross the blockade would result in our sailors executing pre-planned tactics designed to bring the force to that ship," Caine warned. "If need be, board the ship and take her over."

The Navy is tracking vessels through satellites, drones, surveillance aircraft, and commercial shipping history. Vessels approaching the blockade receive radio warnings that they will be boarded for "interdiction and seizure."

Retired Vice Admiral Kevin Donegan, former commander of the Navy's Fifth Fleet, called the operation complex. "Not just from the intelligence standpoint, but positioning all the ships in the right way to make the intercepts if you have to," he said during a Middle East Institute webinar.

Donegan emphasized the blockade is one pressure point among many. He noted it must be paired with diplomacy for effective outcomes.

Adm Brad Cooper, head of US Central Command, made a rare Pentagon briefing appearance. He highlighted allied cooperation in creating "the largest air defense umbrella in the world across the Middle East."

The global shipping crackdown adds significant pressure on Tehran as ceasefire negotiations continue. The scope of contraband now covers both military goods and key industrial materials critical to Iran's economy.

Q: WHAT GOODS ARE NOW CONSIDERED CONTRABAND BY THE US MILITARY?
THE MILITARY LISTS WEAPONS, AMMUNITION, AND MILITARY EQUIPMENT AS "ABSOLUTE CONTRABAND." OIL, IRON, STEEL, ALUMINUM, AND OTHER MATERIALS ARE CLASSIFIED AS "CONDITIONAL CONTRABAND." ELECTRONICS, POWER GENERATION EQUIPMENT, AND HEAVY MACHINERY CAN ALSO BE SEIZED IF INDICATED FOR MILITARY USE.
Q: HOW MANY US WARSHIPS AND TROOPS ARE ENFORCING THE BLOCKADE?

Q: WHAT HAS BEEN THE IMMEDIATE IMPACT ON SHIPPING?
US CENTRAL COMMAND REPORTED 14 SHIPS TURNED AROUND RATHER THAN CONFRONT THE BLOCKADE IN THE FIRST THREE DAYS. SOME IRAN-LINKED VESSELS HAVE HALTED MOVEMENTS, TURNED OFF RADIO TRANSPONDERS, OR RETURNED TOWARD IRAN'S COAST.
