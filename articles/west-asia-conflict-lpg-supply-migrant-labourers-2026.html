West Asia Conflict: Government Doubles 5kg LPG Cylinder Supply for Migrant Labourers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Ministry of Petroleum doubles daily allocation of 5kg free trade LPG cylinders for migrant labourers across all states
  • 02/Daily sales jumped from 37,000 cylinders on March 25 to over 90,000 on April 4, targeting 1 lakh units daily
  • 03/Move comes as Strait of Hormuz remains closed; India imports 60% of LPG consumption from West Asia

The Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas has doubled daily supply of 5 kg free trade LPG cylinders to states. The order was released on April 6, 2026.

The allocation targets migrant labourers specifically. State governments will distribute cylinders through their Food and Civil Supplies departments.

Daily sales of 5kg FTL cylinders have surged significantly. From 37,000 units on March 25, sales crossed 90,000 on April 4. The government aims to push beyond 1 lakh units daily.

India faces mounting supply pressure due to the West Asia conflict. The Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint, remains closed. Officials describe the LPG situation as "concerning."

The country consumed over 33 million tonnes of LPG in FY25. Imports accounted for 20.67 million tonnes. Approximately 60% of consumption and 70% of imports originate from West Asia.

Domestic production currently stands at 46,000-47,000 tonnes per day. India has secured assured supply of 8 lakh tonnes from alternative sources including the US, Russia, and Australia.

PSU oil marketing companies have organised 550 awareness camps across 25 states and UTs. Over 6,700 cylinders were sold through these initiatives.

Oil Secretary Neeraj Mittal met states on April 2. He assured uninterrupted supply to households, hospitals, educational institutions, and migrant workers.
