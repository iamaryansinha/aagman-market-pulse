India To Boost US LPG Imports As West Asia Conflict Disrupts Supplies

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India imports 90% of LPG from West Asia; conflict has disrupted major production facilities in UAE, Qatar and Kuwait
  • 02/US NGL exports to India hit record 139,000 b/d in 2025, up from just 2,000 b/d in 2016
  • 03/US likely to remain key supplier through May-June 2026 as 220,000 b/d of Middle East capacity stays offline

India is pivoting to the United States for liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) imports. Escalating conflict in West Asia has disrupted supplies from the Middle East Gulf region.

The country imports over 90 per cent of its LPG from the Middle East Gulf. This region covers 92 per cent or roughly 720,000 barrels per day of India's total LPG imports.

US natural gas liquids (NGL) exports to India surged to a record 139,000 b/d last year. This marks a dramatic rise from just 2,000 b/d in 2016.

India currently sources 10 per cent of its LPG imports from Washington. Trade sources expect this share to increase further amid ongoing supply uncertainty.

Multiple LPG facilities in the Middle East have faced interruptions. Attacks have hit UAE's Al Ruwais Industrial Complex, Qatar's Pearl GTL plant and Kuwait's Mina Al-Ahmadi refinery.

Around 220,000 b/d of LPG export capacity could remain offline for an extended period. This includes outages at Saudi Arabia's Juaymah terminal and Qatar's Ras Laffan.

"US is likely to remain firmly positioned in the Asian markets at least through May and the first half of June 2026," said Anna Zhminko, Associate Market Analyst at Vortexa.

US NGL exports to India grew by 70,000 b/d in 2025, representing a 101 per cent increase year-on-year. US butane exports to India jumped to 36,000 b/d from just 2,000 b/d in 2024.

America's propane exports to India also hit a record 41,000 b/d last year. The US is the largest LPG supplier outside the Middle East Gulf region.
