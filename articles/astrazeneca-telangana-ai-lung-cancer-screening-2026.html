AstraZeneca Telangana AI Lung Cancer Screening: MoU for 20 Public Health Facilities

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/AstraZeneca Pharma India signs MoU with Telangana Government to deploy AI-powered lung cancer screening across 20 public health facilities
  • 02/Partnership uses Qure.ai's chest X-ray solution to flag high-risk pulmonary nodules and 29 other lung conditions for early detection
  • 03/Telangana projected to record 46,762 new cancer cases in 2026; 80-85% of lung cancer patients currently diagnosed at advanced stage

AstraZeneca Pharma India Ltd (NSE: ASTRAZEN) has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the Telangana Government. The deal was announced on April 7, 2026, in Hyderabad.

The partnership aims to introduce AI-enabled lung cancer screening across public healthcare facilities in the state. It targets both urban and rural populations.

Under the agreement, AstraZeneca will facilitate deployment of Qure.ai's AI-powered chest X-ray solution. The technology will be integrated into routine workflows at public health facilities.

The AI solution helps clinicians flag high-risk pulmonary nodules. These nodules are a predominant precursor of lung cancer. The system also detects 29 other lung conditions.

High-risk patients will be triaged for confirmation or follow-up. This ensures stage-shift of lung cancer at diagnosis, improving treatment outcomes.

The initiative will roll out across 20 public health facilities. It includes training and upskilling of healthcare professionals. Infrastructure enhancements will support seamless integration.

A similar model has been adopted in Goa, Tamil Nadu, and Karnataka. These states have seen early success in improving detection rates.

"Telangana is witnessing a steady rise in cancer cases, and late diagnosis due to limited screening uptake remains a major challenge," said S. Sangeetha Satyanarayana, Commissioner, Health & Family Welfare Directorate, Telangana.

Lung cancer incidence in India is projected to rise from 63,700 cases in 2015 to over 81,200 by 2025. This represents a 27% increase over the decade.

Between 80% and 85% of patients present with advanced disease at diagnosis. This contributes to nearly 60,000 deaths from lung cancer each year.

Cases among non-smokers are rising by 30% to 40%. This makes population-level detection efforts increasingly important.

"Early detection plays a defining role in improving cancer outcomes, particularly in diseases such as lung cancer," said Praveen Rao Akkinepally, Country President & Managing Director, AstraZeneca Pharma India Ltd.

Telangana is projected to record 46,762 new cancer cases among adults in 2026. This will rise to 47,314 by 2030. Women bear a disproportionate share with 25,510 new cases expected this year.
