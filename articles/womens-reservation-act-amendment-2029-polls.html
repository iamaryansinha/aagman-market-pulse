Women's Reservation Act Amendment: Parliament To Fast-Track 33% Quota For 2029 Polls

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Parliament will hold a special session from April 16-18, 2026 to amend the Women's Reservation Act for implementation in the 2029 general elections.
  • 02/The amendment will raise Lok Sabha seats from 543 to 816, with 273 seats reserved for women under the 33% quota.
  • 03/Opposition parties have objected to the timing, citing the ongoing state election model code of conduct.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi has announced that Parliament will amend the Women's Reservation Act to enable 33% reservation for women from the 2029 Lok Sabha polls. A special three-day parliamentary session will convene from April 16 to 18, 2026.

The Union Cabinet cleared the draft amendment bills on April 8, 2026. The proposed changes aim to fast-track the Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam, which was passed in September 2023.

Under the current law, women's reservation is tied to a delimitation exercise after the 2027 Census. This would delay implementation to around 2034.

The amendments will delink the quota from the pending census. The Lok Sabha strength will increase from 543 to 816 seats. Of these, 273 seats will be reserved for women.

PM Modi wrote to floor leaders of all parties seeking consensus. He called the move "one of the most significant decisions of the 21st century" dedicated to women's empowerment.

The Opposition has raised concerns about the session's timing. Congress president Mallikarjun Kharge called it a "clear breach" of the Model Code of Conduct amid Tamil Nadu and West Bengal elections.

Modi has assured states with successful population control records that their representation will not be reduced. The women's quota will be over and above current seat allocations.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE SPECIAL PARLIAMENT SESSION ABOUT?
PARLIAMENT WILL CONVENE FROM APRIL 16-18, 2026 TO AMEND THE WOMEN'S RESERVATION ACT AND IMPLEMENT 33% RESERVATION FOR WOMEN FROM THE 2029 LOK SABHA POLLS.
HOW MANY LOK SABHA SEATS WILL BE RESERVED FOR WOMEN?
THE AMENDMENT WILL INCREASE LOK SABHA SEATS FROM 543 TO 816. OF THESE, 273 SEATS WILL BE RESERVED FOR WOMEN UNDER THE 33% QUOTA.
WHY IS THE CURRENT LAW BEING AMENDED?
THE EXISTING LAW LINKS WOMEN'S RESERVATION TO DELIMITATION AFTER THE 2027 CENSUS, WHICH WOULD DELAY IMPLEMENTATION TO AROUND 2034. THE AMENDMENT REMOVES THIS DEPENDENCY.
WHAT HAS BEEN THE OPPOSITION'S RESPONSE?
CONGRESS AND TMC LEADERS HAVE OBJECTED TO THE SESSION'S TIMING, ARGUING IT VIOLATES THE MODEL CODE OF CONDUCT DURING ONGOING STATE ELECTIONS IN TAMIL NADU AND WEST BENGAL.
WILL ANY STATE LOSE SEATS DUE TO POPULATION CONTROL?
PM MODI HAS ASSURED THAT STATES WITH GOOD POPULATION CONTROL RECORDS, INCLUDING SOUTHERN STATES, WILL NOT LOSE ANY SEATS. THE WOMEN'S QUOTA WILL BE OVER AND ABOVE THE CURRENT ALLOCATION.
