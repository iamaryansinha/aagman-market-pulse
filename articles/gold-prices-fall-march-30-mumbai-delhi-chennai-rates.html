Gold Prices Fall March 30: Check 22K & 24K Rates in Mumbai, Delhi, Chennai

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold prices declined across all major Indian cities on March 30, with 22-carat gold falling by ₹60-75 per gram
  • 02/24-carat gold dropped by ₹63-79 per gram depending on the city; 8-gram prices down by ₹480-632
  • 03/Global gold prices remain steady despite geopolitical tensions pushing crude oil higher

Gold prices declined across major Indian cities on March 30, 2026. The drop affected both 22-carat and 24-carat gold rates.

India's 22-carat gold price stood at ₹13,550 per gram. This marks a decrease of ₹575 from the previous session.

For 8 grams of 22-carat gold, the price was ₹1,08,400. Buyers saved ₹600 on the 8-gram purchase.

24-carat gold prices also retreated. The rate fell to ₹14,228 per gram, down ₹78.

The 8-gram 24-carat gold price dropped to ₹1,13,824. This represents a decline of ₹624.

Mumbai recorded 22-carat gold at ₹13,550 per gram. The 24-carat variant traded at ₹14,228 per gram.

Chennai and Hyderabad saw identical pricing. Both cities quoted ₹13,600 for 22-carat and ₹14,280 for 24-carat gold.

Delhi matched Chennai rates. Ahmedabad quoted slightly higher at ₹13,604 for 22-carat gold.

Bengaluru traded at ₹13,610 for 22-carat gold. Kolkata remained the highest at ₹13,650 per gram.

Global gold prices remained steady despite volatility in other commodities. Crude oil surged past $115 following Houthi attacks.
