Railways Approves ₹398 Crore Optical Fibre Project For Kavach Safety Network

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian Railways approves ₹398 crore Optical Fibre Project to strengthen communication network for train safety
  • 02/The project is part of the broader ₹27,693 crore Kavach implementation plan under Works Programme 2024-25
  • 03/Kavach is India's indigenous automatic train protection system aimed at preventing collisions and accidents

The Union Cabinet has approved a ₹398 crore Optical Fibre Project for Indian Railways. The project will bolster the communication backbone for the Kavach safety system.

Kavach is Indian Railways' indigenous automatic train protection system. It is designed to prevent train collisions and over-speeding incidents across the rail network.

This optical fibre project falls under "Provision of Kavach with LTE communication backbone on Indian Railways' balance routes." The total sanctioned cost for the broader initiative stands at ₹27,693 crore.

The approval comes under the Works Programme 2024-25. This reflects the government's continued focus on railway safety infrastructure.

The LTE communication backbone will enable real-time data transmission between trains and control centres. This is critical for the effective functioning of the Kavach anti-collision system.

Railway safety has been a key priority for the government following several accidents in recent years. The Kavach system is being deployed across high-density routes and vulnerable sections.
