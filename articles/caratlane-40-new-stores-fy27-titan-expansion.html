CaratLane Eyes 40 New Stores in FY27 as Titan's Digital Jewellery Bet

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CaratLane, Titan's wholly-owned jewellery subsidiary, plans to open over 40 new stores in FY27, targeting 425 total outlets across 200+ towns
  • 02/The omnichannel brand reported ₹3,583 crore revenue in FY25 (24% growth) and ₹1,537 crore in Q3 FY26 (42% YoY jump)
  • 03/Expansion focuses on Tier 2-3 cities with 9-carat jewellery range to attract younger, price-conscious buyers

CaratLane plans to open more than 40 new retail stores in FY27. This will expand its network from 365 to over 400 outlets across India.

The company currently operates 365 stores in 149 cities. It targets 425 stores across 200+ towns by March 2027.

CaratLane is a 100% subsidiary of Titan Company (NSE: TITAN). Titan acquired the brand in 2016 and completed full ownership in 2024 for ₹17,000 crore total valuation.

The brand follows an omnichannel strategy. It integrates online sales with physical stores to drive customer acquisition.

CaratLane reported ₹3,583 crore revenue in FY25. This marked a 24% year-on-year growth with EBIT of ₹296 crore.

Q3 FY26 revenue jumped 42% to ₹1,537 crore. EBIT surged 53% to ₹200 crore with margins expanding to 13%.

The company added 24 stores in Q3 FY26 alone. Management stated profit for FY26 "is going to be significantly better."

CaratLane launched 9-carat jewellery in February 2025. This targets younger Indians seeking affordable daily-wear pieces.

Diamonds account for 90% of CaratLane revenue. The studded portfolio grew 35% YoY in Q3 FY26.

The brand plans international expansion with a second US store in Dallas. Two Dubai outlets are planned within eight months.

"Targeted promotions bundling coins resonated well with customers," Titan said. This drove conversions and higher average ticket sizes.

CaratLane contributes about 6% to Titan's overall revenue. It serves as the group's digital-first jewellery play alongside Tanishq.
