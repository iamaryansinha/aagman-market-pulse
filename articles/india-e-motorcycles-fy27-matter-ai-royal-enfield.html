India E-Motorcycles Eye 50,000 Units by FY27 on Matter AI Push, Royal Enfield Debut

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's electric motorcycle market targets 40,000-50,000 units by FY27, implying 130-190% growth from current levels
  • 02/FY26 sales reached 17,173 units per VAHAN data, up 28% YoY from 13,430 units in FY25
  • 03/Royal Enfield Flying Flea hits dealerships mid-April; TVS, Hero, Bajaj plan launches over next 18-24 months

India's electric motorcycle segment is targeting 40,000-50,000 units by FY27. This represents 130-190% growth from current levels.

FY26 sales reached 17,173 units per VAHAN data. This marks a 28% year-on-year increase from 13,430 units in FY25.

The category remains minuscule at under 0.1% of India's overall two-wheeler market. It trails the 13 lakh electric scooters sold during the same period.

Matter Motor Works is betting on AI-powered motorcycles to drive adoption. Its platform learns from rider behaviour to optimise performance and real-world range.

"If electric motorcycles have to go mainstream in India, they have to get smarter—not just cheaper," said Mohal Lalbhai, Founder and Group CEO, Matter.

Matter plans to roll out its first AI-based update by mid-2026. The company targets 20,000 units in FY27 and has raised nearly $100 million to date.

The competitive landscape is shifting rapidly. Revolt Intellicorp volumes declined 10% to 10,444 units in FY26.

Oben Electric grew 298% to 3,719 units from 1,248 units. Ultraviolette Automotive rose 384% to 2,102 units from 547 units.

Pricing pressure is intensifying. Ola Electric cut Roadster prices by up to ₹60,000, bringing entry variants close to 100cc petrol motorcycle levels.

Royal Enfield's Flying Flea debuts in dealerships by the second week of April. This marks the first major legacy player entry into electric motorcycles.

TVS Motor is preparing an electric Apache for late 2026 or early 2027. Hero MotoCorp and Bajaj Auto are expected to step up presence over 18-24 months.

"Electrification in India's two-wheeler market has been predominantly scooter-led," said Hemal Thakkar, Senior Practice Leader at Crisil.

Thakkar added that motorcycles remain ICE-dominated in the near term. Real scale-up depends on competitive pricing, range, and reliability delivered together.
