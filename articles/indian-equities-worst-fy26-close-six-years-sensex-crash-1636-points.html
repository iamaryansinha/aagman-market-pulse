Indian Equities Post Worst FY26 Close in Six Years as Sensex Crashes 1,636 Points

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex plunged 1,636 points in the final FY26 session ending March 31, 2026
  • 02/This marks the worst financial year for Indian equities in six years
  • 03/Watch West Asia war developments and rupee movement past 95 per dollar

Indian equities closed FY26 with their worst performance in six years. The Sensex crashed 1,636 points in the final trading session of the financial year.

The Nifty 50 index opened at 22,549 and briefly peaked at 22,714. It then plummeted to close at 22,283, reflecting deep investor anxiety.

The West Asia war has now entered its fifth week. Escalating geopolitical tensions continue to weigh heavily on market sentiment.

The Indian rupee has weakened past 95 against the US dollar. This currency depreciation adds pressure to import costs and foreign investor returns.

Foreign institutional investors have been net sellers through March. The combination of war risk and currency weakness drove sustained outflows.

Domestic institutional buying failed to offset FII selling pressure. Market breadth remained negative with declining stocks outpacing gainers.

All sectoral indices ended in the red during the final session. Banking and IT stocks led the decline with sharp intraday falls.

Analysts expect volatility to persist into FY27. Global uncertainty and domestic valuation concerns will likely cap near-term upside.
