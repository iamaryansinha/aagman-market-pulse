EID Parry Shuts Kakinada Sugar Refinery Citing Unviability Concerns

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/EID Parry suspends operations at Parry Sugars Refinery India's Kakinada facility due to commercial unviability
  • 02/PSRIPL faces estimated liabilities of ₹998 crore as of March 31, 2026, signalling severe financial stress
  • 03/Shutdown raises questions about sugar sector profitability and coastal refinery economics

EID Parry (India) Ltd has shut down the Kakinada sugar refinery operated by its subsidiary Parry Sugars Refinery India Pvt Ltd (PSRIPL). The closure stems from continued commercial unviability of operations.

The total estimated liabilities of PSRIPL stood at ₹998 crore as of March 31, 2026. This figure underscores the magnitude of financial challenges facing the refinery unit.

The Kakinada facility is a coastal sugar refinery processing imported raw sugar. Such refineries have faced headwinds from volatile global sugar prices and currency fluctuations.

EID Parry is part of the Murugappa Group, one of India's oldest business conglomerates. The group has interests in fertilizers, sugar, farm inputs, and engineering.

The sugar industry has been grappling with cane pricing pressures and export policy uncertainties. Coastal refineries face additional competition from integrated sugar mills located closer to cane-growing regions.

The shutdown decision suggests a strategic reassessment of capital allocation within the group. Investors will watch for any asset restructuring or impairment provisions in upcoming quarterly results.
