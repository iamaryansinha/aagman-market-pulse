Central Mine IPO: Shares List 10% Below ₹172 Issue Price On Debut

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Central Mine shares list at ₹154.80 on BSE and NSE, down 10% from ₹172 IPO price
  • 02/The issue saw modest demand and scraped through on the final day of subscription
  • 03/Stock trades at 5.7% discount at open before sliding further to close 10% below issue

Central Mine Limited shares listed at ₹154.80 on the exchanges. This marks a 10% decline from the ₹172 IPO price.

The Coal India subsidiary's public offer struggled during subscription. It barely scraped through on the final day with modest retail participation.

At the opening bell, shares were down 5.7%. The stock failed to recover and closed the session deeper in the red.

The ₹1,000 crore IPO was priced between ₹165-172 per share. The muted response signals weak investor appetite for mining sector listings.

Coal India holds a majority stake in Central Mine. The IPO was part of the government's divestment push for PSU subsidiaries.

The poor debut contrasts with recent IPO rallies. Several new listings this year had delivered double-digit listing gains.

Analysts attribute the weak showing to sector headwinds. Coal demand remains stable but ESG concerns weigh on institutional interest.

Retail investors who applied at the upper price band face immediate paper losses. The stock may remain under pressure until earnings visibility improves.

WHAT WAS THE IPO PRICE OF CENTRAL MINE LIMITED?
THE IPO PRICE WAS FIXED AT ₹172 PER SHARE AT THE UPPER END OF THE PRICE BAND OF ₹165-172. THE ₹1,000 CRORE PUBLIC ISSUE WAS ENTIRELY AN OFFER FOR SALE BY COAL INDIA.
HOW DID CENTRAL MINE SHARES PERFORM ON THE LISTING DAY?
CENTRAL MINE SHARES LISTED AT ₹154.80 ON BOTH BSE AND NSE. THIS REPRESENTS A 10% DECLINE FROM THE ₹172 IPO PRICE, WITH THE STOCK OPENING 5.7% LOWER AND CLOSING DEEPER IN THE RED.
WHAT WAS THE SUBSCRIPTION STATUS OF THE CENTRAL MINE IPO?
THE IPO SAW MODEST DEMAND AND SCRAPED THROUGH ON THE FINAL DAY OF SUBSCRIPTION. THE RESPONSE REMAINED SUBDUED ACROSS RETAIL AND INSTITUTIONAL SEGMENTS DESPITE THE PSU BACKING.
WHY DID CENTRAL MINE LIST AT A DISCOUNT?
WEAK INVESTOR APPETITE FOR MINING SECTOR LISTINGS, ESG CONCERNS AFFECTING INSTITUTIONAL INTEREST, AND OVERALL MARKET SENTIMENT CONTRIBUTED TO THE DISCOUNTED LISTING, ANALYSTS SUGGEST.
WHAT SHOULD INVESTORS DO NOW?
EXISTING INVESTORS FACE IMMEDIATE PAPER LOSSES GIVEN THE 10% DISCOUNT TO ISSUE PRICE. ANALYSTS RECOMMEND HOLDING FOR LONG-TERM OR EXITING ON ANY RECOVERY RALLY UNTIL EARNINGS VISIBILITY IMPROVES.
