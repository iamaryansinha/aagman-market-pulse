Why Unilever Is Selling Its Food Empire to McCormick

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Unilever negotiating $15.7 billion deal to merge food division with McCormick
  • 02/Move accelerates CEO's strategy to focus on personal care and beauty
  • 03/Food division grew just 2.5% last year, below company's 4-6% target

Unilever announced on Tuesday it is in advanced talks to combine its food business with McCormick, the American spice maker. The deal would deliver $15.7 billion in cash to Unilever and give its shareholders majority control of the merged entity.

This marks a sharp acceleration of CEO Fernando Fernandez's efforts to reshape the British-Dutch consumer giant. The playbook is familiar: double down on personal care and beauty where margins are fatter, while shedding food brands.

Unilever's shares have slipped to their lowest level since mid-2024. Investors worry that Fernandez could get distracted by yet another corporate restructuring.

The food business contributes more than a quarter of Unilever's total sales. It houses iconic brands like Knorr, Hellmann's, and Marmite. Last year, it generated €2.9 billion in operating profit with margins of 22.6%, above the group's average of 20%.

By Barclays estimates, the division carries an enterprise value of roughly €30 billion.

Yet the food division grew just 2.5% last year, lagging behind the rest of the group and falling short of Unilever's 4-6% annual growth target. Since the pandemic highs, underlying sales growth in foods has repeatedly disappointed.

Consumer habits are shifting against packaged foods. Politicians including US Health Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. have amplified concerns about ultra-processed foods. The rise of weight-loss drugs like Ozempic is changing what people buy and eat.

The proposed deal with McCormick would exclude Unilever's operations in India. The company did not elaborate, but India remains a crown jewel where food brands still command loyalty.

For Fernandez, the logic is clear. Personal care and beauty offer better margins, stronger pricing power, and demographic tailwinds.
