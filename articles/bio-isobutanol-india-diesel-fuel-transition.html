From Ethanol To Bio-Isobutanol: India's Diesel Fuel Transition

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India plans to blend bio-isobutanol with diesel after achieving 20% ethanol blending in petrol ahead of the 2025-26 target
  • 02/Diesel accounts for 40% of petroleum consumption; ethanol faces technical barriers for diesel blending including poor miscibility
  • 03/ARAI pilot project launched September 2025 runs until early 2027 to test blends up to 10% for commercial viability

India is pivoting from ethanol to bio-isobutanol for diesel blending. The move follows successful implementation of 20% ethanol blending in petrol under the Ethanol Blended Petrol (EBP) Programme.

Union Minister Nitin Gadkari announced the plan in September 2025. The Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MORTH) directed the Bureau of Indian Standards to set quality specifications for diesel-isobutanol blends up to 10% v/v.

Diesel accounts for nearly 40% of India's petroleum product consumption. It powers freight, agriculture, and industrial activity with direct pass-through to inflation across the economy.

Ethanol faces inherent technical barriers for diesel applications. These include low cetane number, poor miscibility, fuel stability issues, and altered fuel class characteristics.

Bio-isobutanol offers superior compatibility with diesel engines. It has higher cetane index, better miscibility, lower corrosiveness, and works with existing diesel infrastructure without expensive retrofitting.

The Automotive Research Association of India (ARAI) launched an 18-month pilot project in September 2025. The study includes lab tests, field trials, and engine performance assessments across Indian climatic conditions until early 2027.

Ethanol blending has already reduced crude import requirements by 4.5 crore barrels annually. The bio-isobutanol programme aims to extend these energy security benefits to the diesel segment.

"India's energy future will not be anchored in a single solution, but in a diversified portfolio of fuels," says Dr. Pramod Chaudhari, Founder Chairman of Praj Industries. The industry now has flexibility to manufacture both ethanol and bio-isobutanol.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
Q: WHAT IS BIO-ISOBUTANOL AND WHY IS INDIA EXPLORING IT?
BIO-ISOBUTANOL IS A FOUR-CARBON ALCOHOL DERIVED FROM FERMENTATION OF SUGARCANE, MOLASSES, AND AGRICULTURAL RESIDUES. INDIA IS EXPLORING IT BECAUSE ETHANOL FACES TECHNICAL BARRIERS FOR DIESEL BLENDING INCLUDING POOR MISCIBILITY, LOW CETANE NUMBER, AND FUEL STABILITY ISSUES THAT MAKE IT UNSUITABLE FOR DIESEL ENGINES.
