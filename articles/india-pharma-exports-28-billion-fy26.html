India's Pharma Exports Cross $28.29 Billion in April-February FY26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's pharmaceutical exports touched $28.29 billion during April-February FY26, registering 5.6% year-on-year growth
  • 02/The sector, currently valued at $60 billion, is projected to reach $130 billion by 2030 with $65 billion export target
  • 03/US accounts for 34% of exports followed by Europe at 19%; over 60% shipments go to highly regulated markets

India's pharmaceutical exports stood at $28.29 billion during the April-February period of FY26. The sector registered 5.6% growth compared to the same period last year, as per Pharmexcil data.

The growth has been driven by formulations, biologicals, vaccines and AYUSH products. These segments have shown resilience despite global pricing pressures and trade volatility.

"Despite global challenges, pharmaceutical exports have been among the few sectors to maintain growth momentum," said K Raja Bhanu, Director General of Pharmexcil. The sector is expected to end FY26 close to last year's performance of $30.47 billion.

India ranks third globally in pharmaceutical production by volume. The country supplies medicines to over 200 markets worldwide, earning the moniker "pharmacy of the world."

More than 60% of India's pharma exports are directed towards highly regulated markets. This underscores the industry's compliance with stringent global quality standards.

The United States accounts for 34% of total pharmaceutical exports. Europe follows at 19%, making these the two largest destination markets for Indian drug makers.

India currently supplies approximately 20% of global generic medicines. This dominant position in the generics market has been built over decades of cost-effective manufacturing capabilities.

Pharmexcil has set an ambitious export target of $65 billion by 2030. This will be achieved through policy prioritisation, market diversification and improved regulatory efficiency.

The sector is currently valued at around $60 billion. It is projected to more than double to $130 billion by 2030, underlining strong long-term growth prospects.
