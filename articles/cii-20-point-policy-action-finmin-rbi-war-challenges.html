CII Proposes 20-Point Action Plan For Finmin, RBI To Address War Impact On Economy

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CII submits 20-point policy action plan to Finance Ministry and RBI to address economic challenges from ongoing war
  • 02/Key recommendation: 3-month temporary moratorium and restructuring window for MSME exporters and ancillary units
  • 03/Proposal targets supply chain disruptions affecting export-linked businesses; awaits government response

The Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) has proposed a 20-point policy action plan. The recommendations are directed at the Finance Ministry and the Reserve Bank of India (RBI).

The proposal comes amid mounting economic pressures from ongoing geopolitical conflicts. Industry bodies are seeking proactive measures to protect vulnerable sectors.

A key recommendation involves temporary relief for MSMEs. CII has urged RBI to consider a three-month moratorium for small businesses.

The moratorium would specifically target exporters and ancillary units. These businesses are facing disruptions in export supply chains due to war-related challenges.

The proposal includes a clearly defined restructuring window. This would allow MSMEs to recalibrate operations without immediate repayment pressures.

Export-oriented units have been hit by shipping delays and payment uncertainties. The three-month window aims to provide breathing space for these businesses.

CII has also likely recommended additional fiscal and monetary measures. The 20-point plan covers multiple dimensions of economic resilience.

The Finance Ministry and RBI are yet to formally respond. Market participants will closely watch for policy announcements in the coming weeks.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT HAS CII PROPOSED TO THE FINANCE MINISTRY AND RBI?
CII HAS SUBMITTED A 20-POINT POLICY ACTION PLAN ADDRESSING ECONOMIC CHALLENGES STEMMING FROM ONGOING WAR CONDITIONS. THE RECOMMENDATIONS TARGET BOTH FISCAL AND MONETARY POLICY INTERVENTIONS.
WHAT IS THE KEY RECOMMENDATION FOR MSMES?
CII HAS PROPOSED A TEMPORARY THREE-MONTH MORATORIUM AND RESTRUCTURING WINDOW FOR MSMES. THIS APPLIES SPECIFICALLY TO EXPORTERS AND ANCILLARY UNITS LINKED TO EXPORT SUPPLY CHAINS.
WHY IS THE MORATORIUM BEING PROPOSED NOW?
EXPORT-ORIENTED MSMES ARE FACING SIGNIFICANT DISRUPTIONS DUE TO WAR-RELATED SUPPLY CHAIN CHALLENGES. THE MORATORIUM AIMS TO PROVIDE TEMPORARY BREATHING ROOM FOR THESE BUSINESSES.
WHICH MSMEs WOULD BENEFIT FROM THIS PROPOSAL?
THE PROPOSAL TARGETS MSMES INVOLVED IN EXPORTS AND ANCILLARY UNITS CONNECTED TO EXPORT SUPPLY CHAINS. THESE BUSINESSES HAVE BEEN DISPROPORTIONATELY IMPACTED BY GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS.
HAS THE GOVERNMENT OR RBI RESPONDED TO CII'S PROPOSALS?
AS OF THE PUBLICATION DATE (APRIL 5, 2026), THE FINANCE MINISTRY AND RBI HAVE NOT YET ISSUED A FORMAL RESPONSE TO CII'S 20-POINT POLICY ACTION PLAN.
