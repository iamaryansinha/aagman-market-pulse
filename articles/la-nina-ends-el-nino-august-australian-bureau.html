La Niña Ends: El Niño Likely To Emerge By August, Says Australian Bureau

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/La Niña has ended according to the Australian Bureau of Meteorology's latest Southern Hemisphere monitoring report
  • 02/All climate models forecast El Niño conditions emerging by end of winter (August), with some showing thresholds reached as early as May
  • 03/Previous El Niño in 2023 persisted 11 months, caused 2024 to be warmest on record and reduced Indian foodgrain production

La Niña has officially ended. The Australian Bureau of Meteorology confirmed this in its Southern Hemisphere monitoring report on March 31, 2026.

Oceanic and atmospheric indicators of the El Niño–Southern Oscillation (ENSO) are now neutral. This marks the transition phase between climate patterns.

All models forecast the tropical Pacific to continue warming. Neutral ENSO conditions are expected to persist through late autumn (March-May).

El Niño thresholds could be reached as early as May. However, most models indicate warming consistent with El Niño by end of winter (August).

For an El Niño state to last, ocean and atmosphere must work together. Both systems need to strengthen each other for the pattern to persist.

The Niño-3.4 index value was -0.42°C for the week ending March 29, 2026. This falls within the ENSO-neutral range of -0.80 to +0.80 °C.

A recent pulse of warming in the subsurface suggests further warming of sea surface temperatures is likely in coming weeks.

Past El Niño events have disrupted Indian monsoon patterns. They have led to either late arrival of south-west monsoon or deficient rainfall.

The 2023 El Niño broke out in June and persisted for 11 months. It resulted in 2024 being the warmest year on record.

Foodgrain crops, particularly paddy and pulses, were affected. This led to lower production and contributed to food inflation in India.

The 30-day Southern Oscillation Index (SOI) is +10.4 as of March 29. This remains above the La Niña threshold of +7.

The Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD) is currently neutral at -0.25°C. Model forecasts indicate the IOD will remain neutral until at least the end of May.
