La Niña Ends, El Niño Likely By August: Australian Weather Bureau Alert

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/La Niña has officially ended according to Australian Bureau of Meteorology's latest assessment
  • 02/Neutral ENSO phase currently dominates with 70% probability of El Niño emergence by August 2026
  • 03/Indian agriculture faces monsoon uncertainty; kharif crop planning may need adjustments

La Niña has officially ended. The Australian Bureau of Meteorology confirmed neutral ENSO conditions are now in effect globally.

Most climate models indicate steady warming of Pacific Ocean temperatures. The bureau estimates a 70% probability of El Niño developing by August 2026.

El Niño typically brings erratic monsoon patterns to India. This could impact rainfall distribution during the crucial June-September period.

The shift comes after a prolonged La Niña phase. La Niña generally supports above-normal monsoon rainfall in the Indian subcontinent.

Indian agricultural output is highly sensitive to monsoon performance. Kharif crops like rice, soybean, and cotton depend heavily on timely rains.

The transition period creates uncertainty for farmers. Sowing decisions for the 2026 kharif season may need contingency planning.

Global commodity markets monitor ENSO cycles closely. El Niño events historically correlate with volatility in agricultural futures prices.
