IMD Forecasts More Heatwave Days in May-June; Above-Normal April Rainfall Expected

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IMD forecasts above-normal heatwave days in May-June across east, central, northwest India and southeast Peninsula
  • 02/April rainfall expected above normal at over 112% of Long Period Average of 39.2 mm
  • 03/Heat stress may impact Boro rice, wheat, mustard, maize, and vegetables; advisories issued for farmers

The India Meteorological Department has released its summer season forecast for April-June 2026. Above-normal heatwave days are expected in May and June across east, central and northwest India.

April daytime temperatures will be normal to below normal across most of India. However, east, northeast, northwest India, and the southern peninsula will see above-normal maximum temperatures.

Above-normal minimum temperatures are likely over most parts of the country. Only Maharashtra and Telangana may see normal to below-normal minimum temperatures.

April rainfall is forecast at above-normal levels, exceeding 112% of the Long Period Average of 39.2 mm. Northeast India may see below-normal rainfall.

IMD Director-General M Mohapatra stated that coastal Odisha, West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, Puducherry, and Andhra Pradesh could see 2 extra heatwave days beyond the normal 3-5 days.

Heat stress may hit key crops during reproductive stages. Boro rice, maize, green gram, black gram, tomato, chilli, and brinjal face poor grain setting and flower drop risks.

Terminal heat stress may impact late-sown wheat, mustard, and barley in northwest India. This could cause grain shrivelling and reduced productivity.

The Agromet division has issued advisories for farmers. Light and frequent irrigation is recommended at critical growth stages for rice, maize, pulses, and oilseeds.

Farmers should apply foliar spray of potassium nitrate or anti-transpirants. Early harvesting of wheat and mustard is advised to minimise terminal heat stress losses.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS IMD'S FORECAST FOR HEATWAVE DAYS IN MAY-JUNE 2026?
IMD FORECASTS ABOVE-NORMAL NUMBER OF HEATWAVE DAYS IN PARTS OF EAST, CENTRAL, NORTH-WEST INDIA, AND THE SOUTH-EAST PENINSULA DURING MAY-JUNE 2026. COASTAL AREAS OF ODISHA, WEST BENGAL, TAMIL NADU, PUDUCHERRY, AND ANDHRA PRADESH MAY SEE 2 EXTRA HEATWAVE DAYS BEYOND THE NORMAL 3-5 DAYS.
WHAT IS THE RAINFALL FORECAST FOR APRIL 2026?
RAINFALL DURING APRIL 2026 IS MOST LIKELY TO BE ABOVE NORMAL, EXCEEDING 112 PER CENT OF THE LONG PERIOD AVERAGE OF 39.2 MM. HOWEVER, NORTH-EAST INDIA MAY EXPERIENCE BELOW-NORMAL RAINFALL.
WHICH CROPS ARE AT RISK FROM HEAT STRESS?
SIGNIFICANT HEAT STRESS MAY IMPACT BORO RICE, MAIZE, GREEN GRAM, BLACK GRAM, TOMATO, CHILLI, BRINJAL, WHEAT, CHICKPEA, LENTIL, MUSTARD, BARLEY, MANGO, AND BANANA. IMPACTS INCLUDE POOR GRAIN SETTING, FLOWER DROP, REDUCED YIELDS, AND GRAIN SHRIVELLING.
WHAT ADVISORIES HAS IMD ISSUED FOR FARMERS?
IMD HAS ADVISED FARMERS TO PROVIDE LIGHT AND FREQUENT IRRIGATION AT CRITICAL GROWTH STAGES. FOLIAR SPRAY OF POTASSIUM NITRATE OR ANTI-TRANSPIRANTS IS RECOMMENDED. EARLY HARVESTING OF WHEAT AND MUSTARD IS ADVISED FOR NORTH-WEST INDIA.
WHAT IS THE TEMPERATURE OUTLOOK FOR APRIL 2026?
MAXIMUM TEMPERATURES ARE LIKELY TO BE NORMAL TO BELOW NORMAL OVER MOST PARTS OF INDIA. EXCEPTIONS INCLUDE EAST AND NORTH-EAST INDIA, EASTERN PARTS OF CENTRAL INDIA, AND ADJOINING PENINSULAR REGIONS WHERE ABOVE-NORMAL TEMPERATURES ARE EXPECTED.
