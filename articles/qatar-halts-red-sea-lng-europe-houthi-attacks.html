Qatar Halts Red Sea LNG Shipments To Europe Amid Houthi Attacks Crisis

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/QatarEnergy halted all LNG tanker shipments through the Red Sea on January 15, 2024, following Houthi attacks on commercial vessels
  • 02/The diversion around Africa's Cape of Good Hope extends voyage times from 18 days to 27-29 days, tightening global LNG shipping capacity
  • 03/European countries including UK, Italy, and Netherlands face delayed deliveries despite high gas storage levels currently mitigating price spikes

QatarEnergy suspended all LNG shipments through the Red Sea on January 15, 2024. The state-owned company diverted at least six tankers bound for Europe around Africa's Cape of Good Hope.

The decision followed over 190 Houthi attacks on commercial vessels since November 2023. Yemen-based militants targeted ships in the Bab al-Mandeb Strait linking the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden.

Four Qatari LNG carriers were initially held up. The Al Ghariya, Al Huwaila, and Al Nuaman paused off Oman's coast on January 14. The Al Rekayyat stopped mid-Red Sea on January 13.

Qatar is the world's second-largest LNG exporter. The Gulf nation supplied 79.8 million tonnes globally in 2023. Europe received 15.1 million tonnes, representing 19% of Qatar's total exports.

The Suez Canal route from Qatar to Rotterdam takes approximately 17-18 days. The Cape of Good Hope alternative extends this journey to 27-29 days, adding 10-14 days of transit time.

European buyers including Italy's Edison experienced immediate delivery delays. A scheduled early February cargo to Edison's Adriatic terminal was pushed back by two weeks.

Major European importers affected include the UK, Netherlands, Italy, and Belgium. These nations rely on Qatari LNG for baseload power generation and industrial supply.

Dutch TTF benchmark gas futures initially dropped 8.5% below €30 per MWh. High European storage levels at 79% capacity and mild winter weather muted immediate price reactions.

The Red Sea normally handles 12% of global shipping traffic and 8% of LNG trade. Over 30% of global container traffic passes through this critical chokepoint.

QatarEnergy has maintained production levels despite shipping disruptions. The company is reshuffling global supply swaps to meet contractual obligations with European buyers.

The broader geopolitical context includes the Israel-Hamas war and Iran's regional influence. Houthi rebels claim their attacks support Palestinians in Gaza.

Insurance premiums for Red Sea transits surged 40% during the crisis. War risk coverage became prohibitively expensive for many LNG operators.

European LNG demand surged nearly 50% since Russia's 2022 Ukraine invasion. The EU now depends on LNG for 40% of its gas imports from alternative sources.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY DID QATAR HALT RED SEA LNG SHIPMENTS TO EUROPE?
QATARENERGY SUSPENDED RED SEA SHIPMENTS ON JANUARY 15, 2024, DUE TO SECURITY RISKS FROM HOUTHI MILITANT ATTACKS ON COMMERCIAL VESSELS. OVER 190 ATTACKS OCCURRED SINCE NOVEMBER 2023 IN THE BAB AL-MANDEB STRAIT AND RED SEA REGION.
WHICH EUROPEAN COUNTRIES ARE AFFECTED BY THE SHIPPING DIVERSION?
THE UK, ITALY, NETHERLANDS, AND BELGIUM ARE PRIMARY AFFECTED MARKETS. ITALY'S EDISON ENERGY CONFIRMED DELAYS TO ITS ADRIATIC TERMINAL. NORTHWESTERN EUROPEAN COUNTRIES DEPEND ON QATARI LNG FOR APPROXIMATELY 16-19% OF THEIR IMPORTS.
HOW MUCH LONGER DOES THE ALTERNATIVE CAPE ROUTE TAKE?
THE CAPE OF GOOD HOPE ROUTE ADDS 10-14 DAYS TO THE JOURNEY. QATAR TO ROTTERDAM TAKES 17-18 DAYS VIA SUEZ CANAL VERSUS 27-29 DAYS AROUND AFRICA. THIS EXTENDED TRANSIT TIES UP SHIPPING CAPACITY AND DELAYS SUBSEQUENT CARGOES.
WHAT IS QATAR'S POSITION IN GLOBAL LNG MARKETS?
QATAR IS THE WORLD'S SECOND-LARGEST LNG EXPORTER WITH APPROXIMATELY 19-20% GLOBAL MARKET SHARE. IN 2023, QATAR EXPORTED 79.8 MILLION TONNES OF LNG. THE US LEADS WITH 21-25% MARKET SHARE FOLLOWED BY QATAR AND AUSTRALIA AT 20%.
HOW HAVE EUROPEAN GAS PRICES RESPONDED TO THE SUPPLY DISRUPTION?
EUROPEAN BENCHMARK GAS PRICES INITIALLY DECLINED 8.5% TO BELOW €30 PER MWH DESPITE THE DISRUPTION. HIGH STORAGE LEVELS AT 79% CAPACITY AND MILD WINTER WEATHER OFFSET SUPPLY CONCERNS. PRICES REMAINED NEAR SIX-MONTH LOWS AS ALTERNATIVE LNG SOURCES COMPENSATED.
