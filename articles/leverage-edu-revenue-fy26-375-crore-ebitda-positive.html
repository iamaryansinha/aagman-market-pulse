Leverage Edu FY26 Revenue Doubles To ₹375 Crore, Turns EBITDA-Positive

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Leverage Edu revenue more than doubled to ₹375 crore in FY26 from ₹173 crore in FY25, achieving full-year EBITDA profitability
  • 02/The study-abroad platform placed over 10,000 students in FY26 and added 55,000 new users, taking total customer base to 175,000+
  • 03/Company achieved positive free cash flow and is exploring IPO plans for 2026 after hitting profitability milestones

Leverage Edu has reported revenue of approximately ₹375 crore for FY26. This marks a 116% jump from ₹173.3 crore in FY25.

The study-abroad platform turned EBITDA-positive for the full year. Founder Akshay Chaturvedi confirmed the milestone to VCCircle.

The company posted an EBITDA loss of ₹78.8 crore in FY25. The swing to profitability represents a bottom-line movement of over ₹85 crore.

Leverage Edu also achieved positive free cash flow in FY26. This marks a significant turnaround for the eight-year-old startup.

The company placed over 10,000 students in FY26. This is more than double the approximately 1,500 placements just a few years ago.

Student placement services contributed 75% of revenue. The UK accounted for 52% of placements, followed by Germany at 22%.

The platform added over 55,000 new users during FY26. Total customer base now exceeds 175,000 students.

Leverage Edu expanded its global footprint to 28 countries. It opened 15 experience centres in India, with about half already profitable.

"We have doubled revenue while turning EBITDA-positive, improved our unit economics, and continued investing in new products and geographies," said Akshay Chaturvedi, Founder and CEO.

The company's remittance vertical processed ₹2,000 crore in transactions. Accommodation services also more than doubled year-on-year.

Leverage Edu maintained cost discipline despite growth. Employee and marketing expenses rose 50% over two years, while corporate overheads increased less than 10%.

The startup is now weighing a potential IPO in India. The company expects to decide after hitting the $100 million revenue milestone in 2026.

India contributed 58% of Leverage Edu's student base. The company is expanding into Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Vietnam, and Malaysia.
