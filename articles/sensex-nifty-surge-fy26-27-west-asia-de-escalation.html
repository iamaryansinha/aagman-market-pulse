Sensex Nifty Surge 2.6% on First Day of FY26-27 as West Asia Tensions Ease

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex surges 1,911.93 points or 2.66% to trade at 73,859.48 on April 1, 2026
  • 02/Nifty 50 climbs 581.15 points or 2.60% to 22,912.55 on first day of FY26-27
  • 03/Rally driven by hopes of de-escalation in West Asia geopolitical tensions

Indian equity markets opened the new financial year with a sharp rally. The BSE Sensex surged over 2.6% in morning trade on April 1, 2026.

The 30-share Sensex opened at 73,762.43 and gained 1,911.93 points. It was trading at 73,859.48, up 2.66% from previous close.

The NSE Nifty 50 mirrored the uptrend with similar strength. The index opened at 22,899.00 and climbed to 22,912.55.

Nifty gained 581.15 points or 2.60% during the trading session. All sectoral indices traded firmly in the green.

The rally comes amid rising hopes of de-escalation in West Asia. Geopolitical tensions had kept markets volatile in recent weeks.

Foreign portfolio investors have been net buyers in March. Domestic institutional flows remain supportive at current valuations.

Analysts expect volatility to persist despite the strong opening. Global cues and quarterly earnings will drive sentiment this week.
