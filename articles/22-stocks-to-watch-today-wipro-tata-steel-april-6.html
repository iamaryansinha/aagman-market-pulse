22 Stocks To Watch Today: Wipro's $1B Deal, Tata Steel Faces Rs 1,755 Crore Notice

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Wipro secures 8-year $1 billion transformation deal with Olam Group and acquires Mindsprint for $375 million
  • 02/Tata Steel receives Rs 1,755.1 crore demand notice from Jharkhand for alleged excess coal mining during FY01-FY07
  • 03/Bharat Coking Coal faces operational disruption at ABOCP Mine; Emami to acquire Axiom Ayurveda for Rs 200 crore

Indian equity markets open against a backdrop of significant corporate developments across sectors. Twenty-two stocks are in focus today driven by major deals, regulatory actions, and strategic expansions.

Wipro Limited (NSE: WIPRO) leads the headlines with a landmark 8-year strategic transformation deal with Singapore-based Olam Group. The contract exceeds $1 billion in value with $800 million committed spend.

The deal includes Wipro's acquisition of Olam's IT arm Mindsprint for $375 million. Mindsprint employs over 3,200 professionals and brings proprietary platforms like Farmsprint and Tradesprint.

Tata Steel (NSE: TATASTEEL) faces headwinds after receiving a Rs 1,755.1 crore demand notice from the District Mining Office, Jharkhand. The notice alleges extraction of 1.62 crore metric tonnes of excess coal from West Bokaro Colliery between FY 2000-01 and FY 2006-07.

The company stated the demand lacks justification and substantive basis. Legal options are being evaluated to challenge the notice dated March 30, 2026.

Bharat Coking Coal, a Coal India subsidiary, reported disruption at its ABOCP Mine under Block-II. Unorganised individuals halted mining and dispatch operations since April 2. An FIR has been lodged at Baghmara police station.

Emami Limited (NSE: EMAMILTD) announced acquisition of the remaining 73.5% stake in Axiom Ayurveda for up to Rs 200 crore. The first tranche of 36.7% has been completed, taking Emami's holding to over 63%.

Axiom owns the AloFrut beverage brand and is expected to achieve Rs 180 crore topline in FY26. The deal marks Emami's formal entry into the health beverage segment.

Shree Cement (NSE: SHREECEM) board approved a Rs 1,800 crore integrated cement plant in Meghalaya. The facility will have 0.95 mtpa clinker capacity and 0.99 mtpa cement capacity. Completion is targeted by March 2028.

Control Print (NSE: CONTROLPR) announced Northeast expansion with Rs 8.61 crore investment. The company secured 60-year lease with Assam Industrial Development Corporation under the UNNATI scheme.

Prestige Estates Projects launched Prestige Golden Grove in Hyderabad. The 28.6-acre development has 10.36 million sq ft saleable area with estimated GDV exceeding Rs 9,500 crore.

J Kumar Infraprojects bagged a Rs 1,184 crore order for an international exhibition-cum-convention centre in Lucknow. The project includes a 10,000-person capacity convention centre.

Vikran Engineering received two LOAs aggregating Rs 530.80 crore from MSEDCL for distribution infrastructure development across Nashik and Kolhapur zones.

SRM Contractors secured a Rs 483 crore contract from Maharashtra State Infrastructure Development Corporation. Triton Valves won a multi-year TPMS supply contract with projected revenue of Rs 112 crore.

Marico's subsidiary completed acquisition of 75% stake in Vietnam-based Skinetiq. Premier Energies completed acquisition of 51% stake in Transcon Ltd.

Other stocks in focus include Senores Pharmaceuticals, SPR Auto Technologies, Shreeji Shipping Global, JK Lakshmi Cement, Kernex Microsystems, and Capital Trust.
