SEBI Confirms Interim Directions Against Par Drugs Chemicals Over ₹95 Crore Slump Sale

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI confirms interim directions against Par Drugs Chemicals Ltd (NSE: PAR) on March 25, 2026, upholding September 2025 order
  • 02/The proposed slump sale to promoter-related Phal-Jig Fine Chemicals was valued at ₹95 crore against pre-announcement market cap of ₹428 crore
  • 03/SEBI directs NSE to appoint independent valuer and obtain fairness opinion; PDCL restrained from asset disposal until further notice

SEBI has confirmed its interim directions against Par Drugs and Chemicals Ltd (NSE: PAR) in a confirmatory order dated March 25, 2026. The order prevents the Vadodara-based API manufacturer from proceeding with its proposed slump sale to a promoter-linked entity.

The regulator restrained PDCL from executing the Business Transfer Agreement signed on February 14, 2025, with Phal-Jig Fine Chemicals Pvt Ltd (PJFCPL). The proposed transaction was valued at ₹95 crore.

The slump sale would transfer PDCL's core profit-making business undertaking. This unit accounted for 99.44% of the company's total revenue, according to SEBI's investigation.

SEBI found prima facie irregularities in the valuation methodology adopted by PDCL. The valuers focused exclusively on six physical assets including industrial land and plant machinery. No value was attributed to intangibles such as employees, goodwill, brand equity, or technical knowledge.

The market capitalisation of PDCL has eroded by almost 70% since the slump sale announcement. It fell from ₹428.76 crore in December 2024 to approximately ₹139.74 crore by September 2025.

The company informed SEBI that the proposed slump sale was sought to be completed by September 20, 2025. SEBI's Whole Time Member Kamlesh Chandra Varshney noted that allowing the transaction would cause grave injustice to public shareholders.

SEBI directed the National Stock Exchange to appoint an independent registered valuer. The valuer must re-examine the business undertaking using recognised valuation standards.

PDCL, its management, and signatories to the BTA have been directed to cease and desist from entering into any transaction leading to disposal of assets. The company must fully cooperate with the independent valuer and submit all required information.

The confirmatory order noted that PDCL has failed to rebut prima facie findings recorded in the interim order. The detailed investigation remains in progress.

This action forms part of SEBI's broader crackdown on market manipulation and related-party transactions. The regulator has recently taken action against Jane Street Group for alleged index manipulation involving disgorgement of ₹4,843 crore.

Par Drugs and Chemicals was incorporated in 1982 and manufactures active pharmaceutical ingredients and fine chemicals. Promoter holding stands at 73.41% as of recent filings.

The order has come into effect immediately and will remain operational until further directions. PDCL has 21 days to file its reply or objections to the order.

WHAT IS THE SEBI ORDER AGAINST PAR DRUGS CHEMICALS?
SEBI HAS CONFIRMED INTERIM DIRECTIONS AGAINST PAR DRUGS AND CHEMICALS LTD (NSE: PAR) ON MARCH 25, 2026, RESTRAINING THE COMPANY FROM PROCEEDING WITH A PROPOSED ₹95 CRORE SLUMP SALE TO PROMOTER-RELATED ENTITY PHAL-JIG FINE CHEMICALS PVT LTD.

SEBI IDENTIFIED VALUATION IRREGULARITIES INCLUDING EXCLUSION OF INTANGIBLE ASSETS LIKE GOODWILL, BRAND EQUITY, AND TECHNICAL KNOWLEDGE. THE VALUATION FOCUSED ONLY ON SIX PHYSICAL ASSETS. THE BUSINESS UNIT ACCOUNTS FOR 99.44% OF PDCL REVENUE.

PDCL MARKET CAPITALISATION HAS ERODED BY ALMOST 70% SINCE THE SLUMP SALE ANNOUNCEMENT, FALLING FROM ₹428.76 CRORE TO APPROXIMATELY ₹139.74 CRORE. THE 52-WEEK RANGE IS ₹81.01 TO ₹348.45.
WHAT ARE THE SPECIFIC SEBI DIRECTIONS ISSUED?
SEBI DIRECTED NSE TO APPOINT AN INDEPENDENT VALUER, OBTAIN A FAIRNESS OPINION FROM A MERCHANT BANKER, AND RESTRAINED PDCL FROM EXECUTING THE BUSINESS TRANSFER AGREEMENT. THE COMPANY MUST CEASE ALL ASSET DISPOSAL TRANSACTIONS.
HOW LONG WILL THESE RESTRICTIONS REMAIN IN PLACE?
THE ORDER HAS COME INTO EFFECT IMMEDIATELY AND WILL REMAIN OPERATIONAL UNTIL FURTHER DIRECTIONS FROM SEBI. THE DETAILED INVESTIGATION IS ONGOING AND A COMPREHENSIVE PICTURE IS EXPECTED AFTER FINAL FINDINGS.
