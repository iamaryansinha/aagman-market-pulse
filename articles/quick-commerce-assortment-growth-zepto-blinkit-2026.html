Quick Commerce Platforms Shift Focus From Speed To Assortment Expansion

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Zepto offers nearly 40,000 SKUs, leading quick commerce platforms in product assortment depth
  • 02/Blinkit and Swiggy Instamart are expanding SKU range without disclosing exact counts in latest filings
  • 03/Industry shifts focus from 10-minute delivery races to assortment breadth and private labels

Quick commerce platforms in India are shifting strategy from speed to assortment.

Zepto currently leads the segment with approximately 40,000 SKUs available on its platform. This marks a significant expansion from the grocery-focused origins of quick commerce.

Rivals Blinkit and Swiggy Instamart have not disclosed exact SKU counts in their latest filings. Both platforms are actively broadening product categories beyond groceries.

The pivot comes as the India quick commerce market reaches $5.5 billion in 2026. The sector is projected to grow at 40% CAGR through 2030.

Market leader Blinkit holds nearly 50% market share as of September 2025. The Zomato-owned platform is planning private label expansion to improve margins.

Swiggy Instamart operates over 1,100 dark stores across 100+ cities. Average delivery time dropped from 17 minutes to 13 minutes in FY25.

Zepto commands approximately 28-29% market share with 8-10 minute delivery times. The platform secured fresh funding in 2025 to sustain city-level density expansion.

The shift to assortment depth reflects maturing consumer expectations. Speed is now baseline; selection drives retention and wallet share.
