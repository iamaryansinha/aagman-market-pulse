AM/NS India CEO Dilip Oommen to Retire; CFO Amit Harlalka Named Successor

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/AM/NS India CEO Dilip Oommen will retire on June 30 after 22 years with the company, including 7 years as chief executive
  • 02/CFO Amit Harlalka appointed new CEO effective July 1; he joined the company in December 2019 and became CFO in January 2023
  • 03/Oommen to continue as Board member of AM/NS India and Vice-Chairman of ArcelorMittal India after retirement

ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel (AM/NS) India has announced a leadership transition. CEO Dilip Oommen will retire on June 30, ending a 22-year association with the company.

Oommen, 68, served as the company's first CEO for seven years. He will continue as a Board member of AM/NS India and take on the role of Vice-Chairman at ArcelorMittal India.

The company has appointed CFO Amit Harlalka as the new CEO, effective July 1. Harlalka has also been appointed to the ArcelorMittal Group Management Committee.

Harlalka joined AM/NS India in December 2019 as Deputy Director, Finance. He was elevated to CFO in January 2023 after three years with the company.

Before his India role, Harlalka held finance positions across ArcelorMittal's global operations. His assignments included CFO in Mexico, project controller in Zenica, and financial controller in Luxembourg.

Harlalka is a chartered accountant from ICAI and holds an MBA in finance from IIM Bangalore. He began his career at Hindustan Unilever.

Aditya Mittal, Chairman of AM/NS India and CEO of ArcelorMittal, praised Oommen for bringing stability during change. He credited Oommen with building the ArcelorMittal Nippon Steel brand in India.

Mittal expressed confidence in Harlalka's operational knowledge. He noted Harlalka's involvement in growth plans spanning steel production and renewable energy initiatives.

Harlalka said his focus will be on unlocking the company's potential. He emphasized safety and supporting India's industrial growth ambitions as key priorities.
