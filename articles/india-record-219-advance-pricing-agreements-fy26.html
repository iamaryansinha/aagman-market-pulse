India Signs Record 219 Advance Pricing Agreements in FY26, Total Reaches 1,034

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India signed 219 Advance Pricing Agreements in FY26, marking the highest annual total since the program began in 2012
  • 02/The cumulative tally of APAs now stands at 1,034 agreements, demonstrating sustained government focus on tax certainty
  • 03/The APA program aims to reduce transfer pricing disputes and provide predictability for multinational corporations operating in India

India's Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) signed a record 219 Advance Pricing Agreements during FY26. This marks the highest annual count since the program's launch in 2012.

The cumulative tally of APAs has now reached 1,034 agreements. This milestone reflects the government's sustained commitment to providing tax certainty.

Advance Pricing Agreements are bilateral or unilateral arrangements between taxpayers and tax authorities. They determine transfer pricing methodologies for related-party transactions in advance.

The APA program helps reduce prolonged transfer pricing disputes. It provides predictability for multinational corporations operating in India.

FY26's record figure surpasses all previous annual tallies. The program has gained traction among MNCs seeking to mitigate tax uncertainty.

India has faced criticism for aggressive transfer pricing adjustments in the past. The APA mechanism offers a collaborative alternative to adversarial audits.

The rising APA adoption signals improved tax administration. It also indicates greater willingness among corporates to engage with the framework.

WHAT IS THE TOTAL NUMBER OF APAS SIGNED BY INDIA TO DATE?
THE CUMULATIVE TOTAL OF ADVANCE PRICING AGREEMENTS SIGNED BY INDIA NOW STANDS AT 1,034 AGREEMENTS.
WHAT IS AN ADVANCE PRICING AGREEMENT (APA)?
AN APA IS A FORMAL ARRANGEMENT BETWEEN A TAXPAYER AND TAX AUTHORITY THAT DETERMINES IN ADVANCE THE APPROPRIATE TRANSFER PRICING METHODOLOGY FOR RELATED-PARTY TRANSACTIONS OVER A SPECIFIED PERIOD.
WHY ARE APAS IMPORTANT FOR MULTINATIONAL CORPORATIONS?
APAS PROVIDE TAX CERTAINTY AND PREDICTABILITY, REDUCE THE RISK OF PROLONGED TRANSFER PRICING DISPUTES, AND ELIMINATE DOUBLE TAXATION CONCERNS FOR MULTINATIONAL CORPORATIONS.
WHEN DID INDIA'S APA PROGRAM BEGIN?
INDIA'S ADVANCE PRICING AGREEMENT PROGRAM WAS LAUNCHED IN 2012 BY THE CENTRAL BOARD OF DIRECT TAXES (CBDT) TO OFFER A NON-ADVERSARIAL ROUTE FOR RESOLVING TRANSFER PRICING MATTERS.
