BEL Posts Record ₹26,750 Crore Turnover In FY26, Stock Jumps 6%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Bharat Electronics reports record provisional turnover of ₹26,750 crore for FY26, marking 16.2% growth over previous year
  • 02/Export sales rose 33.65% to $141.9 million; total order book stands at ₹74,000 crore as of April 1, 2026
  • 03/BEL secured new orders worth ₹30,000 crore during FY26; stock jumped 5.83% to ₹424 on the NSE

Bharat Electronics Limited (BEL) has reported provisional and unaudited turnover of approximately ₹26,750 crore for FY2025-26. The Navratna defence public sector undertaking announced the figures on Wednesday.

The turnover marks a 16.2% rise over the previous year's ₹23,024 crore. The results remain provisional; audited figures are expected at a later date.

Export sales came in at around $141.9 million for FY26. This represents a 33.65% jump from $106.17 million in the prior year.

BEL secured new business worth ₹30,000 crore during the fiscal year. Export orders alone accounted for USD 346 million.

Major defence contracts included avionics for Light Combat Aircraft and mountain radars. The company also bagged electronic warfare suites for helicopters and fire control systems for tanks.

On the non-defence front, BEL won orders for IT infrastructure at AIIMS hospitals. The company also secured contracts for airport surveillance radars and electronic voting machines.

The company's total order book as of April 1, 2026 stood at approximately ₹74,000 crore. This includes an export order book of USD 495 million.

Manoj Jain, Chairman and Managing Director, said geopolitical developments underscored the need for self-reliance in strategic sectors. He added that BEL was focusing on reducing import dependency while investing in AI, cybersecurity, and autonomous platforms.

BEL shares were trading at ₹424.00 as of 12:10 pm on Wednesday. The stock gained ₹23.35 or 5.83% from the previous close of ₹400.65.

The scrip touched an intraday high of ₹430.20 during Wednesday's session.
