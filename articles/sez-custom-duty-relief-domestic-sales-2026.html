Finance Ministry Notifies Custom Duty Relief on SEZ Goods Sold Domestically

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Ministry notifies custom duty relief on SEZ goods sold in domestic tariff area
  • 02/Import duty reduced to 5-12.5% depending on product category for one year
  • 03/Move aims to boost SEZ competitiveness and support domestic manufacturing

The Finance Ministry has notified custom duty relief on goods manufactured in Special Economic Zones (SEZs) and sold domestically.

The import duty on such goods will be lowered to a range of 5% to 12.5%. The relief will remain effective for a period of one year from the date of notification.

SEZ units currently pay full customs duty when selling goods to the domestic market. This has made SEZ-manufactured products less competitive against imports and domestic goods.

The reduced duty structure aims to level the playing field for SEZ manufacturers. It will help them compete better in the domestic tariff area.

The relief applies to a wide range of products manufactured in SEZs. The exact duty rate will depend on the product category and classification.

Industry bodies have welcomed the move as a positive step. It addresses a long-standing demand from SEZ developers and manufacturing units.

The notification follows budgetary announcements to review SEZ duty structures. The one-year window allows stakeholders to assess the impact before a permanent framework.
