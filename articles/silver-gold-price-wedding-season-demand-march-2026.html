Silver Jumps ₹7,000, Gold Rises ₹3,700 on Wedding Season Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Silver prices jumped ₹7,000 to ₹96,400 per kg, a sharp 3.04% single-day surge driven by wedding season buying
  • 02/Gold gained ₹3,700 to ₹90,700 per 10 grams, rising 2.5% as retail demand strengthens across major cities
  • 03/Metals recovering from recent significant losses; outlook tied to remaining wedding season and global cues

Silver prices surged ₹7,000 to settle at ₹96,400 per kilogram on March 30, 2026. The 3.04% jump reflects robust wedding season demand across India.

Gold prices climbed ₹3,700 to reach ₹90,700 per 10 grams in the same session. The yellow metal gained 2.5% as retailers restocked inventory.

The rally comes despite both metals posting significant losses in recent weeks. Precious metals had faced pressure from stronger dollar and weak global cues.

India's wedding season typically runs from November through May. Gold and silver purchases peak during this period for jewelry and gift purposes.

Silver's industrial applications add another demand layer beyond investment and ornamental use. The metal finds usage in electronics, solar panels, and medical equipment.

Retail jewelers in Mumbai, Delhi, and Chennai reported brisk buying activity. Physical demand remains strong even as institutional investors remain cautious.

Analysts expect volatility to continue through the remaining wedding season. Global Fed policy direction and rupee movement against dollar will influence further price action.
