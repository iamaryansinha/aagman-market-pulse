Cotton Prices Surge Above MSP as Global Rally Fuels Domestic Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Cotton Corporation of India (CCI) hiked its floor price by ₹1,300 per candy on Monday, bringing total increases since early March to ₹3,200 per candy (356 kg)
  • 02/The move aligns with a 15% surge in global ICE cotton futures since March 1, with May delivery crossing 70.12 cents/lb
  • 03/Domestic raw cotton (kapas) prices have now climbed above the MSP of ₹8,110/quintal in key markets like Raichur and Adoni, trading between ₹8,400-8,700

The cotton market is finally catching a break. After months of languishing below support levels, domestic cotton prices have punched above the minimum support price (MSP) threshold—driven by a potent mix of global momentum and resilient domestic demand.

Cotton Corporation of India (CCI), the state-run agency that procures and sells cotton to stabilize prices, raised its floor price by ₹1,300 per candy on Monday. This marks the third hike since early March, with cumulative increases now totaling ₹3,200 per candy (356 kg) for the 2025-26 crop.

The revision tracks a sharp rally in international markets. ICE cotton futures have gained roughly 15% since the first week of March, with May delivery contracts breaching 70.12 cents per pound and July delivery hovering at 73.28 cents. The global firmness reflects tightening supply expectations and steady demand from textile-consuming regions.

On the ground, the impact is visible. Raw cotton (kapas) prices in key producing regions—Raichur, Adoni, parts of Maharashtra and Gujarat—have moved decisively above the MSP of ₹8,110 per quintal. In Raichur, rates are holding at ₹8,500-8,600; in Adoni, they're fetching ₹8,500-8,700.

"Kapas prices have finally moved above the MSP levels after ruling below the support price for most part of the marketing season," notes Ramanuj Das Boob, a sourcing agent in Raichur. "After today's price increase, the kapas prices may go up further to ₹8,800 levels—encouraging farmers to plant more in the upcoming kharif season."

The psychology shift matters. For months, farmers have faced depressed realizations despite the MSP safety net. Now, with market prices offering a premium over support levels, the incentive structure is reversing. Higher prices typically translate to expanded acreage in the following season—critical for a commodity where planting decisions are highly price-sensitive.

Demand-side dynamics reinforce the bullish setup. Despite CCI's upward price revisions, buying interest from traders and millers hasn't wavered. On Monday alone, CCI offloaded close to 3 lakh bales. For premium-quality lots, buyers are reportedly paying above the notified rates—a sign of underlying tightness in the physical market.

The cotton value chain—spinners, traders, and exporters—now faces a recalibration. Input costs are rising at a time when global textile demand remains uneven. How much of this cost pressure gets passed downstream will determine whether the current rally sustains or faces resistance from end-user markets.

For now, the trend favors producers. With most of the 2025-26 crop already marketed, the late-season price surge benefits farmers holding residual stocks while setting a constructive tone for the kharif planting window ahead.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS CCI AND WHY DOES IT SET FLOOR PRICES?
COTTON CORPORATION OF INDIA IS A GOVERNMENT AGENCY THAT PROCURES COTTON FROM FARMERS AT MSP AND SELLS IT TO MILLS/TRADERS. IT ADJUSTS SALE PRICES PERIODICALLY TO ALIGN WITH MARKET DYNAMICS WHILE ENSURING FARMER INCOMES REMAIN PROTECTED.
HOW BIG IS ONE CANDY OF COTTON?
ONE CANDY EQUALS 356 KILOGRAMS. IT'S THE STANDARD UNIT FOR BULK COTTON TRADING IN INDIA.
WHY DID COTTON PRICES RISE SUDDENLY?
TWO FACTORS: (1) GLOBAL ICE COTTON FUTURES RALLIED ~15% SINCE EARLY MARCH DUE TO SUPPLY CONCERNS; (2) STRONG DOMESTIC BUYING INTEREST FROM TRADERS AND TEXTILE MILLS, EVEN AT HIGHER PRICE LEVELS.
WHAT DOES THIS MEAN FOR FARMERS?
POSITIVE. PRICES ARE NOW ABOVE MSP (₹8,110/QUINTAL) IN MAJOR MARKETS, MEANING FARMERS GET BETTER REALIZATIONS THAN THE GOVERNMENT-GUARANTEED MINIMUM. THIS MAY ALSO ENCOURAGE MORE COTTON PLANTING IN THE UPCOMING KHARIF SEASON.
WILL COTTON PRICES RISE FURTHER?
MARKET PARTICIPANTS SEE POTENTIAL FOR KAPAS TO REACH ₹8,800/QUINTAL. HOWEVER, MUCH DEPENDS ON GLOBAL FUTURES MOVEMENTS AND THE PACE OF DOMESTIC DEMAND FROM TEXTILE MILLS.
WHAT IS THE MSP FOR COTTON?
FOR THE 2025-26 SEASON, THE MSP FOR MEDIUM STAPLE COTTON IS ₹8,110 PER QUINTAL.
HOW MUCH COTTON DID CCI SELL ON MONDAY?
CCI SOLD APPROXIMATELY 3 LAKH BALES ON MONDAY ALONE, INDICATING STRONG DEMAND DESPITE HIGHER PRICES.
