Electric 4-Wheeler Registrations Surge 88% in FY26; Tata Motors Lead Narrows Amid Rising Competition

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Electric 4-wheeler registrations surged 88% in FY26 to 1.91 lakh units, up from 1.01 lakh in FY25
  • 02/Tata Motors retained leadership but market share dropped sharply from 53.3% to 38.7%
  • 03/Mahindra Electric emerged as a strong challenger with share climbing from 7.5% to 20.8%

Electric passenger vehicle registrations in India reached 1.91 lakh units in FY26. This marks an 88% jump from 1.01 lakh units recorded in the previous fiscal year.

The data from the government's Vahan portal reflects strong consumer demand. An expanding product lineup from multiple manufacturers drove this growth.

Tata Motors maintained its market leadership position in FY26. However, its dominance weakened significantly with market share falling from 53.3% to 38.7%.

JSW MG Motor, the second-largest player, also witnessed a decline in market share. The company had emerged as Tata's closest challenger over the past year.

Mahindra Electric Automobile emerged as the biggest gainer. Its market share climbed sharply from 7.5% to 20.8%, driven by strong demand for its electric SUV portfolio.

New entrants disrupted the market in FY26. Maruti Suzuki and VinFast began registrations during the fiscal and gained traction in the second half.

In March 2026 alone, Maruti Suzuki and VinFast displaced JSW MG Motor and BYD India. They claimed the fourth and fifth positions respectively, highlighting a rapidly evolving landscape.

Models such as the Maruti e-Vitara, Mahindra XEV 9s, and Tata Punch EV significantly boosted volumes. These models were absent from the market a year ago.

The top three players now control nearly 87% of the market. This signals a shift from a single-leader phase to what experts call a "high-growth, competitive triopoly."

"The shifting market shares point to a transition phase in India's EV market, where early movers are facing pressure from newer launches with longer range, faster charging and aggressive pricing," said Puneet Gupta, Director at S&P Global Automotive.

Analysts noted a significant structural shift in the competitive dynamics. JSW MG Motor and Mahindra Electric together have overtaken Tata Motors in combined volume terms.

Improved charging infrastructure and wider OEM participation could accelerate adoption further. Geopolitical volatility in West Asia is also reinforcing the long-term case for electrification.
