ED Files Chargesheet Against Ex-Reliance Communications Director Punit Garg in ₹40,000 Crore Fraud Case

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ED files prosecution complaint against Punit Garg, former RCom director, in alleged ₹40,000 crore money laundering case linked to bank fraud
  • 02/Chargesheet alleges diversion of funds to purchase $8.3 million Manhattan condo, sold fraudulently during CIRP without Resolution Professional consent
  • 03/Case to be heard at Rouse Avenue Court on March 28; Garg's interim bail plea also scheduled for hearing

The Enforcement Directorate has filed a chargesheet against Punit Garg, former Director of Reliance Communications Ltd. The prosecution complaint was submitted at a Rouse Avenue Court in Delhi on March 27, 2026.

Garg was arrested by the ED on January 29, 2026. He served in senior managerial and directorial positions at RCom from 2001 to 2025.

The case involves an alleged ₹40,000 crore bank fraud. The ED claims Garg was actively involved in acquisition, possession, concealment, layering, and dissipation of proceeds of crime.

The agency alleges laundered funds were diverted through multiple foreign subsidiaries and offshore entities of RCom. Investigators traced proceeds to a luxury condominium purchase in Manhattan, New York.

The property was allegedly offloaded during RCom's Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process. Garg sold it fraudulently without the Resolution Professional's knowledge or consent.

Sale proceeds of $8.3 million were remitted from the USA under a sham investment arrangement. The funds went to a Dubai-based entity controlled by a Pakistan-linked individual.

The ED also found that part of public bank loans was routed for Garg's personal expenses. This included overseas education-related payments for his children.

The chargesheet has been marked to Special Judge Ajay Gupta. It will come up for hearing on Saturday, March 28, 2026. Garg's interim bail application will also be heard the same day.

This is a fresh chargesheet separate from an earlier December 2025 case. In that matter, the ED filed a prosecution complaint against Reliance Power Ltd and 10 others for an alleged fake bank guarantee of ₹68 crore.
