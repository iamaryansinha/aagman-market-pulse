APSEZ Sets Up India's First Port of Refuge at Dighi and Gopalpur Ports

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/APSEZ designates Dighi Port (west coast) and Gopalpur Port (east coast) as India's first official Ports of Refuge for distressed vessels
  • 02/Dighi will support traffic across Arabian Sea and Persian Gulf routes; Gopalpur covers Bay of Bengal maritime corridors
  • 03/Move strengthens India's maritime safety infrastructure and positions APSEZ as a critical player in regional shipping security

Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone Ltd (NSE: ADANIPORTS) has operationalised India's first Port of Refuge (PoR) facilities. The company designated Dighi Port on the west coast and Gopalpur Port on the east coast as official shelters for vessels in distress.

A Port of Refuge is a location where ships facing emergencies can seek shelter. These facilities prevent further damage to vessels and protect crew safety, cargo, and marine environment.

Dighi Port will support traffic across the Arabian Sea and routes to the Persian Gulf. Gopalpur Port covers maritime corridors along the Bay of Bengal and eastern shipping routes.

This marks the first time India has designated official Ports of Refuge. The move aligns with International Maritime Organization (IMO) guidelines on places of refuge for ships in need of assistance.

APSEZ currently operates 15 ports across India. The company handles approximately 27% of the country's total port volumes, making it the largest private port operator in India.

The development comes as global shipping faces increasing risks from container fires, structural damage, and severe weather events. Access to designated refuge ports reduces environmental hazards and salvage costs.

APSEZ shares closed at ₹1,375.90 on March 25, 2026. The company commands a market capitalization of approximately ₹3.17 lakh crore.

JPMorgan recently initiated coverage on APSEZ with an 'Overweight' rating and price target of ₹1,944. The brokerage cited structural growth opportunities in India's port sector and high entry barriers for competitors.

The company's cargo volumes grew 16% year-on-year in February 2026. It handled 42.5 million metric tonnes during the month.

Q: WHAT IS A PORT OF REFUGE AND WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?
A PORT OF REFUGE IS A DESIGNATED LOCATION WHERE VESSELS IN DISTRESS CAN SEEK SHELTER TO STABILIZE THEIR CONDITION, PROTECT HUMAN LIFE, AND PREVENT ENVIRONMENTAL DAMAGE. IT IS RECOGNIZED UNDER INTERNATIONAL MARITIME LAW AS A CRITICAL SAFETY INFRASTRUCTURE FOR SHIPS FACING MECHANICAL FAILURES, STRUCTURAL DAMAGE, FIRES, OR SEVERE WEATHER.
