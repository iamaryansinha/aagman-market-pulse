La Niña Ends, El Niño Likely by August: Australian Weather Bureau

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Australian Bureau of Meteorology confirms La Niña has ended, neutral ENSO conditions currently in effect
  • 02/Climate models forecast El Niño development by August 2026, with some predicting thresholds as early as May
  • 03/El Niño historically causes delayed or deficient Indian monsoon, impacting foodgrain production

The Australian Bureau of Meteorology has officially declared the end of La Niña. Oceanic and atmospheric indicators now show neutral ENSO conditions across the tropical Pacific.

All climate models forecast continued warming of the tropical Pacific in coming months. Neutral conditions are expected to persist through late autumn.

El Niño thresholds are projected to be reached by the end of Southern Hemisphere winter in August. Some models suggest thresholds could be hit as early as May.

The Niño-3.4 index stood at -0.42°C for the week ending March 29, 2026. This falls within the neutral range of -0.80°C to +0.80°C.

A warming pulse in the subsurface ocean suggests further sea surface temperature increases ahead. This supports the projected transition toward El Niño.

Historical El Niño events have disrupted India's south-west monsoon patterns. They typically cause delayed onset, deficient rainfall, or prolonged dry spells.

The 2023 El Niño event lasted 11 months and affected Indian monsoon performance. It contributed to 2024 becoming the warmest year on record.

Foodgrain crops including paddy and pulses suffered during the previous El Niño. Production declines led to elevated food inflation across India.

Interestingly, India may receive above-normal rainfall before El Niño fully develops. This window could benefit early agricultural planting cycles.

The Indian Ocean Dipole remains neutral at -0.25°C as of March 29. Model forecasts suggest it will stay neutral through at least May.
