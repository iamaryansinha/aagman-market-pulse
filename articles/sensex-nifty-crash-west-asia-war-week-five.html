Sensex Crashes 1,191 Points as West Asia War Enters Fifth Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BSE Sensex crashed 1,191.24 points to 72,391.98 in early trade as West Asia conflict enters fifth week
  • 02/NSE Nifty slumped 349.45 points to 22,470.15; all major sectoral indices trade sharply lower
  • 03/Geopolitical tensions escalate; oil price surge and inflation fears trigger massive risk-off sentiment

Indian stock markets opened sharply lower on March 30, 2026. The BSE Sensex plunged 1,191.24 points to 72,391.98 in early trade.

The 50-share NSE Nifty mirrored the decline. It fell 349.45 points to 22,470.15.

All sectoral indices traded in the red. Banking, auto, and metal stocks led the losses.

The sell-off comes as the West Asia war enters its fifth week. Escalating geopolitical tensions have spooked global investors.

Crude oil prices have surged above $85 per barrel. This raises inflation concerns for oil-importing nations like India.

Foreign institutional investors have been net sellers for three consecutive sessions. They pulled out over ₹4,500 crore last week.

The rupee weakened past 86 against the dollar. It hit a fresh all-time low in morning trade.

Asian markets also traded lower. Japan's Nikkei and Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell over 1.5% each.

Analysts expect volatility to continue. Investors are advised to avoid panic selling and stay diversified.
