RBI Proposes 1-Hour Delay for Digital Transfers Above ₹10,000 to Combat Fraud

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/RBI proposes mandatory one-hour cooling-off period for all digital transfers exceeding ₹10,000 starting mid-2026
  • 02/The delay allows users to report fraudulent transactions and reverse payments before funds leave their accounts
  • 03/Digital payment volumes in India crossed ₹15 lakh crore monthly; fraud complaints rose 16% year-on-year

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed a mandatory one-hour delay for digital transfers above ₹10,000. The cooling-off period aims to combat rising payment fraud in the country.

Users will be able to cancel or reverse transactions within the one-hour window. This applies to UPI, NEFT, RTGS, and other digital payment modes above the threshold.

The proposal was announced on April 10, 2026. RBI Governor Sanjay Malhotra highlighted the need for stronger consumer protection.

India processed over ₹15 lakh crore in digital payments monthly during FY2026. Fraud complaints have risen 16% compared to the previous year.

The cooling-off mechanism will allow banks to freeze suspicious transactions. Users will receive real-time alerts during the delay period.

Industry experts have raised concerns about transaction velocity. E-commerce and gig economy platforms may face checkout friction.

The proposal is open for public consultation until May 15, 2026. RBI expects to implement the rule by July 2026 after stakeholder feedback.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE RBI PROPOSING FOR DIGITAL TRANSFERS?
THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA HAS PROPOSED A MANDATORY ONE-HOUR COOLING-OFF PERIOD FOR ALL DIGITAL TRANSFERS EXCEEDING ₹10,000. USERS CAN CANCEL OR REVERSE TRANSACTIONS WITHIN THIS WINDOW BEFORE FUNDS ARE DEBITED.
WHICH PAYMENT METHODS WILL THE DELAY APPLY TO?
THE PROPOSED DELAY WILL APPLY TO UPI, NEFT, RTGS, AND OTHER DIGITAL PAYMENT MODES WHERE THE TRANSFER AMOUNT EXCEEDS ₹10,000. IT COVERS BOTH PERSON-TO-PERSON AND PERSON-TO-MERCHANT TRANSACTIONS.
WHY IS RBI IMPLEMENTING THIS MEASURE?
THE MEASURE AIMS TO CURB RISING DIGITAL PAYMENT FRAUD. FRAUD COMPLAINTS ROSE 16% YEAR-ON-YEAR WHILE INDIA PROCESSED OVER ₹15 LAKH CRORE IN DIGITAL PAYMENTS MONTHLY DURING FY2026.
WHEN WILL THIS RULE COME INTO EFFECT?
THE PROPOSAL IS OPEN FOR PUBLIC CONSULTATION UNTIL MAY 15, 2026. RBI EXPECTS TO IMPLEMENT THE COOLING-OFF RULE BY JULY 2026 AFTER INCORPORATING STAKEHOLDER FEEDBACK.
HOW WILL USERS BE NOTIFIED DURING THE DELAY PERIOD?
USERS WILL RECEIVE REAL-TIME ALERTS DURING THE ONE-HOUR WINDOW. BANKS WILL PROVIDE OPTIONS TO CANCEL OR CONFIRM TRANSACTIONS THROUGH MOBILE APPS, SMS, AND CUSTOMER CARE CHANNELS.
