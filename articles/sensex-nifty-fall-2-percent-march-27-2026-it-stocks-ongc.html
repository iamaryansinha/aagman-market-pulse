Sensex Nifty Fall 2% March 27: IT Stocks Defy Global Selloff, ONGC Leads Gainers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BSE Sensex plunged 1,319.42 points or 1.75% to 73,954.03 by 1:10 pm on March 27, 2026
  • 02/Nifty 50 declined 381.50 points or 1.64% to 22,924.95 amid global selloff and weak sentiment
  • 03/IT stocks bucked the trend with Nifty IT Index trading in green; ONGC, TCS, Wipro led gainers

Indian equity benchmarks crashed sharply on Friday, March 27, 2026. The BSE Sensex fell 1,319.42 points or 1.75% to 73,954.03 by 1:10 pm.

The Nifty 50 index declined 381.50 points or 1.64% to 22,924.95. The selloff was triggered by rising geopolitical tensions and weaker global economic outlook.

The Indian rupee tumbled to a fresh all-time low of 94.70 against the US dollar. Elevated oil prices and a stronger greenback pressured the domestic currency.

The Nifty IT Index emerged as the sole sector trading in positive territory. IT heavyweights bucked the broader market trend amid the widespread selloff.

Oil and Natural Gas Corporation (NSE: ONGC) led the Nifty 50 gainers. Tata Consultancy Services (NSE: TCS) and Wipro (NSE: WIPRO) also posted gains defying the rout.

Bajaj Finance, Bajaj Finserv, and IndiGo emerged as top losers on Nifty 50. TMPV and Reliance Industries also weighed heavily on the benchmarks.

Broader markets mirrored the weakness with midcap and smallcap indices declining nearly 2% each. The selloff was widespread across market segments.

Market breadth remained sharply negative on the National Stock Exchange. Only 491 stocks advanced while 2,702 declined and 76 remained unchanged.

PSU banks, financials, chemicals, media and auto stocks dropped between 2% and 3%. Nearly 477 stocks hit their 52-week lows including Hindustan Aeronautics.

The decline reversed part of the previous session's rally. On Wednesday, Sensex had surged 1,205 points or 1.63% to close at 75,273.45.
