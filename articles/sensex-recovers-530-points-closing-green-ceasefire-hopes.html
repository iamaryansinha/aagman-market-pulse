Sensex Recovers 530 Pts To Close Green On Iran-Israel Ceasefire Hopes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex recovers over 530 points from morning lows to close 311 points higher at 74,602
  • 02/Iran-Israel ceasefire hopes trigger buying across sectors; oil prices retreat from multi-week highs
  • 03/Nifty holds 22,500; market breadth improves with advances outpacing declines

Indian equity markets staged a dramatic recovery on Monday. The BSE Sensex swung over 530 points from its intraday low to close firmly in positive territory.

The 30-share Sensex ended at 74,602, up 311 points or 0.42%. It had dropped to 74,058 in early trade, down 233 points from previous close.

The broader Nifty50 settled at 22,546, gaining 80 points or 0.36%. The index defended the psychologically important 22,500 mark throughout the session.

Geopolitical tensions eased as reports emerged of potential ceasefire talks between Iran and Israel. This triggered aggressive short-covering across global markets.

Brent crude oil prices cooled from recent highs, trading around $70 per barrel. Cooling oil prices eased concerns about India's import bill and inflation outlook.

Buying was broad-based with banking, auto, and IT stocks leading the charge. The advance-decline ratio improved significantly in the final hour of trade.

Market breadth turned positive with 1,850 stocks advancing against 1,620 declines. Midcap and smallcap indices outperformed, gaining 0.6% and 0.8% respectively.

Analysts cautioned that volatility may persist until geopolitical clarity emerges. Traders are advised to maintain hedged positions amid ongoing global uncertainty.
