Coal India Absorbs Price Shock Despite 44% Rise In Ammonium Nitrate Costs

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Coal India is absorbing rising operational costs instead of passing them to consumers, shielding India's coal users from price hikes
  • 02/Ammonium Nitrate costs jumped 44% from ₹50,500/ton to ₹72,750/ton as of April 1, 2026; diesel costs also elevated
  • 03/Stock traded flat at ₹453.80 on NSE as company prioritizes economic stability over margin protection

Coal India Ltd (NSE: COALINDIA) is absorbing the price shock from sharply rising operational costs. The state-run miner is insulating India's coal users from escalating cost burdens instead of passing them through.

The cost of Ammonium Nitrate (AN) has surged 44% from pre-war levels. It jumped from ₹50,500 per metric ton to ₹72,750 per metric ton as of April 1, 2026.

AN constitutes approximately 60% of the material composition used in manufacturing explosives. These explosives are critical for Coal India's opencast mining operations.

The company is also compensating contractors for increased industrial diesel prices. Contractors operating in CIL's mines purchase diesel in bulk quantities.

Any pass-through of mounting input costs would create a cascading effect across the economy. Power tariffs and industrial input costs would rise significantly.

Coal India shares traded flat on the NSE at ₹453.80. The market appears to have priced in the margin compression from cost absorption.

The move aligns with Coal India's role as a public sector entity prioritizing energy security. It also demonstrates commitment to economic stability over short-term profitability.

The company accounts for over 80% of India's domestic coal production. Its pricing decisions have significant implications for power generation and industrial activity nationwide.
