India Relaxes Procurement Rules for State Firms to Source Critical Equipment from China

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India relaxes procurement rules for BHEL and SAIL to source critical equipment from China without government committee registration
  • 02/BHEL authorised for 21 types of equipment; SAIL approved for certain critical components amid calibrated China reset
  • 03/Coal-gasification equipment also exempted; follows PM Modi's first China visit in 7 years after 2020 border clash tensions

India has relaxed procurement restrictions for state-run firms Bharat Heavy Electricals Ltd (BHEL) and Steel Authority of India Ltd (SAIL). Both companies can now source critical equipment from China without mandatory government committee registration.

BHEL has been authorised to procure 21 types of critical equipment from Chinese suppliers. SAIL received approval for certain critical components under the relaxed guidelines.

Coal-gasification equipment is also exempted from registration requirements for other state-run firms. The exemption eliminates the need for political and security clearance approvals from government panels.

The policy shift follows Prime Minister Narendra Modi's visit to China in August 2025. It marked the first visit by an Indian PM to China in seven years.

The easing comes after years of strained relations following the 2020 border clash. India had previously tightened restrictions on Chinese imports amid security concerns.

The relaxation represents a calibrated reset in bilateral ties. It comes amid global trade realignments and shifting geopolitical dynamics.

State firms previously faced delays due to mandatory committee approvals. The exemption will streamline procurement for critical infrastructure projects.
