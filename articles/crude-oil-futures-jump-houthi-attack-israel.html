Crude Oil Futures Jump 2.4% After Houthi Attack on Israel

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Brent crude futures surge 2.42% to $107.87 per barrel after Houthi attack on Israel
  • 02/WTI crude gains 1.64% to $101.27 as geopolitical tensions raise supply disruption fears
  • 03/Oil markets remain on edge amid escalating Middle East conflict

Crude oil futures jumped sharply on Monday following a Houthi attack on Israel. June Brent oil futures rose 2.42% to $107.87 per barrel.

May WTI crude oil futures gained 1.64% to $101.27. The spike reflects renewed geopolitical risk in the Middle East.

Houthi rebels launched missiles targeting Israeli territory over the weekend. The attack has heightened fears of supply disruptions from the region.

Middle East tensions have consistently impacted oil prices this year. Any conflict near key shipping routes raises supply concerns.

Brent crude has now crossed the $107 mark after weeks of volatility. Traders are pricing in elevated geopolitical risk premiums.

WTI's move above $100 signals strong buying interest. Both benchmarks are trading at multi-week highs.

Analysts warn that sustained conflict could push prices higher. Supply chains remain vulnerable to disruptions in the region.

WHAT ARE BRENT AND WTI CRUDE OIL?
BRENT IS THE GLOBAL BENCHMARK FOR OIL PRICING, WHILE WTI (WEST TEXAS INTERMEDIATE) IS THE PRIMARY U.S. CRUDE OIL BENCHMARK.
WHY DO MIDDLE EAST TENSIONS AFFECT OIL PRICES?
THE MIDDLE EAST ACCOUNTS FOR A SIGNIFICANT PORTION OF GLOBAL OIL SUPPLY. ANY CONFLICT NEAR KEY PRODUCTION OR SHIPPING ROUTES CREATES SUPPLY RISK.
WHAT SHOULD INVESTORS WATCH NEXT?
INVESTORS SHOULD MONITOR DEVELOPMENTS IN THE ISRAEL-HOUTHI CONFLICT AND ANY POTENTIAL SUPPLY DISRUPTIONS FROM THE REGION.
