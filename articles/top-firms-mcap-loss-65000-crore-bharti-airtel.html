Top 6 Firms Lose ₹65,000 Cr Mcap; Bharti Airtel Leads Decline As Markets Slip

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Six of India's top-10 most valued firms shed nearly ₹65,000 crore in combined market capitalization last week
  • 02/Bharti Airtel emerged as the biggest laggard among the top-10 companies during the weekly trading session
  • 03/Sensex declined 263.67 points (0.35%) and Nifty fell 106.5 points (0.46%) as markets faced selling pressure

Six of India's top-10 most valued firms lost nearly ₹65,000 crore in combined market capitalization last week. The decline reflects broad-based selling pressure across key heavyweight stocks.

Bharti Airtel emerged as the biggest laggard among the blue-chip pack. The telecom major saw significant erosion in its market value during the weekly trading sessions.

The benchmark BSE Sensex declined 263.67 points, or 0.35%, during the week. The NSE Nifty50 index fell 106.5 points, or 0.46%, on a weekly basis.

Market sentiment remained cautious as investors booked profits in frontline stocks. The weakness was primarily driven by concerns over valuations and global cues.

Heavyweight counters across sectors witnessed selective selling pressure. Banking, IT, and telecom stocks contributed significantly to the index decline.

The top-10 most valued companies include Reliance Industries, TCS, HDFC Bank, Bharti Airtel, ICICI Bank, Infosys, LIC, State Bank of India, Hindustan Unilever, and ITC.

Market participants turned risk-averse ahead of key domestic and global events. The FII selling pattern continued to weigh on overall market sentiment during the week.

Investors should monitor global developments and corporate earnings announcements closely. Portfolio rebalancing in heavyweight counters may continue in the near term.
