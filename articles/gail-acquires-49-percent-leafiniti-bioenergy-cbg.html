GAIL Acquires 49% Stake in Leafiniti Bioenergy for ₹130 Million CBG Push

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/GAIL (India) Ltd invests ₹130 million to acquire 49% equity stake in Leafiniti Bioenergy, a subsidiary of TruAlt Bioenergy
  • 02/Joint venture will establish six greenfield CBG plants with combined annual output of 23,976 tonnes, creating 820-1,225 jobs
  • 03/Deal aligns with India's SATAT initiative and GAIL's green energy push under its 'Pankh' startup support programme

GAIL (India) Limited has invested ₹130 million to acquire a 49% equity stake in Leafiniti Bioenergy Private Limited. The investment was completed in March 2026 through an equity share subscription.

The deal follows a Share Subscription-cum-Shareholders' Agreement signed on August 11, 2025. TruAlt Bioenergy will retain a 51% majority stake in Leafiniti post-transaction.

Leafiniti Bioenergy is a wholly-owned subsidiary of TruAlt Bioenergy. It currently operates a 10.2 tonnes per day press-mud-based CBG plant in Karnataka since 2021.

GAIL is a Maharatna public sector undertaking under India's Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas. It is the country's largest natural gas company with over 16,420 km of natural gas pipelines.

The joint venture will roll out six greenfield CBG plants in the first phase. These will be located across Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Odisha.

Each plant will have a daily production capacity of 12 tonnes of compressed biogas. The combined annual output is targeted at 23,976 tonnes of CBG.

The plants will primarily use sugar mill residue as feedstock. This includes press mud and other associated waste products from sugar manufacturing.

The facilities will also produce significant volumes of organic manure annually. Output includes 97,902 tonnes of fermented organic manure and 4,70,862 tonnes of liquid fermented organic manure.

The project is expected to create between 820 and 1,225 direct and indirect employment opportunities. This will help catalyze rural economic activity in the project regions.

Environmentally, the first phase will displace approximately 19,800 tonnes of fossil fuels annually. It will also reduce around 9,300 tonnes of methane emissions per year.

The investment is part of GAIL's 'Pankh' programme launched in July 2017. The programme has a corpus of ₹5 billion and has invested in 38 startups across multiple sectors including bioenergy.

The collaboration supports India's SATAT scheme launched in October 2018. SATAT (Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation) aims to establish 5,000 CBG plants with 15 million metric tonnes annual production capacity.

CBG is chemically similar to natural gas and can replace CNG in automotive and industrial applications. India's CBG production potential is estimated at approximately 62 million metric tonnes.

The government has mandated CBG blending obligations starting FY 2025-26 at 1%, rising to 5% by FY 2028-29. This creates guaranteed demand for CBG producers like the GAIL-Leafiniti joint venture.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE INVESTMENT AMOUNT AND STAKE ACQUIRED BY GAIL IN LEAFINITI BIOENERGY?
GAIL (INDIA) LIMITED HAS INVESTED ₹130 MILLION (APPROXIMATELY $1.4 MILLION) TO ACQUIRE A 49% EQUITY STAKE IN LEAFINITI BIOENERGY PRIVATE LIMITED. TRUALT BIOENERGY RETAINS THE REMAINING 51% MAJORITY STAKE.
WHAT IS THE STRATEGIC PURPOSE OF THIS JOINT VENTURE?
THE JOINT VENTURE AIMS TO ESTABLISH MULTIPLE GREENFIELD COMPRESSED BIOGAS (CBG) PLANTS ACROSS INDIA. IT COMBINES TRUALT'S BIOENERGY PRODUCTION EXPERTISE WITH GAIL'S EXTENSIVE GAS DISTRIBUTION NETWORK AND MARKET PRESENCE TO SCALE UP CBG OPERATIONS.
HOW MANY CBG PLANTS ARE PLANNED AND WHERE WILL THEY BE LOCATED?
THE FIRST PHASE INCLUDES SIX GREENFIELD CBG PLANTS ACROSS KARNATAKA, MAHARASHTRA, AND ODISHA. EACH FACILITY WILL HAVE A PRODUCTION CAPACITY OF 12 TONNES PER DAY, DELIVERING A COMBINED ANNUAL OUTPUT OF 23,976 TONNES OF CBG.
WHAT IS THE SATAT INITIATIVE AND HOW DOES THIS DEAL RELATE TO IT?
SATAT (SUSTAINABLE ALTERNATIVE TOWARDS AFFORDABLE TRANSPORTATION) IS A GOVERNMENT OF INDIA SCHEME LAUNCHED IN OCTOBER 2018 TO PROMOTE CBG PRODUCTION FROM BIOMASS WASTE. THIS JOINT VENTURE ALIGNS WITH SATAT'S GOAL OF ESTABLISHING 5,000 CBG PLANTS AND REDUCING INDIA'S DEPENDENCE ON IMPORTED FOSSIL FUELS.
WHAT ARE THE ENVIRONMENTAL AND ECONOMIC BENEFITS OF THIS PROJECT?
THE FIRST PHASE IS PROJECTED TO DISPLACE APPROXIMATELY 19,800 TONNES OF FOSSIL FUELS ANNUALLY AND REDUCE 9,300 TONNES OF METHANE EMISSIONS. IT WILL ALSO GENERATE 820 TO 1,225 DIRECT AND INDIRECT JOBS AND PRODUCE OVER 5.6 LAKH TONNES OF ORGANIC MANURE ANNUALLY FOR AGRICULTURAL USE.
