India Blocks China-Backed Investment Pact at WTO MC14 in Solo Push-Back

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India stands alone at WTO MC14 in Yaounde, Cameroon blocking the China-backed Investment Facilitation for Development pact from incorporation into the WTO framework
  • 02/Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal cited Gandhian philosophy of "Truth prevailing over conformity" to justify India's solo opposition to the plurilateral agreement
  • 03/Turkey and South Africa previously opposed the IFD but withdrew objections, leaving India as the sole major country defending WTO's consensus-based architecture

India has blocked the China-backed Investment Facilitation for Development (IFD) Agreement at the 14th WTO Ministerial Conference (MC14) in Yaounde, Cameroon. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal announced the opposition on March 28, 2026.

The IFD pact is supported by 128 WTO members including China and countries dependent on Chinese investments. India is now the only influential country continuing to oppose the agreement.

Goyal stated that incorporating IFD risks eroding the functional limits of the WTO. It would undermine the organization's foundational principles and consensus-based decision-making architecture.

India opposes the pact's inclusion as an Annex 4 Agreement, which contains plurilateral trade agreements binding only on accepting members. The country argues investment was explicitly dropped from the WTO agenda in 2004.

The IFD was first mooted in 2017 by China as a Joint Statement Initiative. India maintains it is a non-mandated issue pushed without full membership consensus.

Turkey and South Africa previously opposed the IFD but withdrew objections at MC14. This leaves India isolated in defending the multilateral trading system's integrity.

India argues that allowing plurilateral agreements to become the norm would divert attention from mandated issues like food security and public stockholding. These long-pending concerns affect developing countries and least developed nations.

Goyal emphasized India's openness to good faith discussions under the WTO Reform Agenda. However, the country remains firm on protecting policy space for regulating foreign direct investment.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
Q: WHAT IS THE INVESTMENT FACILITATION FOR DEVELOPMENT (IFD) AGREEMENT?
THE IFD IS A CHINA-LED PLURILATERAL AGREEMENT SUPPORTED BY 128 WTO MEMBERS. IT AIMS TO FACILITATE FOREIGN DIRECT INVESTMENT FLOWS BY ENHANCING TRANSPARENCY OF INVESTMENT FRAMEWORKS AND IMPROVING PREDICTABILITY OF INVESTMENT PROCEDURES.
