Gold Price Falls 0.6% To $4,718 As Dollar Strengthens, Fed Rate-Cut Hopes Fade

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Spot gold fell 0.6% to $4,718.98 per ounce, hitting its lowest level since April 7 earlier in the session
  • 02/Stronger dollar and fading Fed rate-cut expectations are weighing on gold prices as oil surges above $100 per barrel
  • 03/Traders now see little chance of U.S. rate cuts this year; gold has fallen over 11% since the war began on February 28

Spot gold prices dropped 0.6% to $4,718.98 per ounce as of 0222 GMT on April 13, 2026. The metal hit its lowest level since April 7 earlier in the trading session.

U.S. gold futures for June delivery declined 1% to $4,742. The losses reflect mounting pressure from a stronger dollar and diminishing expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts this year.

The dollar strengthened 0.4% against major currencies. A stronger greenback makes dollar-priced bullion more expensive for holders of other currencies, reducing demand.

Oil prices jumped above $100 per barrel following failed U.S.-Iran peace talks. The U.S. Navy is preparing a blockade of the Strait of Hormuz that could restrict Iranian oil shipments.

Higher energy prices threaten to feed into broader inflation. This limits the scope for monetary easing and has caused traders to price out expectations for Fed rate cuts this year.

Before the Middle East war began on February 28, markets were pricing in two rate cuts for 2026. Spot gold has now fallen more than 11% since the conflict started.

"Ceasefire optimism has unwound following the failure of the peace talks, and the resulting push higher by the dollar and oil prices has put gold on the back foot again," said Tim Waterer, chief market analyst at KCM Trade.

While inflation typically boosts gold's appeal as a hedge, elevated interest rates weigh on the non-yielding metal. Higher rates increase the opportunity cost of holding gold.

Among other precious metals, spot silver fell 2.2% to $74.23 per ounce. Platinum lost 0.5% to $2,034.95, while palladium bucked the trend with a 1% gain to $1,535.77.
