Nova Dairy Halts Bulk Sales; Sterling Agro Reports Zero Dispatches in 2026

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nova Dairy suspends bulk milk sales to institutional buyers effective April 1, 2026 citing severe supply constraints
  • 02/Sterling Agro Industries records zero product dispatches in Q1 2026, first time in company's 23-year history
  • 03/Retail milk prices surge 18% YoY; analysts expect further consolidation among mid-tier dairy processors

Nova Dairy has suspended bulk milk sales to institutional buyers starting April 1, 2026. The company cited severe supply constraints and inability to meet contracted volumes.

Sterling Agro Industries reported zero product dispatches during the first quarter of 2026. This marks the first time in the company's 23-year operational history.

The suspensions reflect a deepening crisis across India's dairy supply chain. Multiple states have reported 15-20% lower milk collection volumes compared to last year.

Feed costs have risen 35% over the past 18 months. Fodder shortages following erratic monsoons have squeezed farmer margins and reduced herd sizes.

Nova Dairy supplies approximately 2.5 lakh litres daily to institutional clients. These include hotel chains, confectionery manufacturers, and packaged food companies.

The company has prioritised retail pouches and direct consumer sales. Bulk contracts typically operate at thinner margins, making them unsustainable during shortages.

Sterling Agro's manufacturing facility in Gujarat remains technically operational. However, input cost economics have rendered production unviable at current price points.

The company processes approximately 1.8 lakh litres daily at full capacity. Management has not provided a timeline for resuming normal operations.

Retail milk prices have increased 18% year-on-year across major metros. Full-cream milk now sells at ₹68-72 per litre in Delhi and Mumbai markets.

Industry analysts expect further consolidation among mid-tier dairy processors. Larger players with integrated supply chains are better positioned to weather the crisis.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY HAS NOVA DAIRY SUSPENDED BULK SALES?
NOVA DAIRY HAS SUSPENDED BULK MILK SALES TO INSTITUTIONAL BUYERS DUE TO SEVERE SUPPLY CONSTRAINTS. THE COMPANY CANNOT MEET ITS CONTRACTED VOLUMES AND HAS PRIORITISED RETAIL SALES WHICH OFFER BETTER MARGINS.
WHAT IS THE SIGNIFICANCE OF STERLING AGRO'S ZERO DISPATCHES?
STERLING AGRO INDUSTRIES RECORDED ZERO PRODUCT DISPATCHES IN Q1 2026, THE FIRST TIME IN ITS 23-YEAR HISTORY. THIS INDICATES EXTREME STRESS IN THE DAIRY PROCESSING SECTOR WHERE INPUT COSTS HAVE EXCEEDED VIABLE PRICE POINTS.
WHAT IS CAUSING THE MILK SHORTAGE IN INDIA?
THE SHORTAGE STEMS FROM FEED COSTS RISING 35% OVER 18 MONTHS AND FODDER SHORTAGES FOLLOWING ERRATIC MONSOONS. FARMER MARGINS HAVE SQUEEZED, LEADING TO REDUCED HERD SIZES AND LOWER MILK COLLECTION VOLUMES OF 15-20% IN MULTIPLE STATES.
HOW HAVE RETAIL MILK PRICES BEEN AFFECTED?
RETAIL MILK PRICES HAVE SURGED 18% YEAR-ON-YEAR ACROSS MAJOR METROS. FULL-CREAM MILK NOW SELLS AT ₹68-72 PER LITRE IN DELHI AND MUMBAI MARKETS COMPARED TO ₹58-62 PER LITRE IN EARLY 2025.
WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK FOR THE DAIRY INDUSTRY?
ANALYSTS EXPECT ACCELERATED CONSOLIDATION AMONG MID-TIER DAIRY PROCESSORS. LARGER PLAYERS WITH INTEGRATED SUPPLY CHAINS AND COLD STORAGE INFRASTRUCTURE ARE BETTER POSITIONED TO WEATHER THE CRISIS, WHILE SMALLER OPERATORS MAY FACE ACQUISITION OR CLOSURE.
