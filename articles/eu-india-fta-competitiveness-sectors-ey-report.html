EU-India FTA May Boost India's Competitiveness In Key Sectors: EY Report

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/The EU-India FTA will provide preferential access for over 99% of Indian exports to the EU market, according to EY's European Economic Outlook report
  • 02/Indian textiles get 0% duty from Day 1, neutralizing earlier GSP withdrawal impact and restoring competitiveness
  • 03/Auto duties on CBUs cut from 110% to 10% with 250,000 vehicle quota; EU minerals and steel sectors to benefit from Indian inputs

The EU-India Free Trade Agreement will boost India's competitiveness in key sectors. The EY European Economic Outlook report highlights significant gains for textiles, automobiles, and aerospace.

Over 99% of Indian exports will receive preferential access to the EU market. This includes zero-duty entry for textiles from Day 1 of implementation.

The textile sector stands to gain immediately. Duty-free access neutralizes the earlier Generalized System of Preferences withdrawal impact completely.

Indian textile exports currently face 8-12% duties in EU markets. Competitors like Bangladesh and Vietnam already enjoy preferential access.

The FTA reduces import duties on completely built auto units from 110% to approximately 10%. An annual quota of 250,000 vehicles applies.

Completely Knocked Down imports face duties around 15%. This encourages European, Japanese, and Korean manufacturers to invest in local assembly.

MSMEs and artisans across the textile ecosystem will benefit. Higher export orders and greater market certainty are expected.

EU sectors also stand to gain. Minerals, iron, and steel manufacturers get better access to Indian production inputs.

However, EU textiles and clothing may face pressure. Stronger Indian competition could impact domestic EU producers.

Indian exporters must comply with EU sustainability regulations. EUDR and CBAM remain outside current FTA scope.

The macro impact on EU GDP is roughly neutral. Sectoral effects are more meaningful for both economies.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT SECTORS WILL BENEFIT MOST FROM THE EU-INDIA FTA ACCORDING TO THE EY REPORT?
TEXTILES AND APPAREL WILL GET 0% DUTY FROM DAY 1, RESTORING INDIA'S COMPETITIVENESS. THE AUTO SECTOR SEES DUTIES ON CBUS CUT FROM 110% TO 10% WITH A 250,000 VEHICLE QUOTA. AEROSPACE, DEFENSE, AND IT SECTORS ALSO BENEFIT FROM ENHANCED MARKET ACCESS.
WHAT PERCENTAGE OF INDIAN EXPORTS WILL GET PREFERENTIAL ACCESS TO THE EU MARKET?
OVER 99% OF INDIAN EXPORTS WILL RECEIVE PREFERENTIAL ACCESS TO THE EU MARKET UNDER THE NEW FTA, FOSTERING GREATER TRADE OPPORTUNITIES BETWEEN THE TWO ECONOMIES.
HOW WILL THE FTA IMPACT THE EU ECONOMY?
THE AGGREGATE MACRO IMPACT ON EU GDP IS ROUGHLY NEUTRAL. HOWEVER, SECTORAL EFFECTS ARE MEANINGFUL - EU MINERALS AND IRON AND STEEL SECTORS BENEFIT FROM BETTER ACCESS TO INDIAN INPUTS, WHILE EU TEXTILES AND CLOTHING MAY FACE COMPETITIVE PRESSURE FROM INDIAN PRODUCERS.
WHAT ARE THE KEY BENEFITS FOR INDIAN TEXTILE EXPORTERS?
INDIAN TEXTILES GET 0% DUTY ACCESS FROM DAY 1, FULLY NEUTRALIZING THE IMPACT OF EARLIER GSP WITHDRAWAL. THIS ENABLES INDIAN PRODUCTS TO ENTER THE EU MARKET AT SIGNIFICANTLY MORE COMPETITIVE PRICES COMPARED TO THE PREVIOUS 8-12% DUTY BURDEN.
WHAT COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS MUST INDIAN EXPORTERS MEET?
INDIAN EXPORTERS MUST STAY ALERT TO EVOLVING EU SUSTAINABILITY REGULATIONS SUCH AS EU DEFORESTATION REGULATION (EUDR) AND CARBON BORDER ADJUSTMENT MECHANISM (CBAM), WHICH REMAIN OUTSIDE THE CURRENT FTA SCOPE. REGULATORY ALIGNMENT WITH REACH STANDARDS IS ALSO REQUIRED FOR CHEMICAL EXPORTS.
