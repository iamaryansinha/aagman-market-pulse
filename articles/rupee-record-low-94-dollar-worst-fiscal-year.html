Rupee Hits Record Low Past ₹94 Per Dollar, Set For Worst Fiscal Drop In 20 Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian rupee plunges to record low of 94.84 per dollar, closing at 94.8125 on March 27, 2026
  • 02/Currency has depreciated nearly 9% this fiscal year, worst performance since 2013 taper tantrum
  • 03/RBI intervened heavily with estimated $68-73 billion in dollar sales; forex reserves at $709.8 billion

The Indian rupee crashed to an all-time low of 94.84 per US dollar on March 27, 2026. It closed at 94.8125, marking a 0.9% single-day decline.

This depreciation represents nearly 9% for the fiscal year ending March 2026. It is the worst annual drop in over two decades, surpassing even the 2013 taper tantrum losses.

Foreign portfolio investors pulled over $10 billion from Indian equities in March alone. This exodus follows cumulative outflows of $18 billion in calendar year 2025.

The immediate trigger is escalating conflict in the Middle East. Brent crude prices have surged past $110 per barrel, threatening the Strait of Hormuz.

India imports over 80% of its crude oil requirements. A $10 per barrel rise in oil prices can widen India's current account deficit by approximately 0.4% of GDP.

The Reserve Bank of India has intervened aggressively to curb volatility. The central bank sold an estimated $53 billion between April and December 2025.

March 2026 saw additional RBI dollar sales of $15-20 billion. Total intervention for FY2026 is estimated at $68-73 billion.

These interventions have drained rupee liquidity from the banking system. A liquidity shortage of ₹65,900 crore has pushed the 10-year government bond yield to 6.78%.

India's foreign exchange reserves have declined sharply to $709.8 billion as of mid-March 2026. This is down significantly from peak levels above $700 billion maintained earlier.

The benchmark Sensex fell 1,282 points to 73,990.62 following the currency weakness. Importers hedging dollar exposure before fiscal year-end added further pressure.
