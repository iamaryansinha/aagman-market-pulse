Gold vs Equities 2026: Does Yellow Metal Hold Edge Despite Softening Shine?

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold has returned 16.9% CAGR over 5 years in USD terms, driven by record central bank buying and geopolitical fears
  • 02/BSE 500 TRI including dividends delivered 14.77% over 5 years, but leads gold over 10-20 year periods with 16.14% and 14.05% CAGR
  • 03/Central banks bought 1,006 tonnes annually from 2022-2025, more than double the 458 tonnes/year average from 2010-2021

Gold has delivered striking returns in recent years. In dollar terms, it has returned approximately 16.9% CAGR over the last five years. This surge follows central bank buying spurred by geopolitical tensions.

Central bank gold purchases have more than doubled since 2022. From 2010 to 2021, banks bought an average of 458 tonnes annually. From 2022 to 2025, that figure jumped to roughly 1,006 tonnes per year.

The 2022 freezing of Russia's reserves triggered this shift. Reserve managers sought assets with no counterparty or sanctions risk. Gold held domestically fits that need perfectly.

However, equities still dominate long-term wealth creation. The BSE 500 TRI, which includes dividends, has delivered about 14.77% CAGR over five years. Over ten years, it returns 16.14% annually.

The gap widens further over longer horizons. Over 20 years, the BSE 500 TRI compounded at roughly 14.05% annually. Gold's 20-year annualised return moderates to around 10.5%.

Gold serves best as a hedge and crisis stabiliser. It preserves purchasing power during inflation and currency weakness. But it produces no cash flow, dividends, or earnings growth.

"Gold has won the recent fear cycle. Equities still hold the long-term edge," writes Ankit Patel, Co-founder at Arunasset Investment Services. His analysis emphasizes productive businesses versus non-yielding metal.

The verdict is clear for portfolio construction. Gold belongs as an insurance allocation. Equities remain the engine for long-term compounding and wealth creation.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT HAS BEEN GOLD'S RETURN OVER THE LAST 5 YEARS?
IN DOLLAR TERMS, GOLD HAS DELIVERED APPROXIMATELY 16.9% CAGR OVER THE LAST FIVE YEARS, DRIVEN BY CENTRAL BANK BUYING AND GEOPOLITICAL UNCERTAINTY.
HOW DO EQUITY RETURNS COMPARE TO GOLD OVER THE LONG TERM?
THE BSE 500 TRI HAS DELIVERED ABOUT 14.77% CAGR OVER 5 YEARS, 16.14% OVER 10 YEARS, AND 14.05% OVER 20 YEARS. GOLD'S 20-YEAR RETURN MODERATES TO AROUND 10.5%.
WHY ARE CENTRAL BANKS BUYING SO MUCH GOLD?
AFTER THE 2022 FREEZING OF RUSSIA'S RESERVES, CENTRAL BANKS SOUGHT TO DIVERSIFY AWAY FROM DOLLAR CONCENTRATION. GOLD HELD DOMESTICALLY CARRIES NO COUNTERPARTY OR SANCTIONS RISK.
HOW MUCH GOLD DID CENTRAL BANKS BUY RECENTLY?
FROM 2022 TO 2025, CENTRAL BANKS BOUGHT AN AVERAGE OF 1,006 TONNES ANNUALLY, MORE THAN DOUBLE THE 458 TONNES/YEAR AVERAGE FROM 2010 TO 2021. SPECIFIC YEARS: 1,080 TONNES (2022), 1,037 TONNES (2023), 1,045 TONNES (2024), AND 863 TONNES (2025).
SHOULD INVESTORS CHOOSE GOLD OR EQUITIES FOR WEALTH CREATION?
GOLD WORKS BEST AS A PORTFOLIO HEDGE AND CRISIS STABILISER. FOR LONG-TERM WEALTH CREATION, EQUITIES REMAIN SUPERIOR DUE TO CASH FLOW GENERATION, DIVIDENDS, AND PARTICIPATION IN ECONOMIC GROWTH.
