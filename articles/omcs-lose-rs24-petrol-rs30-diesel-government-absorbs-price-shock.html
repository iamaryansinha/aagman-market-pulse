OMCs Lose ₹24/Litre On Petrol, ₹30/Litre On Diesel As Govt Absorbs Price Shock

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Oil marketing companies are incurring losses of ₹24 per litre on petrol and ₹30 per litre on diesel as retail prices remain frozen despite surging global crude
  • 02/International crude prices jumped from $70/barrel to $122/barrel in one month due to ongoing West Asia conflict and Strait of Hormuz disruptions
  • 03/Government cut excise duty on petrol to ₹3 from ₹13 and scrapped diesel levy entirely, absorbing an estimated ₹1.55 lakh crore annualized fiscal hit

Oil marketing companies (OMCs) are bleeding ₹24 per litre on petrol and ₹30 per litre on diesel. The losses mount as retail fuel prices remain unchanged despite global crude oil prices skyrocketing past $122 per barrel.

Petroleum Minister Hardeep Singh Puri confirmed the under-recoveries on March 27, 2026. He said the government has taken a "huge hit" on taxation revenues to reduce losses at state-owned oil firms.

International crude prices have surged from around $70 per barrel to $122 per barrel in just one month. The spike follows escalating tensions in West Asia and disruptions to oil shipments through the Strait of Hormuz.

Global fuel prices have risen sharply across regions. Southeast Asian countries saw 30-50% increases. North America recorded 30% hikes. Europe saw 20% rises. African nations faced 50% price jumps.

India has chosen a different path. Rather than passing costs to consumers, the Modi government absorbed the burden through excise duty cuts.

The government reduced excise duty on petrol to ₹3 per litre from ₹13 earlier. It completely eliminated the ₹10 per litre excise duty on diesel. These moves aim to cushion OMCs without raising pump prices.

Madhavi Arora, Chief Economist at Emkay Global Financial Services, estimates the annualized fiscal hit at ₹1.55 lakh crore. She said the measure will absorb 30-40% of annualized OMC losses on auto fuels at current prices.

The three major state-owned OMCs are Indian Oil Corporation (NSE: IOC), Bharat Petroleum Corporation (NSE: BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation (NSE: HPCL). Together they dominate approximately 90% of India's domestic fuel market.

To prevent refiners from profiting through exports while domestic supply remains constrained, the government imposed additional export duties. Diesel exports now face ₹21.5 per litre duty. Aviation turbine fuel (ATF) exports attract ₹29.5 per litre.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman said the export duties ensure sufficient domestic availability. India is a net exporter of ATF, and global prices have surged over 105% since February 2026.

The market has punished OMC stocks for the margin squeeze. Shares of IOC, BPCL, and HPCL have fallen 23-25% in March 2026 alone. The losses mirror the under-recovery period following the Russia-Ukraine conflict in 2022-23.

The Asian Development Bank warned that prolonged West Asia conflict could reduce Asia's growth by 1.3 percentage points. Inflation could spike 3.2 percentage points over 2026-2027 if disruptions continue.
