Indian Rupee Hits Record Low of 95.22 Against US Dollar Before Recovering

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian rupee touched an all-time low of 95.22 against the US dollar on March 30, 2026
  • 02/The currency recovered to settle at 94.78 after RBI intervened to cap banks' net open positions
  • 03/Market watchers expect continued volatility amid global economic uncertainty and dollar strength

The Indian rupee hit a record low of 95.22 against the US dollar during intraday trade on March 30, 2026. The currency later pared losses to settle at 94.78.

The sharp decline came despite early gains following RBI's intervention. The central bank moved to cap banks' net open positions to reduce speculative trading.

The move failed to hold the rupee's initial momentum. Selling pressure resumed as the session progressed, pushing the currency past the 95 threshold for the first time.

The 95.22 level marks the weakest the rupee has ever traded against the dollar. The previous record low was breached during heightened global risk aversion.

Forex dealers noted heavy dollar demand from importers and foreign portfolio outflows. These flows offset any support from the RBI's position curbs.

The rupee has faced sustained pressure amid a resurgent greenback. Emerging market currencies broadly weakened against the dollar this quarter.
