Telangana Assembly Approves ₹15,000 Cr Hyderabad Metro Takeover from L&T

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Telangana Assembly passes resolution to take over 69 km Hyderabad Metro Phase I from L&T at ₹15,000 crore on March 29, 2026
  • 02/Takeover includes ₹13,000 crore in debt liabilities and ₹2,000 crore for equity stake buyout
  • 03/Move clears path for Phase II expansion spanning 76-86 km across Shamshabad, Medchal, and other corridors

The Telangana Legislative Assembly has approved a resolution to take over Hyderabad Metro Phase I from Larsen & Toubro (L&T). The takeover is valued at ₹15,000 crore.

The state cabinet had earlier cleared the acquisition comprising ₹13,000 crore in debt liabilities. The remaining ₹2,000 crore covers equity buyout from the private operator.

Phase I spans 69 km across Red (LB Nagar-Miyapur), Blue (Nagole-Raidurg), and Green (JBS-MGBS) corridors. The network has been operational since 2017-2019 under a Public-Private Partnership model.

The Hyderabad Metro Rail Limited (HMRL) will now assume control of operations and maintenance. This consolidation enables seamless integration with planned Phase II expansions.

The Assembly also urged the Central Government to expedite approval for Phase II. The expansion covers 76.4 km under Phase II-A and 86.1 km under Phase II-B.

Key Phase II corridors include Shamshabad Airport to Future City (39.6 km), JBS to Medchal (24.5 km), and JBS to Shamirpet (22 km). Total project cost is estimated at ₹19,579-24,269 crore.

The state plans to refinance high-cost debt through instruments like Indian Railway Finance Corporation. Current loans carry approximately 8% interest.

Larsen & Toubro (NSE: LT) has operated the metro since its inception. The exit marks one of India's largest infrastructure asset transfers from private to state control.
