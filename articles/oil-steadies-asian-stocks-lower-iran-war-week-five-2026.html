Oil Steadies Near $107 as Iran War Enters Fifth Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Brent crude at $107.37/barrel, US crude at $102.93 as Iran war enters fifth week
  • 02/Asian stocks mostly lower — Kospi plunged 4.3%, Nikkei dropped 1.6%, Hang Seng fell 0.3%
  • 03/Drone struck Kuwaiti oil tanker in Dubai waters; Strait of Hormuz disruptions persist

Oil markets held steady Tuesday as investors parsed mixed signals on whether the Iran war might de-escalate after nearly five weeks of conflict.

Brent crude futures slipped less than 0.1% to $107.37 per barrel. Benchmark US crude rose 0.1% to $102.93. Prices have surged more than 40% since hostilities began on February 28.

Asian equity markets reflected the uncertainty. Tokyo's Nikkei 225 fell 1.6% to 51,063.72, erasing year-to-date gains. South Korea's Kospi suffered the steepest drop, plunging 4.3% to 5,052.46. Hong Kong's Hang Seng declined 0.3% to 24,678.17, while China's Shanghai Composite slipped 0.8% to 3,891.86.

The conflict shows no signs of immediate resolution. President Donald Trump claimed negotiations were underway with Iran's parliamentary speaker, a statement Tehran swiftly denied.

A fresh incident heightened tensions Tuesday when a drone struck a Kuwaiti oil tanker in Dubai waters, causing a fire. The attack underscores the continued threat to regional energy infrastructure.

The Strait of Hormuz remains the critical chokepoint. Roughly one-fifth of global oil shipments normally pass through this narrow waterway. Iran has effectively established what US officials describe as a "toll booth" there.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio indicated that President Trump has "options available" to respond to Tehran's threats. Meanwhile, US Gulf allies are privately urging continued military pressure.

Safe-haven assets attracted bids. Gold rose 0.7% to $4,590.30 per ounce. Silver jumped 2.8% to $72.56 per ounce.
