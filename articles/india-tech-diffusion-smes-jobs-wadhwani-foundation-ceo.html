India Can Lead In Tech Diffusion, SMEs To Drive Jobs: Wadhwani Foundation CEO

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Wadhwani Foundation CEO Ajay Kela says India has immense opportunity to lead in diffusion of technology that reaches millions of end-users
  • 02/SMEs and deep-tech startups will be the biggest job creators in coming years, with drug discovery and molecular discovery accelerating growth
  • 03/Foundation aims to create 2.5 million jobs and enable 6 million placements via training by 2030

India has an immense opportunity to take the lead in diffusion of technology. Ajay Kela, CEO and board member of Wadhwani Foundation, made this statement in an interview with PTI on March 26, 2026.

Kela emphasized that diffusion means building national-scale use cases of technology. These use cases must reach end-users across the country.

"The benefits of AI will all come in the application layer which touches the end population," Kela said. He noted that many foundation models are already open source. India is building smaller models that other countries may not develop.

On jobs and concerns about employment loss due to AI, Kela pointed to SMEs and deep-tech startups. He said they will be major drivers of employment in the coming years.

Drug discovery and molecular discovery will accelerate deep-tech startups. These sectors will be game-changing for job creation.

Recently, Wadhwani Foundation announced a strategic roadmap. The foundation aims to create 2.5 million jobs in India. It also plans to enable 6 million placements via training by 2030.

"From micro-entrepreneurs to USD 20-30 million businesses where big players don't really support and help... we come in, to help them accelerate their growth," Kela explained.

The goal is to help SMEs grow 10-15 per cent incrementally. This growth will add another 5-10 per cent of jobs they would not have added otherwise.

Kela highlighted a major shift in recent years. The focus has moved from four-year degree programmes to upskilling.

"There are many such jobs where you don't need to go through four years of rigour and 5,000 hours of training," he said. Kela noted that 500-1,000 hours of training can prepare an 18-year-old or a 40-year-old displaced worker for mid-skill entry-level jobs.

Digital marketing, data visualisation, home healthcare worker, dental hygienist, and the entire BPO industry are examples. These roles do not require traditional four-year degrees.

The Wadhwani Foundation has partnered with IIT Bombay. It has set up a Wadhwani Hub for Biosciences, Bioengineering, Health and Medicine. The Wadhwani Innovation and Translation Centre (WITC) will promote research and innovation.

The centre will be inaugurated later this year. It comes up at a cost of Rs 300 crore. The foundation's contribution is Rs 100 crore.
