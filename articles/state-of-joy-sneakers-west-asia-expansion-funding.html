State of Joy Sneakers Targets West Asia Expansion, Eyes Funding Round Within a Year

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/State of Joy plans expansion into West Asia markets targeting Indian diaspora demand, with inbound enquiries already coming in
  • 02/The bootstrapped brand currently operates across 800+ multi-brand outlets and 3,000+ stores nationally, with offline sales exceeding 50%
  • 03/Founders targeting institutional fundraising within approximately one year after hitting internal benchmarks

Sneaker brand State of Joy is planning overseas expansion into West Asia and aims to raise institutional funding within a year. The bootstrapped startup is positioning itself to scale across India's fast-growing footwear market.

The brand was co-founded by shoe technologist Pullela Gopalakrishna Bachi and footwear veteran Dasari Raju Ramesh. Bachi is a certified shoe engineer from Germany's Deutsche Schuh Fach-schule in Pirmasens. Ramesh brings over 30 years of footwear industry experience, including 17 years in the European market.

State of Joy currently retails across more than 800 multi-brand outlets. It maintains presence in over 3,000 stores nationally. Offline channels account for over 50 per cent of total sales.

The company has set a target sales mix of around 30 per cent from exclusive brand outlets. Online channels are expected to contribute approximately 20 per cent of sales. Talks for standalone brand stores are currently underway.

Mumbai served as the brand's first major market launch, chosen for its status as India's fashion capital. Hyderabad, Chennai, and Bangalore are next in the expansion pipeline. Select northern markets are also under consideration.

"Once we build it over here, there is acceptance also in countries where there are a lot of Indians already," said Ramesh in a conversation with businessline. Inbound enquiries from West Asia have already begun to come in.

The brand positions itself between mass-market domestic labels and premium international names. Price points are described as premium for an Indian brand yet aspirational for younger consumers.

"We have achieved a good scale in a short period of time," Ramesh said regarding the bootstrapped growth. Founders plan to hit internal benchmarks before approaching external investors.

The brand also houses a sub-category called 'Anatom' targeting older consumers prioritising comfort. Future product categories beyond footwear are being explored as the brand evolves.

"I don't think we have even started scratching the surface," Ramesh said about India's footwear market potential.
