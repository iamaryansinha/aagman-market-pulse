VinFast Delays Break-Even Target To Post-2027 On Aggressive Growth Push

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/VinFast delays break-even target to after 2027, pushing back from earlier 2027 guidance due to aggressive growth investments
  • 02/Vietnam EV maker targets 300,000 car deliveries in 2026, a 50% jump from 2025 volumes
  • 03/E-scooter deliveries expected to rise 2.5-fold helped by Vietnam's planned mid-2026 petrol bike ban

Vietnam's electric vehicle maker VinFast has delayed its break-even target to beyond 2027. The company was previously targeting profitability by 2027.

The delay comes as VinFast pursues an aggressive global expansion strategy. Growth investments are weighing on near-term profitability.

VinFast aims to deliver 300,000 cars globally in 2026. This represents a 50% increase over its 2025 delivery volumes.

The company also expects e-scooter deliveries to surge 2.5 times this year. Vietnam's planned mid-2026 ban on petrol-powered motorbikes is driving demand.

The petrol bike ban will force consumers to switch to electric alternatives. VinFast is positioning itself as the dominant local player.

VinFast is racing to capture market share in the global EV market. However, profitability remains elusive as competition intensifies.

The delayed break-even timeline reflects the capital-intensive nature of EV manufacturing. Scale remains critical for margin improvement.

Investors will watch execution on the 300,000 delivery target. The e-scooter business offers a faster path to volume growth.
