PM Modi Inaugurates Noida International Airport Phase 1 at Jewar

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Phase 1 of Noida International Airport in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh on March 28, 2026
  • 02/Phase 1 features 12 million annual passenger capacity, one runway, and cargo terminal handling 2.5 lakh metric tonnes yearly
  • 03/Total project investment approximately ₹15,000 crore; commercial operations expected to begin by May 2026 after regulatory approvals

Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Phase 1 of Noida International Airport (NIA) at Jewar on March 28, 2026. The airport carries IATA code DXN and is Delhi-NCR's second international airport.

Phase 1 has been developed at an investment of approximately ₹11,282 crore. The total four-phase project is estimated at ₹29,560 crore, aligned with the initially projected ₹15,000-20,000 crore range.

The first phase covers 1,334 hectares with one 4-km runway and one terminal building. It can handle 12 million passengers annually and features a cargo terminal with initial capacity of 2.5 lakh metric tonnes.

The airport is developed by Yamuna International Airport Private Limited. This is a 100% subsidiary of Zurich Airport International AG under a 40-year concession partnership with Uttar Pradesh government.

The cargo complex will support electronics, pharmaceuticals, and perishable agricultural products. This positions Jewar as a major air freight gateway for North India.

PM Modi stated the airport will serve as "North India's logistic gateway." The project benefits farmers in western UP by enabling swift export of fruits, vegetables, and fish to international markets.

MSMEs in Aligarh, Meerut, Moradabad, Agra, and Saharanpur will gain easier access to foreign markets. Sectors include sports goods, brassware, furniture, footwear, and the famous Agra petha.

The airport is strategically located along the Yamuna Expressway. It connects Agra, Mathura, and Vrindavan within two hours and will integrate with road, rail, and metro networks.

Once fully expanded to five runways, capacity will reach 70 million passengers annually. Uttar Pradesh will become India's first state with five international airports.

Commercial operations are expected to begin by May 2026. Airlines including IndiGo, Akasa Air, and Air India Express have confirmed operations from the airport.
