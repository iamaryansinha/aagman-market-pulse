Shell Steps Up LNG Supplies To India, Wins Major Fertiliser Tenders After Gulf Disruptions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Shell secured 4 trillion British thermal units out of 6 TBtu tendered by Indian fertiliser companies in March 2026
  • 02/Shell became India's largest supplier of imported natural gas in March, importing its largest-ever monthly LNG volumes
  • 03/Elevated imports likely to continue in April; Shell expected to bid for 10-12 TBtu mid-April tender

Global energy major Shell plc has significantly increased natural gas supplies to India. The move comes after disruptions from the West Asia conflict impacted supplies from Qatar.

Shell's global LNG portfolio allowed it to step in quickly. It became India's largest imported gas supplier in March 2026.

Industry sources said Shell emerged as a leading supplier in last month's bulk LNG procurement. It secured 4 trillion British thermal units out of 6 TBtu volumes tendered.

The government moved fast to ensure feedstock availability for urea production. This was critical amid constrained supplies from the Gulf region.

Shell's India arm imported its largest-ever monthly LNG volumes in March. The company supplied gas to fertiliser plants, industrial users, and retailers.

Shell's ability to scale up is backed by its 5 million tonnes-per-year LNG import terminal at Hazira in Gujarat. It also has associated storage infrastructure.

As the world's largest LNG portfolio player, Shell sources cargoes from multiple regions. These include Oman, Australia, and Nigeria.

The company operates one of the largest LNG shipping fleets with over 65 chartered carriers. This helped deliver gas when India's main supplier declared force majeure.

India imports roughly half of its natural gas requirement. The fuel is used for fertiliser production, power generation, CNG transport, and piped cooking gas.

About 45-50 per cent of LNG imports typically come from Qatar under long-term contracts. These supplies stopped after Iran's retaliation to US and Israeli strikes.

Force majeure declared by QatarEnergy disrupted about 11.2 million tonnes of India's roughly 27 million tonnes of LNG imports.

State-run firms like GAIL (India) Limited sourced cargoes from alternative suppliers including the US and Russia. But shipping capacity remained a constraint.

Voyages from distant sources like the US can take up to 45 days. Shell was insulated from this due to its global portfolio and own shipping fleet.

Domestic gas production stands at about 92 million standard cubic metres per day. Shell's supplies complemented this to stabilise availability.

Industrial consumers initially faced supply cuts of up to 40 per cent. Supplies were prioritised for fertiliser plants and city gas distribution.

Urea plant supplies ramped up from 70 per cent to nearly 90 per cent from April 6. This increased to around 95 per cent from April 9.

Gas availability to other industrial and commercial sectors increased by 10 per cent from April 6. This includes City Gas Distribution networks.

Sources said Shell's elevated imports are likely to continue in April. It is expected to be a major bidder in the 10-12 TBtu tender planned by mid-April.
