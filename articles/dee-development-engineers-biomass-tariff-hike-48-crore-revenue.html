DEE Development Engineers Biomass Unit Gets 49% Tariff Hike, Targets ₹48 Crore Revenue

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/PSERC revises tariff for Malwa Power's 6 MW biomass plant to ₹5.224/kWh, up 49% from interim ₹3.50/kWh for 10-year term
  • 02/Subsidiary expects ₹47.71 crore annual revenue combining power generation and new biomass pellet plant operations
  • 03/Company evaluating appeal to APTEL on fixed cost component, claims PSERC used outdated 2012 CERC norms

DEE Development Engineers Ltd (NSE: DEE) has secured a major tariff revision for its biomass subsidiary. The Punjab State Electricity Regulatory Commission (PSERC) issued the order on March 27, 2026.

Malwa Power Pvt Ltd (MPPL), the wholly owned subsidiary, operates a 6 MW biomass power plant in Muktsar, Punjab. The revised tariff is ₹5.224 per kWh, effective for an extended 10-year term.

The new rate represents a 49 per cent increase over the interim ₹3.50 per kWh that Punjab State Power Corporation Limited (PSPCL) had been paying. The variable component will escalate at 5 per cent annually.

The order triggers retrospective recovery of approximately ₹5.80 crore. This covers 3.37 crore units supplied between May 2025 and February 2026 at the lower interim rate.

For FY 2026-27, MPPL projects power generation revenue of ₹24.31 crore. This assumes an 85 per cent plant load factor for the biomass unit.

The company is also commissioning a 72,000 MT per annum Biomass Pellet Plant. At 50 per cent capacity, this facility is projected to generate ₹23.40 crore annually.

Combined revenue from both operations is estimated at ₹47.71 crore per year. Shares of DEE Development Engineers rose 4.05 per cent to ₹279.75 on Wednesday following the disclosure.

However, management flagged reservations about the fixed cost component set at ₹0.97 per kWh. The company contends PSERC applied outdated CERC regulations from 2012 rather than the 2024 norms.

The 2024 CERC norms prescribe O&M charges of ₹54.70 lakh per MW. MPPL is evaluating an appeal before the Appellate Tribunal for Electricity (APTEL) in New Delhi.

The plant was originally commissioned in April 2005. It operated under a 20-year PPA that expired in April 2025.

WHAT IS THE NEW TARIFF APPROVED FOR MALWA POWER'S BIOMASS PLANT?
PSERC HAS REVISED THE TARIFF TO ₹5.224 PER KWH FOR A 10-YEAR TERM, REPRESENTING A 49 PER CENT INCREASE FROM THE INTERIM RATE OF ₹3.50 PER KWH.
HOW MUCH RETROSPECTIVE RECOVERY IS MALWA POWER ENTITLED TO?
THE COMPANY CAN RECOVER APPROXIMATELY ₹5.80 CRORE FOR 3.37 CRORE UNITS SUPPLIED BETWEEN MAY 2025 AND FEBRUARY 2026 AT THE LOWER INTERIM RATE.
WHAT IS THE TOTAL PROJECTED ANNUAL REVENUE FOR MPPL?
THE SUBSIDIARY ESTIMATES TOTAL REVENUE OF ₹47.71 CRORE ANNUALLY, COMBINING ₹24.31 CRORE FROM POWER GENERATION AND ₹23.40 CRORE FROM THE BIOMASS PELLET PLANT.
WHAT IS THE COMPANY'S OBJECTION TO THE PSERC ORDER?
MANAGEMENT CONTENDS THE FIXED COST COMPONENT OF ₹0.97 PER KWH IS BASED ON OUTDATED CERC 2012 REGULATIONS INSTEAD OF THE 2024 NORMS THAT PRESCRIBE O&M CHARGES OF ₹54.70 LAKH PER MW.
WHAT ACTION IS THE COMPANY CONSIDERING ON THE FIXED COST ISSUE?
MALWA POWER IS EVALUATING AN APPEAL BEFORE THE APPELLATE TRIBUNAL FOR ELECTRICITY (APTEL) IN NEW DELHI TO SEEK AN UPWARD REVISION OF THE FIXED COST COMPONENT.
