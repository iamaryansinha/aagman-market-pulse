Pharma Industry Must Cut Import Dependence: Panacea Biotec Executive Rajesh Jain

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //

India's pharmaceutical industry faces a critical supply chain vulnerability.

Despite being the world's pharmacy, India imports approximately 70% of its Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs) from China. This dependence rises to nearly 90% for life-saving antibiotics including cephalosporins, azithromycin, and penicillin.

Dr. Rajesh Jain, Joint Managing Director at Panacea Biotec, has issued an urgent call to reduce this import dependence. The industry leader emphasizes that strategic government incentives and policy support are essential to boost domestic API manufacturing.

The COVID-19 pandemic exposed these supply chain vulnerabilities. Border tensions with China in 2020 disrupted API supplies, forcing India to recognize the national security implications of pharmaceutical import dependence.

The government's Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme allocates approximately $920 million (₹6,940 crore) to revive domestic manufacturing. Incentives range from 5% to 20% on revenue for domestically produced APIs, vaccines, and key starting materials.

The scheme specifically targets 53 crucial APIs where import dependence poses the greatest risk to essential drug access. These include critical inputs for antibiotics, anti-retrovirals, and other life-saving medications.

However, structural barriers persist. Domestic manufacturing costs remain significantly higher than China: raw materials cost ~25% more, electricity ~20% more, and other operational expenses ~30% more.

Complex regulatory procedures add friction. License renewals require approvals from multiple bodies including environment, pollution control, drug control, and explosive departments. This bureaucratic complexity discourages domestic investment.

Infrastructure gaps compound the challenge. Limited availability of world-class QC laboratories, common testing facilities, and inadequate bio-waste management systems increase compliance costs for domestic manufacturers.

Panacea Biotec itself has initiated backward integration efforts at its Lalru facility. The company is developing in-house capabilities to substitute imported materials with domestically produced alternatives.

The Indian pharmaceutical sector, valued at approximately $50 billion, supplies over 50% of global vaccine demand and 40% of US generic demand. Yet this dominant position in formulations masks critical upstream vulnerabilities.

Chinese API imports to India increased 20% from FY21 to reach ₹23,273 crore in FY22. This growing trade deficit in pharmaceutical inputs contradicts India's strategic goal of self-reliance.

Without addressing structural weaknesses, India's position as the pharmacy of the world remains vulnerable to geopolitical tensions and global supply disruptions.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT PERCENTAGE OF APIs DOES INDIA IMPORT FROM CHINA?
INDIA IMPORTS APPROXIMATELY 65-70% OF ITS ACTIVE PHARMACEUTICAL INGREDIENTS (APIS) FROM CHINA. FOR CERTAIN LIFE-SAVING ANTIBIOTICS INCLUDING CEPHALOSPORINS, AZITHROMYCIN, AND PENICILLIN, THIS DEPENDENCE REACHES NEARLY 90%.
WHO IS RAJESH JAIN AND WHAT IS HIS POSITION?
DR. RAJESH JAIN IS THE JOINT MANAGING DIRECTOR AT PANACEA BIOTEC, A LEADING INDIAN PHARMACEUTICAL COMPANY. HE HAS BEEN VOCAL ABOUT THE NEED FOR POLICY REFORMS TO REDUCE IMPORT DEPENDENCE AND SUPPORT DOMESTIC MANUFACTURING.
WHAT IS THE PLI SCHEME FOR PHARMACEUTICALS?
THE PRODUCTION LINKED INCENTIVE (PLI) SCHEME ALLOCATES APPROXIMATELY $920 MILLION (₹6,940 CRORE) TO BOOST DOMESTIC API MANUFACTURING. IT PROVIDES INCENTIVES RANGING FROM 5% TO 20% ON REVENUE FOR DOMESTICALLY MANUFACTURED APIS, VACCINES, AND KEY STARTING MATERIALS.
WHY IS DOMESTIC API MANUFACTURING MORE EXPENSIVE IN INDIA?
DOMESTIC MANUFACTURING COSTS ARE HIGHER DUE TO: RAW MATERIALS COSTING ~25% MORE THAN CHINA, ELECTRICITY ~20% MORE EXPENSIVE, OTHER OPERATIONAL COSTS ~30% HIGHER, COMPLEX REGULATORY PROCEDURES, AND INADEQUATE INFRASTRUCTURE FOR QC LABORATORIES AND WASTE MANAGEMENT.
WHAT ARE THE NATIONAL SECURITY IMPLICATIONS OF API IMPORT DEPENDENCE?
HEAVY DEPENDENCE ON IMPORTED RAW MATERIALS, PARTICULARLY FOR LIFE-SAVING MEDICINES, CREATES SUPPLY CHAIN VULNERABILITIES EXPOSED DURING THE COVID-19 PANDEMIC AND BORDER TENSIONS. THIS POSES RISKS TO INDIA'S ABILITY TO ENSURE CONTINUOUS ACCESS TO ESSENTIAL DRUGS DURING GEOPOLITICAL CRISES.
