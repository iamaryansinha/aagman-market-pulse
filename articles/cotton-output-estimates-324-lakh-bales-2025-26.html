Cotton Association Revises Output Estimates To 324 Lakh Bales For 2025-26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Cotton Association of India revises 2025-26 output estimate to 324 lakh bales, up from 320.50 lakh bales
  • 02/Maharashtra leads with 105 lakh bales (up 7 lakh), offsetting declines in Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh
  • 03/Despite higher supply estimates, cotton prices crossed ₹60,000/candy for the first time this season

The Cotton Association of India has revised its cotton production estimate for the 2025-26 season upward to 324 lakh bales. Each bale weighs 170 kilograms. This is up from the previous estimate of 320.50 lakh bales.

The revised estimate also represents an increase from the 312.40 lakh bales produced in 2024-25. The total cotton supply for the current season is now estimated at 356.30 lakh bales.

Maharashtra has emerged as the top contributor with 105 lakh bales, an increase of 7 lakh bales from the previous estimate. The state's cotton belt has benefited from favorable weather and improved farming practices.

Gujarat, traditionally the largest cotton-producing state, saw its estimate revised downward by 3 lakh bales to 95 lakh bales. Unseasonal rains affected yields in key growing regions.

Madhya Pradesh's estimate was marginally reduced by 0.5 lakh bales to 22 lakh bales. Telangana and Andhra Pradesh maintained their estimates at 55 lakh and 30 lakh bales respectively.

Despite the higher production estimates, cotton prices have surged. Domestic prices crossed ₹60,000 per candy for the first time this season. The Cotton Corporation of India raised its purchase price by ₹300 per candy.

Global cotton prices are tracking ICE futures at approximately 73 cents per pound. The spread between Indian and international prices has narrowed, reducing import competitiveness.

Total cotton arrivals in mandis have reached 284 lakh bales as of early April. The arrival pace is expected to accelerate as farmers bring remaining crop to market.

The export outlook remains constrained by higher domestic prices. Bangladesh, Vietnam, and China are key export destinations, but Indian cotton is currently uncompetitive.

Mill consumption is estimated at 310 lakh bales for the season. Spinning mills are operating at 80-85% capacity utilization, supported by steady yarn demand.
