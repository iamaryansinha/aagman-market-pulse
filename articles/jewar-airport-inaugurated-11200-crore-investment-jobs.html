Jewar Airport Inaugurated: ₹11,200 Crore Phase I To Drive Investment, Jobs

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Union Civil Aviation Minister Ram Mohan Naidu inaugurates Jewar Airport Phase I built at ₹11,200 crore
  • 02/Airport features 4km runway, 40-acre MRO facility, and integrated cargo infrastructure
  • 03/Project developed as "aerotropolis" with Zurich Airport and Tata Projects as partners

Union Civil Aviation Minister Kinjarapu Ram Mohan Naidu inaugurated the Jewar Airport on March 28, 2026. The Phase I development cost approximately ₹11,200 crore.

The minister called the airport "another heartbeat" for the national capital region. He said it will transform the area into a hub for employment, investment, and economic growth.

The airport features a 4-kilometre-long runway fully equipped for international travel. Its terminal design reflects the "identity and pride of Uttar Pradesh" aligning with PM Modi's vision of "development along with heritage."

The project is being developed as an "aerotropolis" with integrated cargo facilities. A dedicated 40-acre MRO facility will provide employment opportunities for local youth.

Benefits will extend beyond Uttar Pradesh to the entire country. The minister emphasized the airport's role in boosting regional and national economic activity.

UP Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath received credit for improvements in law and order, connectivity, and investor confidence. These factors enabled the project's successful execution.

The development involves Zurich Airport and Tata Projects as key partners. Prime Minister Modi marked 8,936 days as head of government during the inauguration event.
