Tiger Global Tax Case: IT Dept Resumes Reassessment, Rejects GAAR Relief Impact

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Income Tax Department to resume reassessment proceedings against Tiger Global following Supreme Court ruling in favour of tax recovery
  • 02/March 31 CBDT notification protecting pre-April 2017 investments from GAAR does not apply to Tiger Global case
  • 03/Additional tax demands may arise beyond the ₹967.52 crore refund already withheld by authorities

The Income Tax Department is set to resume reassessment proceedings against Tiger Global. The Supreme Court ruling in January cleared the path for tax recovery from the US-based fund.

Tax officials confirmed the March 31 CBDT notification will not alter the Tiger Global case. The amendment protects legacy investments made before April 1, 2017 from GAAR ambit.

"The notification is not about any specific case," a senior official stated. Proceedings will continue as per law following the Supreme Court's favourable ruling.

The CBDT amended Income Tax Rules on March 31, 2026. The change explicitly shields pre-April 2017 investment transfers from GAAR, even for exits after that date.

The Supreme Court held that Tiger Global's 2018 Flipkart stake sale gains were taxable in India. The transaction was deemed an impermissible tax avoidance arrangement.

Tiger Global claimed exemption under grandfathering provisions. The fund sought a ₹967.52 crore refund for assessment year 2019-20, which tax authorities withheld.

The Delhi High Court had earlier granted relief to Tiger Global. The Supreme Court reversed that decision, ruling GAAR provisions were applicable to the transaction.

Recovery may extend beyond the withheld refund. An IT official did not rule out additional tax demands following reassessment proceedings.

Tiger Global's estimated gains exceeded ₹14,500 crore from the Flipkart stake sale. The three Mauritius entities involved claimed treaty benefits under the India-Mauritius DTAA.

The Supreme Court judgment distinguished between genuine legacy investments and arrangements lacking commercial substance. The amended rules protect the former while enforcement proceeds in this case.

THE SUPREME COURT RULED THAT TIGER GLOBAL'S MAURITIUS ENTITIES WERE NOT ENTITLED TO TREATY BENEFITS UNDER THE INDIA-MAURITIUS DTAA AS THE TRANSACTION CONSTITUTED AN IMPERMISSIBLE TAX AVOIDANCE ARRANGEMENT LACKING COMMERCIAL SUBSTANCE.
