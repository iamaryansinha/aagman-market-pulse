Delhi EV Mandate 2028 & Windfall Tax Hike: Impact on Oil Exporters, EV Makers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Ministry hikes windfall gain tax on export-bound diesel by 158% to ₹55.5 per litre and ATF by 42% to ₹42 per litre with immediate effect
  • 02/Delhi government proposes compulsory EV mandate: electric three-wheelers from January 2027, electric two-wheelers from April 2028
  • 03/Shares of IOC, BPCL, HPCL, Reliance Industries, Ola Electric Mobility, TVS Motor, Bajaj Auto and Hero MotoCorp will remain in focus this week

The Finance Ministry has hiked the windfall gain tax on export-bound diesel by over 158 per cent. The levy has increased from ₹21.5 per litre to ₹55.5 per litre with immediate effect.

The windfall levy on aviation turbine fuel (ATF) for exports has been raised by 42 per cent. The tax has gone up from ₹29.5 per litre to ₹42 per litre.

Export duty on petrol continues to remain nil. This is the first revision since the levy was reimposed on March 26, 2026.

Government officials clarified the revision aims to prevent exporters from taking undue advantage due to price differences. It is not primarily for boosting government revenue.

Shares of Indian Oil Corporation Ltd (NSE: IOC), Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (NSE: BPCL), Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd (NSE: HINDPETRO) and Reliance Industries Ltd (NSE: RELIANCE) will remain in focus.

The Delhi government has released a draft electric vehicle policy proposing mandatory EV adoption. New registrations will be restricted to electric three-wheelers starting January 2027.

Electric two-wheelers will become mandatory from April 2028. This marks a shift from incentives-led adoption to a deadline-driven transition in the capital.

Shares of Ather Industries, Ola Electric Mobility Ltd (NSE: OLAELEC), TVS Motor Company Ltd (NSE: TVSMOTOR), Bajaj Auto Ltd (NSE: BAJAJ-AUTO) and Hero MotoCorp Ltd (NSE: HEROMOTOCO) will be closely watched.

The compulsory EV mandate represents a significant policy shift. It could accelerate EV adoption and infrastructure development in Delhi over the next three years.

Oil marketing companies face margin pressure from higher export taxes. The windfall tax hike could impact their refining and export earnings in the near term.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE NEW WINDFALL TAX ON DIESEL EXPORTS?
THE WINDFALL GAIN TAX ON EXPORT-BOUND DIESEL HAS BEEN HIKED BY 158 PER CENT TO ₹55.5 PER LITRE. THIS IS UP FROM THE PREVIOUS RATE OF ₹21.5 PER LITRE AND IS EFFECTIVE IMMEDIATELY.
WHAT IS THE NEW WINDFALL TAX ON AVIATION TURBINE FUEL EXPORTS?
THE WINDFALL LEVY ON EXPORT-BOUND AVIATION TURBINE FUEL HAS BEEN RAISED BY 42 PER CENT TO ₹42 PER LITRE. THIS IS UP FROM THE PREVIOUS RATE OF ₹29.5 PER LITRE.
WHICH COMPANIES WILL BE IMPACTED BY THE WINDFALL TAX HIKE?
INDIAN OIL CORPORATION LTD (NSE: IOC), BHARAT PETROLEUM CORPORATION LTD (NSE: BPCL), HINDUSTAN PETROLEUM CORPORATION LTD (NSE: HINDPETRO) AND RELIANCE INDUSTRIES LTD (NSE: RELIANCE) WILL BE MOST IMPACTED BY THE HIGHER EXPORT TAXES ON DIESEL AND ATF.
WHAT IS THE DELHI GOVERNMENT'S PROPOSED EV MANDATE?
THE DELHI GOVERNMENT HAS PROPOSED THAT NEW REGISTRATIONS BE RESTRICTED TO ELECTRIC THREE-WHEELERS STARTING JANUARY 2027 AND ELECTRIC TWO-WHEELERS FROM APRIL 2028. THIS REPRESENTS A SHIFT FROM INCENTIVE-BASED TO MANDATORY EV ADOPTION.
WHICH EV MAKERS WILL BENEFIT FROM THE DELHI EV POLICY?
ATHER INDUSTRIES, OLA ELECTRIC MOBILITY LTD (NSE: OLAELEC), TVS MOTOR COMPANY LTD (NSE: TVSMOTOR), BAJAJ AUTO LTD (NSE: BAJAJ-AUTO) AND HERO MOTOCORP LTD (NSE: HEROMOTOCO) ARE EXPECTED TO BENEFIT FROM THE COMPULSORY EV MANDATE IN DELHI.
