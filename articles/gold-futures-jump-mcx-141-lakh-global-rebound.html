Gold Futures Jump ₹1,997 To ₹1.41 Lakh/10g On MCX As Global Prices Rebound

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/MCX gold futures for April delivery jumped ₹1,997 or 1.43% to ₹1,41,490 per 10 grams on Friday, March 27, 2026
  • 02/The rebound followed a sharp ₹4,604 decline on Thursday; global Comex gold rose $89.1 to $4,465.4 per ounce
  • 03/Analysts expect near-term gains to continue tracking strength in overseas markets amid geopolitical tensions

Gold futures surged ₹1,997 to ₹1,41,490 per 10 grams on the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) on Friday. The April delivery contract gained 1.43% as traders engaged in bargain buying.

The rebound comes after a steep fall on Thursday when gold had dropped ₹4,604 or 3.2%. That session had closed at ₹1,39,493 per 10 grams amid profit booking.

The June contract also appreciated, adding ₹1,811 or 1.27% to settle at ₹1,44,325 per 10 grams. It had plunged ₹4,926 in the previous trading session.

Commodity markets were closed in the morning session on Thursday for Ram Navami. Trading resumed in the evening session.

In international markets, gold for April delivery on Comex rose $89.1 or 2.04% to $4,465.4 per ounce. The June contract advanced $80.55 to $4,489.55 per ounce.

"Gold rose above USD 4,400 per ounce after a sharp decline in the previous session, as US President Donald Trump pushed back his deadline for Iran to secure a deal to end the war," said Jigar Trivedi, Senior Research Analyst at IndusInd Securities.

Trump pledged to refrain from targeting Iranian energy facilities until April 6. He also noted that Iran allowed 10 oil tankers to pass through the Strait of Hormuz this week.

Gold had dropped nearly 3% globally on Thursday amid doubts over a potential ceasefire. Rising energy costs had fueled inflation concerns and heightened expectations of interest rate hikes.

Trivedi added that gold futures may extend gains in the near term tracking strength in overseas markets. MCX silver also gained, with May contracts rising sharply.
