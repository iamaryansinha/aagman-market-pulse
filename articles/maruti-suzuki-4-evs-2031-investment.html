Maruti Suzuki To Launch 4 EVs By 2031 With ₹70,000 Crore Investment

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Maruti Suzuki to launch 4 EVs by 2031 with ₹70,000 crore total investment through FY2030-31
  • 02/New Khoraj plant: ₹35,000 crore investment with 1 million units annual capacity
  • 03/e-Vitara launched December 2025; faces competition from Tata.ev (39.7% market share)

Maruti Suzuki India has announced an ambitious electric vehicle roadmap through 2031. The company plans to launch 4 electric vehicles with a total investment of ₹70,000 crore through FY2030-31.

The e-Vitara, launched in December 2025, is the first EV in Maruti's lineup. Three more passenger EVs are planned along with two commercial EVs to complete the portfolio.

The investment plan includes ₹35,000 crore for a new Khoraj plant with 1 million units annual capacity. An additional ₹7,410 crore will upgrade the Kharkhoda plant for EV-ready flexible manufacturing.

SMG Gujarat expansion will receive ₹3,200 crore for EV component manufacturing. The company is building a comprehensive EV ecosystem from manufacturing to after-sales service.

Maruti enters the EV market as a latecomer. Tata.ev dominates with 39.7% market share, followed by MG at 25.1% and Mahindra at 22.1%. The top three control 87% of the market.

Maruti's strategy leverages scale and service network advantages. The company has enabled 1,500 EV service workshops and introduced a Battery-as-a-Service model to reduce upfront costs.

Partnership with Toyota provides technology access and shared platforms. This collaboration helps Maruti catch up on EV technology development where it lags competitors.

The e-Vitara targets the premium SUV segment with 400-500 km range. Pricing is expected to be competitive given Maruti's manufacturing scale advantages.

Commercial EVs will target fleet operators and last-mile delivery. This segment is growing rapidly with e-commerce expansion and government fleet electrification mandates.

Analysts view Maruti's EV entry as necessary but challenging. The company must execute flawlessly to gain market share in an increasingly crowded segment.
