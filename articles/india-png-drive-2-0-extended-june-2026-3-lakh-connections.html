India PNG Drive 2.0 Extended Till June 2026; 3.1 Lakh Connections Added In March

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Over 3.1 lakh PNG connections activated in March 2026 amid LPG supply concerns from West Asia conflict
  • 02/National PNG Drive 2.0 extended till June 2026; 2.67 lakh additional applications under processing
  • 03/Government targets 60 lakh LPG households for migration to piped natural gas

India's National PNG Drive 2.0 has been extended by three months till June 2026. The Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board (PNGRB) launched the drive in January 2026.

Over 3.1 lakh PNG connections were activated in March 2026 alone. This includes domestic, commercial, hostel, mess, and canteen connections. Another 2.67 lakh applications are being processed.

The extension follows strong consumer response and ongoing LPG supply disruptions. India imports 60% of its LPG from West Asia, where conflict has affected shipments.

As of January 2026, India has approximately 1.65 crore PNG connections. The country also has 8,687 CNG stations operational across various city gas distribution networks.

The government aims to migrate 60 lakh LPG consumers to PNG. These households already have pipeline access but continue using cylinder-based LPG.

PNG offers continuous supply without cylinder logistics. It is also 50-60% less polluting than coal and significantly cheaper than market-priced LPG in metro regions.

PNGRB held a video conference with CGD entities on March 30, 2026. Regulators directed all city gas companies to expedite domestic PNG connections immediately.

The long-term target is 12.64 crore PNG connections by 2034. Current penetration stands at 1.65 crore households against 33 crore LPG users nationwide.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MANY PNG CONNECTIONS WERE ACTIVATED IN MARCH 2026?
OVER 3.1 LAKH PNG CONNECTIONS WERE ACTIVATED IN MARCH 2026. THIS INCLUDES DOMESTIC, COMMERCIAL, HOSTEL, MESS, AND CANTEEN CONNECTIONS. AN ADDITIONAL 2.67 LAKH APPLICATIONS ARE BEING PROCESSED.
WHAT IS THE NATIONAL PNG DRIVE 2.0?
THE NATIONAL PNG DRIVE 2.0 IS A CAMPAIGN LAUNCHED BY PNGRB IN JANUARY 2026 TO EXPAND PIPED NATURAL GAS CONNECTIVITY. IT WAS ORIGINALLY A THREE-MONTH DRIVE BUT HAS BEEN EXTENDED TILL JUNE 2026 DUE TO STRONG CONSUMER RESPONSE.
WHY IS THE GOVERNMENT PUSHING FOR PNG MIGRATION NOW?
THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT HAS DISRUPTED LPG IMPORTS. INDIA PROCURES 60% OF ITS LPG FROM THE REGION. THE GOVERNMENT WANTS TO MIGRATE 60 LAKH HOUSEHOLDS THAT ALREADY HAVE PNG ACCESS BUT STILL USE LPG CYLINDERS.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT STATUS OF PNG IN INDIA?
AS OF JANUARY 2026, INDIA HAS APPROXIMATELY 1.65 CRORE PNG CONNECTIONS AND 8,687 CNG STATIONS. THE TARGET IS TO REACH 12.64 CRORE PNG CONNECTIONS BY 2034.
HOW DOES PNG COMPARE TO LPG FOR CONSUMERS?
PNG OPERATES AT LOWER PRESSURE AND ELIMINATES CYLINDER BOOKING, DELIVERY, AND STORAGE. IT IS CHEAPER THAN MARKET-PRICED LPG IN METRO AREAS LIKE DELHI, MUMBAI, AND AHMEDABAD. PNG ALSO BURNS CLEANER WITH 50-60% LOWER POLLUTION THAN COAL.
