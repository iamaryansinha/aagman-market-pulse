Gold Futures Jump ₹1,997 to ₹1.41 Lakh on Global Rebound After 3% Drop

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold futures surge ₹1,997 (1.43%) to ₹1,41,490 per 10 grams on MCX, reversing Thursday's 3.2% decline
  • 02/Comex gold rises $89.1 (2.04%) to $4,465.4 per ounce as Trump extends Iran deadline to April 6
  • 03/Near-term outlook positive with bargain buying, but US-Iran ceasefire uncertainty keeps markets volatile

Gold futures rebounded sharply on March 27, 2026, jumping ₹1,997 to settle at ₹1,41,490 per 10 grams on the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX). The 1.43% gain tracked overnight recovery in global markets following a steep decline in the previous session.

The April delivery contract had plummeted ₹4,604 (3.2%) to ₹1,39,493 on March 26. Bargain hunters stepped in after the sharp correction triggered by geopolitical uncertainty.

The June contract also gained ₹1,811 (1.27%) to close at ₹1,44,325 per 10 grams. It had dropped ₹4,926 (3.34%) in the previous trading session.

Commodity markets were closed in the morning session on March 26 for Ram Navami. Trading resumed in the evening session, allowing the full impact of global developments to filter through domestic prices.

In international markets, Comex gold for April delivery rose $89.1 (2.04%) to $4,465.4 per ounce. The June contract advanced $80.55 (1.83%) to $4,489.55 per ounce.

The rebound followed a near 3% drop in global gold prices on March 26. Doubts over a potential ceasefire between the United States and Iran drove the selloff, coupled with inflation fears from rising energy costs.

U.S. President Donald Trump pushed back his deadline for Iran to secure a deal until April 6. He pledged to refrain from targeting Iranian energy facilities until that date, providing some relief to markets unsettled by nearly a month of hostilities.

Trump also said Iran had allowed 10 oil tankers to pass through the Strait of Hormuz this week as a present to the United States. This gesture was viewed as a positive sign, though tensions remain elevated.

Iran rejected Washington's 15-point peace plan and submitted its own conditions. Tehran demanded recognition of its authority over the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint that handles approximately 20% of global oil shipments.

Gold has experienced significant volatility over the past month. Prices are down approximately 14.75% from the highs seen just four weeks ago, when gold traded near $5,190 per ounce. The metal remains up over 46% compared to the same period last year.

The sharp correction has created an entry opportunity for long-term investors who missed the earlier rally. Retail demand for physical gold typically increases during such price dips, particularly ahead of the wedding and festival season in India.

Silver futures also joined the rebound, tracking the upward momentum in gold. MCX silver contracts posted gains as global spot silver recovered from recent lows.

Gold prices have come under pressure this month as sharp rises in energy prices fueled inflation fears. The conflict-related oil supply shock has prompted expectations that major central banks may need to hike interest rates or delay planned cuts.

While gold typically benefits from inflationary pressures, it faces headwinds when real yields rise. Higher interest rates increase the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold.

"Gold rose above $4,400 per ounce after a sharp decline in the previous session as President Trump pushed back his deadline for Iran," said Jigar Trivedi, Senior Research Analyst at IndusInd Securities.

Trivedi added that gold futures may extend gains in the near term, tracking strength in overseas markets. However, traders should expect continued volatility until the geopolitical situation stabilizes.

Technical analysts suggest gold has found support near the $4,350 level, which corresponds to approximately ₹1,39,000 in domestic markets. Resistance is seen around $4,600 or ₹1,45,000 per 10 grams.

A sustained break above the resistance zone could trigger further short-covering and fresh buying. Conversely, a break below support may lead to deeper corrections.

Investors are advised to maintain a cautious approach and consider systematic accumulation rather than lump-sum investments given the volatile environment.

The rupee's movement against the dollar also impacts domestic gold prices. Continued FII selling in Indian equities has put pressure on the currency, which provides some support to local gold rates even when international prices decline.
