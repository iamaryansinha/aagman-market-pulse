South India To Lead $10 Trillion Economy Charge: CII President-Designate Mukundan

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CII President-Designate R Mukundan states South India will spearhead India's journey toward a $10 trillion economy by 2047
  • 02/South India currently contributes 31% to India's GDP and attracts 40% of national FDI inflows with 6.3% growth rate
  • 03/States target $1 trillion each: Tamil Nadu by 2030, Karnataka by 2032, Telangana by 2036, Kerala by 2047

R Mukundan, President-Designate of CII and MD & CEO of Tata Chemicals Ltd, has declared that South India will lead India's march to a $10 trillion economy. He made the statement at the CII Mystic South Global Linkages Summit 2025 in Chennai.

The summit unveiled a report titled '2047 Southern Odyssey: Powering Ahead to a $10 Trillion Economy.' It outlines the roadmap for South India's economic transformation over the next two decades.

South India currently contributes 31% to India's GDP despite having only about 20% of the population. The region attracts 40% of the country's FDI inflows and boasts a GDP growth rate of 6.3%, significantly higher than the rest of India's 5%.

The five southern states are Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana. Tamil Nadu leads manufacturing, contributing 20.4% to South India's manufacturing GDP.

Tamil Nadu and Karnataka together generate over 22 GW of wind and solar power. Tamil Nadu spearheads electric two-wheeler and auto component manufacturing.

The region has a highly skilled workforce exceeding 150 million people. Per capita income in South India has grown at 5% annually, surpassing the national average of 4.2%.

Andhra Pradesh and Telangana have achieved over 50% women's participation in the workforce. They are the only Indian states to reach this milestone.

R Nandini, Chairperson of CII Southern Region, highlighted that sustainable industrialization is non-negotiable. She emphasized investments in solar energy, green hydrogen, and EV infrastructure as vital for progress.

The region's vast coastline presents a tremendous opportunity for international trade. South India holds the longest coastline among global regions, giving it a geographic edge.
