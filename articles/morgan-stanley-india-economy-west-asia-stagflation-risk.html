Morgan Stanley: India Economy Resilient But West Asia Tensions Pose Stagflation Risk

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Morgan Stanley report highlights resilient domestic demand but warns of stagflation risks from West Asia geopolitical tensions
  • 02/West Asia accounts for 15% of India's exports and 38% of remittances, creating external vulnerability
  • 03/RBI maintains policy rate at 5.25% with proactive liquidity management; interbank liquidity remains in surplus

India's economic outlook shows resilience backed by strong domestic demand. However, rising West Asia geopolitical tensions pose significant stagflation risks, according to a Morgan Stanley report.

The report highlights broad-based strength across high-frequency indicators. GST collections remain stable while auto sales and credit growth show consistent improvement.

Manufacturing PMI has strengthened though services PMI has edged down slightly. This suggests some moderation in the services sector activity.

The labour market outlook is improving in CY2025 alongside rising employee expenses among BSE-500 companies. Corporate revenue held steady in the December 2025 quarter.

Nominal growth is expected to improve in FY2027E. Monthly SIP flows remain upbeat indicating sustained retail investor participation in equity markets.

External risks remain a key concern for policymakers. India is vulnerable to energy price volatility given West Asia accounts for 38% of total remittances and 15% of exports.

The Reserve Bank of India maintains the policy rate at 5.25%. Proactive liquidity management has ensured surplus interbank liquidity conditions.

"Domestic demand remains resilient; however, headwinds are emerging as ongoing geopolitical tensions create a stagflationary risk," the Morgan Stanley report stated.

WHAT DOES MORGAN STANLEY SAY ABOUT INDIA'S CURRENT ECONOMIC OUTLOOK?
MORGAN STANLEY'S REPORT HIGHLIGHTS THAT INDIA'S ECONOMIC OUTLOOK REMAINS SUPPORTED BY STRONG DOMESTIC DEMAND AND IMPROVING HIGH-FREQUENCY INDICATORS INCLUDING GST COLLECTIONS, AUTO SALES, AND CREDIT GROWTH.
WHAT IS THE STAGFLATION RISK MENTIONED BY MORGAN STANLEY?
THE REPORT WARNS THAT RISING GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS IN WEST ASIA POSE SIGNIFICANT STAGFLATION RISKS, AS PROLONGED DISRUPTION COULD CREATE DOWNSIDE RISKS TO GROWTH WHILE WORSENING MACROECONOMIC STABILITY THROUGH ENERGY PRICE VOLATILITY.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT RBI POLICY RATE AND LIQUIDITY POSITION?
THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA HAS MAINTAINED THE POLICY RATE AT 5.25 PER CENT. INTERBANK LIQUIDITY REMAINS IN SURPLUS FOLLOWING PROACTIVE LIQUIDITY MANAGEMENT BY THE CENTRAL BANK.
HOW SIGNIFICANT IS WEST ASIA FOR INDIA'S EXTERNAL SECTOR?
WEST ASIA ACCOUNTS FOR APPROXIMATELY 15 PER CENT OF INDIA'S TOTAL EXPORTS AND 38 PER CENT OF TOTAL REMITTANCES, MAKING THE REGION CRITICAL FOR EXTERNAL SECTOR STABILITY.
WHAT DO HIGH-FREQUENCY INDICATORS SHOW ABOUT INDIA'S ECONOMY?
HIGH-FREQUENCY INDICATORS SHOW BROAD-BASED STRENGTH WITH RESILIENT GST COLLECTIONS, IMPROVING AUTO SALES, BETTER CREDIT GROWTH, AND UPBEAT MONTHLY SIP FLOWS INDICATING SUSTAINED RETAIL PARTICIPATION.
