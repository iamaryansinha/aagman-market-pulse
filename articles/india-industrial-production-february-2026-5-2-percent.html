India Industrial Production Grows 5.2% in February, Manufacturing Leads at 6%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's Index of Industrial Production (IIP) grew 5.2% in February 2026, driven by strong manufacturing performance
  • 02/Manufacturing sector expanded 6%, while mining and electricity grew 3.1% and 2.3% respectively
  • 03/Broad-based growth across all three sectors indicates sustained industrial momentum

India's industrial output expanded 5.2% in February 2026. The growth was driven primarily by the manufacturing sector.

Manufacturing activity recorded a robust 6% expansion during the month. This marks a notable acceleration in factory output.

The mining sector grew 3.1% compared to the same period last year. Electricity generation also rose by 2.3%.

These figures reflect sustained momentum in the industrial economy. The data comes amid ongoing global trade uncertainties.

The 6% manufacturing growth signals strong domestic demand. It also indicates improving capacity utilization across key industries.

The electricity sector's 2.3% growth points to stable power demand. This typically correlates with industrial and commercial activity.

Analysts view the broad-based growth across all three sectors positively. It suggests resilience in the face of external headwinds.
