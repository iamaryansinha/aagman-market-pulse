Hindustan Zinc Mined Metal Production Rises 2% to Record 1,114 KT in FY26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Hindustan Zinc records highest-ever mined metal production at 1,114 KT in FY26, up 2% from 1,095 KT in FY25
  • 02/Refined zinc production increased 3% to 851 KT while refined lead declined 13% to 197 KT in FY26
  • 03/Silver production fell 9% to 627 tonnes; Q4 refined metal output hit record 282 KT, up 5% YoY

Hindustan Zinc Ltd (NSE: HINDZINC) has reported its highest-ever mined metal production. The company achieved 1,114 thousand tonnes (KT) in FY26, marking a 2% increase from 1,095 KT in FY25.

The growth in mined metal production was attributed to higher ore production and better grades. Q4 mined metal production stood at 315 KT compared to 310 KT in Q4 FY25.

Refined metal production for FY26 stood at 1,048 KT, a marginal decline of 0.4% from 1,052 KT in FY25. Refined zinc production increased 3% to 851 KT, driven by de-bottlenecking projects.

Refined lead production declined 13% to 197 KT. The company attributed this to Pyro operations running partially on lead mode and lower plant availability.

Silver production fell 9% to 627 tonnes in FY26, down from 687 tonnes. This was in line with lead production and affected by lower silver input from mines according to mining sequence.

Q4 FY26 saw record refined metal production at 282 KT, up 5% from 270 KT in Q4 FY25. This was driven by additional capacity unlocked at Chanderiya and Dariba facilities.

Wind power generation increased 7% to 372 million units in FY26. However, Q4 wind power declined 11% to 56 million units.
