GE Aerospace Scales AI From Pilots To Production; India Team Leads Global Push

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/GE Aerospace has reduced false positives by over 50% and improved lead time by more than 60% using machine learning-led engine monitoring systems
  • 02/Over 50% of the company's AI team is now based in Bengaluru out of 2,500 India employees; global workforce is 57,000
  • 03/AI spending has increased 2.5 to 3 times over the last 2.5 years; generative AI delivering 20-25% productivity gains in software development

GE Aerospace is moving artificial intelligence from pilot projects to core operations. The company has reduced false positives by over 50% and improved lead time by more than 60%.

These gains come from machine learning-led engine monitoring systems. The algorithms detect anomalies that humans would struggle to identify.

"These algorithms pick up anomalies that would be very difficult for a human to detect. The impact is very direct and significant in how we monitor our commercial engines," said Dinakar Deshmukh, Executive Director, Data Science and AI at GE Aerospace.

Generative AI is still at an early stage but showing tangible results. The company is seeing 20-25% productivity gains in software development.

"We are still learning when it comes to generative AI, we haven't fully conquered it yet," Deshmukh noted. "We already have generative AI applications in production driving real business outcomes."

India is increasingly anchoring GE Aerospace's global AI efforts. Over 50% of its AI team is based in Bengaluru. The company has around 2,500 employees in India out of a global workforce of 57,000.

The company has increased AI spending by 2.5 to 3 times over the last two-and-a-half years. Scaling from proof of concept to production remains the key challenge.

"Scaling from proof of concept to production is the hardest part, and that's where we focus," Deshmukh said. GE combines lean operating principles with AI to ensure solutions scale effectively.

"We don't try to apply AI everywhere, we focus on operations that are critical to business performance," he said. "Our approach is to let the problem define the model, not the other way around."
