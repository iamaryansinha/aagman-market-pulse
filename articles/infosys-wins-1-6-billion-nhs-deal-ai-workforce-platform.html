Infosys Wins $1.6 Billion NHS Deal to Build AI-Driven Workforce Platform

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Infosys wins landmark £1.2 billion ($1.59 billion) contract from UK's NHS Business Services Authority for 15 years
  • 02/Deal will modernize workforce management and payroll systems for 1.9 million NHS employees across England and Wales
  • 03/Platform will integrate Infosys Topaz AI to handle over £55 billion in annual transactions

Infosys Ltd (NSE: INFY) has secured a landmark £1.2 billion ($1.59 billion) contract from the UK's National Health Service Business Services Authority (NHSBSA). The 15-year agreement was announced in October 2025.

The deal represents one of Infosys' biggest wins in the public sector. It comes at a time when the $283-billion Indian IT industry faces global economic headwinds.

Under the contract, Infosys will design and implement a new data-driven workforce management platform. This will replace the NHS's existing Electronic Staff Record (ESR) system.

The platform will handle payroll and HR functions for 1.9 million NHS employees. These employees are spread across England and Wales.

The system will manage transactions worth over £55 billion annually. This makes it one of the largest workforce management platforms globally.

According to Infosys, the platform will integrate artificial intelligence and analytics. It will leverage the company's proprietary Infosys Topaz technology suite.

The AI-driven solution aims to enhance workforce planning capabilities. It will also streamline operations and improve user experience for NHS staff.

The platform supports the NHS's long-term digital transformation goals. It aligns with the organization's 10-year health workforce strategy.

"This partnership goes far beyond simply replacing ESR — it will be a strategic enabler for building a workforce that is fit for the future," said Michael Brodie, Chief Executive of NHSBSA.

Salil Parekh, CEO and Managing Director of Infosys, highlighted the company's global experience. He noted Infosys will deliver "a platform that drives efficiency today and empowers the NHS into the future."

This deal follows Infosys' recent strong performance in large deal wins. The company reported $3.8 billion in large deal TCV in Q1 FY26 with 55% net new deals.
