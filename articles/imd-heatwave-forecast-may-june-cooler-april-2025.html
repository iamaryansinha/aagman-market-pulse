IMD Forecasts More Heatwave Days in May-June as April Stays Cooler Than Expected

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IMD forecasts 10-11 heatwave days for eastern UP, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh and Odisha in May-June, double the typical 4-7 days
  • 02/April 2025 expected to be cooler with normal rainfall at 88-112% of long-term average of 39.2 mm
  • 03/Power demand projected to rise 9-10% this summer following 2024's record peak of 250 GW

The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has issued its seasonal outlook for April-June 2025. The forecast indicates more heatwave days in May and June compared to a relatively cooler April.

India typically records four to seven heatwave days between April and June. This year, however, many regions may see two to four additional days of extreme heat, according to IMD chief Mrutyunjay Mohapatra.

Some states are expected to face significantly higher heatwave activity. Eastern Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh and Odisha could experience 10 to 11 heatwave days during this period.

April 2025 is forecast to be cooler than the peak summer months. Normal rainfall is expected at between 88 to 112 percent of the long-term average of 39.2 mm.

The above-normal heatwave activity will impact a wide geographic area. States including Rajasthan, Gujarat, Haryana, Punjab, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh and parts of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu are all at risk.

Maximum temperatures are likely to soar across most of India from May onwards. Minimum temperatures are also expected to stay above average in most areas.

The forecast has significant implications for power consumption. Experts warn of a 9-10 percent spike in power demand this summer. In 2024, India's all-time peak power demand hit 250 gigawatts on May 30.

India faced a punishing summer in 2024, logging 536 heatwave days. This was the highest in 14 years. Government data recorded 41,789 suspected heat stroke cases and 143 heat-related deaths.

"Heatwave will be more compared to the last year, especially during May and sometime in June in North India," said Dr M. Ravichandran, Secretary, Ministry of Earth Sciences.

The Centre has instructed states to assess hospital preparedness for heatstroke and related illnesses. Heat action plans are being activated across vulnerable regions.

The IMD has also forecast an above-normal monsoon for 2025 at 105% of the Long Period Average. This could provide relief after the intense heat period.
