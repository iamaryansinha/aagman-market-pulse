Air China Resumes Beijing-Delhi Flights From April 21 After 6 Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Air China will resume Beijing-Delhi flights from April 21, 2026, operating thrice weekly with Airbus A330-300 aircraft
  • 02/The service adds approximately 1,800 two-way weekly seats, becoming the only nonstop route linking the two capitals
  • 03/Capacity between India and China expected to reach 16,900 two-way weekly seats by June 2026

Air China will restart direct flights between Beijing Capital Airport and Delhi's Indira Gandhi International Airport on April 21, 2026. The Star Alliance carrier will operate the route thrice weekly using Airbus A330-300 aircraft.

The airline last served India in February 2020. Services were suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent geopolitical tensions following the June 2020 Galwan Valley clash.

Air China becomes the fourth airline to resume services after the bilateral agreement in August 2025. IndiGo, China Eastern Airlines, and Air India have already restarted operations.

Current nonstop routes include IndiGo's Delhi-Kolkata-Guangzhou services and China Eastern's Shanghai-Delhi flights. Air India operates 4x weekly Delhi-Shanghai service.

Over 60,000 passengers have flown on non-stop flights between Delhi and China since November 2025. Traffic totaled 753,000 two-way passengers in 2025, up 16% year-on-year.

Capacity remains below pre-pandemic levels. Current weekly seats stand at 11,654 versus 22,934 in 2019. Chinese carriers dominated the market then with a 75% combined share.

China Eastern will add Kolkata-Kunming service from April 18. Carriers from both sides will operate around 40 flights per week in the summer schedule.

"The resumption of Air China's flights to Delhi is a positive move and will help improve people-to-people ties," said Anil Kalsi, Vice President of Travel Agents Federation of India.

Visa processing remains a challenge. Indian applicants face extended timelines and higher rejection rates despite visa restrictions being eased in 2025.
