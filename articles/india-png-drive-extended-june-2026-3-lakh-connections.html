India PNG Drive Extended Till June 2026; 3 Lakh Connections Added In March

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Over 3.1 lakh PNG connections activated in March 2026; 2.7 lakh additional applications processed
  • 02/PNG Drive 2.0 extended for three months till June 2026 due to strong consumer response
  • 03/Government targets migrating 60 lakh LPG consumers to PNG amid West Asia conflict uncertainty

The Indian government has extended PNG Drive 2.0 till June 2026. The drive was launched in January 2026 by PNGRB.

Over 3.1 lakh PNG connections were activated in March 2026. This includes domestic, commercial, hostel, and canteen connections.

An additional 2.67 lakh new applications have been processed. These are currently being gasified by city gas companies.

The extension comes amid ongoing West Asia conflict. India procures 60 per cent of its LPG requirements from this region.

The government aims to migrate 60 lakh LPG consumers to piped natural gas. This is a strategic shift to reduce import dependency.

As of January 2026, India has 1.65 crore PNG connections. The country also has 8,687 CNG stations operational.

Supply concerns persist for LPG imports. However, no dry-outs have been reported at LPG distributorships across the country.

Online LPG cylinder bookings have increased to 92 per cent. PSU Oil Marketing Companies delivered over 50 lakh cylinders daily since March 1.

"During March, more than 3.1 lakh connections including domestic, commercial, hostel, mess, canteen etc. have been gasified," Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas said in its statement.
