Switch Mobility Overtakes Tata Motors to Crown India's FY26 E-Bus Market

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/The Indian electric bus market hit an inflection point in FY26 with Switch Mobility doubling its share to claim the top spot
  • 02/Tata Motors plummeted from 35% to 4% market share as the shift signals structural change where winners bundle hardware with charging infrastructure, financing, and maintenance
  • 03/Pure manufacturing play is no longer enough as FY26 registrations clocked 4,341 units, up 44% from 3,016 the previous year

India's electric bus market is no longer a pilot project. FY26 registrations clocked 4,341 units, up 44% from 3,016 the previous year. But the headline isn't the growth—it's the bloodbath at the top.

Switch Mobility emerged as India's largest e-bus player with 1,144 units sold, capturing 26.4% of the market. That's up from 413 buses in FY25. The Ashok Leyland-backed subsidiary beat PMI Electro Mobility (1,113 units, 25.6%) and JBM Auto (1,052 units, 24.2%) to claim the crown.

PMI's rise follows a depot-first strategy targeting Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities with end-to-end solutions. JBM Auto retained its top-tier position through aggressive tender participation. Together, these three now control three-quarters of the market.

Tata Motors, FY25's leader with 1,058 buses and 35.1% share, fell to 177 units and 4.1% in FY26. The company is reportedly pivoting toward asset-light models and non-CESL tenders—a strategic retreat that has cost it dearly in the government's flagship procurement program.

Olectra Greentech suffered a similar fate: volumes rose modestly from 710 to 853 units, but market share collapsed from 23.5% to 19.6% as competitors scaled faster. The company remains strong in Mumbai and Pune but is increasingly looking like a regional specialist rather than a national contender.

The winners share one trait: they don't just sell buses. Switch Mobility, PMI, and JBM bundle vehicles with charging infrastructure, financing, and long-term maintenance. This "tender-to-tarmac" capability lets them clear procurement bottlenecks faster and win large-scale orders under the PM e-Bus Sewa scheme.

Legacy OEMs optimized for manufacturing volume. The market now rewards integrated service delivery.

"We are seeing a clear shift from pilot projects to large-scale deployments," says Sanyam Gandhi, Whole-Time Director at Chartered Speed Ltd, which secured a 1,750-bus contract in Bengaluru. "Electric buses are now viable on total cost of ownership even without subsidies."

The momentum is expected to continue, with electrification expanding into Tier-2 and Tier-3 markets. For incumbent automakers, the message is clear: adapt the business model, or watch challengers eat your lunch.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT DROVE TATA MOTORS' COLLAPSE IN E-BUS MARKET SHARE?
STRATEGIC PIVOT. THE COMPANY IS RETREATING FROM CESL TENDERS TO FOCUS ON ASSET-LIGHT MODELS AND NON-GOVERNMENT PROCUREMENT. THE MOVE IS FINANCIALLY PRUDENT BUT HAS CEDED THE VOLUME LEADERSHIP TO COMPETITORS WILLING TO PLAY THE BUNDLED-SERVICES GAME.
HOW DID SWITCH MOBILITY SCALE SO RAPIDLY?
COMBINATION OF ASHOK LEYLAND'S MANUFACTURING BACKBONE AND THE AGILITY OF A DEDICATED EV SUBSIDIARY. STRONG EXECUTION ON HIGH-VOLUME CORRIDORS LIKE CHENNAI AND BENGALURU, PLUS THE ABILITY TO OFFER INTEGRATED SOLUTIONS BEYOND JUST HARDWARE.
WHAT IS THE "ZERO ALLOCATION TRAP"?
ANALYST TERM FOR LEGACY PLAYERS LIKE VECV THAT STRUGGLE TO SECURE GOVERNMENT ALLOCATIONS DESPITE BRAND STRENGTH. THE TRAP SPRINGS WHEN PROCUREMENT CRITERIA FAVOR SERVICE-LED BUNDLED OFFERINGS OVER PURE MANUFACTURING CAPABILITY.
IS THE 44% MARKET GROWTH SUSTAINABLE?
INDUSTRY EXECUTIVES BELIEVE SO. PAYMENT SECURITY MECHANISMS UNDER PM E-BUS SEWA ARE BUILDING OPERATOR CONFIDENCE, AND E-BUSES ARE NOW TCO-VIABLE WITHOUT SUBSIDIES. EXPECT EXPANSION INTO TIER-2 AND TIER-3 CITIES TO CONTINUE.
WHO LOST THE MOST MARKET SHARE?
TATA MOTORS, DROPPING FROM 35.1% TO 4.1%. OLECTRA GREENTECH ALSO DECLINED SIGNIFICANTLY, FROM 23.5% TO 19.6%, DESPITE GROWING ABSOLUTE VOLUMES.
