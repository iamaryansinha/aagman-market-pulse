Infosys Shares Swing On $560 Million Acquisition Plans; Brokerages Stay Positive

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Infosys announces two US acquisitions worth $560 million: Optimum Healthcare IT for $465 million and Stratus for $95 million
  • 02/Brokerages Nomura and Emkay Global maintain Buy ratings with targets of Rs 1,810 and Rs 1,450 respectively
  • 03/Nifty 50 plunged over 1% to 22,990 on March 27, while Infosys shares swung between Rs 1,273-1,295 before turning flat

Infosys Ltd (NSE: INFY) announced two strategic acquisitions worth a combined $560 million on March 27, 2026. The company will acquire US-based Optimum Healthcare IT for $465 million and Stratus Global for $95 million in all-cash deals.

The stock gained over 1% in early trade, touching a day's high of Rs 1,294.80. It later turned flat at Rs 1,276.70 by mid-morning, swinging within a Rs 21 range.

Optimum Healthcare IT brings over 1,600 employees with deep healthcare expertise. The acquisition strengthens Infosys' presence in the healthcare provider segment and adds new client relationships.

Stratus contributes more than 450 professionals specializing in insurance technology. The deal expands Infosys' capabilities in property and casualty insurance platforms.

Brokerage Nomura maintained its Buy rating with a target price of Rs 1,810. It estimates the acquisitions could add around 225 basis points to FY27 revenue growth.

Emkay Global also reiterated its Buy call with a target of Rs 1,450. The brokerage noted the deals enhance vertical-focused consulting across healthcare IT and insurance.

The Nifty 50 index plunged over 1% to 22,990 on March 27 amid weak global cues and Iran tensions. However, the Nifty IT Index showed resilience, with TCS, HCL Tech, and Infosys among the top gainers.

Both acquisitions are expected to close in Q1 FY27. The deals mark Infosys' second-largest acquisition since 2025, behind only TCS's $700 million Coastal Cloud purchase.
