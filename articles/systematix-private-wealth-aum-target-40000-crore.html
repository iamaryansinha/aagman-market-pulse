Systematix Private Wealth Targets ₹40,000 Crore AUM In Five Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Systematix Private Wealth targets ₹38,000-40,000 crore AUM in five years from current base of ₹9,000 crore
  • 02/Expansion plan covers 17 cities starting with Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Indore, Pune in year one
  • 03/Minimum ticket size ₹5 crore for HNIs and ₹25 crore for ultra-HNIs; faces competition from HDFC, ICICI, 360 ONE Wealth

Systematix Private Wealth has set an ambitious target of expanding assets under management to ₹38,000-40,000 crore over the next five years. The firm is starting from an AUM base of approximately ₹9,000 crore, representing more than four-fold growth.

The wealth management vertical formally launched operations in April 2026 with equity capital of ₹100-110 crore. It is betting on asset allocation-led advisory to carve out space in India's burgeoning wealth market.

"This wealth management practice is fundamentally based out of right asset allocation. It is never product-driven," said Bhaskar Hazra, Joint Managing Director and CEO, in an interview with businessline.

The firm is targeting three distinct client segments. These include professionals such as CEOs and CXOs, family offices and business owners, and corporate treasury departments.

Minimum ticket sizes are set at ₹5 crore for high-net-worth individuals and ₹25 crore for ultra-HNIs. The platform offers services spanning equities, fixed income, alternatives, commodities, and offshore investments.

Geographic expansion is central to the strategy. Systematix plans to operate across six cities in year one: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Ahmedabad, Indore, and Pune.

By the end of year five, the footprint will expand to 17 cities. The roadmap includes tier-II wealth hubs such as Surat, Jaipur, Baroda, Lucknow, Hyderabad, and Goa.

The firm estimates that nearly $400 billion of high-net-worth wealth in India remains informally managed. Millionaires in India are rising by 12% annually, with the wealthy population controlling over a trillion dollars.

"So it gives us a lot of conviction that there is opportunity in India at this point, and the opportunity has been growing at a substantial pace," Hazra added.

India's formal wealth management penetration stands at just 11-12%. This compares to 60-75% in developed economies, according to Parth Sengupta, Joint MD and CEO.

A key pillar of the strategy is an in-house fund selection model backed by quantitative analytics. The firm claims its back-testing shows nearly 70% of selected funds remain consistent performers over four years.

"Recency bias often distorts portfolio construction. Our model addresses this systematically," Sengupta explained.

Staffing will grow from approximately 80 employees in year one to 230 by year five. The firm is targeting experienced talent with 12-15 years average experience.

A unique retention strategy includes offering ESOPs to relationship managers. The goal is to shift mindset from employee to entrepreneur and reduce industry-high attrition rates.

The wealth management sector is fiercely competitive. Systematix faces established players including HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, 360 ONE Wealth, and Motilal Oswal.
