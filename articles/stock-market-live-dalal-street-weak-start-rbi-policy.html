Stock Market Live: Dalal Street Braces For Weak Start; RBI Policy, Global Cues In Focus

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian stock markets (Sensex, Nifty) expected to open lower tracking weak global cues
  • 02/All eyes on RBI MPC meeting outcome; policy stance and rate decision due this week
  • 03/Foreign portfolio investors continue selling; watch for key support levels on Nifty

Indian equity markets are bracing for a weak opening on Monday. Benchmark indices Sensex and Nifty are expected to start in the red tracking negative global cues.

Traders are closely watching the Reserve Bank of India's monetary policy committee meeting. The RBI is scheduled to announce its policy decision amid persistent inflation concerns.

Global markets painted a mixed to negative picture overnight. Wall Street closed lower while Asian markets opened with cautious sentiment on Monday morning.

Foreign portfolio investors have been net sellers in recent sessions. The sustained FPI outflows are adding pressure on domestic equities and the rupee.

Analysts suggest the RBI may maintain its 'neutral' stance. However, any shift in language or guidance could trigger volatility across banking and rate-sensitive stocks.

Key technical levels are being closely monitored by traders. A break below crucial support on the Nifty could accelerate selling pressure from institutional players.

Sectorally, banking, auto, and consumption stocks may remain under pressure. IT and pharma could find support if rupee weakness continues.

Investors are advised to stay cautious ahead of the policy announcement. Global developments and FPI flow trends will remain critical market drivers.
