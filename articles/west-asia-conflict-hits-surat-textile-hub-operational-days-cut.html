West Asia Conflict Hits Surat Textile Hub: Operational Days Cut to 5 Per Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/South Gujarat Textile Processors Association limits operational days to 5 per week from 7 due to rising costs from West Asia conflict
  • 02/Industry facing losses of ₹90-100 crore daily; output dropped by almost half from 7 crore metres to 3.5 crore metres per day
  • 03/Imported raw material costs surged 30-35%; over 2,000 migrant workers left city creating 35% labour shortage

Gujarat's textile hub in Surat is facing severe pressure from the West Asia conflict. The industry is grappling with rising input costs and operational challenges.

The South Gujarat Textile Processors Association has reduced operational days. Units will now operate 5 days a week instead of 7 to manage costs.

"Due to rising costs of raw materials and coal, the textile processing industry is facing a crisis," said Jitendra Vaktania, president of the association.

Several units have scaled down production from 24 hours to 12 hours daily. This has significantly impacted overall output.

Industry estimates indicate losses of ₹90-100 crore per day. Surat produces nearly half of India's man-made fabric output.

The crisis has been compounded by a 35% labour shortage. Over 2,000 migrant workers have left the city in recent weeks.

Imported raw material costs including man-made fiber have surged 30-35%. Work has declined by 25-30% across weaving, processing and trading segments.

Prior to the crisis, the industry produced nearly 7 crore metres of fabric daily. Output has now dropped by almost half.
