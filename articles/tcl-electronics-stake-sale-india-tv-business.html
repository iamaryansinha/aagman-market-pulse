TCL Electronics Considers $200 Million Stake Sale In India TV Business

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/TCL Electronics Holdings is considering selling a stake in its Indian television manufacturing unit to raise at least $200 million
  • 02/The move follows Haier Smart Home's similar deal selling 49% of its India unit to Bharti Enterprises and Warburg Pincus
  • 03/TCL shares surged 30% in two weeks after announcing a TV-making joint venture with Sony Group Corp

TCL Electronics Holdings Ltd. (HKEX: 1070) is exploring a stake sale in its Indian television manufacturing operations. The Chinese company aims to raise at least $200 million from the transaction.

The company is working with an adviser to identify local buyers. A deal would help TCL expand its manufacturing footprint in India.

Considerations remain preliminary. The company may decide against proceeding with any transaction.

The potential sale mirrors a similar move by fellow Chinese electronics maker Haier Smart Home Co. (SHA: 600690). Haier agreed last year to sell a 49% stake in its Indian unit to Bharti Enterprises Ltd. and Warburg Pincus.

Haier stated its goal is to bolster its "Made in India, for India" strategy. TCL appears to be pursuing a comparable approach.

TCL did not respond to requests for comment on the potential stake sale.

TCL shares have gained approximately 70% over the past 12 months in Hong Kong trading. The stock rallied 30% in the two weeks through Friday.

The recent surge followed TCL's announcement of a TV-making joint venture with Japan's Sony Group Corp. (NYSE: SONY).
