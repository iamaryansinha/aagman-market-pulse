Indian Markets Shut for Good Friday After Volatile Session; Indices Down 11%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian stock markets shut for Good Friday following a volatile trading session
  • 02/Benchmark indices have declined over 11% since West Asia tensions began escalating
  • 03/Broader market mood remains fragile amid geopolitical uncertainty and oil price surge

Indian stock markets are closed for Good Friday. The holiday follows a volatile trading session.

Benchmark indices have suffered significant losses recently. The Nifty and Sensex are down over 11%.

The decline began when West Asia tensions escalated. Oil prices have surged amid the conflict.

Thursday's session saw late recovery attempts. However, the broader market mood remains fragile.

Foreign institutional investors have been net sellers. Outflows reflect risk-off sentiment globally.

Rising crude prices pose inflation and fiscal risks. These concerns are weighing on investor sentiment.

Markets will resume trading on Monday. Global cues over the long weekend will be watched.
