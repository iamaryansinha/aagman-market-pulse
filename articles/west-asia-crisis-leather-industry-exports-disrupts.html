West Asia Crisis Disrupts Leather Industry Exports, Raises Input Costs

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Shipping disruptions through Hormuz Strait have tripled freight costs for leather exporters over the past month
  • 02/Major Indian tanning hubs Kanpur, Jalandhar and Tamil Nadu units face raw material shortages and order cancellations
  • 03/Alternative routing around Cape of Good Hope adds 12-15 days and $2,500 per container

Leather exporters are grappling with severe supply chain disruptions as West Asia tensions choke the Hormuz Strait shipping route. The crisis has sent shipping costs surging by nearly 300% over the past month.

India's leather industry relies heavily on imported raw hides and chemicals. Container freight rates from Europe and Latin America have jumped from $1,200 to $4,500 per TEU since January.

Major tanning clusters in Kanpur, Jalandhar, and Ambur are facing acute shortages of chromium sulfate and finishing chemicals. These inputs typically transit through Dubai and Jebel Ali ports.

Export orders worth ₹850 crore face potential delays or cancellations. Italian and German buyers are demanding delivery guarantees that exporters cannot provide.

Alternative shipping routes via the Cape of Good Hope add 12-15 days to transit times. Each diverted container incurs an additional $2,500 in freight and fuel costs.

The Council for Leather Exports has sought government intervention. Industry representatives met Commerce Ministry officials on April 4 to discuss freight subsidies and insurance coverage.

Leather exports from India totaled $4.2 billion in FY2025. The current crisis threatens to reduce FY2026 volumes by 15-20% if disruptions persist beyond June.

Footwear and leather goods manufacturers in Agra and Chennai report 30% production cuts. Workers face reduced shifts as raw material inventories deplete.
