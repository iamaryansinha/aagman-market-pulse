Rupee Hits All-Time Low, Breaches 95 Per Dollar Before Recovering to 94.78

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rupee breached the 95 per dollar mark for the first time during intra-day trade on March 30, 2026
  • 02/The USD/INR pair swung 165 paise during the session before settling at 94.78 against the U.S. dollar
  • 03/West Asia crisis enters 31st day; forex traders cite geopolitical tensions keeping energy markets nervous

The Indian rupee hit a historic low on March 30, 2026. It breached the 95 per dollar mark during intra-day trade for the first time ever.

The USD/INR pair witnessed extreme volatility throughout the trading session. The currency swung 165 paise from its intra-day high to low.

Forex traders said the rupee recovered partially by the close. It settled at 94.78 against the U.S. dollar.

The West Asia crisis entered its 31st day. This has kept energy markets on edge and increased safe-haven demand for the dollar.

The sharp depreciation reflects global risk-off sentiment. Rising crude oil prices have added pressure on the trade deficit-sensitive currency.

The 165-paise intra-day swing is unusually wide for the rupee. It indicates heightened uncertainty among currency traders.

The Reserve Bank of India is likely intervening to curb excessive volatility. However, the underlying trend remains weak for the rupee.
