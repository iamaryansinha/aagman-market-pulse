FPIs Dump Record ₹1.14 Lakh Crore In March As West Asia War Rattles Markets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/FPIs sold Indian equities worth ₹1,13,810 crore in March 2026, the highest monthly outflow on record surpassing October 2024's ₹94,017 crore
  • 02/West Asia conflict and soaring crude oil prices above $100/barrel triggered global risk-off sentiment, dragging the rupee to a historic low of 94.30 per dollar
  • 03/Financial services sector bore the brunt with ₹31,831 crore in outflows; outlook hinges on geopolitical stability and crude price moderation

Foreign Portfolio Investors dumped Indian equities worth ₹1,13,810 crore in March 2026. This marks the highest monthly outflow ever recorded, surpassing the previous peak of ₹94,017 crore in October 2024.

The sustained selling has pushed total FPI outflows in 2026 to ₹1,27,157 crore. This follows a brief respite in February when foreign investors bought shares worth ₹22,615 crore.

The Iran-Israel war in West Asia is the primary driver behind the exodus. Escalating geopolitical tensions have pushed Brent crude prices above $100 per barrel, triggering fears of supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz.

The Indian rupee plunged to a historic low of 94.30 per US dollar on March 27, 2026. The currency has depreciated nearly 4% in March alone, eroding returns for foreign investors.

"The weakness in global equity markets following the war in West Asia, the steady depreciation of the rupee, fears of decline in remittances from the Gulf region and concerns surrounding the impact of high crude price on India's growth contributed to the sustained selling," said Dr. VK Vijayakumar, Chief Investment Strategist at Geojit Investments.

Sectorally, financial services stocks absorbed the worst blow. FPIs offloaded shares worth ₹31,831 crore from the BFSI sector during the first fortnight alone, accounting for roughly 60% of total equity selling.

Other sectors facing heavy outflows included automobiles, telecom, construction, oil and gas, healthcare, FMCG, and realty. The Nifty 50 settled at 22,819.60, down 2.09%, while the BSE Sensex closed at 73,583.22, declining 2.25%.

FPIs were net sellers on every trading day through March 20, marking one of the most sustained periods of outflows. DIIs provided partial support with net buying of ₹3,566 crore on March 27.

A reversal in FPI sentiment likely requires an end to West Asia hostilities and a decline in crude oil prices. Until then, market volatility is expected to persist.
