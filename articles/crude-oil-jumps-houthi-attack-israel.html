Crude Oil Jumps 2.4% as Houthi Attack on Israel Sparks Supply Fears

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/June Brent crude futures rise 2.41% to $107.86 per barrel on Monday following Houthi attack
  • 02/May WTI crude gains 2.05% to $101.68 as geopolitical tensions escalate in Middle East
  • 03/Watch for potential supply disruptions and further escalation in the region

Crude oil futures surged on Monday after Houthi militants attacked Israel. June Brent futures climbed 2.41% to $107.86 per barrel.

May WTI crude contracts gained 2.05% to settle at $101.68. The rally follows renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.

Houthi forces launched an attack on Israeli territory over the weekend. The incident raises concerns about potential supply disruptions from the region.

Middle East tensions often trigger sharp moves in oil markets. The region accounts for nearly one-third of global crude production.

Brent crude has now crossed the $107 mark for the first time in recent sessions. Traders are pricing in elevated risk premiums.

WTI's move above $100 reflects heightened supply anxiety. Both benchmarks have gained significant ground amid ongoing conflicts.

Investors should monitor developments in the Israel-Houthi conflict. Further escalation could drive prices higher in the near term.

Supply chain disruptions remain a key risk factor. Oil markets remain sensitive to any threats to Middle East exports.
