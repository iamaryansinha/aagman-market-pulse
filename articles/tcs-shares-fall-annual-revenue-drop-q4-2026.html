TCS Shares Fall as First Annual Revenue Drop in 20 Years Dulls Q4 Earnings Beat

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/TCS reports Q4 net profit of ₹13,718 crore, up 12% YoY, beating analyst estimates
  • 02/Full-year FY26 revenue declines 2.4% in constant currency, first annual drop in 20 years
  • 03/Operating margin hits four-year high of 25.3%; deal wins total $12 billion in Q4

Tata Consultancy Services Ltd (NSE: TCS) reported a 12% year-on-year jump in Q4 net profit to ₹13,718 crore. Revenue from operations rose 10% YoY to ₹70,698 crore for the quarter ended March 31, 2026.

Despite beating quarterly estimates, TCS shares fell as investors digested the full-year numbers. FY26 revenue declined 2.4% YoY in constant currency terms, marking the company's first annual revenue drop in two decades.

The rare annual decline overshadowed strong quarterly performance. Operating margins hit a four-year high of 25.3%, up 10 basis points sequentially. Net margin stood at 19.4%.

"We are pleased to report the third consecutive quarter of sequential growth," said K Krithivasan, CEO and Managing Director. Three mega deals and $12 billion in total contract value drove the quarterly momentum.

The board declared a final dividend of ₹31 per share, subject to AGM approval. Including the interim dividend of ₹79, total FY2026 payout reaches ₹110 per share. This translates to an 11,000% dividend on the face value of Re 1.

TCS closed FY26 with an employee headcount of 584,519. Attrition rose slightly to 13.7% from 13.5% in Q3. The company added 2,356 employees in Q4, reversing three quarters of decline.

Annualised AI revenue reached $2.3 billion, up from $1.8 billion in Q3. The BFSI vertical grew 31.6% while consumer business rose 15.7% in the March quarter.
