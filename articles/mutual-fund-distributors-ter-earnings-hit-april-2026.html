Mutual Fund Distributors Face 15% Earnings Hit as New TER Regime Kicks In April 1

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Mutual fund distributors face up to 15% income drop as new TER regime takes effect April 1, 2026
  • 02/GST on commissions will be deducted from payouts; 85-90% of 2 lakh distributors are unregistered
  • 03/SEBI unbundles statutory levies from TER; brokerage caps halved for cash and derivative trades

Mutual fund distributors are bracing for a sharp earnings decline from April 1, 2026. The new Total Expense Ratio regime announced by SEBI in December 2025 introduces structural changes to commission payouts.

Under the revised framework, AMCs will pay distributor commissions after deducting 18% GST. An unregistered distributor earning ₹1 lakh monthly will now receive ₹82,000 after the GST deduction.

SEBI has unbundled all statutory levies from the TER structure. GST on management fees, Securities Transaction Tax, and stamp duty will now be excluded from the base expense ratio.

The MF industry has approximately 2 lakh distributors with active ARN codes. Industry estimates suggest 85-90% of these distributors are not registered for GST.

For investors, the new framework improves cost transparency. Fund management fees will form the Base Expense Ratio, while statutory costs will be charged separately on actuals.

Brokerage costs have been reduced significantly under the new rules. Cash market transaction caps are halved from 12 basis points to 6 basis points.

Derivative brokerage limits have been cut from 5 basis points to 2 basis points. The additional 5 basis points exit load allowance has also been abolished.

Aditya Agarwal, co-founder of Wealthy.in, estimates a 15% income impact for distributors below the ₹20 lakh GST threshold. However, equity AUM growth may offset this within a year.

Shashwat Kumar, an Indore-based MFD managing ₹15 crore AUM, said compliance costs for GST registration and filing can overwhelm smaller distributors without backend support.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE NEW TER REGIME EFFECTIVE APRIL 1, 2026?
SEBI HAS RESTRUCTURED THE TOTAL EXPENSE RATIO FRAMEWORK TO UNBUNDLE STATUTORY LEVIES. THE NEW STRUCTURE SEPARATES BASE EXPENSE RATIO (BER) FOR FUND MANAGEMENT FEES FROM STATUTORY COSTS LIKE GST, STT, AND STAMP DUTY WHICH WILL NOW BE CHARGED SEPARATELY.
HOW WILL DISTRIBUTOR COMMISSIONS CHANGE UNDER THE NEW RULES?
AMCS WILL NOW PAY COMMISSIONS AFTER DEDUCTING 18% GST. A DISTRIBUTOR EARNING ₹1 LAKH MONTHLY WHO IS NOT GST-REGISTERED WILL RECEIVE ONLY ₹82,000 AFTER THE DEDUCTION, REPRESENTING AN 18% REDUCTION IN TAKE-HOME PAY.
HOW MANY DISTRIBUTORS WILL BE AFFECTED BY THE GST CHANGES?
THE MF INDUSTRY HAS ABOUT 2 LAKH DISTRIBUTORS WITH ACTIVE ARN CODES. INDUSTRY ESTIMATES INDICATE 85-90% OF DISTRIBUTORS ARE NOT GST-REGISTERED, MEANING UP TO 1.8 LAKH DISTRIBUTORS COULD FACE EARNINGS IMPACTS.
WHAT ARE THE NEW BROKERAGE CAPS UNDER THE SEBI REGULATIONS?
CASH MARKET BROKERAGE CAPS HAVE BEEN REDUCED FROM 12 BASIS POINTS TO 6 BASIS POINTS. DERIVATIVE BROKERAGE LIMITS ARE CUT FROM 5 BASIS POINTS TO 2 BASIS POINTS. THE ADDITIONAL 5 BASIS POINTS EXIT LOAD ALLOWANCE HAS BEEN ABOLISHED.
WHAT SHOULD DISTRIBUTORS DO TO MINIMIZE THE EARNINGS IMPACT?
DISTRIBUTORS CAN REGISTER FOR GST TO CLAIM THE DEDUCTED AMOUNT AFTER REMITTING TAX TO THE GOVERNMENT AND SUBMITTING PROOF TO AMCS. HOWEVER, THIS INVOLVES COMPLIANCE COSTS, TRACKING MULTIPLE AMCS, AND MAINTAINING FILING RECORDS.
