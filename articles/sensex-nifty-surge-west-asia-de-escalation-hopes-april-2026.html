Sensex Jumps 1,900 Points on West Asia De-Escalation Hopes; Nifty Reclaims 22,900

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BSE Sensex surged 1,899.53 points to 73,847.08 in early trade on April 1, 2026
  • 02/NSE Nifty jumped 572.55 points to 22,903.95 on hopes of West Asia war de-escalation
  • 03/Market optimism driven by potential easing of geopolitical tensions in the region

Indian equity markets opened sharply higher on Tuesday. The BSE Sensex jumped 1,899.53 points to 73,847.08 in early trade.

The 50-share NSE Nifty surged 572.55 points to 22,903.95. This marks a significant recovery after recent volatility.

The rally was driven by hopes of potential de-escalation in the West Asia war. Investors cheered prospects of easing geopolitical tensions.

Broader market sentiment turned positive across sectors. Buying interest was visible in banking, energy, and IT stocks.

Global cues also supported the domestic rally. Asian markets traded higher tracking overnight gains on Wall Street.

Crude oil prices cooled slightly on de-escalation hopes. This provided relief to oil-importing economies like India.

Analysts caution that volatility may persist until a formal ceasefire is announced. Traders are advised to watch diplomatic developments closely.

The rupee also strengthened against the dollar in early trade. Foreign institutional investors turned net buyers in the cash segment.

Market participants will track updates from West Asia throughout the session. Any escalation could trigger profit-booking at higher levels.
