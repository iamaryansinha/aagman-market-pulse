Rupee Breaches 95 Against Dollar For First Time, Recovers To 93.57

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian rupee breaches the 95-level against the US dollar for the first time in history during intraday trade
  • 02/The currency recovered to close at 93.57, registering a gain of 128 paise from its previous close
  • 03/Volatility driven by global forex movements; traders watching RBI intervention levels

The Indian rupee breached the 95-level against the US dollar for the first time ever. The currency touched this historic low during volatile intraday trading on March 30, 2026.

At the interbank foreign exchange, the rupee opened at 93.62 against the greenback. It then strengthened to settle at 93.57, marking a recovery of 128 paise.

The sharp intraday swing highlights ongoing volatility in forex markets. Global dollar strength and capital flow movements continue to pressure emerging market currencies.

The previous close for the rupee was significantly weaker than Monday's closing level. The 128-paise gain represents one of the sharpest single-day recoveries in recent sessions.

Forex traders are closely monitoring Reserve Bank of India intervention levels. The central bank has been actively defending the currency through dollar sales in spot markets.

The rupee's breach of 95 marks a psychological threshold for market participants. Technical analysts suggest the next support levels will be critical for determining near-term direction.

The dollar index remains elevated against a basket of major currencies. This continues to exert downward pressure on the rupee despite the day's recovery.
