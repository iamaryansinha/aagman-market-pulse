Nifty Plunges 487 Points, Marks Fifth Straight Weekly Fall On War Fears

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nifty plunged 487 points to settle at 22,819, marking its fifth straight weekly decline amid escalating US-Iran war tensions
  • 02/Sensex tumbled 1,690 points to 73,583; Nifty down 10% since conflict began on February 28, 2026
  • 03/Rupee hit record low of 93.94 vs dollar; Brent crude above $115/barrel stoking inflation fears

The Nifty 50 plunged 487 points on Friday to close at 22,819. The 50-share index shed over 2% as war fears gripped global markets.

This marks the fifth consecutive weekly decline for the benchmark. The Nifty has now fallen 10% since the US-Iran war began on February 28.

The 30-share BSE Sensex tumbled 1,690 points to settle at 73,583. Both indices hit their lowest levels since April 2025.

The US-Israel-Iran conflict entered its fourth week this Friday. Reports of Iranian strikes on US military bases in Qatar and Saudi Arabia escalated tensions.

Brent crude surged above $115 per barrel on supply disruption fears. India faces inflationary pressure as a net oil importer.

The Indian rupee hit a record low of 93.94 against the US dollar. Foreign institutional investors sold ₹86,781 crore in March alone.

Domestic institutional investors have bought ₹2.08 lakh crore year-to-date. However, DII inflows have failed to offset FII selling pressure.

India's volatility index jumped 15% to the 26 level. This marks the highest reading since June 2024.

Market capitalisation of BSE-listed companies has eroded by ₹47.53 lakh crore in 23 trading sessions. The benchmark now trades 14% below its lifetime high.

"The weakness reflects significant deterioration in global risk sentiment," said Hariprasad K, Founder of Livelong Wealth. Analysts advise selective accumulation of large-cap stocks on declines.
