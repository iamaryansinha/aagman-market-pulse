Gold, Silver To Remain Range-Bound Ahead Of Powell Speech, West Asia In Focus

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold trades at $4,493 and silver at $69.74, both expected to remain range-bound with key support at $4,400 and $64 respectively
  • 02/Fed Chair Powell speaks on March 30; hawkish tone could pressure gold toward $4,114 while dovish comments may lift it to $4,738
  • 03/April 6 deadline for Iran to reopen Strait of Hormuz creates binary risk; escalation could spike safe-haven demand

Gold and silver are expected to trade in tight ranges this week as markets await Fed Chair Jerome Powell's speech on March 30 and the April 6 deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

Spot gold closed at $4,493 on March 28, down 17% from its record high of $5,417 hit on March 3. Silver trades at $69.74, marking a 44% correction from its February all-time high of $122.74.

Powell's speech on Monday is the week's first major catalyst. A hawkish reiteration could push gold toward support at $4,114, while a dovish surprise may trigger a bounce to $4,738 resistance.

Silver faces immediate resistance at $76.61, the Fibonacci 0.618 level. A decisive break above this zone is needed to reverse the bearish trend. Support sits at $64.07.

The West Asia crisis adds a layer of geopolitical uncertainty. The US has set an April 6 deadline for Iran to reopen Hormuz, which carries 20% of global oil shipments. Escalation could trigger safe-haven buying.

WTI crude trades at $101.17 with a $14-18 war premium embedded. A ceasefire could reprice oil lower by $10-15, while escalation risks pushing WTI toward $115-120.

Goldman Sachs maintains a $4,900 year-end gold target. JPMorgan forecasts $4,753 average for 2026 with potential to reach $5,000 by Q4.

Traders are advised to reduce position sizes ahead of the weekend deadline. The binary nature of the Hormuz outcome creates unquantifiable gap risk for Monday's Asian open.
