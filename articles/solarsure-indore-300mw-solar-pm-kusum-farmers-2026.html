Solarsure Indore: 300 MW Solar Plants Commissioned Under PM-KUSUM to Benefit 50,000 Farmers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indore-based Solarsure commissions over 300 MW solar capacity under PM-KUSUM scheme, benefiting 50,000+ farmers across India
  • 02/Company charges ₹0.5-₹1 per watt and has 1.5 GW projects in pipeline; farmer incomes rise 30% through reduced input costs
  • 03/Solarsure uses AI and satellite data from ISRO/NASA for risk evaluation; expansion planned under upcoming PM-KUSUM 2.0

Indore-based Solarsure has commissioned solar power plants with over 300 MW capacity under the PM-KUSUM scheme. The company has enabled more than 50,000 farmers to access irrigation through solar power.

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Uttan Mahabhiyan (PM-KUSUM) scheme aims to reduce farmers' dependence on erratic power supply and diesel. Solarsure is a leader in this segment.

Farmers have seen their incomes increase by 30 per cent after installing the solar infrastructure. The company gives farmers predictability: timely irrigation, lower input costs, and surplus power generation in many cases.

Founder Bhavesh Patidar said the company's objective extends beyond installation. In regions with historically unreliable electricity, the shift is directly impacting crop cycles and household financial stability.

Solarsure operates mainly in Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh. The company charges a nominal fee of ₹0.5 to ₹1 per watt, translating to ₹5-7 lakh for a 500 kW plant.

Farmers can sell power to the government after signing power purchase agreements (PPAs). Solarsure sets up plants on farmer land and handles the entire development cycle.

The company uses artificial intelligence for risk evaluation and plant design. It combines satellite data from ISRO and NASA to assess weather risks and optimize power generation.

"What if wind speed reaches 100 miles per hour? Can the land withstand such conditions?" said Patidar. The AI helps forecast output and ensures plants last their 25-year lifetime.

Solarsure also works with the carbon-neutral industrial (CNI) sector. The company manufactures equipment and is expanding into transmission infrastructure to address grid connectivity issues.
