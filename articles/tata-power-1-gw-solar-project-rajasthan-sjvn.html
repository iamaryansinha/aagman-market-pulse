Tata Power Renewable Commissions 1 GW Solar Project In Rajasthan For SJVN

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Tata Power Renewable Energy Ltd commissioned a 1,000 MW solar project for SJVN in Bikaner, Rajasthan on December 30, 2025
  • 02/The project cost INR 54.9 billion ($611 million) and is TPREL's largest EPC execution to date
  • 03/With this commissioning, TPREL's total renewable utility-scale capacity reaches 11.6 GW

Tata Power Renewable Energy Ltd (TPREL) has commissioned a 1 GW solar project for SJVN Ltd. The facility spans Bandarwala and Karnisar Bhatiyan sites in Bikaner, Rajasthan.

The project was commissioned on December 30, 2025. It represents TPREL's largest solar EPC execution to date.

The facility has an AC capacity of 1,000 MW and DC capacity of 1,400 MWp. Project cost is INR 54.9 billion ($611 million).

All 2.4 million solar modules were manufactured at TP Solar Ltd in Tirunelveli, Tamil Nadu. This reinforces Tata Power's commitment to domestic manufacturing under the Make in India initiative.

Capacity allocation includes 500 MW to Rajasthan Urja Vikas and IT Services, 300 MW to Jammu and Kashmir Power, and 200 MW to Uttarakhand Power Corporation.

The project is expected to generate approximately 2,454.84 million units of green electricity in its first year. It will offset nearly 1.74 million tonnes of CO2 emissions annually.

The facility was designed for extreme climatic conditions. Temperatures range from 50°C peak summers to 3°C winter lows.

The project employed over 300 local workers and developed more than 25 local vendors. This created sustained livelihood opportunities in the region.

With this commissioning, TPREL's total renewable utility-scale capacity has reached 11.6 GW. This includes 4.9 GW executed as third-party EPC projects.

Tata Power Company Ltd (NSE: TATAPOWER) currently operates 5.8 GW of its own renewable capacity. An additional 5.8 GW is under various stages of implementation.

The project supports India's goal of achieving 500 GW of non-fossil fuel capacity by 2030. It also advances the nation's net-zero emissions target by 2070.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE CAPACITY OF THE TATA POWER SOLAR PROJECT COMMISSIONED IN RAJASTHAN?
THE PROJECT HAS AN AC CAPACITY OF 1,000 MW (1 GW) AND A DC CAPACITY OF 1,400 MWp. IT IS LOCATED ACROSS THE BANDARWALA AND KARNISAR BHATIYAN SITES IN BIKANER, RAJASTHAN.
WHO IS THE CLIENT FOR THIS SOLAR PROJECT?
THE PROJECT WAS COMMISSIONED FOR SJVN LTD, AN INDIAN STATE-OWNED RENEWABLE ENERGY DEVELOPER. IT IS SJVN'S LARGEST SOLAR PROJECT COMPLYING WITH INDIA'S DOMESTIC CONTENT REQUIREMENT (DCR) RULES.
WHAT IS THE EXPECTED POWER GENERATION FROM THIS PROJECT?
THE PROJECT IS EXPECTED TO GENERATE APPROXIMATELY 2,454.84 MILLION UNITS OF GREEN ELECTRICITY IN ITS FIRST YEAR OF OPERATION. IT WILL OFFSET NEARLY 1.74 MILLION TONNES OF CO2 EMISSIONS ANNUALLY.
WHICH STATE UTILITIES WILL RECEIVE POWER FROM THIS PROJECT?
THE CAPACITY IS ALLOCATED TO THREE STATE UTILITIES: 500 MW TO RAJASTHAN URJA VIKAS AND IT SERVICES LIMITED (RUVITL), 300 MW TO JAMMU AND KASHMIR POWER LIMITED (JKPL), AND 200 MW TO UTTARAKHAND POWER CORPORATION LIMITED (UPCL).
WHAT IS THE TOTAL RENEWABLE CAPACITY OF TATA POWER RENEWABLE ENERGY LTD AFTER THIS COMMISSIONING?
TPREL'S TOTAL RENEWABLE UTILITY-SCALE CAPACITY HAS REACHED 11.6 GW, INCLUDING 4.9 GW EXECUTED AS THIRD-PARTY EPC PROJECTS. TATA POWER CURRENTLY OPERATES 5.8 GW OF ITS OWN RENEWABLE CAPACITY WITH AN ADDITIONAL 5.8 GW UNDER IMPLEMENTATION.
