Gold Hits Record High of ₹88,500 Per 10 Grams on Global Trade War Fears

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold prices jumped ₹2,430 to hit a lifetime high of ₹88,500 per 10 grams in Delhi on February 10, 2025
  • 02/The surge was driven by Donald Trump's announcement of 25% tariffs on steel and aluminum imports to the US
  • 03/Investors should watch for further escalation in trade tensions and potential Fed rate decisions

Gold prices surged ₹2,430 to hit a lifetime high of ₹88,500 per 10 grams in Delhi on Monday. The previous close was ₹86,070 per 10 grams according to the All India Sarafa Association.

The rally was triggered by US President Donald Trump's announcement of fresh 25% tariffs on all steel and aluminum imports to the US. This intensified fears of a global trade war and sent investors rushing to safe-haven assets.

Gold of 99.5% purity also hit a record high of ₹88,100 per 10 grams in local markets. The precious metal breached the psychological $2,900 per ounce level in global spot markets for the first time.

Silver prices increased ₹1,000 to ₹97,500 per kilogram. Heavy buying by jewellers and retailers further boosted prices across the bullion market.

On MCX, gold contracts for April delivery rallied ₹940 to hit a new lifetime high of ₹85,828 per 10 grams. The June delivery contract surged ₹1,015 to ₹86,636 per 10 grams.

"Gold witnessed strong gains as Trump's new round of tariffs on metal products intensified trade war concerns," said Jateen Trivedi, VP Research Analyst at LKP Securities.

Globally, Comex gold futures appreciated $45.09 per ounce to hit a record high of $2,932.69 per ounce. Spot gold breached the $2,900-mark as investors dumped riskier assets like stocks.

The rally was further supported by a weak Indian rupee against the US dollar. Currency depreciation makes gold more expensive for domestic buyers.
