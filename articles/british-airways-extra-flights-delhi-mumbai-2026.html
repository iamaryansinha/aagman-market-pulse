British Airways Adds Extra Flights To Delhi, Mumbai Until May 2026

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/British Airways will operate extra flights from London to Delhi and Mumbai until end of May 2026 to meet strong passenger demand
  • 02/Delhi gets third daily service from April 7 to May 31; Mumbai gets third daily from May 15 to 31 amid West Asia flight disruptions
  • 03/Capacity rises to 63 weekly flights with over 1,000 additional seats per week between India and UK

British Airways will operate additional flights from London to Delhi and Mumbai until May 31, 2026. The airline announced the short-term capacity boost on March 26, 2026.

From April 7 to May 31, British Airways will operate a third daily service from Delhi to London Heathrow. The Mumbai route gets its third daily flight between May 15 and May 31.

With this additional capacity, the airline will operate up to 63 weekly flights. This adds more than 1,000 seats per week between India and the UK.

The extra services respond to strong travel demand. They also address continued disruption of flights in West Asia that has reduced regional capacity.

British Airways currently operates 56 flights per week to India. It serves five cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Chennai.

India represents the second largest market for British Airways after the United States. The carrier has operated in the India-UK market for over 100 years.

"As we continue to respond to the evolving situation in West Asia, we've been able to reallocate additional capacity to meet strong demand on other destinations across our route network," said Neil Chernoff, Chief Planning and Strategy Officer at British Airways.

The UK-India aviation market operates under a nearly open skies arrangement. UK airlines are limited to 70 weekly flights between London and Delhi or Mumbai combined.
