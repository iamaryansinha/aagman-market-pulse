Nykaa Q4 FY25 Results: Revenue Up 24%, PAT Surges 110% To ₹19 Crore

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/FSN E-Commerce (Nykaa) reports 24% YoY revenue growth to ₹2,062 crore in Q4 FY25, the strongest quarterly growth in three years
  • 02/Net profit jumps 110% YoY to ₹19 crore; EBITDA surges 43% to ₹133 crore with margins expanding to 6.5%
  • 03/Beauty segment GMV grows 31% YoY to ₹3,058 crore; watch for fashion vertical recovery and margin sustainability

FSN E-Commerce Ventures Ltd (NSE: NYKAA), which operates Nykaa, reported its strongest quarterly revenue growth in three years for Q4 FY25. Revenue from operations rose 24% year-on-year to ₹2,062 crore.

Net profit for the quarter surged 110% YoY to ₹19 crore, compared to ₹9 crore in Q4 FY24. Profit before tax more than doubled, growing 102% to ₹40 crore.

EBITDA expanded 43% YoY to ₹133 crore in Q4 FY25. EBITDA margins improved to 6.5% from 5.6% in the year-ago quarter.

Gross margins strengthened by 51 basis points to 44.1% for the quarter. Gross profit grew 28% YoY to ₹909 crore.

The beauty and personal care segment delivered robust performance. Beauty GMV jumped 31% YoY to ₹3,058 crore in Q4 FY25.

For the full year FY25, Nykaa's beauty vertical GMV reached ₹11,775 crore, posting 30% YoY growth. The segment significantly outpaced overall industry growth.

Nykaa Fashion showed recovery momentum with Q4 GMV growth at 18% YoY. Full-year fashion GMV stood at ₹3,804 crore with 12% growth.

The company's offline expansion accelerated with 50 new stores added in FY25. The total store count now stands at 237 across 79 cities.

Physical retail delivered strong results with 31% YoY GMV growth and 15% same-store sales growth. Nykaa remains India's largest specialized beauty retailer.

The Superstore B2B distribution business grew 57% YoY to ₹941 crore GMV in FY25. It now serves over 276,000 retailers across 1,100+ cities.

Customer base expanded 28% YoY to 42 million users. The company added 800+ new brand launches across fashion and beauty during the year.

"Each business is improving its margins, but the mix of these businesses affects the overall margin outlook," said Anchit Nayar, Executive Director, on the earnings call.

JM Financials maintains a 'Buy' rating with a target price of ₹250. Nomura retains 'Neutral' with a ₹216 target, citing margin growth needs to accelerate.
