Jet Fuel Prices Cross ₹2 Lakh/kl For First Time, Commercial LPG Hiked

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Aviation turbine fuel prices surged to ₹2,07,031 per kilolitre in Delhi, crossing ₹2 lakh mark for the first time in history
  • 02/ATF prices have more than doubled from previous levels, driven by rising global crude oil prices and supply concerns
  • 03/Domestic cooking gas rates remain unchanged after March 7 hike of ₹60 per 14.2 kg cylinder

Aviation turbine fuel prices have crossed ₹2 lakh per kilolitre for the first time ever. State-owned oil marketing companies raised ATF rates to ₹2,07,031 per kl in Delhi effective April 1.

The price represents a more than 100% increase from previous levels. This marks an unprecedented milestone in Indian fuel pricing history.

Commercial LPG cylinder rates have been increased by ₹195.50. The revised prices apply to non-domestic 19 kg and 47.5 kg cylinders used by hotels and businesses.

Domestic cooking gas LPG prices remain unchanged at current levels. The last hike of ₹60 per 14.2 kg cylinder was implemented on March 7, 2026.

The ATF price surge follows elevated global crude oil prices. International Brent crude has traded above $75 per barrel amid supply constraints.

Airlines now face significantly higher operating costs. Fuel accounts for 35-40% of an airline's total expenses, impacting profitability margins.

The price revision comes into effect from April 1, 2026. Oil companies adjust rates at the start of each month based on international benchmarks.
