Delhi Mandates Electric 3-Wheelers By 2027, 2-Wheelers By 2028

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Delhi mandates electric 3-wheelers only from January 1, 2027; electric 2-wheelers only from April 1, 2028
  • 02/₹3,954 crore total allocation: ₹1,718 Cr scrapping, ₹1,236 Cr purchase incentives, ₹1,000 Cr charging infrastructure
  • 03/First Delhi policy with tax exemptions for strong hybrids: 100% for EVs, 50% for strong hybrids

The Delhi government has announced a bold electric vehicle policy mandating complete transition to electric 3-wheelers and 2-wheelers. From January 1, 2027, only electric 3-wheelers will be allowed registration in Delhi. From April 1, 2028, the same applies to 2-wheelers.

The policy allocates ₹3,954 crore total funding. This breaks down into ₹1,718 crore for scrapping old vehicles, ₹1,236 crore for purchase incentives, and ₹1,000 crore for charging infrastructure development.

This is the first Delhi EV policy to include tax exemptions for strong hybrids. Pure electric vehicles get 100% road tax and registration fee exemption. Strong hybrids receive 50% exemption, creating a new category of incentives.

The auto industry faces significant adaptation challenges. Legacy ICE manufacturers must accelerate EV product launches. CNG 3-wheeler makers like Bajaj and TVS face the biggest disruption.

Winners from this policy include EV-first players like Ather Energy. Hybrid leaders Toyota and Maruti Suzuki benefit from the new hybrid incentive category. Established E2W players TVS and Bajaj with existing EV portfolios are positioned well.

The charging infrastructure plan mandates dealers to set up public charging stations. Single-window clearance will be provided for home and community charging installations.

Industry response has been generally positive from EV manufacturers. Concerns remain about the "registration cliff" and whether charging infrastructure can scale in time.

The policy makes Delhi the first Indian state to set hard deadlines for EV transition. Other states are expected to follow with similar mandates as air quality concerns mount.

Stock market implications are positive for Tata Motors, Maruti Suzuki, TVS Motor, and Mahindra & Mahindra. Companies to watch include Hero MotoCorp and Ola Electric.

The policy aligns with India's 2070 net-zero commitment. Transport sector accounts for 14% of Delhi's emissions, making this transition critical for air quality goals.
