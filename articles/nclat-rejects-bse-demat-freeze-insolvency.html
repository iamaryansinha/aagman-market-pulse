NCLAT Rejects BSE Pleas On Demat Freeze During Insolvency Proceedings

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NCLAT Delhi dismisses BSE Limited's appeals against NCLT Mumbai orders directing de-freezing of corporate debtors' demat accounts
  • 02/Ruling covers Future Corporate Resources and Liz Traders cases where accounts were frozen for non-payment of listing fees
  • 03/Judgment reinforces IBC Section 60(5) jurisdiction over securities law when dues are crystallized and recovery is pending

The National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) dismissed appeals filed by BSE Limited on March 25, 2026. The appellate tribunal upheld NCLT Mumbai orders directing stock exchanges to lift debit freezes on corporate debtors' demat accounts during insolvency proceedings.

The ruling covers two cases. The first involves Future Corporate Resources Pvt. Ltd., where the NCLT ordered de-freezing on October 31, 2025. The second concerns Liz Traders and Agents Pvt. Ltd., with an order dated July 31, 2024.

BSE had frozen the demat accounts due to non-payment of annual listing fees by group companies. The exchange argued that SEBI circulars and securities law governed these restrictions. It contended that NCLT lacked jurisdiction to interfere.

The NCLAT bench comprising Justice Mohammad Faiz Alam Khan and Naresh Salecha rejected BSE's arguments. The tribunal held that once dues stand crystallized and the dispute relates to recovery, jurisdiction shifts to the insolvency forum.

The appellate tribunal cited Section 60(5) and Section 238 of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016. It ruled that the NCLT has authority over corporate debtor assets even when actions originate under securities law.

The judgment allows resolution professionals to take custody of shares held in frozen demat accounts. These assets form part of the insolvency estate for distribution to creditors.

The ruling impacts thousands of shareholders and investors in companies undergoing insolvency. It clarifies that IBC moratorium protections override exchange-level restrictions when CIRP is active.

Legal experts note this sets a precedent for similar cases pending before NCLT benches across India. The decision strengthens the hand of resolution professionals in asset recovery.

WHICH CASES WERE COVERED IN THIS RULING?
THE JUDGMENT COVERS TWO CASES: FUTURE CORPORATE RESOURCES PVT. LTD. (ORDER DATED OCTOBER 31, 2025) AND LIZ TRADERS AND AGENTS PVT. LTD. (ORDER DATED JULY 31, 2024).
WHY WERE THE DEMAT ACCOUNTS FROZEN?
BSE HAD FROZEN THE ACCOUNTS DUE TO NON-PAYMENT OF ANNUAL LISTING FEES BY GROUP COMPANIES AND NON-COMPLIANCE WITH SEBI LISTING OBLIGATIONS AND DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS REGULATIONS.
WHAT LEGAL PROVISIONS DID NCLAT RELY ON?
THE TRIBUNAL CITED SECTION 60(5) AND SECTION 238 OF THE INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE (IBC), 2016. THESE PROVISIONS GIVE NCLT JURISDICTION OVER CORPORATE DEBTOR ASSETS DURING INSOLVENCY.
WHAT IS THE IMPACT ON SHAREHOLDERS AND INVESTORS?
THE RULING ALLOWS RESOLUTION PROFESSIONALS TO TAKE CONTROL OF SHARES IN FROZEN DEMAT ACCOUNTS. THESE ASSETS BECOME PART OF THE INSOLVENCY ESTATE FOR DISTRIBUTION TO CREDITORS, POTENTIALLY IMPROVING RECOVERY RATES.
