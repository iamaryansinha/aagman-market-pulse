Maersk Retrofits 200 Ships With Wind Tech to Cut Fuel Costs

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Maersk partners with 50 shipowners to retrofit around 200 vessels in its time-chartered fleet for improved fuel efficiency
  • 02/Program includes 1,500+ completed projects with 1,000 more scheduled by 2027; costs shared between Maersk and owners
  • 03/Supports Maersk's target of 35% absolute emissions reduction by 2030 vs 2022 baseline

A.P. Moller-Maersk is retrofitting approximately 200 vessels in its time-chartered fleet. The program involves 50 shipowners and aims to reduce both fuel costs and greenhouse gas emissions.

The initiative targets improved fuel efficiency and increased cargo-carrying capacity. This dual approach is designed to lower slot costs while cutting operational expenses.

Maersk and its partners have already executed over 1,500 individual retrofit projects. An additional 1,000 projects are scheduled for completion by 2027.

Investment costs for the retrofits are split between Maersk and vessel owners. Ahmed Hassan, Head of Asset Strategy at Maersk, calls this a "win-win" model.

Key retrofit solutions include propeller replacements and bulbous bow installations. These modifications reduce drag and improve hydrodynamic efficiency for vessels originally designed for higher speeds.

Additional upgrades feature pre-swirl devices, waste heat recovery systems, and shaft generators. Cargo capacity is being enhanced through wheelhouse elevation and lashing bridge improvements.

The program supports Maersk's 2030 target of a 35% absolute reduction in scope 1 emissions. The company has also committed to net-zero emissions by 2040.

Shipping accounts for nearly 3% of global man-made carbon dioxide emissions. Fuel costs remain a critical pressure point for the industry amid volatile oil prices.

"While fuel transition is essential for our net-zero journey, retrofitting existing vessels delivers tangible progress now," Hassan explained.
