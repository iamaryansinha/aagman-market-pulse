India's Securitisation Market Hits Record ₹2.55 Lakh Crore in FY26 on NBFC Surge

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's securitisation market hit a record ₹2.55 lakh crore in fiscal 2026, up 9% from the previous year
  • 02/NBFCs drove the surge with 30% YoY growth, filling the gap as banks' share plummeted from 26% to just 3%
  • 03/Gold loan-backed securitisation overtook mortgages to become the second-largest asset class at 15% market share

India's securitisation market reached an all-time high of ₹2.55 lakh crore in fiscal 2026. The milestone marks a 9% annual growth driven primarily by non-banking financial companies.

NBFC originations surged 30% year-on-year, effectively offsetting a sharp decline in bank activity. Banks saw their share of total originations collapse to just 3%, down from 26% in fiscal 2025.

The January-March quarter alone witnessed transactions exceeding ₹65,000 crore. This represents a 20% growth compared to the same quarter in the previous fiscal year.

The market structure has shifted significantly. Vehicle loans maintained the largest share at 40%, though down from 47% previously. Gold loan-backed securitisation emerged as the second-largest asset class.

Gold loans now account for 15% of total transactions, overtaking mortgages which fell to 14%. The mortgage decline was attributed to subdued participation by a major private bank.

Pass-Through Certificates (PTCs) now dominate the market at approximately 60% of total volume. Investors increasingly prefer PTCs for their structural protections, especially in unsecured segments.

Market participation is broadening. The number of originators expanded to over 190 in fiscal 2026. The top 20 originators now account for 65% of the market, down from 71% previously.

"Increased NBFC activity reinforces the attractiveness of securitisation as a strong alternative fund-raising tool. Robust performance of cherry-picked pools and structural credit enhancements continue to support investor confidence," said Aparna Kirubakaran, Director at Crisil Ratings.

On the investor side, public and private sector banks remain primary backers. However, mutual funds and foreign banks are showing growing interest in the market.

Insurance companies and pension funds have also begun investing in select transactions. This diversified investor base is expected to support continued momentum into fiscal 2027.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS SECURITISATION AND WHY DO NBFCS USE IT?
SECURITISATION IS THE PROCESS OF POOLING ILLIQUID ASSETS LIKE LOANS AND CONVERTING THEM INTO MARKETABLE SECURITIES. NBFCS USE IT TO RAISE FUNDS, MANAGE BALANCE SHEETS, AND DIVERSIFY FUNDING SOURCES BEYOND TRADITIONAL BANK BORROWING. IT PROVIDES CAPITAL RELIEF AND HELPS MEET PRIORITY SECTOR LENDING REQUIREMENTS.
WHAT WERE THE KEY NUMBERS FOR INDIA'S SECURITISATION MARKET IN FY26?
THE MARKET REACHED A RECORD ₹2.55 LAKH CRORE IN FY26, UP 9% YOY. Q4 ALONE SAW ₹65,000 CRORE IN TRANSACTIONS, UP 20% FROM THE PREVIOUS YEAR. NBFC ORIGINATIONS SURGED 30% YOY WHILE BANKS' SHARE PLUMMETED TO JUST 3%.
WHICH ASSET CLASSES DOMINATED THE SECURITISATION MARKET?
VEHICLE LOANS LED WITH 40% MARKET SHARE, FOLLOWED BY GOLD LOANS AT 15% AND MORTGAGES AT 14%. BUSINESS AND PERSONAL LOANS ACCOUNTED FOR 17% COMBINED, WHILE MICROFINANCE STOOD AT 12%.
WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN PTC AND DIRECT ASSIGNMENT?
PASS-THROUGH CERTIFICATES (PTCS) ARE SECURITIES ISSUED BY A SPECIAL PURPOSE VEHICLE THAT PASS CASH FLOWS FROM UNDERLYING ASSETS TO INVESTORS. DIRECT ASSIGNMENTS (DAS) INVOLVE THE DIRECT SALE OF LOAN ASSETS TO ANOTHER ENTITY. PTCS NOW ACCOUNT FOR ~60% OF THE MARKET.
WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK FOR THE SECURITISATION MARKET?
CRISIL RATINGS EXPECTS CONTINUED MOMENTUM INTO FISCAL 2027. NBFCS ARE LIKELY TO REMAIN THE PRIMARY DRIVERS OF ISSUANCE. GROWING INTEREST FROM MUTUAL FUNDS, FOREIGN BANKS, INSURANCE COMPANIES, AND PENSION FUNDS WILL SUPPORT MARKET EXPANSION.
