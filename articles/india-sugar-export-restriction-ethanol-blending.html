India May Restrict Sugar Exports To Boost Ethanol Blending With Petrol

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India is considering restricting sugar exports to divert sugarcane towards ethanol production for higher petrol blending
  • 02/Export curbs were imposed in 2022-23 due to shortage; continued in 2023-24 despite bumper output of over 34 million tonnes
  • 03/Policy decision expected before new crushing season begins; ethanol blending target raised to 20% by 2025-26

India is likely to impose fresh restrictions on sugar exports. The move aims to divert sugarcane towards ethanol production for blending with petrol.

The government plans to increase ethanol blending in petrol. Current levels stand at around 15%, with a target of 20% by 2025-26.

India faced severe sugar shortages during the 2022-23 season. Domestic production dropped due to erratic monsoon rains in key cane-growing states.

Export restrictions were imposed in 2022-23 to ensure domestic supply. Despite a bumper output of over 34 million tonnes in 2023-24, no export shipments were permitted.

The 2023-24 sugar output exceeded consumption needs significantly. Yet the government maintained the export ban to rebuild buffer stocks and stabilize prices.

Higher ethanol blending reduces India's crude oil import bill. It also provides better returns to sugarcane farmers through diversified revenue streams.

The official policy announcement is expected before the new crushing season. Sugar mills await clarity on export quotas and ethanol procurement prices.

Global sugar prices have remained volatile amid supply concerns. India is the world's second-largest sugar producer after Brazil.

WHY IS INDIA CONSIDERING RESTRICTING SUGAR EXPORTS?
INDIA IS CONSIDERING RESTRICTING SUGAR EXPORTS TO DIVERT SUGARCANE TOWARDS ETHANOL PRODUCTION. THIS WILL HELP ACHIEVE HIGHER ETHANOL BLENDING LEVELS WITH PETROL AND REDUCE CRUDE OIL IMPORTS.
WHAT HAPPENED TO SUGAR EXPORTS IN 2022-23 AND 2023-24?
SUGAR EXPORTS WERE RESTRICTED IN 2022-23 DUE TO DOMESTIC SHORTAGES CAUSED BY ERRATIC MONSOONS. DESPITE A BUMPER OUTPUT OF OVER 34 MILLION TONNES IN 2023-24, NO EXPORT SHIPMENTS WERE PERMITTED TO REBUILD BUFFER STOCKS.
WHAT IS INDIA'S ETHANOL BLENDING TARGET?
INDIA HAS SET A TARGET TO ACHIEVE 20% ETHANOL BLENDING WITH PETROL BY 2025-26. CURRENT BLENDING LEVELS STAND AT APPROXIMATELY 15%.
HOW DOES DIVERTING CANE TO ETHANOL BENEFIT FARMERS?
DIVERTING SUGARCANE TO ETHANOL PRODUCTION PROVIDES BETTER RETURNS TO FARMERS THROUGH DIVERSIFIED REVENUE STREAMS. IT CREATES STABLE DEMAND AND REDUCES DEPENDENCE ON VOLATILE GLOBAL SUGAR PRICES.
WHEN WILL THE OFFICIAL POLICY BE ANNOUNCED?
THE OFFICIAL POLICY ANNOUNCEMENT IS EXPECTED BEFORE THE NEW SUGAR CRUSHING SEASON BEGINS. SUGAR MILLS ARE AWAITING CLARITY ON EXPORT QUOTAS AND ETHANOL PROCUREMENT PRICES.
