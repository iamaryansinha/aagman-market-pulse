India Reinstates Export Duties On Diesel, ATF; Refiners Face Margin Squeeze

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government reinstates export duties on diesel (₹21.50/litre) and ATF (₹29.50/litre) effective March 26, 2026
  • 02/Petrol exports remain exempt; domestic excise cut by ₹10/litre to offset consumer impact
  • 03/SEZ exemption clarity crucial for Reliance Industries which operates 35.2 MTPA export-focused refinery

India has reinstated export duties on refined petroleum products after a nearly two-year hiatus. The new levies took effect on March 26, 2026.

Diesel exports now attract ₹21.50 per litre. Aviation turbine fuel (ATF) exports face ₹29.50 per litre. Petrol exports remain exempt from any duty.

The decision follows a sharp surge in global crude prices. Brent crude jumped from $70 to nearly $122 per barrel in the past month.

Petroleum Minister Hardeep Singh Puri announced the measures on March 27. The government aims to prioritise domestic fuel availability.

India has also reduced domestic excise duties by ₹10 per litre on petrol and diesel. This partially offsets under-recoveries for state-owned oil marketing companies.

The Special Additional Excise Duty (SAED) on diesel has been eliminated entirely. Petrol SAED was reduced from ₹13 to ₹3 per litre.

Export duties translate to approximately $36 per barrel for diesel and $50 per barrel for ATF. Despite these levies, current crack spreads of $65-70 per barrel keep exports economically viable.

A critical uncertainty remains around Special Economic Zone (SEZ) refineries. Reliance Industries operates a 35.2 MTPA SEZ refinery at Jamnagar accounting for 75% of its diesel production.

State-owned refiners IOC, BPCL, and HPCL face full exposure. They operate exclusively domestic refineries with no SEZ facilities.

Analysts estimate the net fiscal impact at ₹30,000-100,000 crore annually. The government gains from export duties while losing excise revenue from domestic sales.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT EXPORT DUTIES HAS INDIA IMPOSED ON REFINED PRODUCTS?
INDIA HAS IMPOSED EXPORT DUTIES OF ₹21.50 PER LITRE ON DIESEL AND ₹29.50 PER LITRE ON AVIATION TURBINE FUEL (ATF) EFFECTIVE MARCH 26, 2026. PETROL EXPORTS REMAIN EXEMPT FROM ANY DUTY.
WHY DID THE GOVERNMENT REINSTATE THESE EXPORT DUTIES?
THE MOVE ADDRESSES SHARPLY RISING GLOBAL CRUDE OIL PRICES THAT SURGED FROM $70 TO NEARLY $122 PER BARREL. THE GOVERNMENT AIMS TO ENSURE ADEQUATE DOMESTIC SUPPLY AND PROTECT INDIAN CONSUMERS FROM GLOBAL PRICE VOLATILITY.
HOW DO THESE DUTIES IMPACT INDIAN REFINERS' MARGINS?
EXPORT DUTIES TRANSLATE TO APPROXIMATELY $36 PER BARREL FOR DIESEL AND $50 PER BARREL FOR ATF. HOWEVER, WITH CURRENT GLOBAL CRACK SPREADS AT $65-70 PER BARREL, REFINERS STILL EARN NET MARGINS OF $15-25 PER BARREL ON EXPORTS.
WHAT IS THE SEZ EXEMPTION UNCERTAINTY?
IT REMAINS UNCLEAR WHETHER SPECIAL ECONOMIC ZONE (SEZ) REFINERIES WILL BE EXEMPT FROM EXPORT DUTIES. RELIANCE INDUSTRIES' 35.2 MTPA JAMNAGAR SEZ REFINERY PRODUCES 75% OF ITS DIESEL, MAKING THIS CLARITY CRITICAL FOR ITS COMPETITIVE POSITION.
WHAT DOMESTIC MEASURES ACCOMPANY THE EXPORT DUTIES?
THE GOVERNMENT REDUCED DOMESTIC EXCISE DUTIES BY ₹10 PER LITRE ON PETROL AND DIESEL. THE SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY (SAED) ON DIESEL WAS ELIMINATED, WHILE PETROL SAED WAS REDUCED FROM ₹13 TO ₹3 PER LITRE TO PROVIDE RELIEF TO OIL MARKETING COMPANIES.
