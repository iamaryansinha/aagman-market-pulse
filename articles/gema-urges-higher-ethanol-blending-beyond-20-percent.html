GEMA Urges Govt to Raise Ethanol Blending Beyond 20% to Support Biofuel Growth

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/GEMA urges government to raise ethanol blending from 20% to 21-22% within existing vehicle tolerance limits
  • 02/Industry body calls for roadmap to reach 25-30% blending and fast-track flex-fuel vehicle adoption nationwide
  • 03/Proposal aims to reduce oil imports, cut carbon emissions, and boost demand for sugarcane, maize, and surplus rice

The Grain Ethanol Manufacturer's Association (GEMA) has urged the government to increase ethanol blending beyond the current 20% target. The apex industry body wants an immediate hike to 21-22% based on existing vehicle tolerance levels.

India achieved its 20% ethanol blending target for 2025 under the Ethanol Blended Petrol (EBP) program. GEMA now seeks a clear roadmap for higher blends to align with global best practices.

Dr. CK Jain, President of GEMA, stated that enhancement to 25-30% may not be immediately feasible. However, increasing blending by 1-2% above 20% is possible within current vehicle tolerance limits.

"There is an immediate need to enhance the blending up to the tolerance level of the existing vehicles to give some relief to the Grain Ethanol Industry," said Dr. Jain.

GEMA is pushing India to follow Brazil's successful model. Brazil uses E27 as base petrol and achieves up to 55% petrol replacement through flex-fuel vehicles (FFVs).

Higher ethanol blends can significantly reduce carbon emissions compared to conventional petrol. This supports India's 2030 climate targets and net-zero emissions goal by 2070.

Increased blending would lower India's oil import bill and enhance energy security. It would also boost rural economic growth by creating demand for agricultural feedstocks like sugarcane, maize, and surplus rice.

The industry has already invested to cater to enhanced requirements. GEMA says it is ready to collaborate on upgrading distribution infrastructure.

"Timely government policies and cohesive inter-ministerial coordination will be essential to maintain the momentum," Dr. Jain added.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS GEMA REQUESTING FROM THE GOVERNMENT?
GEMA (GRAIN ETHANOL MANUFACTURER'S ASSOCIATION) IS URGING THE GOVERNMENT TO IMMEDIATELY INCREASE ETHANOL BLENDING FROM THE CURRENT 20% TO 21-22% WITHIN EXISTING VEHICLE TOLERANCE LIMITS. THEY ALSO WANT A CLEAR ROADMAP FOR HIGHER BLENDS AND FAST-TRACKED ADOPTION OF FLEX-FUEL VEHICLES (FFVS).
WHY CAN'T INDIA JUMP DIRECTLY TO 25-30% ETHANOL BLENDING?
ENHANCEMENT TO 25-30% BLENDING IS NOT IMMEDIATELY FEASIBLE AS IT REQUIRES FLEX-FUEL ENGINES AND INFRASTRUCTURE UPGRADES. HOWEVER, GEMA SAYS INCREASING BY 1-2% ABOVE THE CURRENT 20% IS POSSIBLE WITHIN THE TOLERANCE LIMITS OF EXISTING VEHICLES.
WHAT IS THE BRAZIL MODEL THAT GEMA WANTS INDIA TO FOLLOW?
BRAZIL USES E27 (27% ETHANOL, 73% PETROL) AS ITS BASE PETROL AND ACHIEVES UP TO 55% PETROL REPLACEMENT THROUGH FLEX-FUEL VEHICLES (FFVS). GEMA WANTS INDIA TO ADOPT A SIMILAR PROGRESSIVE BLENDING APPROACH.
WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS OF HIGHER ETHANOL BLENDING?
HIGHER ETHANOL BLENDS SIGNIFICANTLY CUT CARBON EMISSIONS, HELPING INDIA MEET ITS 2030 CLIMATE TARGETS AND NET-ZERO GOAL BY 2070. IT ALSO LOWERS OIL IMPORT BILLS, ENHANCES ENERGY SECURITY, AND BOOSTS RURAL ECONOMIES BY CREATING DEMAND FOR SUGARCANE, MAIZE, AND SURPLUS RICE.
WHAT CHALLENGES NEED TO BE ADDRESSED FOR FLEX-FUEL ADOPTION?
FLEX-FUEL READINESS REQUIRES DEVELOPING APPROPRIATE VEHICLE STANDARDS, UPGRADING FUELING INFRASTRUCTURE, AND CONDUCTING CONSUMER AWARENESS CAMPAIGNS. COLLABORATION BETWEEN POLICYMAKERS, THE DEPARTMENT OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY, AND AUTOMOBILE MANUFACTURERS IS ESSENTIAL.
