Indian Exports to West Asia Near-Total Halt as Crisis Hits Four-Week Mark

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian exports to West Asia have virtually stopped since March 1 as Strait of Hormuz remains blocked; 16% of engineering exports affected
  • 02/India's total goods exports to West Asia stood at $65.54 billion in FY25, accounting for 15% of country's total exports
  • 03/Freight costs via Cape of Good Hope up $2,000 per container; LPG/PNG shortages hit manufacturing; payments stuck due to liquidity crunch

Indian exports to West Asia have come to a near-standstill four weeks into the widening conflict. The Strait of Hormuz blockage has severed the primary gateway to the region.

"As much as 16 per cent of our exports go to West Asia but since March 1 exports to the region have virtually come to a standstill," said Pankaj Chadha, Chairman, Engineering Export Promotion Council.

No shipments are being dispatched. Air cargo movement is negligible. The disruption has created a compounding crisis for Indian exporters across multiple sectors.

India's total goods exports to West Asia reached $65.54 billion in FY25. This accounts for approximately 15 per cent of the country's total export value.

Shipments rerouted via the Cape of Good Hope face cost increases up to $2,000 per container. Engineering units are suffering from LPG and PNG shortages required to run furnaces.

"Our payments are stuck as money is not coming from West Asia," said Sanjay Jain, a major garments and textiles exporter. Importers claim their economies are badly hurt and buying has collapsed.

The leather and footwear industry faces 20-30 per cent price increases on petroleum-based inputs. Rubber, PUs, and synthetic linings are driving production costs up by at least 10 per cent.

"The continuing turbulence in West Asia is deeply concerning for the MSME-dominated textiles sector," said CITI Secretary General Chandrima Chatterjee.

Rupee depreciation of 3-4 per cent against the US dollar has failed to offset cost increases. Manufacturing and freight costs continue to rise faster than currency benefits.

The government has announced relief measures but exporters await normalcy in the region as FY26 draws to a close.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MUCH OF INDIA'S EXPORTS GO TO WEST ASIA?
APPROXIMATELY 15-16 PER CENT OF INDIA'S TOTAL EXPORTS GO TO WEST ASIA. IN FY25, TOTAL GOODS EXPORTS TO THE REGION STOOD AT $65.54 BILLION.
WHY HAVE EXPORTS TO WEST ASIA HALTED?
THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ, THE PRIMARY GATEWAY TO WEST ASIA, HAS BEEN EFFECTIVELY BLOCKED DUE TO THE ONGOING CONFLICT. THIS HAS PREVENTED BOTH SEA AND AIR CARGO MOVEMENT SINCE MARCH 1, 2026.
WHAT ARE THE ALTERNATIVE ROUTES AND THEIR COSTS?
SHIPMENTS ARE BEING REROUTED VIA THE CAPE OF GOOD HOPE IN SOUTHERN AFRICA. THIS ADDS APPROXIMATELY 3,500 NAUTICAL MILES AND $2,000 PER CONTAINER IN ADDITIONAL COSTS.
WHICH SECTORS ARE MOST AFFECTED BY THE CRISIS?
ENGINEERING, GARMENTS AND TEXTILES, LEATHER AND FOOTWEAR ARE AMONG THE MOST AFFECTED. THESE SECTORS FACE INPUT SHORTAGES, PAYMENT DELAYS, AND SURGING RAW MATERIAL COSTS OF 20-30 PER CENT.
WHAT RELIEF MEASURES HAS THE GOVERNMENT ANNOUNCED?
THE GOVERNMENT HAS RESTORED FULL BENEFITS FOR EXPORTERS INCLUDING DUTY EXEMPTIONS AND REMISSION SCHEMES. THE CENTRE ALSO CUT SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY ON PETROL AND DIESEL BY ₹10 PER LITRE TO EASE INPUT COSTS.
