Gold Prices Fall as Strong US Jobs Data Dim Fed Rate-Cut Hopes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold falls sharply after hitting record highs above $3,100 last week as strong US jobs data dampens Fed rate-cut expectations
  • 02/US non-farm payrolls added significantly more jobs than expected, signaling economic resilience and hawkish monetary policy outlook
  • 03/Watch Fed Chair Powell's next speech and Iran-Israel conflict developments for directional moves in precious metals

Gold prices declined sharply on Monday, dropping below $3,020 per ounce. The yellow metal is retreating from recent all-time highs above $3,100.

The selloff follows unexpectedly strong US non-farm payrolls data released Friday. The economy added far more jobs than economists had predicted.

Robust employment figures suggest the US economy remains resilient. This reduces the likelihood of aggressive Federal Reserve rate cuts this year.

Higher interest rates diminish gold's appeal as a non-yielding asset. The opportunity cost of holding bullion rises when bond yields climb.

Geopolitical risk premium from Iran-Israel tensions has also faded slightly. Safe-haven demand that pushed gold higher is now cooling.

Technical support for gold is seen near $2,980 per ounce. Resistance lies at the previous high around $3,050-$3,100 range.

Analysts expect continued volatility as markets price in evolving Fed policy expectations. Gold remains up over 15% year-to-date despite the pullback.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY ARE GOLD PRICES FALLING TODAY?
GOLD PRICES ARE DECLINING DUE TO STRONGER-THAN-EXPECTED US NON-FARM PAYROLLS DATA. THE ROBUST JOBS REPORT REDUCES EXPECTATIONS FOR FEDERAL RESERVE INTEREST RATE CUTS, WHICH DIMINISHES GOLD'S APPEAL AS A NON-YIELDING ASSET.
WHAT WAS THE RECENT HIGH FOR GOLD PRICES?
GOLD HIT RECORD HIGHS ABOVE $3,100 PER OUNCE LAST WEEK. THE METAL HAS RALLIED OVER 15% YEAR-TO-DATE DRIVEN BY GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS AND EARLIER EXPECTATIONS OF FED RATE CUTS.
HOW DOES US JOBS DATA AFFECT GOLD PRICES?
STRONG JOBS DATA SIGNALS ECONOMIC RESILIENCE, WHICH REDUCES THE NEED FOR THE FED TO CUT INTEREST RATES. HIGHER RATES INCREASE THE OPPORTUNITY COST OF HOLDING GOLD, TYPICALLY LEADING TO LOWER PRICES.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE FOR GOLD?
IMMEDIATE SUPPORT FOR GOLD IS SEEN NEAR $2,980 PER OUNCE. RESISTANCE LIES AT THE PREVIOUS HIGH AROUND $3,050-$3,100 RANGE. A BREAK BELOW SUPPORT COULD TRIGGER FURTHER SELLING.
WHAT FACTORS SHOULD INVESTORS WATCH NEXT FOR GOLD?
INVESTORS SHOULD MONITOR FEDERAL RESERVE CHAIR POWELL'S NEXT SPEECH FOR POLICY CLUES. DEVELOPMENTS IN THE IRAN-ISRAEL CONFLICT AND ANY CHANGES TO US INFLATION DATA WILL ALSO DRIVE GOLD PRICE MOVEMENTS.
