India Signs Record 219 Advance Pricing Agreements in FY26, Cumulative APAs Cross 1,000

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CBDT signs record 219 APAs in FY26, crossing 1,000 cumulative agreements milestone for the first time
  • 02/84 bilateral APAs signed with 13 treaty partners including new agreements with France, Ireland, Indonesia, Sweden
  • 03/Safe Harbour threshold raised to ₹2,000 crore from ₹300 crore; IT services margin fixed at 15.5%

The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) has signed a record 219 Advance Pricing Agreements in FY 2025-26. This marks the highest ever single-year tally, up from 174 in FY25.

The milestone takes India's cumulative APA signings to 1,034. The programme has scaled from just 5 agreements in FY14 to over 1,000 in FY26.

A key highlight is the surge in bilateral APAs. CBDT signed 84 BAPAs in FY26, expanding the bilateral network to 13 treaty partners.

New bilateral agreements were signed with France, Ireland, Indonesia, and Sweden for the first time. India is now the largest treaty partner for the United States in bilateral APAs.

Bilateral APAs eliminate double taxation risks for multinationals. They ensure income is not taxed twice across jurisdictions.

The Finance Act 2026 has revamped the Safe Harbour regime alongside the APA push. The eligibility threshold has been increased from ₹300 crore to ₹2,000 crore.

IT services now have a uniform margin of 15.5% under Safe Harbour. This consolidates multiple technology service categories into one segment.

Union Budget 2026 proposed fast-tracking unilateral APAs. Target timeline is now 2 years, down from over 3 years earlier.

Companies entering APAs get tax certainty for up to 5 years. Rollback provisions can extend this visibility to 9 years.

The reforms target IT, pharmaceuticals, manufacturing, and financial services sectors. These sectors account for the bulk of India's transfer pricing disputes.

HOW MANY ADVANCE PRICING AGREEMENTS DID INDIA SIGN IN FY26?
THE CENTRAL BOARD OF DIRECT TAXES (CBDT) SIGNED A RECORD 219 ADVANCE PRICING AGREEMENTS IN FY 2025-26. THIS IS THE HIGHEST EVER IN A SINGLE FINANCIAL YEAR, UP FROM 174 IN FY25 AND 125 IN FY24.
WHAT IS THE TOTAL NUMBER OF CUMULATIVE APAS SIGNED BY INDIA?
INDIA'S CUMULATIVE APA SIGNINGS NOW STAND AT 1,034 AGREEMENTS. THE PROGRAMME HAS GROWN FROM JUST 5 APAS IN FY14 TO CROSSING THE 1,000-MARK IN FY26.
HOW MANY BILATERAL APAS WERE SIGNED AND WHY DO THEY MATTER?
CBDT SIGNED 84 BILATERAL APAS IN FY26, TAKING THE BILATERAL NETWORK TO 13 TREATY PARTNERS INCLUDING NEW AGREEMENTS WITH FRANCE, IRELAND, INDONESIA, AND SWEDEN. BILATERAL APAS ELIMINATE DOUBLE TAXATION RISKS BY ENSURING INCOME IS NOT TAXED TWICE IN DIFFERENT JURISDICTIONS.
WHAT ARE THE KEY SAFE HARBOUR CHANGES IN FINANCE ACT 2026?
THE SAFE HARBOUR ELIGIBILITY THRESHOLD HAS BEEN RAISED FROM ₹300 CRORE TO ₹2,000 CRORE. IT SERVICES NOW HAVE A UNIFORM MARGIN OF 15.5%, CONSOLIDATING MULTIPLE TECHNOLOGY CATEGORIES INTO ONE SEGMENT.
WHAT TAX CERTAINTY DO COMPANIES GET FROM APAS?
COMPANIES ENTERING APAs SECURE TAX CERTAINTY FOR UP TO 5 YEARS WITH ROLLBACK PROVISIONS COVERING EARLIER YEARS. THIS EFFECTIVELY PROVIDES UP TO 9 YEARS OF VISIBILITY ON TAX LIABILITIES, REDUCING THE RISK OF RETROSPECTIVE TAX DEMANDS.
