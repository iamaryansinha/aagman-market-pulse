Ola Electric Reclaims Fifth Spot in March with 139% Sales Surge

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Ola Electric sold 9,496 units in March, jumping from 3,973 units in February — a 139% month-on-month recovery
  • 02/The company reclaimed fifth position in India's electric two-wheeler market after slipping in previous months
  • 03/Aggressive discounting strategy with entry prices at ₹49,999 and benefits up to ₹50,000 drove the rebound

Ola Electric Mobility Ltd (NSE: OLA) staged a sharp recovery in March 2026. The company sold 9,496 electric two-wheeler units, reclaiming fifth position in the market.

Sales jumped 139% from February's 3,973 units. The recovery comes after a prolonged slump that saw the company lose market share to rivals.

Bhavish Aggarwal, Chairman and MD, announced the turnaround on X. He reported 1,000+ daily orders and 10,000+ registrations in March. "150%+ MoM growth. Market share up. Playing to our strengths and winning back customer trust," he posted.

The rebound was driven by aggressive discounting. Ola launched its #EndICEAge campaign, cutting entry prices to ₹49,999. The offer includes a Service Trust Guarantee, Buyback Guarantee, and benefits up to ₹50,000.

The company also ran its Navratri Mahotsav campaign. This limited-period offer provided benefits exceeding ₹30,000 across S1 scooters and Roadster motorcycles.

Despite the recovery, Ola remains behind market leaders. TVS retained the top spot with 46,859 units (47% MoM growth). Bajaj followed with approximately 43,000 units (68% MoM growth).

Ather Energy held third position with 33,600 units (19% growth). Hero MotoCorp ranked fourth with 19,764 units (59% jump from February).

Aggarwal remains confident about reclaiming leadership. "We'll reclaim leadership soon — thanks to our customers," he stated.
