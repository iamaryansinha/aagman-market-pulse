Government Plans Scale-Up of Induction Heater, Cooktop Production Amid LPG Concerns

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government planning measures to boost domestic production of induction heaters and cooktops
  • 02/Demand surge driven by consumer concerns over LPG cylinder availability and rising fuel costs
  • 03/Policy intervention aims to provide alternative cooking solutions to households

The government is considering steps to scale up production of induction heaters and cooktops. The move comes as these products witness high demand due to concerns over LPG availability.

Rising global tensions have put pressure on oil and gas supplies. This has led to worries about LPG cylinder availability and pricing for domestic consumers.

Induction cooktops offer an electric alternative to gas-based cooking. They are energy-efficient and increasingly affordable for Indian households.

The proposed production scale-up would involve coordination with appliance manufacturers. It may include incentives for domestic manufacturing under the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme.

India has over 300 million LPG connections under the Ujjwala scheme. Any supply disruption would impact a large portion of the population.

The induction appliance market has grown 25% year-over-year. Major players include Philips, Prestige, Bajaj, and Havells.

The policy push could accelerate the shift toward electric cooking. It aligns with India's renewable energy goals and reduces import dependence for cooking fuel.
