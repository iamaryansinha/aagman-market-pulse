Cotton Prices Rise as CCI Sells 39 Lakh Bales, Rupee Weakens

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CCI raised cotton prices by Rs 300 per candy (356 kg), marking a Rs 1,900 increase since early March
  • 02/The state-run agency sold 39 lakh bales (170 kg each) in March from total procurement of 1.05 crore bales
  • 03/Weakening rupee and 14% surge in ICE cotton futures making imports expensive, boosting domestic demand

Cotton prices in India have gained further on strong demand from spinning mills and traders. The Cotton Corporation of India (CCI) raised prices by Rs 300 per candy on Friday.

The price revision follows a global uptrend and weakening rupee. Domestic prices have climbed Rs 1,900 per candy since the beginning of March.

CCI is currently the largest cotton stock holder in India. The agency procured 1.05 crore bales this season.

"We have sold 39 lakh bales in March alone," said Lalit Gupta, Chairman and Managing Director of CCI. "There is good demand for cotton and yarn."

ICE cotton futures have surged over 14% since early March. May 2026 delivery trades above 69 cents per pound.

The depreciating rupee is making imports expensive. Global prices have risen to levels where Indian cotton looks attractive.

"Demand is rising as the strong dollar makes imports costly," said Ramanju Das Boob, a sourcing agent in Raichur.

Yarn exports to China, Bangladesh and Vietnam have also increased. Global supply chain disruptions are driving buyers toward Indian cotton.

HOW MUCH COTTON HAS CCI SOLD IN MARCH?
CCI HAS SOLD 39 LAKH BALES OF 170 KG EACH IN MARCH 2026. THIS IS OUT OF THE TOTAL PROCUREMENT OF 1.05 CRORE BALES FOR THE 2025-26 SEASON.
WHY ARE DOMESTIC COTTON PRICES RISING?
PRICES ARE RISING DUE TO STRONG DEMAND FROM SPINNING MILLS, A WEAKENING RUPEE MAKING IMPORTS EXPENSIVE, AND A 14% SURGE IN GLOBAL ICE COTTON FUTURES SINCE EARLY MARCH.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT GLOBAL COTTON PRICE TREND?
ICE COTTON FUTURES HAVE GAINED OVER 14% SINCE EARLY MARCH. MAY 2026 DELIVERY IS HOVERING ABOVE 69 CENTS PER POUND, WHILE JULY DELIVERY IS ABOVE 71 CENTS PER POUND.
WHO ARE THE MAJOR BUYERS OF INDIAN COTTON YARN?
DEMAND FOR INDIAN COTTON YARN HAS INCREASED FROM CHINA, BANGLADESH, AND VIETNAM. MULTINATIONAL TRADING FIRMS ARE ALSO FINDING INDIAN PRICES ATTRACTIVE DUE TO THE WEAKENING RUPEE.
