Rupee Plunges 49 Paise To 93.32 Against US Dollar On Geopolitical Tensions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rupee depreciated 49 paise to 93.32 against the US dollar in early trade on Monday, April 13, 2026
  • 02/Brent crude surged 7.28% to $102.13 per barrel after US announced blockade of Iranian ports
  • 03/Dollar index rose 0.38% to 98.81; investors pulled funds from domestic equities amid West Asia tensions

The Indian rupee depreciated 49 paise to 93.32 against the US dollar in early trade on Monday. The domestic currency opened at 93.30 at the interbank foreign exchange market.

The rupee's previous closing level was 92.83 on Friday. It had settled 32 paise lower that day.

The decline was driven by a surge in crude oil prices and a firm US dollar. Renewed tensions in West Asia weighed heavily on the currency.

Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, jumped 7.28% to $102.13 per barrel. The US announced it would enforce a maritime blockade of Iranian ports starting Monday.

The dollar index, which gauges the greenback against six currencies, rose 0.38% to 98.81. Safe-haven demand strengthened the American currency.

Foreign investors rushed to withdraw funds from domestic equities. This put additional pressure on the rupee, forex traders said.

The 30-share Sensex plummeted 1,600.73 points or 2.06% to 75,949.52. The Nifty tumbled 468.85 points or 1.95% to 23,581.75.

India's forex reserves stood at $697.121 billion as of April 3, 2026. The RBI reported a $9.063 billion jump from the previous week.
