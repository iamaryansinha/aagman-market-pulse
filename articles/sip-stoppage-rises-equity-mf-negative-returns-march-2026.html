SIP Stoppage Rises to 76% as 1-2 Year Equity MF Returns Turn Negative

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SIP stoppage ratio climbed to 76% in February 2026, up 1 percentage point month-on-month amid negative equity returns
  • 02/Small cap funds lost 15% in 1 year and 7% over 2 years; flexi-cap funds fell 14% and 5% respectively
  • 03/Experts recommend continuing SIPs as rupee-cost averaging works better during market corrections

India's equity mutual fund investors are hitting the pause button. SIP stoppage ratio rose to 76% in February, up 1 percentage point from the previous month.

Negative returns across equity categories are driving the exodus. Small cap funds have delivered -15% over 1 year and -7% over 2 years, per Geojit Research data.

Flexi-cap schemes declined 14% in one year and 5% over two years. Large cap and multi-cap funds posted losses of 14% and 13% respectively in the 1-year period.

"When SIP returns turn negative, there is a natural nervousness that comes in," said Shweta Rajani, Head of Mutual Funds at Anand Rathi. She noted that first-time investors are particularly prone to discontinuing.

Harsh Gahlaut, CEO at FinEdge, highlighted a double impact. Higher stoppages combined with a slowdown in new SIP registrations are affecting overall inflows.

However, seasoned advisors see this as an opportunity. "SIPs are designed for longer horizons," said Shashank Udupa, Co-founder of smallcase. Short-term underperformance is normal and expected.

Ponmudi R, CEO at Enrich Money, echoed this view. Weak returns enable more effective rupee-cost averaging, allowing investors to accumulate more units at lower prices.

Hariprasad K, Founder of Livelong Wealth, observed a divergence. Investors who entered during the post-2020 rally are staying strong. Those with 2-3 years in the market are more likely to pause.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE CURRENT SIP STOPPAGE RATIO AND WHY IS IT RISING?
THE SIP STOPPAGE RATIO INCREASED TO 76% IN FEBRUARY 2026, UP 1 PERCENTAGE POINT MONTH-ON-MONTH. THE RISE IS ATTRIBUTED TO NEGATIVE RETURNS IN EQUITY MUTUAL FUND SCHEMES OVER 1-YEAR AND 2-YEAR PERIODS.
HOW MUCH HAVE SMALL CAP AND LARGE CAP FUNDS DECLINED OVER 1 AND 2 YEARS?
SMALL CAP FUNDS HAVE DECLINED 15% OVER 1 YEAR AND 7% OVER 2 YEARS. LARGE CAP FUNDS ARE DOWN 14% IN 1 YEAR AND 5% OVER 2 YEARS. MULTI-CAP FUNDS FELL 13% AND 5% RESPECTIVELY.
WHAT DO EXPERTS SAY ABOUT STOPPING SIPs DURING MARKET DOWNTURNS?
EXPERTS ADVISE AGAINST STOPPING SIPs. SHWETA RAJANI OF ANAND RATHI ACKNOWLEDGES NATURAL NERVOUSNESS BUT EMPHASIZES LONG-TERM INVESTING. SHASHANK UDUPA OF SMALLCASE STATES SIPs ARE DESIGNED FOR LONGER HORIZONS AND SHORT-TERM UNDERPERFORMANCE IS NORMAL.
WHY IS CONTINUING SIPs DURING WEAK MARKETS CONSIDERED BENEFICIAL?
WEAK RETURNS ENABLE MORE EFFECTIVE RUPEE-COST AVERAGING, AS NOTED BY PONMUDI R OF ENRICH MONEY. INVESTORS ACCUMULATE MORE UNITS AT LOWER PRICES, WHICH CAN LEAD TO BETTER RETURNS WHEN MARKETS RECOVER.
WHICH CATEGORY OF INVESTORS IS MORE LIKELY TO STOP THEIR SIPs?
ACCORDING TO HARIPRASAD K OF LIVELONG WEALTH, INVESTORS WHO HAVE BEEN IN THE MARKET FOR 2-3 YEARS ARE MORE LIKELY TO PRESS PAUSE. INVESTORS WHO ENTERED DURING THE POST-2020 RALLY ARE STAYING STRONG AND CONTINUING THEIR INVESTMENTS.
