PM Modi to Inaugurate Kaynes Semicon ₹3,307 Crore OSAT Plant at Sanand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate Kaynes Semicon's OSAT facility at Sanand, Gujarat on March 31, 2026, with Union Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw and Gujarat CM in attendance
  • 02/The ₹3,307 crore project has a capacity of 6.3 million chips per day and becomes the second plant under India Semiconductor Mission 1.0 to begin commercial production after Micron
  • 03/Kaynes Technology (NSE: KAYNES) has committed ₹400 crore so far; government subsidies cover 70% of total investment with break-even expected in 2.5 years

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate Kaynes Semicon's outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) facility at Sanand, Gujarat on March 31, 2026. Union Minister for Electronics and IT Ashwini Vaishnaw and Gujarat Chief Minister are expected to attend the event.

The ₹3,307 crore facility will have a production capacity of 6.3 million chips per day. It marks the second semiconductor plant under India Semiconductor Mission (ISM) 1.0 to start commercial production, following Micron's ATMP unit which began operations in February 2026.

Kaynes Semicon is a 100% subsidiary of Kaynes Technology India Ltd (NSE: KAYNES). The Mysuru-headquartered electronics manufacturing services company received Union Cabinet approval for the project in September 2024.

The funding structure includes 50% (₹1,653.5 crore) from the Central government, 20% (₹661.4 crore) from Gujarat government, and 30% (₹992.1 crore) from Kaynes. The company has already invested ₹400 crore into the facility.

The Sanand OSAT plant has already completed validation and early shipments. It shipped 900 units of IPM5 (Intelligent Power Module) to anchor customer Alpha Omega Semiconductor (AOS) in October 2025, marking India's first full production run of a high-complexity multi-die semiconductor module.

The facility focuses on wire bond and substrate packaging for automotive, industrial, consumer electronics, and telecom sectors. About 70% of capacity is allocated to advanced packaging, with the remainder for traditional packaging.

Kaynes has partnered with Globetronics (Malaysia), AOI (Japan), and Mitsui & Co for technology and raw materials supply. The company targets break-even within 2.5 years of commercial operations.

India has approved 10 semiconductor projects worth ₹1.6 lakh crore under ISM 1.0 across six states. Four projects including Kaynes, Micron, CG Power, and Tata Electronics are expected to enter production in 2026.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE KAYNES SEMICON OSAT FACILITY THAT PM MODI IS INAUGURATING?
THE KAYNES SEMICON OSAT (OUTSOURCED SEMICONDUCTOR ASSEMBLY AND TEST) FACILITY IS A ₹3,307 CRORE SEMICONDUCTOR PACKAGING PLANT LOCATED AT SANAND, GUJARAT. IT HAS A CAPACITY OF 6.3 MILLION CHIPS PER DAY AND WILL MANUFACTURE SEMICONDUCTOR PACKAGES FOR AUTOMOTIVE, INDUSTRIAL, CONSUMER ELECTRONICS, AND TELECOM SECTORS.
WHEN AND WHERE IS THE INAUGURATION TAKING PLACE?
PRIME MINISTER NARENDRA MODI WILL INAUGURATE THE FACILITY ON MARCH 31, 2026, AT SANAND IN GUJARAT. UNION MINISTER FOR ELECTRONICS AND IT ASHWINI VAISHNAW AND GUJARAT CHIEF MINISTER ARE ALSO EXPECTED TO ATTEND THE EVENT.
HOW IS THE ₹3,307 CRORE PROJECT FUNDED?
THE PROJECT RECEIVES 50% (₹1,653.5 CRORE) FUNDING FROM THE CENTRAL GOVERNMENT, 20% (₹661.4 CRORE) FROM THE GUJARAT GOVERNMENT, AND 30% (₹992.1 CRORE) FROM KAYNES TECHNOLOGY. KAYNES HAS ALREADY COMMITTED ₹400 CRORE TO THE FACILITY SO FAR.
WHAT MAKES THIS FACILITY SIGNIFICANT FOR INDIA'S SEMICONDUCTOR ECOSYSTEM?
THIS IS THE SECOND SEMICONDUCTOR PLANT UNDER INDIA SEMICONDUCTOR MISSION (ISM) 1.0 TO BEGIN COMMERCIAL PRODUCTION, FOLLOWING MICRON'S ATMP UNIT. IT MARKS THE ENTRY OF AN INDIAN-ORIGIN EMS PLAYER INTO SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING AND HELPS BUILD DOMESTIC PACKAGING CAPACITY, A KEY GAP IN INDIA'S CHIP ECOSYSTEM.
WHO ARE THE KEY PARTNERS AND CUSTOMERS OF KAYNES SEMICON?
KAYNES HAS STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIPS WITH GLOBETRONICS (MALAYSIA), AOI (JAPAN), AND MITSUI & CO FOR TECHNOLOGY AND RAW MATERIALS. ITS ANCHOR CUSTOMER IS ALPHA OMEGA SEMICONDUCTOR (AOS), WHICH IS EXPECTED TO UTILISE ABOUT 60% OF THE FIRST-PHASE CAPACITY. THE COMPANY HAS ALSO SIGNED AN MOU WITH LIGHTSPEED PHOTONICS FOR ADVANCED PACKAGING SOLUTIONS.
