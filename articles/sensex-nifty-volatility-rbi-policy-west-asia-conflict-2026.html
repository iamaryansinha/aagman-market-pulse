Sensex, Nifty Brace For Volatility: RBI Policy, West Asia Conflict Key Triggers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex declined 263.67 points (0.35%) and Nifty fell 106.5 points (0.46%) in the holiday-shortened trading week
  • 02/RBI monetary policy meeting scheduled this week; rate decision on April 9, 2026 will set market direction
  • 03/Escalating West Asia conflict poses risk to oil prices, inflation, and foreign fund flows into India

Indian stock markets ended the truncated week in negative territory. The BSE Sensex closed 263.67 points lower at 74,222.19, down 0.35% week-on-week.

The NSE Nifty50 mirrored the weakness. It slipped 106.5 points or 0.46% to settle at 22,567.50.

Market participants are now bracing for heightened volatility. Two major events are converging this week.

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) will announce its monetary policy on April 9. Analysts are divided on whether the central bank will cut rates or hold steady.

Simultaneously, tensions in West Asia are escalating rapidly. Any military confrontation could disrupt oil supplies and spike crude prices.

Higher crude oil prices pose a direct threat to India's trade deficit. They also complicate the RBI's inflation management objectives.

Foreign portfolio investors (FPIs) have been cautious in recent sessions. Geopolitical uncertainty typically triggers capital flight from emerging markets like India.

Analysts at Motilal Oswal warn that volatility could spike 15-20%. They recommend hedging positions ahead of the RBI decision.

"Markets are pricing in a status quo from the RBI," said Siddhartha Khemka, Head of Retail Research at Motilal Oswal. "But the West Asia situation adds a layer of unpredictability."

The Nifty has support at 22,400 and resistance at 22,800. A decisive break below 22,400 could trigger further selling pressure.

THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA (RBI) WILL ANNOUNCE ITS MONETARY POLICY DECISION ON APRIL 9, 2026. THE MONETARY POLICY COMMITTEE (MPC) MEETING IS UNDERWAY THIS WEEK.
WHY IS THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT IMPORTANT FOR INDIAN MARKETS?
ESCALATING TENSIONS IN WEST ASIA COULD DISRUPT GLOBAL OIL SUPPLIES. INDIA IMPORTS OVER 80% OF ITS CRUDE OIL REQUIREMENTS. HIGHER OIL PRICES WOULD WIDEN THE TRADE DEFICIT, FUEL INFLATION, AND POTENTIALLY TRIGGER FPI OUTFLOWS.
WHAT ARE THE KEY TECHNICAL LEVELS TO WATCH FOR NIFTY?
THE NIFTY50 HAS IMMEDIATE SUPPORT AT 22,400 AND RESISTANCE AT 22,800. A SUSTAINED BREAK BELOW 22,400 COULD SEE SELLING PRESSURE EXTEND TOWARDS 22,200. ON THE UPSIDE, A BREAK ABOVE 22,800 COULD OPEN THE PATH TO 23,000.
WHAT STRATEGY SHOULD INVESTORS ADOPT IN THE CURRENT MARKET?
ANALYSTS RECOMMEND HEDGING EXISTING EQUITY POSITIONS GIVEN THE DUAL UNCERTAINTIES OF RBI POLICY AND GEOPOLITICAL RISK. TRADERS SHOULD AVOID HEAVY POSITIONING AHEAD OF THE RBI DECISION. LONG-TERM INVESTORS MAY USE VOLATILITY TO ACCUMULATE QUALITY STOCKS ON DIPS.
