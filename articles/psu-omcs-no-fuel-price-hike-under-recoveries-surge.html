PSU OMCs Won't Raise Petrol, Diesel Prices Despite ₹2,400 Crore Daily Losses

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/PSU OMCs absorbing under-recoveries of ₹26 per litre on petrol and ₹81.90 per litre on diesel; combined daily loss is ₹2,400 crore
  • 02/Government cuts excise duty by ₹10/litre on both fuels to offset losses; retail pump prices remain unchanged since April 2022
  • 03/Fiscal impact estimated at ₹1.55 lakh crore annually; export duties imposed on diesel and ATF to ensure domestic supply

Public sector oil marketing companies (OMCs) will not raise petrol and diesel prices despite mounting under-recoveries. The government confirmed on March 27, 2026 that retail pump rates will remain frozen.

Under-recoveries currently stand at ₹26 per litre on petrol and ₹81.90 per litre on diesel. The three state-run OMCs — Indian Oil Corporation (NSE: IOC), Bharat Petroleum Corporation (NSE: BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation (NSE: HPCL) — are absorbing combined daily losses of approximately ₹2,400 crore.

The government reduced special additional excise duty on petrol to ₹3 per litre from ₹13 per litre. Diesel duty has been cut to nil from ₹10 per litre, effective March 27, 2026.

Oil Minister Hardeep Singh Puri stated the government chose to absorb the fiscal impact rather than pass price hikes to citizens. Prime Minister Narendra Modi decided to take the hit on government finances to shield consumers from international volatility.

Retail petrol and diesel prices have remained unchanged since April 2022. While global fuel prices surged 30-50% in many regions, Indian consumers have been insulated from these increases.

The excise duty reduction will not lower pump prices. Instead, it directly reduces the under-recoveries absorbed by OMCs, offsetting ₹10 per litre of their losses.

Economist Madhavi Arora of Emkay Global estimates the annualised fiscal hit at ₹1.55 lakh crore. The duty cuts will absorb approximately 30-40% of annualised OMC losses on auto fuels at current prices.

The government also imposed export duties on diesel and aviation turbine fuel (ATF). These measures aim to disincentivise exports and ensure adequate domestic supply.

India has sufficient crude oil inventory with supplies lined up for the next two months. All refineries are operating at full or above capacity to meet demand.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT ARE THE CURRENT UNDER-RECOVERIES FOR OMCs ON PETROL AND DIESEL?
AT CURRENT INTERNATIONAL CRUDE PRICES, UNDER-RECOVERIES STAND AT APPROXIMATELY ₹26 PER LITRE ON PETROL AND ₹81.90 PER LITRE ON DIESEL. THE COMBINED DAILY UNDER-RECOVERY ABSORBED BY OMCs IS AROUND ₹2,400 CRORE.
WHAT EXCISE DUTY CUTS HAS THE GOVERNMENT ANNOUNCED?
THE GOVERNMENT CUT SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY ON PETROL TO ₹3 PER LITRE FROM ₹13 PER LITRE. DIESEL DUTY HAS BEEN REDUCED TO ZERO FROM ₹10 PER LITRE, EFFECTIVE MARCH 27, 2026.
WILL PETROL AND DIESEL PRICES AT PUMPS COME DOWN?
NO, RETAIL PUMP PRICES WILL REMAIN UNCHANGED. THE EXCISE REDUCTION IS NOT BEING PASSED ON AS A PRICE CUT TO CONSUMERS. IT DIRECTLY REDUCES THE UNDER-RECOVERIES BEING ABSORBED BY PSU OMCs.
WHAT IS THE FISCAL IMPACT OF THE EXCISE DUTY CUT ON THE GOVERNMENT?
THE ANNUALISED FISCAL HIT IS ESTIMATED AT ₹1.55 LAKH CRORE ACCORDING TO EMKAY GLOBAL. RATINGS AGENCY ICRA ESTIMATES A ₹10-PER-LITRE CUT LEADS TO REVENUE SHORTFALL OF ₹1.5-1.6 LAKH CRORE.
WHICH ARE THE THREE MAJOR PSU OMCs IN INDIA AND HOW ARE THEIR STOCKS PERFORMING?
THE THREE MAJOR PSU OMCs ARE INDIAN OIL CORPORATION (IOC), BHARAT PETROLEUM CORPORATION (BPCL), AND HINDUSTAN PETROLEUM CORPORATION (HPCL). THEIR STOCKS HAVE DECLINED 15-25% IN MARCH 2026 DUE TO MARGIN PRESSURES FROM RISING CRUDE PRICES AND FROZEN RETAIL FUEL PRICES.
