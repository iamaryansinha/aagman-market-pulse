China Resells Record LNG Volumes in March, Hits 1.31 Million Tons YTD

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/China reloaded 8 to 10 LNG cargoes in March 2026, marking the highest monthly total on record according to ICIS, Kpler and Vortexa data
  • 02/Year-to-date resales hit 1.31 million metric tons (19 cargoes), exceeding full-year 2025 total of 0.82 million tons and 2023's 0.98 million tons
  • 03/China's March LNG imports plunged to 3.68 million tons, lowest since April 2018, as weak domestic demand and high spot prices drive reload strategy

Chinese energy firms have resold record volumes of liquefied natural gas in March. They reloaded 8 to 10 cargoes, the highest monthly total on record, per ICIS, Kpler and Vortexa data.

So far in 2026, China has resold 1.31 million metric tons of LNG. This represents 19 cargoes delivered to South Korea, Thailand, Japan, India and the Philippines.

The resales exceed China's full-year 2025 total of 0.82 million tons. They also surpass the 0.98 million tons resold in 2023, which was the second-highest annual total.

China's own LNG demand has plateaued due to weaker industrial activity. Meanwhile, domestic gas production and pipelined Russian supply continue to grow.

The country has taken a different approach with refined fuels. Last month, China banned exports of refined fuels to preserve domestic supply amid war-driven crude constraints.

Asian LNG spot prices have jumped 85% since February 28. The surge followed U.S. and Israel strikes on Iran that disrupted shipments through the Strait of Hormuz.

"Against a backdrop of weak domestic demand, it made more sense for buyers to resell LNG cargoes overseas," said Wang Yuanda, analyst at ICIS. "Spot prices are good so China can reload cargoes."

China's March LNG imports fell to 3.68 million tons. This marks the lowest monthly import level since April 2018.

CNOOC's Binhai terminal in Jiangsu province handled almost half of China's reloads in March, according to Vortexa analysts.

ICIS expects April imports to remain low at 3.7 million tons. "China will not enter the market and fight for cargoes with other countries at all," Wang added.
