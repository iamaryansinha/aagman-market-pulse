RBI MPC April 2026: Repo Rate Likely Unchanged on Inflation Risks

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/RBI Monetary Policy Committee expected to keep repo rate unchanged at 6.5% in April 2026 meeting
  • 02/Economists cite persistent inflation risks as key reason for maintaining neutral policy stance
  • 03/Central bank closely monitoring fuel cost pass-through to inflation before any future action

The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee is expected to maintain the repo rate at 6.5%. The three-day meeting begins April 2, 2026, with the decision due April 4.

Economists widely predict a status quo on rates. Inflation risks remain elevated despite recent moderation in headline numbers.

The central bank is expected to maintain a neutral stance. This signals no immediate bias toward rate cuts or hikes.

Fuel costs have shown limited pass-through to broader inflation so far. However, the RBI remains cautious about potential second-round effects.

The MPC is monitoring how the situation evolves before taking further action. Global crude oil prices and domestic demand conditions are key variables.

India's retail inflation had eased to near the RBI's 4% target in recent months. But food price volatility and global uncertainty keep the central bank vigilant.

A pause in rate cuts would align with expectations from major brokerages. Most analysts had priced in no change until the second half of 2026.
