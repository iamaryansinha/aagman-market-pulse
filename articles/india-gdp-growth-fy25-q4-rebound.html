India GDP Growth Slows to 6.5% in FY25, Q4 Rebounds to 7.4%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's GDP growth slowed to 6.5% in FY25, the slowest pace in four years, down from 9.2% in FY24
  • 02/Q4 FY25 GDP rebounded strongly to 7.4%, a four-quarter high, beating analyst estimates of 6.8-7.0%
  • 03/RBI projects 6.5% growth for FY26; India remains the world's fastest-growing major economy

India's economic growth slowed to 6.5% in the financial year ending March 2025. This marks a four-year low, down sharply from 9.2% recorded in FY24.

The fourth quarter delivered a surprise rebound. GDP expanded 7.4% in Q4 FY25, the fastest pace in four quarters. This beat analyst estimates that ranged from 6.8% to 7.0%.

The full-year slowdown reflects subdued private investment amid global uncertainties. However, strong government capital expenditure and resilient services exports provided support.

Agricultural growth accelerated to 4.6% in FY25, up from 2.7% the previous year. Q4 farm activity grew 5.4%, driven by healthy rabi harvests and good reservoir levels.

The construction sector remained robust, expanding 9.4% for the full year. Q4 construction growth hit 10.8%, supported by continued infrastructure push.

Manufacturing growth cooled significantly to 4.5% in FY25, down from 12.3% in FY24. Q4 manufacturing stood at 4.8%, compared to 11.3% in the year-ago period.

Private consumption jumped 7.2% YoY, up from 5.6% in FY24. Rural demand improved due to moderating food prices and festival season spending.

The Reserve Bank of India has projected 6.5% GDP growth for FY26. Quarterly estimates are Q1 at 6.5%, Q2 at 6.7%, Q3 at 6.6%, and Q4 at 6.3%.

"While external uncertainties pose challenges, India continues to benefit from strong service sector performance and improving manufacturing output," said Dr. Manoranjan Sharma, Chief Economist at Infomerics Valuations.

India's economy is expected to surpass Japan's by end of 2025, reaching $4.18 trillion. The IMF projects India will become the world's fourth-largest economy.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT WAS INDIA'S GDP GROWTH IN FY25?
INDIA'S GDP GREW 6.5% IN THE FINANCIAL YEAR 2024-25, ACCORDING TO DATA RELEASED BY THE NATIONAL STATISTICAL OFFICE. THIS REPRESENTS A FOUR-YEAR LOW, DOWN FROM 9.2% IN FY24.
WHAT WAS THE Q4 FY25 GDP GROWTH RATE?
INDIA'S GDP EXPANDED 7.4% IN Q4 FY25, THE FASTEST PACE IN FOUR QUARTERS. THIS BEAT ANALYST ESTIMATES OF 6.8-7.0%.
WHICH SECTORS DROVE THE Q4 REBOUND?
THE Q4 REBOUND WAS DRIVEN BY STRONG CONSTRUCTION GROWTH AT 10.8%, ACCELERATING AGRICULTURAL OUTPUT AT 5.4%, AND RESILIENT SERVICES SECTOR PERFORMANCE.
WHAT IS THE RBI'S GROWTH FORECAST FOR FY26?
THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA HAS PROJECTED 6.5% GDP GROWTH FOR FY26, WITH QUARTERLY ESTIMATES OF 6.5% IN Q1, 6.7% IN Q2, 6.6% IN Q3, AND 6.3% IN Q4.
HOW DOES INDIA COMPARE TO OTHER MAJOR ECONOMIES?
DESPITE THE SLOWDOWN, INDIA REMAINS THE WORLD'S FASTEST-GROWING MAJOR ECONOMY. THE IMF EXPECTS INDIA TO SURPASS JAPAN AND BECOME THE FOURTH-LARGEST ECONOMY BY END OF 2025.
