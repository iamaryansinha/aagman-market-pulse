PM-KUSUM Scheme: How India's Solar Push Is Cutting Diesel Use In Farms

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Over 20 lakh farmers have benefited from PM-KUSUM scheme with 70% completion in standalone solar pump installations
  • 02/Farmers switching from 5 HP diesel pumps to solar save approximately ₹60,000 annually on fuel costs
  • 03/Scheme target is 34,800 MW solar capacity by March 2026 with total central financial assistance of ₹34,422 crore

India's PM-KUSUM scheme is transforming agricultural power consumption. Over 20 lakh farmers have already benefited from the solar initiative.

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan was launched in 2019. It aims to reduce diesel dependence in farming and boost rural incomes.

Under Component B, farmers receive standalone solar pumps for irrigation. A typical 5 HP diesel pump user saves roughly ₹60,000 yearly after switching to solar.

The scheme offers 60% central subsidy, 30% institutional loan, and requires 10% farmer contribution. The investment payback period is less than one year.

Component A allows farmers to lease barren land for solar plants. They can earn up to ₹80,000 per hectare annually by selling power to DISCOMs.

Component C focuses on solarizing existing grid-connected pumps. This enables farmers to sell surplus solar power back to the grid.

Standalone solar pump installations have seen 4.2x growth in FY 2025. Component C solarization jumped 25x compared to the previous year.

The scheme targets 34,800 MW solar capacity addition by March 2026. Total central financial assistance stands at ₹34,422 crore.

Diesel savings are estimated at 1.2 billion liters annually. CO2 emissions are expected to reduce by 27 million tonnes per year.

Implementation progress varies across components. While standalone pumps show strong traction, grid-connected plants and pump solarization lag behind.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE PM-KUSUM SCHEME AND WHEN WAS IT LAUNCHED?
PRADHAN MANTRI KISAN URJA SURAKSHA EVAM UTTHAAN MAHABHIYAN (PM-KUSUM) WAS LAUNCHED IN 2019 TO PROMOTE SOLAR ENERGY USE IN AGRICULTURE. THE SCHEME AIMS TO ENSURE ENERGY SECURITY FOR FARMERS AND INCREASE NON-FOSSIL-FUEL POWER CAPACITY TO 40% BY 2030.
HOW MUCH CAN FARMERS SAVE BY SWITCHING FROM DIESEL TO SOLAR PUMPS?
FARMERS USING A TYPICAL 5 HP DIESEL PUMP CAN SAVE APPROXIMATELY ₹60,000 EACH YEAR ON FUEL COSTS AFTER SWITCHING TO SOLAR. THE COST OF INSTALLING SOLAR PUMPS IS RECOVERED IN LESS THAN ONE YEAR.
WHAT IS THE FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE STRUCTURE UNDER PM-KUSUM?
FARMERS RECEIVE 60% CENTRAL GOVERNMENT SUBSIDY, 30% INSTITUTIONAL LOAN, AND CONTRIBUTE ONLY 10% OF THE TOTAL PROJECT COST. THIS MAKES SOLAR ADOPTION FINANCIALLY VIABLE FOR SMALL AND MARGINAL FARMERS.
WHAT ARE THE THREE COMPONENTS OF THE PM-KUSUM SCHEME?
COMPONENT A INVOLVES SETTING UP 10,000 MW OF DECENTRALIZED SOLAR PLANTS ON BARREN LAND. COMPONENT B COVERS INSTALLATION OF 14 LAKH STANDALONE SOLAR PUMPS. COMPONENT C TARGETS SOLARIZATION OF 35 LAKH EXISTING GRID-CONNECTED PUMPS.
WHAT IS THE TARGET AND CURRENT STATUS OF THE SCHEME?
THE SCHEME TARGETS ADDING 34,800 MW OF SOLAR CAPACITY BY MARCH 2026 WITH ₹34,422 CRORE CENTRAL FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE. OVER 20 LAKH FARMERS HAVE BENEFITED SO FAR WITH 70% COMPLETION IN STANDALONE SOLAR PUMP INSTALLATIONS.
