India Raises Windfall Levy on Diesel Exports to ₹55.5/Litre, ATF to ₹42/Litre

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Windfall levy on export-bound diesel raised by 158% to ₹55.5 per litre from ₹21.5 per litre, effective immediately
  • 02/ATF export levy increased 42% to ₹42 per litre from ₹29.5 per litre; petrol export duty remains nil
  • 03/Government says move prioritizes domestic fuel availability, not revenue generation; duties to be reviewed fortnightly

The Finance Ministry has sharply increased windfall taxes on petroleum exports. Diesel exporters now face a levy of ₹55.5 per litre, up from ₹21.5 per litre.

Aviation turbine fuel (ATF) exports will attract ₹42 per litre, rising from ₹29.5 per litre. The revised rates took effect on April 11, 2026.

This marks the first revision since the levies were reimposed on March 26, 2026. Export duty on petrol continues to remain nil.

Government officials clarified the objective is not revenue generation. The move aims to prevent exporters from taking undue advantage of price differences.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman had stated on March 26 that the levy ensures adequate domestic availability. Refiners must prioritize local supply over exports.

The revision comes as global crude prices slipped from over $120 per barrel to around $95-96 per barrel. However, refined product prices remain elevated.

India's refineries maintain high capacity and export significant petroleum volumes. The new levies target this export-oriented segment.

Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs Chairman Vivek Chaturvedi said duties will be reviewed every fortnight. The situation remains dynamic amid global uncertainty.

Reliance Industries, India's largest fuel exporter, faces margin pressure. Its shares fell 4.6% on March 27, wiping out approximately ₹88,000 crore in market value.

The Jamnagar SEZ refinery contributes 75% of diesel and 35% of ATF production. SEZ status previously exempted it from windfall taxes, creating uncertainty now.

Analysts estimate the levies equate to $36-50 per barrel. Global refining margins for diesel and ATF currently range at $65-70 per barrel.
