Vedanta Demerger April 2026: Five Separate Companies to List by May 15

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Vedanta demerger effective date is April 1, 2026; five separate companies to list between April 1 and May 15, 2026
  • 02/Shareholders get 1:1 ratio - for every Vedanta Ltd share held, receive one share in each of five new listed entities
  • 03/Watch for debt allocation across demerged companies and post-listing valuations of pure-play commodity businesses

Vedanta Ltd (NSE: VEDL) will complete its demerger into five separately listed companies effective April 1, 2026. Group CFO Ajay Goel confirmed all five entities will list on domestic bourses between April 1 and May 15, 2026.

The five demerged companies will be Vedanta Limited (housing base metals), Vedanta Aluminium, Talwandi Sabo Power, Vedanta Steel and Iron, and Malco Energy (oil and gas). The process follows NCLT approval received in December 2025.

Shareholders will receive a 1:1 ratio benefit. For every one share held in Vedanta Ltd, investors will get one additional share in each of the four newly demerged companies. This effectively creates five separate equity holdings from one.

Chairman Anil Agarwal described the move as transforming "one Vedanta into five Vedantas." He stated each demerged entity has potential to grow to the scale of the current parent company, unlocking significant shareholder value.

The restructuring plan received overwhelming approval at the February 18, 2025 shareholders meeting. Equity shareholders voted 99.99% in favor, while secured and unsecured creditors backed the plan with 99.59% and 99.95% votes respectively.

The ₹48,000 crore debt will be allocated to demerged entities based on respective cash flows. Vedanta shares have gained over 67% in the past year, reflecting investor optimism ahead of the split.

Analysts view the demerger as value-accretive. Brokerage targets range from ₹620 to ₹650 per share. The pure-play structure aligns Vedanta with global mining majors that operate focused commodity businesses.

The demerger was originally announced in September 2023. Regulatory clearances are now complete, and the company has confirmed no disruption to capital expenditure plans or dividend policy post-restructuring.
