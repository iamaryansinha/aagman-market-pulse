Coal India To Invest ₹3,300 Crore In Eight New Coking Coal Washeries

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Coal India announces ₹3,300 crore investment for eight new coking coal washeries with combined capacity of 21.5 MT/Y
  • 02/Five washeries under Central Coalfields (14.5 MT/Y) and three under Bharat Coking Coal (7 MT/Y); operational by FY2030
  • 03/Additional ₹300 crore for renovation of existing units; partnership with Tata Steel for technical expertise

Coal India Ltd (NSE: COALINDIA) will invest ₹3,300 crore to set up eight new coking coal washeries. The state-run miner announced the plan in a stock exchange filing on Friday.

The new washeries will have a combined washing capacity of 21.5 million tonnes per year (MT/Y). They are expected to become operational by FY2030.

Five washeries with 14.5 MT/Y capacity will be set up under Central Coalfields Ltd. Three washeries with 7 MT/Y capacity will come under Bharat Coking Coal Ltd.

This expansion adds to Coal India's existing network of 10 coking coal washeries. These current facilities have a cumulative capacity of 18.35 MT/Y.

The company will also invest ₹300 crore in renovation and modernization of existing washeries. Total capital outlay for the washery program stands at ₹3,600 crore.

The initiative aims to improve quality of domestic coking coal. Indian coking coal has ash content of 25-45%, much higher than global standards.

India currently imports approximately 90% of its coking coal requirements. The steel industry consumes 95% of total coking coal demand.

Coal India is collaborating with Tata Steel under a public-private partnership model. This leverages Tata Steel's washing capacity and technical expertise.

"The synergy results in substitution of imported coking coal, reduction in forex outgo and increased industrial competitiveness," Coal India stated.

The company also plans to monetize three older, non-operative washeries. This aligns with the National Monetisation Policy.

Coal India shares traded at ₹447.15 as of 2:04 PM on Friday. The stock has gained nearly 12% over the past 12 months.
