Coal India to Invest ₹3,300 Crore in 8 New Coking Coal Washeries

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Coal India to set up 8 new coking coal washeries at ₹3,300 crore, adding 21.5 million tonnes per year capacity by FY30
  • 02/Five washeries in Central Coalfields (14.5 MT/Y) and three in Bharat Coking Coal (7 MT/Y) under public-private model with TATA Steel
  • 03/Additional ₹300 crore for renovating 10 existing washeries; move targets reducing coking coal imports and forex outgo

State-run Coal India Ltd (NSE: COALINDIA) will set up eight new coking coal washeries. The estimated capital outlay is ₹3,300 crore. The company announced this in a stock exchange filing on Friday.

The washeries are expected to be operational by FY30. They will have a combined washing capacity of 21.5 million tonnes per year (MT/Y).

Of the eight new facilities, five will be located in Central Coalfields. These will add 14.5 MT/Y capacity. Three will be in Bharat Coking Coal with 7 MT/Y capacity.

Coal India also operates 10 existing coking coal washeries. These have a cumulative capacity of 18.35 MT/Y. The company will invest an additional ₹300 crore to renovate and modernise these aging facilities.

India relies heavily on imported coking coal despite having 389 billion tonnes of geological coal reserves. Domestic coking coal has high ash content of 25-45%. This is significantly higher than global standards.

The company is leveraging TATA Steel's washing capacity and technical expertise. This public-private collaboration aims to enhance supply of quality coking coal to the domestic steel sector.

The expansion is designed to moderate import dependence. It will also reduce forex outgo and improve industrial competitiveness. India imports most of its coking coal needs due to scarce domestic high-quality reserves.
