Cochin Shipyard, HBL Engineering JV For Electric Mobility, Energy Storage Tech

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Cochin Shipyard and HBL Engineering sign JV agreement on March 26, 2026 to develop electric mobility and energy storage technologies for maritime sector
  • 02/JV starts with ₹9 crore capital; HBL holds 60% stake, Cochin Shipyard holds 40% stake in the joint venture company
  • 03/Focus on advanced marine batteries, battery management systems, electric motors, and charging infrastructure for domestic and global markets

Cochin Shipyard Ltd (NSE: COCHINSHIP) and HBL Engineering Ltd have signed a joint venture agreement on March 26, 2026. The JV will develop and commercialise electric mobility and energy storage technologies for the maritime sector.

The collaboration focuses on advanced marine batteries, battery management systems (BMS), electric motors, power electronics, and charging infrastructure. It aims to serve both domestic and international markets.

The joint venture company will have an initial capital of ₹9 crore. HBL Engineering will hold a 60% stake while Cochin Shipyard will hold 40%.

The board of Cochin Shipyard had approved the JV proposal on January 28, 2026. HBL Engineering's board also cleared the partnership on February 7, 2026.

"This represents a watershed moment for CSL," said Jose VJ, Director (Finance) and CMD (Additional Charge) at Cochin Shipyard. "Collaborations with home-grown technology companies are the need of the hour to indigenously develop state-of-the-art marine batteries."

AJ Prasad, CMD of HBL Engineering, said, "CSL has taken an unprecedented initiative to expedite indigenisation of electric propulsion systems for the maritime sector. HBL's decades of work on specialised batteries and 10 years of work on motors will support this venture."

The initiative supports India's green shipping requirements and the global transition towards sustainable maritime solutions. It aligns with the government's Aatmanirbhar Bharat vision for indigenous capabilities.

Cochin Shipyard reported Q3 FY26 revenue of ₹1,350 crore, up 17.7% year-on-year. Net profit declined 18.3% to ₹144.7 crore due to margin pressure.
