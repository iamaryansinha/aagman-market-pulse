Adani Green Adds 5+ GW Capacity In FY26, Khavda Project Reaches 9.4 GW Installed

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Adani Green Energy Ltd adds over 5 GW renewable capacity in FY26, driven primarily by Khavda project expansion
  • 02/Khavda renewable energy park now has 9.4 GW installed capacity out of total 30 GW under development
  • 03/Company scales up BESS push alongside solar and wind capacity; watch for FY26 operational update and commissioning timeline

Adani Green Energy Ltd has added over 5 GW of renewable capacity in FY2026. The majority of this capacity addition came from the Khavda renewable energy park in Gujarat.

The Khavda project is a 30 GW renewable energy park under active development. Out of this, 9.4 GW has already been installed and operationalised by the company.

The capacity expansion includes both solar and wind installations. Adani Green is also scaling up its Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) deployment alongside generation assets.

This 5 GW addition marks significant progress in the company's capacity build-out plans. Adani Green remains India's largest renewable energy company by operational capacity.

The Khavda project is one of the world's largest single-location renewable energy installations. Located in the Rann of Kutch, it benefits from high solar irradiance and wind potential.

BESS integration is becoming a key focus area for the company. Storage solutions help address intermittency challenges and improve grid stability for renewable power.

Adani Green has been rapidly expanding its operational footprint across multiple states. The company targets substantial capacity additions through organic growth and acquisitions.

Investors should watch for the company's Q4 FY26 operational update. This will provide detailed commissioning timelines and revenue recognition from new capacity.
