Gold ETFs Outpace Nifty 50 Returns Across All Time Horizons

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold ETFs delivered 52%+ one-year returns as of March 2026, significantly outpacing the Nifty 50 TRI which posted negative returns
  • 02/Indian gold ETF AUM tripled to ₹1.28 lakh crore in 2025; net inflows hit $4.37 billion with holdings at 95 tonnes
  • 03/Gold ETFs have beaten Nifty ETFs in 7 out of 10 calendar years since 2016

Gold exchange-traded funds have delivered superior returns across all time horizons compared to the Nifty 50 Total Return Index. Data compiled by ACEMF as of March 23, 2026 shows most Gold ETFs posted one-year CAGR returns exceeding 50 per cent.

Aditya Birla SL Gold ETF led with 52.8 per cent returns. Nippon India ETF Gold BeES and SBI Gold ETF followed closely at 52.3-52.5 per cent. This compares against negative returns for the Nifty 50 TRI over the same one-year period.

The outperformance extends to longer horizons. Gold ETFs delivered 3-year annualised returns of around 30 per cent. Five-year CAGR returns stood near 24 per cent, while 7-year annualised gains approached 22 per cent.

Over a 10-year horizon, Gold ETFs delivered annualised growth above 15 per cent. The Nifty 50-TRI posted approximately 12.7 per cent over the same period. This 240 basis point annual advantage compounds significantly over time.

SIP investments in Gold ETFs showed equally compelling results. A monthly SIP of ₹1,000 grew to roughly ₹14,000 in one year, implying XIRR returns of 38-40 per cent. The corpus reached approximately ₹61,000 in three years and ₹1.23 lakh in five years.

Investor participation has surged in tandem with performance. Gold ETF AUM in India tripled from ₹44,600 crore to nearly ₹1.28 lakh crore ($14.2 billion) during 2025. Total holdings increased 65 per cent year-on-year to reach 95 tonnes by December.

Net inflows hit a record $4.37 billion (₹430 billion) in 2025, more than tripling from $1.28 billion in 2024. This marked the third-highest inflows globally, behind only the US at $50 billion and China at $15.5 billion.

The investor base expanded sharply with folios rising 60 per cent to 10.2 million accounts. A record 3.8 million new accounts were added during 2025. December alone saw net inflows of $1.29 billion, marking the eighth consecutive month of additions.
