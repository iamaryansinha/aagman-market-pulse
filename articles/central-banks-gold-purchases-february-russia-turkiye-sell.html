Central Banks Gold Purchases Net Up in February Despite Russia, Turkiye Sales

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Global central banks were net buyers of gold in February 2026 despite some selling
  • 02/National Bank of Poland purchased 20 tonnes, raising its holdings to 570 tonnes
  • 03/Russia and Turkiye were notable sellers during the month

Central banks globally were net buyers of gold in February 2026. The purchases came despite selling activity from Russia and Turkiye.

The National Bank of Poland led buying activity with a 20-tonne purchase. This raised Poland's total gold reserves to 570 tonnes.

Poland has been actively diversifying its foreign exchange reserves. Gold now represents a growing share of its total reserve assets.

Russia was a net seller during the month. The country has been using gold reserves to support its economy amid sanctions.

Turkiye also reduced its gold holdings in February. Turkish banks had accumulated significant gold in previous months.

Central banks have purchased over 1,000 tonnes of gold annually since 2022. This reflects concerns about dollar dominance and geopolitical risks.

Gold prices traded at $3,020 per ounce on April 3, 2026. The metal has gained 15% year-to-date.
