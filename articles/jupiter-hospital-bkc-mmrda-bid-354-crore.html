Jupiter Hospital Wins MMRDA Bid for BKC Land at ₹354 Crore for 80-Year Lease

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Jupiter Life Line Hospitals wins MMRDA bid for 10,026.44 sqm plot in Bandra Kurla Complex at ₹354 crore for 80-year lease
  • 02/Plot is located at SF5 in GTxT-Block, marking hospital chain's strategic real estate acquisition in Mumbai's prime business district
  • 03/Shares traded flat at ₹1,279; company plans to double bed capacity from 1,061 to 2,500 beds through ₹1,400 crore capex

Jupiter Life Line Hospitals (NSE: JLHL) has won a bid for prime land in Mumbai's Bandra Kurla Complex. The company will pay ₹354 crore for an 80-year lease.

The land parcel measures 10,026.44 square metres. It is located at Plot SF5 in GTxT-Block of BKC, Maharashtra.

The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority (MMRDA) accepted Jupiter's tender. The company disclosed the development in a stock exchange filing on March 27, 2026.

Jupiter Life Line Hospitals currently operates 1,061 beds across three locations. These include Thane, Pune, and Indore.

The BKC acquisition adds to the hospital chain's aggressive expansion strategy. The company plans to double its capacity to 2,500 beds by 2029.

Jupiter has earmarked ₹1,400 crore for capital expenditure. This includes greenfield hospitals in Dombivli, Mira-Bhayandar, and Pune's Bibwewadi.

The Dombivli project is already 50% complete. It will be Jupiter's largest facility at 800,000 square feet. The first phase with 200-250 beds is expected by Q1FY27.

Jupiter shares traded flat at ₹1,279 on the NSE after the announcement. The stock has declined 12% over the past year from a 52-week high of ₹1,770.

BKC is Mumbai's most expensive commercial district. Land deals in the area have fetched between ₹468 crore to ₹2,538 crore in recent auctions.

The company follows an asset-heavy greenfield model. It prefers owning land over leasing to maintain quality control and long-term value.
