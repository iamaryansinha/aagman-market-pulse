SC Orders Time-Bound Probe Into Reliance Anil Ambani Group Financial Irregularities

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Supreme Court directs CBI and ED to complete time-bound investigation into Reliance Anil Ambani Group alleged fraud within four weeks
  • 02/ED's SIT probing eight cases; CBI investigating seven cases including five recent FIRs with total claims of ₹73,006 crore
  • 03/Reliance Industries shares trade steady around ₹1,405; analysts note Mukesh Ambani's RIL is separate entity from Anil Ambani's RAAG

The Supreme Court has directed central investigative agencies to conduct a time-bound, fair and transparent probe into alleged financial irregularities linked to the Reliance Anil Ambani Group (RAAG). The directive came on April 6, 2026, while hearing a PIL filed by former Union secretary EAS Sarma.

A bench headed by the Chief Justice, along with Justices Joymalya Bagchi and Vipul M Pancholi, reviewed status reports from the Directorate of Enforcement (ED) and Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI). The court stressed the need for coordinated efforts by senior officials to uncover any irregularities.

The ED informed the court that a Special Investigation Team (SIT) was constituted on February 12, 2026. The team comprises senior ED officials, forensic analysts, and banking representatives. Investigations have begun in eight cases with several documents already seized.

The agency flagged a suspected "Project Help" under which insolvency proceedings were allegedly initiated through unrelated lenders. The ED claimed claims worth approximately ₹2,983 crore were settled for just ₹26 crore, indicating possible financial misconduct.

The CBI submitted that seven cases are under active investigation, including five recent FIRs. The agency noted alleged wrongful loss in one case alone amounts to ₹2,223 crore. Total claims across all cases reach approximately ₹73,006 crore. The CBI is also examining roles of public servants and potential collusion with financial institutions.

Solicitor General Tushar Mehta assured the bench that all efforts would be made to complete the investigation within four weeks. The court directed all regulatory bodies and financial institutions to fully cooperate with the ED, warning that any delay must be reported back.

Reliance Industries Ltd (NSE: RELIANCE) shares traded around ₹1,405 on the BSE. Analysts clarified that Mukesh Ambani's RIL is a separate entity from Anil Ambani's RAAG, following the 2005 group split.

Morgan Stanley maintains an 'Overweight' rating on RIL with a target price of ₹1,803, implying 28% upside. The brokerage cited elevated refining margins and the company's ability to process heavy crude as key strengths.

The matter has been posted for further hearing on April 30, 2026. The court clarified it was not expressing any opinion on the merits of the allegations at this stage.
