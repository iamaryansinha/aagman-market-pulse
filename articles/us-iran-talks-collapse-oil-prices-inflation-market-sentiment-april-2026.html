US-Iran Talks Collapse, Oil Prices & Inflation Data to Drive Market Sentiment This Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/US-Iran talks in Pakistan collapse without deal; Iran cites "excessive demands" by American side led by VP JD Vance
  • 02/Markets face volatility return after Sensex rallied 4,231 points (5.77%) and Nifty surged 1,338 points (5.88%) last week
  • 03/Key triggers this week: CPI inflation (April 13), WPI inflation (April 14), Q4 earnings from HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Wipro

US-Iran talks in Pakistan have ended without a deal. Iranian officials blamed "excessive demands" made by the American side.

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baqaei acknowledged that diplomacy continues. However, US Vice President JD Vance confirmed no peace deal was reached.

Vance cited Tehran's refusal to forgo its nuclear programme as a key sticking point. The US presented its "final and best offer," which Iran rejected.

Baqaei stated both sides reached consensus on some issues. But they held different views on "2-3 important matters."

Markets had rallied sharply last week on ceasefire hopes. Crude oil prices had dropped below USD 100 per barrel.

The BSE Sensex jumped 4,230.7 points, or 5.77 per cent, last week. The NSE Nifty surged 1,337.5 points, or 5.88 per cent.

"The Nifty-50 enters the upcoming week at a critical inflexion point. After staging a sharp recovery and reclaiming the 24,000 mark, the market had begun to reflect cautious optimism," said Hariprasad K, Research Analyst and founder, Livelong Wealth.

"With negotiations ending without a resolution, markets are now bracing for a return of volatility that characterised earlier phases of the conflict," he added. Benchmark indices are expected to open with a significant gap down.

Stock markets will remain closed on Tuesday for Baba Saheb Ambedkar Jayanti. Trading activity will be compressed in this holiday-shortened week.

"With the onset of the Q4 FY26 earnings season, key results from heavyweight companies, such as Wipro, HDFC Bank, and ICICI Bank, will be closely monitored," said Ajit Mishra, SVP, Research, Religare Broking Ltd.

Foreign investors maintained their aggressive sell-off in Indian equities. They withdrew Rs 48,213 crore (USD 5.14 billion) this month alone.
