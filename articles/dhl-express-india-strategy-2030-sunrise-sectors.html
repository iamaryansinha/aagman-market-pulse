DHL Express India Strategy 2030: €1Bn Bet On Life Sciences, New Energy, E-Commerce

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/DHL Express is sharpening India focus under global Strategy 2030, targeting life sciences, new energy, e-commerce and digital sales for growth
  • 02/Group has committed €1 billion investment across India business units by 2030; India among 20 priority countries identified for expansion
  • 03/SMEs contribute nearly 60% of DHL's India business; company aims to grow global top line 50% from 2023 base

DHL Express is intensifying its India operations as part of global Strategy 2030. The company aims to grow its worldwide top line by 50% from its 2023 base.

India ranks among 20 priority countries identified for future growth. The logistics major has committed €1 billion across its India business units by 2030.

R S Subramanian, SVP South Asia at DHL Express, said India will be among top markets for opportunity and scale. The first year of strategy execution has seen strong investments.

The company is prioritising four sunrise sectors. These include life sciences, new energy, e-commerce, and digital sales channels.

In life sciences, DHL has strengthened capabilities through GDP-certified temperature-controlled infrastructure. The CRYOPDP acquisition enhances clinical trials and specialised pharma logistics capabilities.

"These are no longer niche segments," Subramanian said. "They are already contributing and growing at a strong pace."

New energy presents significant untapped potential across the ecosystem. This spans installation to maintenance of renewable assets including solar and wind projects.

E-commerce remains a key growth driver for the company. Investments are flowing into network capacity, automation, and last-mile efficiency improvements.

Small and medium enterprises contribute nearly 60% of DHL's India business. The company is deepening SME engagement through digital tools and DGFT partnerships.

Bengaluru plays a central role as gateway for South India. The city handles significant inbound and outbound shipment volumes.

Despite geopolitical disruptions, DHL has maintained operational continuity. The company has reconfigured networks and leveraged alternate routes while keeping service levels stable.

"Growth is the core focus," Subramanian said. "Everything else is aligned to support that ambition."
