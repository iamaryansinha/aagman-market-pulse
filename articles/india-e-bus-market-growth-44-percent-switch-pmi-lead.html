India E-Bus Market Grows 44% as Switch, PMI Lead; Tata Motors Loses Share

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's electric bus market surged 44%, driven by aggressive expansion from newer manufacturers Switch Mobility and PMI Electro
  • 02/Legacy players like Tata Motors face sharp market share erosion as competition intensifies in the e-bus segment
  • 03/Watch for further consolidation and pricing pressure as more entrants join India's electrification push

India's electric bus market has grown 44% in the latest period. New manufacturers Switch Mobility and PMI Electro are leading this surge.

Legacy commercial vehicle makers are feeling the heat. Tata Motors has seen a sharp erosion in its market share.

The shift reflects changing buyer preferences. Fleet operators are increasingly favoring newer electric-focused players over traditional manufacturers.

Switch Mobility and PMI Electro have capitalized on this trend. Their specialized focus on electric vehicles gives them a competitive edge.

Tata Motors dominated India's commercial vehicle space for decades. The company now faces disruption from agile, EV-native competitors.

The e-bus segment is attracting significant investment. Government incentives and state-level electrification mandates are driving demand.

Market share dynamics are shifting rapidly. Traditional players must accelerate their electric transition to remain competitive.

The 44% growth signals strong momentum. India's public transport electrification is gaining pace across cities.
