Wheat Procurement 2026: Centre Targets 303 Lakh Tonnes, Begins With 18,000 Tonnes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Centre begins wheat procurement season with initial purchase of 18,000 tonnes across key procurement centres
  • 02/Full-year procurement target fixed at 303 lakh tonnes amid prevailing low mandi prices concern
  • 03/Adverse weather conditions may impact procurement volumes and target achievement for the season

The Centre has officially kicked off its wheat procurement season for 2026. Initial purchases have reached 18,000 tonnes across designated procurement centres.

The government has set an ambitious procurement target of 303 lakh tonnes. This target reflects the Centre's commitment to building adequate buffer stocks.

Low mandi prices pose a significant challenge to procurement efforts. Farmers may hold back produce if they find government rates unattractive compared to market prices.

Adverse weather conditions add another layer of uncertainty. Unseasonal rains or heatwaves could affect wheat quality and availability in key producing states.

The Food Corporation of India (FCI) and state agencies will handle procurement operations. Payments will be made directly into farmers' bank accounts within 72 hours.

The 303-lakh-tonne target supports the public distribution system. It ensures subsidised wheat availability for ration card holders nationwide.

Last year, the Centre faced similar challenges with weather disruptions. The government had to relax quality norms in some regions to meet procurement needs.

Analysts remain cautious about target achievement. They point to the gap between initial momentum and sustained procurement needed over the full season.

THE CENTRE HAS SET A PROCUREMENT TARGET OF 303 LAKH TONNES OF WHEAT FOR THE 2026 SEASON.

THE CENTRE HAS PROCURED 18,000 TONNES OF WHEAT AS THE INITIAL PURCHASE TO KICK OFF THE PROCUREMENT SEASON.

LOW MANDI PRICES AND ADVERSE WEATHER CONDITIONS ARE THE TWO MAIN CHALLENGES THAT COULD AFFECT THE CENTRE'S ABILITY TO ACHIEVE THE 303-LAKH-TONNE TARGET.

THE PROCURED WHEAT IS USED TO BUILD BUFFER STOCKS THAT SUPPLY THE PUBLIC DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM, ENSURING SUBSIDISED WHEAT AVAILABILITY FOR RATION CARD HOLDERS ACROSS THE COUNTRY.
HOW ARE FARMERS PAID FOR THEIR WHEAT?
FARMERS RECEIVE PAYMENTS DIRECTLY INTO THEIR BANK ACCOUNTS WITHIN 72 HOURS OF DELIVERY THROUGH THE FOOD CORPORATION OF INDIA (FCI) AND STATE PROCUREMENT AGENCIES.
