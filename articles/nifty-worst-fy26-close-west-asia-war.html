Nifty Crashes To Worst FY26 Close As West Asia War Enters Week Five

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nifty posts worst FY26 close at 22,283 after falling from intraday high of 22,714
  • 02/Sensex plunges 1,636 points as West Asia war enters its fifth week
  • 03/Rupee bleeds past 95 against dollar; investors watch geopolitical developments

Indian equity markets witnessed heavy selling on March 30, 2026. The Nifty 50 posted its worst closing of FY26.

The index opened at 22,549 and briefly touched 22,714. It then crashed to close at 22,283.

The Sensex mirrored this decline, falling 1,636 points during the session. Investors fled risk assets amid escalating tensions.

The West Asia war has now entered its fifth week. Supply chain disruptions and oil price volatility continue to weigh on sentiment.

The Indian rupee breached the 95 mark against the US dollar. This marks fresh lows for the domestic currency.

Weakness in the rupee typically triggers foreign portfolio outflows. FPIs have been net sellers for three consecutive sessions.

Analysts expect volatility to persist until geopolitical clarity emerges. Traders are advised to maintain defensive positions.
