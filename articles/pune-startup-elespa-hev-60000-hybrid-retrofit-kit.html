Pune Startup ELESPA HEV Unveils ₹60,000 Hybrid Retrofit Kit for Two-Wheelers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Pune-based ELESPA HEV launches ₹60,000 ARAI-validated hybrid retrofit kit, undercutting Kawasaki's ₹9 lakh hybrid motorcycles by 93%
  • 02/Portable plug-in system retrofits onto existing petrol scooters/motorcycles with 100 km electric-only range and swappable batteries
  • 03/Company targets India's 210-million-strong two-wheeler fleet; currently accelerating Maharashtra rollout with government-backed ARAI support

Pune-based startup ELESPA HEV has launched a hybrid retrofit kit priced at ₹60,000. The ARAI-validated solution retrofits existing petrol two-wheelers into plug-in hybrids.

Founded by Prathamesh Choudhary, the company targets India's 210 million-strong two-wheeler fleet. The kit costs 93% less than Kawasaki's ₹9 lakh hybrid motorcycles.

The plug-in series-parallel hybrid system includes a portable, swappable battery pack. It delivers 100 km of electric-only range per charge.

A patented dual-throttle mechanism enables seamless switching between petrol and electric modes. Riders can toggle power sources based on riding conditions.

Connected features include app-based monitoring and anti-theft functionality. Users can track battery status, location, and vehicle health remotely.

Running costs drop to approximately 12 paise per kilometre. Annual fuel and maintenance savings reach roughly ₹60,000 compared to conventional petrol vehicles.

The payback period for the retrofit investment is roughly one year. This makes it economically viable for mass-market two-wheeler owners.

ELESPA HEV is ARAI-incubated and ARAI-validated for quality assurance. The government contributed approximately ₹28.91 crore to the ARAI industry accelerator fund supporting such innovations.

The company is currently accelerating its rollout across Maharashtra. Expansion plans target other states as production scales up.
