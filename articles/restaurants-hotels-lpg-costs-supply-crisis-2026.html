Restaurants, Hotels Face Revenue Drop as LPG Costs Triple Amid Supply Crisis

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Commercial LPG costs have surged nearly 3x for restaurants as supply shortages force grey market purchases at inflated prices
  • 02/Around 30% of hotels in Chennai have shut down while 20% of Mumbai restaurants closed due to supply disruptions
  • 03/International tourist footfalls declining; MICE and wedding events being planned around LPG availability

India's hospitality sector is facing a severe crisis as commercial LPG supply disruptions drive costs up nearly threefold. Restaurants, hotels, and institutional food services are cutting operational hours, reducing menus, or shutting down entirely.

The shortage stems from the West Asia war disrupting shipments through the Strait of Hormuz. India imports 88% of its LPG needs, with 40% passing through this critical waterway.

Sanjay Kumar, CEO & MD of Rassense, said his catering company's commercial LPG costs have risen nearly 3x. "We are securing LPG supplies wherever possible at steep costs while using boilers and firewood for central kitchens," he added.

Pranav Rungta, VP of NRAI, noted that despite central government directives, state-level bottlenecks persist. Many players are forced to procure LPG in the grey market at significantly higher prices.

Around 30% of Chennai hotels have shut down according to M Ravi, President of Chennai Hotels Association. Members are in "survival mode" facing salaries, rent, and bank loan obligations.

In Mumbai, approximately 20% of hotels and restaurants have already closed. Industry representatives warn up to 50% could shut if supplies are not restored immediately.

International tourist footfalls are also declining. City hotels report lower occupancies with fresh international travel slowing down. MICE and wedding events are now being planned based on LPG availability.

Commercial LPG cylinder prices have risen ₹114.50 to ₹1,883. Black market prices have surged even higher, with cylinders reportedly selling at ₹2,000-2,500 in some areas.

Kolkata hotels are now using electricity for 70% of energy needs as LPG shortages force rapid conversion to induction cooktops. Tamil Nadu hoteliers have permanently removed high gas-consuming items from menus.
