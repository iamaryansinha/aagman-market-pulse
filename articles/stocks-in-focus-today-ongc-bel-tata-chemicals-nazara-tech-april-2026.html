Stocks In Focus Today: ONGC, BEL, Tata Chemicals, Nazara Tech Eye Major Moves

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BEL secures additional orders worth ₹1,660 crore including satellite communication and electronic warfare systems
  • 02/Premier Energies commissions 5.6 GW solar facility taking total capacity to 11.1 GW
  • 03/Nazara Tech announces ₹500 crore preferential issue of warrants at ₹260 per share

Over two dozen stocks are set to remain in focus on Wednesday. Key names include ONGC (NSE: ONGC), Bharat Electronics (NSE: BEL), Tata Chemicals (NSE: TATACHEM), and Nazara Technologies (NSE: NAZARA).

Bharat Electronics Ltd has secured additional orders worth ₹1,660 crore since March 17. The orders span satellite communication networks, electronic warfare systems, avionics, and munitions.

ONGC announced that its Daman Upside Development Project has achieved a key milestone. Gas flow has commenced from Platform B-12-24P located 180 km northwest of Mumbai.

The project has a capital expenditure of approximately $1 billion. Gas is being sent to the Hazira Plant following successful commissioning.

Tata Chemicals reported that its Mithapur facility in Gujarat has reached 1 million tonnes of soda ash production in FY26. This marks a significant operational milestone for the company.

Nazara Technologies has announced a preferential issue of warrants aggregating to ₹500 crore. Each warrant is convertible into one equity share at ₹260 per warrant.

The issue includes participation from Riambel Capital, S Gupta Family Investments, and Founders Collective. The pricing reflects a premium to current market price.

Premier Energies Ltd has commissioned its 5.6 GW solar module manufacturing facility at Seetharampur, Telangana. The facility spans 75 acres and will create approximately 2,000 jobs.

With this addition, Premier Energies' total solar module capacity has reached 11.1 GW. This strengthens its position as one of India's largest solar manufacturers.

Dixon Technologies' subsidiary Dixon Display Technologies has received approval under the Electronics Component Manufacturing Scheme. The company will undertake display module sub-assembly manufacturing.

Welspun Corp has secured a large order for pipe supply from its US facility valued at approximately ₹1,000 crore. The company's consolidated global order book now stands at ₹24,700 crore.

IndiQube Spaces has signed a workspace leasing agreement valued at ₹75 crore over five years. The client, a healthcare technology GCC, has taken up 48,000 sq.ft. in Bengaluru.

Automobile stocks including Maruti Suzuki (NSE: MARUTI), Tata Motors (NSE: TATAMOTORS), and Mahindra & Mahindra (NSE: M&M) will be in focus. Companies are set to announce March and FY26 sales volumes today.
