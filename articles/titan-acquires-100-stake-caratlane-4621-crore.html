Titan Acquires 100% Stake in CaratLane for ₹4,621 Crore at $2B Valuation

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Titan Company (NSE: TITAN) acquires 27.18% additional stake in CaratLane for ₹4,621 crore, taking total ownership to 98.28%
  • 02/Transaction values CaratLane at ₹17,000 crore (~$2 billion), making it India's second-largest e-commerce founder exit after Flipkart
  • 03/Titan later acquired remaining 1.72% stake (including ESOPs) for ₹292 crore in 2024, completing 100% ownership

Titan Company Limited (NSE: TITAN) has completed the acquisition of 100% stake in CaratLane Trading Private Limited. The Tata Group company paid ₹4,621 crore for the 27.18% stake held by founder Mithun Sacheti and his family in August 2023.

The transaction valued CaratLane at ₹17,000 crore, or approximately $2 billion. This marked the formal entry of the omnichannel jewellery brand into the unicorn club.

Titan's stake in CaratLane increased from 71.09% to 98.28% after this acquisition. The company later purchased the remaining 1.72% stake held by employees through ESOPs for ₹292 crore in 2024.

This deal represents the second-largest founder exit in Indian e-commerce history. Only Flipkart founders Sachin and Binny Bansal's sale to Walmart ranked higher.

"We have great faith in the India consumer story and believe that the growth journey of CaratLane has only begun," said CK Venkataraman, Managing Director of Titan. "We thank Mithun Sacheti for having jointly built a customer-centric brand that all of us in Tata Group can be proud of."

Titan first entered CaratLane in 2016, acquiring a 62% stake for ₹357.24 crore. At that time, the company was valued at just ₹575 crore. Between 2016 and 2023, the valuation grew 30x.

CaratLane was founded in 2008 by Mithun Sacheti and Srinivasa Gopalan. It started as an online diamond and jewellery platform when e-commerce was still nascent in India.

The company now operates 233+ stores across 93 cities in India. It also ships to 132 countries globally including the USA, UK, Singapore, Dubai, Australia, and Canada.

Revenue has grown significantly under Titan's ownership. CaratLane reported ₹2,177 crore in FY23, up 72% from ₹1,267 crore in FY22. For FY24, revenue rose further to ₹3,080 crore.

The company has been profitable for the last three years. In Q1 FY24, it reported EBIT of ₹35 crore on revenue of ₹640 crore, representing a 5.5% margin.

Tiger Global Management was the only venture investor in CaratLane prior to Titan's entry. The US fund invested approximately $58 million across Series A to D rounds between 2011 and 2015.

Tiger Global exited completely when Titan first acquired the 62% stake in 2016. The fund likely recovered its investment amount but missed the significant upside from the $2 billion valuation.

CaratLane positioned itself as India's largest digitally native omnichannel jewellery brand. It competes with Bluestone, GIVA, and traditional players like Kalyan and Senco Gold.

The acquisition strengthens Titan's dominance in the jewellery market. The company already operates Tanishq, Mia, Zoya, and other brands under its portfolio.

The transaction was financed through a combination of cash balances, internal accruals, and debt. It closed in October 2023 after receiving regulatory approvals.
