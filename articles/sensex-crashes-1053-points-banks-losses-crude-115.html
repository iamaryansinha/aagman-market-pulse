Sensex Crashes 1,053 Points Midday: Banks Lead Bloodbath as Crude Hits $115

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex crashes 1,052.90 points (1.43%) to 72,530.32; Nifty 50 drops 304.85 points (1.34%) to 22,514.75
  • 02/Banking stocks lead sectoral losses as crude oil prices surge past $115 per barrel, stoking inflation fears
  • 03/Sharp midday selloff reflects mounting concerns over global supply shocks and their impact on Indian markets

Indian equity markets witnessed a sharp selloff at midday on March 30, 2026. The BSE Sensex crashed 1,052.90 points (1.43%) to trade at 72,530.32.

The NSE Nifty 50 mirrored the decline, falling 304.85 points (1.34%) to 22,514.75. All sectoral indices were trading in deep red territory.

Banking stocks emerged as the biggest drag on the indices. Rising crude oil prices and bond yield concerns weighed heavily on lender valuations.

Crude oil surged past $115 per barrel, stoking inflation fears across emerging markets. Higher energy costs threaten to widen India's trade deficit further.

The sharp correction comes amid global risk-off sentiment. Investors are fleeing to safer assets as geopolitical tensions escalate supply chain disruptions.

Midday volumes remained elevated, indicating sustained selling pressure. Foreign portfolio outflows likely accelerated the decline in benchmark indices.

The correction erases recent gains for Indian equities. Year-to-date returns for the Sensex have turned sharply negative following today's plunge.
