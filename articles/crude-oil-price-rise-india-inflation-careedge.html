Crude Oil Price Rise May Add 60 Bps To India's Inflation: CareEdge

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Every $10 per barrel increase in crude oil prices could add 60 basis points to India's inflation according to CareEdge Global
  • 02/Oil marketing companies may initially absorb some cost increases to shield consumers from immediate price hikes
  • 03/Sustained high crude prices could eventually result in higher consumer prices and increased inflation

Every $10 rise in crude oil prices could add 60 basis points to India's inflation. The estimate comes from CareEdge Global, a leading credit rating agency.

Revati Kasture, CEO of CareEdge Global, shared the analysis. She highlighted the direct link between global crude prices and domestic inflation.

Oil marketing companies may absorb some initial cost increases. This strategy helps shield consumers from immediate price shocks at the pump.

However, sustained high crude prices cannot be absorbed indefinitely. Companies will eventually pass these costs to consumers through higher retail fuel prices.

The pass-through effect extends beyond fuel costs. Transportation and logistics expenses rise, impacting prices of goods across the economy.

India imports over 85% of its crude oil requirements. This makes the economy highly sensitive to global oil market volatility.

RBI has been working to keep inflation within its 4% target. Rising crude prices pose a significant challenge to these efforts.

"While oil marketing companies might initially absorb some costs, sustained high prices could result in higher consumer prices and increased inflation" - Revati Kasture, CEO, CareEdge Global
