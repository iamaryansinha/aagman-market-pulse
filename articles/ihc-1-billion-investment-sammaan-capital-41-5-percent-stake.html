IHC Invests $1 Billion to Acquire 41.5% Stake in Sammaan Capital

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IHC is acquiring 41.5% stake in Sammaan Capital through a structured $1 billion deal
  • 02/First tranche of ₹5,652 crore ($600 million) already received; remaining ₹3,198 crore over 18 months
  • 03/Open offer at ₹139/share could take IHC stake to 63.3%

Sammaan Capital Ltd has secured a major capital infusion from International Holding Company (IHC), one of the Middle East's largest investment conglomerates. The deal positions IHC as the new promoter of the non-banking financial company.

IHC is executing the acquisition through its affiliate Avenir Investment RSC Limited. The structure involves a preferential allotment of equity shares and warrants. Sammaan Capital has already received the first tranche of ₹5,652 crore, equivalent to approximately $600 million.

An additional ₹3,198 crore, or around $338 million, is scheduled for infusion over the next 18 months. This second tranche will be released upon conversion of warrants into fully paid equity shares.

The company disclosed in its stock exchange filing that all requisite regulatory approvals have been secured. Approximately 26.9% stake has already been acquired. The remaining portion will be completed in phases through warrant conversion.

The open offer has been announced at ₹139 per fully paid equity share. Assuming full uptake in the open offer, IHC's stake through Avenir will expand to 63.3%.

Sammaan Capital stated that the partnership with IHC aligns with its long-term growth ambitions. The capital injection strengthens its position among India's well-capitalised financial institutions.
