Nifty, Sensex Plunge 10.5% in March 2026: Worst Monthly Fall Since Covid

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nifty 50 and Sensex have declined approximately 10.5% each in March 2026
  • 02/This marks the worst monthly performance since the Covid-induced crash of March 2020
  • 03/Investors brace for continued volatility as global and domestic headwinds persist

Indian equity benchmarks Nifty 50 and Sensex are heading for their worst monthly decline in six years. Both indices have shed approximately 10.5% during March 2026.

The sharp fall mirrors the pandemic-induced crash of March 2020. That month, markets plummeted as lockdowns triggered panic selling across global exchanges.

The current selloff stems from multiple factors. Rising geopolitical tensions, stubborn inflation, and foreign institutional investor outflows have dampened sentiment.

FII selling has accelerated through the month. Overseas investors have pulled out over ₹35,000 crore from Indian equities in March alone.

Broader markets have fared worse than the benchmarks. Midcap and smallcap indices have declined 12-15% as risk appetite evaporated.

Sectorally, banking and IT stocks led the decline. The Nifty Bank index has fallen 11% while the IT index dropped 9%.

Analysts remain cautious on near-term outlook. "Markets are pricing in slower growth and tighter liquidity conditions," said a Nomura strategist.

The volatility is expected to persist. Investors are advised to focus on quality stocks with strong balance sheets and consistent cash flows.

WHEN WAS THE LAST TIME MARKETS FELL THIS SHARPLY?
THE LAST TIME INDIAN MARKETS SAW SUCH A STEEP MONTHLY DECLINE WAS IN MARCH 2020 DURING THE COVID-19 PANDEMIC CRASH.
WHAT IS DRIVING THE CURRENT MARKET SELLOFF?
THE SELLOFF IS DRIVEN BY A COMBINATION OF RISING GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS, PERSISTENT INFLATION CONCERNS, AND HEAVY FOREIGN INSTITUTIONAL INVESTOR OUTFLOWS EXCEEDING ₹35,000 CRORE IN MARCH.
WHICH SECTORS HAVE BEEN HIT THE HARDEST?
BANKING AND IT STOCKS HAVE LED THE DECLINE WITH THE NIFTY BANK INDEX FALLING 11% AND THE IT INDEX DROPPING 9%. MIDCAP AND SMALLCAP INDICES HAVE DECLINED 12-15%.
WHAT SHOULD INVESTORS DO IN THIS MARKET ENVIRONMENT?
INVESTORS SHOULD FOCUS ON QUALITY STOCKS WITH STRONG BALANCE SHEETS, CONSISTENT CASH FLOWS, AND AVOID PANIC SELLING. VOLATILITY IS EXPECTED TO PERSIST IN THE NEAR TERM.
