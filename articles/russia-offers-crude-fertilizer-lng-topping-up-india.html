Russia Offers to Top Up Crude, Fertiliser, LNG Supplies to India Amid West Asia Conflict

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Russia has offered to increase supplies of crude oil, fertilisers and LNG to India
  • 02/Offer comes amid escalating West Asia conflict and potential Hormuz Strait disruption
  • 03/India is deepening energy reliance on Russia as alternative to West Asian sources

Russia has offered to top up supplies of crude oil, fertilisers and LNG to India. The offer comes amid escalating conflict in West Asia.

The Hormuz Strait disruption risk has pushed India to diversify energy sources. Russia is positioned as a reliable alternative supplier.

India already imports over 40% of its crude oil from Russia. The discounted Russian crude has helped manage import bills.

Fertiliser supplies are critical for India's agricultural sector. Russia is a major global producer of urea and potash.

LNG imports from Russia could help meet growing domestic gas demand. City gas distribution networks are expanding across India.

The offer aligns with India's strategy to secure energy supplies. Geopolitical uncertainties in the Middle East threaten traditional supply routes.

India's oil import bill was $135 billion in FY25. Russian crude has provided significant cost savings.

THE OFFER COMES AS POTENTIAL DISRUPTION TO THE HORMUZ STRAIT THREATENS TRADITIONAL WEST ASIAN ENERGY SUPPLIES, PUSHING INDIA TO DEEPEN RELIANCE ON RUSSIAN SOURCES.

RUSSIA IS A MAJOR GLOBAL PRODUCER OF UREA AND POTASH, MAKING ITS FERTILISER SUPPLIES CRITICAL FOR INDIA'S AGRICULTURAL SECTOR AND FOOD SECURITY.
