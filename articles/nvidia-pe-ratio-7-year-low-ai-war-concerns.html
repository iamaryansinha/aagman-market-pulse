Nvidia Valuation Hits 7-Year Low as AI Demand Fears, Tariff War Deepen Selloff

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nvidia stock fell 2.2% on Friday as Wall Street selloff deepens amid tariff war and AI demand concerns
  • 02/Valuation metric PE ratio sinks to lowest level in seven years, reflecting investor pessimism on growth outlook
  • 03/Shares on track to lose approximately 10% for the first quarter of 2026

Nvidia Corporation (NASDAQ: NVDA) declined 2.2% on Friday as broader Wall Street weakness weighed on semiconductor stocks. The chipmaker is set to lose roughly 10% in the first quarter of 2026.

The stock's price-to-earnings (PE) ratio has dropped to a seven-year low. This signals deepening investor anxiety over AI demand sustainability and geopolitical tensions.

Trade war fears continue to pressure the technology sector. Tariff uncertainty has created volatility across chipmakers with significant China exposure.

Nvidia remains the dominant player in AI accelerators with over 80% market share. However, investor concerns about Datacenter spending cycles have intensified.

The seven-year low valuation suggests markets are pricing in a significant growth slowdown. Some analysts view this as a potential entry point for long-term investors.

The broader semiconductor index has underperformed the S&P 500 this quarter. AI-related stocks have faced particular pressure amid questions about return on investment.

Nvidia reports quarterly earnings next month. Guidance commentary will be critical for determining whether the selloff represents opportunity or continued risk.
