Crude Oil Prices Surge As Trump Threatens Iran Over Hormuz Strait Deadline

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Brent crude futures rise 1.44% to $111.35 and WTI jumps 2.66% to $115.40 as Trump threatens Iran with intensified attacks
  • 02/Trump sets Tuesday 8 pm EDT deadline for Iran to open Strait of Hormuz; warns Iran could be 'taken out' in one night
  • 03/MCX crude oil futures trade at ₹10,781, up 1.82%; investors watch for Iran's response and potential Gulf energy infrastructure attacks

Crude oil futures surged on Tuesday as US President Donald Trump issued an ultimatum to Iran. Trump threatened intensified attacks if Iran did not agree to open the Strait of Hormuz by 8 pm EDT Tuesday.

At 9:19 am IST, June Brent oil futures traded at $111.35, up 1.44%. May WTI crude futures climbed 2.66% to $115.40.

On MCX, April crude oil futures traded at ₹10,781 against the previous close of ₹10,588. This marks a 1.82% increase during the initial trading hour.

May MCX crude futures rose 1.38% to ₹9,444 from the previous close of ₹9,315.

Trump addressed a press conference with stark warnings. He stated Iran could be 'taken out' in one night. That night might be Tuesday night, he added.

US armed forces have conducted over 10,000 combat flights over Iran in the past 37 days. These strikes have targeted more than 13,000 locations.

Trump claimed regime change in Iran had resulted in a more moderate government. He said he had given Iran opportunities to end the war, but these were not accepted.

Iran responded with caution and threats of its own. It warned of retaliation if the US attacks Iranian infrastructure. Iran also threatened to attack energy infrastructure across the Gulf region.

Iran rejected a temporary ceasefire proposed by mediators. It sought a permanent end to hostilities and urged for sanctions to be lifted.

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil supply. Any disruption impacts energy markets worldwide.
