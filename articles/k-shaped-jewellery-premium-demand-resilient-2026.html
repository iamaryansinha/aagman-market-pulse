K-Shaped Recovery in Jewellery: Premium Demand Strong as Mass Market Squeezes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's jewellery demand splits into two tracks — premium high-carat jewellery resilient, mass market shifts to lighter pieces and alternatives
  • 02/Gold prices surge 50%+ YoY to ₹1.45-1.59 lakh/10g for Akshaya Tritiya 2026 vs ₹96,000-97,000 in 2025
  • 03/Listed jewellers report 37-51% revenue growth in Q3 FY2026 despite volume decline, driven by price-led increases

India's jewellery market is exhibiting a classic K-shaped demand pattern. Premium high-end jewellery continues to see healthy buying while the mass market grapples with severe affordability constraints.

Gold prices have breached ₹1.59 lakh per 10 grams in April 2026. This represents a 50%+ jump from Akshaya Tritiya 2025 levels of ₹96,000-97,000 per 10 grams.

The K-shaped dynamic is stark. High-net-worth buyers remain largely unaffected by price increases. They continue purchasing premium high-carat, high-end jewellery with minimal compromise on design or weight.

Middle-class and budget-conscious consumers are responding differently. Many have shifted to lighter-weight gold jewellery, lower purity options (18k and 14k), or postponed discretionary purchases entirely.

Listed jewellery retailers reported strong revenue growth of 37-51% year-on-year in the October-December 2025 quarter. However, this growth was largely price-led rather than volume-driven.

Exchange schemes now drive over 40% of jewellery sales at several retailers. Consumers are trading old jewellery for new purchases to manage costs.

Lab-grown diamonds and silver jewellery have emerged as winners. Both categories offer fine jewellery aesthetics at accessible price points, attracting younger and first-time buyers.

The World Gold Council notes that jewellery demand remains resilient in value terms despite volume pressures. India's stronger macroeconomic outlook differentiates it from China where demand has softened more significantly.

Industry executives expect the K-shaped trend to persist through 2026. Brands are adjusting inventory toward lighter gold designs, lab-grown diamonds, and silver to capture the budget-conscious segment.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE K-SHAPED TREND IN INDIA'S JEWELLERY MARKET?
THE K-SHAPED TREND REFERS TO THE DIVERGENT DEMAND PATTERN WHERE PREMIUM HIGH-END JEWELLERY REMAINS RESILIENT AMONG AFFLUENT BUYERS WHILE THE MASS MARKET FACES SEVERE AFFORDABILITY CONSTRAINTS. HIGH-NET-WORTH CONSUMERS CONTINUE BUYING HIGH-CARAT PIECES, WHILE MIDDLE-CLASS BUYERS SHIFT TO LIGHTER DESIGNS OR ALTERNATIVES.
HOW MUCH HAVE GOLD PRICES RISEN COMPARED TO LAST YEAR?
GOLD PRICES HAVE SURGED OVER 50% YEAR-ON-YEAR. FOR AKSHAYA TRITIYA 2026, 24-CARAT GOLD IS TRADING BETWEEN ₹1.45 LAKH TO ₹1.59 LAKH PER 10 GRAMS, COMPARED TO ₹96,000-₹97,000 PER 10 GRAMS IN APRIL 2025.
HOW ARE LISTED JEWELLERS PERFORMING DESPITE HIGH PRICES?
LISTED JEWELLERS REPORTED STRONG REVENUE GROWTH OF 37-51% YEAR-ON-YEAR IN THE OCTOBER-DECEMBER 2025 QUARTER. HOWEVER, THIS GROWTH IS PRIMARILY PRICE-LED, AS VOLUMES HAVE DECLINED WHILE AVERAGE SELLING PRICES INCREASED BY OVER 15% DURING THE PERIOD.
WHAT ALTERNATIVES ARE CONSUMERS TURNING TO?
CONSUMERS ARE INCREASINGLY OPTING FOR LIGHTER-WEIGHT GOLD JEWELLERY, LOWER PURITY OPTIONS (18K AND 14K), LAB-GROWN DIAMONDS, AND SILVER JEWELLERY. EXCHANGE SCHEMES — WHERE OLD JEWELLERY IS TRADED FOR NEW — NOW ACCOUNT FOR OVER 40% OF SALES AT SOME RETAILERS.
WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK FOR JEWELLERY DEMAND IN 2026?
THE K-SHAPED TREND IS EXPECTED TO PERSIST THROUGH 2026. PREMIUM DEMAND SHOULD REMAIN STEADY, WHILE THE MASS MARKET WILL LIKELY CONTINUE SHIFTING TOWARD AFFORDABLE ALTERNATIVES. BRANDS ARE ADJUSTING INVENTORY MIX TOWARD LIGHTER GOLD, LAB-GROWN DIAMONDS, AND SILVER TO CAPTURE BUDGET-CONSCIOUS SEGMENTS.
