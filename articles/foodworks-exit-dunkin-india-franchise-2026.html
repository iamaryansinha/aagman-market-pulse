FoodWorks to Exit Dunkin' India Franchise Agreement After 2026

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/FoodWorks announces plan to exit Dunkin' India franchise agreement after 2026 calendar year ends
  • 02/Company evaluating operational rationalisation or sale of franchise rights in consultation with brand owners
  • 03/Exit reflects strategic portfolio realignment amid challenging competitive QSR market conditions

FoodWorks has announced plans to exit its Dunkin' India franchise agreement. The company will cease operations after the 2026 calendar year concludes.

The decision was disclosed through an exchange filing on March 30, 2026. The company is actively exploring multiple strategic options for the transition.

These options include operational rationalisation of existing Dunkin' outlets. FoodWorks may also pursue the sale of franchise rights in consultation with the brand's owners.

Dunkin' entered India over a decade ago to tap the growing coffee market. The brand has faced intense competition from established players like Starbucks and Cafe Coffee Day.

The quick-service restaurant (QSR) segment in India remains highly competitive. Rising real estate costs and changing consumer preferences have pressured margins for several chains.

FoodWorks' exit signals a strategic portfolio realignment. The company appears to be focusing resources on more profitable or scalable business verticals.

The move will be closely watched by investors tracking QSR sector trends. It highlights the challenges foreign brands face in building sustainable India operations.
