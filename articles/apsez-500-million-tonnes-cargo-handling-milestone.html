APSEZ Crosses 500 Million Tonnes Cargo Handling Milestone, Eyes 1 Billion By 2030

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/APSEZ achieves landmark milestone of handling over 500 million tonnes of cargo across its port network
  • 02/Company evolved from single port vision in 1998 to 19 ports and terminals across India and overseas today
  • 03/Targeting 1 billion tonnes annual cargo handling by 2030 as next major milestone

Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone Ltd (APSEZ) (NSE: ADANIPORTS) has crossed the 500 million tonnes cargo handling milestone. The achievement marks a defining moment in the company's growth journey.

APSEZ started with a single port-led vision in 1998. It has now built a network of 19 ports and terminals across India and overseas. The company took 16 years to achieve its first 100 million tonnes.

Each subsequent milestone was achieved at a faster pace. This reflects accelerating scale and execution capabilities at APSEZ.

Gautam Adani, chairman of Adani Group, said: "APSEZ crossing 500 million tonnes is a signal of our decades-long confidence in the India growth story. Over the years, we have built not just ports, but an unparalleled interconnected transport and logistics platform spanning ports, rail, roads, trucking, warehouses and cargo gateways."

The company has developed an integrated logistics infrastructure network. This spans ports, rail, roads, trucking, warehouses and cargo gateways.

APSEZ remains firmly on track to achieve its long-term target. The company aims to handle 1 billion tonnes of cargo annually by 2030.
