MCX in Focus as NSE Launches Brent Crude Futures; 20 Stocks to Watch Today

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NSE to launch Dated Brent Crude Oil futures from April 13, putting MCX in focus amid competition concerns
  • 02/TVS Holdings invests ₹526.79 crore in Home Credit India; JSW Steel completes BPSL business transfer
  • 03/Oberoi Realty signs Peddar Road redevelopment; RailTel bags ₹444 crore order

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) will launch Dated Brent Crude Oil futures from April 13, 2026. This expands NSE's commodity derivatives offerings linked to global oil benchmarks.

MCX (NSE: MCX) will remain in focus as the new launch intensifies competition. SEBI has approved the new contracts for the commodity derivatives segment.

TVS Holdings (NSE: TVSHOLDING) has invested ₹526.79 crore in subsidiary Home Credit India. The company subscribed to over 22.91 crore equity shares at ₹22.99 per share.

The investment increases TVS Holdings' stake in Home Credit India to 80.39%. This strengthens its position in the consumer finance segment.

JSW Steel (NSE: JSWSTEEL) has completed the transfer of Bhushan Power and Steel's steel business undertaking. The assets moved to subsidiary JSW Sambalpur Steel Limited via slump sale.

This move paves the way for a strategic 50:50 joint venture with Japan's JFE Steel Corporation. The partnership was approved by the JSW Steel board in December 2025.

Oberoi Realty (NSE: OBEROIRLTY) has signed a redevelopment agreement on Peddar Road. The project covers two adjacent housing societies spanning approximately 4,800 sq m.

RailTel Corporation (NSE: RAILTEL) has received a Letter of Intent from Centre For E-Governance. The estimated order size is ₹444.44 crore including tax.

BSE (NSE: BSE) faces regulatory headwinds as NCLAT dismissed its appeals. The tribunal upheld NCLT's power to defreeze Demat accounts of companies undergoing insolvency.

PC Jeweller (NSE: PCJEWELLER) executed an MoU with National Skill Development Corporation. The partnership aims to develop 2 lakh micro-entrepreneurs over five years.

Coal India (NSE: COALINDIA) received a ₹1,057 crore Letter of Award from Telangana Power Generation Corporation. The project involves setting up a 750 MWh battery energy storage system.
