Govt Clears ₹7,104 Cr Electronics Component Investment Proposals Under PLI Scheme

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government clears 29 investment proposals worth ₹7,104 crore under the Electronics Component Manufacturing Scheme
  • 02/New applications expected to generate electronics component production worth ₹84,515 crore
  • 03/Approved under the Production Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme to boost domestic manufacturing

The government has approved 29 investment proposals worth ₹7,104 crore under the Electronics Component Manufacturing Scheme. The clearance was announced on March 30, 2026.

The new applications will lead to production of electronics components worth ₹84,515 crore. This includes semiconductors, display fabs, and other critical components.

Meity Secretary S Krishnan confirmed the approval details. The scheme falls under the broader Production Linked Incentive (PLI) framework for electronics manufacturing.

India has been aggressively pushing domestic electronics manufacturing to reduce import dependence. The PLI scheme offers incentives based on incremental sales of goods manufactured in India.

The electronics component sector is critical for India's goal of becoming a global manufacturing hub. Component manufacturing has higher value addition compared to assembly operations.

The ₹7,104 crore investment is expected to create significant employment opportunities. It will also strengthen the domestic supply chain for electronics manufacturers.
