Goldman Sachs Downgrades India To 'Market Weight', Cuts Nifty Target To 25,900 On Oil Shock Risk

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Goldman Sachs downgrades Indian equities to 'market weight' from 'overweight', cuts 12-month Nifty target to 25,900 from 29,300
  • 02/Warns of "energy-shock-led" earnings downgrade cycle as US-Iran conflict drives higher-for-longer oil prices
  • 03/2026 GDP growth cut by 1.1pp to 5.9%, earnings growth lowered to 8%/13% for CY26/27 from 16%/14% previously

Goldman Sachs has downgraded Indian equities to "market weight" from "overweight". The global brokerage also slashed its 12-month Nifty 50 target to 25,900 from 29,300 previously.

The downgrade reflects concerns over an "energy-shock-led" earnings downgrade cycle. The firm expects elevated oil prices amid the US-Iran conflict to pressure corporate earnings over the next few quarters.

Goldman now forecasts Brent crude to average $105 in March and $115 in April. Prices are expected to taper gradually to $80 by Q4 2026.

"Within Asia, India stands out as particularly vulnerable to potential energy shortages," the report notes. India is a lower per capita income economy with high energy import dependence.

Since the Gulf war began in late February, Goldman has lowered India's 2026 GDP growth forecast by 1.1 percentage points to 5.9%. CPI inflation has been raised by 70 basis points.

The current account deficit is now projected at 2% of GDP. The brokerage has also added 50 basis points of rate hikes to its 2026 forecast, with year-end repo rate seen at 5.75%.

Goldman's VAR-based analysis suggests that if oil averages $45 per barrel higher for 3 months, India's full-year earnings growth could drop by approximately 9%. This impact exceeds the 6% hit expected for broader Asia.

The firm has cut its earnings growth forecast materially for India by 9 percentage points cumulatively over the next 2 years. MSCI India earnings growth is now projected at 8% for CY26 and 13% for CY27, down from 16%/14% prior to the Iran conflict.

"We anticipate significant cuts to consensus earnings forecasts over the next 2-3 quarters," the strategists wrote. Goldman is now about 11 percentage points below consensus cumulatively over the next 2 years.

Historical analysis shows MSCI India's earnings got downgraded by 6-13% (median 7%) in the 12 months after past oil-supply disruptions. These include the 2011 Libyan civil war, 2012 EU oil embargo on Iran, and 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine.

"Forward returns have been muted at starting multiples of 18-20x when earnings are in a downgrade cycle," Goldman noted. The fair value P/E multiple has been lowered to 19.5x from 20.8x previously.

The implied 13% return in INR terms over the next 12 months trails the 19% USD upside expectation for broader Asia. Goldman cautions that the path of returns is likely to be back-loaded.

"We see risks tilted to the downside in the next 3 to 6 months," the report states. The market may not be fully pricing in the extent of earnings downgrades.

Sectorally, Goldman remains overweight banks, defensive consumption (staples), telecom, defence, and upstream energy. It has upgraded upstream energy (refiners and E&P) to overweight on tight refining capacity.

Downstream oil marketing companies (OMCs) have been cut from overweight to underweight. The firm cites limited pass-through of higher crude prices at gas stations.

Autos and durables are lowered from overweight to marketweight. Demand is sensitive to higher inflation and interest rates. Margins face vulnerability from higher input and logistics costs.

NBFCs are also cut to marketweight on rising cost of funds in a tightening environment. Goldman stays underweight on industrials, chemicals, IT, and pharma.

Earlier this week, Bernstein had also reduced Nifty's year-end target to 26,000. It flagged the risk of the headline index falling to as low as 19,000 in a worst-case scenario.
