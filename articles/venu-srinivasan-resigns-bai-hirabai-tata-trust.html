Venu Srinivasan Resigns From Bai Hirabai Tata Trust Amid Eligibility Dispute

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Venu Srinivasan resigns from Bai Hirabai Jamsetji Tata Navsari Charitable Institution on April 4, 2026, citing other business commitments
  • 02/Resignation follows former trustee Mehli Mistry's legal challenge questioning eligibility of Srinivasan and Vijay Singh
  • 03/Mistry claims trustees must be Parsi Zoroastrian faith and Mumbai residents per Trust Deed clauses 6 and 18

Venu Srinivasan has resigned from the Bai Hirabai Jamsetji Tata Navsari Charitable Institution. The TVS Motor Company Chairman Emeritus stepped down on April 4, 2026.

Srinivasan cited preoccupation with other business commitments as the reason for his departure. He also serves as Vice Chairman of seven Tata Group trusts.

The resignation follows an escalating governance dispute within the trust. Former trustee Mehli Mistry has challenged the eligibility of Srinivasan and fellow trustee Vijay Singh.

Mistry filed an objection application with the Maharashtra Charity Commissioner. He contends the two trustees never met qualifications required under the Trust Deed.

The dispute centers on Clauses 6 and 18 of the Trust Deed. These clauses require trustees to be of Parsi Zoroastrian faith and permanent Mumbai residents.

Mistry argues Srinivasan and Singh do not meet these criteria. Under the Trust Deed, any disqualified trustee must be treated as "deemed dead."

The Bai Hirabai Jamsetji Tata Navsari Charitable Institution was established in 1923. It focuses on Parsi and community welfare in Navsari, Gujarat, particularly education and healthcare.

The trust shares a common board of trustees with the Sir Ratan Tata Trust. It is among the allied Tata Trusts that control 66% of Tata Sons.

This marks Mistry's second governance challenge in recent months. In February 2026, he objected to a proposal to scrap vice-chairman positions across Tata Trusts.

Srinivasan's exit adds fresh tension to one of India's most influential philanthropic networks. The Tata Trusts hold controlling stake in the $165 billion Tata Group.
