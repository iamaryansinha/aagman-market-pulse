Bajaj Alternate Investment Management Secures SEBI PMS Licence

April 6, 2026 | 3 min read

TL;DR

  • • Bajaj Alts receives SEBI PMS licence to offer customised portfolio management services
  • • Targeting HNIs and UHNIs with equity, debt, and hybrid strategies
  • • Expands from AIFs to create diversified investment platform; joins competitive PMS market

Bajaj Alternate Investment Management has secured a Portfolio Management Services (PMS) licence from SEBI, marking the Bajaj group's formal entry into the rapidly growing discretionary investment management space.

The licence allows Bajaj Alts to offer customised investment strategies for high-net-worth individuals and ultra-HNIs, complementing its existing Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) offerings.

Strategic Expansion

The PMS licence represents a strategic expansion for Bajaj Alts, which has been primarily focused on AIFs. PMS allows for greater flexibility and customisation compared to mutual funds, with investment minimums starting at ₹50 lakh.

The company plans to offer equity, debt, and hybrid strategies tailored to client risk profiles and return objectives.

Competitive Landscape

Bajaj Alts enters a competitive PMS market dominated by established players like ASK Investment Managers, Motilal Oswal PMS, and newer entrants like Dezerv and Unifi Capital.

India's PMS industry manages over ₹30 lakh crore across more than 1,000 registered portfolio managers, with the segment growing at 15-20% annually.

Management Commentary

Lakshmi Iyer, CEO of Bajaj Alts, said the PMS licence allows the firm to offer "bespoke investment solutions" to sophisticated investors seeking differentiated strategies beyond traditional products.

CIO Jitendra Gohil noted that the firm's research capabilities and Bajaj brand trust would be key differentiators in attracting clients.
