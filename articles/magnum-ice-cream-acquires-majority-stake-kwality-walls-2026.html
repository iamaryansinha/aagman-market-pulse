Magnum Ice Cream Acquires 61.9% Stake in Kwality Wall's, Appoints New Chairman

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Magnum Ice Cream Netherlands acquired 61.9% majority stake in Kwality Wall's (India) Ltd
  • 02/Transaction completed March 30, 2026; involves 145.44 crore equity shares from outgoing promoters Unilever
  • 03/Abhijit Bhattacharya appointed Chairman; Ritesh Tiwari resigns from board

Magnum Ice Cream Netherlands has completed the acquisition of a 61.9% majority stake in Kwality Wall's (India) Ltd (KWIL), marking a significant change in ownership structure for the ice cream manufacturer.

The transaction was executed on March 30, 2026, when Magnum acquired the sale shares from outgoing promoters in accordance with the Share Purchase Agreement (SPA) and other applicable laws, KWIL disclosed in a regulatory filing.

The acquisition involves 145.44 crore equity shares, representing 61.9% of the equity share capital of the company. The shares were acquired from outgoing promoters Unilever.

The SPA was originally executed on June 25, 2025. Parties to the agreement include The Magnum Ice Cream Company Netherlands BV and Unilever PLC, among others.

Following the completion of this transaction, Magnum has been classified as the "promoter" of the company under Listing Regulations. The outgoing promoters have been reclassified from the "promoter" category to the public category.

Alongside the ownership change, KWIL has announced board-level appointments. Abhijit Bhattacharya has been approved as an Additional (Non-Executive and Non-Independent) Director of the company and Chairman of the board.

Ritesh Tiwari has resigned as Additional (Non-Executive and Non-Independent) Director of the company.

The development signals a major restructuring of KWIL's ownership and governance framework under the new promoter group.
