HAL Delivers 4 ALH Mk III Helicopters To Indian Coast Guard

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/HAL delivers 4 ALH Mk III helicopters to Indian Coast Guard on April 10, 2026, ahead of schedule
  • 02/Delivery is part of ₹2,901 crore contract signed March 2026 for 6 maritime role helicopters
  • 03/Remaining 2 helicopters expected by year-end; contract involves 200+ MSMEs and 6.5 million man-hours

Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (NSE: HAL) has delivered 4 ALH Mk III helicopters to the Indian Coast Guard. The handover took place on April 10, 2026.

This delivery is part of a ₹2,901 crore contract signed on March 3, 2026. The Ministry of Defence awarded HAL the order for 6 Advanced Light Helicopters in Maritime Role (MR) configuration.

The contract includes operational equipment, engineering support, and performance-based logistics. It was signed under the Buy (Indian-IDDM) category.

The ALH Mk III helicopters are twin-engine platforms designed for maritime security missions. They can operate from shore bases and ship flight decks.

These helicopters feature advanced surveillance radar, Electronic Warfare suite, and medical intensive care units. They will support search-and-rescue, coastal patrolling, and offshore installation protection.

The project involves over 200 MSMEs in the supply chain. It will generate approximately 6.5 million man-hours of employment.

HAL shares closed at ₹4,024 on April 10, 2026. The stock has gained 18% over the past year despite recent volatility.

The remaining 2 helicopters are scheduled for delivery by December 2026. This order reinforces HAL's position as India's leading aerospace and defence manufacturer.

THE CONTRACT IS VALUED AT ₹2,901 CRORE AND INCLUDES 6 ALH MK-III HELICOPTERS WITH OPERATIONAL EQUIPMENT, ENGINEERING SUPPORT PACKAGE, AND PERFORMANCE-BASED LOGISTICS SUPPORT.
WHAT ARE THE KEY FEATURES OF THE ALH MK III HELICOPTERS?
THE ALH MK III IS A TWIN-ENGINE HELICOPTER WITH ADVANCED SURVEILLANCE RADAR, ELECTRONIC WARFARE SUITE, AND MEDICAL INTENSIVE CARE UNIT. IT CAN OPERATE FROM BOTH SHORE BASES AND SHIPS FOR MARITIME SECURITY MISSIONS.
UNDER WHICH PROCUREMENT CATEGORY WAS THIS CONTRACT SIGNED?
THE CONTRACT WAS SIGNED UNDER THE BUY (INDIAN-IDDM) CATEGORY, WHICH STANDS FOR INDIGENOUSLY DESIGNED, DEVELOPED AND MANUFACTURED, SUPPORTING THE AATMANIRBHAR BHARAT INITIATIVE.
WHEN WILL THE REMAINING HELICOPTERS BE DELIVERED?
THE REMAINING 2 HELICOPTERS UNDER THE CONTRACT ARE EXPECTED TO BE DELIVERED TO THE INDIAN COAST GUARD BY DECEMBER 2026.
