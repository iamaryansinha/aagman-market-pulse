Rentomojo DRHP Filing: Accel-Backed Rental Firm Plans ₹1,100 Crore IPO

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rentomojo files DRHP with SEBI for an IPO valued at ₹1,100-1,200 crore, comprising fresh issue and offer for sale
  • 02/Accel India will offload 78.47 lakh shares via OFS; founder Geetansh Bamania to sell 20.07 lakh shares
  • 03/Company reported operating revenue of ₹177 crore and net profit of ₹61 crore in H1 FY26

Rentomojo has filed its draft red herring prospectus with SEBI for an IPO. The furniture rental startup plans to raise ₹1,100-1,200 crore through the public offering.

The IPO comprises a fresh issue of shares worth ₹150 crore. The remaining comprises an offer for sale of up to 2.84 crore shares by existing investors.

Accel India will sell 78.47 lakh shares via the OFS route. Chiratae Ventures plans to offload 29.03 lakh shares, while Edelweiss will sell 32.03 lakh shares.

Founder and CEO Geetansh Bamania will sell 20.07 lakh shares in the offering. He holds over 14% stake in the Bengaluru-based company.

Rentomojo will utilise ₹70 crore from the fresh issue to repay debt. Another ₹42.5 crore will fund lease payments for warehouses and offline centres.

The company operates a subscription-based rental model for furniture and appliances. It runs 21 warehouses across 22 Indian cities with 7.29 lakh products.

For H1 FY26, Rentomojo posted operating revenue of ₹176.6 crore. Net profit stood at ₹61.4 crore, including a deferred tax credit of ₹32.8 crore.

Full-year FY25 revenue rose 38% YoY to ₹266 crore. Net profit surged 92% to ₹43.1 crore from ₹22.4 crore in FY24.

Merchant bankers Motilal Oswal, Axis Capital and IIFL Capital are managing the IPO. The company converted to a public limited entity last month ahead of the filing.

Founded in 2014, Rentomojo has raised ₹400 crore from investors. Its last funding round in February 2024 valued the company at ₹850-900 crore.
