Pharma Sector Q4FY26 Preview: HDFC Expects Steady Revenue But Margin Pressure To Continue

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/HDF Securities projects 15% YoY revenue growth for India pharma coverage universe versus IPM growth of ~12% in Q4FY26
  • 02/EBITDA margins expected to remain under pressure due to higher input costs, US pricing pressure, and absence of high-margin gRevlimid opportunity
  • 03/CRDMO business likely to sustain margins as new capacities mature; hospital segment faces pressure from lower international payor mix

Indian pharmaceutical and healthcare companies are expected to report steady revenue growth in Q4FY26. However, margin pressure will likely persist across the sector, according to HDF Securities' preview report.

The Indian Pharma Market (IPM) grew 12% year-on-year in January-February 2026. This was driven by 16% growth in the chronic segment and approximately 9% growth in the acute segment, per IQVIA data.

HDF Securities expects its coverage universe to clock 15% YoY growth in India business. Companies like Sun Pharma and Zydus are driving traction through specialty portfolios and chronic therapies.

Margins remain the key concern for the sector. Multiple headwinds are converging this quarter, including rising input costs and pricing pressures in the US market.

The absence of the high-margin generic Revlimid opportunity will hit year-on-year comparisons. Steady R&D spends and higher SG&A costs, including increased freight expenses, will further compress margins.

The Contract Research and Development Manufacturing Organization (CRDMO) segment is expected to sustain margins. New capacities are maturing, providing operational leverage.

Hospital companies face pressure from lower international payor mix and costs associated with new bed additions. However, steady occupancy rates and ARPOB growth provide some cushion.

Sun Pharma (NSE: SUNPHARMA) is expected to lead with 13% revenue growth. The company's specialty portfolio continues to drive strong performance in both India and US markets.

Lupin (NSE: LUPIN) is projected to see 25% YoY revenue growth. Its US business momentum continues with key products like Tolvaptan and gSpiriva.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT REVENUE GROWTH DOES HDF SECURITIES EXPECT FOR INDIAN PHARMA COMPANIES IN Q4FY26?
HDF SECURITIES PROJECTS 15% YEAR-ON-YEAR REVENUE GROWTH FOR ITS INDIA PHARMA COVERAGE UNIVERSE. THIS COMPARES TO THE INDIAN PHARMA MARKET (IPM) GROWTH OF APPROXIMATELY 12% IN JANUARY-FEBRUARY 2026.
WHY ARE PHARMA MARGINS EXPECTED TO REMAIN UNDER PRESSURE?
MARGINS FACE PRESSURE FROM MULTIPLE FACTORS: INCREASING INPUT COSTS, PRICING PRESSURES IN THE US BUSINESS, ABSENCE OF HIGH-MARGIN GENERIC REVLIMID OPPORTUNITIES, STEADY R&D EXPENDITURES, AND HIGHER SG&A COSTS INCLUDING INCREASED FREIGHT EXPENSES.
WHICH PHARMA SEGMENTS ARE EXPECTED TO SUSTAIN MARGINS?
THE CRDMO (CONTRACT RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT MANUFACTURING ORGANIZATION) BUSINESS IS EXPECTED TO SUSTAIN MARGINS AS NEW CAPACITIES MATURE AND ACHIEVE BETTER UTILIZATION.
WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK FOR HOSPITAL COMPANIES IN Q4FY26?
HOSPITAL COMPANIES COULD SEE SOME MARGIN PRESSURE DUE TO LOWER INTERNATIONAL PAYOR MIX AND COSTS ASSOCIATED WITH NEW BED ADDITIONS. HOWEVER, STEADY OCCUPANCY RATES AND ARPOB (AVERAGE REVENUE PER OCCUPIED BED) GROWTH PROVIDE SUPPORT.
WHICH PHARMA COMPANIES ARE EXPECTED TO LEAD GROWTH IN Q4FY26?
SUN PHARMA IS EXPECTED TO POST 13% REVENUE GROWTH DRIVEN BY ITS SPECIALTY PORTFOLIO. LUPIN IS PROJECTED TO SEE 25% YOY GROWTH WITH STRONG US BUSINESS MOMENTUM FROM PRODUCTS LIKE TOLVAPTAN AND GSPIRIVA.
