India CCTV Costs Rise 20% as Supply Chain Shifts Away From Chinese Components

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's ban on non-certified CCTV cameras effective April 1, 2026 drives 15-20% cost increase as supply chains diversify away from Chinese components
  • 02/Domestic vendors now control 80% market share; Hikvision and Dahua effectively barred from selling internet-connected cameras in India
  • 03/507 camera models certified under new STQC regime; bill of materials rising as companies source from US, Taiwan, Europe instead of China

India's CCTV market is undergoing a structural reset. The government's ban on non-certified internet-connected cameras takes effect April 1, 2026, ending a two-year transition period.

The new regulations require STQC certification and compliance with Essential Requirements (ER) under MeitY guidelines. Authorities are refusing to certify hardware with Chinese-origin chipsets or firmware.

Industry executives estimate a 15-20% increase in camera prices. The shift from low-cost Chinese components to alternatives from Taiwan, the US, and Europe is driving up the bill of materials.

Major Chinese players Hikvision and Dahua held one-third of India's market until 2024. Both brands are now effectively shut out, with Dahua's business contracting by 80%.

Domestic vendors have surged to control 80% of market share as of February 2026. Indian brands CP Plus, Qubo, Prama, Matrix, and Sparsh are expanding rapidly.

CP PLUS has diversified its chipset base, working with Qualcomm, Ambarella, and Realtek. Managing Director Aditya Khemka said the company's IP portfolio is now built on 100% non-Chinese chipsets.

"This transition has not slowed us down but has made us stronger," Khemka said. "It has enabled us to build a resilient and future-ready supply ecosystem."

The India CCTV camera market is projected at $2.42 billion in 2026. It is expected to reach $8.20 billion by 2033, implying a CAGR of 19-20%.

Only 507 CCTV camera models have received government certification under the new security framework. This represents a dramatic consolidation from hundreds of models previously available.

Dr. Ajai Chowdhry, Co-Founder of HCL and Distinguished Fellow at NITI Aayog, said the ban will encourage fabless companies to invest in design of safe and secure chips.

India does not currently have SoC chip manufacturing capability. Startups are working on it, but industry estimates suggest a three to five year timeline for real product deployment.

The policy is creating opportunities for Indian semiconductor startups. Mindgrove Technologies is working with Bosch to co-develop system-on-chip solutions for surveillance applications.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE NEW CCTV REGULATION IN INDIA STARTING APRIL 1, 2026?
THE MINISTRY OF ELECTRONICS AND INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (MEITY) HAS MANDATED THAT ALL INTERNET-CONNECTED CCTV CAMERAS SOLD IN INDIA MUST HAVE STQC CERTIFICATION AND COMPLY WITH ESSENTIAL REQUIREMENTS (ER). THE TWO-YEAR TRANSITION PERIOD ENDS ON APRIL 1, 2026, AFTER WHICH NON-COMPLIANT CAMERAS CANNOT BE LEGALLY SOLD, MANUFACTURED, OR IMPORTED.
WHY ARE CCTV CAMERA PRICES INCREASING BY 15-20%?
MANUFACTURERS ARE DIVERSIFYING SUPPLY CHAINS AWAY FROM CHINESE CHIPSETS TO SOURCES IN THE US, TAIWAN, EUROPE, AND JAPAN. THE PRICE DIFFERENTIAL BETWEEN CHINESE COMPONENTS AND NON-CHINESE ALTERNATIVES IS SUBSTANTIAL, LEADING TO HIGHER BILL OF MATERIALS COSTS THAT ARE BEING PASSED TO CONSUMERS.
WHICH CHINESE CCTV BRANDS ARE AFFECTED BY THE BAN?
HIKVISION AND DAHUA, WHICH COLLECTIVELY HELD ONE-THIRD OF THE INDIAN MARKET UNTIL 2024, ARE THE PRIMARY TARGETS. AUTHORITIES ARE EXPLICITLY REFUSING TO CERTIFY HARDWARE UTILIZING CHINESE-ORIGIN CHIPSETS OR FIRMWARE. DAHUA'S BUSINESS HAS CONTRACTED BY 80%, AND HIKVISION HAS ONLY SIX CERTIFIED MODELS THROUGH ITS INDIAN SUBSIDIARY.
WHAT INDIAN COMPANIES ARE BENEFITING FROM THE POLICY SHIFT?
DOMESTIC VENDORS NOW CONTROL 80% OF MARKET SHARE. LEADING BRANDS INCLUDE CP PLUS (ADITYA INFOTECH), SPARSH CCTV, MATRIX COMSEC, QUBO, AND PRAMA. THESE COMPANIES HAVE OBTAINED STQC CERTIFICATION AND ARE RAPIDLY EXPANDING THEIR MARKET PRESENCE AS CHINESE COMPETITORS EXIT.
HOW BIG IS THE INDIA CCTV MARKET AND WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK?
THE INDIA CCTV CAMERA MARKET IS PROJECTED AT $2.42 BILLION IN 2026 AND IS EXPECTED TO REACH $8.20 BILLION BY 2033, IMPLYING A CAGR OF 19-20%. ONLY 507 CAMERA MODELS ARE CURRENTLY CERTIFIED UNDER THE NEW FRAMEWORK, REPRESENTING A DRAMATIC CONSOLIDATION FROM THE PRE-REGULATION MARKET.
