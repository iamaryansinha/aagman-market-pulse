Dugar Finance Bags $5M Pre-Series A to Scale MSME Lending in Bharat's Hinterland

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Chennai-based NBFC Dugar Finance has closed a $5 million (₹45 crore) Pre-Series A round led by growth equity focused Category II AIF HegdInvst
  • 02/The capital fuels expansion of its secured MSME lending franchise alongside its established vehicle finance business, with eyes on tier 2-6 semi-urban and rural markets
  • 03/The company targets ₹2,000 crore AUM within 3-4 years while maintaining GNPA below 2% and RoA of 4-5%

Dugar Finance, a Chennai-headquartered non-banking financial company, has secured $5 million in Pre-Series A funding as it pivots from pure-play vehicle finance to a diversified secured lending platform targeting India's underserved Bharat markets.

The round was led by HegdInvst, a Category II Alternative Investment Fund with a mandate for growth equity investments. The capital injection comes just months after the company raised approximately $18 million (₹160 crore) in structured debt from a consortium including Symbiotics, British International Investment (BII), and multiple Indian banks in December 2025.

The funding architecture reveals a clear strategic intent: scale the secured MSME lending vertical while deepening the existing commercial and passenger vehicle finance business across tier 2-6 markets. The deployment blueprint covers four critical pillars—technology infrastructure enhancement, analytics-driven underwriting capabilities, centralized risk management systems, and senior talent acquisition across key functions.

"We are entering the next phase of growth, where diversification and institutional disciplined scaling become critical," said Ramesh Dugar, Founder and Managing Director. "Vehicle finance gave us a strong foundation, and we are now leveraging that to build a broader secured lending platform."

The geographical expansion roadmap is equally ambitious. Operating currently across six states, Dugar Finance plans to extend its footprint to ten states over the next three years alongside branch network expansion.

The unit economics present a disciplined growth story. The company targets ₹2,000 crore in assets under management within 3-4 years while maintaining gross non-performing assets below 2% and return on assets in the 4-5% range—metrics that signal a quality-over-scale approach rare in the aggressive NBFC landscape.

Beyond the capital check, HegdInvst brings strategic value through governance framework strengthening, international capital market access, and professional management team building—elements critical for institutionalizing a founder-led lending business.

The transaction underscores continued investor appetite for secured lending models targeting Bharat's credit-starved MSME segment, particularly those with demonstrated underwriting discipline and rural distribution capabilities.
