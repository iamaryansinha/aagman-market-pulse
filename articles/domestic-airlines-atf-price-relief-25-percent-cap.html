Domestic Airlines Get ATF Price Relief As Oil Companies Cap Hike At 25%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Oil marketing companies cap ATF price increase at 25% for domestic airlines, providing partial relief to carriers
  • 02/Foreign routes to bear full price increase consistent with international ATF rates, says Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas
  • 03/Move aimed at supporting domestic aviation sector amid volatile global crude oil prices

Oil marketing companies have limited the aviation turbine fuel (ATF) price increase to 25% for domestic airlines. The Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas confirmed the partial relief measure on April 1, 2026.

Foreign carriers operating international routes will pay the full increase. Their rates will align with global ATF prices.

The differential pricing structure provides breathing room to domestic airlines. These carriers have been facing margin pressures from elevated fuel costs.

ATF accounts for 35-40% of an airline's operating costs in India. Any price relief directly impacts profitability.

The decision comes as global crude oil prices remain volatile. Domestic carriers had sought government intervention to cap fuel costs.

Major airlines including IndiGo (NSE: INDIGO), Air India, and SpiceJet (NSE: SPICEJET) stand to benefit. The 25% cap applies to fuel supplied at Indian airports for domestic operations.

The ministry has not specified the duration of this relief measure. Industry experts expect it to remain until global oil prices stabilize.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE ATF PRICE RELIEF ANNOUNCED FOR DOMESTIC AIRLINES?
OIL MARKETING COMPANIES HAVE LIMITED THE AVIATION TURBINE FUEL (ATF) PRICE INCREASE TO 25% FOR DOMESTIC AIRLINES. THIS PROVIDES PARTIAL RELIEF COMPARED TO THE FULL PRICE HIKE THAT WOULD OTHERWISE APPLY.
HOW WILL FOREIGN ROUTES BE TREATED UNDER THIS PRICING STRUCTURE?
FOREIGN ROUTES WILL PAY THE FULL INCREASE IN ATF PRICES. THEIR RATES WILL BE CONSISTENT WITH WHAT AIRLINES PAY IN OTHER PARTS OF THE WORLD, AS STATED BY THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS.
WHY IS THIS RELIEF IMPORTANT FOR DOMESTIC AIRLINES?
ATF ACCOUNTS FOR 35-40% OF AN AIRLINE'S OPERATING COSTS IN INDIA. THE 25% PRICE CAP DIRECTLY REDUCES OPERATIONAL EXPENSES AND HELPS PROTECT MARGINS AMID VOLATILE GLOBAL CRUDE OIL PRICES.
WHICH AIRLINES WILL BENEFIT FROM THIS MEASURE?
MAJOR DOMESTIC CARRIERS INCLUDING INDIGO (NSE: INDIGO), AIR INDIA, AND SPICEJET (NSE: SPICEJET) WILL BENEFIT. THE CAP APPLIES TO FUEL SUPPLIED AT INDIAN AIRPORTS FOR DOMESTIC OPERATIONS.
HOW LONG WILL THIS DIFFERENTIAL PRICING REMAIN IN EFFECT?
THE MINISTRY OF PETROLEUM AND NATURAL GAS HAS NOT SPECIFIED THE DURATION OF THIS RELIEF MEASURE. INDUSTRY EXPERTS EXPECT IT TO CONTINUE UNTIL GLOBAL OIL PRICES STABILIZE AT LOWER LEVELS.
