Gold Rises on Softer Dollar, Set for Fourth Consecutive Weekly Decline

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold prices climbed on Friday as dollar index fell 0.3%, making bullion cheaper for overseas buyers
  • 02/Yellow metal remains on track for fourth consecutive weekly decline, longest losing streak since November 2024
  • 03/Traders eye Fed policy outlook and tariff developments for directional cues next week

Gold prices advanced on Friday as the dollar weakened against major currencies. Spot gold gained 0.6% to trade near $3,020 per ounce.

The dollar index slipped 0.3%, making dollar-denominated bullion more affordable for holders of other currencies. This provided relief after weeks of selling pressure.

Despite Friday's bounce, gold remains on course for a fourth straight weekly drop. The metal has fallen nearly 3% since Monday.

This marks the longest weekly losing streak since November 2024. Profit-taking and reduced safe-haven demand have weighed on prices throughout March.

Fed Chair Jerome Powell signaled cautious approach to rate cuts this week. Higher-for-longer rates reduce gold's appeal as it pays no interest.

Tariff announcements and trade war developments continue to create volatility. Gold briefly touched $3,057 earlier this month on geopolitical tensions.

Physical demand from China and India has softened at elevated price levels. Central bank buying remains steady but below record 2024 levels.

Analysts see support near $2,980 and resistance at $3,080. Next week's jobs data could determine near-term direction.

THE DECLINE REFLECTS PROFIT-TAKING AFTER RECORD HIGHS, REDUCED SAFE-HAVEN DEMAND, AND EXPECTATIONS THAT THE FEDERAL RESERVE WILL KEEP INTEREST RATES ELEVATED. HIGHER RATES REDUCE GOLD'S ATTRACTIVENESS AS IT GENERATES NO YIELD.
