Centre Not Taking State Powers on Agri Bonus: FM Sitharaman Clarifies Advisory Nature

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Minister Sitharaman states Centre is not seizing State powers over agricultural bonuses; letter was advisory, not directive
  • 02/Response to Tamil Nadu CM Stalin's allegations that Centre ordered stopping of paddy cultivation incentives
  • 03/Centre encourages crop diversification toward pulses, oilseeds, millets to reduce import dependence and boost farmer incomes

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman has categorically stated that the Centre is not taking away State powers over agricultural bonuses. The clarification comes after Tamil Nadu Chief Minister M.K. Stalin alleged the Union Government ordered States to stop paddy cultivation incentives.

Sitharaman dismissed Stalin's allegations as "factually baseless, politically motivated and a deliberate distortion" intended to mislead farmers. The Finance Ministry clarified the Expenditure Department's letter serves as a non-binding advisory.

The communication was sent by the Expenditure Secretary on January 1, 2026. It advised Chief Secretaries to align bonus policies to promote pulses, oilseeds and millets with national priorities.

The letter aims to support nutritional security, Aatmanirbharta (self-reliance), and sustainable agriculture. The Ministry emphasized it was not a directive but a suggestion for policy alignment.

Declaring a bonus over and above the Minimum Support Price remains entirely the prerogative of State Governments. "No one has taken that power away," Sitharaman stated categorically.

The Centre's suggestion is rooted in encouraging crop diversification. The goal is self-sufficiency in pulses and oilseeds where domestic demand far outstrips supply.

Import dependence on edible oils has fallen from 63.2% in 2015-16 to 56.25% in 2023-24. Between 2014-15 and 2024-25, oilseeds area increased 18%, production rose 55%, and productivity grew 31%.

Expanding domestic production reduces reliance on imports exposed to international uncertainty and price volatility. The Centre has consistently aligned MSP increases to favor pulses and oilseeds over water-intensive crops.
