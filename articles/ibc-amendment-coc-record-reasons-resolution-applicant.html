IBC Amendment: CoC Must Record Reasons For Selecting Resolution Applicants

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Parliament passes bill amending IBC; CoC must now record detailed reasons for selecting successful resolution applicants
  • 02/Measure aims to increase transparency and accountability in corporate insolvency resolution process
  • 03/Amendment applies to all ongoing and future insolvency proceedings under the IBC framework

The Committee of Creditors (CoC) will now be required to record detailed reasons for selecting successful resolution applicants. The new mandate follows the passage of a bill to amend the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC).

The amendment applies to all corporate insolvency resolution proceedings. It brings greater transparency to the decision-making process of creditors.

The CoC previously selected resolution applicants without mandatory documentation requirements. The new rule ensures accountability in the selection process.

Resolution applicants are entities that submit plans to take over distressed companies. Their proposals outline how they will revive the business and repay creditors.

The IBC was enacted in 2016 to consolidate insolvency and bankruptcy laws. It provides a time-bound framework for resolving stressed assets.

The amendment addresses concerns about opaque decision-making in high-profile cases. Several resolutions have faced legal challenges regarding the selection process.

The government has been strengthening the IBC framework through periodic amendments. The code has resolved over 1,000 cases since its inception.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT DOES THE NEW IBC AMENDMENT REQUIRE?
THE AMENDMENT REQUIRES THE COMMITTEE OF CREDITORS (COC) TO RECORD AND DOCUMENT DETAILED REASONS FOR SELECTING THE SUCCESSFUL RESOLUTION APPLICANT IN INSOLVENCY PROCEEDINGS.
WHY WAS THIS AMENDMENT INTRODUCED?
THE AMENDMENT AIMS TO ENHANCE TRANSPARENCY AND ACCOUNTABILITY IN THE CORPORATE INSOLVENCY RESOLUTION PROCESS. IT ADDRESSES CONCERNS ABOUT OPAQUE DECISION-MAKING IN CREDITOR COMMITTEES.
WHEN WAS THE IBC ORIGINALLY ENACTED?
THE INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE WAS ENACTED IN 2016 TO PROVIDE A TIME-BOUND FRAMEWORK FOR RESOLVING STRESSED ASSETS AND CONSOLIDATE BANKRUPTCY LAWS IN INDIA.
DOES THIS APPLY TO ONGOING CASES?
YES, THE AMENDMENT APPLIES TO ALL ONGOING AND FUTURE INSOLVENCY PROCEEDINGS UNDER THE IBC FRAMEWORK, NOT JUST NEW FILINGS.
WHAT IS A RESOLUTION APPLICANT?
A RESOLUTION APPLICANT IS AN ENTITY THAT SUBMITS A PLAN TO TAKE OVER AND REVIVE A DISTRESSED COMPANY. THE PLAN OUTLINES HOW THEY WILL REPAY CREDITORS AND RESTORE THE BUSINESS TO VIABILITY.
