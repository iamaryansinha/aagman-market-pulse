Voice of Global South: India Blocks China-Led IFD Pact at WTO MC14

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India was the sole dissenter among 166 WTO members blocking the China-led Investment Facilitation for Development agreement at MC14 in Yaoundé, Cameroon.
  • 02/The IFD pact was endorsed by 128 members including 90 developing and 26 LDCs, but India argued it bypasses the consensus rule and falls outside WTO's core mandate.
  • 03/India, Oman and South Africa submitted a draft declaration on WTO reforms emphasizing Special and Differential Treatment for developing countries.

India stood alone as the sole dissenter at the WTO's 14th Ministerial Conference in Yaoundé, Cameroon. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal blocked the China-led Investment Facilitation for Development agreement despite 128 members endorsing it.

The IFD pact sought to streamline cross-border investment procedures and improve transparency. India argued incorporating this plurilateral deal would bypass the consensus-based decision-making principle. This stance came even after fellow opponents South Africa and Türkiye withdrew their objections.

"India ensured that the voice of the Global South was well-articulated and the needs and aspirations of developing countries and LDCs were given primacy in the negotiating room," Goyal stated on March 29, 2026.

The United States and European Union pressed for overhauling Special and Differential Treatment provisions at MC14. Washington demanded objective criteria for developing country status, seeking to end self-designation rights. India countered that the Marrakesh Agreement explicitly protects developing country rights.

On agriculture, India stressed that permanent solutions for public stockholding, special safeguard mechanism, and cotton remain long-pending mandated issues. These must be delivered before taking up new non-mandated topics.

India also defended small-scale fishermen in fisheries subsidies negotiations. New Delhi emphasized that overcapacity stems from heavily subsidized industrial fleets, not artisanal fishers in developing nations. About 9 million fishermen depend on the sector in India alone.

The e-commerce tax moratorium negotiations went down to the wire on March 29. The US pushed for a permanent moratorium while India called for careful reconsideration. Sources indicated pressure on India to accept a 4-5 year extension instead of the usual 2-year norm.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT DID INDIA BLOCK AT WTO MC14?
INDIA BLOCKED THE INVESTMENT FACILITATION FOR DEVELOPMENT (IFD) AGREEMENT FROM BEING INCORPORATED INTO THE WTO FRAMEWORK. NEW DELHI WAS THE SOLE DISSENTER AMONG 166 MEMBERS AFTER SOUTH AFRICA AND TÜRKIYE WITHDREW THEIR OBJECTIONS.
WHY DID INDIA OPPOSE THE IFD AGREEMENT?
INDIA ARGUED THAT THE IFD PACT, BACKED BY 128 MEMBERS, WOULD BYPASS THE CONSENSUS RULE AND SET A DANGEROUS PRECEDENT. INVESTMENT ISSUES FALL OUTSIDE THE WTO'S CORE MANDATE AND COULD SIDELINE PRIORITIES LIKE FOOD SECURITY AND PUBLIC STOCKHOLDING.
WHAT IS SPECIAL AND DIFFERENTIAL TREATMENT (S&DT)?
S&DT IS A DECADES-OLD WTO PRINCIPLE THAT ALLOWS DEVELOPING COUNTRIES TO FACE LESS ONEROUS TRADE OBLIGATIONS THAN RICH NATIONS. THE US AND EU ARE PRESSING TO OVERHAUL THIS SYSTEM BY REMOVING SELF-DESIGNATION RIGHTS.
WHAT IS INDIA'S STANCE ON E-COMMERCE MORATORIUM?
INDIA CALLED FOR CAREFUL RECONSIDERATION OF THE E-COMMERCE TAX MORATORIUM FIRST INTRODUCED IN 1998. NEW DELHI WANTS TO PRESERVE REVENUE AND POLICY SPACE, WHILE THE US DEMANDS A PERMANENT MORATORIUM FOR ITS TECH GIANTS.
WHAT ARE INDIA'S KEY AGRICULTURAL DEMANDS AT WTO?
INDIA IS DEMANDING PERMANENT SOLUTIONS FOR PUBLIC STOCKHOLDING FOR FOOD SECURITY, SPECIAL SAFEGUARD MECHANISM, AND COTTON ISSUES. THESE ARE LONG-PENDING MANDATED ISSUES THAT HAVE REMAINED UNRESOLVED FOR OVER A DECADE.
