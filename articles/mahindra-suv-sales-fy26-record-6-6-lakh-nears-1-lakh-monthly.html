Mahindra SUV Sales FY26: Record 6.6 Lakh Units, Nears 1 Lakh Monthly Mark

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Mahindra & Mahindra sold 6,60,276 SUVs in FY26, marking a 20% YoY increase and an all-time high
  • 02/March total volumes reached 99,969 units, just shy of 1 lakh, with SUVs at 60,272 units up 25%
  • 03/Three-wheelers emerged as fastest-growing segment at 39% in March; electric SUVs BE6 and XEV 9e gain traction

Mahindra & Mahindra Ltd (NSE: M&M) closed FY26 with record SUV sales of 6,60,276 units. This marks a 20% year-on-year increase and an all-time high for the company.

March 2026 proved exceptional as total volumes touched 99,969 units. The company came within striking distance of the 1 lakh monthly sales milestone.

Domestic utility vehicle sales in March stood at 60,272 units, up 25% YoY. Total SUV volumes including exports reached 62,109 units for the month.

SUVs now account for roughly 60% of Mahindra's total volumes. This highlights the company's increasing reliance on the utility vehicle segment.

"The financial year ended on a very positive note, with Mahindra clocking its highest-ever volumes in both SUVs and LCVs," said Nalinikanth Gollagunta, CEO, Automotive Division, M&M Ltd.

The growth was driven by strong traction across key models. Bolero, Scorpio-N, Thar, and XUV700 continue to anchor volumes across urban and rural markets.

Mahindra's passenger vehicle market share has climbed to 20.8% from 7.5% earlier. The company is now a strong challenger to Tata Motors in the electric SUV space.

The recently launched electric SUVs BE6 and XEV 9e have seen strong initial demand. These models are contributing to the company's rising market share.

Three-wheelers emerged as the fastest-growing segment. March volumes rose 39% to 10,801 units, while full-year sales grew 30% to 1,12,003 units.

Commercial vehicle performance remained mixed. The LCV 2-3.5 tonne segment grew 13% in March and 16% for the full year.

Smaller LCVs below 2 tonnes remained flat in March. They declined 2% over FY26, pointing to a shift towards higher-tonnage vehicles.

Export performance was volatile, declining 4% in March to 3,968 units. However, full-year exports rose 18% to 40,990 units.
