Shriram Finance Credit Rating Upgraded To AAA After MUFG's ₹39,618 Crore Investment

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CRISIL, ICRA, and CARE upgraded Shriram Finance to 'AAA/Stable' following MUFG's ₹39,618 crore investment for 20% stake
  • 02/Cost of funds expected to reduce by approximately 100 basis points over 18-24 months
  • 03/Tier-1 capital adequacy to surge from ~20% to over 35% post investment

Shriram Finance has received credit rating upgrades to 'AAA/Stable' from CRISIL, ICRA, and CARE following Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group's ₹39,618 crore investment. The investment gives MUFG a 20% stake in the retail NBFC.

The rating upgrades reflect the strengthened capital position and improved funding access. Shriram Finance's cost of funds is expected to reduce by approximately 100 basis points over the next 18-24 months.

The AAA rating differential typically translates to 50-75 basis points lower borrowing costs compared to the previous AA+ rating. This will improve net interest margins significantly.

Tier-1 capital adequacy ratio is projected to surge from approximately 20% to over 35% post-investment. This provides substantial buffer for growth and regulatory requirements.

MUFG's investment is the largest foreign investment in India's NBFC sector. The Japanese banking giant brings global expertise and potential synergies in funding and risk management.

Shriram Finance primarily serves the used commercial vehicle and small truck financing segments. The company has a deep presence in rural and semi-urban markets.

The funds will be used to retire high-cost debt and support balance sheet growth. Management has indicated plans to expand into new asset classes while maintaining core focus.

The transaction is subject to regulatory approvals including RBI and CCI clearance. Completion is expected in the next 3-4 months.

Shriram's asset quality has remained stable despite sectoral challenges. Gross NPA ratio stood at 6.8% as of December 2025 with adequate provisioning.

The rating upgrade positions Shriram Finance among the highest-rated NBFCs in India. This will attract a broader investor base and reduce dependence on bank funding.
