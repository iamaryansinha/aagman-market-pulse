LPG Tanker Jag Vasant Arrives At Vadinar With 47,000 MT Cargo Amid Supply Concerns

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/LPG carrier Jag Vasant arrived at Gujarat's Vadinar port carrying approximately 47,000 MT of liquefied petroleum gas
  • 02/Vessel transited Strait of Hormuz amid ongoing West Asia geopolitical tensions affecting regional supply chains
  • 03/Ship began unloading Saturday via ship-to-ship transfer to daughter vessel Rose Gas at anchorage

LPG carrier Jag Vasant has arrived at Gujarat's Vadinar port. The vessel is carrying approximately 47,000 MT of liquefied petroleum gas.

The tanker transited through the Strait of Hormuz. This passage comes amid heightened geopolitical tensions in West Asia.

Unloading operations began on Saturday. The cargo is being transferred via ship-to-ship (STS) operations at the anchorage.

The LPG is being moved to daughter vessel Rose Gas. This method allows discharge without berthing at the port terminal.

The arrival is significant for India's energy security. Supply chains remain on edge due to ongoing regional conflicts.

Earlier on Wednesday, Pyxis Pioneer berthed at Vadinar. It carried 18,000 MT of LPG sourced from the United States.

This US cargo highlights diversification efforts. India is expanding energy sourcing beyond traditional West Asian suppliers.

Tanker Desh Bhakt is also at anchorage. It carries 50,741 MT of crude oil destined for Indian Oil Corporation.

Over the next three days, three or more vessels are expected. They will deliver approximately 3 lakh MT of crude oil.

These shipments are destined for Nayara Energy and IOCL. Vadinar serves as a critical hub for India's petroleum imports.

The government confirmed 20 Indian-flagged ships are in the Persian Gulf. Approximately 540 Indian seafarers are onboard, all reported safe.
