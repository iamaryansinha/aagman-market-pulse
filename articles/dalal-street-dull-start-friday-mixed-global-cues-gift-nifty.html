Dalal Street Set For Dull Start Friday Amid Mixed Global Cues, Gift Nifty Signals Soft Open

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gift Nifty at 24,153 indicates a marginal decline for the Nifty 50 at Friday's opening bell amid mixed global signals
  • 02/Geopolitical tensions persist as Strait of Hormuz disruptions threaten 20% of global oil supply; US-Iran talks progress remains uncertain
  • 03/Key levels to watch: support at 24,100, resistance at 24,300; India VIX holds above 18 indicating elevated volatility

Indian stock markets are expected to open on a cautious note on Friday, April 17, 2026. Gift Nifty at 24,153 signals a marginal decline at the opening bell.

Mixed global cues are driving the subdued sentiment. While optimism around potential progress in US-Iran talks initially supported risk appetite, fresh developments around disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz have reintroduced uncertainty.

Partial blockages and rising tensions along this critical oil route have kept global markets on edge. Nearly 20% of global oil supply passes through the Strait of Hormuz, making any disruption a significant concern for energy markets and economic stability.

"While optimism around potential progress in U.S.-Iran talks had initially supported risk appetite, fresh developments around disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz have reintroduced uncertainty," said Ponmudi R, CEO of Enrich Money.

Equities across the Asia Pacific region are also down in early deals on Friday. The market environment remains highly news-driven with volatility likely to persist.

On the domestic front, stock-specific action is likely to dominate as the earnings season gathers pace. Wipro will be in focus after announcing a ₹15,000 crore buyback at ₹250 per share. Jio Financial Services is also expected to draw attention ahead of its Q4 results.

Liquidity conditions will be closely monitored following the Reserve Bank of India's announcement of a ₹2 lakh crore seven-day variable rate reverse repo auction. This move indicates an intent to absorb excess liquidity from the system, effectively tightening financial conditions at the margin.

"Such actions tend to influence short-term interest rates and can have a broader impact on borrowing costs, thereby indirectly shaping consumption and investment trends," noted Hariprasad K, SEBI-registered Research Analyst and Founder of Livelong Wealth.

Volatility remains a key factor for traders. India VIX has cooled from recent highs but continues to hold above the 18 mark. This suggests that option premiums remain elevated, reflecting an underlying layer of uncertainty.

From the derivatives perspective, the Put-Call Ratio (PCR) stands near 0.86, indicating a neutral stance amid recent volatility. Option data suggests that call writing is clustered around 24,300-24,500, capping the upside.

Put writing at 24,000-23,800 is offering immediate support, reinforcing a defined trading range for the Nifty 50 index. "Overall, the index appears to be in a consolidation phase, with 24,100 acting as a strong base and 24,260-24,300 as a key resistance band," said Dhupesh Dhameja, Derivatives Research Analyst at SAMCO Securities.

A decisive breakout on either side is required to establish the next directional move for the Indian equity markets.
