CSK Unlisted Shares Jump 25% as IPL Franchise Deals Hit $3.4 Billion

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CSK unlisted shares jumped over 25% from ₹250-260 to ₹315 following record IPL franchise deals worth $3.41 billion combined
  • 02/RCB acquired for $1.78 billion by Aditya Birla-Blackstone consortium; Rajasthan Royals sold for $1.63 billion to Kal Somani-led group
  • 03/CSK trades at ₹11,000 crore market cap, a 30% discount to RCB benchmark; only IPL franchise actively traded in unlisted market

Chennai Super Kings unlisted shares have surged over 25% in recent days. The stock jumped from ₹250-260 levels to ₹315 per share.

The rally follows two blockbuster IPL franchise deals announced this week. Royal Challengers Bengaluru was acquired for $1.78 billion. Rajasthan Royals was sold for $1.63 billion.

The combined $3.41 billion transactions have triggered a valuation re-rating across IPL assets. Investors are rushing to capitalize on the pricing gap in unlisted shares.

CSK shares are currently seeing sell orders around ₹315 apiece. This implies a market capitalization of roughly ₹11,000 crore.

The franchise is trading at approximately 30% discount to the RCB benchmark. Krishna Patwari of Wealth Wisdom India attributes this to dispersed ownership and lack of strategic sale visibility.

CSK remains the only IPL franchise actively traded in the unlisted market. The stock was created after its 2015 demerger from India Cements.

CSK reported revenues of ₹643 crore in FY25 with profit after tax of ₹180 crore. The five-time IPL champions have a brand value of $235 million.

The total business value of the IPL has climbed 13% year-on-year to $18.5 billion. Brand value stands at $3.9 billion according to recent estimates.

The rally has spilled into listed markets as well. Sun TV Network and RPSG Ventures shares surged up to 20% on renewed investor enthusiasm.

Abhay Doshi of Unlisted Arena noted that enquiries have increased significantly. Tight bid-ask spreads of 1-2% reflect active trading with prices likely to rise further.
