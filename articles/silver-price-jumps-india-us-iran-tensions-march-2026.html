Silver Price Jumps ₹6,911 to ₹2.26 Lakh/kg as US-Iran Tensions Ease Temporarily

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Silver futures for May delivery surged ₹6,911 (3.14%) to ₹2,26,785 per kg on MCX after US-Iran tensions temporarily eased
  • 02/The rebound follows a sharp ₹14,960 (6.37%) plunge in the previous session as geopolitical concerns triggered volatility
  • 03/Analysts expect silver to consolidate in a range of $66-75 per ounce (₹2.19-2.38 lakh/kg) as diplomatic negotiations remain fragile

Silver prices rebounded sharply by ₹6,911 to ₹2.26 lakh per kilogram in futures trade on Friday, March 27, 2026. The surge came as investors returned to the market following a temporary pause in tensions between the United States and Iran.

On the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX), silver for May delivery jumped ₹6,911, or 3.14 per cent, to settle at ₹2,26,785 per kg. This marks a significant recovery after the previous session's heavy losses.

In the previous trading session, silver futures had plunged ₹14,960, or 6.37 per cent, to close at ₹2,19,874 per kg. Commodity markets were closed in the morning session on Thursday on account of Ram Navami and resumed trading in the evening session.

In the overseas market, silver futures for the May contract edged up by $2.02, or 3 per cent, to $69.96 per ounce on the Comex division of the New York Mercantile Exchange.

Analysts said silver prices rebounded after US President Donald Trump paused military strikes on Iran's energy infrastructure for 10 days. This move signalled a potential window for diplomacy between Washington and Tehran.

However, the upside remains capped due to ongoing uncertainties. "A temporary pause on targeting energy infrastructure until April 6 provided limited relief; the absence of a clear resolution continues to weigh on sentiment and cap upside in bullion," said Renisha Chainani, Head of Research at Augmont.

Negotiations remain fragile as Iran rejected Washington's 15-point peace proposal. Tehran has demanded recognition over the Strait of Hormuz, keeping markets volatile and investor sentiment cautious.

Chainani noted that while prolonged conflict raises inflation risks, it also increases the probability of an economic slowdown in the US. This creates a mixed outlook for precious metals in the near term.

Technically, silver is holding above key support at $66 per ounce, which translates to approximately ₹2.19 lakh per kg. The white metal is expected to consolidate in a broad range of $66-75 per ounce (₹2.19-2.38 lakh/kg) in the near term.

Silver's sharp volatility reflects its dual role as both a precious metal and an industrial commodity. Unlike gold, silver demand is heavily influenced by industrial usage in electronics, solar panels, and manufacturing sectors.
