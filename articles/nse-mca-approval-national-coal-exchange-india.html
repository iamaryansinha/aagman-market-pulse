NSE Gets MCA Approval to Set Up National Coal Exchange of India Limited

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/National Stock Exchange receives MCA approval to reserve name 'National Coal Exchange of India Limited' for its coal trading platform
  • 02/NSE plans initial capital infusion of up to Rs 100 crore, retaining 60% stake while offering 40% to other shareholders
  • 03/Next step involves obtaining license from Coal Controller Organisation to operationalize the exchange platform

National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) has received approval from the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). The approval reserves the name "National Coal Exchange of India Limited" for its planned coal trading subsidiary.

The NSE board had approved the subsidiary formation in February 2026. The exchange plans to incorporate a wholly-owned subsidiary for operating the coal exchange.

NSE will infuse up to Rs 100 crore as initial capital. The exchange will retain 60% stake while offering 40% to other shareholders.

The coal exchange initiative marks NSE's diversification into commodity trading. The platform aims to create a transparent, market-driven trading mechanism for coal.

The proposed exchange will address market inefficiencies through transparent price discovery. It will improve allocation efficiency in the coal sector.

The Coal Controller Organisation (CCO) will register and regulate coal exchanges. Draft rules require applicants to have net worth of at least Rs 1 billion.

India's coal production is forecast to exceed 1.5 billion tonnes by 2030. Coal India Limited (CIL) produces approximately 75% of domestic coal.

Indian Energy Exchange (IEX) is also exploring coal exchange platform under draft rules. This signals emerging competition in the coal trading space.

NSE is also launching Dated Brent Crude Oil futures contracts on April 13, 2026. This is part of its broader commodity derivatives strategy.

The coal exchange venture faces regulatory hurdles including final licensing from CCO. Operationalizing the exchange may take three to four years.
