Bellatrix Aerospace Raises $20 Million in Pre-Series B Funding Round

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Spacetech startup Bellatrix Aerospace raised $20 million in Pre-Series B growth round led by Cactus Partners
  • 02/New investors include Hero Investment Office, 35 North Ventures, Indusbridge Ventures, and Monarch Holdings alongside existing backers
  • 03/Funds will expand manufacturing facilities and establish high-throughput production lines for satellite propulsion systems

Bellatrix Aerospace has raised $20 million in a Pre-Series B funding round. The spacetech startup announced the funding on March 28, 2026.

The round was led by Cactus Partners. New investors include Hero Investment Office, 35 North Ventures, Indusbridge Ventures, and Monarch Holdings.

Existing investors Inflexor, Pavestone, GrowX, Startup Xseed, and Survam Partners also participated. The total funding raised by the company now exceeds $30 million.

Founded in 2015 by Rohan Ganapathy and Yashas Karanam, the company develops electric and green propulsion systems. These systems power satellites in low-earth orbit constellations.

Bellatrix follows a vertically integrated business model. The company handles design, manufacturing, and testing in-house at its Bangalore facility.

The fresh capital will expand manufacturing capacity. The company is building high-throughput production lines to slash lead times under six months.

"This funding signals confidence in our operational scalability," said CEO Rohan Ganapathy. "We are building a repeatable, reliable, world-class production propulsion system."

"We've moved from flight-proven to factory-ready," added COO Yashas Karanam. "Our goal is slashing lead times to under 6 months."

Amit Sharma of Cactus Partners praised the founders. "Rohan and Yashas invested more than a decade in creating world-class core technologies."

Global demand for satellite propulsion systems is rising rapidly. Low-earth orbit constellations require efficient thrusters for station-keeping and orbital maneuvers.
