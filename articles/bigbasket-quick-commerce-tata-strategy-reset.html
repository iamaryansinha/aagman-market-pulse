BigBasket Plays Catch-Up in Quick Commerce as Tata Resets Strategy

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BigBasket accelerating its pivot from scheduled delivery to quick commerce model
  • 02/Company was late mover, allowing rivals Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart and Zepto to consolidate
  • 03/Tata Group resetting strategy to compete in the instant grocery delivery segment

BigBasket is playing catch-up in India's quick commerce market. The online grocer has been a relatively late mover in pivoting to instant fulfilment.

The company built its business on scheduled delivery slots. This model is now being replaced by 10-30 minute delivery promises across the industry.

Rivals Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart, and Zepto have consolidated market share. These players invested heavily in dark stores and hyperlocal logistics.

Tata Group acquired BigBasket in 2021 for approximately $2 billion. The conglomerate is now resetting strategy to compete with quick commerce leaders.

BigBasket has launched BB Now for instant deliveries. The service promises 15-30 minute delivery in select cities.

The quick commerce market in India is valued at $5 billion. It is growing at 40% annually despite concerns about unit economics.

The shift requires significant capital investment in dark stores. BigBasket must balance instant delivery promises with profitability.
