CEA Nageswaran Warns of Considerable Downside Risk to FY27 GDP Growth Forecast

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran says FY27 GDP growth forecast of 7-7.4% faces "considerable downside" risk due to West Asia crisis
  • 02/Forecast was raised to 7-7.4% from 6.8-7.2% after GDP base year revision to 2022-23 on February 27
  • 03/Current account deficit expected to widen significantly; high-frequency April-May data will clarify growth trajectory

Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran has warned of "considerable downside" risks to India's GDP growth forecast of 7-7.4% for financial year 2026-27. The warning comes amid escalating tensions in West Asia and rising global trade uncertainties.

The CEA made the remarks in the preface of the Monthly Economic Review prepared by the Economic Affairs Department. He noted that data for March 2026 will not reveal much as businesses rush to meet full-year targets.

High-frequency data for April and May 2026 will provide better clarity on the likely growth rate for the new financial year. The current account deficit is also expected to widen significantly during FY27.

The FY27 growth forecast was revised upward to 7-7.4% from the earlier projection of 6.8-7.2% made in the January Economic Survey. This revision followed the GDP base year change to 2022-23 from 2011-12 on February 27, 2026.

Nageswaran outlined four channels through which the West Asia conflict will impact India. First, supply disruptions to oil, gas, fertilizers, and exports. Second, higher import prices across key commodities.

Third, increased logistics costs including freight and insurance. Fourth, a possible decline in remittances by Indians working in Gulf countries.

"The combined impact across the four channels on growth, inflation, the fiscal balance, and external balances is likely to be significant," Nageswaran stated. He called for re-prioritisation of government spending to build long-term buffers.

India's economy grew 7.8% in the December quarter under the new GDP series. Full-year FY26 growth is projected at 7.6%, revised up from 7.4% earlier.
