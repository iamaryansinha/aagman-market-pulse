ONGC Chief Warns India Must Rethink West Asia Energy Reliance After Supply Shock

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ONGC Chairman Arun Kumar Singh says India must rethink energy reliance on West Asia after biggest supply shock in decades
  • 02/India depends on West Asia for 50% crude oil, 30% gas and 85-90% LPG imports; six-week Hormuz shutdown triggered crisis
  • 03/Singh urges strategic storage expansion, aggressive domestic exploration and energy diversification as "existential necessity"

ONGC Chairman Arun Kumar Singh has issued a stark warning. India must rethink its reliance on West Asia for energy supplies.

The call comes after the biggest energy shock in decades. A six-week shutdown of the Strait of Hormuz disrupted global supply chains.

India currently sources nearly 50% of crude oil imports from West Asia. The region also supplies 30% of gas and 85-90% of LPG.

"Thinking that West Asia is nearest to us and therefore all their resources can be accessed easily, we should take it with a pinch of salt," Singh said. He was speaking at an Energy Security conclave organised by PNGRB.

The Strait of Hormuz shutdown was the only shipping lane for Gulf exports. It carries crude oil, natural gas and cooking gas LPG to importing nations.

Singh warned that shifting global order and rising geopolitical tensions are undermining energy security assumptions.

"A paradigm shift has happened," he stated. "If the world gets more and more de-globalised, we will have more and more problems."

He stressed that boosting domestic production is now critical. "We have been doing it very intensely except for deep water," he said.

Singh called domestic output an "existential necessity." He urged India to aggressively pursue exploration and build strategic storage.

"We should chase wherever oil or gas is in our country at any cost, because in a crisis nobody will help," he warned.

On LPG, Singh noted India has boosted domestic supply. It rose from 30% earlier to 60% now.

He also suggested prioritising piped natural gas over CNG for households. "At least kitchens will not stop," he said.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY IS ONGC CHIEF WARNING ABOUT WEST ASIA ENERGY RELIANCE?
ARUN KUMAR SINGH WARNS THAT THE SIX-WEEK STRAIT OF HORMUZ SHUTDOWN CAUSED THE BIGGEST ENERGY SHOCK IN DECADES. HE SAYS RISING GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS AND DEGLOBALISATION MEAN INDIA CAN NO LONGER ASSUME EASY ACCESS TO WEST ASIAN ENERGY SUPPLIES.
HOW MUCH DOES INDIA DEPEND ON WEST ASIA FOR ENERGY?
INDIA SOURCES NEARLY 50% OF CRUDE OIL IMPORTS, 30% OF NATURAL GAS AND 85-90% OF LPG FROM WEST ASIA. THIS HIGH DEPENDENCE MAKES INDIA ESPECIALLY VULNERABLE TO SUPPLY DISRUPTIONS.
WHAT CAUSED THE RECENT ENERGY CRISIS?
A SIX-WEEK SHUTDOWN OF THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ, THE ONLY SHIPPING LANE USED BY GULF COUNTRIES TO EXPORT CRUDE OIL, NATURAL GAS AND LPG. THIS FORCED INDIA TO PRIORITISE GAS SUPPLY AMONG IMPORTING NATIONS.
WHAT SOLUTIONS DOES THE ONGC CHIEF PROPOSE?
SINGH URGES THREE KEY ACTIONS: BUILD STRATEGIC STORAGE CAPACITY AS INSURANCE, AGGRESSIVELY PURSUE DOMESTIC OIL AND GAS EXPLORATION, AND DIVERSIFY ENERGY TYPES AND SUPPLY CHAINS TO REDUCE IMPORT DEPENDENCE.
HOW HAS INDIA'S DOMESTIC LPG SUPPLY CHANGED?
INDIA HAS SIGNIFICANTLY BOOSTED DOMESTIC LPG SUPPLY FROM 30% EARLIER TO 60% NOW. REFINERS RECONFIGURED OPERATIONS TO SUPPORT THIS SHIFT, THOUGH IT CAME AT A COST TO OPERATIONS.
