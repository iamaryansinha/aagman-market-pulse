XED Withdraws GIFT City IPO Amid Weak Investor Demand And Global Uncertainty

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/XED withdraws its planned IPO at GIFT City following weak demand from investors during global market uncertainty
  • 02/The issue faced earlier delays due to escalating tensions in the Gulf region affecting cross-border fundraising
  • 03/Investors should watch how global crises continue to impact Indian primary market offerings and foreign fundraising

XED has withdrawn its proposed IPO at GIFT City following tepid response from investors. The decision comes amid ongoing global uncertainty and market volatility.

The issue had already faced delays earlier this month. Escalating tensions in the Gulf region forced the company to postpone its fundraising plans.

This marks another casualty in the primary market this quarter. Cross-border fundraising has become increasingly challenging for Indian companies.

GIFT City was established to attract foreign capital and offshore listings. However, global geopolitical tensions are dampening investor appetite.

The withdrawal highlights the vulnerability of emerging market IPOs to external shocks. Foreign investors are pulling back from riskier assets worldwide.

Market participants expect more companies to defer listings. Uncertainty around oil prices and currency volatility remains a concern.
