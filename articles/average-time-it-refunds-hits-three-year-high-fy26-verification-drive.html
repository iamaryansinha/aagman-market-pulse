Average Time for IT Refunds Hits Three-Year High in FY26 Amid Verification Drive

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • •• Average time for income tax refunds reached 35 days in FY26, a three-year high
  • •• Delays caused by investigations into bogus donations and fraudulent deductions
  • •• Nearly 27 lakh refund applications were processed with delays beyond 90 days
  • •• CBDT used nudge approach to encourage voluntary compliance rather than scrutiny

The average time for processing income tax refunds climbed to 35 days in fiscal year 2026, marking a three-year high, according to data from the Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT). The delays were caused by extensive verification exercises targeting bogus donations and fraudulent tax deductions. [FACT: Average time for issuing IT refunds hit a three-year high in FY26]

According to a report submitted to a Parliamentary panel, nearly 27 lakh income tax refund applications were processed with delays beyond the normative 90-day period, the highest in three years. [FACT: Nearly 27 lakh refund applications were delayed beyond 90 days] The CBDT chairman attributed the delays to verification exercises conducted in July 2025 to identify fraudulent deductions under Section 80G and other provisions.

Rather than immediately subjecting suspected cases to detailed scrutiny, the tax department adopted a "nudge" approach, encouraging taxpayers to review and revise their returns voluntarily. [FACT: CBDT nudged taxpayers to file over 50 lakh updated and revised returns, reducing refund claims by ₹2,000 crore] More than 50 lakh updated and revised returns were filed, resulting in refund claim reductions of approximately ₹2,000 crore.

The Parliamentary panel has recommended the aggressive implementation of AI-driven risk-scoring algorithms to streamline refund processing. This would allow instant separation of genuine low-risk filings from suspicious ones, ensuring honest taxpayers receive timely refunds while high-risk cases undergo appropriate scrutiny.

While the 35-day average remains within Taxpayer Charter limits, the volume of significant delays has raised concerns about taxpayer inconvenience and the efficiency of refund processing systems.
