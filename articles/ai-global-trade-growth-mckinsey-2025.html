AI Infrastructure Powers One-Third of Global Trade Growth in 2025, McKinsey Finds

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/AI-related trade became the most substantial engine of global commerce in 2025
  • 02/AI infrastructure hardware accounted for approximately 33% of global trade growth
  • 03/Shipments of AI hardware increased by almost 40% during the year

The global trade landscape is undergoing a structural transformation. Artificial intelligence infrastructure has emerged as the dominant force driving cross-border commerce, even as traditional trade faces headwinds from geopolitical tensions.

According to a new report from the McKinsey Global Institute, AI-related trade has become the most substantial engine of growth in global commerce.

The numbers tell a striking story. Shipments of hardware needed to develop and run AI technology increased by almost 40% in 2025. These shipments alone accounted for roughly one-third of all global trade growth during the period.

This surge reflects a worldwide race to build data centres. Countries and corporations are investing heavily in the physical infrastructure required to power the AI revolution.

Asia sits at the heart of this transformation. Taiwan, South Korea, and parts of Southeast Asia have become critical production hubs.

The United States has emerged as the largest demand centre. America added roughly half of the world's new data centre capacity in 2025. US trade in AI hardware and infrastructure goods grew by approximately 66%.

Geopolitical alignment is increasingly shaping trade flows. A significant share of AI-related trade now occurs between politically aligned economies.

China continues to play a significant role despite facing constraints. Restrictions on advanced semiconductor imports have forced Beijing to rely more heavily on domestic production.

Europe maintains its position as a key supplier of specialized equipment. Advanced lithography machines remain a European strength.

The report signals a clear inflection point for global commerce. Trade is no longer driven primarily by traditional manufacturing and consumer goods alone. AI and the ecosystem supporting it have become foundational pillars of international exchange.
