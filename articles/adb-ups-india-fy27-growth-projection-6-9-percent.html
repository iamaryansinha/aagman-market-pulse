ADB Raises India FY27 Growth Forecast To 6.9% On Domestic Demand, Trade Deals

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ADB raises India's FY27 GDP growth forecast to 6.9%, up from its previous estimate of 6.5%
  • 02/Economy grew 7.6% in FY26; ADB sees further acceleration to 7.3% in FY28
  • 03/Risks from West Asia conflict persist; higher energy prices could derail momentum

The Asian Development Bank has upgraded India's GDP growth forecast for FY27 to 6.9%. The Manila-based lender raised its projection from the earlier estimate of 6.5%.

The revision comes from the ADB's Asian Development Outlook (ADO) April 2026 report. India remains the fastest-growing major economy despite global headwinds.

The economy is projected to grow 7.3% in FY28. This follows an estimated 7.6% growth in FY26, according to government data.

Resilient domestic consumption anchors the outlook. The ADB cited improving real incomes in both rural and urban areas as key drivers.

Supportive policy measures boosted the forecast. The RBI's cumulative 125 basis point rate cuts will lift demand for consumer durables.

Recent trade agreements provide tailwinds. Lower US tariffs and preferential market access could boost textiles, pharmaceuticals, and gems exports.

Investment activity is expected to strengthen further. Healthy corporate balance sheets and improved bank asset quality support the capex cycle.

Inflation is projected at 4.5% in FY27. This marks a rise from the 2.1% recorded in FY26, but aligns with RBI's 4% medium-term target.

The West Asia conflict poses the biggest risk. Prolonged escalation could spike energy prices and disrupt trade flows and remittance inflows.

Across developing Asia-Pacific, growth will slow to 5.1% in 2026 and 2027. This compares to 5.4% in 2025, weighed down by geopolitical tensions.

"Governments must maintain sound macroeconomic policies and extend targeted support to vulnerable households," said ADB Chief Economist Albert Park.

Regional inflation is expected to rise to 3.6% in 2026. Higher energy costs and fertilizer supply disruptions remain concerns.

WHAT DROVE THE UPGRADE IN INDIA'S GROWTH PROJECTION?
THE ADB CITED RESILIENT DOMESTIC CONSUMPTION, IMPROVING REAL INCOMES IN RURAL AND URBAN AREAS, SUPPORTIVE POLICY MEASURES INCLUDING RBI RATE CUTS, AND LOWER US TARIFFS UNDER RECENT TRADE AGREEMENTS.
WHAT IS THE GDP GROWTH OUTLOOK FOR FY28?
THE ADB PROJECTS INDIA'S ECONOMY WILL GROW AT 7.3% IN FY28, ACCELERATING FROM THE 6.9% PROJECTED FOR FY27.
WHAT ARE THE KEY RISKS TO INDIA'S GROWTH OUTLOOK?
THE ONGOING WEST ASIA CONFLICT IS THE PRIMARY RISK. PROLONGED ESCALATION COULD DRIVE UP ENERGY PRICES, DISRUPT TRADE FLOWS, AND WEAKEN REMITTANCE INFLOWS FROM THE GULF REGION, WHICH ACCOUNTS FOR 40% OF INDIA'S REMITTANCES.
WHAT IS THE INFLATION PROJECTION FOR FY27?
THE ADB EXPECTS INFLATION TO RISE TO 4.5% IN FY27, UP FROM 2.1% IN FY26, BEFORE SETTLING AROUND THE RBI'S 4% MEDIUM-TERM TARGET.
