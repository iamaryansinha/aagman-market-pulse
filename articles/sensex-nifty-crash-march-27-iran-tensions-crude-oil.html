Sensex Crashes 1,563 Points, Nifty Below 23,000 on Iran Tensions, Crude Spike

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex crashed 1,563.26 points (2.08%) to close at 73,710.19; Nifty 50 fell 456.50 points (1.96%) to 22,849.95 on March 27, 2026
  • 02/Rising crude oil prices above $108/barrel, escalating US-Iran tensions, and sustained FII selling dragged markets deep into the red
  • 03/Critical support for Nifty at 22,900; breach could trigger further fall to 22,600; volatility expected to persist amid geopolitical uncertainty

Indian equity markets suffered a sharp selloff on Friday, March 27, 2026. The BSE Sensex crashed 1,563.26 points or 2.08% to settle at 73,710.19.

The NSE Nifty 50 plunged 456.50 points or 1.96% to close at 22,849.95. The index slipped below the crucial 23,000 psychological level.

Escalating US-Iran tensions drove investor panic. Rising crude oil prices above $108 per barrel added to inflation worries.

The Indian rupee hit a record low, breaching 94 against the US dollar. Bond yields climbed on fiscal concerns and oil surge.

Foreign Institutional Investors continued their selling spree. FIIs net sold ₹1,508.89 crore in the cash market on March 25.

Domestic Institutional Investors bought ₹5,173.01 crore. However, this could not offset the heavy FII outflows.

Aviation, energy, and financial stocks led the decline. IndiGo and Shriram Finance were among the top Nifty losers.

ONGC bucked the trend, gaining 0.85% on higher crude realizations. Oil explorers typically benefit from elevated crude prices.

Goldman Sachs downgraded Indian equities to 'market weight'. The brokerage cited elevated energy prices and declining earnings growth outlook.

The government cut excise duty on petrol from ₹13 to ₹3 per litre. Diesel duty was eliminated entirely to shield consumers from rising crude costs.

The fiscal hit from fuel duty cuts is estimated at ₹1.55 lakh crore annually. This raised concerns about the government's fiscal deficit target.

Market breadth remained heavily negative throughout the session. Several stocks hit their 52-week lows amid broad-based selling.

India VIX, the volatility index, signaled heightened fear. Investors are closely watching geopolitical developments for direction.

Nifty is approaching a critical support zone at 22,900. A break below this level could trigger a sharper fall towards 22,600.

Analysts advise caution until tensions ease and crude prices stabilize. Short-term volatility is expected to persist.
