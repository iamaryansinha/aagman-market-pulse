Rupee Surges 119 Paise to 93.62 After RBI Caps Banks' FX Positions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rupee opens at 93.62 per USD, gaining 119 paise from previous close of 94.81 which was a record low
  • 02/RBI imposes caps on banks' foreign exchange positions to curb speculation and stabilize the currency
  • 03/Markets to watch for sustained recovery as rupee had been under pressure from strong dollar and capital outflows

The Indian Rupee opened sharply stronger at 93.62 per US dollar on Monday. This marks a 119 paise gain from the previous session's close of 94.81.

The rupee had hit a record low of 94.81 on Friday. The currency has been under sustained pressure from a strengthening dollar and foreign portfolio outflows.

The Reserve Bank of India intervened by capping banks' foreign exchange positions. This measure aims to reduce speculative trading and stabilize the currency.

The RBI tightened limits on onshore FX positions held by banks. The move restricts how much banks can speculate in the currency derivatives market.

The NDF (Non-Deliverable Forward) market had shown signs of recovery earlier. The onshore spot market followed through with the sharp opening gain.

Currency traders remain cautious about the sustainability of this recovery. The dollar index continues to trade near multi-month highs against global currencies.

Forex reserves have declined in recent weeks as the RBI defended the rupee. The central bank has been actively intervening to prevent disorderly depreciation.

Analysts expect volatility to continue in the near term. The rupee's trajectory will depend on FII flows and global risk sentiment.
