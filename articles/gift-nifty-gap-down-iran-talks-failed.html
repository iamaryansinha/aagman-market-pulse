Gift Nifty Signals 300-Point Gap Down As Iran Peace Talks Fail

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gift Nifty trading at 23,770 signals a 300-350 point gap down opening for Nifty50 on Monday
  • 02/Failed US-Iran peace talks led by VP JD Vance sent crude oil prices surging above $105 per barrel
  • 03/FPIs have sold Rs 48,905 crore in April, taking total 2026 outflows to Rs 1,90,046 crore

Gift Nifty was trading at 23,770 at 7:25 AM IST on Monday. This indicates a gap down opening of 300-350 points for the Nifty50 benchmark.

The previous trading session closed with Nifty at 24,050.60. The sharp drop follows failed peace negotiations between the US and Iran.

US Vice-President JD Vance led the collapsed talks. Despite the breakdown, Gift Nifty did not show the sharp weakness initially feared by traders.

Crude oil prices surged back above $105 per barrel. Prices had corrected to the $94-100 range after a temporary ceasefire announcement.

"This development is critical as the route carries a significant portion of global oil supply," said Ponmudi R, CEO of Enrich Money.

India depends on the Strait of Hormuz for over 85% of crude oil imports. Rising oil prices could pressure the current account deficit and weaken the rupee.

Foreign Portfolio Investors continued selling in April. Up to April 11, FPIs sold Rs 48,905 crore through exchanges.

Total FPI selling for 2026 now stands at Rs 1,90,046 crore. This follows record selling of Rs 1,22,182 crore in March.

"Markets are likely to shift back into a risk-off mode," said Dr. VK Vijayakumar, Chief Investment Strategist at Geojit Investments Ltd.

Domestic flows provide some cushion. Equity mutual fund inflows hit Rs 40,450 crore in March. Monthly SIP inflows reached Rs 32,087 crore.

CRUDE OIL PRICES SURGED BACK ABOVE $105 PER BARREL FROM THE $94-100 RANGE. THE US HAS REPORTEDLY MOVED TO RESTRICT ACCESS THROUGH THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ FOLLOWING THE FAILED NEGOTIATIONS.
