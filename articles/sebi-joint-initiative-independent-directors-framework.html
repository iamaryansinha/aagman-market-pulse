SEBI Plans Joint Initiative To Strengthen Independent Directors Framework

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI Chairman Tuhin Kanta Pandey announces joint initiative for capacity building of independent directors at scale
  • 02/Initiative follows recent corporate governance failures and mass resignations at PTC India, Gensol Engineering
  • 03/Training framework to focus on governance standards, board effectiveness, and regulatory compliance

SEBI plans a joint initiative to strengthen independent director training and capability building. SEBI Chairman Tuhin Kanta Pandey announced the move at the CII Governance Summit on April 6, 2026.

The initiative targets improved corporate governance standards across Indian companies. It aims to scale up training programmes for independent directors systematically.

The announcement comes amid rising concerns over board independence in India. 549 independent directors resigned voluntarily in FY2025, with 154 exits since January 2025 alone.

Recent controversies have exposed governance gaps. Three independent directors quit PTC India Financial Services in September 2025 citing inability to function independently.

Gensol Engineering saw three independent director resignations in early 2025. SEBI subsequently flagged alleged financial irregularities involving the company's promoters.

Pandey emphasised moving from periodic reviews to continuous oversight. He called this transition essential for improving governance outcomes.

The SEBI chief flagged a "gap in translation" of governance norms into practice. He stressed improving quality of engagement at the board level.

India's top 200 listed companies have 53% independent directors on average. This trails global peers like FTSE 100 (74%) and S&P 100 (86%).

The joint initiative will likely involve corporates, academia, and professional bodies. No specific implementation timeline was disclosed.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE SEBI JOINT INITIATIVE FOR INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS?
SEBI IS PLANNING A JOINT INITIATIVE TO SCALE UP TRAINING AND CAPACITY-BUILDING PROGRAMMES FOR INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS. THE INITIATIVE AIMS TO ENHANCE CORPORATE GOVERNANCE STANDARDS ACROSS LISTED COMPANIES.
WHO ANNOUNCED THIS INITIATIVE AND WHEN?
SEBI CHAIRMAN TUHIN KANTA PANDEY ANNOUNCED THE INITIATIVE ON APRIL 6, 2026 AT THE 19TH CII GOVERNANCE SUMMIT IN MUMBAI.
WHY IS THIS INITIATIVE NEEDED NOW?
THE INITIATIVE FOLLOWS A SURGE IN INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR RESIGNATIONS—549 IN FY2025 ALONE—AND RECENT GOVERNANCE FAILURES AT COMPANIES LIKE PTC INDIA FINANCIAL SERVICES AND GENSOL ENGINEERING.
WHAT WILL THE TRAINING PROGRAMME COVER?
THE CAPACITY-BUILDING EFFORTS WILL FOCUS ON BOARD EFFECTIVENESS, GOVERNANCE STANDARDS, REGULATORY COMPLIANCE, AND MOVING FROM PERIODIC REVIEWS TO CONTINUOUS OVERSIGHT.
IS THERE A TIMELINE FOR IMPLEMENTATION?
SEBI HAS NOT DISCLOSED A SPECIFIC IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE. THE INITIATIVE IS DESCRIBED AS A COLLABORATIVE FRAMEWORK INVOLVING CORPORATES, ACADEMIA, AND PROFESSIONAL BODIES.
