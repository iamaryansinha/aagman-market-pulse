India's Marine Exports Hit ₹62,408 Crore in 2024-25, Government Sets ₹1 Lakh Crore Target

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's marine product exports reached ₹62,408 crore in 2024-25, more than doubling from ₹30,213 crore in 2013-14
  • 02/Seafood exports grew at 7% average annual rate over 11 years, with shrimp contributing ₹43,334 crore
  • 03/Government urges exporters to achieve ₹1 lakh crore target through market diversification and value addition

India's marine product exports hit a record ₹62,408 crore in the financial year 2024-25. The figure marks a 106% increase from ₹30,213 crore recorded in 2013-14.

Fisheries Minister Rajiv Ranjan Singh announced the numbers at the Seafood Exporters Meet 2026. He urged exporters to work towards achieving the ₹1 lakh crore export target.

Seafood exports have grown at a consistent 7% average annual rate over the past 11 years. Despite global uncertainties, the sector recorded robust growth driven by strong performance in non-US markets.

Shrimp exports remain the dominant contributor, valued at ₹43,334 crore. Frozen shrimp continues to be India's flagship seafood product in international markets.

The United States remains the largest export destination, accounting for 36.42% of total value. Other key markets include China, the European Union, Southeast Asia, Japan, and the Middle East.

India exports over 350 varieties of seafood products to nearly 130 global markets. The export basket includes frozen shrimp, frozen fish, squid, cuttlefish, surimi-based products, and live seafood.

The government is actively pursuing diversification under the Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY). Focus areas include quality fish seed production, brackish-water aquaculture expansion, and promotion of export-oriented species.

Investments are being made to strengthen post-harvest infrastructure, cold-chain networks, modern fishing harbours, and fish landing centres. These measures aim to reduce losses and improve export competitiveness.

The Minister emphasized strict regulatory compliance, including adherence to antibiotic bans and strengthened traceability systems. The government assured support from EIC, NCDC, NABARD, and the Ministry of Food Processing Industries.
