LPG Shortage Crisis: Government Raises Commercial Allocation to 70%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government raises commercial LPG allocation to 70% for industries to address severe supply shortage
  • 02/LPG crisis forces restaurants and tea stalls to shut, hitting tea demand at Kochi auctions significantly
  • 03/Additional 20% allocation available subject to registration with oil marketing companies and PNG reform adoption

The Indian government has raised commercial LPG allocation to 70% for industries amid a severe supply shortage. The move aims to support businesses struggling with cooking gas availability.

The crisis has hit tea demand at Kochi auctions as restaurants and tea stalls remain shut. Many eateries have been forced to close due to lack of cooking gas supply.

Tea purchases at Kochi auctions have dropped significantly as buyers struggle to operate. The shortage has disrupted the food service sector across multiple states.

An additional 20% LPG allocation is available for industries meeting specific conditions. Companies must register with oil marketing companies and adopt PNG reforms.

Exemptions apply for industries requiring specific LPG types that cannot switch to alternatives. These businesses will receive priority allocation based on their unique requirements.

The measures were announced on March 27, 2026, as the government responds to mounting pressure from industry groups. The allocation increase applies immediately to registered commercial users.
