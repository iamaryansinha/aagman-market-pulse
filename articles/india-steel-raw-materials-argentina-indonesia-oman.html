India Steel Raw Material Hunt: Eyes Argentina, Indonesia, Oman for Supply Security

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India is in talks with Argentina, Indonesia, and Oman to secure steelmaking raw materials including coking coal and iron ore, sources say
  • 02/Move comes ahead of global steel summit where supply chain security and decarbonization are key agenda items
  • 03/Government aims to reduce dependence on Australia and South Africa; deals may be finalized by Q2 2026

India has initiated discussions with Argentina, Indonesia, and Oman to diversify its steelmaking raw material sources. The negotiations cover coking coal, iron ore, and scrap metal supplies, according to government and industry sources.

The push comes as New Delhi prepares to host a major global steel summit in April 2026. Supply chain resilience and green steel transition are expected to dominate the agenda.

Currently, India imports over 85% of its coking coal requirements. Australia and South Africa remain the dominant suppliers, creating concentration risk.

Argentina offers high-quality metallurgical coal reserves. Indonesia provides both thermal and coking coal with shorter shipping routes. Oman has emerged as a scrap metal hub with competitive pricing.

The Ministry of Steel has set a target to reduce import dependence to 70% by 2030. This diversification drive is central to that strategy.

A steel ministry official said the government is exploring long-term supply agreements. Price volatility and geopolitical disruptions have made diversification urgent.

The global steel summit on April 15-16 will bring together producers from 25 countries. India aims to position itself as a reliable demand center for raw material suppliers.

Domestic steel production crossed 140 million tonnes in FY2026. Raw material security is critical to sustain the 300 million tonne target by 2030.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHICH COUNTRIES IS INDIA TARGETING FOR STEEL RAW MATERIALS?
INDIA IS IN ACTIVE DISCUSSIONS WITH ARGENTINA, INDONESIA, AND OMAN TO SECURE SUPPLIES OF COKING COAL, IRON ORE, AND SCRAP METAL. THESE COUNTRIES OFFER GEOGRAPHIC DIVERSIFICATION AND COMPETITIVE PRICING COMPARED TO CURRENT SUPPLIERS.
WHY IS INDIA DIVERSIFYING ITS RAW MATERIAL SOURCES NOW?
INDIA CURRENTLY IMPORTS OVER 85% OF ITS COKING COAL NEEDS, PRIMARILY FROM AUSTRALIA AND SOUTH AFRICA. GEOPOLITICAL RISKS AND PRICE VOLATILITY HAVE MADE DIVERSIFICATION A STRATEGIC PRIORITY TO ENSURE SUPPLY CHAIN RESILIENCE FOR ITS GROWING STEEL SECTOR.
WHAT IS THE GLOBAL STEEL SUMMIT AND WHEN IS IT HAPPENING?
THE GLOBAL STEEL SUMMIT IS SCHEDULED FOR APRIL 15-16, 2026, IN NEW DELHI. IT WILL BRING TOGETHER PRODUCERS FROM 25 COUNTRIES TO DISCUSS SUPPLY CHAIN SECURITY, DECARBONIZATION, AND GREEN STEEL TRANSITION INITIATIVES.
WHAT ARE INDIA'S STEEL PRODUCTION TARGETS?
INDIA PRODUCED OVER 140 MILLION TONNES OF STEEL IN FY2026. THE GOVERNMENT HAS SET A TARGET TO REACH 300 MILLION TONNES BY 2030, MAKING RAW MATERIAL SECURITY CRITICAL FOR SUSTAINABLE GROWTH.
WHAT IS THE TIMELINE FOR FINALIZING THESE RAW MATERIAL DEALS?
SOURCES INDICATE THAT PRELIMINARY AGREEMENTS MAY BE FINALIZED BY Q2 2026. THE MINISTRY OF STEEL IS PRIORITIZING LONG-TERM SUPPLY CONTRACTS TO REDUCE IMPORT DEPENDENCE FROM 85% TO 70% BY 2030.
