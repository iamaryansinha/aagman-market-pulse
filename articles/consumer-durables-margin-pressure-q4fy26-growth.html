Consumer Durables Face Margin Pressure Despite Steady Q4FY26 Growth Outlook

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Consumer durables companies face 200-240 bps margin compression due to rising raw material costs, with copper prices surging from $8,500 to $12,000-13,000 per tonne
  • 02/Q4FY26 revenue outlook remains steady at 15% YoY growth for majors like Havells, driven by strong cables and AC demand despite margin pressures
  • 03/Summer demand expected to boost sales of ACs, fans, and coolers; GST reduction from 28% to 18% may offset some cost pressures

India's consumer durables sector is navigating a challenging operating environment in Q4FY26. Rising raw material costs are squeezing margins even as revenue growth holds steady.

Copper prices have surged significantly, climbing from approximately $8,500 per tonne to $12,000-13,000 per tonne. This sharp increase is pressuring production costs across the industry.

Aluminum and plastic prices have also shown volatility. While aluminum saw some correction, PP prices remain elevated, creating a mixed cost environment for manufacturers.

Havells India, the second-largest player in consumer electricals, has seen operating margins decline from 11.57% in Q4 FY25 to 9.24% in Q3 FY26. This 240 basis point compression reflects intense competitive pressures and input cost challenges.

Crompton Greaves faces similar headwinds. The company's cost of materials consumed is forecast to rise 12% year-on-year in 2026 to ₹18.2 billion, according to S&P Global analysis.

Despite margin pressures, revenue growth remains resilient. Havells is expected to clock 15% YoY revenue growth in Q4FY25, driven by its cables and wires segment expanding 18-20%.

The Lloyd brand under Havells is witnessing strong performance. Lloyd sales jumped 40% YoY in recent quarters, supported by scorching pre-summer temperatures driving AC demand.

Voltas maintains its leadership position with roughly 18% market share in air conditioners. The company aims to reach 20% market share by 2030, targeting 1% growth annually.

Summer season demand is expected to provide relief. Dealers are actively stocking inventory ahead of the season, with AC inquiries rising 25-30% in early 2025 compared to last year.

The GST reduction on air conditioners from 28% to 18%, announced in September 2025, is expected to boost consumer sentiment. However, rising copper prices have offset much of this benefit.

Wires and cables continue to outperform other segments. KEI Industries, Polycab, and RR Kabel are seeing 15-20% revenue growth driven by infrastructure demand and copper restocking.

Analysts expect margin stabilization in Q4FY26 as seasonal demand peaks. Management commentary on pricing actions and cost control measures will be critical monitorables.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS CAUSING MARGIN PRESSURE IN THE CONSUMER DURABLES SECTOR?
RISING RAW MATERIAL COSTS, PARTICULARLY COPPER WHICH SURGED FROM $8,500 TO $12,000-13,000 PER TONNE, ARE SQUEEZING MARGINS. ALUMINUM AND PLASTIC PRICES ALSO SHOW VOLATILITY. HAVELLS' OPERATING MARGINS DECLINED FROM 11.57% IN Q4 FY25 TO 9.24% IN Q3 FY26, A 240 BPS COMPRESSION.
HOW IS Q4FY26 REVENUE GROWTH EXPECTED TO PERFORM?
REVENUE GROWTH REMAINS STEADY AT AROUND 15% YOY FOR MAJOR PLAYERS. HAVELLS' CABLES AND WIRES SEGMENT IS EXPECTED TO GROW 18-20%, WHILE ITS LLOYD AC BRAND HAS REPORTED 40% YOY GROWTH. THE OVERALL FMEG SEGMENT IS EXPECTED TO GROW 4.9% YOY.
WHICH COMPANIES ARE MOST AFFECTED BY MARGIN PRESSURE?
HAVELLS INDIA HAS SEEN SIGNIFICANT MARGIN COMPRESSION WITH OPERATING MARGINS BELOW 9.5% FOR CONSECUTIVE QUARTERS. CROMPTON GREAVES FACES MATERIAL COST RISES OF 12% YOY TO ₹18.2 BILLION IN 2026. VOLTAS HAS ALSO EXPERIENCED MARGIN PRESSURE IN ITS UCP AND LLOYD SEGMENTS.
WHAT IS THE SUMMER SEASON DEMAND OUTLOOK FOR 2025?
DEALERS ARE STOCKING INVENTORY AGGRESSIVELY AFTER LEARNING FROM PAST SHORTAGES. AC INQUIRIES HAVE RISEN 25-30% IN MARCH 2025 COMPARED TO LAST YEAR. DEMAND FOR FANS IS STRONG IN SOUTHERN INDIA, WHILE AIR COOLER DEMAND IS EXPECTED TO RISE NATIONWIDE AS TEMPERATURES CLIMB.
HOW WILL THE GST REDUCTION IMPACT CONSUMER DURABLES?
THE GST ON AIR CONDITIONERS WAS REDUCED FROM 28% TO 18% IN SEPTEMBER 2025, WHICH SHOULD REDUCE PRICES BY ABOUT 7.8%. HOWEVER, RISING COPPER PRICES AND DOLLAR FLUCTUATIONS HAVE OFFSET MUCH OF THIS BENEFIT, WITH AC PRICES EXPECTED TO RISE 5-15% DESPITE THE TAX CUT.
