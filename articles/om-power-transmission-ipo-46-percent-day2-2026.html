Om Power Transmission IPO Subscribed 46% on Day 2, QIBs Lead Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Om Power Transmission IPO subscribed 0.46 times on day 2 morning, with QIB category leading at 0.78 times
  • 02/Issue priced at ₹166-175 per share aims to raise ₹150 crore through fresh issue of 75.75 lakh shares and 10 lakh shares OFS
  • 03/IPO subscription closes on April 13, 2026; company reported ₹279 crore revenue in FY25 with 52% CAGR

Om Power Transmission Ltd's IPO has received bids for 27.82 lakh shares against 60.03 lakh shares on offer. The subscription stands at 0.46 times as of 11:06 AM on day 2.

The qualified institutional buyer (QIB) category leads with 0.78 times subscription. QIBs have bid for 13.37 lakh shares against 17.15 lakh shares reserved.

Foreign institutional investors dominate QIB demand with 11.43 lakh shares. This indicates strong institutional interest in the power transmission EPC player.

The retail individual investor (RII) category is subscribed at 0.42 times. RIIs have bid for 12.48 lakh shares against 30.01 lakh shares reserved.

The non-institutional investor (NII) category trails at 0.15 times. NIIs have bid for just 1.97 lakh shares against 12.86 lakh shares on offer.

The IPO is priced at ₹166–175 per share. It aims to raise ₹150 crore at the upper price band.

The issue comprises a fresh issue of 75.75 lakh shares and an offer for sale of 10 lakh shares by three promoters.

Net proceeds will fund long-term working capital (₹55 crore), debt repayment (₹25 crore), and machinery purchase (₹11.2 crore).

The Ahmedabad-based company reported revenue of ₹279 crore in FY25. It grew at a CAGR of 52 per cent between FY23 and FY25.

Its unexecuted order book stood at ₹744.6 crore as of December 2025. The book-to-bill ratio stands at 2.7 times.

SBI Securities has recommended subscribing for the long term. The issue is valued at 19.2 times annualised 9MFY26 earnings at the upper price band.

The IPO closes on April 13, 2026.
