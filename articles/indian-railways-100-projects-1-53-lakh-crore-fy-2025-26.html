Indian Railways Sanctions 100 Projects Worth ₹1.53 Lakh Crore for FY 2025-26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian Railways clears 100 major projects worth ₹1.53 lakh crore for FY 2025-26, marking a 56% increase over 64 projects last year
  • 02/Investment commitment jumps over 110% from ₹72,869 crore in FY 2024-25; network coverage surges 114% to 6,000 km
  • 03/Maharashtra (17), Bihar (11), Jharkhand (10), and Madhya Pradesh (9) emerge as top focus states for rail expansion

Indian Railways has sanctioned 100 major projects worth ₹1.53 lakh crore for FY 2025-26. This marks a 110% jump in investment compared to the previous financial year.

The projects cover over 6,000 kilometres of railway network. This represents a 114% surge in route coverage from FY 2024-25.

A total of 64 projects worth ₹72,869 crore covering 2,800 km were sanctioned last year. Project approvals have increased by 56% year-on-year.

The sanctioned works include new lines, doubling, multitracking, bypass lines, flyovers and chord lines. These aim to decongest saturated routes and improve punctuality.

Maharashtra leads with 17 projects, followed by Bihar with 11 and Jharkhand with 10. Madhya Pradesh accounts for 9 projects in the pipeline.

Over 35 projects exceed ₹1,000 crore each. These form the backbone of corridor-level upgrades across the network.

Major projects include the Kasara-Manmad 3rd and 4th line (131 km) at ₹10,150 crore. The Kharsia-Naya Raipur-Parmalkasa 5th & 6th line (278 km) is valued at over ₹8,740 crore.

The Itarsi-Nagpur 4th line (297 km) comes at over ₹5,450 crore. The Secunderabad-Wadi 3rd and 4th line (173 km) exceeds ₹5,000 crore.

These four projects alone account for over ₹28,000 crore. They are strategically aligned with the Mission 3000 MT cargo capacity initiative.

Energy corridor projects dominate the portfolio. These facilitate faster coal and mineral movement, strengthening India's energy security.

The investments are expected to generate substantial employment. They will stimulate demand in core sectors like steel and cement while reducing logistics costs.

"This is not incremental progress; this is India laying the tracks for its next economic leap," the official press note stated. The projects align with PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan for inclusive growth.
