Saudi Arabia Hikes Arab Light OSP To Record $19.50 Per Barrel For Asia In May

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Saudi Aramco hikes May Arab Light OSP for Asia to $19.50 per barrel, a record premium over Dubai-Oman benchmark
  • 02/The price increase reflects strong Asian demand and tightening global supply amid OPEC+ output cuts
  • 03/Indian refiners face higher import costs; analysts warn of margin pressure for state-run processors

Saudi Aramco has raised the Official Selling Price for Arab Light crude to Asian buyers for May loading. The new OSP is set at $19.50 per barrel above the Dubai-Oman benchmark, marking a historic high.

The May premium represents a sharp increase from April's $16.90 per barrel differential. This is the highest premium Saudi Arabia has ever charged Asian refiners for its flagship crude grade.

The price hike reflects robust demand from Asian buyers. China's reopening and India's industrial activity have driven up crude consumption in the region.

Tight global supply conditions support the move. OPEC+ production cuts have reduced available barrels in the market. Saudi Arabia itself has maintained output discipline.

Indian refiners will face significant cost pressure from this increase. State-run processors like Indian Oil, BPCL, and HPCL import substantial volumes of Saudi crude monthly.

Higher import costs may squeeze refining margins. Analysts warn that passing on costs to consumers could prove difficult given regulatory constraints on fuel pricing.

The OSP formula links Saudi crude prices to the Dubai-Oman benchmark. This regional marker reflects spot market conditions for Middle Eastern sour crudes.

Market reaction has been mixed. Some traders anticipated the hike given strong refining margins. Others view the premium as unsustainable at current levels.

Dubai crude swaps have rallied on the news. Asian sour crude differentials remain elevated amid supply constraints.

The outlook depends on OPEC+ policy. Further production cuts could support even higher differentials. Demand resilience in China remains the key variable.

Indian refiners may seek alternatives. Russian and African crudes could become more attractive if discounts persist.

The premium signals Saudi confidence in Asian demand. It also tests the willingness of refiners to absorb higher feedstock costs.
