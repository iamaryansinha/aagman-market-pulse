CEA Nageswaran Warns Of Downside Risk To FY27 Growth Forecast At 7-7.4%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran flags considerable downside risks to India's FY27 growth forecast of 7-7.4%
  • 02/West Asia crisis poses threats through oil supply disruptions, higher import costs, and potential decline in Gulf remittances
  • 03/March high-frequency indicators show moderation in economic activity; government calls for spending re-prioritisation

Chief Economic Advisor V Anantha Nageswaran has issued a stern warning. He flagged considerable downside risk to India's FY27 growth projections.

The growth forecast was recently revised upward to 7-7.4%. This followed the GDP base year revision announced on February 27, 2026. The earlier estimate stood at 6.8-7.2%.

The West Asia crisis presents multiple threat channels. Supply disruptions to oil, gas, and fertilisers top the concerns. Export routes face potential blockages.

Higher import prices will squeeze margins across sectors. Logistics costs including freight and insurance are climbing steadily. These add inflationary pressure to the economy.

Remittances from Gulf countries may decline significantly. This would impact household incomes in key states like Kerala and Karnataka. The services trade surplus could narrow.

The current account deficit will widen in FY27. Oil imports and reduced export competitiveness are primary drivers. External sector vulnerabilities need close monitoring.

March 2026 high-frequency indicators reveal cooling momentum. E-way bills dropped 5.3% month-on-month. However, they remain 9.4% higher year-on-year.

Flash PMI data shows softening output growth. Manufacturing expansion has decelerated from peak levels. New order flows are moderating sequentially.

Vehicle registrations rose 19.1% year-on-year in March. This signals resilient consumer demand in the automobile sector. Rural consumption may provide a buffer.

Digital payments continue posting double-digit growth. UPI transaction volumes indicate strong formalisation trends. Financial inclusion metrics remain healthy.

Rural sentiment has softened according to the RECSS survey. Agricultural income pressures are weighing on non-urban demand. Monsoon performance will be critical for recovery.

Nageswaran called for urgent re-prioritisation of government spending. Targeted relief measures should focus on vulnerable sections. Fiscal space is constrained by revenue uncertainties.

The government remains vigilant on energy availability. Strategic petroleum reserves are being monitored closely. Inflation management stays the top policy priority.
