Stock Picks Today: Wipro, BPCL, DCM Shriram, DCX Systems in Focus

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Wipro board approves ₹15,000 crore share buyback through tender route at ₹250 per share, representing 5.7% of equity
  • 02/BPCL's Brazilian oil project gets Final Investment Decision; DCM Shriram forms PolyTek JV with Teknor Apex
  • 03/DCX Systems wins ₹9.30 crore order from Israel; RVNL emerges L1 bidder for ₹967.99 crore railway project

Wipro (NSE: WIPRO) has announced a major buyback program worth ₹15,000 crore. The board approved the proposal on Thursday to repurchase up to 60 crore shares at ₹250 per share through the tender route.

The buyback represents 5.7% of Wipro's total paid-up equity share capital. Company promoters have indicated their intention to participate in the offer, signaling confidence in the IT major's valuation.

Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (NSE: BPCL) received a boost through its Brazilian operations. Petrobras approved the Final Investment Decision for the SEAP-I oil and gas project in the BM-SEAL-11 Concession.

BPCL holds a 65.40% equity stake in IBV Brasil Petróleo Ltd through its subsidiary Bharat PetroResources Ltd. IBV owns a 40% participating interest in the Brazilian concession alongside Petrobras as operator.

DCM Shriram Ltd (NSE: DCMSHRIRAM) has expanded its polymer business through a joint venture. Its subsidiary Shriram Polytech entered into a 50:50 partnership with Teknor Apex B.V. to operate under the brand name PolyTek.

The JV combines Shriram Polytech's domestic manufacturing capabilities with Teknor Apex's global formulation expertise. The partnership aims to deliver high-performance specialty polymer solutions for industrial applications.

DCX Systems Ltd (NSE: DCXINDIA) has secured a purchase order from IAI-ELTA Systems Limited of Israel. The order is for manufacture and supply of Transmitter Receiver Modules valued at approximately ₹9.30 crore.

Rail Vikas Nigam Ltd (NSE: RVNL) emerged as the Lowest Bidder (L1) from East Coast Railway. The EPC mode order is valued at ₹967.99 crore including 18% GST for railway infrastructure development.

KPI Green Energy received an Inter-State Power Trading Licence (Category IV) from CERC. This enables the company to trade electricity across state boundaries and participate in national power markets.

Pace Digitek reported robust order inflows of ₹6,459.7 crore for FY2026. The energy segment contributed significantly, reflecting strong traction in Battery Energy Storage Systems and renewable energy projects.

MosChip Technologies approved acquisition of a 73% stake in Vayavya Labs. This strengthens MosChip's software capabilities in the automotive and semiconductor segments.

Dodla Dairy's subsidiary secured 7.15 acres in Bihar's Dumaria for a new dairy manufacturing facility. The company will produce milk, paneer, ghee, and other milk products under a 90-year lease from BIADA.

Aditya Infotech executed a 50:50 JV agreement with Orient Cables. The partnership will manufacture electric cables, LAN cables, CCTV cables, and allied products for the security sector.

Refex Industries secured a ₹32.45 crore order for transportation of pond ash to NHAI and PWD road construction sites.

Rishabh Instruments' subsidiary Lumel SA won a €3 million (₹30 crore) order from a Germany-based energy company. The order covers supply of electronic devices for industrial automation through 2027.
