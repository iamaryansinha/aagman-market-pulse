Silver Futures Jump ₹2,273 to ₹2.34 Lakh Per Kg on Global Rally, Iran Tensions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/MCX silver futures surge ₹2,273 to ₹2,34,373 per kg, tracking gains in global spot markets and safe-haven demand
  • 02/Gold-silver ratio stands at 85, indicating silver remains undervalued relative to gold
  • 03/Resistance seen at ₹2,40,000 per kg; Iran tensions could push prices higher if conflict escalates

Silver futures on the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) jumped ₹2,273 to ₹2,34,373 per kg on April 6. The rally follows firm global silver trends and safe-haven buying.

Global spot silver climbed above $32 per ounce, hitting multi-week highs. The US Federal Reserve's dovish stance on interest rates weakened the dollar.

A weaker dollar makes precious metals cheaper for overseas buyers. This has boosted demand across COMEX and Asian markets alike.

Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East added further support. Iran's missile strikes and retaliatory threats drove investors toward safe-haven assets.

The gold-silver ratio currently trades near 85 levels. Historically, ratios above 80 suggest silver is undervalued versus gold.

Traders expect immediate resistance at ₹2,40,000 per kg. Support is placed around ₹2,28,000 per kg for the near term.

Retail and industrial demand in India remains steady ahead of the festive season. Imports have picked up as price corrections attracted buyers.

Analysts suggest a break above ₹2,40,000 could trigger further short-covering. Volatility is expected to remain elevated amid geopolitical risks.
