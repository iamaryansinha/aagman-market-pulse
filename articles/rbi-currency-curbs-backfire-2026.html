RBI's Currency Curbs Risk Backfiring as Banks Face $100 Million Position Limit

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/RBI limits authorised dealers' net open position in rupee to $100 million at end of each business day
  • 02/Rupee has depreciated 4% since West Asia war began on February 28, 2026, hitting record lows near 95 per dollar
  • 03/Banks must comply by April 10, 2026; critics warn curbs could backfire by reducing market liquidity

The Reserve Bank of India has imposed a $100 million cap on banks' net open positions in the rupee. Authorised dealers must comply by April 10, 2026.

The circular targets the onshore deliverable market. It aims to curb speculative bets against the weakening rupee.

The rupee has declined 4% since the West Asia war began on February 28, 2026. It hit a record low of 95.23 per US dollar on March 31, 2026.

Rising crude oil prices and foreign portfolio outflows have intensified dollar demand. The rupee is on track for its first fiscal-year decline in over a decade.

Banks had built sizeable long-dollar positions through arbitrage trades. They bought dollars onshore and sold in offshore NDF markets to exploit price gaps.

The cap forces banks to unwind these exposures. This theoretically supports the rupee by creating dollar selling pressure.

However, critics argue the curbs could backfire. Reduced bank participation may shrink market liquidity and increase volatility.

The measure has limited direct impact on offshore NDF markets. Global hedge funds and banks continue trading without RBI oversight.

YES Securities noted rising energy costs are adding to inflation concerns. The near-term rupee outlook remains weak despite regulatory intervention.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE RBI'S NEW CAP ON BANK CURRENCY POSITIONS?
THE RESERVE BANK OF INDIA HAS DIRECTED AUTHORISED DEALERS TO MAINTAIN THEIR NET OPEN POSITION IN INDIAN RUPEE (NOP-INR) WITHIN $100 MILLION AT THE END OF EACH BUSINESS DAY IN THE ONSHORE DELIVERABLE MARKET.
WHAT IS THE DEADLINE FOR BANKS TO COMPLY WITH RBI'S CURRENCY CURBS?
AUTHORISED DEALERS MUST ENSURE COMPLIANCE WITH THE $100 MILLION POSITION LIMIT AT THE EARLIEST BUT NO LATER THAN APRIL 10, 2026.
WHY HAS THE RBI IMPOSED THESE CURRENCY CURBS?
THE MOVE AIMS TO CURB SPECULATION AGAINST THE RUPEE AMID 4% DEPRECIATION SINCE THE WEST ASIA WAR BEGAN ON FEBRUARY 28, 2026. THE RUPEE HAS HIT MULTIPLE RECORD LOWS, REACHING 95.23 PER US DOLLAR.
HOW COULD THESE CURBS BACKFIRE?
CRITICS WARN THAT CAPPING BANK POSITIONS COULD REDUCE MARKET LIQUIDITY, INCREASE VOLATILITY, AND PUSH MORE TRADING TO OFFSHORE MARKETS BEYOND RBI'S DIRECT CONTROL, POTENTIALLY UNDERMINING RUPEE STABILITY.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT STATE OF THE RUPEE?
THE RUPEE HAS DEPRECIATED APPROXIMATELY 4% SINCE LATE FEBRUARY 2026, PRESSURED BY RISING ENERGY COSTS, FOREIGN PORTFOLIO OUTFLOWS FROM DOMESTIC EQUITY MARKETS, AND A STRENGTHENING US DOLLAR.
