Black Buck Stock: Elara Capital Initiates Buy Rating With ₹1,315 Target

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Elara Capital initiates coverage on Black Buck with Buy rating and DCF-based target price of ₹1,315, representing 123% upside from CMP of ₹589
  • 02/Core business (payments and telematics) contributes 92% of FY25 revenue; SuperLoads growth initiative targets 10x revenue expansion by FY28
  • 03/Key risks include toll fee economics changes, operational complexity in scaling SuperLoads, and competition from banks and fintech players

Elara Capital has initiated coverage on Black Buck with a Buy rating. The brokerage has set a DCF-based target price of ₹1,315, implying 123% upside from the current market price of ₹589.

Black Buck, formerly known as Zinka Logistics Solutions Ltd, operates India's largest digital platform for truck operators. The company serves 3.5 million truckers across payments, telematics, and freight marketplace services.

The core business comprising payments and telematics generated 92% of revenue in FY25. This segment is profitable with 90%+ contribution margins and strong cash flow generation.

SuperLoads represents Black Buck's major growth initiative. The digital freight marketplace targets a $135-140 billion unorganised freight opportunity in India.

Elara expects SuperLoads revenue to grow 10x by FY28 through geographic replication. Core profits will fund this expansion without diluting shareholder value.

The brokerage values the core business at ₹665 per share. SuperLoads contributes ₹149 to the target price based on its growth potential.

Adjusted EBITDA margins may moderate to 28-29% by FY28. This reflects reinvestment needs as SuperLoads scales across India.

Black Buck holds approximately 50% market share in commercial vehicle tolling. The FASTag-based payments network serves over 7.9 lakh monthly transacting truck operators.

The company also operates 4.2 lakh active telematics devices. This represents 23% of India's total trucker base.

Elara notes three key risks. Adverse toll fee economics could compress take rates. Scaling SuperLoads without capital intensity remains critical. Competition from banks and fintech platforms could pressure market share.

The stock has gained 42% over the past year. It currently trades at a P/E ratio of 28x with a market capitalisation of ₹10,716 crore.
