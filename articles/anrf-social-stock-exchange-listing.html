ANRF Social Stock Exchange Listing: Govt Plans SSE Debut for Research Funding

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government plans to list Anusandhan National Research Foundation on Social Stock Exchange to diversify funding beyond annual budgetary grants
  • 02/Parliamentary Standing Committee report found ANRF did not utilize any budget in FY 2023-24 and FY 2024-25, spent only 61% of Rs 2,000 crore in FY 2025-26
  • 03/Listing aims to tap global CSR funds and Indian diaspora remittances worth $138 billion annually for India's research ecosystem

The government plans to list the Anusandhan National Research Foundation on the Social Stock Exchange. This will enable India's apex research funding body to access global CSR funds and diaspora contributions.

ANRF was established under the ANRF Act passed in August 2023. It coordinates scientific research across universities, industry, and government institutions nationwide.

The SSE listing aims to create a diversified funding base less dependent on annual budgetary grants. The Parliamentary Standing Committee on Science and Technology recommended this move in its recent report.

The Committee flagged serious fund utilization issues at ANRF. The foundation did not use any budget allocation in fiscal years 2023-24 and 2024-25.

For the current fiscal year 2025-26, ANRF has spent only 61% of its Rs 2,000 crore budget. However, the foundation has disbursed ₹961.35 crore in research funds as of last week.

The Social Stock Exchange was first announced in Budget 2019-20 by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman. SEBI subsequently created the framework, and both BSE and NSE launched SSE segments in 2023.

CSR spending by Indian companies grew 30% year-on-year to Rs 19,208 crore in FY2024. Indian diaspora remittances reached $138 billion annually, creating a large potential funding pool.

The first SSE listing was SGBS Unnati Foundation in December 2023. Swades Foundation raised Rs 10 crore through the SSE in 2024.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS ANRF AND WHY IS IT BEING LISTED ON THE SOCIAL STOCK EXCHANGE?
ANRF (ANUSANDHAN NATIONAL RESEARCH FOUNDATION) IS INDIA'S APEX BODY FOR FUNDING AND COORDINATING SCIENTIFIC RESEARCH. THE GOVERNMENT PLANS TO LIST IT ON THE SOCIAL STOCK EXCHANGE TO DIVERSIFY FUNDING SOURCES AND TAP GLOBAL CSR FUNDS AND DIASPORA CONTRIBUTIONS BEYOND ANNUAL BUDGETARY GRANTS.
WHAT DID THE PARLIAMENTARY STANDING COMMITTEE REPORT REVEAL ABOUT ANRF'S FUND UTILIZATION?
THE COMMITTEE FOUND THAT ANRF DID NOT UTILIZE ANY OF ITS BUDGET ALLOCATION IN FISCAL YEARS 2023-24 AND 2024-25. IN THE CURRENT FISCAL YEAR 2025-26, IT HAS ONLY SPENT 61% OF ITS RS 2,000 CRORE BUDGET ALLOCATION.
WHAT IS THE SOCIAL STOCK EXCHANGE AND WHEN WAS IT ESTABLISHED?
THE SOCIAL STOCK EXCHANGE IS A SEBI-REGULATED PLATFORM ON BSE AND NSE THAT ALLOWS SOCIAL ENTERPRISES AND VOLUNTARY ORGANIZATIONS TO RAISE CAPITAL. IT WAS FIRST ANNOUNCED IN BUDGET 2019-20 AND OPERATIONALIZED IN 2023.
HOW MUCH HAS ANRF DISBURSED IN RESEARCH FUNDS SO FAR?
AS OF LAST WEEK, ANRF HAS DISBURSED ₹961.35 CRORE IN RESEARCH FUNDS. THE FOUNDATION SUPPORTS SCHEMES LIKE THE PRIME MINISTER'S EARLY CAREER RESEARCH GRANT, ADVANCED RESEARCH GRANT, AND MISSION-MODE PROGRAMS IN AI, MEDTECH, AND ELECTRIC MOBILITY.
WHAT IS THE POTENTIAL FUNDING POOL AVAILABLE THROUGH CSR AND DIASPORA CONTRIBUTIONS?
CORPORATE SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITY SPENDING BY INDIAN COMPANIES REACHED RS 19,208 CRORE IN FY2024, UP 30% YEAR-ON-YEAR. INDIAN DIASPORA REMITTANCES TOTAL $138 BILLION ANNUALLY, WITH INDIAN-AMERICAN PHILANTHROPIC CONTRIBUTIONS ESTIMATED AT $4-5 BILLION IN 2024.
