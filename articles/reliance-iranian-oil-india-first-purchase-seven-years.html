Reliance Buys 5 Million Barrels Iranian Crude: India's First Purchase in Seven Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Reliance Industries purchases 5 million barrels of Iranian crude, marking India's first import from Iran in nearly seven years
  • 02/US issued 30-day sanctions waiver allowing oil loaded by March 20 and discharged by April 19, 2026
  • 03/Crude priced at $7 premium to ICE Brent; other Indian refiners including IOC and BPCL exploring similar purchases

Reliance Industries Ltd has purchased 5 million barrels of Iranian crude oil. The transaction marks India's first import from Iran since May 2019.

The deal was executed under a 30-day US sanctions waiver issued on March 20, 2026. The waiver permits purchase of Iranian oil loaded on or before March 20 and discharged by April 19.

Reliance bought the crude from National Iranian Oil Company (NIOC) at a premium. The cargo was priced approximately $7 per barrel above ICE Brent futures.

The purchase comes nearly seven years after India halted Iranian oil imports. Washington reimposed sanctions on Tehran in 2018, forcing Indian refiners to stop purchases by May 2019.

At its peak in 2016-17, Iran supplied 27.1 million tonnes of crude to India. This accounted for 12.6% of India's total oil imports that year.

Indian state refiners including IOC, BPCL, and HPCL are evaluating Iranian purchases. MRPL and HMEL have also returned to the market after avoiding Russian crude since December.

The waiver coincides with surging global oil prices. Brent crude trades around $101-$102 per barrel amid US-Israel conflict and Strait of Hormuz disruptions.

China's Sinopec has declined to purchase Iranian crude under the waiver. This leaves India as the primary Asian buyer competing for an estimated 170 million barrels of Iranian oil at sea.
