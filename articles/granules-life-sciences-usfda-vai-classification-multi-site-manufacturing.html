Granules Life Sciences Bags VAI Classification from USFDA, Bolstering Multi-Site Manufacturing Capabilities

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Granules Life Sciences, a wholly owned subsidiary of Granules India, has received a Voluntary Action Indicated (VAI) classification from the US Food and Drug Administration following a cGMP and pre-approval inspection of its Shameerpet facility
  • 02/The inspection, conducted December 15-19, 2025, concluded with no regulatory action recommended
  • 03/Chairman Krishna Prasad Chigurupati emphasized this milestone strengthens the company's finished dosage manufacturing capabilities and enables multi-site production for approved products

The US Food and Drug Administration (USFDA) has closed its inspection of Granules Life Sciences' manufacturing facility at Shameerpet, Hyderabad, issuing a Voluntary Action Indicated (VAI) classification—a designation that signals acceptable compliance levels with no immediate enforcement action required.

The Establishment Inspection Report (EIR) follows a comprehensive current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) and pre-approval inspection (PAI) of the oral solid dosage manufacturing operations conducted between December 15 and 19, 2025. The company confirmed the inspection is now formally closed with no regulatory action recommended.

Granules Life Sciences operates as a wholly owned subsidiary of Granules India, the Hyderabad-based active pharmaceutical ingredient (API) and formulations major.

Chairman Krishna Prasad Chigurupati framed the development as both a validation and a commitment: "While receiving the classification is a step in the right direction, we recognise that quality is not a one-time milestone but an ongoing commitment. It will continue to remain a core pillar of utmost importance across all Granules sites, guiding our actions, investments, and culture every day."

Strategically, the VAI classification carries significant weight for Granules India's operational flexibility. The clearance enables multi-site manufacturing for approved products—allowing the company to distribute production across multiple facilities rather than concentrating it at a single location. This capability de-risks supply chains and provides scalability for contract manufacturing and proprietary formulations targeting the US market.

The Shameerpet facility's clearance comes at a time when Indian pharma manufacturers continue navigating heightened USFDA scrutiny. A VAI classification—indicating voluntary improvements may be warranted but no official action is required—positions Granules favorably relative to competitors facing more severe classifications like Official Action Indicated (OAI) or import alerts.

For Granules India, which derives a substantial portion of revenue from regulated markets, the multi-site manufacturing enablement could translate into faster product launches, improved supply reliability for existing customers, and enhanced bidding power for new contracts.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
Q: WHAT IS A VAI CLASSIFICATION FROM THE USFDA?
VOLUNTARY ACTION INDICATED (VAI) MEANS INSPECTORS IDENTIFIED OBSERVATIONS THAT WARRANT VOLUNTARY CORRECTIVE ACTION BY THE COMPANY, BUT THE VIOLATIONS ARE NOT SERIOUS ENOUGH TO WARRANT OFFICIAL ENFORCEMENT ACTION. IT IS CONSIDERED A FAVORABLE OUTCOME, SITTING BETWEEN "NO ACTION INDICATED" (BEST CASE) AND "OFFICIAL ACTION INDICATED" (OAI, WHICH TRIGGERS REGULATORY ACTION).
