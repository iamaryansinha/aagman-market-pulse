Sensex Crashes 1,563 Pts to 73,710; Nifty Below 22,450 as Iran War Rattles Markets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BSE Sensex crashed 1,563.26 points (2.08%) to settle at 73,710.19, its lowest level in over a year
  • 02/Nifty 50 plunged below 22,450, marking a 12-month low as Iran-US-Israel war escalates
  • 03/Brent crude trades near $115/barrel; analysts warn Nifty may test 22,000 if conflict persists

The BSE Sensex crashed 1,563.26 points or 2.08% to close at 73,710.19 on March 27, 2026. The Nifty 50 index plunged below the 22,450 mark, hitting its lowest level in over 12 months.

The sharp selloff was driven by escalating Iran war tensions entering their fourth week. US President Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Iran over the Strait of Hormuz closure.

Crude oil prices remained elevated with Brent trading near $115 per barrel. WTI crude hovered around $100 per barrel, marking a sharp surge since the conflict began on February 28.

Foreign institutional investors (FIIs) have sold equities worth ₹86,780 crore in March alone. Domestic institutional investors (DIIs) bought ₹1.11 lakh crore, but the buying failed to stem the decline.

All 16 major sectoral indices ended in the red. Nifty Bank fell 2.34%, while Nifty Auto, Metal, and Realty indices dropped 3-4% each. Midcap and Smallcap indices tumbled nearly 3%.

Adani Ports, Tata Steel, Bajaj Finance, Titan, and L&T were the biggest drags on the indices, falling 3-5%. Only IT stocks TCS, HCL Tech, and Tech Mahindra showed marginal gains.

The India VIX volatility index surged 15%, reflecting heightened fear among investors. Market capitalization of BSE-listed firms has fallen by ₹47.53 lakh crore since the war began.

"The uncertainty related to the war in West Asia will loom large over the market. The major risk is the energy risk arising from the surge in crude," said Dr. VK Vijayakumar, Chief Investment Strategist at Geojit Investments.

Normura has cut its Nifty target to 24,900 from 29,300 for 2026. CITI lowered its target to 27,000 from 28,500, citing prolonged geopolitical risks.

India imports over 85% of its crude requirements. Sustained oil prices above $100 could shave 50-60 basis points from GDP growth and widen the current account deficit significantly.

The Strait of Hormuz carries roughly 20 million barrels of oil daily. Iran has threatened a full blockade, disrupting over 40% of India's crude oil imports.

Global markets also faced heavy selling. Japan's Nikkei dropped 5%, while South Korea's KOSPI plunged over 6%, triggering a trading halt on KOSPI 200 futures.

Analysts warn that if the Nifty breaks below 22,450 decisively, the next support level lies at 22,000. Investors are advised to remain cautious and avoid aggressive fresh positions.
