5paisa Capital Rights Issue Oversubscribed 1.24 Times, Raises ₹468.8 Crore

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/5paisa Capital's ₹468.8 crore rights issue oversubscribed 1.24 times with bids for 1.93 crore shares against 1.56 crore offered
  • 02/Retail investors participated strongly despite ₹450 per share pricing at marginal premium to market rate
  • 03/Proceeds to fund customer acquisition, technology platform upgrades, and working capital requirements

Digital brokerage platform 5paisa Capital Limited has successfully closed its ₹468.8 crore rights issue. The offer received bids for approximately 1.93 crore shares against 1.56 crore shares offered, translating to an oversubscription of 1.24 times.

The issue opened on March 27 and closed on April 10, 2026. It was priced at ₹450 per share, representing a marginal premium to the prevailing market rate.

Retail investor participation was robust despite the pricing. This signals confidence in the company's strategic direction and the broader discount broking opportunity in India.

The rights issue was offered in the ratio of 1 equity share for every 5 equity shares held. Promoter IIFL Holdings participated fully in the offer, demonstrating their commitment.

Proceeds will be utilized for customer acquisition initiatives, technology platform upgrades, and working capital requirements. The company aims to strengthen its position in the competitive discount broking space.

5paisa reported 4.2 million active users as of March 2026. The platform has been expanding its product offerings beyond equity broking to include mutual funds, insurance, and lending products.

The Indian discount broking market has witnessed intense competition with Zerodha, Upstox, and Groww dominating market share. 5paisa's rights issue signals its intent to stay competitive.

Customer acquisition costs in the broking industry have risen sharply. The fresh capital provides runway for 5paisa to invest in growth without immediate profitability pressure.

The successful rights issue comes at a time when capital markets are experiencing volatility. The oversubscription indicates investor appetite for well-positioned fintech plays.
