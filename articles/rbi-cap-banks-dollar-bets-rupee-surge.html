RBI Caps Bank Dollar Bets at $100M; Rupee Could Surge 3% on Unwind

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/RBI mandates $100 million daily cap on banks' net open rupee positions, forcing unwind of long dollar bets by April 10
  • 02/Banks estimated to hold $40 billion in aggregate dollar positions; unwinding could trigger mark-to-market losses of ₹4,000 crore
  • 03/Rupee expected to surge up to 3% as banks liquidate dollar holdings; currency hit record low of 94.84 on March 27

The Reserve Bank of India has directed banks to cap their net open positions in the rupee at $100 million. The directive issued on March 27 requires compliance by April 10, marking the first such intervention in 15 years.

The move forces banks to unwind substantial long dollar positions accumulated in the onshore market. Dealers estimate aggregate positions could be as high as $40 billion across all banks.

Large banks currently hold $250-300 million in net open positions each. They must now liquidate excess dollar exposure within two weeks.

The mandatory unwind is expected to trigger significant mark-to-market losses. Industry estimates suggest total losses could reach ₹4,000 crore if the rupee strengthens sharply.

The rupee closed at a record low of 94.84 per dollar on March 27. The currency has declined 4% since the Middle East conflict intensified in late February.

Analysts predict the rupee could surge up to 3% as banks rush to exit dollar positions. The forced selling of dollars against limited buyers may trigger sharp appreciation.

The RBI action under Sections 10(4) and 11(1) of FEMA targets speculative positioning. Governor Sanjay Malhotra aims to curb volatility while maintaining orderly market conditions.

Foreign portfolio investors have withdrawn over $11 billion from Indian equities in March. Record outflows of $1.6 billion from index-eligible bonds have added further pressure on the currency.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE RBI'S NEW FOREX POSITION CAP FOR BANKS?
THE RBI HAS DIRECTED AUTHORISED DEALER BANKS TO MAINTAIN THEIR NET OPEN POSITION IN RUPEE (NOP-INR) WITHIN A LIMIT OF $100 MILLION AT THE CLOSE OF EACH BUSINESS DAY. THIS IS A SIGNIFICANT REDUCTION FROM PREVIOUS LEVELS WHERE BANKS COULD HOLD UP TO 25% OF TOTAL CAPITAL.
WHEN MUST BANKS COMPLY WITH THE NEW CAP?
BANKS MUST ENSURE COMPLIANCE WITH THE $100 MILLION NOP-INR LIMIT BY APRIL 10, 2026. THE RBI ISSUED THE DIRECTIVE ON MARCH 27, 2026, GIVING BANKS APPROXIMATELY TWO WEEKS TO UNWIND THEIR EXISTING POSITIONS.
HOW MUCH DOLLAR EXPOSURE DO BANKS CURRENTLY HOLD?
LARGE BANKS ARE ESTIMATED TO HOLD $250-300 MILLION IN NET OPEN POSITIONS EACH. AGGREGATE LONG DOLLAR POSITIONS ACROSS ALL BANKS COULD BE AS HIGH AS $40 BILLION, ACCORDING TO DEALER ESTIMATES.
WHAT LOSSES COULD BANKS FACE FROM THE UNWIND?
BANKS COULD FACE MARK-TO-MARKET LOSSES OF UP TO ₹4,000 CRORE IF THE RUPEE STRENGTHENS SHARPLY DURING THE UNWIND PROCESS. LOSSES ARISE IF THE ONSHORE RATE FALLS BELOW LEVELS WHERE OFFSHORE POSITIONS WERE ESTABLISHED.
HOW MUCH COULD THE RUPEE APPRECIATE DUE TO THIS MOVE?
ANALYSTS PREDICT THE RUPEE COULD SURGE UP TO 3% AS BANKS ARE FORCED TO LIQUIDATE DOLLAR POSITIONS. THE CURRENCY HIT A RECORD LOW OF 94.84 ON MARCH 27, AND THE UNWIND COULD TRIGGER SIGNIFICANT APPRECIATION TOWARD THE 92-93 LEVEL.
