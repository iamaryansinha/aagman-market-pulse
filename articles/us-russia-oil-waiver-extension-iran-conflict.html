US to Extend Russian Oil Waiver Amid Iran Conflict-Driven Price Shock

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Trump administration likely to extend 30-day waiver allowing purchases of sanctioned Russian oil expiring April 11
  • 02/Waiver covers approximately 100 million barrels, nearly equal to one day of global crude output
  • 03/Decision follows Strait of Hormuz disruption affecting 20% of daily world oil shipments amid US-Israeli war with Iran

The Trump administration is expected to extend a waiver allowing limited purchases of sanctioned Russian oil as soon as Friday. The 30-day exemption expires April 11, according to sources familiar with the matter.

The U.S. Treasury Department first permitted these purchases in mid-March. The move aims to control global energy prices during the ongoing conflict between the U.S., Israel, and Iran.

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent met with President Trump at the White House on Thursday. Both agreed extending the waiver was advisable, one source said.

Russia's presidential envoy Kirill Dmitriev stated the original waiver would free 100 million barrels of Russian crude. This volume equals almost one full day of global oil output.

Oil prices have surged since the war began due to partial closure of the Strait of Hormuz. Approximately 20% of the world's daily oil and gas shipments previously passed through this critical chokepoint.

The 32-nation International Energy Agency has warned the war is creating the biggest oil supply disruption in history. This has intensified pressure on the administration to act.

Fuel prices represent a major political concern for Trump and Republicans heading into November's midterm elections. The waiver extension reflects these domestic pressures.

However, the move has triggered backlash from both parties. Republican Senator Jerry Moran criticized the policy, stating waiving sanctions "advantages the countries that wish to do us harm."

Democratic lawmakers including Representative Gregory Meeks introduced legislation Thursday to block the extension. They argue the waiver gives Vladimir Putin "a free pass" to profit from rising energy costs.

European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen has publicly opposed relaxing sanctions against Russia. The waiver could strain Washington's relationships with key allies.

India, a U.S. ally vulnerable to oil price shocks, has anticipated the waiver's renewal. New Delhi relies heavily on energy imports and faces significant economic pressure from the conflict.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE RUSSIAN OIL WAIVER BEING EXTENDED BY THE US?
THE WAIVER ALLOWS COUNTRIES TO PURCHASE SANCTIONED RUSSIAN OIL AND PETROLEUM PRODUCTS THAT WERE AT SEA WHEN SANCTIONS WERE IMPOSED. THE ORIGINAL 30-DAY WAIVER EXPIRES ON APRIL 11, 2026.
HOW MUCH RUSSIAN OIL DOES THE WAIVER COVER?
APPROXIMATELY 100 MILLION BARRELS OF RUSSIAN CRUDE, WHICH IS NEARLY EQUAL TO ONE FULL DAY OF GLOBAL OIL PRODUCTION.
WHY IS THE US EXTENDING THE WAIVER NOW?
THE EXTENSION AIMS TO CONTROL GLOBAL ENERGY PRICES AMID THE ONGOING US-ISRAELI WAR WITH IRAN, WHICH HAS DISRUPTED SHIPMENTS THROUGH THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ AFFECTING 20% OF WORLD OIL TRADE.
WHAT ARE THE POLITICAL CONCERNS SURROUNDING THE WAIVER?
CRITICS INCLUDING LAWMAKERS FROM BOTH PARTIES ARGUE THE WAIVER UNDERMINES SANCTIONS PRESSURE ON RUSSIA AND HELPS FUND ITS WAR IN UKRAINE. EUROPEAN ALLIES HAVE ALSO EXPRESSED OPPOSITION.
HOW IS THE IRAN CONFLICT IMPACTING GLOBAL OIL MARKETS?
THE INTERNATIONAL ENERGY AGENCY HAS CALLED IT THE BIGGEST OIL SUPPLY DISRUPTION IN HISTORY. THE PARTIAL CLOSURE OF THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ HAS CAUSED OIL PRICES TO SPIKE SHARPLY.
