India Assures Adequate Fuel Supply Amid War Disruption, Urges Calm

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government assures citizens that fuel supplies remain adequate despite disruptions from Strait of Hormuz closure
  • 02/About 51 lakh LPG cylinders delivered daily; 95% demand met through online bookings; no shortage at distributor points
  • 03/Excise duty cut by ₹10/litre on petrol and diesel; over 3,700 raids conducted against hoarding and black marketing

The government has urged citizens to avoid panic buying of petrol, diesel and LPG. Fuel supplies remain adequate despite disruptions linked to the Strait of Hormuz closure.

The Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas issued a statement on Saturday. It said the government is taking proactive steps to ensure uninterrupted availability of petroleum products.

Domestic LPG and piped natural gas supplies have been prioritised. Hospitals and essential services are receiving priority allocation.

About 51 lakh cylinders were delivered on Friday. Online bookings accounted for 95 per cent of total demand.

Commercial LPG supplies have been capped at 70 per cent of pre-crisis levels. Alternative fuels like kerosene and coal are being deployed to ease demand.

All refineries are operating at high capacity. They have adequate crude inventories. Petrol pumps across the country remain fully stocked.

The government has cut excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre. Export levies have been imposed on diesel and aviation fuel.

More than 3,700 raids were conducted against hoarding and black marketing. Around 1,000 show-cause notices were issued to LPG distributors. 27 dealerships have been suspended.

State governments have been asked to intensify monitoring. They must counter misinformation and ensure smooth distribution of fuels.
