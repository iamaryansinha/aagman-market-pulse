Gold ETF Inflows Surge 283% YoY as Investors Ditch Jewellery for Digital

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold ETF net inflows surged 283% YoY to ₹429.6 billion in 2025, making India the world's 3rd largest gold ETF market
  • 02/Jewellery demand dropped 24% to 430.5 tons while digital gold AUM jumped 88% to ₹64,777 crore
  • 03/Young investors prefer app-based gold; ETFs delivered 76.5% returns vs Nifty 50's 10.5%

India's gold ETF market recorded its best year in 2025. Net inflows surged 283% year-on-year to ₹429.6 billion.

This propelled India to become the third-largest gold ETF market globally. Assets under management jumped 88% to ₹64,777 crore.

Holdings reached a record 95 tons, up 65% from the previous year. Investment demand now contributes 40% of total gold consumption.

The shift is coming at the expense of traditional jewellery. Physical gold jewellery demand fell 24% YoY to 430.5 tons in 2025.

Jewellery carries making charges of 8-25% that buyers never recover on resale. Digital gold reflects pure metal value without such losses.

Millennials and Gen Z are driving the transformation. They prefer simple, app-based investing aligned with digital-first lifestyles.

Gold delivered exceptional returns of 76.5% in 2025. The Nifty 50 managed just 10.5% during the same period.

January 2026 saw record monthly inflows of ₹24,040 crore. Total AUM touched ₹1.84 lakh crore that month.

High gold prices have made physical jewellery unaffordable for many. Consumers are shifting to lightweight and lower-purity options.

"The World Gold Council predicts a gradual structural shift where investment demand continues to outpace traditional jewellery consumption," notes the author.
