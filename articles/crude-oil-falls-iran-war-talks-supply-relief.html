Crude Oil Prices Fall to $98 as Iran War Talks Signal Supply Relief

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Brent crude fell 1.35% to $98.05 a barrel and WTI dropped 1.74% to $93.40 on early Friday trade
  • 02/Trump says U.S. and Iran may meet this weekend; Tehran offered 20-year nuclear weapon-free pledge
  • 03/Strait of Hormuz closure has blocked roughly one-fifth of global oil supply for seven weeks

Crude oil prices declined sharply in early Asian trade on Friday. Brent crude futures dropped $1.34, or 1.35%, to $98.05 a barrel at 0021 GMT.

U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude futures fell $1.65, or 1.74%, to $93.40 a barrel. Both benchmarks trimmed gains from the previous session.

The decline follows renewed optimism about ending the Middle East conflict. A 10-day ceasefire between Lebanon and Israel took effect recently.

President Donald Trump announced potential U.S.-Iran talks this weekend. He stated Tehran has offered not to possess nuclear weapons for over 20 years.

"We're going to see what happens. But I think we're very close to making a deal with Iran," Trump told reporters outside the White House on Thursday.

The Iran war has closed the Strait of Hormuz for seven weeks. This has choked off roughly one-fifth of the world's oil supply.

Analysts from ING estimate that roughly 13 million barrels per day of oil flow has been disrupted. The closure has significantly tightened global supply.

Oil prices had climbed 50% in March during a record run. Prices only recently fell below the $100 per barrel mark but remained within the $90 range this week.

U.S. and Iranian negotiators have scaled back expectations for a comprehensive peace deal. They are now seeking a temporary memorandum to prevent conflict resumption.

An end to hostilities could restore critical oil supply routes. This would ease the supply crunch affecting global markets.
