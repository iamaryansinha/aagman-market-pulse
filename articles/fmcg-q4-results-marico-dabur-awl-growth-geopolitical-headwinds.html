FMCG Q4 Results: Marico, Dabur, AWL Report Growth Amid Global Headwinds

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Marico Q4 FY25 revenue grew 20% YoY to ₹2,730 crore, PAT up 8% to ₹343 crore amid stable demand environment
  • 02/AWL Agri Business Q4 volume grew 8% YoY with revenue surging 38% to ₹18,230 crore, driven by edible oils and food FMCG
  • 03/Dabur Q4 revenue flat at ₹2,830 crore but international business delivered strong 19% constant currency growth

FMCG majors Marico, Dabur and AWL Agri Business have reported steady growth in Q4 FY2025. The results reflect resilience amid geopolitical headwinds and a stabilizing demand environment.

Marico Ltd (NSE: MARICO) posted consolidated revenue of ₹2,730 crore for Q4 FY25. This represents a 20% year-on-year increase from ₹2,278 crore in Q4 FY24.

The company's profit after tax rose 8% to ₹343 crore. EBITDA grew 4% to ₹458 crore, though margins contracted to 16.8% from 19.4% due to higher input costs.

Dabur India Ltd (NSE: DABUR) reported flat revenue growth in Q4 FY25. Revenue stood at ₹2,830 crore, up just 0.5% YoY from ₹2,814.6 crore.

Net profit declined 8.4% to ₹320 crore amid margin pressures. However, the international business shone with 19% constant currency growth, mitigating domestic weakness.

AWL Agri Business Ltd reported strong Q4 performance with revenue of ₹18,230 crore. This marks a 38% YoY increase, driven primarily by the edible oils segment.

Volume growth across segments stood at 8% YoY. Food & FMCG revenue grew 9% to ₹1,464 crore with underlying volume growth of 10%.

The FMCG sector faced mixed demand trends during the quarter. Rural markets continued to outperform urban areas for the sixth consecutive quarter.

Geopolitical headwinds including US tariffs on Indian goods created uncertainty. However, stable copra and vegetable oil prices provided some relief to input costs.

Marico's India business posted sequential uptick in underlying volume growth. Market shares improved across key franchises including Parachute and Saffola.

AWL achieved its highest-ever full-year EBITDA of ₹2,482 crore in FY25. This represents a 119% YoY increase, driven by strong edible oil profitability.

Dabur's board recommended a final dividend of ₹5.25 per share. This translates to 525% on the face value of Re 1 per share.
