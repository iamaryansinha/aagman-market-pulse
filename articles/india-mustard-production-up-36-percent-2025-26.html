India Mustard Production Up 3.6% in 2025-26 on Higher Acreage

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's mustard production increases 3.6% in 2025-26 crop year driven by higher acreage
  • 02/Cultivation area rises to 93.91 lakh hectares with improved yields from favourable weather
  • 03/Better agronomic practices support output growth; prices may stabilize on improved supply

India's mustard production is projected to rise 3.6% in the 2025-26 crop year. The increase comes on the back of expanded cultivation area.

Total area under mustard cultivation reached 93.91 lakh hectares this season. This represents a significant expansion from the previous year.

Yields have improved marginally due to favourable weather conditions. Farmers also adopted better agronomic practices across key growing regions.

The rapeseed-mustard crop is crucial for India's edible oil security. The country meets nearly 40% of its domestic demand through mustard oil.

Higher production could ease domestic edible oil prices. India remains one of the largest importers of vegetable oils globally.

The mustard harvest season typically runs from February to March. Output estimates are released by the agriculture ministry post-harvest.
