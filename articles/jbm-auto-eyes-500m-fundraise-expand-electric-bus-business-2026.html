JBM Auto Eyes $500 Million Fundraise to Expand Electric Bus Business

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/JBM Auto is planning a $500 million fundraising round to scale its electric bus operations in India
  • 02/The company already raised over $250 million and holds 24% market share with nearly 10,000-bus order book
  • 03/JBM aims to double its pipeline within two years as India's e-bus market grows at 20%+ CAGR

JBM Auto is exploring a potential $500 million fundraise to accelerate its electric mobility business as it looks to execute and expand an order book of nearly 10,000 electric buses. The fundraising comes as the company has already secured over $250 million for its electric mobility platform, including $100 million in FY26 alone.

The planned capital raise signals a decisive shift from pilot projects to scaled deployments in India's rapidly electrifying public transport sector. Industry sources indicate the funds will support execution of existing orders and expansion of JBM's pipeline, which Vice Chairman Nishant Arya expects to double over the next one to two years.

JBM has emerged as India's largest electric bus manufacturer by volume, registering over 1,200 electric buses in FY26 and capturing approximately 24% market share. The company operates one of the world's largest integrated electric bus manufacturing facilities outside China, with an annual capacity of 20,000 units.

The company has strategically expanded its in-house battery capacity from around 3 GWh to about 6 GWh, giving it greater control over costs and supply chains as the market scales. This vertical integration positions JBM well as battery packs represent 30-40% of total e-bus costs.

Previous funding rounds have attracted major multilateral institutions. In September 2025, JBM Ecolife Mobility secured $100 million from the International Finance Corporation (IFC), a member of the World Bank Group. In November 2024, the company raised $100 million from the Asian Development Bank (ADB) and Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) to supply and operate approximately 650 electric buses across multiple Indian states.

The Indian electric bus market is experiencing robust growth, valued at approximately $1.17 billion in 2025 and projected to reach $2.92 billion by 2029. The market is expanding at a CAGR of 20-23%, driven by aggressive government procurement schemes including PM E-DRIVE and PM-eBus Sewa, which together target 24,028 electric buses.

India currently has only 8 electric buses per million people compared to the global average of 94, indicating significant headroom for growth. The market is witnessing a shift toward the Gross Cost Contract (GCC) model, where private operators assume technology and maintenance risks while state transport undertakings pay per-kilometer charges.

JBM faces intensifying competition from well-funded rivals. Switch Mobility (backed by Ashok Leyland) captured 26.4% market share in FY26, while PMI Electro Mobility secured 25.6% after receiving $310 million in commitments from KKR. Other key players include Olectra Greentech with 19.6% market share.

The company is expanding beyond traditional public transport into corporate mobility, institutional transport, and intercity routes where utilization levels and economics are more predictable. JBM is targeting cumulative e-kilometer operations of over 3 billion globally over the next few years.

Payment security mechanisms backed by multilateral agencies have improved the bankability of e-bus projects, attracting institutional capital. Electric buses are increasingly viewed as infrastructure assets with long-term contracts underpinning predictable cash flows.
