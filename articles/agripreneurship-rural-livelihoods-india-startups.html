Agripreneurship To Drive Rural Livelihoods: 1,554 Startups, ₹111 Cr Support

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Over 1,554 agri-startups received government support worth ₹111.57 crores through various incubation programs
  • 02/DeHaat leads with 2M+ farmers, $270M+ funding; AIF has ₹1 lakh crore corpus for agri-infrastructure
  • 03/Agri-tech adoption delivers 15-40% income increase for farmers; 387 women-led startups funded

India stands at the threshold of an agricultural revolution led not by traditional conglomerates but by agripreneurs armed with technology and innovation. Agriculture employs nearly 45% of India's workforce and contributes around 18% to GDP.

As of 2024-25, over 1,554 agri-startups have received government support worth ₹111.57 crores. These ventures address critical pain points across the agricultural ecosystem.

DeHaat leads the pack. Founded in 2012, it serves 2 million+ farmers through 12,000 micro-entrepreneurs across 110,000 villages. The company has raised over $270 million in funding.

Other notable players include CropIn (farm management software), AgroStar (input delivery), Ninjacart (fresh produce supply chain), and Jai Kisan (credit for farmers).

Government support mechanisms are substantial. The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) has a ₹1 lakh crore corpus with 44,912+ projects sanctioned. RKVY-RAFTAAR supports agripreneurs with mentorship and funding.

The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) provides early-stage capital. PM Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PMFME) offers credit-linked subsidies.

NABARD supports agri-startups through dedicated funds. Atal Innovation Mission (AIM) promotes agri-tech innovation at the grassroots level.

Income impacts are measurable. Farmers using agri-tech platforms report 15-40% income increases through better input access, reduced post-harvest losses, and direct market linkages.

Women empowerment is a key focus. 387 women-led agri-startups have received funding. PM-KISAN has disbursed ₹1.01 lakh crore to women beneficiaries.

Challenges remain. Only 12% of farmers currently use agri-tech solutions. Digital literacy, connectivity issues, and trust deficits limit adoption in rural areas.

The opportunity is massive. With 80% of farmers being small or marginal, technology-enabled solutions can transform livelihoods at scale.
