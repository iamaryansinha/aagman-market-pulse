Sensex Crashes 1600 Points, Nifty Drops 480 Points On Iran Blockade; Crude Tops $102

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BSE Sensex opened 1,613 points lower at 75,937.16 on April 13, 2026 after US-Iran peace talks collapsed over the weekend
  • 02/Nifty 50 plunged 479 points or 1.99% to 23,571.15 as geopolitical risk rattled global investor sentiment
  • 03/June Brent crude jumped 7.54% to $102.38 and May WTI surged 8.81% to $105.08 following US naval blockade on Iran

BSE Sensex opened 1,613 points lower at 75,937.16 on Monday morning. The benchmark index fell from its previous close of 77,550.25.

By 9:16 am, the Sensex traded 1,624.75 points lower at 75,925.50. This represents a decline of 2.10% from Friday's close.

Nifty 50 dropped 479.45 points or 1.99% to 23,571.15. The index breached the 23,600 level within minutes of market open.

The sharp sell-off was triggered by geopolitical developments over the weekend. US President Donald Trump announced a naval blockade on all maritime traffic entering and exiting Iranian ports.

US-Iran peace talks collapsed over the weekend without reaching an agreement. The diplomatic breakdown reignited fears over crude supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz.

June Brent oil futures jumped 7.54% to $102.38 on Monday morning. May crude oil futures on WTI surged 8.81% to $105.08.

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil trade. Any disruption threatens supply to major economies including India.

By 10:00 am, Sensex had recovered slightly but was still down 1,313.79 points to 76,236.46. Nifty traded at 23,671.10, down 379.50 points.
