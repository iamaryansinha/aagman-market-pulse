Air India CEO Campbell Wilson Steps Down After Three-Year Tenure

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Campbell Wilson has resigned as CEO of Air India, ending a tenure that began in 2022 with over 30 years of aviation experience
  • 02/Air India reported a consolidated loss of ₹10,859 crore in FY25 despite revenue of ₹78,636 crore and Vistara merger completion
  • 03/Tata Group is reportedly searching for a replacement; Wilson was serving his notice period as of April 7, 2026

Air India Chief Executive Officer Campbell Wilson has resigned from his position. Sources confirmed his departure on April 7, 2026.

Wilson was appointed CEO and Managing Director in 2022. His five-year contract was scheduled to run until July 2027.

He brought over 30 years of aviation industry experience to the role. This included experience across both full-service and low-cost carriers.

Prior to joining Air India, Wilson served as CEO of Scoot. Scoot is the wholly-owned low-cost subsidiary of Singapore Airlines.

Wilson began his career with Singapore Airlines in 1996 as a Management Trainee in New Zealand. He subsequently held various roles in Canada, Hong Kong, and Japan.

He returned to Singapore in 2011 as the founding CEO of Scoot. He held this position until 2016 before serving as Senior Vice President at SIA.

Air India has faced significant financial challenges during Wilson's tenure. The airline posted a consolidated net loss of ₹10,859 crore in FY25.

Revenue for FY25 stood at ₹78,636 crore, marking a 15% increase year-on-year. However, losses continued to weigh on the airline's financial performance.

The Vistara-Air India merger was completed in November 2024. Singapore Airlines now holds a 25.1% stake in the combined entity.

Singapore Airlines reported that Air India's losses impacted its own profitability. The SIA Group's share of losses from associated companies was SGD 122 million in Q1 FY26.

Air India's standalone loss dropped 21% YoY compared to FY24. This improvement was driven by expanded flight capacity and an improved route network.

The airline currently operates a fleet of 142 aircraft. It has launched 35 new routes since the Tata Group took over in 2022.

Tata Group has reportedly begun searching for a new CEO to replace Wilson. The process may extend until investigations into recent incidents are concluded.
