Oil Prices Drop as Trump Pauses Iran Strikes, Traders Nervous on Inflation

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Oil prices dropped 4% after President Trump paused planned military strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities
  • 02/Brent crude settled at $68.50/barrel, down from $71.20; WTI at $65.10/barrel on de-escalation hopes
  • 03/Stock traders remain nervous as inflation fears persist; Asian markets mixed with Nikkei flat

Oil prices fell sharply after President Donald Trump paused planned military strikes against Iran. Brent crude dropped 4% to settle at $68.50 per barrel on March 27, 2026.

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude declined to $65.10 per barrel. The pullback came after Trump announced a 30-day pause on military action.

The temporary pause follows weeks of rising tensions over Iran's nuclear program. Diplomatic channels have reportedly reopened through Qatar and Oman.

Despite the oil price drop, stock traders remain nervous. Inflation concerns continue to weigh on equity markets across Asia.

Japan's Nikkei 225 traded flat at 38,450. Hong Kong's Hang Seng slipped 0.3% amid mixed signals on regional stability.

Gold prices rose 0.8% to $2,950 per ounce. Investors sought safe-haven assets despite the de-escalation in the Gulf.

Analysts warn the pause may be temporary. Military action remains possible if nuclear talks fail to progress within 30 days.

Energy sector stocks underperformed the broader market. Exxon Mobil shares fell 1.2% in pre-market trading.
