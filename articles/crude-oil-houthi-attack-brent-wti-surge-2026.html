Crude Oil Jumps After Houthi Attack: Brent Hits $108, WTI Above $101

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/June Brent oil futures climb to $107.87, up 2.42%, following Houthi attack on Israel
  • 02/May WTI crude futures rise to $101.27, gaining 1.64% as geopolitical tensions escalate
  • 03/Traders watch for potential supply disruptions and retaliatory actions in the region

Crude oil futures surged on Monday after a Houthi attack on Israel raised supply concerns. June Brent oil futures hit $107.87, rising 2.42%.

West Texas Intermediate (WTI) for May delivery climbed to $101.27. This marks a 1.64% increase from the previous session.

The attack has reignited fears of broader Middle East conflict. Oil markets are pricing in potential supply disruption risks.

Brent crude has now crossed the $107 mark for the first time in weeks. Traders are closely monitoring the situation.

The Houthi strike adds to existing tensions in the oil-rich region. Any escalation could impact critical shipping routes.

Supply risks remain elevated as markets assess Israel's response. Energy traders are positioning for potential volatility ahead.

Both benchmarks have gained significantly amid geopolitical uncertainty. Investors are seeking safety in commodities as tensions rise.
