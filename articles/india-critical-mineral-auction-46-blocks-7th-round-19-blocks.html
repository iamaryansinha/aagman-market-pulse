India Critical Mineral Auctions: 46 Blocks Sold, 19 More in 7th Round

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India has successfully auctioned 46 critical and strategic mineral blocks through six completed tranches since amending the MMDR Act in August 2023
  • 02/Union Minister G. Kishan Reddy launched the 7th tranche on March 23, 2026 offering 19 additional blocks across multiple states
  • 03/The blocks include lithium, graphite, rare earth elements, tungsten, vanadium, titanium, glauconite, and rock phosphate for clean energy and strategic sectors

India has auctioned 46 critical and strategic mineral blocks through six completed tranches. The auctions follow amendments to the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act in August 2023.

The government designated 24 minerals as critical and strategic under the amended Act. This empowers the Central Government to conduct auctions while revenue flows to respective State Governments.

Union Minister of Coal and Mines G. Kishan Reddy launched the 7th tranche on March 23, 2026. The new round offers 19 additional mineral blocks across several states.

The 7th tranche includes blocks under Mining Lease and Composite Licence categories. These comprise lithium, graphite, rare earth elements, tungsten, vanadium, titanium, glauconite, and rock phosphate.

Critical minerals are essential for clean energy, advanced technologies, fertilizers, and strategic industries. Global demand has surged for materials needed in electric vehicles, renewable energy, and defence manufacturing.

The auction framework uses a two-stage ascending forward auction process. Successful bidders are selected based on the highest percentage of mineral value dispatched.

Recent reforms have streamlined post-auction timelines. The Mineral (Auction) Second Amendment Rules, 2025 accelerated performance security submission and upfront payment processes.

The Mineral (Auction) Amendment Rules, 2026 introduced Insurance Surety Bonds as an alternative to bank guarantees. This enhances flexibility for bidders in meeting financial commitments.

India's critical mineral push addresses supply chain vulnerabilities. Domestic production aims to reduce import dependencies for materials concentrated in limited geographic regions.

The 24 designated critical minerals span multiple industrial applications. These range from battery manufacturing to steel production and advanced materials development.
