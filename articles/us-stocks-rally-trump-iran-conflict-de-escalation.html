US Stocks Rally Sharply as Trump Signals Potential Iran Conflict End

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/S&P 500 jumps 2.91% to 6,528.52, Nasdaq rallies 3.83% to 21,590.63 on Iran conflict hopes
  • 02/Biggest one-day gains since May 2025 as Trump signals willingness to end Iran military campaign
  • 03/Despite rally, Q1 2026 remains worst quarter since 2022 for major indexes

Wall Street surged sharply on Tuesday as optimism around a potential de-escalation in the Middle East eased concerns over oil prices and inflation.

All three major U.S. indexes rallied after reports that President Donald Trump told aides he was willing to end the military campaign against Iran.

The S&P 500 jumped 2.91% to close at 6,528.52 points. This marked its biggest one-day gain since May 2025.

The Nasdaq Composite rallied 3.83% to finish at 21,590.63. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 2.49% to 46,341.51.

Big tech stocks led the charge. Nvidia gained 5.6%, Alphabet added 5.1%, and Meta Platforms jumped 6.7%.

The PHLX semiconductor index surged 6.24% in its strongest session in nearly a year.

Volume was heavy with 22.4 billion shares traded. This exceeded the 20-session average of 20.3 billion shares.

Nine of 11 S&P 500 sector indexes rose. Communication services led with a 4.42% gain, followed by information technology at 4.24%.

The energy index fell 1.2% as oil price concerns eased. It remained up 10% for March.

Despite Tuesday's rally, the quarter ended poorly for equities. The S&P 500 is down 4.6% year-to-date.

The Nasdaq has lost 7.1% and the Dow has fallen 3.6% in 2026. Both indexes confirmed corrections last week.

"What you're seeing in capital markets today is speculation around an earlier off-ramp, or a cessation of hostilities," said Bill Northey, senior investment director at U.S. Bank Wealth Management.

The month-long Iran conflict has driven oil prices higher. This revived fears of global inflation and potential Fed rate hikes.
