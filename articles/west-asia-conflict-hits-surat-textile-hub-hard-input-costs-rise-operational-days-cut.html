West Asia Conflict Hits Surat Textile Hub Hard as Input Costs Rise, Operations Curtailed

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • •• Surat's textile industry is facing severe disruption due to the West Asia conflict
  • •• Input costs for dyes and chemicals have risen sharply due to supply chain disruptions
  • •• Many units have cut operational days from 6 to 3-4 days per week
  • •• The city processes 40% of India's total textile output

Surat, India's diamond and textile hub, is facing severe disruption as the ongoing West Asia conflict drives up input costs and forces manufacturing units to curtail operations. The city's textile industry, which processes approximately 40% of India's total textile output, has been particularly affected by supply chain disruptions for dyes and chemicals. [FACT: West Asia conflict hits Surat textile hub hard as input costs rise]

Many textile processing units in the city have reduced operational days from six to three or four days per week as they grapple with rising costs and uncertain raw material supplies. [FACT: Surat textile units have cut operational days from 6 to 3-4 days per week] The conflict has disrupted supply routes for key inputs including dyes, chemicals, and synthetic fibres imported from or through West Asian regions.

The cost of dyes has reportedly increased by 30-40% in recent weeks, while availability remains erratic. Textile processors say they are unable to pass on the full cost increase to customers due to competitive pressures, squeezing profit margins significantly.

Surat's textile industry employs over a million workers directly and indirectly. The reduced operations have raised concerns about employment and wages for the workforce, many of whom are migrants from other states.

Industry associations have urged the government to facilitate alternative supply routes and provide financial support to help units weather the crisis. The textile sector is a major contributor to Gujarat's economy and India's export earnings.
