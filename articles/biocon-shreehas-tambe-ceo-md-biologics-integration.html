Biocon Names Shreehas Tambe as CEO & MD as Biologics Integration Completes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Biocon Ltd appoints Shreehas Tambe as CEO & MD effective April 1, 2026, making him first chief executive of the integrated biosimilars-generics business
  • 02/Integration values Biocon Biologics at $5.5 billion; company ranks among world's top 5 biosimilar players with presence in 120+ countries
  • 03/Kedar Upadhye appointed CFO; Siddharth Mittal transitions to group leadership role after overseeing generics business

Biocon Limited (NSE: BIOCON, BSE: 532523) has appointed Shreehas Tambe as its Chief Executive Officer and Managing Director. The appointment takes effect April 1, 2026.

Tambe will become the first CEO of the combined entity. This follows the full integration of Biocon Biologics as a wholly-owned subsidiary into Biocon Limited.

The restructuring creates a unified global biopharma platform. It spans biosimilars, insulins, complex generics, and peptides including GLP-1 therapies for diabetes and obesity.

Kedar Upadhye has been appointed as Chief Financial Officer. Siddharth Mittal, the outgoing CEO, will transition to a leadership role within the Biocon Group.

Tambe joined Biocon nearly three decades ago as a management trainee. He has held multiple leadership roles across the organization.

As CEO of Biocon Biologics, he led the transformational acquisition of Viatris' biosimilars business. This positioned Biocon Biologics among the world's top five biosimilar companies with a valuation of $5.5 billion.

"It is an honor to lead Biocon at such a pivotal moment," Tambe said. He emphasized focus on consolidating operations and accelerating sustainable growth.

Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw, Executive Chairperson, highlighted Tambe's strategic rigor. She noted his deep scientific expertise and commitment to advancing affordable healthcare.

Biocon Biologics reported Q2 FY26 revenue of ₹2,721 crore, up 25% year-on-year. EBITDA grew over 40% to ₹669 crore with margins expanding to 25%.

The Generics business posted Q2 FY26 revenue of ₹774 crore, up 24% YoY. The segment benefited from recently launched products in the U.S. and EU markets.

The combined entity will focus on high-growth therapy areas. These include oncology and immunology, which together account for nearly 40% of global pharmaceutical revenues.

Biocon is uniquely positioned as the only company globally with both biosimilar insulins and generic GLP-1 peptides. This addresses the rapidly expanding diabetes and obesity markets.

The company markets 10 biosimilars and over 90 generic medicines. It serves more than 6.3 million patients across 120+ countries.

Biocon shares closed at ₹379.65 on March 27, 2026. The company commands a market capitalization of approximately ₹60,000 crore.

The integration was completed through a share swap arrangement. Biocon acquired minority stakes from Serum Institute Life Sciences, Tata Capital Growth Fund II, and Viatris.
