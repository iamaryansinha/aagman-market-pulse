OMCs Pay Refiners ₹60/Litre Discount Amid Fuel Price Freeze

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/OMCs fixed refinery transfer prices at discounts of up to ₹60 per litre to import costs effective March 26, 2026
  • 02/Under-recoveries on petrol at ₹26/litre and diesel at ₹81.90/litre; daily losses of ₹2,400 crore absorbed by OMCs
  • 03/Standalone refiners MRPL, CPCL, HMEL face sharper margin squeeze; integrated OMCs IOC, BPCL, HPCL can offset impact

State-owned oil marketing companies fixed refinery transfer prices at discounts of up to ₹60 per litre to import costs. The move took effect on March 26, 2026.

OMCs are struggling with mounting under-recoveries as retail fuel prices remain frozen since April 2022. International crude prices surged from $70 per barrel to over $100 amid the West Asia conflict.

The discounts apply to refinery transfer price (RTP), the internal rate at which refineries sell fuel to marketing arms. This prevents refiners from fully passing on higher crude costs.

Standalone refiners face the biggest hit. MRPL, CPCL, and HMEL have minimal retail presence and sell most output to the three OMCs. These companies rely entirely on market-linked RTP for revenue.

Integrated firms IOC, BPCL, and HPCL can partially offset the impact. Their refining arms take a hit while marketing divisions benefit from lower input costs.

Under-recoveries currently stand at ₹26 per litre for petrol and ₹81.90 per litre for diesel. Combined daily losses absorbed by OMCs total approximately ₹2,400 crore.

The government cut excise duty by ₹10 per litre on March 26 to provide partial relief. However, retail pump prices remain unchanged as the reduction offsets OMC losses rather than passing benefits to consumers.

Private refiners Reliance Industries and Nayara Energy could also face impact if the discount structure applies to their supplies to OMCs.
