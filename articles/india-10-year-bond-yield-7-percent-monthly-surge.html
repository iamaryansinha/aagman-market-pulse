India 10-Year Bond Yield Crosses 7%, Biggest Monthly Jump in 9 Years

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's 10-year government bond yield surged past 7% on Monday, marking its biggest monthly jump in nine years
  • 02/The rupee plunged to an all-time low of 95.21 against the US dollar as foreign investors pulled capital
  • 03/Equity markets also declined sharply; bond yields may rise further if RBI intervention remains limited

India's benchmark 10-year government bond yield crossed the 7% threshold on Monday. The surge marks the steepest monthly climb in nine years.

The yield has risen sharply through March 2026. Bond prices and yields move in opposite directions.

The rupee simultaneously hit a record low of 95.21 against the US dollar. Currency weakness typically pushes bond yields higher as inflation risks increase.

Indian equity markets also declined sharply during the session. Foreign portfolio investors have been net sellers across asset classes.

The bond yield surge reflects risk-off sentiment among global investors. Rising US Treasury yields and Federal Reserve policy uncertainty are driving capital outflows from emerging markets.

Higher bond yields increase government borrowing costs. This could widen the fiscal deficit if sustained over multiple quarters.

The Reserve Bank of India has intervened in both currency and bond markets. However, intervention intensity has been lower compared to previous episodes of rupee weakness.

Analysts expect yields to remain elevated near term. The 7.20% level is seen as the next technical resistance for the 10-year benchmark.
