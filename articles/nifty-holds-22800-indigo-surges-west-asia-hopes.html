Nifty Holds Above 22,800, IndiGo Surges 8% On West Asia Hopes

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nifty 50 trades at 22,842.25, gaining 510.85 points or 2.29% in midday session
  • 02/Sensex jumps 1,729.14 points to 73,676.69, reflecting broad-based buying
  • 03/InterGlobe Aviation (IndiGo) surges 8% on hopes of easing tensions in West Asia

Indian equity markets extended gains during midday trade on April 1, 2026. The NSE Nifty 50 held firm above the 22,800 mark at 22,842.25.

The index gained 510.85 points or 2.29% from previous close. This marks a strong recovery session for benchmark indices.

The BSE Sensex traded at 73,676.69, up 1,729.14 points or 2.40%. All sectoral indices traded in positive territory.

InterGlobe Aviation Ltd, which operates IndiGo, surged 8% during the session. The rally came on hopes of de-escalation in West Asia tensions.

Aviation stocks have been volatile amid geopolitical concerns in recent weeks. Any easing of regional tensions benefits airlines with international exposure.

IndiGo is India's largest airline by market share and fleet size. The carrier has been expanding its international network significantly.

Broader market sentiment improved on positive global cues. Investors are tracking developments in West Asia closely for further direction.

The rally reflects renewed risk appetite among domestic institutional investors. Midday volumes remained healthy across cash and derivatives segments.
