Macquarie Sells Safeway Stake to VINCI Highways for ₹15,000 Crore

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Macquarie Asset Management sells Safeway Concessions to VINCI Highways for ₹15,000 crore ($1.7 billion) enterprise value
  • 02/Portfolio includes nine toll highway concessions across Andhra Pradesh and Gujarat covering nearly 700 km of roads
  • 03/Deal expected to close by end of 2026 pending regulatory approvals; marks VINCI's first entry into India's roads sector

Macquarie Asset Management has agreed to sell its stake in Safeway Concessions to VINCI Highways. The deal values the portfolio at approximately ₹15,000 crore ($1.7 billion).

The transaction includes nine toll highway concessions located across Andhra Pradesh and Gujarat. These assets cover nearly 700 kilometers of highway sections.

The portfolio sits on the NH-16 corridor in Andhra Pradesh, part of India's Golden Quadrilateral connecting Kolkata to Chennai. In Gujarat, the assets serve key industrialized regions.

Safeway was established under the National Highways Authority of India's Toll Operate Transfer (TOT) model in 2018. Macquarie acquired the platform the same year through its Macquarie Asia Infrastructure Fund 2.

Under Macquarie's ownership, Safeway expanded to three offices and implemented global standards for safety and governance. The platform also rolled out sustainability initiatives including LED lighting and electric vehicles in 2023.

The deal is subject to customary closing conditions and regulatory approvals. Financial close is expected by the end of 2026.

This acquisition marks VINCI Highways' first entry into India's roads sector. The French infrastructure giant continues to expand its global mobility infrastructure footprint.

"India has the world's second-largest road network with rapidly growing demand," said Verena Lim, Co-Head of Asia-Pacific Infrastructure at Macquarie Asset Management. "We continue to see compelling long-term opportunities across transport, digital and energy infrastructure."
