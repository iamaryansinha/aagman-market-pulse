Mcap Of Top-10 Firms Decline By ₹65k Cr, Bharti Airtel Leads Losses

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Six of India's top-10 most valued companies lost ₹65,000 crore in combined market capitalization last week
  • 02/Bharti Airtel emerged as the biggest laggard among the pack during the weekly market decline
  • 03/BSE Sensex declined 263.67 points (0.35%) and NSE Nifty fell 106.5 points (0.46%) last week

Six of India's top-10 most valued firms witnessed a combined erosion of ₹65,000 crore in market capitalization last week. The decline came amid negative momentum in the broader markets.

Bharti Airtel emerged as the biggest laggard among the top-10 companies. The telecom major's market value took the hardest hit during the week.

The benchmark BSE Sensex declined 263.67 points, or 0.35 per cent, during the week. The NSE Nifty50 fell 106.5 points, or 0.46 per cent.

The market cap losses were concentrated in a select few heavyweights. Four of the top-10 firms managed to buck the negative trend.

The weekly decline reflects continued volatility in Indian equities. Investors remain cautious amid global economic uncertainties.

Heavyweight stocks dragged the broader indices lower. The top-10 firms collectively represent a significant portion of India's total market capitalization.
