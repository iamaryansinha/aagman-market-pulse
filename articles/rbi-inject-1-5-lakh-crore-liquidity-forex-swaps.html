RBI to Inject ₹1.5 Lakh Crore Liquidity via Forex Swaps and OMOs

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/RBI announces measures to inject ₹1.5 lakh crore liquidity via $5 billion USD/INR buy-sell swap, ₹60,000 crore OMO purchases, and ₹50,000 crore VRR auction
  • 02/Banking system faced worst liquidity crunch in 15 years with deficit peaking at ₹3.15 lakh crore in January 2025 due to advance tax outflows and forex interventions
  • 03/The $5 billion forex swap auction received bids worth $25.59 billion from 253 participants, showing strong market demand for rupee liquidity

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) announced comprehensive liquidity injection measures totaling ₹1.5 lakh crore. The package includes a $5 billion USD/INR buy-sell swap auction, OMO purchases worth ₹60,000 crore, and a 56-day Variable Rate Repo auction of ₹50,000 crore.

Governor Sanjay Malhotra unveiled these measures to address the worst liquidity crunch in 15 years. The banking system deficit peaked at ₹3.15 lakh crore on January 23, 2025, driven by advance tax payments, FPI outflows, and RBI's forex interventions to stabilize the rupee.

The $5 billion forex swap auction conducted on January 31 saw overwhelming response. Banks bid for $25.59 billion, oversubscribing the auction five-fold. RBI accepted 28 bids totaling $5.1 billion at a premium cut-off of 96.81 paisa.

The OMO purchases were scheduled in three tranches: ₹20,000 crore on January 30, February 13, and February 20. These purchases inject durable liquidity by adding rupee funds permanently to the banking system.

The liquidity crunch originated from multiple factors. Advance tax payments in December 2024 drained significant funds. Foreign Portfolio Investors withdrew over ₹1.5 lakh crore from Indian equities in 2025. RBI sold over $50 billion from forex reserves to defend the rupee.

The central bank later doubled down on liquidity support. In December 2025, RBI announced additional measures including ₹1 lakh crore OMO purchases and a $5 billion three-year USD/INR swap. This brought total liquidity injection commitments to over ₹2 lakh crore.

Anitha Rangan, Chief Economist at RBL Bank, noted that more liquidity may be needed in Q4. "The fourth quarter marks a high credit season, and with banks already facing deposit growth constraints, the RBI should infuse more liquidity beyond ₹1.5 lakh crore."

The liquidity injection aims to stabilize overnight interest rates near the repo rate. It also facilitates transmission of the 125 basis points rate cuts delivered in 2025 to borrowers.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE TOTAL LIQUIDITY INJECTION ANNOUNCED BY RBI?
THE RBI ANNOUNCED MEASURES TO INJECT ₹1.5 LAKH CRORE THROUGH A COMBINATION OF $5 BILLION USD/INR BUY-SELL SWAP (APPROXIMATELY ₹43,000 CRORE), ₹60,000 CRORE OMO PURCHASES, AND ₹50,000 CRORE VRR AUCTION. LATER MEASURES EXPANDED THIS TO OVER ₹2 LAKH CRORE.
WHY DID RBI NEED TO INJECT LIQUIDITY?
THE BANKING SYSTEM FACED ITS WORST LIQUIDITY CRUNCH IN 15 YEARS WITH A DEFICIT PEAKING AT ₹3.15 LAKH CRORE. THIS WAS CAUSED BY ADVANCE TAX OUTFLOWS, FPI WITHDRAWALS OF OVER ₹1.5 LAKH CRORE IN 2025, AND RBI'S FOREX INTERVENTIONS TO STABILIZE THE RUPEE.
WHAT IS A USD/INR BUY-SELL SWAP?
IN A BUY-SELL SWAP, RBI BUYS DOLLARS FROM BANKS SPOT AND SELLS THEM BACK AT A FUTURE DATE. THIS INJECTS RUPEE LIQUIDITY INTO THE BANKING SYSTEM IMMEDIATELY WHILE ALLOWING RBI TO MANAGE CURRENCY STABILITY. THE JANUARY 2025 SWAP HAD A 6-MONTH TENOR.
HOW DID MARKETS RESPOND TO THE FOREX SWAP AUCTION?
THE $5 BILLION FOREX SWAP AUCTION WAS OVERSUBSCRIBED FIVE-FOLD WITH BIDS TOTALING $25.59 BILLION FROM 253 PARTICIPANTS. RBI ACCEPTED 28 BIDS WORTH $5.1 BILLION AT A PREMIUM CUTOFF OF 96.81 PAISA.
WHAT IMPACT WILL THESE MEASURES HAVE ON INTEREST RATES?
THE LIQUIDITY INJECTION AIMS TO KEEP OVERNIGHT RATES NEAR THE REPO RATE OF 6.25% AND FACILITATE TRANSMISSION OF RATE CUTS TO BORROWERS. ANALYSTS EXPECT 10-YEAR BOND YIELDS TO EASE TO 6.30-6.20% RANGE.
