Govt Asks Auto Sector to Optimise Output as Iran War Hits Energy Supply

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Heavy Industries Ministry issues directive asking automakers to optimise production amid disruptions to oil and energy supplies from Iran conflict
  • 02/Government pushes accelerated shift from oil-based manufacturing to electric vehicle production lines
  • 03/Auto sector directed to increase use of recycled materials to reduce dependency on energy-intensive raw material imports

The Ministry of Heavy Industries has issued a fresh directive to India's automobile manufacturers. The order demands immediate optimisation of production capacities amid escalating energy supply disruptions caused by the Iran war.

The directive comes as global crude oil prices surge past $140 per barrel. Shipping routes through the Strait of Hormuz face severe disruptions, hitting India's energy imports.

Automakers must now accelerate their transition from oil-dependent processes to electric-focused manufacturing. The government wants 40% of new production lines to be EV-ready by December 2026.

The ministry has also mandated increased use of recycled steel and aluminium. This reduces dependence on energy-intensive primary metal production that relies heavily on imported oil and gas.

Major manufacturers including Maruti Suzuki, Tata Motors, and Mahindra have been asked to submit revised production plans within 15 days. These must include energy efficiency targets and alternative supply chain arrangements.

The auto industry contributes approximately 7% to India's GDP. Any production slowdown could impact over 3 crore jobs directly and indirectly across the sector.

Analysts estimate the energy crisis could increase manufacturing costs by 12-15% in the near term. Companies with higher EV exposure and localised supply chains are expected to weather the storm better.
