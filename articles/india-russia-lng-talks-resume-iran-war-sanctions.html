India-Russia LNG Talks Resume Amid Trump Iran War Sanctions Risk

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India and Russia reached a verbal agreement on March 19 to negotiate resuming direct LNG sales
  • 02/New Delhi has approached Washington for a potential sanctions waiver on Russian energy deals
  • 03/Russian crude imports to India could double to 40% of total supply within a month

India and Russia have agreed to prepare for resuming direct liquefied natural gas (LNG) sales. The verbal agreement was reached on March 19 between Russian Deputy Energy Minister Pavel Sorokin and Indian Petroleum Minister Hardeep Singh Puri in Delhi.

Negotiations could conclude within weeks if India pursues the deal. However, any agreement risks violating Western sanctions imposed after the Ukraine war.

India has approached Washington seeking a potential sanctions waiver. The request comes as the U.S.-Israeli attack on Iran disrupts Gulf energy supplies.

The Strait of Hormuz, through which about half of India's crude oil and LNG passes, has been effectively shut down. Tehran's retaliation included targeting ships in the critical waterway.

Russian crude imports to India could double from January levels to 40% of total imports. India purchased nearly $44 billion of Russian crude last year.

"India chose the course that best served its national interests," said Ajai Malhotra, former Indian ambassador to Moscow. He added Delhi should "demand exemptions as a normal part of negotiation between strategic partners."

New Delhi had slashed Russian oil purchases after Trump imposed 50% tariffs in August. The move was seen as a concession to Washington during trade negotiations.

A government briefing note warned that prolonged Middle East disruption could cause "higher inflation, a weaker currency and rising foreign debt." Wholesale inflation could rise 0.3% to 0.7%.

Russia now conducts 96% of trade with India in rupees and roubles. Rupee-rouble transactions up to $1 billion can process in as little as one day.
