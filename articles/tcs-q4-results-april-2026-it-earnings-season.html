TCS Q4 Results April 2026: IT Bellwether To Set Tone For Earnings Season

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/TCS board to approve Q4 FY26 results on April 9, 2026, with revenue expected at ₹69,150-69,850 crore, up 3.5-4.2% QoQ
  • 02/Net profit seen rising to ₹13,850-13,980 crore as one-time Q3 charges don't repeat; EBIT margin expected at 25.1-25.4%
  • 03/Key watchpoints include $8-10 billion deal TCV, BFSI vertical recovery, AI monetization progress, and FY27 growth guidance

Tata Consultancy Services Ltd (NSE: TCS) will announce its fourth-quarter results for FY26 on Thursday, April 9, 2026. The board meeting is scheduled after market hours, with results expected between 3:40 pm and 4:20 pm IST.

TCS shares were trading flat ahead of the announcement, reflecting investor caution. The stock has declined over 20% year-to-date, underperforming the Nifty 50 index.

The IT bellwether's performance will set the tone for the entire sector's earnings season. TCS is India's largest IT services exporter with a market capitalization of ₹9.28 lakh crore.

Analysts expect a quarter of "normalization" after Q3 FY26 was hit by one-time charges. These included legal provisions and new labor code impacts totaling approximately ₹2,128 crore.

Revenue is expected to grow 3.5-4.2% sequentially to ₹69,150-69,850 crore. Dollar revenue is seen at $7.6-7.7 billion, with constant currency growth of 1.2% QoQ.

EBIT margins are projected to improve to 25.1-25.4%, aided by currency tailwinds and absence of one-offs. Q3 margins stood at 25.2% excluding exceptional items.

Net profit is expected to rise to ₹13,850-13,980 crore, up from ₹13,438 crore in Q3. The year-on-year growth would be approximately 13-14%.

The BFSI vertical accounts for over 30% of TCS revenue. Investors are watching for signs of recovery in discretionary spending from US and UK banks.

Deal wins will be a critical metric. TCV is expected in the $8-10 billion range, though lower than the year-ago period which included a large renewal.

TCS reported strong $9.3 billion TCV in Q3 FY26, with BFSI contributing $3.8 billion and North America $4.9 billion. The book-to-bill ratio was healthy at 1.4x.

AI monetization is a key focus area. TCS AI services now generate $1.8 billion in annualized revenue, growing 17.3% QoQ in constant currency.

The company has been restructuring its workforce amid AI disruption. Approximately 12,000 roles were impacted, representing 2% of the workforce.

Senior-level attrition has risen to 16%, well above the historical 4-5% annual churn. Over 300 senior executives have exited in the eight months to March 31.

The company has already paid ₹79 per share in dividends during FY26. The board will consider a final dividend recommendation for approval at the 31st AGM.

An earnings conference call is scheduled for 7:00 pm on April 9. Management commentary on client budgets, hiring plans, and FY27 guidance will be closely tracked.

The IT sector is expected to deliver muted sequential growth in Q4 FY26. FY27 growth is projected at 3-5% by most brokerages amid geopolitical uncertainties.

"We are confident of a good CY2026 based on client conversations and strong deal momentum," said CEO K Krithivasan in the Q3 earnings call.

JM Financial maintains an 'Add' rating with a target price of ₹2,660. Axis Direct expects 2.6% QoQ revenue growth led by BFSI and Hi-tech verticals.

Investors will also watch for attrition trends, utilization rates, and any update on campus hiring for FY27. TCS hired 40,000 freshers through campus recruitment in FY26.

The results come amid AI-led disruption in the $297 billion global IT services industry. TCS is positioning itself as an AI-led technology services company.
