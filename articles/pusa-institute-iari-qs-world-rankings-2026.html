Pusa Institute Debuts in QS World Rankings 2026, Ranks 151-200 in Agriculture

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ICAR-IARI (Pusa Institute) debuts in QS World Rankings 2026, placed in 151-200 band in Agriculture & Forestry category
  • 02/Only four Indian institutions feature in this band including IARI, BHU, IIT Kharagpur, and Delhi University
  • 03/Institute tops NIRF Agriculture rankings 2025; recognition reflects 121 years of research excellence

ICAR-Indian Agricultural Research Institute (IARI), popularly known as Pusa Institute, has made its debut in the QS World University Rankings 2026. The premier agricultural institution has been placed in the 151-200 band in the Agriculture & Forestry subject category.

The 121-year-old institute is one of only four Indian institutions to feature in this band. The others include Banaras Hindu University, IIT Kharagpur, and University of Delhi.

The QS Rankings evaluate institutions on academic reputation, employer reputation, research citations, and international collaboration. IARI scored 43.9 in employer reputation, matching IIT Kharagpur's score.

"ICAR is tirelessly working towards strengthening world-class multidisciplinary research-intensive education ecosystem," said M L Jat, Director General of ICAR. He emphasized Prime Minister Modi's focus on skilled human capital for Viksit Bharat.

The institute was established in 1905 and shifted to Delhi in 1936 after the Bihar earthquake. It attained deemed university status in 1958.

IARI played a pivotal role in India's Green Revolution during the 1970s. It developed famous "Pusa Varieties" of wheat and other high-yielding crop varieties.

The 500-hectare campus houses 20 divisions and 5 multi-disciplinary centres. It continues to lead research in crop science, genetics, plant breeding, and climate-resilient agriculture.

IARI secured the top position in NIRF Agriculture rankings 2025 with 89.23 points. It has maintained this leadership position for three consecutive years.

The QS debut positions ICAR-IARI as a global leader in science-led agriculture. It reflects sustained contributions to agri-food systems and sustainable development goals.
