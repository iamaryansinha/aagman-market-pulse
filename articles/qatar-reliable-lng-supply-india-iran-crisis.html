Qatar Vows Reliable LNG Supply To India Amid Iran Crisis

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Qatar affirms commitment to remain India's reliable LNG supplier amid escalating West Asia tensions
  • 02/India depends on Qatar for approximately 40% of its total liquefied natural gas imports annually
  • 03/Energy security cooperation strengthened as Iran conflict raises concerns over regional supply disruptions

Qatar has vowed to maintain stable LNG supplies to India. The commitment comes amid rising tensions in West Asia.

The assurance was conveyed during high-level diplomatic consultations on April 10, 2026. Qatar is India's largest LNG supplier.

India imports approximately 40% of its total LNG from Qatar annually. The supply relationship spans over two decades.

The Iran-Israel conflict has raised concerns about energy security. Regional instability threatens shipping routes through the Strait of Hormuz.

Qatar's commitment provides relief to Indian energy planners. Disruption would severely impact power generation and industrial operations.

India consumed 31.8 billion cubic meters of LNG in 2025. Qatar supplied roughly 12.7 billion cubic meters of this volume.

State-run Petronet LNG operates long-term contracts with QatarEnergy. The Dahej and Kochi terminals receive regular Qatari cargoes.

Experts say the pledge reflects Qatar's reliability as a supplier. It also underscores the strategic importance of the bilateral energy partnership.

ANY DISRUPTION WOULD SEVERELY IMPACT INDIA'S POWER GENERATION SECTOR, FERTILIZER PRODUCTION, AND INDUSTRIAL OPERATIONS THAT RELY HEAVILY ON NATURAL GAS AS FEEDSTOCK AND FUEL.
