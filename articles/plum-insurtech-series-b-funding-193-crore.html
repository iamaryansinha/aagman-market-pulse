Plum Insurtech Raises ₹193 Crore In Series B Led By Peak XV Partners

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Plum Benefits raises ₹193 crore ($20.5 million) in Series B funding round led by Peak XV Partners with participation from Tanglin Venture Partners and GMO Venture Partners
  • 02/The profitable insurtech serves 6,000+ organizations including CRED and Meesho, with industry-leading claims NPS of 79
  • 03/Funds will deploy towards AI-driven claims operations, enterprise security, and expanding healthcare stack including telehealth and mental wellness

Plum Benefits Pvt. Ltd has raised ₹193 crore ($20.5 million) in a Series B funding round. Peak XV Partners led the investment with participation from existing investor Tanglin Venture Partners and new backer GMO Venture Partners.

The Bengaluru-based insurtech platform announced the funding on March 26, 2026. The company is now cash flow profitable and has recorded 60x growth in claims processed.

Plum serves over 6,000 organizations including CRED, Meesho, Groww, and Unacademy. The startup provides employee health benefits through a B2B2C model.

Founded in 2019 by Abhishek Poddar and Saurabh Arora, Plum offers group health insurance, wellness programs, and healthcare services. The company processes over 100,000 claims annually.

Plum's claims NPS stands at 79, significantly higher than industry benchmarks. The median cashless claim resolution time is under 20 minutes. Reimbursement claims close in hours versus industry standard of days.

The fresh capital will strengthen technology infrastructure and build enterprise-grade security layers. The company plans to scale AI-driven claims operations and expand integrations with HR and payroll systems.

Healthcare now contributes 20% of total revenue. The company will broaden its healthcare stack across preventive care, primary care, mental wellness, and telehealth.

"We made a decision on day one that our north star would be the claims experience," said Abhishek Poddar, co-founder and CEO. "This round gives us the capital to move faster on what we know works."
