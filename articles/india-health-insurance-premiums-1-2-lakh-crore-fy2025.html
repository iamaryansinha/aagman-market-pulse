India's Health Insurance Premiums Cross ₹1.2 Lakh Crore in FY2025

3 MIN READ

TLDR

  • 01/India's health insurance premiums cross ₹1.2 lakh crore in FY2025, marking 9% growth over previous year
  • 02/IRDAI mandates faster cashless claims: 1-hour pre-authorization, 3-hour final authorization
  • 03/58 crore lives covered but per capita premium at $97 vs global average of $943 shows penetration gap

India's health insurance sector has achieved a major milestone. Total premium collections exceeded ₹1.2 lakh crore in financial year 2024-25. The 9% year-on-year growth reflects rising awareness and improved access to healthcare financing. Increasing demand for financial protection against medical expenses also drove the surge. Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman highlighted this achievement in Parliament. She noted that 58 crore lives are now covered under various health insurance schemes. The premium breakdown reveals balanced contribution across sectors. Public sector insurers account for ₹42,420 crore. Private sector insurers contribute ₹37,752 crore. Standalone health insurance companies generate ₹37,331 crore. However, penetration remains low. Per capita premium stands at just $97. The global average is $943. This gap indicates significant room for growth. IRDAI has mandated specific timelines for cashless claims. Pre-authorization must be approved within 1 hour. Final authorization must complete within 3 hours. These measures aim to minimize delays. Patients will receive prompt access to medical care. Policyholder experience is the focus. Claims settlement ratios have improved. The ratio reached 87.50% in 2024-25. This is up from 82.46% in the previous fiscal year. The government aims for universal health insurance by 2033. Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY currently covers 12 crore families. Each family gets ₹5 lakh coverage. The scheme expanded in October 2024. It now includes 6 crore senior citizens aged 70 and above. As of February 2026, 43.52 crore Ayushman cards have been issued.
