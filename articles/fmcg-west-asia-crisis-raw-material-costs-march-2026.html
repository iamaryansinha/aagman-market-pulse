FMCG Firms Face Raw Material Crisis as West Asia Conflict Completes One Month

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/FMCG sector faces packaging cost surge one month into West Asia conflict; PET resin prices hit ₹143/kg, expected to cross ₹150/kg next week
  • 02/Packaged drinking water, edible oils, paints sectors have already implemented price hikes; Asian Paints, Berger Paints, Kansai Nerolac announced increases
  • 03/If conflict continues, second round of price hikes expected by late April or early May as companies monitor inflation and crude oil trends

One month into the West Asia conflict, FMCG companies are grappling with surging packaging material costs. Rising crude oil prices and rupee depreciation have created significant cost pressures across the sector.

PET resin prices have jumped to ₹143 per kg. Industry executives expect prices to cross ₹150 per kg by next week.

The packaged drinking water industry has already taken price hikes. A ₹18 pack is now priced at ₹20. Further increases may follow if crude prices persist.

Paint manufacturers have moved first. Asian Paints, Berger Paints, and Kansai Nerolac have announced price hikes. Analysts expect a second round if crude stays elevated.

Edible oil prices are closely linked to crude. Imported edible oil prices have risen 10-15% across the board due to the conflict.

"The edible oil prices are closely linked to crude oil. Due to the West Asia conflict, all the imported edible oil prices are already up by 10-15 per cent across the board and so that price increase is already reflecting," said Shrikant Kanhere, MD & CEO, AWL Agri Business.

Commercial LPG rationalization has added pressure. Some production lines face challenges due to rationing and availability issues.

Mayank Shah, Vice President of Parle Products, said, "We are closely monitoring the situation. Higher crude oil prices have led to higher packaging costs. Price of commercial LPG has also gone up and since there is rationing there have been some challenges."

Parle Products is using alternatives wherever possible. However, some production lines have faced impact. The industry has urged the government to consider packaged food manufacturing plants as essential for LPG allocations.

Dabur India is also watching the situation closely. A company spokesperson said, "We are closely watching the inflation and evaluating price hikes wherever necessary."

A BNP Paribas report noted that FMCG companies saw improved demand trends in Q3 FY26. However, the West Asia conflict has created fresh headwinds around costs, export orders, and LPG availability.

If the conflict continues, industry experts say price hike decisions will need to be taken by late April or early May. Companies are absorbing costs for now but cannot sustain this indefinitely.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS CAUSING THE SURGE IN FMCG RAW MATERIAL COSTS?
THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT HAS LED TO RISING CRUDE OIL PRICES AND RUPEE DEPRECIATION. PACKAGING MATERIALS DERIVED FROM PETROLEUM, SUCH AS PET RESIN, HAVE SEEN PRICES JUMP TO ₹143 PER KG WITH EXPECTATIONS OF CROSSING ₹150 PER KG SOON.
WHICH FMCG SECTORS HAVE ALREADY IMPLEMENTED PRICE HIKES?
PACKAGED DRINKING WATER, EDIBLE OILS, AND PAINTS HAVE ALREADY SEEN PRICE INCREASES. ASIAN PAINTS, BERGER PAINTS, AND KANSAI NEROLAC HAVE ANNOUNCED HIKES. A ₹18 WATER PACK IS NOW PRICED AT ₹20.
HOW MUCH HAVE EDIBLE OIL PRICES INCREASED?
IMPORTED EDIBLE OIL PRICES HAVE RISEN 10-15% ACROSS THE BOARD DUE TO THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT, ACCORDING TO AWL AGRI BUSINESS MD & CEO SHRIKANT KANHERE.
WHAT IMPACT HAS COMMERCIAL LPG RATIONALIZATION HAD?
COMMERCIAL LPG RATIONING HAS CREATED PRODUCTION CHALLENGES FOR SOME MANUFACTURERS. PARLE PRODUCTS REPORTED IMPACT ON SOME PRODUCTION LINES AND HAS URGED THE GOVERNMENT TO CLASSIFY PACKAGED FOOD MANUFACTURING AS ESSENTIAL FOR LPG ALLOCATIONS.
WHEN CAN CONSUMERS EXPECT MORE PRICE HIKES?
IF THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT CONTINUES, INDUSTRY EXPERTS SAY COMPANIES WILL NEED TO DECIDE ON ADDITIONAL PRICE HIKES BY LATE APRIL OR EARLY MAY. PAINT COMPANIES ARE ALREADY EXPECTED TO TAKE A SECOND ROUND OF HIKES IF CRUDE OIL PRICES REMAIN ELEVATED.
