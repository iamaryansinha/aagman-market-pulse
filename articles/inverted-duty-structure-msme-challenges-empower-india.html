Inverted Duty Structure Hits MSMEs: Input Tax Refund Gap Widens, Says Empower India

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/MSMEs face blocked working capital as input services and capital goods remain excluded from GST refund mechanism under inverted duty structure
  • 02/Empower India reports structural disadvantage is threatening viability of manufacturing businesses across the sector
  • 03/Industry body calls for comprehensive GST reform to include all inputs in refund calculations

MSMEs in India are struggling with a structural GST disadvantage. The inverted duty structure blocks refunds on input services and capital goods.

Empower India, an MSME advocacy body, flagged this issue on March 31, 2026. The exclusion creates a severe working capital crunch for small manufacturers.

Under the current GST framework, output tax rates often fall below input tax rates. This creates an accumulation of unutilized input tax credits.

The refund mechanism only covers inputs on goods, not services. This leaves MSMEs unable to recover taxes paid on essential services like logistics and consulting.

Capital goods — machinery and equipment critical for production — face the same exclusion. Manufacturers cannot claim refunds on these high-value purchases.

This structural gap hits MSMEs harder than large corporations. Smaller firms lack the cash reserves to absorb blocked credits running into lakhs of rupees.

The manufacturing sector employs over 27 million workers in MSME units. Viability threats here carry direct employment consequences.

Industry experts estimate the locked-up credit amounts to thousands of crores nationwide. This capital could otherwise fund expansion and job creation.

Empower India has called for immediate policy intervention. The demand includes extending refund eligibility to all inputs without goods-services distinction.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE PROBLEM FACING MSMES?
UNDER GST, WHEN THE TAX RATE ON INPUTS IS HIGHER THAN THE TAX RATE ON OUTPUTS, BUSINESSES ACCUMULATE UNUTILIZED INPUT TAX CREDITS. THIS STRUCTURAL ISSUE, KNOWN AS INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE, BLOCKS REFUNDS FOR INPUT SERVICES AND CAPITAL GOODS, LOCKING UP WORKING CAPITAL.
WHICH INPUTS ARE CURRENTLY EXCLUDED FROM THE GST REFUND MECHANISM?
THE CURRENT REFUND MECHANISM EXCLUDES INPUT SERVICES SUCH AS LOGISTICS, CONSULTING, AND PROFESSIONAL SERVICES. CAPITAL GOODS INCLUDING MACHINERY AND PRODUCTION EQUIPMENT ARE ALSO EXCLUDED FROM REFUND ELIGIBILITY.
WHY ARE MSMES MORE AFFECTED THAN LARGE CORPORATIONS?
MSMES LACK THE CASH RESERVES AND CREDIT ACCESS THAT LARGE CORPORATIONS ENJOY. BLOCKED INPUT TAX CREDITS OF SEVERAL LAKHS CREATE IMMEDIATE WORKING CAPITAL CRISES FOR SMALL MANUFACTURERS, THREATENING THEIR OPERATIONAL VIABILITY.
WHAT HAS EMPOWER INDIA RECOMMENDED TO ADDRESS THIS ISSUE?
EMPOWER INDIA HAS CALLED FOR COMPREHENSIVE GST REFORM TO EXTEND REFUND ELIGIBILITY TO ALL INPUTS WITHOUT DISTINGUISHING BETWEEN GOODS AND SERVICES. THIS WOULD ALLOW MSMES TO CLAIM REFUNDS ON INPUT SERVICES AND CAPITAL GOODS PURCHASES.
WHAT IS THE POTENTIAL IMPACT ON EMPLOYMENT IF THIS ISSUE PERSISTS?
THE MANUFACTURING SECTOR EMPLOYS OVER 27 MILLION WORKERS IN MSME UNITS. IF THE INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE CONTINUES TO THREATEN BUSINESS VIABILITY, EXPERTS WARN OF POTENTIAL LAYOFFS AND REDUCED HIRING ACROSS THE SECTOR.
