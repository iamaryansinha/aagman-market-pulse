Kotak Mahindra Bank Ltd (NSE: KOTAKBANK) shares hit a 52-week low on March 30, 2026. The decline came as the bank issued a clarification regarding an FDR case.

The stock faced heavy selling pressure following the emergence of a media report. Market sensitivity to governance-related developments in the banking sector has increased significantly.

The bank's management issued a clarification to address investor concerns. Details of the FDR case and its financial implications remain under scrutiny by market participants.

Kotak Mahindra Bank has been one of India's leading private sector lenders. The stock has faced headwinds amid broader concerns over banking sector governance standards.

The 52-week low indicates sustained pressure on the counter. Investors are reassessing valuations in light of the recent developments.

Banking stocks have been particularly sensitive to regulatory and governance news. Any clarity from the management could help stabilize the stock price.

Market participants await further details on the FDR case resolution. The bank's response and regulatory stance will be key factors to watch.
