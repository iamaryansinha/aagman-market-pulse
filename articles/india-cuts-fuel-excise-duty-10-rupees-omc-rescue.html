India Cuts Fuel Excise Duty By ₹10 To Rescue Oil Companies From Losses

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government cuts excise duty on petrol to ₹3 per litre and diesel to nil, reducing levies by ₹10 each
  • 02/OMCs BPCL, HPCL, IOC were losing ₹24-30 per litre as crude hit $100/barrel amid Iran tensions
  • 03/Pump prices remain unchanged; benefit flows to oil companies. Annual fiscal cost estimated at ₹1.55 lakh crore

The Indian government has slashed excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre each. The move reduces petrol duty from ₹13 to ₹3 per litre and diesel duty from ₹10 to nil.

Petroleum Minister Hardeep Singh Puri announced the decision on March 27, 2026. The cut aims to shield state-run oil marketing companies from mounting losses.

OMCs including Indian Oil Corporation (NSE: IOC), Bharat Petroleum (NSE: BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum (NSE: HPCL) were bleeding heavily. Under-recoveries reached ₹24 per litre on petrol and ₹30 per litre on diesel.

Global Brent crude prices had surged to $100 per barrel recently. Prices settled around $94 on March 27 as Iran war tensions kept markets volatile.

The government will absorb the revenue loss instead of passing burden to consumers. Retail pump prices in Delhi remain frozen at ₹94.77 for petrol and ₹87.67 for diesel.

Emkay Global analyst Madhvi Arora estimates the annualized fiscal hit at ₹1.55 lakh crore. The cut will absorb 30-40% of OMCs' annualized auto fuel losses.

OMC stocks swung wildly on the news. HPCL surged 5% before slipping, while BPCL and IOC reversed early gains to trade flat or lower.

Balance sheets remain strong with BPCL's net-debt-to-equity at 0.3x, IOC at 0.8x, and HPCL at 1.4x. The excise cut should ease cash flow pressures.

Former HPCL CMD MK Surana said pump prices are unlikely to fall. The benefit will instead repair marketing margins and stabilize OMC finances.

Retail fuel prices have remained unchanged since February 2022. The government has prioritized inflation control over market pricing amid volatile crude.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MUCH HAS THE EXCISE DUTY BEEN CUT ON PETROL AND DIESEL?
THE GOVERNMENT HAS CUT EXCISE DUTY BY ₹10 PER LITRE ON BOTH PETROL AND DIESEL. PETROL DUTY IS REDUCED FROM ₹13 TO ₹3 PER LITRE, WHILE DIESEL DUTY IS CUT FROM ₹10 TO NIL.
WHY DID THE GOVERNMENT CUT EXCISE DUTY ON FUEL?
THE CUT WAS NECESSITATED BY SURGING GLOBAL CRUDE PRICES THAT HIT $100 PER BARREL. OIL MARKETING COMPANIES WERE FACING UNDER-RECOVERIES OF ₹24 PER LITRE ON PETROL AND ₹30 PER LITRE ON DIESEL, THREATENING THEIR FINANCIAL STABILITY.
WILL PETROL AND DIESEL PRICES AT PUMPS COME DOWN?
NO, RETAIL PUMP PRICES ARE EXPECTED TO REMAIN UNCHANGED. PETROL IN DELHI STAYS AT ₹94.77 PER LITRE AND DIESEL AT ₹87.67. THE EXCISE CUT BENEFIT WILL FLOW TO OMCs TO OFFSET THEIR LOSSES RATHER THAN TO CONSUMERS.
WHAT IS THE FISCAL IMPACT OF THIS EXCISE DUTY CUT?
ACCORDING TO EMKAY GLOBAL, THE ANNUALIZED FISCAL HIT TO THE GOVERNMENT WILL BE APPROXIMATELY ₹1.55 LAKH CRORE. THE MOVE WILL ABSORB AN ESTIMATED 30-40% OF THE ANNUALIZED LOSSES FACED BY OMCs ON AUTO FUELS.
HOW WILL THIS IMPACT OIL COMPANY STOCKS LIKE BPCL, HPCL AND IOC?
OMC STOCKS INITIALLY SURGED UP TO 5% ON THE NEWS BUT REVERSED GAINS TO TRADE MIXED. THE CUT EASES UNDER-RECOVERIES AND IMPROVES CASH FLOWS, BUT INVESTOR CAUTION PERSISTS DUE TO ONGOING GLOBAL CRUDE VOLATILITY AND GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS.
