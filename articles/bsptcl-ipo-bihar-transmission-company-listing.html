BSPTCL IPO: Bihar Plans Stock Market Debut for Power Transmission Company

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Bihar State Power Transmission Company Ltd plans IPO on NSE, becoming India's first listed state-owned transmission utility
  • 02/BSPTCL reports net worth of ₹12,069.55 crore with FY25 profit of ₹286 crore and total income of ₹1,968 crore
  • 03/State Energy Department has issued Expression of Interest to onboard merchant bankers for the listing process

Bihar State Power Transmission Company Ltd (BSPTCL) is preparing for a stock market debut. The company will become the first state-owned transmission utility in India to go public.

State Energy Secretary Manoj Kumar Singh confirmed the listing plans in a March 2026 interview. The government has issued an Expression of Interest to onboard merchant bankers for the IPO process.

BSPTCL reported a net worth of ₹12,069.55 crore as of early 2026. The company posted a profit after tax of ₹286 crore on total income of ₹1,968 crore for FY25.

The transmission utility has been profitable for over a decade. This consistent performance makes it an attractive candidate for public listing.

The IPO proceeds will fund a ₹16,194 crore expansion plan. This includes upgrading substations and high-voltage transmission lines across Bihar.

Bihar recorded peak power demand of 8,800 MW in 2025. Demand is expected to reach 9,500 MW in 2026 and cross 13,000 MW by 2030.

"Our transmission company has been consistently profitable for over a decade. We want the public to also share in this growth journey," Singh said.

The exact IPO size will be determined after merchant banker appointment. The structure may include fresh issue, offer for sale, or a combination.

BSPTCL Managing Director Rahul Kumar said the company plans to launch the IPO within six months. SEBI regulatory compliance is currently underway.

The government may also list distribution companies NBPDCL and SBPDCL in the future. These will follow after BSPTCL's revenue stream stabilises.
