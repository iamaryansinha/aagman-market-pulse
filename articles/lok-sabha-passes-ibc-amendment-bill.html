Lok Sabha Passes IBC Amendment Bill To Reduce Resolution Time

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Lok Sabha passes amendment bill to the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code introduced in August 2025
  • 02/Finance Minister states changes will help maximise value for all stakeholders in insolvency proceedings
  • 03/Bill proposes provisions to reduce the time taken for resolution of distressed assets

The Lok Sabha has passed the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) Amendment Bill. The bill was introduced by the government on August 12, 2025.

The amendments propose changes to streamline the insolvency resolution process. Key provisions aim to reduce the time taken for resolution of distressed assets.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman spoke during the passage of the bill. She stated the amendments will help maximise value for all stakeholders.

The IBC was enacted in 2016 to consolidate insolvency laws. It provides a time-bound process for resolving insolvency in companies and individuals.

The latest amendments seek to further improve the efficiency of the resolution process. Faster resolution can help preserve asset value and protect creditor interests.

The bill will now proceed to the Rajya Sabha for approval. Upon passage, it will receive presidential assent to become law.
