5 Reasons Why Gold & Silver May Not Glitter This Year: Precious Metals Outlook

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold has surged from $2,600 to over $5,000 per ounce in 12 months, leading to stretched valuations and profit-booking risks
  • 02/Strong US dollar and rising 10-year Treasury yields at 4.2-4.3% increase opportunity cost of holding non-yielding precious metals
  • 03/Fading Fed rate cut expectations and hawkish policy pivot could trigger gold price correction of 5-20% according to World Gold Council

Gold and silver prices have reached record highs, but multiple headwinds suggest limited upside in 2026. After rallying from $2,600 to over $5,000 per ounce, gold faces mounting pressure from macroeconomic forces.

The US Dollar Index has climbed above 100.2, its highest level since May 2025. A stronger dollar makes gold and silver more expensive for international buyers using other currencies. This directly reduces global demand for precious metals.

The 10-year Treasury yield currently sits at 4.2-4.3%. Higher yields increase the opportunity cost of holding gold, which pays no interest or dividends. Investors are rotating toward income-generating assets like bonds as real yields turn positive.

Federal Reserve policy expectations have shifted dramatically. Markets have priced out several expected rate cuts for 2026. The March FOMC meeting maintained rates amid inflation concerns, reducing the probability of June cuts from 57% to below 49%.

Silver faces additional industrial demand risks. The metal derives over 50% of demand from industrial uses including solar panels and electronics. Economic growth concerns and trade war uncertainties threaten manufacturing activity. Silver dropped to $77 per ounce in March, down from January highs near $120.

Profit booking presents another near-term risk. After gaining 120% in 2025, silver positions appear crowded. Gold ETF holdings could see sustained outflows if risk-on sentiment returns to equity markets.

Commerzbank maintains one of the more cautious 2026 forecasts, targeting $4,400 for gold. The World Gold Council warns that a "reflation return" scenario could trigger gold price corrections of 5-20% from current levels. Support levels to watch include $4,550 and the critical $4,200 zone representing the 200-day moving average.
