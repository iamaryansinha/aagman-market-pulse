ONGC Mumbai High Platform Fire: 10 Injured In SHP Offshore Incident

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Fire broke out at ONGC's SHP platform in Mumbai High at 5:45 PM on April 3, injuring 10 personnel
  • 02/All injured are stable and receiving medical treatment; operations have been normalized at the offshore facility
  • 03/Cause of fire remains undisclosed; investigation likely as company assures further updates

A fire incident occurred at Oil and Natural Gas Corporation's SHP Platform under the Mumbai High Asset on Friday evening. The blaze was reported at approximately 5:45 PM (17:45 IST) on April 3, 2026.

ONGC confirmed that 10 personnel sustained minor injuries in the incident. All injured individuals are currently under medical care and reported to be in stable condition.

Emergency response measures were immediately activated following the incident. The company stated the fire was "promptly brought under control and extinguished" without escalation.

Operations at the offshore platform have been restored to normal following the incident. "Operations have been normalised. Further updates will follow," ONGC said in its official statement posted on X.

The SHP platform is part of the Mumbai High field located approximately 160 kilometers off the Mumbai coast in the Arabian Sea. The field currently produces about 135,000 barrels of oil per day, representing approximately 38% of India's domestic oil production.

Notably, the SHP platform had recently undergone digital transformation. In January 2026, ONGC went live with its Digital SHP platform as part of the company's push toward autonomous offshore operations and Zero Manning platforms.

The incident evokes memories of the catastrophic July 2005 fire at Mumbai High North that killed 22 personnel and destroyed the platform. That facility was later replaced in 2012 at a cost of ₹6,060 crore.

ONGC shares closed at ₹287.70 on April 2, 2026. The stock has gained approximately 24% over the past year.
