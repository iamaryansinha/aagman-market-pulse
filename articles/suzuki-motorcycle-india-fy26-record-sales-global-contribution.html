Suzuki Motorcycle India FY26 Sales Hit Record 14.4 Lakh Units, Eyes 50% Global Contribution

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SMIPL achieved record FY26 sales of 14,39,415 units, up 15% YoY from 12.56 lakh units in FY25
  • 02/India contributes nearly 50% to Suzuki Motor Corporation's global two-wheeler sales, making it the largest market
  • 03/New Rs 1,174 crore Kharkhoda plant with 7.5 lakh capacity coming up in 2027 to boost domestic and export volumes

Suzuki Motorcycle India Pvt Ltd (SMIPL) reported its highest-ever annual sales in FY26. The company sold 14,39,415 units, marking a 15% growth over 12,56,155 units sold in FY25.

Domestic sales reached 11,74,874 units in FY26, up 12% from 10,45,662 units in the previous year. Exports grew faster at 26%, touching 2,64,541 units compared to 2,10,493 units in FY25.

Managing Director Kenichi Umeda stated that India contributes nearly 50% to Suzuki Motor Corporation's global two-wheeler sales. "Same as automobiles, the Suzuki motorcycle unit is almost 50 per cent from India," he said.

All new two-wheeler models, including the electric scooter e-Access, were launched in India first globally. This underscores the strategic importance of the Indian market for the parent company.

SMIPL exports to around 77 countries worldwide. The company is now setting up a new manufacturing plant at Kharkhoda, Haryana, with an investment of Rs 1,174 crore.

The new facility will have an annual production capacity of 7.5 lakh units. It is expected to become operational by 2027, supplementing the current Kherki Daula plant that can produce 14 lakh units annually.

"The reason why we are setting up a new plant is also (because) we will have more 'Make in India', and SMC is also emphasising the Indian market," Umeda noted. The company aims to increase India's contribution to SMC's global sales beyond the current 50% level.
