Share Buyback Revival Signals Confidence Amid Market Volatility: Investment Bankers

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Buyback activity is seeing a revival as companies turn to flexible open market routes amid ongoing market volatility
  • 02/Investment bankers note buybacks can aid price stability while signalling management confidence in undervalued stocks
  • 03/The open market mechanism allows companies to purchase shares at favourable prices without fixed timelines

Share buyback activity is staging a comeback on Dalal Street. Companies are increasingly using the flexible open market route to support their stock prices amid market volatility.

Investment bankers say this revival signals management confidence in underlying business fundamentals. Buybacks offer a way to return excess cash while addressing undervaluation concerns.

The open market buyback mechanism allows companies to purchase shares over an extended period. Unlike tender offers, there is no fixed price or timeline, providing strategic flexibility.

Market participants note that buybacks can act as a floor for stock prices during volatile periods. They demonstrate that insiders believe the stock is trading below intrinsic value.

The trend comes as several mid-cap and large-cap stocks trade at significant discounts to historical valuations. Companies with strong cash positions see buybacks as an efficient capital allocation tool.

Sebi regulations permit open market buybacks up to 15% of paid-up capital and free reserves. Companies must complete the buyback within 6 months from the date of opening.

Analysts caution that buybacks alone cannot drive sustained stock performance. Operational performance and earnings growth remain the primary drivers of long-term shareholder returns.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS A SHARE BUYBACK?
A SHARE BUYBACK IS WHEN A COMPANY PURCHASES ITS OWN SHARES FROM THE OPEN MARKET OR THROUGH A TENDER OFFER. THIS REDUCES THE NUMBER OF OUTSTANDING SHARES AND CAN INCREASE EARNINGS PER SHARE FOR REMAINING SHAREHOLDERS.
WHY ARE COMPANIES REVIVING BUYBACK ACTIVITY NOW?
COMPANIES ARE REVIVING BUYBACKS AMID MARKET VOLATILITY TO SIGNAL CONFIDENCE IN THEIR BUSINESS FUNDAMENTALS, SUPPORT STOCK PRICES, AND EFFICIENTLY DEPLOY EXCESS CASH WHEN SHARES ARE TRADING AT ATTRACTIVE VALUATIONS.
WHAT IS THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN OPEN MARKET AND TENDER OFFER BUYBACKS?
OPEN MARKET BUYBACKS ALLOW COMPANIES TO PURCHASE SHARES GRADUALLY OVER TIME AT PREVAILING MARKET PRICES. TENDER OFFERS INVITE SHAREHOLDERS TO SELL AT A FIXED PREMIUM PRICE WITHIN A SPECIFIC WINDOW, USUALLY 15-30 DAYS.
HOW DO BUYBACKS SUPPORT SHAREHOLDER CONFIDENCE?
BUYBACKS SIGNAL THAT MANAGEMENT BELIEVES THE STOCK IS UNDERVALUED. THEY ALSO REDUCE SHARES OUTSTANDING, WHICH CAN BOOST EARNINGS PER SHARE AND RETURN ON EQUITY FOR REMAINING SHAREHOLDERS.
WHAT ARE THE SEBI REGULATIONS FOR OPEN MARKET BUYBACKS?
COMPANIES CAN BUY BACK UP TO 15% OF PAID-UP CAPITAL AND FREE RESERVES THROUGH THE OPEN MARKET ROUTE. THE BUYBACK MUST BE COMPLETED WITHIN 6 MONTHS FROM THE DATE OF OPENING.
