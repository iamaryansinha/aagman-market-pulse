Trump's Iran War Pushes India To Rekindle Russia Friendship For Energy Security

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/US-Israel "Operation Epic Fury" strikes on Iran starting Feb 28, 2026 disrupt Gulf oil supplies through Strait of Hormuz
  • 02/India turns to Russia for energy security, with Russian crude imports jumping from 2% in FY21 to 35% in FY25 worth over $50 billion
  • 03/Bilateral trade hits $68 billion as India clears ₹10,000 crore for 288 S-400 missiles and seeks 5 additional air defence squadrons

The US-Israel military operation against Iran is reshaping global energy flows. India has accelerated its pivot toward Russia to secure oil supplies and defence equipment.

President Trump confirmed "Operation Epic Fury" strikes on February 28, 2026. Coordinated aerial attacks targeted Iranian nuclear facilities, missile silos, and drone factories near Isfahan and Tehran.

Brent crude spiked 14.2% to $85.40 per barrel within 30 minutes of the announcement. Maritime insurance premiums for Gulf-bound tankers jumped 400%, according to Lloyd's of London.

India's dependence on Russian oil has surged dramatically. Russian crude imports rose from just 2% of India's total in FY21 to 35% in FY25. Imports are valued at over $50 billion annually.

Total India-Russia bilateral trade reached $68 billion in FY25, growing at a 70% compound annual growth rate since FY21. However, the trade remains heavily skewed. India imports $65 billion while exporting only $5 billion to Russia.

The strategic partnership extends beyond energy. India's Defence Acquisition Council approved ₹10,000 crore ($1.1 billion) for 288 S-400 surface-to-air missiles in March 2026. The purchase covers 120 short-range and 168 long-range missiles.

This replenishment follows operational use during "Operation Sindoor" against Pakistan in May 2025. The S-400 systems achieved the longest recorded kill distance of 314 km during that conflict.

India now plans to request five additional S-400 squadrons during diplomatic engagements with Moscow. Russia has also proposed co-production of the advanced S-500 missile system.

The BrahMos supersonic cruise missile programme remains the flagship India-Russia defence collaboration. Advanced variants including BrahMos NG with 400+ km range are under discussion.

India-Iran trade has collapsed amid sanctions and regional conflict. Bilateral trade fell from $17 billion in 2018 to just $1.68 billion in 2024-25. India halted Iranian oil imports after US sanctions were reinstated in May 2019.

Trump recently suggested India would buy oil from Venezuela instead of Iran. The US granted India a 30-day window in March 2026 to purchase Russian oil before potential restrictions.

Russia has established a rupee-rouble payment mechanism to bypass Western sanctions. Russian equity inflows into India tripled to $18.45 million in FY25 from $5.16 million in FY24.

Kremlin spokesperson Dmitry Peskov called India "a very friendly partner" whose approach rests on "deep historic background of mutual understanding." Russian President Vladimir Putin visited India on December 4-5, 2025.

The Cabinet Committee on Security is set to approve a ₹63,000 crore indigenous upgrade for 84 Sukhoi Su-30MKI jets. India continues evaluating acquisition of 2-3 squadrons of Russia's Su-57 stealth fighters.
