India Retail Inflation March 2026: CPI Seen At 3.5%, 13-Month High On Fuel Surge

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's retail inflation is estimated at 3.5% YoY for March 2026, marking a 13-month high amid rising fuel costs
  • 02/February CPI was 3.2%; if realised, this marks 14th consecutive month below RBI's 4% medium-term target
  • 03/Official data from MoSPI releases April 13; HDFC Bank forecasts FY27 average inflation near 5% assuming oil at $80-85/barrel

India's retail inflation likely accelerated to 3.5% year-on-year in March 2026. The median estimate from a Financial Express poll of seven economists points to a 13-month high.

February CPI stood at 3.2%, while January printed at 2.75%. The March uptick reflects surging global crude prices triggered by the ongoing West Asia conflict.

The Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) will release official data on April 13, 2026. Poll estimates ranged between 3.3% and 4%.

Sakshi Gupta, Principal Economist at HDFC Bank, pegged March inflation at 4%. She noted fuel inflation is estimated to rise 11% YoY versus just 0.1% in February.

"LPG prices are estimated to have registered an increase of 5.6% month-on-month in March," Gupta said. She warned headline CPI could print closer to 4.5% if firewood price pressure persists.

Food inflation is projected between 3.3% and 5.3% YoY in March. Core inflation, excluding food and fuel, is seen at 3.4% to 3.9% versus 3.4% in February.

NR Bhanumurthy, Director at Madras School of Economics, highlighted rupee depreciation as a factor raising costs of intermediate goods across supply chains.

HDFC Bank estimates CPI inflation to average close to 5% in FY27. This assumes the Iran conflict de-escalates and oil prices average $80-85 per barrel.
