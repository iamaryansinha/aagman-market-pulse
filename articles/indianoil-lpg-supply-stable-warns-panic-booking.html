IndianOil Says LPG Supply Stable, Warns Against Panic Booking Amid Global Tensions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IndianOil delivers 28 lakh LPG cylinders daily, consistent with normal levels prior to current geopolitical tensions
  • 02/Nearly 87% of refill bookings made through digital platforms; deliveries authenticated via OTP-based DAC system
  • 03/Over 7,500 inspections conducted; action taken against 141 distributors including suspension of 5; 68,000 raids led to 855 FIRs

Indian Oil Corporation has assured stable LPG supply despite global tensions. The company is delivering around 28 lakh cylinders daily, matching pre-crisis levels.

Domestic LPG supply continues to be the highest priority for the nation's largest oil firm. Supplies remain adequate and under close monitoring.

Nearly 87% of refill bookings are made through digital platforms. Customers use SMS and IVRS for booking convenience.

Deliveries are authenticated through Delivery Authentication Codes. OTP-based systems ensure cylinders reach intended customers.

IOC has stepped up enforcement against malpractices. More than 7,500 inspections have been conducted.

Action has been taken against 141 distributors, including suspension of 5. In coordination with state authorities, around 68,000 raids have been carried out.

The raids have led to 855 FIRs and seizure of over 48,000 cylinders. This is part of efforts to curb black marketing and hoarding.

IOC urges customers not to resort to panic booking. The company confirms there is no shortage of LPG in the country.
