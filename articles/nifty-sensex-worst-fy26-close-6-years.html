Nifty, Sensex Post Worst FY26 Close in 6 Years Amid West Asia Tensions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex fell 1,636 points while Nifty dropped to 22,283, marking the worst fiscal year-end performance in six years
  • 02/Markets pressured by West Asia war entering its fifth week and rupee weakening past 95 against the dollar
  • 03/Investors should watch for continued volatility as geopolitical tensions and currency weakness persist into FY27

Indian equity markets closed FY26 with their worst performance in six years. The Sensex crashed 1,636 points on the final trading day.

The Nifty 50 index opened at 22,549 and briefly touched 22,714. It later plunged to close at 22,283, reversing early gains.

The selloff was driven by the West Asia war entering its fifth week. Escalating geopolitical tensions triggered risk-off sentiment across emerging markets.

The Indian rupee added to investor concerns by breaching the 95-per-dollar mark. Currency weakness reflects capital outflows and safe-haven dollar demand.

This marks the steepest fiscal year-end decline since FY20. Markets had rallied for five consecutive years prior to this downturn.

The Nifty ended FY26 with a double-digit percentage decline. Broader markets saw even sharper corrections as foreign institutional investors reduced exposure.

FY27 begins with elevated uncertainty. Investors are bracing for potential rate decisions and oil price volatility stemming from the regional conflict.
