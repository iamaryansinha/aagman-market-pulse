Coal India Absorbs 44% Input Cost Surge To Shield Consumers From Price Spike

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Coal India is absorbing sharply rising input costs including 44% surge in ammonium nitrate and 54% jump in diesel prices
  • 02/Explosives costs rose 26% to ₹49,783/tonne; CIL consumes 0.9 million tonnes annually without passing costs to consumers
  • 03/Company has lowered e-auction reserve prices and increased auction frequency to maintain affordable coal supply

Coal India Limited is absorbing sharply rising input costs to shield domestic consumers from price spikes. The state-owned miner announced it will not pass on surging expenses for explosives and industrial diesel.

Ammonium nitrate prices have jumped 44% to ₹72,750 per tonne as of April 1, 2026. Prices stood at ₹50,500 per tonne before the West Asian crisis began.

The cost of explosives has risen 26% to ₹49,783 per tonne by end-March. This is up from ₹39,588 per tonne in February 2026.

Coal India's subsidiaries consume approximately 0.9 million tonnes of explosives annually. The entire cost increase is being absorbed by the company.

Industrial diesel prices have surged nearly 54% to ₹142 per litre. Prices were around ₹92 per litre in mid-March 2026.

The company consumed approximately 4.19 lakh kilolitres of diesel in FY2025-26. CIL is also compensating contractors for higher bulk diesel purchases.

Officials warned that passing on these costs would trigger a cascading impact across coal-dependent sectors. The move aims to cap consequent costs for downstream industries.

To maintain affordability, some CIL subsidiaries have reduced reserve prices under Single Window Mode Agnostic e-auctions. The company has also increased both auction frequency and volumes offered.

Ammonium nitrate constitutes about 60% of explosives used in opencast mining operations. Prices held steady from August 2025 through January 2026 before the sharp upward trend.

Coal India remains committed to supplying affordable coal to the country. The measures aim to mitigate broader volatility in fuel markets and protect the economy from escalating energy costs.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT INPUT COSTS IS COAL INDIA ABSORBING?
COAL INDIA IS ABSORBING SHARPLY RISING COSTS OF AMMONIUM NITRATE (UP 44% TO ₹72,750/TONNE), EXPLOSIVES (UP 26% TO ₹49,783/TONNE), AND INDUSTRIAL DIESEL (UP 54% TO ₹142/LITRE).
WHY IS COAL INDIA NOT PASSING ON THESE COSTS?
THE COMPANY WARNED THAT PASSING ON THE COST INCREASES WOULD TRIGGER A CASCADING IMPACT ACROSS SECTORS RELIANT ON COAL, AFFECTING THE BROADER ECONOMY.
HOW MUCH EXPLOSIVES AND DIESEL DOES COAL INDIA CONSUME?
CIL'S SUBSIDIARIES CONSUME APPROXIMATELY 0.9 MILLION TONNES OF EXPLOSIVES ANNUALLY AND 4.19 LAKH KILOLITRES OF DIESEL PER YEAR.
WHAT MEASURES HAS COAL INDIA TAKEN TO KEEP COAL AFFORDABLE?
CIL HAS REDUCED RESERVE PRICES IN SELECT E-AUCTIONS, INCREASED AUCTION FREQUENCY, AND RAISED THE QUANTUM OF COAL OFFERED THROUGH AUCTIONS.
WHAT CAUSED THE SHARP RISE IN INPUT COSTS?
THE SURGE FOLLOWS GLOBAL SUPPLY DISRUPTIONS LINKED TO THE WEST ASIAN CRISIS, WITH AMMONIUM NITRATE PRICES REMAINING STABLE FROM AUGUST 2025 TO JANUARY 2026 BEFORE SHOOTING UPWARD.
