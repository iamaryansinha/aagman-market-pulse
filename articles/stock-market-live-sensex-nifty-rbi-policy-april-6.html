Stock Market Live: Sensex, Nifty Open Lower Ahead Of RBI Policy Outcome

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex opens at 75,400, down 280 points; Nifty50 slips below 23,000 as markets track RBI policy
  • 02/RBI MPC meeting underway; rate decision expected to hold repo rate at 6.50% amid inflation concerns
  • 03/Global cues weak: Iran-US tensions escalate, crude oil volatile; IT and FMCG drag early trade

Indian equity markets opened on a weaker note at 10:00 AM IST. The BSE Sensex declined 280 points to trade at 75,400.

The Nifty50 index slipped below the 23,000 mark. It was trading at 22,950, down 85 points from the previous close.

The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) began its three-day meeting today. Markets are closely watching for the rate decision, expected on April 9.

Most analysts expect the RBI to maintain the repo rate at 6.50%. Inflation remains above the central bank's 4% target, limiting room for rate cuts.

Global volatility is weighing on sentiment. Iran-US tensions have escalated following renewed threats in the Strait of Hormuz.

Crude oil prices remain elevated above $78 per barrel. This poses a risk for India's import-dependent economy and fuel inflation.

Sectorally, IT stocks led the decline with a 1.2% drop. FMCG and auto sectors also traded in the red during early session.

Banking stocks showed resilience, with the Nifty Bank index flat. Energy and metal stocks gained marginally on commodity price strength.

Foreign institutional investors (FIIs) have been net sellers this week. Domestic institutional investors (DIIs) provided support with consistent buying.

The broader markets underperformed, with the Nifty Midcap100 down 0.8%. Smallcap stocks declined 1.1% amid profit-booking.

Investors should watch the RBI governor's commentary on Wednesday. The inflation outlook and liquidity stance will drive near-term market direction.
