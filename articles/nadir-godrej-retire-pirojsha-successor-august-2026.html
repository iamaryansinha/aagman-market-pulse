Nadir Godrej To Retire As Chairperson In August 2026, Pirojsha Named Successor

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Nadir Godrej, 75, will step down as chairperson of Godrej Industries Group in August 2026 after 55-year career
  • 02/Pirojsha Godrej, 42, currently Executive Vice-Chairperson, to succeed as next-generation leader
  • 03/Transition follows April 2024 family split that divided 127-year-old conglomerate into two groups

Nadir Godrej will retire as chairperson of Godrej Industries Group in August 2026. The 75-year-old veteran is stepping down after leading the conglomerate through one of its most significant transformations.

Pirojsha Godrej has been named as his successor. The 42-year-old currently serves as Executive Vice-Chairperson of Godrej Industries Group.

The announcement marks a generational leadership shift at the 127-year-old Godrej conglomerate. The group was founded in 1897 by Ardeshir Godrej and Pirojsha Burjorji Godrej.

Nadir Godrej has spent 55 years at the Godrej Group. He has served as chairperson of Godrej Industries (NSE: GODREJIND) and Godrej Agrovet (NSE: GODREJAGRO).

Pirojsha is the son of Adi Godrej, who stepped down as executive chairperson in 2021. He also chairs Godrej Properties (NSE: GODREJPROP), Godrej Capital, and Godrej Fund Management.

The succession plan was formalized during the April 2024 family settlement. The Godrej empire was split into two groups: Godrej Industries Group led by Adi and Nadir, and Godrej Enterprises Group controlled by cousins Jamshyd and Smita Godrej.

Godrej Industries Group comprises five listed companies: Godrej Industries, Godrej Consumer Products (NSE: GODREJCP), Godrej Properties, Godrej Agrovet, and Astec Lifesciences (NSE: ASTEC).

Godrej Enterprises Group controls Godrej & Boyce and its affiliates. This arm holds the 3,400-acre land bank including prime property in Mumbai.

The family described the split as "an ownership realignment" to better align differing visions. Both groups continue to use the Godrej brand and share heritage.

Pirojsha played a key role in negotiating the family settlement. Since the split, he has emphasized faster decision-making and building a performance-driven culture.

The group is now focusing on six core sectors: FMCG, real estate, chemicals, agri-business, financial services, and real estate private equity.
