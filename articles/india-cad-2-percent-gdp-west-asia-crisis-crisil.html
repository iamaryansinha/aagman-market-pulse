India CAD May Hit 2% Of GDP If West Asia Crisis Prolongs: Crisil

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's current account deficit could widen to 2% of GDP under an adverse scenario of prolonged West Asia crisis, according to Crisil
  • 02/Base case estimate is 1.5% of GDP; risks include 23% YoY rise in crude prices and disruptions to remittances from the region
  • 03/GDP growth may slow to 6.8% from 7.1% baseline if energy price shocks persist; services surplus provides partial cushion

India's current account deficit (CAD) could widen to around 2 per cent of GDP in a prolonged West Asia crisis scenario. Crisil's report warns of rising import bills and weakening external inflows.

The base case estimate stands at 1.5 per cent of GDP. However, escalating geopolitical tensions could push the deficit higher.

A 23 per cent year-on-year rise in crude oil prices is expected to sharply increase the petroleum import bill. Petroleum already constitutes a substantial share of India's total imports.

Higher fertiliser prices and increased gas costs will further expand the import burden. This compounds pressure on the trade deficit.

Disruptions to exports to West Asia, coupled with elevated shipping and insurance costs, will weigh on outbound shipments. Softer global demand adds to the challenge.

Remittance inflows from West Asia face risks, which could adversely impact external receipts in the short term. The region accounts for a significant portion of India's remittances.

Services trade surplus is expected to provide some cushion to the external balance. However, it may not fully offset the widening trade gap.

India's GDP growth could moderate to 6.8 per cent from the baseline estimate of 7.1 per cent. Input cost pressures and reduced gas availability will impact manufacturing and construction.
