Wall Street Ends 5th Straight Losing Week, Worst Streak Since Iran War Began

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Wall Street finishes fifth consecutive losing week, marking the longest losing streak in nearly 4 years
  • 02/Brent crude surged 3.4% to $105.32/barrel, up from $70 pre-war, while US crude jumped 5.5% to $99.64
  • 03/Oil could hit $200/barrel if war continues through June, warns Macquarie; markets await Iranian diplomatic signals

Wall Street extended its losing streak to five consecutive weeks. It marks the longest weekly decline in nearly four years.

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq closed sharply lower on Friday. Investors fled risk assets amid escalating Middle East tensions.

Big Tech led the decline. Amazon dropped 4%, Meta fell 4%, and Nvidia shed 2.2%.

Consumer discretionary stocks took heavy blows. Norwegian Cruise Line plunged 6.9%, Starbucks lost 4.8%, and Chipotle declined 4.1%.

Oil prices surged again. Brent crude climbed 3.4% to $105.32 per barrel, up from $70 before the Iran conflict began.

US crude oil rose 5.5% to $99.64 per barrel. The spike reflects growing supply disruption fears.

The 10-year Treasury yield jumped to 4.43%. It stood at 3.97% before the war started.

"Diplomatic dissonance dismayed investors," said Doug Beath, equity strategist at Wells Fargo.

Macquarie strategists warned oil could reach $200 per barrel. This scenario assumes the war continues through June.

Jim Bianco of Bianco Research noted that only Iranian statements on talks will move markets meaningfully.

Investors now watch Tehran closely. Any diplomatic breakthrough could spark a sharp relief rally.
