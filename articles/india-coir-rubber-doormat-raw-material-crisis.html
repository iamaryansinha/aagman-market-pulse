India Coir Doormat Crisis: Raw Material Shortages Threaten $400M Export Sector Amid Global Disruptions

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's coir and rubber doormat exporters face acute shortages of PVC resin, plasticisers, and synthetic rubber chemicals due to global supply chain disruptions
  • 02/The US accounts for over 60% of India's coir and rubber product exports; new 50% tariff from August 2025 further strains the sector
  • 03/FICEA has urged government intervention including duty relief on imported raw materials and financial assistance to offset rising costs

India's coir and rubber doormat exporters are facing a severe raw material crisis. Ongoing geopolitical tensions and war-related disruptions have triggered supply chain bottlenecks.

The Federation of Indian Coir Exporters Association (FICEA) has sought urgent government intervention. Roby Francis, Chairman, and Sajan B Nair, Secretary General issued a joint statement highlighting unprecedented challenges.

Manufacturers of PVC-backed coir mats and rubber doormats report acute shortages. Essential materials include PVC resin, plasticisers, synthetic rubber chemicals and critical additives.

Most raw materials are imported or tied to global petrochemical supply chains. Availability has declined sharply in recent months. Production schedules face significant disruptions.

Shipments have been delayed due to dwindling inventories. Export contracts were signed months in advance at fixed prices. Manufacturers cannot pass on rising input costs.

Profit margins have been severely eroded across the sector. Inconsistent availability of raw materials continues to plague production schedules. Many exporters struggle to meet commitments.

The US accounts for over 60% of exports in this segment. Major retail chains in the US and Europe have placed large-volume orders. Fulfillment of these orders is now at risk.

A 50% tariff on exports to the US takes effect from August 2025. This compounds the existing raw material crisis. American buyers have already scaled back imports.

Exporters have been forced to offer steep discounts on continuing orders. Profitability has taken a double hit from both input costs and reduced pricing power.

Natural rubber prices briefly surpassed US$2/kg in Q1 2025, an eight-year high. Prices fell 14% in April and May but remain volatile. Global output rose 4% led by Thailand, Indonesia, and Côte d'Ivoire.

Electric vehicles consume 30% more rubber than traditional vehicles. China's EV sector absorbed an additional 340,000 metric tons in H1 2025. This demand surge has tightened global supply.

India's coir exports grew 46.5% from January-April 2025. Export value surged from $140 million in 2024 to $206 million in 2025. Volume increased by 5.9% to 515,000 metric tons.

China remains the largest importer of Indian coir products. Volume reached 215,221 metric tons during January-April 2025. Netherlands and USA followed with 47,571 and 43,176 metric tons respectively.

FICEA has requested temporary duty relief on imported raw materials. The association also seeks financial assistance to offset rising costs. Streamlined import logistics would help ease supply constraints.

Boosting domestic production of critical inputs is another priority. With no immediate resolution to geopolitical tensions, prolonged disruption appears likely. Order cancellations and loss of international clients threaten the sector.

India's position as a leading global supplier of coir and rubber mats faces lasting damage. The industry employs thousands of workers in manufacturing clusters. Livelihoods are at stake if the crisis persists.
