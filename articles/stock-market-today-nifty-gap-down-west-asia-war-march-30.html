Stock Market Today: Nifty Set For 200-Point Gap-Down As West Asia War Escalates

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian equities poised for sharp decline as Gift Nifty at 22,560 indicates 200-point gap-down opening for Nifty 50
  • 02/Escalating war in West Asia region drives risk-off sentiment across global markets
  • 03/Watch support levels at 22,400 and 22,300; volatility expected to remain elevated

Indian stock markets are set for a bruising opening on Monday, March 30, 2026. The Gift Nifty trading at 22,560 signals a gap-down of approximately 200 points.

The sharp decline comes as the war in West Asia continues to escalate. Geopolitical tensions have triggered a risk-off sentiment among global investors.

Benchmark indices Sensex and Nifty 50 are expected to open deep in the red. Traders are pricing in significant downside pressure at the opening bell.

The 200-point drop indicated by Gift Nifty represents a fall of nearly 0.9%. This follows a volatile week driven by ongoing Middle East concerns.

Foreign institutional investors have been net sellers in recent sessions. The conflict escalation may accelerate capital flight from emerging markets.

Key support levels to watch are at 22,400 and 22,300. A breach below these could trigger further selling pressure in the near term.

Oil prices may spike higher on supply disruption fears. This poses additional risks for oil-importing economies like India.

Defensive sectors such as FMCG and pharmaceuticals may see relative outperformance. Banking and financial stocks could face the brunt of selling.
