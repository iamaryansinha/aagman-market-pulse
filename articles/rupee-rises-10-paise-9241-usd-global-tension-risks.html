Rupee Rises 10 Paise To 92.41/USD Amid Global Tension Risks

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rupee gains 10 paise to 92.41 against US dollar in early trade on April 10, 2026
  • 02/High volatility expected as RBI's $100 million overnight position curbs deadline closes today
  • 03/Experts warn limited upside ahead; USD/INR likely to drift toward 93.50-94.00 levels

The Indian rupee appreciated 10 paise to 92.41 against the US dollar in early trade on Friday. The domestic currency gained ground from its opening of 92.58 in the interbank foreign exchange market.

On Thursday, the rupee had settled with a marginal gain of 3 paise at 92.51 per dollar. The currency has shown resilience despite mounting global headwinds.

Forex traders are bracing for high volatility intra-day. The deadline for RBI's instructions to banks to curb overnight positions to $100 million closes today.

An estimated 80-85 per cent of these positions have already been unwound. This means the supportive flow that held the rupee steady is beginning to thin.

"With most of the NOP-related support now fading and global uncertainties still elevated, the scope for further strength seems limited," said Amit Pabari, Managing Director at CR Forex Advisors.

Pabari expects USD/INR to find a base in the 92.20-92.50 zone. A gradual move higher toward 93.50-94.00 levels appears likely in the near term.

The dollar index was trading higher by 0.07 per cent at 98.69. Safe-haven demand has moderated after the ceasefire, though it remains fragile.

Brent crude was up 0.51 per cent at $96.44 per barrel. Ongoing uncertainty over the Strait of Hormuz is keeping oil prices elevated.

The World Bank has flagged that India's FY27 growth target of 6.6 per cent faces risks from rising global tensions, especially the US-Iran conflict.

Foreign Institutional Investors offloaded equities worth ₹1,711.19 crore on Thursday. Domestic equity markets rebounded in early trade with Sensex jumping 630 points.
