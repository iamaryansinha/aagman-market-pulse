India-US LPG Deal 2026: 2.2 MMTPA Import Pact Amid West Asia Conflict

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India signs historic 2.2 MMTPA LPG import deal with US for 2026, replacing Saudi Contract Price with Mont Belvieu benchmark
  • 02/90% of India's LPG imports traditionally come from West Asia via Strait of Hormuz, now facing conflict disruption
  • 03/Deal covers 10% of annual demand as US NGL exports to India surged 101% to 139,000 b/d in 2025

Indian oil PSUs have finalized a one-year contract to import 2.2 million tonnes of LPG from the US Gulf Coast in 2026. The deal represents India's first structured US LPG import contract.

Petroleum Minister Hardeep Singh Puri announced the agreement on November 17, 2025. The contract covers approximately 10% of India's annual LPG import requirement.

The deal is benchmarked to Mont Belvieu pricing. This marks a shift from the long-used Saudi Contract Price benchmark for Indian LPG imports.

The volumes will be sourced from Chevron, Phillips 66, and TotalEnergies Trading. IndianOil, BPCL, and HPCL negotiated the contract after visiting US producers in July 2025.

The move comes as West Asia conflict threatens India's primary LPG supply routes. About 85-90% of India's LPG imports transit through the Strait of Hormuz.

Saudi Aramco declared force majeure on March 2026 loadings from its Juaymah NGL facility. The facility supplies around 15% of India's LPG imports.

US NGL exports to India have surged from 2,000 b/d in 2016 to 139,000 b/d in 2025. This represents a 101% jump year-on-year.

India imports approximately 60% of its LPG demand. Domestic LPG connections have grown from 149 million in 2015 to over 331 million by January 2026.

Monthly LPG demand stands at 3 million tonnes. Total storage capacity covers roughly 22 days of supply.

Analysts expect the US to remain a stable supplier to Asian markets through mid-2026. The deal strengthens Indo-US energy ties amid ongoing trade agreement discussions.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE INDIA-US LPG DEAL ANNOUNCED IN NOVEMBER 2025?
INDIAN PSU OIL MARKETING COMPANIES HAVE SIGNED A ONE-YEAR CONTRACT TO IMPORT 2.2 MILLION TONNES PER ANNUM (MTPA) OF LPG FROM THE US GULF COAST FOR 2026. THIS IS INDIA'S FIRST STRUCTURED LONG-TERM LPG IMPORT CONTRACT FROM THE UNITED STATES.
WHY IS INDIA DIVERSIFYING LPG IMPORTS AWAY FROM WEST ASIA?
THE ONGOING CONFLICT IN WEST ASIA AND THREATS TO THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ HAVE DISRUPTED SUPPLY ROUTES THAT TRADITIONALLY CARRY 85-90% OF INDIA'S LPG IMPORTS. SAUDI ARAMCO DECLARED FORCE MAJEURE AT ITS JUAYMAH NGL FACILITY IN MARCH 2026, SUPPLYING 15% OF INDIA'S LPG.
WHAT IS THE PRICING BENCHMARK FOR THE US LPG DEAL?
THE CONTRACT USES MONT BELVIEU PRICING AS THE BENCHMARK, MARKING A SHIFT FROM THE SAUDI CONTRACT PRICE THAT HAS TRADITIONALLY PRICED INDIAN LPG IMPORTS. THIS PROVIDES PRICE TRANSPARENCY AND DIVERSIFICATION.
WHICH COMPANIES ARE INVOLVED IN THE US LPG IMPORT DEAL?
THE SUPPLIERS ARE CHEVRON, PHILLIPS 66, AND TOTALENERGIES TRADING. THE INDIAN BUYERS ARE INDIANOIL, BPCL, AND HPCL WHO NEGOTIATED THE CONTRACT AFTER VISITING US PRODUCERS IN JULY 2025.
HOW MUCH OF INDIA'S LPG DEMAND IS MET THROUGH IMPORTS?
INDIA IMPORTS APPROXIMATELY 60% OF ITS LPG DEMAND, TOTALING ABOUT 21 MILLION TONNES ANNUALLY. WITH 331 MILLION ACTIVE LPG CONNECTIONS, MONTHLY DEMAND STANDS AT 3 MILLION TONNES AGAINST STORAGE CAPACITY OF 1.93 MILLION MT (22 DAYS COVER).
