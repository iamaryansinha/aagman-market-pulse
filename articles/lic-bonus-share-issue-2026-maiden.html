LIC Bonus Share Issue 2026: Board to Consider Maiden Bonus Issue on April 13

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/LIC Board meets April 13, 2026 to consider maiden bonus share issue; announcement expected post market hours
  • 02/21 lakh retail investors holding 1.52% stake to benefit; Q3 net profit surged 17% YoY to ₹12,958 crore
  • 03/AUM grew 8% to ₹59.16 lakh crore; IRDAI designated LIC as Domestic Systemically Important Insurer

The Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) (NSE: LIC) board meets on April 13 to consider a proposal for bonus shares. If approved, this would mark the insurance giant's first-ever bonus issue since its 2022 listing.

The announcement is expected after market hours on Monday. The proposal will benefit approximately 21 lakh retail investors who collectively hold a 1.52% stake in the company.

LIC reported strong Q3 FY26 results with standalone net profit rising 17% YoY to ₹12,958 crore. Net premium income grew 17.5% to ₹1,25,613 crore.

Income from investments increased 14% to ₹1,07,608 crore during the quarter. Assets Under Management expanded 8% to ₹59,16,680 crore as of December 31, 2025.

For the full fiscal year 2025, LIC posted net profit of ₹48,151 crore, up 18% from ₹40,676 crore in FY24. The insurer maintains dominant market leadership despite private sector competition.

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India recently designated LIC as a Domestic Systemically Important Insurer (D-SII). This classification underscores LIC's critical role in India's financial system.

LIC is also exploring monetization of its real estate portfolio, estimated at approximately ₹45,000 crore. The company plans to increase rental income and explore REIT structures.

JM Financial retained its Buy rating on LIC with a target price of ₹1,111. The brokerage noted LIC outperformed private insurers with 23% retail weighted premium growth in February 2026.
