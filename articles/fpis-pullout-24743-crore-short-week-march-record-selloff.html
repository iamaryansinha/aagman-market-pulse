FPIs Pull Out ₹24,743 Crore in Short Week as March Records Historic Sell-Off

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/FPIs withdrew ₹24,743.74 crore in shortened trading week with only three sessions — March 30, April 1, and April 2
  • 02/March 2026 recorded historic monthly outflows of ₹1.25 lakh crore, with equity selloff at ₹1.17 lakh crore — biggest ever
  • 03/Global headwinds including crude above $100, rupee depreciation, and geopolitical tensions continue to trigger sustained foreign selling

Foreign Portfolio Investors pulled out ₹24,743.74 crore from Indian markets in the truncated week ending April 3, 2026. The week comprised just three trading sessions due to market holidays on March 31 and April 3.

Equities absorbed the heaviest selling at ₹23,801.94 crore through exchanges and primary markets. The debt segment saw mixed flows with FAR route posting outflows of ₹4,309.70 crore.

The weekly outflows follow a record monthly sell-off in March 2026. FPIs recorded total net outflows of ₹1,25,736.40 crore across all asset classes during the month.

March equity outflows hit ₹1,17,774.65 crore — the steepest monthly equity selloff by foreign investors in Indian market history. Dr VK Vijayakumar of Geojit confirmed this marks the biggest ever monthly FPI selling.

Calendar year 2026 has seen cumulative FPI outflows of ₹1,17,172.20 crore. Equity outflows alone have reached ₹1,31,121.53 crore this year.

"Continuation of the war, crude spiking above $100, rupee decline and dollar appreciation triggered this record selling," said Dr Vijayakumar. The rupee has depreciated 4 per cent since the war began.

Himanshu Srivastava of Morningstar noted elevated US bond yields improved relative attractiveness of fixed-income assets. This prompted global investors to rebalance away from equities.

Domestic Institutional Investors have provided a buffer. DIIs bought a record ₹1.28 lakh crore corresponding to FPI outflows of ₹1.11 lakh crore in March. The rupee stood at ₹94.65 to the dollar on April 2.
