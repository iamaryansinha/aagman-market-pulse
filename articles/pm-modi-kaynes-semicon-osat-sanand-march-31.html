PM Modi to Inaugurate Kaynes Semicon OSAT Facility at Sanand on March 31

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate Kaynes Semicon's OSAT facility at Sanand, Gujarat on March 31, 2026
  • 02/The ₹3,300-crore facility approved under India Semiconductor Mission will produce approximately 60 lakh chips per day
  • 03/Kaynes becomes the second OSAT unit after Micron to enter production, marking Sanand's emergence as India's semiconductor hub

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate Kaynes Semicon's outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) facility at Sanand on March 31, 2026. The event will also be attended by Gujarat Chief Minister and Union Ministers including Ashwini Vaishnaw.

The ₹3,300-crore facility is a subsidiary of Kaynes Technology India Limited (NSE: KAYNES). It represents India's first integrated semiconductor backend platform with advanced packaging, assembly, and testing capabilities.

The Union Cabinet approved the project in September 2024 under the India Semiconductor Mission. Once operational, the facility is estimated to produce around 60 lakh chips per day.

The inauguration marks the third semiconductor-related development in Sanand within months. Micron Technology commenced commercial production in February 2026, while CG Semi has also inaugurated its facility in the region.

Sanand is transitioning from an automobile manufacturing base into a semiconductor hub. Gujarat now accounts for four of the 10 major projects approved under the Centre's semiconductor programme.

Raghu Panicker, CEO of Kaynes Semicon, called the inauguration a "defining milestone" for India's technological journey. The facility will serve sectors ranging from automotive and telecommunications to AI systems and defense.

The OSAT plant will unveil its first indigenously assembled semiconductor product during the inauguration. Mass production is scheduled to begin in 2026, supporting India's push for digital sovereignty in chip manufacturing.

OSAT STANDS FOR OUTSOURCED SEMICONDUCTOR ASSEMBLY AND TEST. IT IS AN ESSENTIAL PART OF THE SEMICONDUCTOR VALUE CHAIN INVOLVING TESTING AND PACKAGING OF CHIPS BEFORE SHIPMENT TO END MARKETS.

THE KAYNES FACILITY IS INDIA'S FIRST INTEGRATED SEMICONDUCTOR BACKEND PLATFORM AND THE SECOND OSAT UNIT AFTER MICRON TO ENTER PRODUCTION UNDER THE INDIA SEMICONDUCTOR MISSION, STRENGTHENING DOMESTIC PACKAGING CAPABILITIES.
