Rupee Steadies Near 93 Per Dollar as RBI Unwinding Offsets Iran War Risks

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rupee opens flat-to-higher near 93 per dollar after settling at 93.06 on Monday, supported by RBI-led position unwinding
  • 02/Currency finds near-term equilibrium after swinging 3 rupees in prior sessions; Brent crude climbs to $111 on war risks
  • 03/Trump's Tuesday 8 p.m. EDT deadline for Iran deal looms; escalation could push oil higher and pressure rupee

The Indian rupee opened marginally higher-to-flat against the US dollar on Tuesday. The currency is trading near the 93-per-dollar level after settling at 93.06 on Monday.

Monday saw relatively calmer trading compared to recent volatility. The rupee was confined to a roughly 30-paise band, following three sessions of heightened swings.

The price action included one session where the rupee swung in a 3-rupee range. Bankers now believe the currency has found near-term equilibrium around 93 per dollar.

The recovery comes after extreme uncertainty over RBI steps to support the currency. The rupee had fallen to the 95 level before managing a "respectable" recovery.

Position unwinding by banks through the week should help the rupee hold near 93, according to a currency trader at a private sector bank. However, Iran war-related risk premiums may limit further gains.

US President Donald Trump has warned Iran could be "taken out" if it misses his Tuesday night deadline. The ultimatum expires at 8 p.m. EDT (midnight GMT) for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.

Brent crude for June delivery climbed to $111 on Tuesday. The Strait of Hormuz closure has cut off nearly 21% of global seaborne oil supply.

MUFG Bank maintains that the path towards peace is "narrow and unlikely" given the wide gap in expectations among parties. Any escalation could push oil prices further higher and pressure the rupee.
