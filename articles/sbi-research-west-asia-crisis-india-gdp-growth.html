SBI Research Warns West Asia Crisis May Slow India GDP Growth by 25 Bps

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SBI Research warns prolonged West Asia conflict could slow India's GDP growth by 20-25 basis points in FY27 due to higher oil prices
  • 02/Every $10 per barrel oil price increase may widen current account deficit by 36 bps and push inflation up by 35-40 bps
  • 03/In worst-case scenario with oil at $130/bbl, India's GDP growth could plummet to 6% from assumed 7% baseline

SBI Research has flagged early signs of economic moderation due to the escalating West Asia crisis. The Israel-Iran conflict poses significant risks to India's growth trajectory through oil price shocks and remittance disruptions.

India imports nearly 90% of its crude oil requirements. About 2 million barrels per day transit through the Strait of Hormuz, which the US Energy Information Administration calls the world's most important oil transit chokepoint.

SBI economists estimate that for every $10 per barrel increase in crude oil prices, India's current account deficit may widen by 36 basis points in FY27. The same price rise could push inflation higher by 35-40 basis points.

Higher oil prices may directly slow economic growth by 20-25 basis points. As oil prices rise from $90 to $130 per barrel, the combined effect of wider CAD, higher inflation, and slower growth intensifies.

In a worst-case scenario with oil touching $130 per barrel, SBI Research expects GDP growth to drop to 6%. This assumes a baseline growth estimate of 7% for FY27.

GCC countries account for approximately 38% of India's total remittances. Personal remittances reached $138 billion in FY25, up 15% from $119 billion in FY24.

SBI economists see a 5% chance that higher oil prices could slow inward remittances. Banks and private sector firms have significant exposure to the affected region.

"In 2025 H1, oil flow through the strait averaged 20.9 million barrels per day, or about 26% of global petroleum liquids consumption," said Soumya Kanti Ghosh, Group Chief Economic Advisor at SBI.

India has strategically diversified oil imports to over 40 countries, with increased purchases from Russia since 2022. However, 40% of oil imports still pass through the Hormuz waterway.

Forward contracts for future oil delivery may get partial relief through a 30-day US reprieve window for Russian oil purchases. This could help offset some immediate price pressures.
