CRED Upgrades Kuvera Platform With New Wealth Features and Surplus Product

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CRED upgrades Kuvera platform with deeper analytics, behavioral insights, and portfolio recommendations for wealth creation
  • 02/New 'Surplus' product offers liquid fund investments from ₹1 lakh with ₹4 lakh withdrawal in 5 minutes
  • 03/Kuvera manages ₹33,000 crore across 3 lakh investors; CRED eyes cross-selling to 15 million user base

Fintech unicorn CRED is upgrading its wealth management platform Kuvera with enhanced analytics and a new liquid fund product. The move signals deeper ambitions in the investment space beyond its credit card roots.

The upgrade adds behavioral insights and portfolio recommendations to help users improve decision-making. CRED is targeting discipline, asset allocation, and performance as critical areas for long-term wealth creation.

A new feature called 'Surplus' deploys idle cash into curated liquid fund schemes. The product requires a minimum investment of ₹1 lakh and allows withdrawals of up to ₹4 lakh within five minutes.

The remaining balance is redeemable within 24 hours. CRED has partnered with DSP, ICICI Prudential, Aditya Birla Sun Life, and HDFC Asset Management for the fund basket.

The rollout will initially target Kuvera's roughly 2 lakh monthly active users. The platform manages nearly ₹33,000 crore in assets across about 3 lakh investors overall.

CRED acquired Kuvera in early 2024 in a cash-and-stock deal. Founded in 2017 by Gaurav Rastogi and Neelabh Sanyal, the platform has traditionally focused on mutual fund investing.

About 75 per cent of Kuvera users now also invest in stocks. Equity participation surged during the COVID-19 period as retail investor interest spiked.

The upgrade comes as CRED looks to deepen engagement across its 15 million-strong user base. Nearly 60 per cent of India's multi-card holders are active on the platform.

CRED reported a 16 per cent YoY rise in operating revenue to ₹2,735 crore in FY25. Losses narrowed by 51 per cent to ₹298 crore on higher user engagement.

The company recently received RBI approval to operate as a payment aggregator. This adds to its existing prepaid payment instrument licence as it diversifies beyond credit card payments.
