Govt to Raise Gas Supply to Fertiliser Units, Industries to 90% from April

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government to raise gas supply to fertiliser plants to approximately 90% of six-month average consumption from April 6, 2026, up from current 70-75% levels
  • 02/Move follows disruptions caused by West Asia conflict and Strait of Hormuz closure that forced rationing under Essential Commodities Act since March 2026
  • 03/Industrial and commercial sectors including CGD networks will also see gas supply enhancement by additional 10% from April 6

The government has announced a significant increase in natural gas supply to fertiliser plants, raising allocations to approximately 90% of their average consumption over the preceding six months, effective April 6, 2026. This marks a substantial improvement from the current supply levels of 70-75% that have been in place since March.

The decision comes amid ongoing disruptions to energy supplies caused by the West Asia conflict and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global energy trade. The government had earlier invoked the Essential Commodities Act to prioritise gas allocation, with households receiving 100% supplies for cooking gas and CNG transport, while fertiliser plants were restricted to 70% of their average consumption.

According to a statement from the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas, the increased supply to fertiliser units has been made possible by available inventory and scheduled LNG cargo arrivals. All industrial consumers including fertiliser plants have been advised to provide their additional requirements on a spot basis so that the same may be arranged by gas marketing companies.

The supply enhancement extends beyond the fertiliser sector. Gas supply to other industrial and commercial sectors, including supplies through City Gas Distribution networks, will be enhanced by a further 10% from April 6, 2026. Supplies to industrial and commercial consumers connected on the grid currently stand at 80% of their average consumption.

The crisis had forced several fertiliser companies to advance their annual maintenance schedules or temporarily shut operations due to non-availability of gas. India has 32 functional urea manufacturing plants, of which 31 use gas as feedstock. The daily urea production had dropped by 30,000-35,000 tonnes from normal levels of about 80,000 tonnes per day during the pre-crisis period.

With the kharif sowing season approaching in June, the government has been taking proactive measures to ensure adequate fertiliser availability. The country currently holds over 18 million tonnes of fertiliser stocks against 14.7 million tonnes in the year-ago period, including 6.2 million tonnes of urea.

The government has also implemented several rationalisation measures including enhancing refinery production, increasing booking intervals for LPG, and prioritising sectors for supply. Additionally, alternate fuel options like kerosene and coal have been offered to ease pressure on LPG demand.

The Strait of Hormuz closure has significantly impacted India's energy security, as the country imports roughly 40-42% of its natural gas from Qatar. The government has been in discussions with alternative suppliers including the US, Australia, Canada, Russia and Algeria to diversify sourcing and mitigate supply risks.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE CURRENT GAS SUPPLY LEVEL TO FERTILISER PLANTS?
CURRENTLY, FERTILISER PLANTS ARE RECEIVING AROUND 70-75% OF THEIR SIX-MONTH AVERAGE CONSUMPTION. FROM APRIL 6, 2026, THIS WILL BE INCREASED TO APPROXIMATELY 90%.
WHY WAS GAS SUPPLY TO FERTILISER PLANTS REDUCED EARLIER?
THE REDUCTION WAS NECESSITATED BY DISRUPTIONS CAUSED BY THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT AND CLOSURE OF THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ, WHICH IS A CRITICAL SHIPPING ROUTE FOR LNG SUPPLIES TO INDIA.
HOW MANY UREA PLANTS DOES INDIA HAVE?
INDIA HAS 32 FUNCTIONAL UREA MANUFACTURING PLANTS, OF WHICH 31 USE NATURAL GAS AS FEEDSTOCK WHILE ONE USES COAL GASIFICATION.
WHAT OTHER SECTORS WILL BENEFIT FROM INCREASED GAS SUPPLY?
INDUSTRIAL AND COMMERCIAL SECTORS, INCLUDING SUPPLIES THROUGH CITY GAS DISTRIBUTION NETWORKS, WILL SEE AN ADDITIONAL 10% ENHANCEMENT IN GAS SUPPLY FROM APRIL 6.
WHAT MEASURES HAS THE GOVERNMENT TAKEN TO ENSURE FERTILISER AVAILABILITY FOR KHARIF SEASON?
THE GOVERNMENT HAS PRIORITISED GAS FOR FERTILISER PLANTS, BUILT UP STOCK RESERVES OF OVER 18 MILLION TONNES, AND IS SOURCING LNG FROM ALTERNATIVE SUPPLIERS TO ENSURE ADEQUATE AVAILABILITY FOR THE JUNE SOWING SEASON.
