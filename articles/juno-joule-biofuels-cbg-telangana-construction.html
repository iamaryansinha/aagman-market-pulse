Juno Joule Bio Fuels Begins ₹700 Crore CBG Plant Construction in Telangana

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Juno Joule Bio Fuels commenced construction of its flagship CBG project at Narmetta, Siddipet district, with ground-breaking on Sunday
  • 02/The cluster comprises 10 CBG plants (10 TPD each) with total investment of ₹700 crore; Phase 1 includes 3 plants with ₹210 crore outlay
  • 03/Production expected to begin by May 2027; entire cluster completion targeted for 2029-2030 with offtake tie-ups from GAIL and Bhagyanagar Gas

Juno Joule Bio Fuels has commenced construction of its flagship Compressed Bio-Gas (CBG) project. The ground-breaking ceremony took place on Sunday at Narmetta, Siddipet district, Telangana.

The project is part of a larger cluster development comprising 10 CBG plants. Each plant will have a capacity of 10 tonnes per day (TPD), aggregating to 100 TPD total production capacity.

The total planned investment for the entire cluster is ₹700 crore. The first phase, being launched at Narmetta, comprises three CBG units with an investment of ₹210 crore.

Production is expected to begin by May 2027. The project will be implemented in three phases over the next four to five years.

Phase 1 will have 3 plants, Phase 2 will add 3 more, and Phase 3 will complete the cluster with 4 plants. Full cluster completion is targeted for 2029-2030.

"Telangana is committed to building a future-ready, sustainable industrial ecosystem, and initiatives like this Compressed Bio-Gas project reflect the state's focus on clean energy, innovation, and rural development," said D Sridhar Babu, Telangana Minister for Industries.

"Our cluster-based approach is designed to deliver consistency at scale, unlock value from agricultural waste, and contribute meaningfully to India's energy security and rural economy," said Nagasharath Rayapati, CEO, Juno Joule Green Energy.

The project is being implemented under the Government of India's CBG-CGD Synchronisation Scheme. This scheme aims to integrate compressed bio-gas into the City Gas Distribution (CGD) network.

The project has secured structured offtake arrangements with GAIL (India) Limited and Bhagyanagar Gas Limited (BGL). This ensures integration into the CGD network and enables supply for mobility and industrial applications in the Hyderabad region.
