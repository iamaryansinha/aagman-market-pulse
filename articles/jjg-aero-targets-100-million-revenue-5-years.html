JJG Aero Targets $100 Million Revenue in 5 Years, Eyes Mid-30s CAGR Growth

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/JJG Aero targets $100 million revenue over next 5 years, expects to sustain CAGR in mid-30s from current ₹155 crore in FY26
  • 02/Aerospace division grew 8x in last 6 years from under ₹20 crore in FY21; raised $30 million Series B in January 2026
  • 03/Company building 200,000 sq ft facility in North Bengaluru; revenue split 50:50 between existing and new clients

Bengaluru-based aerospace components maker JJG Aero has set an ambitious revenue target. The company aims to reach $100 million in revenue over the next five years.

The company raised $30 million in Series B funding in January 2026. Norwest Venture Partners led the round to support a new 200,000 sq ft facility in North Bengaluru.

JJG Aero's aerospace division has grown eight-fold in the last six years. Revenue increased from under ₹20 crore in FY21 to approximately ₹155 crore in FY26.

Anuj Jhunjhunwala, CEO of JJG Machining, outlined the growth trajectory. "We expect to maintain a CAGR in the mid-30s from here," he stated.

The company is capitalising on shifting global supply chains and India's emergence as an alternative manufacturing base. India's share in global aerospace supply chain has doubled to under 2% over the past decade.

JJG Aero focuses on deepening relationships with existing clients rather than aggressive new customer acquisition. The revenue split is currently 50:50 between existing and new clients.

Jhunjhunwala noted that moving up the value chain will take time. "I don't see India becoming a build-to-spec nation in commercial aerospace for at least the next 8-10 years," he said.
