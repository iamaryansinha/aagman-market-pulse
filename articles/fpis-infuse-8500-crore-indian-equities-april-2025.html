FPIs Infuse ₹8,500 Crore Into Indian Equities in Holiday-Shortened Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Foreign Portfolio Investors infused ₹8,472 crore into Indian equities during the week ending April 18, 2025, according to NSDL data.
  • 02/The inflows came despite a shorter trading week with markets closed for Ambedkar Jayanti and Good Friday.
  • 03/The buying marks a turnaround from heavy selling earlier in April, though year-to-date outflows remain at ₹1.4 lakh crore.

Foreign Portfolio Investors have made a strong comeback to Indian equity markets. They injected ₹8,472 crore during the week ending April 18, 2025, according to National Securities Depository Limited data.

The trading week was shortened due to holidays. Markets remained closed on Monday for Ambedkar Jayanti and Friday for Good Friday. Despite only three trading sessions, foreign investors showed renewed appetite for Indian stocks.

The activity included a withdrawal of ₹2,352 crore on April 15. This was followed by significant inflows of ₹10,824 crore over the next two days. The sharp reversal signals improving sentiment among global investors.

The buying comes after a brutal start to April. FPIs had pulled out ₹23,103 crore in the first half of the month. Total outflows since January 2025 now stand at ₹1.4 lakh crore.

Two key factors drove the rebound. The US Dollar Index declined to around 100, making emerging markets more attractive. India's economy is forecast to grow at 6% in FY26, outpacing both the US and China.

"The positive fallout of the US tariff scenario is a declining dollar and strengthening of emerging market currencies like the rupee," said Aashish P Sommaiyaa, Executive Director & CEO at WhiteOak Capital. "This makes it easier for FPIs to allocate money into markets like India."

The market focus is shifting toward domestic consumption sectors. These include financial services, telecom, aviation, cement, select auto stocks, and healthcare.

Analysts remain cautious about whether this marks a sustained reversal. VK Vijayakumar, Chief Investment Strategist at Geojit Financial Services, noted that the sustainability of inflows depends on global stability and India's relative valuation comfort.

The April inflows follow a volatile March 2025 when FPIs sold heavily amid trade deal concerns. February 2025 had seen a brief recovery with ₹22,615 crore in inflows, the highest in 17 months.

The Indian rupee has shown resilience against the dollar. This has helped improve returns for foreign investors holding Indian equities. The currency stability is attracting fresh allocations from global funds.

Year-to-date, FPI ownership in NSE-listed companies has dropped to 16.9%, the lowest in over 15 years. India is now the second-largest underweight in global emerging market portfolios, according to HSBC data.

DIIs have absorbed much of the FPI selling. This domestic support has prevented sharper market corrections. The trend highlights India's reducing dependence on foreign capital.
