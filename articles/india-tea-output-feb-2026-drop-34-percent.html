India's Tea Production Plunges 34% in February on Adverse Weather

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's tea production crashed 34.45% to 16.65 million kg in February 2026
  • 02/Assam output collapsed 65%; West Bengal dropped 61.30%
  • 03/North India bore the brunt with 61.87% decline; South India fared better at 14.23%

India's tea output contracted sharply in February 2026, dropping 34.45% year-on-year to 16.65 million kg, according to provisional figures released by the Tea Board.

The steep fall comes on the back of adverse weather conditions and the seasonal dormancy period when North Indian tea bushes remain closed for winter holidays.

North India bore the brunt of the decline. The region's total production plummeted 61.87% to 4.11 million kg, down from 10.78 million kg in February 2025.

Assam, the country's largest tea producing state, saw output collapse 65% to just 0.41 million kg compared to 1.17 million kg in the corresponding period last year.

West Bengal, the second-largest producer, recorded a 61.30% decline with output falling to 3.63 million kg from 9.38 million kg.

South India showed more resilience but still registered a decline. Total production fell 14.23% to 12.54 million kg from 14.62 million kg in February last year.

Tamil Nadu recorded an 18.14% drop with output at 8.44 million kg. Kerala saw a modest 4.23% decline to 3.85 million kg. Karnataka's production fell 13.79% to 0.25 million kg.

By category, CTC tea accounted for the bulk of February production at 13.65 million kg. Orthodox tea output stood at 2.50 million kg, while green tea production was 0.50 million kg.

The sharp contraction in India's tea output could tighten global supply at a time when the beverage remains one of the country's key agricultural exports.
