CBI Raids Reliance Telecom Offices in ₹114.98 Crore SBI Bank Fraud Case

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CBI registered case against Reliance Telecom Ltd and former directors Satish Seth and Gautam Doshi for alleged ₹114.98 crore bank fraud
  • 02/State Bank of India filed complaint alleging fraud linked to consortium loan of ₹735 crore sanctioned by 11 banks
  • 03/Searches conducted at multiple Mumbai locations including company office and directors' residences; documents seized

The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) has registered a case against Reliance Telecom Ltd. The case names former directors Satish Seth and Gautam B. Doshi among the accused.

The alleged fraud involves ₹114.98 crore and is linked to a complaint filed by State Bank of India (SBI). The CBI has invoked charges of criminal conspiracy and cheating under the Indian Penal Code.

Provisions of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 have also been applied. These relate to criminal misconduct and abuse of official position by unknown public servants.

SBI was part of a consortium of 11 banks that sanctioned a term loan facility of ₹735 crore to Reliance Telecom. The complaint alleges fraudulent actions linked to this loan caused financial losses to the bank.

The CBI conducted searches on March 26, 2026 at multiple locations in Mumbai. Raids covered the residential premises of Seth and Doshi, along with the registered office of Reliance Telecom Ltd.

Officials confirmed that several documents related to loan transactions and financial dealings were recovered during the searches. The agency is examining banking records, internal approvals, and correspondence linked to the loan disbursement.

The investigation comes amid broader scrutiny of companies linked to the Reliance Group. Earlier this month, the CBI questioned Reliance Group Chairperson Anil Ambani for nearly eight hours in a separate case.

That case involves an alleged ₹2,929 crore fraud linked to Reliance Communications (RCom). Ambani has been asked to join the probe again as investigations continue.
