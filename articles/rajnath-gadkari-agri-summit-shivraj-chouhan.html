Rajnath Singh, Gadkari To Join 3-Day Agri Summit At Shivraj Chouhan's Turf

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Rajnath Singh inaugurates and Nitin Gadkari closes a 3-day agricultural summit at Shivraj Singh Chouhan's Raisen residence from April 11-13
  • 02/Focus areas include ICAR technology transfer, climate-resilient seeds, fertilizer reforms via Farmers' IDs database with 9.29 crore registrations
  • 03/Event comes as 2.49 lakh hectares of Rabi crops face damage from unseasonal rains and hailstorms across 14 states

Defence Minister Rajnath Singh and Road Transport Minister Nitin Gadkari will join a three-day agricultural summit at Shivraj Singh Chouhan's residence in Raisen, Madhya Pradesh. The event runs from April 11 to 13, 2026.

Rajnath Singh will inaugurate the summit on Saturday. Gadkari will attend the closing session on Monday. The event signals political consolidation of Chouhan's base in his Vidisha constituency.

The summit follows Chouhan's suspended Viksit Krishi Sankalp Yatra. It demonstrates continued BJP rural connectivity and focus on agricultural outreach.

Key focus areas include ICAR technology transfer initiatives. Scientists will present climate-resilient seed varieties to farmers ahead of the Kharif sowing season.

Fertilizer reforms are on the agenda. The Farmers' IDs database now has 9.29 crore registrations, enabling targeted subsidy delivery and grievance redressal.

Decentralized agricultural exhibitions will showcase innovations. State-specific agro-climate roadmaps will be discussed for region-appropriate farming strategies.

The timing is significant. Unseasonal rains and hailstorms have damaged 2.49 lakh hectares of Rabi crops across 14 states. Farmers need immediate support and guidance.

Chouhan's "Lab-to-Land" philosophy underpins the event. The former Chief Minister has a history of nationwide farmer outreach and direct engagement with agricultural communities.

The summit provides a platform for direct scientist-farmer interaction. Researchers can understand ground-level challenges while farmers access expert knowledge.

Grievance redressal mechanisms will be highlighted. Expectations remain high for government response to the current agricultural crisis.
