Reliance SEZ Export Duty Clarity Key Amid India's Fuel Tax Rejig

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India imposed export duties of ₹21.50/litre on diesel and ₹29.50/litre on ATF effective March 26, 2026, while petrol exports remain exempt
  • 02/Reliance's SEZ refinery accounted for 75% of diesel and 35% of jet fuel production in FY25; exemption status remains unclear
  • 03/Fiscal impact estimated at ₹80,000 crore annually without SEZ exemptions, widening to ₹1.5 lakh crore if SEZ-linked exemptions are retained

India imposed fresh export duties on refined fuels effective March 26, 2026. The government levied ₹21.50 per litre on diesel exports and ₹29.50 per litre on aviation turbine fuel (ATF). Petrol exports remain exempt from the new duties.

A critical uncertainty persists around Reliance Industries' SEZ refinery. The company operates two units at Jamnagar: a 33 million tonnes per year domestic facility and a 35.2 million tonnes only-for-exports SEZ unit.

In FY25, the SEZ refinery produced 75% of Reliance's diesel and 35% of its jet fuel. It remains unclear whether SEZ exports will qualify for exemption, as they did under the 2022 windfall tax regime.

UK-based Investec highlighted this ambiguity in a research note. If SEZ exports remain exempt, Reliance's refining margins would be largely insulated from the new levies. Conversely, inclusion under the export duty net would materially compress margins.

Citi Research estimates the export taxes are equivalent to $36 per barrel on diesel and $50 per barrel on jet fuel. However, strong product cracks of $65-70 per barrel continue to support export margins of $15-25 per barrel.

The duty changes accompany a ₹10 per litre cut in domestic excise duties. Petrol SAED was reduced to ₹3 per litre from ₹13, while diesel SAED was fully withdrawn to zero from ₹10.

Oil marketing companies (OMCs) will benefit from reduced marketing losses. Citi estimates integrated losses on petrol and diesel will fall from ₹35-40 per litre to ₹19-23 per litre.

OMCs' breakeven crude price rises from $65 per barrel to $80 per barrel. This provides meaningful relief to public sector retailers facing under-recoveries amid elevated global crude prices.

The fiscal cost is substantial. Investec estimates a net revenue impact of ₹80,000 crore annually without exemptions. This widens to roughly ₹1.5 lakh crore if SEZ-linked exemptions are retained.

Reliance Industries shares tumbled 4.1% on March 27, 2026. The stock hit an intraday low of ₹1,353.20, erasing approximately ₹80,000 crore in market capitalisation.

Clarity on SEZ treatment is awaited by markets. This resolution will be critical in determining the effective impact on Reliance's refining margins and the government's revenue trajectory.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT EXPORT DUTIES HAS INDIA IMPOSED ON REFINED FUELS?
EFFECTIVE MARCH 26, 2026, INDIA HAS IMPOSED EXPORT DUTIES OF ₹21.50 PER LITRE ON DIESEL AND ₹29.50 PER LITRE ON AVIATION TURBINE FUEL (ATF). PETROL EXPORTS REMAIN EXEMPT FROM THE NEW LEVIES.
WHY IS RELIANCE'S SEZ REFINERY STATUS IMPORTANT?
RELIANCE'S SEZ REFINERY AT JAMNAGAR ACCOUNTED FOR 75% OF DIESEL AND 35% OF JET FUEL PRODUCTION IN FY25. IF SEZ EXPORTS REMAIN EXEMPT AS UNDER THE 2022 REGIME, RELIANCE'S REFINING MARGINS WOULD BE LARGELY INSULATED. IF INCLUDED, MARGINS WOULD FACE MATERIAL COMPRESSION.
WHAT IS THE FISCAL IMPACT OF THESE DUTY CHANGES?
INVESTEC ESTIMATES THE NET REVENUE IMPACT AT ₹80,000 CRORE ANNUALLY WITHOUT SEZ EXEMPTIONS. THIS WIDENS TO APPROXIMATELY ₹1.5 LAKH CRORE IF SEZ-LINKED EXEMPTIONS ARE RETAINED.
HOW DO THE DOMESTIC EXCISE CUTS AFFECT OIL MARKETING COMPANIES?
THE ₹10 PER LITRE CUT IN SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY REDUCES OMC MARKETING LOSSES FROM ₹35-40 PER LITRE TO ₹19-23 PER LITRE. THE BREAKEVEN CRUDE PRICE FOR OMCs RISES FROM $65 TO $80 PER BARREL.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT EXPORT MARGIN FOR REFINERS DESPITE THE NEW DUTIES?
DESPITE EXPORT DUTIES EQUIVALENT TO $36 PER BARREL ON DIESEL AND $50 ON JET FUEL, REFINERS CONTINUE TO EARN HEALTHY EXPORT MARGINS OF $15-25 PER BARREL. THIS IS SUPPORTED BY STRONG DIESEL AND ATF CRACKS AT $65-70 PER BARREL.
