IGX Gas Trade Hits Record 76.8 Million MMBtu in FY26, Up 28% YoY

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IGX achieved record annual gas trading volume of 76.8 million MMBtu in FY26, marking 28% growth year-on-year
  • 02/47% of traded volumes were free market gas while 53% was domestic HPHT gas at ceiling price
  • 03/March volumes declined 11% MoM due to Middle East supply disruptions; 93% of trades were domestic priority sector gas

The Indian Gas Exchange (IGX) has posted its highest-ever annual trading volume for FY26. The exchange recorded 76.8 million Metric Million British thermal units (MMBtu) traded during the financial year.

This marks a robust 28 per cent increase compared to FY25. A total of 1,924 trades were executed across various contract types during the year.

The trading mix was nearly evenly split between free market and domestic gas. Around 47 per cent of volumes comprised free market gas, while 53 per cent was domestic High Pressure High Temperature (HPHT) gas at ceiling price.

Monthly contracts dominated trading activity. These accounted for 64 per cent of total volume, followed by fortnightly contracts at 14 per cent. Day-ahead, weekly, and daily contracts each captured 5-6 per cent of volumes.

Among delivery points, Gadimoga emerged as the most active location. It handled 41 per cent of total trade, while Dahej contributed 22 per cent of volumes.

Exchange-linked deliveries stood at 76.3 million MMBtu, equivalent to 5.3 million standard cubic meters per day (MMSCMD). IGX has achieved a compound annual growth rate of 58.6 per cent from FY22 to FY26.

March 2026 saw a slowdown with 4.8 million MMBtu traded. This represents an 11 per cent decline month-on-month and 8 per cent drop year-on-year.

The decline was driven by reduced spot market supply amid Middle East disruptions. Additionally, the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas (MoPNG) notified allocation of gas to priority sectors.

The exchange's benchmark Gas Index (GIXI) for March 2026 stood at Rs 998 or USD 10.8 per MMBtu. This was up 1 per cent MoM but down 18 per cent YoY.
