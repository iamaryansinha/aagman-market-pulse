Toyota Supplier Denso Targets 11% Return on Equity by 2030

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Japanese auto parts giant Denso has laid out an aggressive five-year growth plan targeting 11% return on equity and 10% operating profit margin by 2030
  • 02/The Toyota supplier plans to invest 6.6 trillion yen in business expansion while pursuing a recent bid for chipmaker Rohm to strengthen its position in EV and data center markets
  • 03/The company admits it fell short of its 2025 targets, forecasting only 8.1% ROE for fiscal 2026

Japanese auto parts maker Denso has unveiled ambitious financial targets for 2030, aiming to achieve an 11% return on equity despite missing similar goals in its previous strategic plan.

The company, a major supplier to Toyota, announced on Tuesday that it targets sales of 8 trillion yen ($50 billion) by the end of the decade. This represents a significant step up from current performance levels.

Denso also committed to a 10% operating profit margin by 2030. The company acknowledged it fell short of both the 10% ROE and operating margin targets in its 2025 plan. For fiscal 2026, which begins in April, Denso forecasts an ROE of just 8.1% and an operating margin of 7.2%.

The five-year investment plan totals 6.6 trillion yen through 2030. This breaks down into business investment, dividends of 1 trillion yen, and strategic investments including share repurchases. Over the fiscal 2027 to 2031 period, Denso plans to allocate 8 trillion yen or more toward investment and shareholder returns combined.

The company identified both opportunities and headwinds in its statement. Changes in mobility are accelerating across autonomous driving and software-defined vehicles. These shifts present growth avenues but also execution challenges.

Denso recently made a bid for Rohm, a Japanese chipmaker. The move targets power management semiconductors used in electric vehicles and data centers. This acquisition push signals Denso's strategy to expand beyond traditional auto parts into higher-margin chip technologies.

Market reaction was muted. Denso shares rose 0.8% following the announcement, though gains trimmed before the statement as investors digested the mixed track record on previous targets.
