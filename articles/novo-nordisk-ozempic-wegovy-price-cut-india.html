Novo Nordisk Cuts Ozempic Price By 36%, Wegovy By 48% In India Price War

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Novo Nordisk cuts starting dose price of Ozempic by 36% and Wegovy by 48% in India from April 1, 2026
  • 02/New monthly price for starting dose is Rs 5,660 for both drugs, down from Rs 8,800-17,345 earlier
  • 03/Price war triggered by patent expiry on March 20 and launch of generic semaglutide by Indian pharma companies

Novo Nordisk has slashed prices of its blockbuster diabetes and obesity drugs in India. Ozempic's starting dose price drops 36% while Wegovy sees a 48% reduction.

The new pricing takes effect from April 1, 2026. The monthly cost for the 0.25mg starting dose of both drugs is now Rs 5,660, inclusive of taxes. This works out to Rs 1,415 per week or Rs 202 per day.

The Danish drugmaker's move follows the expiry of semaglutide patents on March 20, 2026. Indian generics have flooded the market since then with prices as low as Rs 1,290 per month.

"We are trying to make best-in-class cardiometabolic care more affordable for as many people with type 2 diabetes, overweight and obesity in India as possible," said Vikrant Shrotriya, Managing Director of Novo Nordisk India.

Ozempic originally launched in India in December 2025 at Rs 8,800-11,175 per month for various doses. Wegovy entered the market in June 2025 priced at Rs 17,345-26,015 per month.

Before this revision, the starting dose cost Rs 2,200 weekly for Ozempic and Rs 2,712 for Wegovy. The new cuts represent one of Novo's steepest pricing moves for a patented therapy in India.

Across all strengths, average price cuts work out to about 24% for Ozempic and 27% for Wegovy. At higher doses, Wegovy still costs up to Rs 16,400 per month.

Competition is fierce. Sun Pharma's Noveltreat costs Rs 900-2,000 per month. Dr Reddy's Obeda is priced at Rs 4,200. Natco and Eris offer vial formats at Rs 1,290 monthly.

Eli Lilly's rival drug Mounjaro costs Rs 13,000-26,000 per Kwikpen. Novo maintains its pen-based injector format as a premium differentiator against generic vials.
