India Honey Market Shifts From Mass Production to Quality Focus

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian honey market projected to reach ₹5,190 crore by 2034, driven by consumer demand for raw, unfiltered natural honey
  • 02/India exported 1.07 lakh MT worth $177.55 million in FY24, ranking 2nd globally after rising from 9th position in 2020
  • 03/77% of tested honey samples failed purity tests, prompting government action through MEP norms and Madhukranti traceability portal

India's honey industry is undergoing a fundamental transformation. The market is projected to reach ₹5,190 crore by 2034. Consumers are shifting away from mass-produced, heavily processed varieties toward natural, pure alternatives.

The familiar amber jars that dominated Indian kitchens for decades are being questioned. Mass production methods stripped honey of pollen, enzymes, and antioxidants. Consumers now demand products that preserve natural health benefits.

India's honey production hit approximately 1.4 lakh metric tonnes in 2024. The country has emerged as the world's second-largest honey exporter. This marks a dramatic rise from 9th position in 2020.

Exports reached 1.07 lakh metric tonnes worth USD 177.55 million in FY 2023-24. Major destinations include the USA, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and Libya. Uttar Pradesh leads domestic production at 17%, followed by West Bengal at 16%.

The National Beekeeping and Honey Mission (NBHM) drives this growth with a ₹500 crore budget. The scheme runs from FY 2020-21 to FY 2025-26. It focuses on scientific beekeeping, productivity improvement, and post-harvest management.

Quality concerns have plagued the industry. A CSE study found 77% of honey samples from major brands were adulterated with C3 and C4 sugar syrups. Only 3 of 13 brands passed advanced NMR purity tests. Indian manufacturers allegedly imported cheap syrups from China to cut costs.

The government responded with strict measures. APEDA imposed a Minimum Export Price of $2,000 per metric tonne through December 2024. The rate was adjusted to $1,400 per metric tonne for 2025-2026. The Madhukranti portal now provides online registration and traceability for honey sources.

Six world-class honey testing labs and 47 mini labs have been sanctioned. The portal has registered 14,859 beekeepers, 269 societies, 150 firms, and 206 companies. Over 97 beekeeper FPOs have been formed to support small producers.

Consumer awareness is reshaping purchasing patterns. Buyers now demand FSSAI and AGMARK certifications. Traceability through batch-level tracking and lab reports has become essential. Raw honey with low hydroxymethylfurfural (HMF) levels commands premium pricing.

Pure honey is finding new applications beyond sweetening. Athletes use it as a natural energy booster. Skincare brands incorporate it for anti-inflammatory properties. Vedic standards emphasizing non-violent extraction are gaining traction among health-conscious consumers.

The shift supports rural livelihoods. In Kupwara, Jammu and Kashmir, over 500 farmers produce 480 quintals of organic honey annually. The turnover exceeds ₹3 crore. GI tagging initiatives for regional varieties like Kupwara Organic Honey are underway.

Climate change poses significant challenges. Erratic rainfall and temperature fluctuations affect flowering patterns. This impacts nectar availability and bee populations. Sustainable practices are becoming essential for long-term industry viability.

The industry outlook remains positive despite headwinds. Export volumes have nearly doubled since FY 2020-21. Domestic consumption is rising as consumers prioritize health over shelf appeal. The transition from mass production to quality focus appears irreversible.
