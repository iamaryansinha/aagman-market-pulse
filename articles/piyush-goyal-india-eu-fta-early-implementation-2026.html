Piyush Goyal Pushes for Early Implementation of India-EU Free Trade Agreement

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Union Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal held high-level discussions with a European Parliamentary delegation on expediting the India-EU Free Trade Agreement
  • 02/The FTA, finalized in January 2026 after prolonged negotiations, aims to boost bilateral trade beyond the current $136.54 billion mark while creating new export opportunities for Indian engineering goods and manufacturing sectors
  • 03/Both sides are targeting early 2027 for the agreement to take effect

Union Minister Piyush Goyal met with a European Parliamentary delegation led by Angelika Niebler in New Delhi on Monday to discuss pathways for the early implementation of the India-European Union Free Trade Agreement. The meeting signals renewed urgency from both sides to operationalize the landmark trade pact concluded earlier this year.

Goyal described the encounter as productive, emphasizing the growing momentum in India-EU relations. In a social media post following the meeting, he highlighted expanding cooperation across trade, technology, green energy, connectivity, defence, space, mobility, education, and people-to-people exchanges.

The India-EU FTA represents a defining milestone in the partnership, according to Goyal. He noted the agreement is positioned to unlock new opportunities for businesses, MSMEs, and skilled professionals on both sides. The minister specifically underlined discussions on pathways for early implementation alongside continued constructive engagement between the two sides.

India and the European Union concluded negotiations for the comprehensive Free Trade Agreement on January 27, 2026. The deal covers trade in goods, services, and investment. Officials from both sides are now working toward bringing the agreement into force by early 2027.

The Ministry of Commerce and Industry has structured the FTA as a future-ready trade framework targeting next-generation sectors of the Indian economy. Engineering and manufacturing receive particular emphasis under the agreement. India has set an ambitious target of $300 billion in engineering exports, and the FTA is expected to facilitate greater access to the $2 trillion European market.

The agreement places special focus on integrating Indian manufacturers into European supply chains. MSMEs and regional industrial clusters stand to benefit significantly, with the framework designed to help smaller enterprises scale operations and secure global contracts.

Trade data underscores the economic stakes involved. The European Union ranks among India's largest trading partners. In fiscal year 2024-25, bilateral trade in goods reached ₹11.5 lakh crore, equivalent to $136.54 billion. Indian exports to the EU totaled ₹6.4 lakh crore ($75.85 billion), while imports stood at ₹5.1 lakh crore ($60.68 billion).

The meeting with the European Parliament Delegation for Relations with India (D-IN) comes at a critical juncture as both sides work through the technical and legal processes required for ratification. Lok Sabha Speaker Om Birla also participated in the discussions, indicating the multi-level engagement between Indian and European institutions on the FTA.
