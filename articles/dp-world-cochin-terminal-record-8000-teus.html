DP World Cochin Terminal Achieves Record 8,000+ TEUs From Single Ship

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/DP World Cochin terminal handled 8,000+ TEUs from a single vessel, setting a new operational record
  • 02/The achievement surpasses the previous record of 6,000+ TEUs for MSC Aurora
  • 03/Record handling demonstrates growing capacity and efficiency at Indian container terminals

DP World Cochin terminal has set a new operational milestone. The facility handled over 8,000 TEUs from a single vessel operation.

This marks the highest volume ever achieved in a single ship call at the terminal. The previous record stood at 6,000+ TEUs for MSC Aurora.

The record-breaking operation was completed on April 5, 2026. The achievement was announced via an official press release.

Terminal efficiency and infrastructure upgrades enabled the milestone. It reflects India's growing capabilities in handling mega-container vessels.

DP World operates multiple terminals across India. The Cochin facility serves as a key gateway for South Indian trade.

Increased handling capacity benefits exporters and importers in the region. It reduces turnaround times for large vessels calling at Indian ports.

The maritime sector continues to see rising container volumes. Port operators are investing in equipment to handle larger ships efficiently.
