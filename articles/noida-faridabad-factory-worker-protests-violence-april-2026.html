Noida, Faridabad Factory Worker Protests Turn Violent; 10,000+ Workers Demand Wage Hike

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Over 10,000 factory workers protest in Noida and Faridabad demanding wage hike, protests turn violent
  • 02/Vandalism, stone pelting and arson reported from Phase-2, Sector 60 Noida and Sector 38 Faridabad
  • 03/Road blocks cause traffic jams on Delhi-Meerut Expressway; Delhi Police on high alert

Factory worker protests demanding wage hikes turned violent in Noida and Faridabad on Monday, April 13, 2026. Over 10,000 workers from companies including Richco Sports and Samvardhana Motherson participated in the demonstrations.

The protests began early Monday morning and escalated within hours. Workers gathered in Phase-2 and Sector 60 areas of Noida, and Sector 38 of Faridabad to press their long-pending demand for salary revision.

Demonstrators raised slogans during the protest. Some participants allegedly vandalised property, pelted stones, and set vehicles on fire. Police personnel were called in to control the situation.

Road blocks created long traffic jams on highways including the Delhi-Meerut Expressway. Commuters faced significant delays during peak morning hours.

Adequate police deployment has been ensured across industrial zones under the Gautam Buddh Nagar Commissionerate. Senior police and administrative officers are monitoring the situation on the ground.

"The situation is under control and is under continuous surveillance," police said in a statement. "Efforts are being made to counsel the workers and maintain peace. Minimum force is being used wherever necessary."

Delhi Police has been put on high alert following the protests in neighbouring areas. Security has been tightened along key entry points into the national capital.

Several police teams have been deployed at all motorable roads connecting Noida and Faridabad to Delhi. Major border points are under surveillance to prevent spill-over of the unrest.
