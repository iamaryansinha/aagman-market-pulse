New Mangalore Port to Receive 23,000 Tonnes LPG Cargo This Week

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/LPG tanker Al Ain with 23,000 tonnes cargo expected to berth at New Mangalore Port during this week
  • 02/Vessel departed Yanbu Industrial port in Saudi Arabia on March 19, marking latest energy shipment to India
  • 03/Arrival adds to India's LPG import volumes as demand remains strong ahead of summer season

LPG tanker Al Ain is scheduled to berth at New Mangalore Port this week. The vessel carries 23,000 tonnes of liquefied petroleum gas cargo.

The tanker departed from Yanbu Industrial port in Saudi Arabia on March 19. It has completed its voyage across the Arabian Sea.

New Mangalore Port serves as a key entry point for energy imports into South India. The port handles regular LPG and crude oil shipments.

India remains one of the world's largest LPG importers. Domestic consumption continues to grow driven by household and industrial demand.

LPG imports are critical for India's energy security. The country meets over 50% of its LPG requirements through imports.

Summer months typically see increased LPG demand. Higher cooking gas usage and industrial activity drive consumption upward.

The arrival of Al Ain adds to India's monthly LPG import volumes. State-run oil marketing companies are the primary importers.
