Broker's Call: Dr Reddy's Lab Gets 'Add' Rating, Target ₹1,315 on GLP-1 Launch

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BusinessLine assigns 'Add' rating on Dr Reddy's Laboratories with target price of ₹1,315, indicating 2.6% upside from CMP of ₹1,281.95
  • 02/Company launched Obeda, India's first DCGI-approved Semaglutide, priced at premium ₹4,200/month versus peers at ₹1,300
  • 03/Prefilled pen format and early-mover advantage in GLP-1 market expected to drive physician preference and patient stickiness

BusinessLine Markets has assigned an 'Add' rating on Dr Reddy's Laboratories Ltd (NSE: DRREDDY). The target price is set at ₹1,315 per share.

The current market price stands at ₹1,281.95. This implies a modest upside of 2.6% from current levels.

The rating follows the company's successful day-1 launch of Semaglutide in India. Dr Reddy's is the first to introduce a DCGI-approved version under the brand name Obeda.

This establishes an early-mover advantage in the domestic GLP-1 market. The segment is witnessing rapid growth amid rising diabetes and obesity concerns.

Semaglutide is a complex peptide product requiring advanced manufacturing capabilities. It is not a simple small-molecule generic.

The company has priced Obeda at ₹4,200 per month. This is significantly higher than peers offering vial-based alternatives at ₹1,300.

The premium reflects the prefilled disposable pen format. This could support greater physician preference and improved patient compliance.

Management indicated they are unlikely to engage in aggressive price cuts. The strategy focuses on quality and delivery format rather than price-led competition.

The launch positions Dr Reddy's strategically within the emerging GLP-1 opportunity. Profitability is likely to improve as the business scales up.

We interacted with Aishwarya Sitharam, Head of IR at Dr Reddy's Lab. The company emphasized its manufacturing strengths and sterile production expertise.

In the medium term, the market is expected to shift towards a more consolidated, quality-led structure. Dr Reddy's is well-placed to benefit given its early entry.

The stock has been trading range-bound amid concerns over US base business weakness. The GLP-1 launch provides a new growth avenue in the domestic market.
