India Notifies TV Rating Policy: Net Worth Norm Cut To ₹5 Crore From ₹20 Crore

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Ministry of Information & Broadcasting notified the TV Ratings Policy on March 27, 2026, cutting net worth eligibility to ₹5 crore from ₹20 crore
  • 02/Rating agencies must scale to 80,000 metered homes within 18 months, with existing agencies given 6 months to comply
  • 03/Landing page viewership will no longer count toward ratings measurement; policy replaces 2014 guidelines

The Ministry of Information & Broadcasting has notified the TV Ratings Policy on March 27, 2026. The new framework aims to ensure transparency, independence, and accountability in audience measurement across India.

The policy lowers net worth eligibility for TV rating agencies significantly. The requirement has been reduced from ₹20 crore to ₹5 crore, making it easier for new players to enter the market.

At least 50% of Board of Directors must now be independent directors. These directors cannot have ties to broadcasters, advertisers, or advertising agencies to prevent conflicts of interest.

Agencies are prohibited from engaging in consultancy roles that could create conflicts. This measure aims to ensure unbiased ratings that advertisers and broadcasters can trust.

Rating agencies must expand their metered homes to 80,000 within 18 months. The target is eventually 1.2 lakh homes for comprehensive nationwide coverage.

Existing rating agencies have been given six months to scale up operations. Data will be captured from all TV viewing screens within the metered homes.

In a significant change, landing page viewership will not count toward ratings. Broadcasters can still use landing pages as marketing tools, but must disclose such arrangements to rating agencies.

The policy mandates a dual-audit system for all agencies. Quarterly internal audits and annual independent external audits are now compulsory.

All operations must comply with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023. Agencies must publish detailed methodology and anonymised data on their websites.

The Ministry will constitute an Audit & Oversight Team for periodic field inspections. A nodal officer must be appointed for grievance redressal, and graded penalties apply for non-compliance.

The new policy replaces the Guidelines for TV Rating Agencies in India released in 2014. It follows consultations with industry stakeholders over the past several months.

TV Distribution Platforms and OTT platforms can now publish periodic viewership data on their websites without obtaining registration under these guidelines.
