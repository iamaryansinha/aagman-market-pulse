AI171 Crash Families Seek Black Box Data Release, Write to PM Modi

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Around 30 bereaved families of AI171 crash victims gathered in Ahmedabad on Saturday to demand answers
  • 02/Families wrote to PM Modi requesting release of cockpit voice recorder and black box data from the crash
  • 03/Air India flight AI171 crashed on June 12, 2025 at Ahmedabad airport killing all 260 people on board

Around 30 bereaved families of the AI171 crash victims met in Ahmedabad on Saturday. They jointly wrote a letter to Prime Minister Narendra Modi requesting the release of critical flight data.

The families are seeking access to the Cockpit Voice Recorder (CVR) and black box data. They believe this evidence is essential to uncover the truth behind the air disaster.

The crash occurred on June 12, 2025 at Ahmedabad airport. All 260 people on board were killed in what became one of India's deadliest aviation accidents.

The families gathered from across Gujarat to voice their collective demand. They want transparency in the investigation process and access to information that could explain what went wrong.

Air India has faced scrutiny following the incident. The airline has not yet publicly responded to the families' latest appeal to the Prime Minister.

The investigation into the crash is ongoing. Aviation safety regulators continue to examine technical data from the wreckage.

The families' letter marks an escalation in their campaign for accountability. They have previously demanded answers from the airline and regulatory authorities.
