NSE IPO Process Begins: Exchange Seeks Shareholder Interest for OFS

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NSE invites expressions of interest from shareholders to identify potential sellers for the upcoming IPO via OFS route
  • 02/Response deadline set for April 27, 2026; marks concrete step toward NSE's long-awaited public market debut
  • 03/Exchange yet to disclose IPO size, valuation, or listing timeline; regulatory approvals pending

National Stock Exchange of India has formally initiated its IPO process. The exchange invited expressions of interest from existing shareholders on March 30.

Shareholders interested in participating in the offer-for-sale must submit their EOI by April 27. This deadline allows NSE to gauge potential supply for the offering.

The move marks a significant step toward NSE's much-anticipated market listing. The exchange has remained privately held for over three decades since its establishment.

NSE is India's largest stock exchange by turnover and market capitalization. It operates the flagship Nifty 50 index and dominates equity derivatives trading globally.

The IPO is expected to provide liquidity to early investors and employees. Several shareholders have awaited an exit route for years as the exchange grew.

Exchange officials have not disclosed the proposed issue size or valuation. Regulatory approvals from SEBI are still pending before the IPO can proceed.
