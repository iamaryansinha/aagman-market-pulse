Adani Defence Delivers 2,000 Prahar LMGs to Indian Army Ahead of Schedule

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Adani Defence & Aerospace delivers first batch of 2,000 Prahar 7.62x51 mm Light Machine Guns to the Indian Army
  • 02/The delivery is 11 months ahead of the contracted schedule; total order comprises 41,000 LMGs expected within 3 years
  • 03/Manufactured at PLR Systems JV facility in Gwalior under Make in India initiative, reducing import dependence

Adani Defence & Aerospace has delivered the first batch of 2,000 Prahar LMGs to the Indian Army. The handover ceremony took place at the company's Small Arms Complex in Gwalior on March 28, 2026.

The Prahar 7.62x51 mm Light Machine Guns are manufactured by PLR Systems. This joint venture between Adani Defence & Aerospace and Israeli Weapon Industries (IWI) operates the fully integrated facility.

The delivery is 11 months ahead of the original contracted schedule. The First-of-Production Model was achieved in just 6 months against a stipulated 18-month timeline.

The total order comprises 41,000 LMGs with an original delivery timeline exceeding 7 years. Adani has committed to completing the full order within just 3 years instead.

The Gwalior facility spans 100 acres with annual manufacturing capacity of 100,000 guns. It features barrel manufacturing, CNC machining, robotics, surface treatment, and a 25-metre underground firing range.

The Prahar LMG weighs 8 kg and has an effective range of 1,000 metres. It significantly reduces India's reliance on imported infantry weapons and strengthens defence self-reliance.

A. Anbarasu, DG Acquisition & Additional Secretary, Ministry of Defence, flagged off the dispatch. He noted the delivery saved 600 days from the original schedule.

Ashish Rajvanshi, CEO of Adani Defence and Aerospace, stated the company has transitioned from component manufacturing to full gun OEM capability. The facility can now produce assault rifles, pistols, snipers, and carbines alongside LMGs.
