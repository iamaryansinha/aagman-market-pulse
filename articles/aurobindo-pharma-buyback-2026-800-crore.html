Aurobindo Pharma Buyback 2026: Board Approves ₹800 Crore at ₹1,475 Per Share

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Aurobindo Pharma board approves ₹800 crore share buyback at ₹1,475 per share, a 10.5% premium to current market price
  • 02/Buyback covers up to 54.23 lakh shares representing 0.93% of equity; company holds net cash of USD 251 million
  • 03/Record date and timeline to be announced; promoters and promoter group eligible to participate

Aurobindo Pharma Limited (NSE: AUROPHARMA) board has approved a proposal to buy back up to 54.23 lakh fully paid-up equity shares. The buyback price is fixed at ₹1,475 per share payable in cash.

The aggregate buyback amount is capped at ₹800 crore. This represents 0.93% of the total paid-up equity share capital of the company.

The buyback price of ₹1,475 per share offers a premium of approximately 10.5% over the stock's closing price of ₹1,335.95 on April 2, 2026. The premium reflects management's confidence in the company's intrinsic value.

The buyback is proposed to be made from all equity shareholders including promoters and members of the promoter group. The record date and specific timeline for the buyback process will be announced subsequently.

Aurobindo Pharma reported a strong net cash position of USD 251 million as of December 31, 2025. This is after accounting for the acquisition of Khandelwal Laboratories, indicating robust liquidity to fund the buyback.

The company generated free cash flows of USD 118 million in Q3 FY26 alone. Revenue from operations grew 8.4% year-on-year to ₹8,646 crore during the quarter.

Net profit for Q3 FY26 stood at ₹910 crore, up 7.6% year-on-year. EBITDA margin before forex and other income was 20.5%, demonstrating operational efficiency.

Europe formulations emerged as the growth driver with 27.4% YoY revenue increase to ₹2,703 crore. US formulations revenue grew 2.2% to ₹3,739 crore.

Elara Capital maintains a Buy rating on Aurobindo Pharma with a target price of ₹1,568. This implies potential upside of approximately 17% from the buyback price.

MarketsMojo upgraded Aurobindo Pharma to a Buy grade on March 24, 2026 with a Mojo Score of 75. The stock has outperformed the Sensex across one-month, three-month, one-year, and year-to-date periods.

The buyback size does not include transaction costs such as brokerage, fees, turnover charges, and applicable taxes. The company has reserves of ₹20,476 crore as of March 2025, providing adequate financial flexibility.
