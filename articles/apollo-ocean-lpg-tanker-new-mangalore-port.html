Apollo Ocean LPG Tanker Berths at New Mangalore Port, Marks 40,000th Vessel Milestone

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/LPG tanker MT Apollo Ocean berths at New Mangalore Port on March 26, 2026, marking the port's 40,000th vessel milestone
  • 02/Vietnam-flagged vessel discharges 16,000 metric tonnes of LPG cargo for Indian Oil and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation
  • 03/Berthing occurs amid West Asia supply disruptions; India has secured 800,000 tonnes of inbound LPG cargoes from US, Russia, Australia

LPG tanker MT Apollo Ocean berthed at New Mangalore Port on March 26, 2026. The vessel arrived at Berth Number 13 at 4 pm and was received by Deputy Chairperson S Shanthi.

The Vietnam-flagged vessel marked a historic milestone as the port's 40,000th vessel. Port officials felicitated the captain to commemorate the occasion.

The tanker is scheduled to discharge 16,000 metric tonnes of LPG. The cargo is designated for Indian Oil Corporation Ltd (IOCL) and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Ltd (HPCL).

The vessel arrived from Vadinar Port in Jamnagar, Gujarat. It is one of several LPG shipments reaching Indian ports amid ongoing West Asia disruptions.

New Mangalore Port is India's seventh-largest port and the only major port in Karnataka. It features the deepest inner harbour on India's west coast.

The port handles critical energy imports including crude oil, petroleum products, and LPG. It serves as a key maritime gateway for the region's industrial and domestic energy needs.

India's LPG production has been ramped up to a record 50,000 tonnes per day. This meets over 60 percent of the country's daily requirement of approximately 80,000 tonnes.

The net daily import requirement has reduced to 30,000 tonnes. The government has secured 800,000 tonnes of assured inbound LPG cargoes from the US, Russia, Australia and other countries.

India has a total reserve capacity of 74 days. Current stock cover stands at around 60 days including crude, petroleum products, and strategic cavern storage.

All 22 Indian ships and 611 sailors in the Persian Gulf region are reported safe. The Shipping Ministry has waived cargo-related charges for crude and LPG at major ports between March 14 and March 31, 2026.
