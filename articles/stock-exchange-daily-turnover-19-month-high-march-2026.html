Stock Exchange Daily Turnover Hits 19-Month High at ₹1.31 Lakh Crore in March

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Combined NSE-BSE average daily turnover hit ₹1.31 lakh crore in March 2026, highest since August 2024
  • 02/Trading activity rose 6.5% from February's ₹1.23 lakh crore despite Nifty's 11.36% monthly decline
  • 03/Record FPI outflows of ₹1.14 trillion marked the highest monthly withdrawal ever; volatility expected to persist

India's stock exchanges recorded their highest trading activity in 19 months during March 2026. The combined average daily turnover (ADT) on NSE and BSE reached ₹1.31 lakh crore.

This marks the highest level since August 2024. The figure represents a 6.5% increase from February's ₹1.23 lakh crore ADT.

The surge in trading volume came despite extreme market volatility. The Nifty 50 index fell 11.36% in March, its worst monthly decline since March 2020.

The India VIX volatility index spiked to 27.75 on March 30. This four-year high reflected heightened investor anxiety amid global uncertainties.

Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) withdrew a record ₹1.14 trillion from Indian equities in March. This marked the highest monthly outflow ever recorded, surpassing the previous record of ₹94,017 crore from October 2024.

FPIs remained net sellers on nearly every trading day of the month. The selling was driven by escalating West Asia tensions and Brent crude prices crossing $100 per barrel.

The financial services sector bore the brunt of FPI selling. Foreign investors offloaded shares worth ₹31,831 crore in the sector during the first half of March alone.

The rupee weakened to near ₹94 against the US dollar during the month. This currency depreciation further eroded returns for foreign investors.

Despite the foreign exodus, domestic investors provided market support. Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) inflows remained steady, cushioning the sell-off impact.

Total FPI outflows for 2026 have now crossed ₹1.27 lakh crore. Analysts expect volatility to persist until geopolitical tensions ease and crude prices stabilize.
