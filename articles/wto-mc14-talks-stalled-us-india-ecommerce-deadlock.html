WTO Talks Stalled on Final Day as US, India Deadlock on E-Commerce Moratorium

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/WTO MC14 talks stalled on final day March 29 as US demands permanent e-commerce moratorium while India, Brazil, South Africa resist
  • 02/The 28-year-old moratorium on customs duties for electronic transmissions expires March 31, 2026, threatening $4.8 trillion in digital trade
  • 03/Ministers face midnight deadline to bridge gap between Washington's all-or-nothing stance and developing nations' demand for policy flexibility

WTO MC14 talks in Yaoundé, Cameroon remain deadlocked as the conference enters its final day on March 29. Trade ministers from 166 member nations failed to reach consensus on the future of the e-commerce moratorium.

The moratorium prohibits customs duties on electronic transmissions. It has been renewed every two years since its inception in 1998. The current extension expires on March 31, 2026.

US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer insisted on a permanent moratorium. "The US is not interested in another temporary extension," Greer stated at MC14 on March 26.

India, Brazil, and South Africa oppose making the moratorium permanent. Indian Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal called for "careful reconsideration" of any extension.

Developing nations cite mounting tariff revenue losses. They also highlight the lack of a clear definition for "electronic transmissions" that could widen scope dangerously.

Global exports of digitally delivered services reached $4.8 trillion in 2024. Developed economies led by Europe and North America account for the bulk of this trade.

Brazil and South Africa may accept a two-year extension at most. India has opposed extension but could accept two years if other terms are favorable.

Washington's all-or-nothing stance pits US tech giants against developing nation sovereignty. Companies like Microsoft, Netflix, Google, and Spotify face potential tariffs if the moratorium lapses.

A temporary compromise of two to four years appears most likely. Sources indicate intense meetings are ongoing to avert a complete collapse of the moratorium.

Failure to agree would allow WTO members to impose duties on digital transmissions. This could fragment the global digital economy into competing regulatory blocs.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
Q: WHAT IS THE WTO E-COMMERCE MORATORIUM?
THE E-COMMERCE MORATORIUM IS A 28-YEAR-OLD WTO AGREEMENT THAT PROHIBITS MEMBER NATIONS FROM IMPOSING CUSTOMS DUTIES ON ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSIONS. IT HAS BEEN RENEWED EVERY TWO YEARS SINCE 1998 AND IS SET TO EXPIRE ON MARCH 31, 2026.
