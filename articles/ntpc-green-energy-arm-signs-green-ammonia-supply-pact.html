NTPC Green Energy Subsidiary Inks 70,000 MT Green Ammonia Supply Deal Under SIGHT Scheme

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NTPC Renewable Energy, a wholly owned subsidiary of NTPC Green Energy, has signed a green ammonia purchase agreement with Solar Energy Corporation of India
  • 02/The deal involves supplying 70,000 metric tonnes per annum of green ammonia under the SIGHT Scheme of the National Green Hydrogen Mission
  • 03/The output will be supplied to Krishna Phoschem's facility in Meghnagar, Madhya Pradesh, with SECI acting as intermediary procurer

NTPC Renewable Energy has signed a green ammonia purchase agreement with Solar Energy Corporation of India. The company is a wholly owned subsidiary of NTPC Green Energy.

The agreement falls under the SIGHT Scheme of the National Green Hydrogen Mission. It involves a supply commitment of 70,000 metric tonnes per annum of green ammonia.

The output will be supplied to Krishna Phoschem. The company operates a facility in Meghnagar, Madhya Pradesh. SECI will act as the intermediary procurer for the transaction.

Green ammonia is produced using renewable energy. It is increasingly viewed as critical for decarbonising hard-to-abate sectors. These include fertilisers, shipping, and heavy industry.

The development marks a major milestone in NTPC Green Energy's expanding green hydrogen and ammonia portfolio. The company is actively building out its clean energy capabilities as part of India's broader energy transition strategy.

On Monday, NTPC Green Energy shares closed at Rs 92.32 on the NSE. The stock declined 2.88 per cent. The market is closed on March 31 for a local holiday.
