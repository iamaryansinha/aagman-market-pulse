India's Wheat Output May Drop 1-1.5% From Record 120 MT Estimate on Unseasonal Rains

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Private survey estimates India's wheat output may drop 1-1.5% from government's record 120.21 million tonne estimate for 2025-26 crop year
  • 02/Unseasonal rains and hailstorms during March 11-22 damaged crops in Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Bihar, Haryana, Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh
  • 03/Four districts face 10-15% production loss; government lifted wheat export ban last month allowing 2.5 MT shipments

India's wheat production may decline by 1-1.5% from the government's record estimate of 120.21 million tonnes. The damage was caused by unseasonal rains and sporadic hailstorms between March 11-22 across major wheat-growing states.

A preliminary report by private agency Agriwatch shows significant crop damage. The survey was commissioned by the Roller Flour Millers' Federation of India.

Four districts across three states may lose 10-15% of their wheat production. Another 21 districts face 5-10% yield loss from the adverse weather.

Uttar Pradesh emerges as the most extensively affected state. Damage ranges across 23 districts including Bijnor which faces high losses.

Punjab recorded high damage in Rupnagar district alone. Fourteen districts in the state reported varying degrees of crop impact.

Bihar saw damage across 10 districts. Begusarai and Supaul face the highest losses at 10-15%.

Haryana, Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan reported mostly low to moderate losses. Damage in West Bengal and Gujarat was negligible.

The government had estimated wheat output at a record 120.21 million tonnes for 2025-26. This represented a 2% increase from the previous year's 117.94 million tonnes.

Last month, the Food Ministry lifted a four-year-old ban on wheat exports. The government allowed shipments of 2.5 million tonnes citing surplus stocks and prospects of a record harvest.

Area under wheat cultivation rose to 33.41 million hectares this season. This is approximately 3% higher than last year.
