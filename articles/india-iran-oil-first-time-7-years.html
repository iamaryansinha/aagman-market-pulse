India Buys Iranian Oil First Time in 7 Years Amid Middle East Supply Crisis

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Indian refiners purchased Iranian oil for the first time since May 2019 amid Middle East supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz
  • 02/The US temporarily lifted sanctions on Iranian oil last month to ease global supply shortages caused by the ongoing conflict
  • 03/India also secured 44,000 metric tons of Iranian LPG; vessel berthed at Mangalore port on Wednesday for discharge

Indian refiners have purchased Iranian oil for the first time in seven years. The oil ministry confirmed the purchase on Saturday amid ongoing Middle East supply disruptions.

India last received Iranian crude in May 2019. This followed years of US pressure to stop buying from Tehran.

The world's third-largest oil importer has been hit hard by supply disruptions. The US-Israel war with Iran has effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz.

"Amid Middle East supply disruptions, Indian refiners have secured their crude oil requirements, including from Iran," the ministry said on X. "There is no payment hurdle for Iranian crude imports."

Last month, the United States temporarily removed sanctions on Iranian oil. The move aims to ease global supply shortages caused by the conflict.

India has also purchased 44,000 metric tons of Iranian liquefied petroleum gas. The sanctioned vessel berthed at Mangalore port on Wednesday and is currently discharging fuel.

"India imports crude oil from 40-plus countries," the ministry added. "Companies have full flexibility to source oil from different sources based on commercial considerations."

The ministry denied reports of payment problems. "Contrary to rumours being circulated," imports face no payment obstacles.

India has fully secured its crude oil requirements for the coming months. The government invoked the Essential Commodities Act to conduct over 12,000 raids against fuel hoarding.

Domestic LPG cylinders normally priced at Rs900-1,000 have been selling illegally for Rs1,800-2,500 due to supply delays.
