Biocon's Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw Calls for Biotech Policy Reforms to Stem Innovation Exodus

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Biocon's Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw warns structural gaps are pushing Indian biotech innovation overseas to US and China markets
  • 02/China now accounts for 20-25% of global new drug R&D and 46% of novel molecules entering human trials in 2025
  • 03/She calls for deeper VC participation, enabling listing frameworks, and regulatory clarity to support clinical-stage innovation

Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw, Executive Chairperson of Biocon Ltd (NSE: BIOCON), has flagged critical structural gaps in India's biotech ecosystem. She warns that weak venture funding, regulatory bottlenecks, and absent exit avenues are driving high-value innovation out of the country.

India continues to struggle with supporting clinical-stage innovation. Companies at this phase are typically pre-revenue but require sustained capital for long development cycles from lab to market.

The absence of well-defined exit pathways makes it harder to attract investors. In contrast, US markets allow clinical-stage biotech firms to access capital earlier, creating liquidity for investors.

China has emerged as a significant pharmaceutical innovator. According to a Goldman Sachs report, China accounts for roughly 20-25% of new drug research and development. It also represents about 46% of novel molecules entering human trials in early 2025.

This progress is supported by strong state backing and faster clinical execution in China. The consequence is a steady migration of Indian-developed science being commercialised overseas.

Mazumdar-Shaw stressed that individual company success cannot substitute for systemic reform. Without policy changes enabling risk capital and credible exit mechanisms, India risks missing out on a globally competitive biotech innovation engine.

"If we create the right ecosystem, innovation itself can become an enormous value driver for India," she said. She underlined that India has no shortage of scientific capability, only the enabling environment to fully realise it.
