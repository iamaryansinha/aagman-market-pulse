India Industrial Growth Hits 5.2% in February on Manufacturing Boost

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's industrial growth accelerated to 5.2% in February 2026, compared to 3.1% growth recorded in January
  • 02/Manufacturing sector output expanded by 6%, driving the overall Index of Industrial Production (IIP) higher
  • 03/Mining and electricity sectors posted modest gains; sustainability of manufacturing momentum remains key to watch

India's industrial output grew 5.2% in February 2026, marking a sharp recovery from January's 3.1% growth. The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) data was released by the Ministry of Statistics on March 30.

The manufacturing sector emerged as the primary growth driver. Output expanded by 6% during the month, reflecting improved factory activity and demand conditions.

Mining sector growth remained modest. The sector posted limited gains compared to the robust manufacturing performance.

Electricity generation also saw modest expansion. Power output growth complemented the overall industrial recovery.

The February uptick suggests a potential turnaround in industrial activity. However, experts caution that sustained momentum depends on rural demand recovery and export performance.

The IIP data covers three sectors: mining, manufacturing, and electricity. Manufacturing carries the highest weight at 77.6% in the index calculation.
