India's Specialty Coffee Boom: From Bean To $1.81 Billion Export Empire

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's coffee exports reached a record USD 1.81 billion in FY2024-25, marking a 40.2% surge from the previous year's USD 1.29 billion
  • 02/The specialty coffee market is valued at USD 3.01 billion in 2025 and projected to reach USD 6.52 billion by 2031 with a 13.75% CAGR
  • 03/Karnataka produces 71% of India's coffee, followed by Kerala at 20% and Tamil Nadu at 5%, with the Coffee Board targeting 9 lakh tonnes production by 2047

India's specialty coffee sector is experiencing unprecedented growth, positioning the country as a rising force in global coffee markets. Coffee exports surged to a record USD 1.81 billion in FY2024-25, representing a 40.2% jump from USD 1.29 billion in FY24. This marks a 125% growth over the past decade from just USD 800 million in FY2014-15.

The domestic specialty coffee market is valued at USD 3.01 billion in 2025. It is projected to more than double to USD 6.52 billion by 2031, growing at a CAGR of 13.75%. This expansion is fueled by rising urban disposable incomes and a burgeoning café culture among millennials and Gen Z consumers.

Coffee cultivation spans 4.91 lakh hectares across India's southern states. Karnataka dominates production with 71% of total output, primarily from Chikmagalur, Coorg, and Hassan districts. Kerala contributes 20% from Wayanad and Travancore regions, while Tamil Nadu adds 5% from the Nilgiri and Shevaroy Hills.

India primarily cultivates two bean varieties: Arabica and Robusta. Robusta accounts for approximately 72% of production, favored for its strong flavor and use in instant coffee and espresso blends. Arabica, grown at higher elevations between 15-25°C, commands premium prices in export markets for its mild, aromatic profile.

The agricultural practices distinguish Indian coffee globally. Shade-grown cultivation under diverse native tree species supports biodiversity conservation. The Coffee Board's INDICOFS sustainability standard promotes eco-friendly practices, with nearly 20% of Indian coffee now Rainforest Alliance Certified. This aligns with emerging EU Deforestation Regulations requiring traceability.

Export markets have expanded significantly, with Italy, Germany, Belgium, Russia, and the UAE as top destinations. India exports over 70% of its total coffee production to more than 120 countries. Instant coffee constitutes roughly 30% of export value, with particular demand from Russia and Turkey.

Domestic consumption patterns are shifting rapidly. Blue Tokai Coffee Roasters now operates 150-plus cafes across 10 cities, achieving Rs 400 crore in annual revenue as of 2024. Third Wave Coffee Roasters has expanded to 165 outlets in 12 cities. Home brewing is gaining traction, with retail coffee sales growing significantly.

Government support plays a crucial role in farmer welfare. Subsidies of USD 2,500-3,500 per hectare are provided for coffee development in traditional regions. Export incentives include Rs 2 per kg for premium green coffee and Rs 3 per kg for value-added retail packs. The Coffee Board aims to triple production to 9 lakh tonnes by 2047.

Challenges remain despite the positive momentum. Erratic rainfall and heatwaves in Karnataka and Kerala have impacted Robusta crops. Climate change poses risks to production stability, with 2025 exports projected to decline by up to 10% due to lower output forecasts. The industry provides direct employment to over 2 million people.

Sustainability initiatives are reshaping the sector. The AIC-CCRI-CED incubation center in Bengaluru has supported over 63 coffee startups and trained more than 3,000 individuals. Direct trade models are improving farmer incomes by connecting growers directly with specialty roasters. Traceability systems are being digitized to meet global compliance standards.
