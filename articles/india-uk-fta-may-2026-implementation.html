India-UK FTA To Take Effect From Second Week Of May 2026

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India-UK Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA) expected to come into force from second week of May 2026
  • 02/99% of Indian exports will enter UK at zero duty; 90% of UK goods to get tariff-free access to India
  • 03/Bilateral trade projected to double from $16 billion to $120 billion within five years of implementation

The India-UK free trade agreement is set to take effect from the second week of May 2026. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal confirmed the timeline following a bilateral meeting with UK Business and Trade Secretary Peter Kyle.

The Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA) was signed on July 24, 2025. Both Prime Ministers Narendra Modi and Keir Starmer attended the signing ceremony in London.

Under the pact, 99% of Indian exports will gain duty-free access to the British market. This includes textiles, leather goods, jewellery, and pharmaceuticals currently facing tariffs of 4-16%.

UK exports will see tariffs cut on 90% of goods. Scotch whisky duties will drop from 150% to 75% immediately, then to 40% over ten years. UK car tariffs will fall from over 100% to 10% under agreed quotas.

The agreement aims to boost bilateral trade from approximately $16 billion to $120 billion within five years. UK government estimates project an annual increase of £25.5 billion in bilateral trade.

A parallel Double Contributions Convention (DCC) will benefit around 75,000 Indian professionals in the UK. Workers can claim exemption from UK National Insurance contributions for up to three years.

Sensitive sectors including dairy, agriculture, rice, and sugar have been protected. These remain excluded from market access provisions under the deal.

"The United Kingdom is very pleased at the speed with which the India-UK Free Trade Agreement passed both Houses of Parliament," Goyal said. The UK Parliament completed scrutiny under the Constitutional Reform and Governance Act 2010.

India has concluded nine FTAs over the past three and a half years. These provide preferential market access to 38 developed nations.
