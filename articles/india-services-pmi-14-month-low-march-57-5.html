India Services PMI Hits 14-Month Low at 57.5 in March on Weak Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/S&P Global India Services PMI falls to 57.5 in March, the lowest reading in 14 months since January 2025
  • 02/New business growth slowed to the weakest pace in 10 months as domestic and international demand muted
  • 03/Rising input costs and softening business sentiment signal potential headwinds for Q2 2026

India's services sector expansion slowed sharply in March 2026. The S&P Global India Services PMI fell to 57.5, marking a 14-month low.

The reading dropped from 59.0 in February. It is now the weakest since January 2025, though still above the 50-mark that separates growth from contraction.

New business growth slowed to its weakest pace in 10 months. Both domestic and international demand showed signs of fatigue.

Service providers reported muted client spending across key segments. The slowdown was broad-based across financial services, IT, and hospitality sectors.

Input cost inflation accelerated during March. Rising wages and higher prices for raw materials squeezed operating margins for service providers.

Employment growth in the sector also moderated from February levels. Companies cited cautious hiring amid uncertain demand visibility.

Business sentiment softened to a four-month low. Firms expressed concerns about sustaining growth momentum in the coming quarter.

The weak services reading follows softer manufacturing PMI data. India's composite output index also slipped, indicating broad-based economic cooling.

Economists expect the RBI to monitor these trends closely. A sustained slowdown could influence the central bank's stance on interest rates.
