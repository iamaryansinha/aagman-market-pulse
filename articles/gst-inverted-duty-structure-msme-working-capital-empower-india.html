GST Inverted Duty Structure Choking MSME Working Capital: Empower India

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Empower India warns that GST inverted duty structure is blocking working capital for MSMEs across pharmaceuticals, FMCG, and ecommerce sectors
  • 02/Input services and capital goods remain excluded from refund mechanism under Section 54(3) of CGST Act despite rate rationalization in September 2025
  • 03/Think tank urges amendment to refund rules and revision of Net ITC definition to restore tax neutrality and competitiveness

Empower India has flagged serious concerns over the inverted duty structure (IDS) under India's GST framework. The think tank says the issue is choking working capital and undermining MSME competitiveness.

The problem arises when tax rates on inputs exceed rates on output supplies. This creates a buildup of unutilized input tax credits (ITC). Businesses cannot recover these credits on input services and capital goods.

The impact spans multiple sectors. Pharmaceuticals, FMCG, insurance, ecommerce, edible oils, and packaged foods are all affected. Input and service tax rates often run higher than output rates in these industries.

GST was designed to eliminate tax cascading. However, the current framework has created the opposite effect. Input tax rates exceed output rates in several sectors. This leaves businesses unable to recover legitimate tax credits.

The exclusion of input services and capital goods from refunds compounds the problem. Section 54(3) of the CGST Act only permits refunds where inversion stems from inputs. This creates structural disadvantages for manufacturing, exports, and services.

"A predictable and efficient input tax credit framework is central to the success of GST," said K. Giri, Director General of Empower India. "The continued exclusion of input services and capital goods constrains liquidity and creates cost pressures."

Smaller players face disproportionate harm. MSMEs lack the capital buffers that larger firms maintain. Credit accumulation locks up working capital that could fund operations and growth.

The GST Council implemented rate rationalization on September 22, 2025. GST 2.0 moved to a simplified two-tier structure of 5% and 18%. However, the IDS refund mechanism remains incomplete.

Empower India recommends four specific reforms. First, expand the refund scope to cover input services and capital goods. Second, amend Section 54(3) of the CGST Act. Third, revise the definition of Net ITC. Fourth, simplify procedures and issue clear guidance.

The think tank submitted these recommendations to Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman. Industry bodies continue to push for automatic, time-bound refund processing to ease MSME liquidity stress.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE (IDS) IN GST?
INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE OCCURS WHEN THE TAX RATE ON INPUTS EXCEEDS THE TAX RATE ON OUTPUT SUPPLIES. THIS RESULTS IN ACCUMULATION OF UNUTILIZED INPUT TAX CREDITS THAT BUSINESSES CANNOT FULLY RECOVER, BLOCKING WORKING CAPITAL.
WHICH SECTORS ARE MOST AFFECTED BY THE GST INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE?
PHARMACEUTICALS, FMCG, INSURANCE, ECOMMERCE, EDIBLE OILS, AND PACKAGED FOODS ARE SIGNIFICANTLY IMPACTED. THESE SECTORS OFTEN FACE HIGHER GST RATES ON INPUTS AND SERVICES COMPARED TO THEIR OUTPUT PRODUCTS.
WHY ARE INPUT SERVICES AND CAPITAL GOODS EXCLUDED FROM IDS REFUNDS?
UNDER SECTION 54(3) OF THE CGST ACT, REFUNDS ARE ONLY PERMITTED WHERE INVERSION STEMS FROM GOODS INPUTS. THE DEFINITION OF NET ITC EXCLUDES INPUT SERVICES AND CAPITAL GOODS, REDUCING REFUND AMOUNTS DESPITE THEIR ROLE IN SUPPLY CHAINS.
WHAT ARE THE RECOMMENDATIONS MADE BY EMPOWER INDIA TO FIX THE IDS ISSUE?
EMPOWER INDIA RECOMMENDS EXPANDING REFUND SCOPE TO COVER INPUT SERVICES AND CAPITAL GOODS, AMENDING SECTION 54(3) OF THE CGST ACT, REVISING THE DEFINITION OF NET ITC, AND SIMPLIFYING PROCEDURES WITH CLEAR GUIDANCE.
HOW DOES THE INVERTED DUTY STRUCTURE IMPACT MSMEs SPECIFICALLY?
MSMEs FACE DISPROPORTIONATE IMPACT DUE TO LIMITED ACCESS TO CAPITAL. LOCKED TAX CREDITS CREATE WORKING CAPITAL BLOCKAGES, INCREASE COST OF DOING BUSINESS, AND CREATE INEFFICIENCIES THAT THREATEN VIABILITY FOR SMALLER PLAYERS.
