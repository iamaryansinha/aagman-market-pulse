Switch Mobility Targets 10,000 EV Fleet As UAE Hub Drives Global Export Push

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Switch Mobility targets doubling its electric vehicle fleet to 10,000 units over the next two years from current 5,000 vehicles on road
  • 02/Ashok Leyland's EV arm achieved EBITDA and PAT breakeven in FY26 with over ₹1,200 crore invested by parent company
  • 03/New Ras Al Khaimah facility to serve as export hub for GCC, Africa and European markets with 10,000 bus annual capacity

Switch Mobility Ltd, the electric vehicle arm of Ashok Leyland (NSE: ASHOKLEY), plans to double its fleet to 10,000 vehicles over the next two years. The company currently operates over 5,000 electric vehicles across India.

The EV maker has achieved EBITDA and PAT breakeven in FY26. Parent Ashok Leyland has invested over ₹1,200 crore into the business so far.

Switch Mobility holds 23% market share in electric buses and over 40% in the 2-3.5 tonne LCV segment. The company grew from 444 buses last year to over 1,200 buses this year.

The company is expanding globally through its Ras Al Khaimah manufacturing facility in UAE. The plant has total capacity to produce up to 10,000 buses annually for GCC, Africa and European markets.

Switch Mobility has already exported approximately 100 buses to Mauritius. It has a further pipeline of around 100 units across Seychelles, Bhutan and Nepal.

The company maintains an order book estimated at around ₹1,500 crore. This provides medium-term revenue visibility as it transitions to a self-sustained operating model.

All vehicles are connected and monitored through a central command centre. The system processes around 12 million data points annually to maintain about 98% uptime.

The EV arm faces rising cost pressures from battery costs, commodity prices and supply chain disruptions. Industry costs are reportedly rising by 20-30% across the sector.

"We are currently self-sufficient and able to manage our growth with the existing business," said Ganesh Mani, CEO of Switch Mobility.
