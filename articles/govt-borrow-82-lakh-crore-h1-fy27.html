Govt to Borrow ₹8.2 Lakh Crore in H1 FY27 via Dated Securities

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Ministry announces ₹8.20 lakh crore borrowing via dated securities for April-September FY27, representing 51% of total annual plan
  • 02/Borrowing front-loading is lower than typical 60%+ in H1, reflecting high 10-year bond yields above 6.9% and global volatility
  • 03/Watch for RBI's OMO operations, quarterly T-bill issuance of ₹24,000 crore weekly, and any further switch auctions to manage debt profile

The Finance Ministry, in consultation with the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), has finalized the government's market borrowing calendar for the first half of fiscal year 2026-27. The Centre will raise ₹8.20 lakh crore through dated securities during April-September.

This amount represents 51 per cent of the total gross market borrowing of ₹16.09 lakh crore for FY27. The reduced front-loading compares to the typical 60 per cent plus borrowed during the first half in normal years.

"As 10-year bond yield is over 6.9 per cent and global situation is too volatile, borrowing is less," said DK Pant, Chief Economist with India Ratings & Research.

The borrowing will be completed through 26 weekly auctions. The securities will span maturities from 3 years to 50 years, including ₹15,000 crore of sovereign green bonds (SGrBs).

The maturity-wise allocation includes: 3-year (8.1%), 5-year (15.4%), 7-year (8.1%), 10-year (29%), 15-year (14.5%), 30-year (7.3%), 40-year (8%), and 50-year (9.6%).

The gross borrowing figure was reduced to ₹16.09 lakh crore from the budgeted ₹17.2 lakh crore. This follows switch operations of government securities conducted since the Budget presentation.

For Treasury Bills, weekly borrowing in Q1 FY27 is set at ₹24,000 crore. This comprises ₹12,000 crore under 91-day T-Bills, ₹6,000 crore under 182-day T-Bills, and ₹6,000 crore under 364-day T-Bills.

The government will carry out switching and buyback of securities to smoothen the redemption profile. It retains the greenshoe option to retain additional subscription up to ₹2,000 crore per security.

The RBI has fixed the Ways and Means Advances (WMA) limit for H1 FY27 at ₹2.50 lakh crore. This facility addresses temporary mismatches in government accounts.

Net market borrowing for FY27 is pegged at ₹11.7 lakh crore. The fiscal deficit target for FY27 stands at 4.3 per cent of GDP.
