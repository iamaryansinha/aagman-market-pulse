Crude Oil Jumps 2% as Middle East Tensions Intensify Supply Fears

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Brent crude gained $2.64 (2.6%) to $104.90/barrel and WTI climbed 3% to $93.05/barrel on March 26, 2026
  • 02/Iran rejected a US proposal to end the conflict while reviewing the terms, keeping Strait of Hormuz closure risks alive
  • 03/Analysts at Macquarie and Nuvama warn Brent could surge to $110-$150/barrel if disruptions continue through April

Crude oil prices surged over 2% on Thursday as supply tightness concerns intensified. Brent crude futures gained $2.64 to settle at $104.90 per barrel while US West Texas Intermediate climbed 3% to $93.05 per barrel.

The rally comes as markets reassessed prospects for Middle East de-escalation. Iran indicated it is reviewing a US proposal delivered via Pakistan but has no plans to engage in direct talks to end the expanding conflict.

The Strait of Hormuz remains a critical flashpoint. The shipping channel typically handles approximately 20 million barrels per day, representing one-fifth of global crude and LNG supplies. The IEA has described the current disruption as the largest oil supply disruption on record.

Adding to supply pressures, approximately 40% of Russia's oil export capacity remains offline. Ukrainian drone strikes, a disputed pipeline attack, and tanker seizures have crippled key export infrastructure according to Reuters calculations.

Kuwait Petroleum Corporation announced renewed production cuts due to Hormuz transit disruptions. Daily output has been slashed from 3 million barrels to 500,000 barrels, with full capacity restoration expected to take up to four months post-conflict.

Macquarie analysts project Brent will find support in the $85-$90 range even if tensions ease. However, the bank warned that if disruptions persist through April, prices could climb to $150 per barrel. Nuvama Institutional Equities shares a similar outlook, citing continued Hormuz closure risks.
