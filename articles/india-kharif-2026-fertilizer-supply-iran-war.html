India's Kharif 2026 Under Threat: Fertilizer Supply Squeeze As Iran War Disrupts Gas

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's Kharif fertilizer supply faces reality check as US-Israel-Iran war enters fourth week with gas supply at 65% of normal capacity
  • 02/Current stock position: 53.08 lt urea, 21.80 lt DAP, 7.98 lt MOP, 48.38 lt complex; total 131.24 lt as of March 23
  • 03/Global urea prices jumped 40% in Southeast Asia; subsidy bill may exceed budgeted ₹1.71 lakh crore for FY2027

India's Kharif 2026 season faces a severe supply squeeze as the US-Israel-Iran war disrupts natural gas flows. Fertilizer Minister J P Nadda assured Parliament on March 27 that adequate reserves exist. However, industry experts warn of production constraints.

As of March 23, India holds 53.08 lakh tonnes (lt) of urea stock. DAP stocks stand at 21.80 lt, MOP at 7.98 lt, and complex fertilizers at 48.38 lt. Total fertilizer inventory is 131.24 lt.

The stock position compares to April 1, 2025 levels of 55.96 lt urea, 9.15 lt DAP, 8.83 lt MOP, and 34.04 lt complex. Urea stocks have declined 5% year-on-year despite government assurances.

India produced 24.23 lt of fertilizers during March 1-24. This includes 13.55 lt urea, 7.62 lt DAP/NPK, and 3.06 lt SSP. Production continues despite gas supply challenges.

Gas supply to urea plants currently stands at 65% of the past six-month average consumption. The government procured an additional 7.31 MMSCMD of natural gas through bidding for March 18-31. This is expected to raise availability to 80% of normal requirements.

Last Kharif season saw urea sales of 193.2 lt against estimated demand of 185.4 lt. Sales rose 4% above estimates. The demand for Kharif 2026 fertilizers is yet to be officially projected.

Anand Chandra, co-founder of Arya.ag, warned of farmgate impact. "For smallholders, even a 10-15 per cent increase in input costs can influence cropping decisions," he said. Higher costs translate to tighter input usage and increased working capital pressure.

Global urea prices have surged 40% in Southeast Asia since the conflict began. Prices jumped 20-30% in other markets. Egyptian benchmarks now exceed $625 per tonne against budget assumptions of $450 per tonne.

India imports 86% of LNG for fertilizer plants from West Asia. Domestic urea production relies heavily on imported gas. The country imports 25% of total urea consumption, 50% of DAP, and 100% of MOP.

The fertilizer subsidy for 2026-27 was budgeted at ₹1.71 lakh crore. CRISIL warns the combination of higher international prices and reduced LNG availability will push requirements above budget. A potential ₹30,000 crore overshoot is estimated.

Non-subsidized fertilizers used by progressive farmers face direct global price impact. Any diesel or power cost increases would add further burden to farm operations. The government has not yet raised fuel prices.

Chandra noted transportation costs are rising through the agri value chain. Higher input costs and uncertain demand conditions can significantly strain farmer incomes in a single season. Storage access, timely finance, and market linkages are critical for managing volatility.
