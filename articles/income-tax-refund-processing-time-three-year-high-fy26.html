Income Tax Refund Processing Time Hits Three-Year High in FY26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Average income tax refund processing time climbed to 35 days in FY26, marking a three-year high per CBDT data
  • 02/Nearly 27 lakh refund applications processed with delays beyond 90-day period due to bogus donation verifications
  • 03/Parliamentary panel recommends AI-driven risk-scoring algorithms to streamline processing and prioritise genuine taxpayers

Income tax refund processing has hit a three-year high in FY26. The average time for issuing refunds climbed to 35 days as of January 31, 2026.

The delays were caused by investigations into bogus donations and fraudulent deductions. The Central Board of Direct Taxes submitted a report to a Parliamentary panel explaining the situation.

Nearly 27 lakh income tax refund applications faced delays beyond the normative 90-day period. This represents the highest volume of delays in three years.

CBDT Chairman explained the verification exercise began in July 2025. Similar to the "Biryani case," the department verified bogus deductions claimed under Section 80G.

"The numbers that emerged were large," the Chairman stated. High-risk refunds were held back while small quantum refunds were released.

More than 50 lakh updated and revised returns were filed voluntarily. Refund claims to the extent of ₹2,000 crore were reduced through these corrections.

The Parliamentary panel recommended deploying AI-driven risk-scoring algorithms. This would separate genuine filings from suspicious ones and protect honest taxpayers.
