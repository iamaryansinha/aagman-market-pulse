Basmati Exporters Seek Relief as Shipping Lines Levy War-Related Charges

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Basmati Rice Farmers and Exporters Development Forum writes to government seeking intervention on shipping charges amid war disruptions
  • 02/Industry body seeks force majeure recognition from March 1 to waive detention, demurrage and storage charges on containers
  • 03/One large exporter charged around ₹30 lakh in detention fees alone by a global shipping major

The Basmati Rice Farmers and Exporters Development Forum has approached the Indian government for urgent intervention. The industry body is seeking relief from shipping charges imposed amid West Asia conflict disruptions.

Priyanka Mittal, chairperson of BFEDF, wrote to APEDA chairman highlighting the crisis. She described the situation as a "national trade crisis" rather than a mere commercial dispute.

Exporters are facing detention, demurrage and storage charges on stranded consignments. The charges have multiplied for containers stuck at high seas and ports.

The forum has requested force majeure recognition from March 1. This would immediately waive and suspend all charges on containers destined for affected West Asian countries.

The body has also requested prohibition of retrospective application of War Risk Surcharge. Any such surcharge should be implemented prospectively with adequate notice.

Shipping lines cannot legally impose charges for delays from their own operational decisions, the industry body contends. Exporters fulfilled contractual obligations before shipments were halted due to service suspensions.

The financial impact has been severe for MSMEs, many dependent on Gulf markets. A joint task force with government authorities, shipping lines and exporters has been suggested.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT RELIEF ARE BASMATI EXPORTERS SEEKING?
EXPORTERS ARE SEEKING FORCE MAJEURE RECOGNITION FROM MARCH 1 TO WAIVE DETENTION, DEMURRAGE AND STORAGE CHARGES ON CONTAINERS DESTINED FOR AFFECTED WEST ASIAN COUNTRIES.
WHY ARE SHIPPING CHARGES BEING IMPOSED?
SHIPPING LINES HAVE IMPOSED CHARGES ON STRANDED CONSIGNMENTS DUE TO OPERATIONAL DISRUPTIONS FROM THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT, DESPITE EXPORTERS FULFILLING CONTRACTUAL OBLIGATIONS.
WHAT IS THE FINANCIAL IMPACT ON EXPORTERS?
ONE LARGE EXPORTER WAS CHARGED AROUND ₹30 LAKH IN DETENTION FEES ALONE BY A GLOBAL SHIPPING MAJOR. MSMEs DEPENDENT ON GULF MARKETS ARE PARTICULARLY AFFECTED.
WHAT IS THE WAR RISK SURCHARGE CONCERN?
EXPORTERS REQUEST PROHIBITION OF RETROSPECTIVE APPLICATION OF WAR RISK SURCHARGE, ENSURING ANY SUCH CHARGE IS IMPLEMENTED PROSPECTIVELY WITH ADEQUATE PRIOR NOTICE.
WHAT SOLUTION HAS BEEN PROPOSED?
THE BFEDF HAS SUGGESTED FORMING A JOINT TASK FORCE COMPRISING GOVERNMENT AUTHORITIES, SHIPPING LINES AND EXPORTER BODIES TO ADDRESS THE CRISIS.
