IntrCity Targets ₹1,000 Crore Revenue, Plans 1,000-Bus Fleet Expansion

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IntrCity targets ₹1,000 crore revenue over next 2-3 years through fleet expansion and rising inter-city travel demand
  • 02/Current fleet of 700 buses to scale to 1,000 buses over 18-24 months; South India accounts for 60% of operations
  • 03/Company operates on 50:50 revenue-sharing model with partners; positions itself as alternative to AC train travel

IntrCity SmartBus, an asset-light inter-city bus platform, has set a revenue target of ₹1,000 crore over the next 2-3 years. The company plans to scale its fleet from the current 700 buses to 1,000 buses within 18-24 months.

The expansion comes as demand for long-distance road travel continues to rise across India. The company currently transports over 20,000 passengers daily with its existing fleet.

IntrCity operates on a unique asset-light model. It partners with private bus operators rather than owning vehicles directly. This structure has helped the company stay capital-efficient while scaling rapidly.

The firm follows a 50:50 revenue-sharing arrangement with operator partners. Both parties cover their respective costs before splitting earnings equally. This model aligns incentives and ensures steady revenue streams.

CEO Manish Rathi said the company benchmarks pricing against AC train fares. IntrCity typically charges ₹2-2.5 per km. A 500-km journey costs approximately ₹1,000, positioning it as a competitive alternative to air-conditioned rail travel.

South India dominates operations, accounting for nearly 60% of the company's business. Strong occupancy levels in this region are driving steady revenue growth.

Over the past decade, IntrCity has invested ₹200-300 crore in technology and customer experience. The platform offers more than 30 boarding lounges, cloud-connected buses with real-time monitoring, and a centralised command centre to track operations.
