Chilli Oleoresin Extraction Falls as Producers Shift to Turmeric on Global Curcumin Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Chilli oleoresin extraction has fallen sharply due to doubled prices and rising Chinese production competing on lower costs
  • 02/Indian producers including Synthite and Akay Naturals have shifted focus to turmeric curcumin extraction with 30-40% output increase
  • 03/Global ban on synthetic colors drives natural curcumin demand; turmeric prices sustained at Rs 125-135 per kg

Chilli oleoresin extraction in India has declined significantly as producers pivot to turmeric processing. Higher chilli prices, ample carry-over stocks, and rising Chinese production have made chilli extraction less viable.

Prices of chillies have nearly doubled over the past year. Lower crop output due to reduced acreage in Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Karnataka has tightened supply.

China has emerged as a major competitor in chilli oleoresin extraction. The country now produces at significantly lower prices, undercutting Indian processors on global markets.

"Chilli extraction is less due to carryover stock and high prices," said Emmanuel Nambusseril, Chairman of All India Spices Exporters Forum and CMO at Akay Naturals Ingredients.

Major oleoresin producers including Synthite, Plant Lipids, and Kancor have focused exclusively on turmeric this year. Processors are utilizing existing chilli stocks purchased at lower prices last season.

Curcumin production has increased by 30-40% as global demand for natural colors accelerates. Countries worldwide are banning synthetic curcumin, creating strong demand for natural alternatives.

"Curcumin demand is strong due to increased demand for natural colours and also for nutraceuticals," Nambusseril added. Turmeric farm gate prices now range Rs 125-135 per kg, up Rs 10-15 from last year.

Historically, turmeric traded below Rs 100 per kg. Prices have sustained above this level for two consecutive years now.

India's spice oils and oleoresins exports reached $535.92 million in 2024-25. Volume exports rose to 20,940 tonnes from 16,997 tonnes in the previous year.
