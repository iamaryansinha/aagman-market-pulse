LPG Shortage Dampens Tea Demand at Kochi Auctions, Prices Drop ₹3-5/kg

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/LPG shortage caused sharp decline in tea purchases at Kochi auctions as many restaurants and tea stalls remained shut
  • 02/Popular liquoring grades saw prices drop by ₹3-5 per kg while medium varieties declined by ₹2-3 per kg
  • 03/CTC dust market fell ₹5/kg with blenders absorbing only 64% of the 6.57 lakh kg on offer

The ongoing LPG shortage has severely dampened tea demand at the Kochi auction market this week. Reduced operations of hotels and roadside eateries led to a sharp decline in local buying.

Traders reported that purchases weakened significantly. Many restaurants and tea stalls remained shut, impacting overall consumption across the state.

Anil George, president of the Tea Trade Association of Cochin, confirmed the demand drop. He noted that bazaar demand declined amid LPG shortages affecting tea outlets throughout the region.

Market sentiment turned decisively bearish. Tightening demand and external disruptions are exerting clear downward pressure on prices across multiple tea grades.

At Sale 13, the CTC dust market dropped by around ₹5 per kg. Blenders absorbed only 64 per cent of the 6,57,741 kg on offer, according to Forbes, Ewart & Figgis.

Good liquoring teas saw prices fall by ₹2 to ₹3 per kg. Powdery grades declined even more sharply, despite an 88 per cent demand absorption rate.

The orthodox dust segment also declined significantly. Exporters and upcountry buyers took up only small quantities of the 10,500 kg offered at the auctions.

Export interest was limited to lower-end teas. Unrest in West Asia led to selective, subdued demand in Gulf markets, though CIS countries remained active buyers.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY HAS TEA DEMAND DECLINED AT KOCHI AUCTIONS?
THE ONGOING LPG SHORTAGE HAS FORCED MANY RESTAURANTS AND TEA STALLS TO REMAIN SHUT, LEADING TO A SHARP DECLINE IN LOCAL TEA PURCHASES AND OVERALL CONSUMPTION IN KERALA.
HOW MUCH HAVE TEA PRICES FALLEN AT THE KOCHI AUCTIONS?
POPULAR LIQUORING GRADES HAVE SEEN PRICES DROP BY ₹3-5 PER KG, WHILE MEDIUM AND PLAINER VARIETIES DECLINED BY ₹2-3 PER KG. CTC DUST MARKET DROPPED BY AROUND ₹5 PER KG.
WHAT WAS THE ABSORPTION RATE FOR CTC DUST AT SALE 13?
BLENDERS ABSORBED ONLY 64 PER CENT OF THE 6,57,741 KG OF CTC DUST ON OFFER AT SALE 13, ACCORDING TO DATA FROM FORBES, EWART & FIGGIS.
WHO CONFIRMED THE DEMAND DECLINE AT KOCHI TEA AUCTIONS?
ANIL GEORGE, PRESIDENT OF THE TEA TRADE ASSOCIATION OF COCHIN, CONFIRMED THAT BAZAAR DEMAND DECLINED AMID LPG SHORTAGES AFFECTING TEA OUTLETS ACROSS THE STATE.
HOW ARE EXPORT MARKETS RESPONDING TO THE CURRENT TEA AUCTIONS?
EXPORT INTEREST HAS BEEN LIMITED TO LOWER-END TEAS. UNREST IN WEST ASIA HAS LED TO SUBDUED DEMAND IN GULF MARKETS, WHILE CIS AND NEIGHBOURING COUNTRIES REMAINED ACTIVE IN PURCHASING ORTHODOX LEAF.
