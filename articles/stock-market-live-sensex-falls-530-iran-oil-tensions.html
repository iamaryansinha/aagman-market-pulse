Stock Market Live: Sensex Falls 530 Points as Iran Tensions, Oil Surge Weigh

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex plunges 530 points at mid-day trade, trading below psychological 75,000 level at 12 PM IST
  • 02/Nifty 50 index declines sharply as oil prices surge on escalating Iran-Israel tensions spooking investors
  • 03/All sectors in red with IT, banking, and auto stocks leading losses; global markets extend sell-off

Indian markets faced intense selling pressure at 12 PM IST on Monday. The BSE Sensex crashed 530 points, breaking below the 75,000 mark.

The NSE Nifty 50 mirrored the decline, slipping below crucial support levels. Broad-based selling dominated the session with all sectoral indices trading in negative territory.

Global cues remained weak as tensions between Iran and Israel escalated over the weekend. The uncertainty triggered a sharp surge in crude oil prices, raising inflation concerns for oil-importing nations like India.

IT stocks emerged as the biggest losers in the morning trade. Heavyweights including TCS and Infosys declined between 2-3% amid risk-off sentiment.

Banking counters extended losses with both private and public sector banks witnessing selling. The Nifty Bank index shed over 1.5%, dragging the broader market lower.

Auto stocks also faced the heat as rising oil prices raised input cost concerns. Maruti Suzuki and Tata Motors declined over 2% each in noon deals.

Foreign institutional investors continued their selling spree, adding to the downside pressure. The rupee weakened marginally against the dollar, tracking the risk-averse mood.

Analysts expect volatility to persist through the week. Traders are advised to maintain caution ahead of key economic data releases and central bank commentary.
