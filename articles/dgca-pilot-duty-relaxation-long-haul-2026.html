DGCA Temporarily Relaxes Pilot Flight Duty Limits For Long-Haul Operations

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/DGCA temporarily relaxes flight duty time limits for long-haul flights amid West Asia airspace restrictions and operational strain
  • 02/Flight time extended by 1.5 hours to 11.5 hours; duty period increased by 1.75 hours to 14.75 hours for two-pilot operations
  • 03/Move follows previous temporary exemption for Air India; regulator aims to ensure pilot availability while maintaining safety standards

India's aviation regulator DGCA has temporarily relaxed flight duty time limitation (FDTL) norms for pilots operating long-haul flights. The move comes as airlines grapple with operational disruptions caused by West Asia airspace restrictions.

The Directorate General of Civil Aviation announced the relaxation on April 7, 2026. Joint Secretary Asangba Chuba Ao said the measure aims to ensure pilot availability for smooth operations during this period of uncertainty.

Under the temporary dispensation, permissible flight time (FT) for two-pilot operations has been extended by 1 hour 30 minutes. The maximum limit now stands at 11 hours 30 minutes, up from the standard 10 hours.

The flight duty period (FDP) has been increased by 1 hour 45 minutes to 14 hours 45 minutes. This covers the entire duty period from reporting to engine shutdown at the end of the final flight.

The relaxation addresses challenges posed by mandatory rerouting of long-haul flights. Iranian and Iraqi airspace closures have forced carriers to operate via alternate corridors through Oman, Saudi Arabia and Egypt.

These longer routes have increased sector flight times significantly. Without duty limit adjustments, airlines faced potential crew shortages and schedule disruptions.

The temporary relaxation follows a similar exemption granted to Air India in March 2026. That exemption was initially valid until April 30, 2026, for flights affected by West Asia airspace restrictions.

Mitigation measures accompany the relaxation. Pilots operating under these provisions receive an additional four hours of rest beyond minimum requirements. Continuous monitoring and regulatory reviews are mandated.

The move comes months after DGCA implemented stricter FDTL norms to combat pilot fatigue. Revised rules introduced in January 2024 increased weekly rest from 36 to 48 hours and limited consecutive night duties.

Airlines faced significant challenges adapting to the new framework. IndiGo cancelled over 1,600 flights in a single day in December 2025 due to crew rostering issues under the stricter norms.

The regulator imposed a penalty of Rs 22.2 crore on IndiGo following an investigation into those disruptions. The airline had received a temporary FDTL exemption until February 10, 2026.

Pilot bodies have raised safety concerns about duty limit relaxations. The Federation of Indian Pilots warned that extending limits for two-pilot operations without crew augmentation could increase fatigue exposure.

International fatigue management guidance indicates alertness declines when duty periods approach or exceed 13 hours. Extended duty periods combined with operational workload may elevate fatigue-related risks.

Flight duty time limitations are designed to prevent pilot fatigue and maintain aviation safety standards. The temporary relaxation balances operational necessities with safety considerations during geopolitical disruptions.
