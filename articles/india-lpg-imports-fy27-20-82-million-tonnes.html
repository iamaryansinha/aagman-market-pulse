India LPG Imports FY27: 20.82 Million Tonnes Expected as Demand Rises

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India is projected to import 20.82 million tonnes of LPG in FY27, meeting over 60% of domestic consumption needs
  • 02/LPG consumption is expected to reach 34.69 mt in FY27, marking 4.5% annual growth driven by household demand
  • 03/West Asia conflict since February 28 has disrupted supplies; India diversifying sources beyond Middle East Gulf region

India is likely to import approximately 20.82 million tonnes of liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) in FY27. The Petroleum Planning & Analysis Cell (PPAC) has projected this import figure based on rising domestic demand.

LPG consumption in India is expected to reach 34.69 mt in the current financial year ending March 2027. This represents a growth of roughly 4.5 per cent on an annual basis.

India consumed 33.21 mt of LPG in FY26, marking a 6 per cent year-on-year increase. This was the highest annual growth in usage since FY19.

The country currently has around 33.37 crore households as domestic LPG consumers. This includes approximately 10.55 crore beneficiaries under the PM Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) scheme.

Around 90 per cent of India's LPG consumption comes from households for cooking purposes. This heavy residential dependence drives consistent demand growth.

India's import dependency has risen significantly over the past two years. In FY25, imports accounted for 66 per cent of consumption at 20.67 mt out of 31.33 mt total usage.

Daily consumption averages around 80,000 tonnes, with imports comprising roughly 54,000 tonnes per day. Domestic production currently stands at 46,000-47,000 tonnes daily.

The latest West Asia conflict starting February 28, 2026, has severely impacted India's LPG supplies. Approximately 90 per cent of imports originate from the Middle East Gulf region.

The Strait of Hormuz closure has forced India to diversify its import sources. The country has expanded suppliers from 10 to 15 nations including the US, Russia, Australia, and Iran.

The government has directed refiners to prioritise LPG production. It is also encouraging consumers to migrate to piped natural gas (PNG) as an alternative cooking fuel.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MUCH LPG WILL INDIA IMPORT IN FY27?
INDIA IS LIKELY TO IMPORT AROUND 20.82 MILLION TONNES OF LPG IN FY27, WHICH ACCOUNTS FOR OVER 60 PER CENT OF THE PROJECTED DOMESTIC CONSUMPTION OF 34.69 MILLION TONNES.
WHAT IS THE GROWTH RATE OF LPG CONSUMPTION IN INDIA?
LPG CONSUMPTION IS EXPECTED TO GROW BY APPROXIMATELY 4.5 PER CENT ANNUALLY IN FY27. IN FY26, CONSUMPTION ROSE BY 6 PER CENT YEAR-ON-YEAR TO 33.21 MILLION TONNES, THE HIGHEST GROWTH SINCE FY19.
WHO ARE THE MAIN LPG CONSUMERS IN INDIA?
AROUND 90 PER CENT OF LPG CONSUMPTION IS BY HOUSEHOLDS FOR COOKING. INDIA HAS APPROXIMATELY 33.37 CRORE HOUSEHOLDS AS DOMESTIC CONSUMERS, INCLUDING ROUGHLY 10.55 CRORE PM UJJWALA YOJANA (PMUY) BENEFICIARIES.
HOW HAS THE WEST ASIA CONFLICT AFFECTED LPG SUPPLIES?
THE CONFLICT STARTING FEBRUARY 28, 2026, HAS SEVERELY IMPACTED SUPPLIES AS AROUND 90 PER CENT OF INDIA'S IMPORTS COME FROM THE MIDDLE EAST GULF REGION. MOST CARGOES FROM UAE, SAUDI ARABIA, AND OMAN TRANSIT THROUGH THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ.
WHAT STEPS IS INDIA TAKING TO ENSURE LPG SECURITY?
INDIA HAS DIVERSIFIED IMPORT SOURCES FROM 10 TO 15 COUNTRIES INCLUDING THE US, RUSSIA, AUSTRALIA, AND IRAN. THE GOVERNMENT HAS DIRECTED REFINERS TO PRIORITISE LPG PRODUCTION AND IS ENCOURAGING CONSUMERS TO MIGRATE TO PIPED NATURAL GAS (PNG).
