India-US Trade Deal: US Tariffs Cut From 25% To 18%, $500 Billion Purchases Pledged

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/The US has reduced reciprocal tariffs on Indian goods from 25% to 18% effective February 7, 2026, following a framework agreement announced February 2
  • 02/India will purchase over $500 billion of US energy, technology products, coal and aircraft parts over the next five years
  • 03/The interim trade deal positions India to regain competitiveness in gems, jewellery, textiles and pharmaceutical exports to the US market

The United States and India announced a historic interim trade agreement on February 2, 2026. The deal reduces US reciprocal tariffs on Indian imports from 25% to 18% effective February 7, 2026.

President Donald Trump signed an Executive Order removing the additional 25% tariff on India. The move recognizes India's commitment to stop purchasing Russian Federation oil.

Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal hailed the agreement as a catalyst for unprecedented opportunities. He noted benefits for farmers, MSMEs, entrepreneurs and skilled workers across multiple sectors.

The gem and jewellery sector stands to gain significantly from the tariff relief. US tariffs on Indian gems and jewellery are restored at 18%, with zero duty on diamonds and coloured gemstones.

The Gems and Jewellery Export Promotion Council projects exports could increase by up to $3 billion. This follows a 45% decline in shipments to the US during April 2025 to January 2026 due to elevated tariffs.

India has committed to purchase over $500 billion of US products over five years. The shopping list includes energy, information and communication technology, coal, aircraft and precious metals.

New Delhi will also eliminate or reduce tariffs on all US industrial goods. Agricultural products including dried distillers grains, red sorghum, tree nuts, fruits, soybean oil, wine and spirits will see lower duties.

Bilateral trade between the two countries currently stands at a record $190 billion annually. The agreement is expected to unlock stalled supply contracts and accelerate new investment commitments.

India previously maintained some of the highest tariffs on US goods among major economies. Agricultural tariffs averaged 37%, with certain auto tariffs exceeding 100%.

The two nations will now work toward finalizing a broader Bilateral Trade Agreement. Negotiations will address remaining tariff barriers, digital trade rules, intellectual property and services trade.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE KEY TARIFF CHANGE IN THE INDIA-US TRADE DEAL?
THE UNITED STATES HAS LOWERED THE RECIPROCAL TARIFF ON INDIAN GOODS FROM 25% TO 18%, EFFECTIVE FEBRUARY 7, 2026. THIS REMOVES THE ADDITIONAL 25% TARIFF IMPOSED ON INDIA.
HOW MUCH WILL INDIA PURCHASE FROM THE US UNDER THIS AGREEMENT?
INDIA HAS COMMITTED TO PURCHASE OVER $500 BILLION OF US ENERGY, INFORMATION AND COMMUNICATION TECHNOLOGY PRODUCTS, COAL, AIRCRAFT PARTS AND PRECIOUS METALS OVER THE NEXT FIVE YEARS.
WHICH INDIAN SECTORS WILL BENEFIT MOST FROM THE TARIFF RELIEF?
GEMS AND JEWELLERY, TEXTILES, ENGINEERING GOODS, PHARMACEUTICALS AND AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS WILL SEE IMMEDIATE BENEFITS. THE GEM AND JEWELLERY SECTOR ALONE COULD GAIN UP TO $3 BILLION IN ADDITIONAL EXPORTS.
WHAT HAS INDIA AGREED TO IN RETURN FOR THE TARIFF REDUCTIONS?
INDIA HAS COMMITTED TO STOP PURCHASING RUSSIAN FEDERATION OIL, ELIMINATE OR REDUCE TARIFFS ON US INDUSTRIAL GOODS AND AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTS, AND ADDRESS NON-TARIFF BARRIERS AFFECTING BILATERAL TRADE.
WHAT IS THE CURRENT STATUS OF INDIA-US BILATERAL TRADE?
BILATERAL TRADE BETWEEN INDIA AND THE US CURRENTLY STANDS AT A RECORD $190 BILLION ANNUALLY. THE TWO NATIONS ARE WORKING TOWARD A BROADER BILATERAL TRADE AGREEMENT TO LOCK IN LONG-TERM BENEFITS.
