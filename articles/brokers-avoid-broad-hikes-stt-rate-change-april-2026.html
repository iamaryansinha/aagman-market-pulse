Brokers Avoid Broad Hikes As STT Rate Change Nears From April 1

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/STT on derivatives increases from April 1: options rise to 0.1% from 0.0625%, futures to 0.02% from 0.0125%
  • 02/Zerodha limits brokerage hike to ₹20 per order only for pledged collateral users with over ₹5 lakh shortfall, impacting under 1% of clients
  • 03/Kotak Securities and Choice Broking keep pricing unchanged; ANMI has urged Finance Ministry to roll back STT hike

Securities Transaction Tax on derivatives is set to rise from April 1, 2026. The Union Budget proposed higher rates for both futures and options segments.

STT on options will jump to 0.1 per cent from 0.0625 per cent. Futures STT will increase to 0.02 per cent from 0.0125 per cent.

Most brokerages are avoiding broad-based hikes to protect trading volumes. They are opting for selective changes instead.

Zerodha has scaled back its proposed brokerage increase. The additional ₹20 per order applies only to specific clients.

These are traders using pledged collateral without maintaining at least 50 per cent margin in cash or cash equivalents. The shortfall must also exceed ₹5 lakh.

Zerodha stated this will impact less than 1 per cent of its active derivatives client base. Earlier plans to double brokerage for all such clients had triggered backlash.

Kotak Securities and Choice Broking have not altered their charges. Sandip Chordia, COO at Kotak Securities, confirmed pricing remains unchanged.

Kotak continues to charge a flat ₹10 per order in F&O trades. This applies to both intraday and carry forward transactions.

Angel One had already reset its pricing structure last year. It increased minimum charge per order to ₹5 from ₹2 in the cash segment.

Brokers face higher funding requirements due to SEBI margin rules. These mandate at least 50 per cent of collateral in cash or equivalents.

Industry body ANMI has urged the Finance Ministry to roll back the STT hike. They cite concerns about higher transaction costs impacting liquidity and retail participation.

Shrey Jain, CEO of Stocko by InCred Money, said the hike will substantially increase costs for retail and high-frequency traders. Transaction cost changes may influence certain trading strategies.

WHAT IS THE NEW STT RATE ON OPTIONS FROM APRIL 1, 2026?
THE SECURITIES TRANSACTION TAX ON SALE OF OPTIONS WILL RISE TO 0.1 PER CENT FROM THE CURRENT 0.0625 PER CENT, REPRESENTING A 60 PER CENT INCREASE IN TAX RATE.
HOW MUCH WILL STT ON FUTURES INCREASE?
STT ON FUTURES WILL INCREASE TO 0.02 PER CENT FROM 0.0125 PER CENT, EFFECTIVE APRIL 1, 2026.
WHAT IS ZERODHA'S NEW BROKERAGE POLICY?
ZERODHA WILL CHARGE AN ADDITIONAL ₹20 PER ORDER ONLY TO TRADERS USING PLEDGED COLLATERAL WITHOUT MAINTAINING AT LEAST 50 PER CENT OF MARGIN IN CASH OR CASH EQUIVALENTS, AND ONLY WHERE THE SHORTFALL EXCEEDS ₹5 LAKH.
HOW MANY ZERODHA CLIENTS WILL BE AFFECTED BY THE BROKERAGE HIKE?
ZERODHA STATED THE REVISED CHARGES WILL IMPACT LESS THAN 1 PER CENT OF ITS ACTIVE DERIVATIVES CLIENT BASE.
WHAT ARE KOTAK SECURITIES' BROKERAGE CHARGES FOR F&O TRADES?
KOTAK SECURITIES CONTINUES TO CHARGE A FLAT ₹10 PER ORDER IN F&O TRADES FOR BOTH INTRADAY AND CARRY FORWARD TRANSACTIONS, WITH NO PLANS TO INCREASE RATES.
