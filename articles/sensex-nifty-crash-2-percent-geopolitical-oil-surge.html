Sensex Nosedives 2,700 Points On Open, Nifty Below 24,700 As Oil Surges On Middle East War

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex crashed over 2,700 points at market open and closed 1,000 points lower at 80,239 as geopolitical tensions spooked investors
  • 02/Nifty 50 plunged below 24,700 mark, ending at 24,866; total market cap loss exceeded ₹6.5 lakh crore on BSE
  • 03/Brent crude surged 6% to $77.08 per barrel with Barclays forecasting $100; Strait of Hormuz risks threaten supply

Indian stock markets crashed over 2% on Monday as escalating Middle East tensions triggered panic selling. The BSE Sensex nosedived 2,743 points at the open before recovering slightly to close 1,048 points lower at 80,239.

The Nifty 50 index fell 519 points at the open and ended the session down 313 points at 24,866. Both benchmarks hit one-month lows. The sharp decline wiped out over ₹6.5 lakh crore in total market capitalisation.

The selloff was driven by the weekend death of Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei in alleged US-Israel missile strikes. Retaliatory attacks by Iran across the Middle East raised fears of a wider regional conflict.

Crude oil prices surged as traders priced in supply disruption risks. Brent crude jumped 6% to $77.08 per barrel while WTI crude rose 5.5% to $70.71. Over 20% of global oil passes through the Strait of Hormuz near the conflict zone.

Barclays raised its Brent crude forecast to $100 per barrel. "Oil markets might have to face their worst fears," the bank said, citing potential supply disruption amid the spiraling security situation.

The Indian rupee weakened sharply, crossing the key 91 mark against the US dollar for the first time in a month. The currency closed at 91.47, down 0.5% in its steepest drop in over a month.

Foreign institutional investors remained net sellers, offloading ₹7,536 crore on Friday. Domestic institutional investors bought ₹12,293 crore, cushioning the fall somewhat.

Nifty Auto was the top sectoral loser, falling 2.2%. Nifty Consumer Durables and Oil & Gas also declined over 2%. Defence stocks bucked the trend with Bharat Electronics gaining over 2%.

VK Vijayakumar, Chief Investment Strategist at Geojit Investments, said uncertainty related to the West Asia war will loom large over markets. Kranthi Bathini of Wealth Mills Securities warned the expanding tensions will bear negative impact in the short-to-medium term.
