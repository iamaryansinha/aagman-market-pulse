Gold Set for Worst Month in Over 17 Years as Rate-Cut Hopes Crumble

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold is on track for its worst monthly performance since October 2008, falling over 13% in March 2026
  • 02/The crash is driven by a stronger US dollar and traders pricing out virtually all Federal Reserve rate cuts for the year
  • 03/Despite Tuesday's 1.1% bounce to $4,561.68 per ounce, bullion faces mounting pressure from persistent inflation and geopolitical uncertainty

Gold is poised for its worst month in more than 17 years, with prices crashing over 13% in March 2026. The decline marks the steepest monthly drop since October 2008.

Spot gold rose 1.1% to $4,561.68 per ounce on Tuesday. US gold futures for April delivery gained 0.7% to $4,590. The modest bounce came as the dollar eased slightly, making greenback-denominated commodities more affordable for foreign buyers.

The uptick followed news that US President Donald Trump told aides he is willing to end the military campaign against Iran. This triggered a risk-on response across financial markets, according to Ilya Spivak, head of global macro at Tastylive.

Gold has been stabilizing for about a week. A rally last Friday stood out, coinciding with a drop in Treasury yields that suggested markets are viewing the Iran conflict as a recession risk.

Despite the recent bounce, the monthly damage is severe. Bullion's 13% plus decline this month puts it on track for the steepest fall since October 2008. Prices remain up about 5% for the quarter, but the monthly slide reflects shifting expectations around Federal Reserve policy.

Traders have almost completely priced out any chance of a Fed rate cut this year. Higher energy prices are threatening to feed into broader inflation, eliminating the monetary easing that gold investors had anticipated.

Before the Middle East conflict erupted, markets expected two Fed rate cuts in 2026, according to CME Group's FedWatch tool. Those expectations have evaporated.

Gold tends to thrive in low-interest-rate environments because it is a non-yielding asset. When rates stay elevated, the opportunity cost of holding gold increases, pressuring prices.

Goldman Sachs maintains a bullish long-term outlook despite the monthly rout. The bank still expects gold to reach $5,400 per ounce by end-2026, citing central bank diversification and eventual Fed easing.

Other precious metals showed strength on Tuesday. Spot silver jumped 2.9% to $72.04 per ounce. Spot platinum gained 0.6% to $1,911.15. Palladium rose 2% to $1,434.23.
