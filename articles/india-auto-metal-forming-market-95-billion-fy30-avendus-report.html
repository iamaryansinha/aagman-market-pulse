India's Auto Metal Forming Market to Hit $95 Billion by FY30: Avendus Report

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's automotive metal forming market projected to reach $90-95 billion by FY30, growing at 12% CAGR
  • 02/Industry surpassed $80 billion in FY25 and transitioned from net importer to net exporter status
  • 03/Exports hit $23 billion in FY25; India emerging as preferred manufacturing destination amid global supply chain shifts

India's automotive metal forming market is expected to reach $90-95 billion by FY30. The sector is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12 per cent.

The industry has already crossed a critical threshold. It surpassed $80 billion in size in FY25.

A key milestone was achieved recently. The sector transitioned from being a net importer to becoming a net exporter.

Exports from the industry stood at approximately $23 billion in FY25. This reflects structural upgrade in manufacturing complexity and quality standards.

The growth is driven by three key factors. Cost competitiveness, engineering talent, and an established supplier ecosystem are powering the expansion.

The industry involves critical manufacturing processes. These include casting, forming, stamping, and machining for automotive applications.

Core metal forming processes remain indispensable across vehicle platforms. Both internal combustion engine (ICE) and electric vehicle platforms require these components.

Demand is increasing for precision, light-weighting, and complexity in metal components. This is creating new opportunities for Indian manufacturers.

A structural shift is underway in global automotive supply chains. Value is concentrating toward process-led manufacturing segments, particularly metal forming.

India is emerging as a preferred manufacturing destination. Geopolitical uncertainties and cost pressures are driving OEMs and Tier-1 suppliers to diversify sourcing bases.

"The Indian auto component sector is moving beyond a scale-driven growth phase into a capability-led consolidation cycle," said Koushik Bhattacharyya, Managing Director and Head of Industrials Investment Banking at Avendus Capital.

"We are seeing a clear shift in value toward process specialists, where deep manufacturing expertise and operational precision are becoming the key differentiators," Bhattacharyya added.

India is also gaining a "last man standing" advantage in ICE components. Global suppliers are reallocating capital toward electrification and reducing investments in ICE-related manufacturing.

This creates a widening supply gap in critical drivetrain components. India is well-positioned to capture opportunities in crankshafts, gears, and axles.

Deal activity in the sector is accelerating. Both global private equity firms and strategic acquirers are showing increasing interest in process-led businesses.
