IndianOil Reassures Public of Stable LPG Supply Amid West Asia Crisis

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • •• IndianOil has assured consumers of stable LPG supply across the country
  • •• The statement comes amid concerns over fuel shortages due to West Asia conflict
  • •• The company says all outlets are fully stocked and operating normally
  • •• India has 50-60 days of fuel cover with no rationing planned

Indian Oil Corporation (IndianOil) has reassured the public that LPG supply remains stable across the country, dismissing rumours of shortages amid the ongoing West Asia conflict. The state-run oil marketing company stated that all its outlets are fully stocked and operating normally. [FACT: IndianOil reassures public of stable LPG supply amid West Asia crisis]

The reassurance comes after social media rumours about potential fuel shortages caused panic in some areas. IndianOil, along with other oil marketing companies Bharat Petroleum and Hindustan Petroleum, issued statements urging citizens not to engage in panic buying. [FACT: IndianOil says outlets are fully stocked and operating normally]

Government sources have indicated that India has 50-60 days of fuel cover and no rationing or price hikes are planned. With 70% of crude oil now sourced from suppliers outside the Strait of Hormuz, India is better positioned than many countries to manage the supply disruption.

The LPG supply chain has faced some pressure due to the West Asia conflict affecting shipping routes, but the government has directed oil companies and refineries to boost production. Domestic LPG production has reportedly increased by 10% in recent days to meet demand.

For piped gas consumers, utility companies have stated that domestic PNG supply is being prioritised with government support. IndianOil's commitment to maintaining uninterrupted supply reflects the strategic importance of cooking fuel for millions of Indian households.
