ATR Eyes India Expansion: Airbus Evaluates Final Assembly Line to Tap Regional Aviation Growth

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/ATR, jointly owned by Airbus and Leonardo, is exploring a final assembly line in India to reinforce its market presence and expand capacity
  • 02/India will need 210 new turboprops by 2044 as UDAN expansion could add 35 million annual passengers on regional routes
  • 03/Airbus India President Juergen Westermeier met Aviation Minister Ram Mohan Naidu to discuss local manufacturing under Make in India

ATR, the Franco-Italian regional aircraft manufacturer, is actively evaluating options to reinforce its India presence. The company is exploring a final assembly line in the country to meet growing regional aviation demand.

Airbus, which co-owns ATR with Italy's Leonardo, has held discussions with Indian stakeholders. The talks focus on establishing a final assembly line for ATR turboprop aircraft.

IndiGo currently operates approximately 50 ATR 72-600 aircraft in India. Regional carrier FLY91 operates six ATR aircraft, with plans to double its fleet in the near term.

India's aviation ministry has approved a revamped Regional Connectivity Scheme worth Rs 28,840 crore over 10 years. The scheme targets upgrading 100 airstrips into functional airports.

ATR's latest market forecast anticipates demand for 210 new turboprops in India by 2044. The company estimates that 35 million of 90 million additional annual passengers could only be served through regional expansion.

Airbus India and South Asia President Juergen Westermeier recently met Union Aviation Minister Ram Mohan Naidu. Discussions centered on strengthening local manufacturing and enhancing component sourcing from India.

Airbus already operates two final assembly lines in India with Tata Advanced Systems. The Vadodara facility produces C295 military transport aircraft, while the Karnataka plant assembles H125 commercial helicopters.

Embraer has announced competing plans to establish an FAL in India with the Adani Group. The Brazilian manufacturer seeks a firm order for 200 aircraft to proceed.

The ATR 72-600 seats up to 78 passengers and emits 45% less CO2 than similar-size regional jets. ATR delivered 35 aircraft in 2024 and secured 56 orders, marking a 40% jump from 2023.

Airbus established its H125 helicopter FAL in Karnataka without prior domestic orders. Sources indicate the ATR FAL announcement would follow the same unconditional approach.
