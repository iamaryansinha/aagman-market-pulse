Govt to Release ₹17,500 Crore LPG Compensation to OMCs in FY26

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government will disburse ₹17,500 crore compensation to OMCs for LPG under-recoveries in FY26, part of a ₹30,000 crore package announced in August 2025
  • 02/The remaining ₹17,500 crore will be released in seven tranches during FY27, completing the one-time compensation scheme
  • 03/IOCL, BPCL, and HPCL are the primary beneficiaries; HPCL has received ₹1,320 crore and BPCL ₹1,270 crore so far in FY26

The government will release the remaining ₹17,500 crore compensation to oil marketing companies (OMCs) for LPG under-recoveries in FY26. This is part of a ₹30,000 crore one-time package announced in August 2025.

The total compensation of ₹30,000 crore is being paid in instalments extending up to FY27. The first tranche of ₹12,500 crore was allocated for FY26, with the remaining ₹17,500 crore scheduled for FY27.

The three major OMCs – Indian Oil Corporation (IOCL), Bharat Petroleum Corporation (BPCL), and Hindustan Petroleum Corporation (HPCL) – are the primary beneficiaries of this scheme.

LPG under-recoveries for the three OMCs combined ballooned to ₹41,000 crore in FY25. This significantly impacted their profitability during the period.

In FY26, HPCL has already received ₹1,320 crore while BPCL got ₹1,270 crore as part of the compensation package. The remaining amount will be recognized in Q4 FY26.

The compensation is expected to boost Q4 FY26 earnings for all three OMCs. Analysts view this as a major positive development for the sector.

Jefferies noted that the government approval of this compensation package provides much-needed relief to OMCs who were bearing the burden of subsidized LPG prices.
