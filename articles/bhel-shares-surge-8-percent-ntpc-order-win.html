BHEL Shares Surge 8% on Major NTPC Order Win, Order Book Strengthens

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/BHEL shares surged nearly 8% to hit a fresh 52-week high of ₹130.55 on the NSE following major order wins from NTPC
  • 02/The state-run PSU secured a ₹4,000 crore order from Adani Power for 2x800 MW Raigarh Phase-II thermal power plant in Chhattisgarh
  • 03/BHEL's order book stands at over ₹2,22,800 crore as of December 2025, with power sector contributing 80%

Bharat Heavy Electricals Ltd (NSE: BHEL) shares surged nearly 8% to hit a fresh 52-week high of ₹130.55 on September 1, 2023. The rally was fueled by investor optimism following improving order inflows.

The stock has gained over 26% in just five trading sessions. Strong volumes accompanied the price action, with over 4 crore shares traded on the NSE.

BHEL received a ₹4,000 crore order from Adani Power Ltd in March 2024. The Letter of Award was received on March 27, 2024, for setting up the 2x800 MW Raigarh Phase-II thermal power plant in Chhattisgarh.

The order includes supply of equipment and supervision of erection and commissioning. This marks a significant win for the state-owned engineering major in the private power sector.

Following the announcement, BHEL shares gained as much as 4.36% to hit ₹253.7 on the NSE. The stock closed at ₹253.3 on BSE, reflecting strong investor confidence.

BHEL has been on an order bagging spree recently. In August 2023, the company won a ₹2,242 crore order from NHPC Ltd for 12×240 MW electro-mechanical equipment.

The company's cumulative order inflows during the first nine months of FY26 surpassed ₹45,900 crore. The power sector dominated with 76% share of these inflows.

As of December 31, 2025, BHEL's order book stood at over ₹2,22,800 crore. This represents a 39% increase compared to ₹1,60,157 crore in December 2024.

Analysts at Nuvama Research estimate fresh thermal ordering at ~30GW over the next four to five years. BHEL could bag 50% of these orders, implying ~3GW per year of thermal order inflows.

The stock has delivered 117% returns over the past 12 months. It outperformed the Nifty50 by nearly 12-fold during this period.
