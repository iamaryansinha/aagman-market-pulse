Mann Ki Baat: Modi Credits 10 Years Of India's Global Diplomacy, Economic Strength

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/PM Modi's Mann Ki Baat radio program completed 10 years on March 30, 2026, with the 115th episode highlighting India's global diplomatic achievements
  • 02/India attracted $667.41 billion in FDI between April 2014-March 2024, representing 67% of total FDI received over the last 24 years
  • 03/Key initiatives include G20 presidency, Global Biofuels Alliance, International Solar Alliance, and 39 new embassies opened since 2014

Prime Minister Narendra Modi addressed the nation in the 115th episode of Mann Ki Baat on March 30, 2026. The monthly radio program completed 10 years of continuous broadcast since its launch on October 3, 2014.

PM Modi credited listeners as the "real anchors" of the program. He highlighted India's transformation into a confident global power over the past decade.

India's G20 presidency from December 1, 2022, to November 30, 2023, marked a defining chapter in global diplomacy. The country hosted over 200 meetings across 60 cities with more than one lakh delegates participating.

The New Delhi Leaders' Declaration was adopted unanimously despite deep global divides. This achievement underscored India's growing stature as a consensus-builder on the world stage.

The Global Biofuels Alliance was launched during India's G20 presidency. The International Solar Alliance, co-founded by India, has emerged as a key platform for clean energy cooperation.

Between April 2014 and March 2024, India attracted $667.41 billion in foreign direct investment. This represents nearly 67% of the total FDI received over the last 24 years.

The Make in India initiative has generated Rs 12.50 lakh crore in production and Rs 4 lakh crore in exports. It has created 9.5 lakh jobs while receiving Rs 1.46 lakh crore in investments by August 2024.

India opened 39 new embassies and consulates between 2014 and 2024. The total number of Indian diplomatic missions now stands at 219, expanding the country's global footprint.

The India-Middle East-Europe Economic Corridor agreement was concluded during the G20 Summit. The Act East Policy, upgraded in 2014, has strengthened ties with ASEAN and the Indo-Pacific region.

India launched the MAHASAGAR doctrine in Mauritius in March 2025. This reinforces the goal of Mutual and Holistic Advancement for Security and Growth Across Regions.
