Sensex Falls 1,200 Points, Nifty Near 22,950 on Iran Tensions and Global Weakness

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex crashed 1,199.25 points (1.59%) to close at 74,074.20 while Nifty 50 dropped 348.45 points (1.50%) to 22,958.00
  • 02/Indian markets opened sharply lower after a holiday, erasing all gains from Wednesday's 1,205-point Sensex rally
  • 03/Traders eye 22,950–22,900 as critical Nifty support zone; rupee hit record low past 94 per dollar

Indian equity markets suffered heavy losses on Friday, March 27, 2026.

The BSE Sensex plummeted 1,199.25 points or 1.59% to settle at 74,074.20. The NSE Nifty 50 tumbled 348.45 points or 1.50% to close at 22,958.00.

Markets opened sharply lower following the Ram Navami holiday. At 9:19 am, Sensex had already dropped 828.66 points to 74,444.79. By 11 am, losses extended to 1,073.13 points.

Weak global cues and escalating Iran tensions drove the sell-off. Asian markets including Japan's Nikkei and South Korea's Kospi posted sharp losses.

Sentiment worsened after Goldman Sachs downgraded Indian equities and cut its Nifty target. The downgrade added to existing geopolitical worries.

The rupee breached the critical 94 per dollar mark for the first time ever. Crude oil prices remained elevated with June Brent futures trading near $101 per barrel.

The government announced excise duty cuts on auto fuels to cushion the impact. Petrol duty was slashed from ₹13 to ₹3 per litre. Diesel duty was reduced from ₹10 to nil.

However, the fuel tax relief provided only limited support. The estimated fiscal hit is approximately ₹1.55 lakh crore annually.

Market breadth remained weak throughout the session. The advances-to-declines ratio showed strong selling pressure across sectors.

FII data from March 25 showed net selling of ₹1,508.89 crore. DIIs absorbed the selling with net purchases of ₹5,173.01 crore.

Top Nifty gainers included TCS (+1.58%), HCL Tech (+1.43%), and Infosys (+1.04%). Shriram Finance (-2.81%), Eternal (-2.74%), and Bajaj Finance (-2.07%) led the losers.

Analysts say Nifty faces a make-or-break test at 22,950–22,900 support. A break below this zone could trigger a sharper fall towards 22,600.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MUCH DID THE SENSEX AND NIFTY FALL ON MARCH 27, 2026?
THE BSE SENSEX FELL 1,199.25 POINTS (1.59%) TO CLOSE AT 74,074.20. THE NSE NIFTY 50 DROPPED 348.45 POINTS (1.50%) TO SETTLE AT 22,958.00.
WHAT CAUSED THE INDIAN STOCK MARKET CRASH TODAY?
THE SELL-OFF WAS DRIVEN BY WEAK GLOBAL CUES, ESCALATING GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS SURROUNDING THE IRAN CONFLICT, AND A DOWNGRADE OF INDIAN EQUITIES BY GOLDMAN SACHS. ASIAN MARKETS INCLUDING JAPAN'S NIKKEI AND SOUTH KOREA'S KOSPI ALSO POSTED SHARP LOSSES.
WHAT IS THE CRITICAL SUPPORT LEVEL FOR NIFTY 50?
ANALYSTS IDENTIFY THE 22,950–22,900 ZONE AS CRITICAL SUPPORT FOR NIFTY. A HOLD AND BOUNCE COULD TRIGGER RECOVERY TOWARDS 23,200+, WHILE A BREAK BELOW 22,900 MAY LEAD TO A SHARPER FALL TOWARDS 22,600.
WHAT FUEL PRICE RELIEF DID THE GOVERNMENT ANNOUNCE?
THE GOVERNMENT CUT SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY ON PETROL FROM ₹13 PER LITRE TO ₹3 PER LITRE. THE LEVY ON DIESEL WAS REDUCED FROM ₹10 PER LITRE TO NIL. HOWEVER, RETAIL PRICES REMAINED UNCHANGED AS OIL MARKETING COMPANIES ABSORBED THE COST.
WHAT IS HAPPENING WITH THE RUPEE AND CRUDE OIL?
THE INDIAN RUPEE HIT A RECORD LOW, BREACHING 94 PER US DOLLAR FOR THE FIRST TIME EVER AMID RISING ENERGY RISKS. CRUDE OIL PRICES REMAINED VOLATILE WITH BRENT FUTURES TRADING NEAR $101 PER BARREL AS TRUMP EXTENDED THE IRAN STRIKE DEADLINE TO APRIL 6.
