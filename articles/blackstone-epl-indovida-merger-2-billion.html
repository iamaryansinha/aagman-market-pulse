Blackstone EPL, Indovida to Merge in $2 Billion Packaging Deal

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/EPL and Indovida sign definitive merger agreement to create a $2 billion packaging platform with combined annual revenue of $1 billion
  • 02/Indorama Ventures will hold 51.8% stake as co-promoter; Blackstone retains ~16.6% in the merged entity
  • 03/Deal values EPL shares at ₹339, representing a 70% premium over recent closing price; transaction expected to close in 12 months

EPL Limited and Indovida India Private Limited have signed definitive merger agreements. The combined entity will be valued at approximately $2 billion with annual revenue of $1 billion.

The merger combines EPL's flexible packaging expertise with Indovida's rigid PET packaging capabilities. This creates a multi-format platform serving global and regional customers across high-growth emerging markets.

EPL is valued at approximately $1.2 billion under the deal. Indovida carries a valuation of roughly $700 million.

The transaction values EPL shares at ₹339 each. This represents a sharp 70% premium over the stock's recent closing price.

Indorama Ventures will emerge as co-promoter with a 51.8% stake in the merged company. Blackstone will hold approximately 16.6% following the merger.

The leadership structure remains largely unchanged. Hemant Bakshi will continue as Group CEO, while Sunil Marwah will head the Indovida business and report to Bakshi.

The merger aligns with Indorama Ventures' IVL 2.0 strategy to expand its India presence. It leverages the country's structural growth opportunity through an integrated packaging platform with public markets access.

Nearly three-fourths of the new entity's business will come from emerging markets. The combined operations are expected to deliver procurement savings and supply chain efficiencies.

The merged company's operating margins are projected to rise. Returns on capital are also expected to improve post-integration.

The transaction will be implemented through a scheme of amalgamation. Indovida will merge with EPL, which will continue as the listed entity.

The deal is subject to shareholder, regulatory and court approvals. Goldman Sachs is acting as financial advisor to the transaction.

BDO and Duff & Phelps have provided the joint valuation report. EY has provided fairness opinion on the swap ratio.

In May 2025, Indorama Ventures acquired a 24.9% stake in EPL. This merger represents the next phase of their strategic partnership.
