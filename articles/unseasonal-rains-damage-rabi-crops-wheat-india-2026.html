Unseasonal Rains Damage 2.49 Lakh Ha Rabi Crops, Wheat Hit Hardest

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Unseasonal rains and hailstorms have damaged rabi crops across 2.49 lakh hectares till April 8, with wheat bearing the maximum impact
  • 02/Horticulture crops including mango and litchi also severely affected; IMD forecasts more rainfall from April 9-15 due to Western Disturbances
  • 03/Government hikes nutrient-based subsidy to Rs 41,534 crore for Kharif 2026 to ensure smooth supply ahead of sowing season

Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan announced on April 10 that unseasonal rains and hailstorms have damaged standing rabi crops across 2.49 lakh hectares. Wheat has borne the brunt of the damage.

The reported damage covers data till April 8. Horticulture crops including mango and litchi have also been severely affected. Three departments are currently conducting detailed surveys.

"The Modi government stands firmly with farmers in this crisis," Chouhan assured reporters ahead of the Unnat Krishi Mela launch in Madhya Pradesh.

The India Meteorological Department reported heavy rain across northeast, central, south and northwest India from April 2-8. Hailstorms hit 13 states including Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Punjab, and Haryana.

IMD forecasts more rain from April 9-15 due to Western Disturbances over Jammu and Kashmir. Cyclonic circulations over Uttar Pradesh, Bangladesh, Assam and Odisha will add to the disruption.

Central teams have been sent to Rajasthan and Haryana to assess crop damage. State governments have requested relaxation in quality norms for wheat procurement.

The government targets to buy 303.36 lakh tonnes of wheat for Central Pool stock by June 30. Quality degradation of up to 30% has been reported in affected areas.

India has estimated wheat production at a record 120.21 million tonnes this year. However, Agriwatch estimates a 5-10% production drop due to unseasonal weather.

HOW MUCH RABI CROP AREA HAS BEEN DAMAGED BY UNSEASONAL RAINS?
UNSEASONAL RAINS AND HAILSTORMS HAVE DAMAGED STANDING RABI CROPS ACROSS 2.49 LAKH HECTARES AS OF APRIL 8, 2026. THREE DEPARTMENTS ARE CONDUCTING ONGOING SURVEYS AND THE FIGURE MAY REVISE UPWARDS.
WHICH CROPS ARE MOST AFFECTED BY THE UNSEASONAL RAINS?
WHEAT HAS BORNE THE MAXIMUM BRUNT OF THE DAMAGE. HORTICULTURE CROPS INCLUDING MANGO AND LITCHI HAVE ALSO BEEN SEVERELY AFFECTED BY THE UNSEASONAL RAINS AND HAILSTORMS.
WHICH STATES HAVE BEEN HIT BY THE HAILSTORMS AND HEAVY RAINS?
HAILSTORMS HAVE HIT 13 STATES INCLUDING MADHYA PRADESH, RAJASTHAN, HIMACHAL PRADESH, HARYANA, UTTAR PRADESH, UTTARAKHAND, PUNJAB, TELANGANA, JAMMU AND KASHMIR, ODISHA, JHARKHAND, WEST BENGAL AND ASSAM-MEGHALAYA.
WHAT IS THE WEATHER FORECAST FOR THE COMING WEEK?
THE INDIA METEOROLOGICAL DEPARTMENT FORECASTS MORE RAIN FROM APRIL 9-15 DUE TO WESTERN DISTURBANCES OVER JAMMU AND KASHMIR. CYCLONIC CIRCULATIONS OVER UTTAR PRADESH, BANGLADESH, ASSAM AND ODISHA WILL BRING ADDITIONAL RAINFALL.
WHAT MEASURES IS THE GOVERNMENT TAKING TO SUPPORT FARMERS?
THE GOVERNMENT HAS SENT CENTRAL TEAMS TO RAJASTHAN AND HARYANA TO ASSESS DAMAGE. THE NUTRIENT-BASED SUBSIDY FOR PHOSPHATIC AND POTASSIC FERTILISERS HAS BEEN HIKED TO RS 41,534 CRORE FOR KHARIF 2026. QUALITY NORM RELAXATIONS FOR WHEAT PROCUREMENT ARE ALSO BEING CONSIDERED.
