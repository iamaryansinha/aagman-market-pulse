Sensex Crashes 1,056 Points, Nifty Down 300+ as FIIs Exit ₹1.14L Cr in March

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex crashes 1,056 points and Nifty falls over 300 points as markets open deep in red
  • 02/FIIs have dumped ₹1.14 lakh crore worth of Indian equities in March 2026 amid geopolitical tensions
  • 03/Brent crude surges past $108 per barrel as war escalates in West Asia region

Indian equity markets opened in deep red on March 30, 2026. The Sensex crashed 1,056 points while the Nifty slipped over 300 points.

The sharp decline comes as geopolitical tensions escalate in West Asia. Brent crude oil prices have surged above $108 per barrel.

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) have been relentless sellers. They have dumped ₹1.14 lakh crore worth of Indian equities in March alone.

The sustained FII outflows reflect risk-off sentiment among global investors. Emerging markets like India are bearing the brunt of the flight to safety.

Rising crude prices pose a twin threat to India. Higher oil imports widen the current account deficit and fuel inflationary pressures.

Market volatility is expected to persist until geopolitical clarity emerges. Investors are advised to exercise caution and focus on fundamentally strong stocks.
