8.45 Lakh Government Jobs Vacant: One in Five Central Posts Lie Empty Despite Hiring Push

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/8.45 lakh sanctioned civilian posts lie vacant in central government as of March 2024
  • 02/Group B non-gazetted positions worst hit at 31% vacancy rate
  • 03/Vacancies lowest in five years but gap still impacts service delivery

India's central government is grappling with a massive staffing crisis. Over 8.45 lakh sanctioned civilian positions remain unfilled, representing roughly 20% of all authorized posts across ministries and departments.

The latest Annual Report on Pay and Allowances from the Finance Ministry's Expenditure Department paints a stark picture. While sanctioned posts stand at over 40 lakh, only 31.5 lakh employees are currently in position. This marks the lowest vacancy level in five years, indicating marginal improvement from 2023 when nearly every fourth post was empty.

The mismatch between retiring employees and fresh recruitment drives the persistent gap. Recruitment remains a continuous process, yet the pace fails to match administrative requirements across states.

Group B non-gazetted posts bear the heaviest burden with 31% vacancy rates. Even at the elite Group A level, nearly 20% of sanctioned positions remain unfilled. The Indian Administrative Service faces a 23% shortfall with only 5,577 officers against an authorized strength of 6,877. The Indian Police Service fares slightly better at 10% vacancy.

Five ministries concentrate 92% of central government manpower. Railways leads with 39% of total strength, followed by Home Affairs at 33%, Defence Civil at 11%, Posts at 6%, and Revenue at 3.5%.

The staffing deficit raises serious concerns about service delivery and administrative efficiency. Despite recent hiring improvements, the scale of vacancies continues to strain government operations nationwide.
