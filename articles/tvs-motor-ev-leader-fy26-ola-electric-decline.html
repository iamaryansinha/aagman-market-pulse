TVS Motor Tops India EV Two-Wheeler Market FY26; Ola Electric Falls 53%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/TVS Motor Company Ltd (NSE: TVSMOTOR) emerged as India's EV two-wheeler market leader in FY26 with 330,145 units sold
  • 02/Ola Electric's volumes crashed 53% to 160,558 units from over 3.44 lakh units in FY25 amid service challenges
  • 03/Legacy manufacturers now dominate as EV adoption shifts from early adopters to mainstream family mobility buyers

TVS Motor Company Ltd has claimed the top spot in India's electric two-wheeler market for FY26. The company sold 330,145 units according to Vahan portal data.

This marks a 39% year-on-year growth for TVS Electric. It became only the second company after Ola Electric to cross the 3-lakh annual sales milestone.

The overall EV two-wheeler segment grew 17.3% YoY to 1.35 million units. The market is expected to close near 1.5 million units by fiscal year-end.

Bajaj Auto ranked second with 276,518 units. Ather Energy secured third place with 229,565 units.

Hero MotoCorp's Vida brand recorded 138,558 units, up 184% YoY. It emerged as the fastest-scaling large player in the segment.

Ola Electric witnessed a dramatic reversal. Its volumes plummeted 53% to 160,558 units from over 3.44 lakh units in FY25.

The decline was driven by service-related challenges and supply constraints. Ola's market share eroded significantly from around 30% to under 12%.

"The modern Indian buyer is no longer experimenting with electric mobility; they are replacing their internal combustion engines with platforms that offer a seamless transition into the commuter segment," said a top analyst at a global consulting house.

The market has pivoted toward "family mobility" products. TVS iQube, Bajaj Chetak, and Ather Rizta now contribute nearly 70% of overall sales.

FY26 marks a decisive structural shift. Legacy players with superior distribution networks and service depth have outpaced startup competitors.

CRISIL expects EV two-wheeler volumes to grow 20-22% in FY27. This significantly outpaces the 4-6% growth projected for ICE two-wheelers.
