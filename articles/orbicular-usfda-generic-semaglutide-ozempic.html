Orbicular Gets USFDA Tentative Nod For Generic Semaglutide Injection

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Orbicular gets first USFDA tentative approval for generic semaglutide injection, the active ingredient in Ozempic and Wegovy
  • 02/GLP-1 market projected to reach $150 billion by 2030; Novo Nordisk currently dominates with $26B in sales
  • 03/Generic entry expected to reduce prices by 60-80% once final approval granted after patent expiry

Orbicular Pharmaceutical Technologies has received tentative approval from the U.S. FDA for its generic semaglutide injection. This marks the first tentative approval for a generic version of the blockbuster diabetes and obesity drug.

Semaglutide is the active ingredient in Novo Nordisk's Ozempic and Wegovy, which generated $26 billion in sales last year. The GLP-1 receptor agonist market is projected to reach $150 billion by 2030.

The approval is tentative because Novo Nordisk's patents on semaglutide remain in force until the late 2030s. Final approval will only be granted after patent expiration or successful patent challenges.

Achieving bioequivalence for semaglutide is technically challenging. The drug is a complex peptide requiring recombinant DNA biotechnology manufacturing, unlike small molecule generics.

Novo Nordisk has built a "patent fortress" around Ozempic with over 20 patents. These cover not just the molecule but also formulation, delivery devices, and specific dosages.

Once final approval is granted, generic semaglutide is expected to be priced 60-80% below the branded version. This would significantly expand access for diabetes and obesity patients globally.

Other Indian pharmaceutical companies are also developing generic semaglutide. Sun Pharma, Lupin, and Dr. Reddy's have disclosed GLP-1 programs in various stages.

The tentative approval signals Orbicular's technical capabilities in complex peptide manufacturing. It positions the company favorably for the generic launch when patents expire.

Novo Nordisk's stock has fallen on news of patent challenges and generic competition. However, the company continues to innovate with next-generation GLP-1 drugs and combination therapies.

The U.S. represents the largest market for GLP-1 drugs, but European and emerging market approvals will follow. India is expected to be a significant market given high diabetes prevalence.
