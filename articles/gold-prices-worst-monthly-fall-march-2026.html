Gold Prices Crash 12% in March, Worst Monthly Drop Since 2013

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold prices recorded their worst monthly decline in over a decade, falling 12% during March 2026
  • 02/The drop marks the steepest monthly fall since 2013, reversing gains from earlier record highs
  • 03/Market volatility and shifting interest rate expectations drove the sharp correction in bullion prices

Gold prices crashed 12% in March 2026. This marks the worst monthly decline for the yellow metal in over a decade.

The drop is the steepest since 2013. Bullion had touched record highs earlier this year before the sharp reversal.

Global market volatility triggered the selloff. Investors adjusted positions amid uncertainty around interest rate trajectories.

Rising opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets weighed on gold. Bond yields strengthened as rate-cut expectations faded.

The precious metal typically struggles when real rates climb. March saw renewed strength in the US dollar index.

Safe-haven demand weakened as risk appetite returned to equity markets. Gold's pullback was broad-based across global exchanges.

Analysts expect continued volatility in the near term. Price action remains sensitive to Federal Reserve policy signals.
