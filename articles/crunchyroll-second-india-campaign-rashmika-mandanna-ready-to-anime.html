Crunchyroll Second India Campaign: Rashmika Mandanna Returns With 'Ready to Anime?'

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Crunchyroll launches second India brand campaign 'Ready to Anime?' on February 12, 2026, featuring returning ambassador Rashmika Mandanna and new face Shubman Gill
  • 02/First film premiered during ICC Men's T20 World Cup; second film scheduled for coming months with Shark Tank India Season 5 integrations on Sony LIV
  • 03/62% of Indian entertainment consumers now like or love anime; teenage engagement hits 74% according to 2025 NRG study commissioned by Crunchyroll

Crunchyroll has launched its second India brand campaign titled 'Ready to Anime?' on February 12, 2026. The campaign marks actor Rashmika Mandanna's return as brand ambassador, this time joined by cricketer Shubman Gill.

The first campaign, 'Wish Your World Was Anime,' launched in April 2024 featuring Mandanna alongside Tiger Shroff. The new two-part ad series represents an expanded push into mainstream Indian entertainment.

The first film premiered during the ICC Men's T20 World Cup broadcast. Additional integrations are planned across Shark Tank India Season 5 on Sony LIV, targeting prime-time streaming audiences.

The multi-platform rollout spans live sports broadcasts, connected TV, digital platforms, and social media. Campaign development was handled by Tilt Brand Solutions with production by StudioQ under Quotient Ventures Pvt Ltd.

A 2025 study by National Research Group found 62% of surveyed Indian entertainment consumers either "like" or "love" Japanese anime. Teenage engagement is even higher at 74%, making them the most enthusiastic demographic segment.

The ad film opens on a shoot set where Mandanna, portraying an anime enthusiast, recommends the popular series Solo Leveling to Gill. The narrative uses humor as Gill appears distracted by his phone while Mandanna looks on knowingly.

Mandanna brings authenticity as a self-professed anime fan. Gill provides a relatable entry point for newer audiences exploring the genre, bridging cinema and cricket.

Shubman Gill was appointed Crunchyroll's India brand ambassador on January 16, 2026. His addition signals the platform's strategy to reach beyond traditional anime audiences into India's cricket-loving masses.

Raúl González Bernal, vice president of regional marketing at Crunchyroll, stated the company sees anime becoming routine in Indian entertainment habits. He noted that pairing an established fan with a newcomer reflects the widening audience base.

Crunchyroll's internal data shows minutes watched per viewer in India doubled over the past year. Total watch time has grown 3.5 times, with Indian anime fans averaging over 60 minutes of daily viewing.
