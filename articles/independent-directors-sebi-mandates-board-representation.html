Independent Directors: SEBI Mandates One-Third Board Representation for 8,000 Listed Firms

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI mandates minimum one-third independent directors for all listed companies, rising to 50% if board chair is non-independent
  • 02/India's 8,000+ listed companies (NSE: 2,720, BSE: 5,595) create estimated 26,000-39,000 independent director positions
  • 03/2025 SEBI amendments require compulsory ESG training and standardized performance evaluation for independent directors

Independent directors serve as the cornerstone of corporate governance in India. They bring objective oversight and independent judgment to boardrooms across the country.

Under the Companies Act 2013 and SEBI LODR Regulations 2015, every listed company must ensure at least one-third of its board comprises independent directors. If the chairperson is an executive or related to promoters, at least half the board must be independent.

The total number of companies listed on NSE and BSE as of March 2025 were 2,720 and 5,595 respectively. With over 8,000 listed companies, this translates to an estimated 26,000-39,000 independent director positions across corporate India.

Within the top 200 listed entities, independent directors make up 53% of board seats in 2025. This remains below global peers where independent directors hold 67% in Singapore's STI 30, 74% in UK's FTSE 100, and 86% in US S&P 100.

Independent directors evaluate management performance and scrutinise financial integrity. They ensure risk management and internal controls function effectively while safeguarding stakeholder interests.

Under Regulation 25 of SEBI LODR, independent directors must participate in at least one exclusive meeting of independent directors annually. They must also undergo familiarisation programmes and ensure board decisions align with shareholder interests.

The 2025 SEBI amendments introduced compulsory ESG training for independent directors. Listed companies must now equip board members with skills to govern sustainability initiatives and evaluate long-term environmental risks.

Female representation in Indian boards stands at 13.8%, below the global average of 16.9%. SEBI requires top 1,000 listed entities to appoint at least one independent woman director.

Independent directors anchor key board committees including audit, nomination and remuneration, and stakeholder relationship committees. Their presence reinforces monitoring and governance across corporate India.

Q: WHAT IS THE MINIMUM REQUIREMENT FOR INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS ON INDIAN COMPANY BOARDS?
SEBI MANDATES AT LEAST ONE-THIRD OF THE BOARD MUST COMPRISE INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS FOR ALL LISTED COMPANIES. IF THE BOARD CHAIRPERSON IS AN EXECUTIVE OR RELATED TO PROMOTERS, AT LEAST 50% OF THE BOARD MUST BE INDEPENDENT DIRECTORS.
