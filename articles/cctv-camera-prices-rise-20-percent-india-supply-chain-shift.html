CCTV Camera Prices Rise 20% in India as Supply Chains Shift Away From China

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/CCTV camera prices in India expected to increase 15-20% as manufacturers shift supply chains away from China
  • 02/Government restrictions on non-certified cameras with foreign-linked components driving structural reset in surveillance ecosystem
  • 03/Indian semiconductor startups like Mindgrove Technologies and IndiesemiC gaining traction as demand for locally-designed silicon accelerates

CCTV camera prices in India are set to rise 15-20%. The increase comes as manufacturers diversify supply chains away from Chinese-linked components.

The government's new restrictions target non-certified cameras with foreign-linked parts. This policy is forcing companies to rework sourcing strategies and accelerate localisation efforts.

CP PLUS Managing Director Aditya Khemka said the company has diversified its chipset base. It now works with Qualcomm, Ambarella and Realtek to meet regulatory requirements.

"This transition has not slowed us down but has made us stronger," Khemka said. The company's IP portfolio is now built on 100% non-Chinese chipsets.

Sparsh CCTV Chief Growth Officer Sparsh Sehgal confirmed the sourcing shift. "We are diversifying our sourcing across Europe, the US and Japan," she stated.

India currently lacks domestic SoC chip manufacturing capability. Sehgal estimates real product use from local startups is still three to five years away.

The policy changes are creating opportunities for Indian semiconductor startups. Mindgrove Technologies is working with Bosch to co-develop system-on-chip solutions for surveillance applications.

"The policy creates a structural shift in the surveillance ecosystem," said Mindgrove CEO Shashwath TR. It accelerates demand for secure, locally designed silicon.

IndiesemiC co-founder Nikul Shah noted CCTV systems are becoming more connected and data-intensive. The policy pushes companies to focus on compliance, quality and technology source.

HCL Co-Founder Dr. Ajai Chowdhry said the ban encourages fabless companies to invest in secure chip design. A few Indian fabless companies have already announced replacement products.

Industry executives cite growing data security concerns as a key driver. "CCTV cameras are becoming a critical part of a country's defence ecosystem," Sehgal noted.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHY ARE CCTV CAMERA PRICES INCREASING IN INDIA?
CCTV CAMERA PRICES ARE EXPECTED TO RISE 15-20% DUE TO SUPPLY CHAIN DIVERSIFICATION. MANUFACTURERS ARE SHIFTING SOURCING AWAY FROM CHINESE-LINKED COMPONENTS TO SUPPLIERS IN THE US, EUROPE AND JAPAN. THIS RESET COMES AS THE GOVERNMENT RESTRICTS NON-CERTIFIED CAMERAS WITH FOREIGN-LINKED COMPONENTS.
WHICH COMPANIES ARE DIVERSIFYING THEIR SUPPLY CHAINS?
MAJOR PLAYERS LIKE CP PLUS (ADITYA INFOTECH LTD.) AND SPARSH CCTV ARE DIVERSIFYING SOURCING. CP PLUS IS WORKING WITH QUALCOMM, AMBARELLA AND REALTEK. SPARSH CCTV IS SOURCING FROM EUROPE, THE US AND JAPAN INSTEAD OF RELYING ON ANY ONE GEOGRAPHY.
WHAT OPPORTUNITIES ARE OPENING FOR INDIAN SEMICONDUCTOR STARTUPS?
THE POLICY CHANGES ARE CREATING DEMAND FOR LOCALLY-DESIGNED SILICON. STARTUPS LIKE MINDGROVE TECHNOLOGIES AND INDIESEMIC ARE GAINING TRACTION. MINDGROVE IS CO-DEVELOPING SOC SOLUTIONS WITH BOSCH FOR SURVEILLANCE APPLICATIONS.
WHEN WILL INDIA HAVE DOMESTIC CHIP MANUFACTURING CAPABILITY?
INDIA DOES NOT CURRENTLY HAVE A DOMESTIC SOC CHIP MANUFACTURER. STARTUPS ARE WORKING ON IT, BUT REAL PRODUCT USE IS ESTIMATED AT THREE TO FIVE YEARS. ONCE INDIA GETS ITS OWN FABS AND PACKAGING UNITS, DOMESTICALLY DESIGNED CHIPS CAN BE PRODUCED LOCALLY.
WHAT IS DRIVING THE GOVERNMENT'S RESTRICTIONS ON FOREIGN-LINKED CCTV COMPONENTS?
GROWING CONCERNS AROUND DATA SECURITY AND NATIONAL DEFENCE ARE KEY DRIVERS. CCTV CAMERAS ARE BECOMING CRITICAL PARTS OF COUNTRY'S DEFENCE ECOSYSTEMS. THE DATA, IF COMPROMISED, CAN EXPOSE CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE, MAKING TRUSTED SUPPLY CHAINS FROM CHIPS TO CLOUD ESSENTIAL.
