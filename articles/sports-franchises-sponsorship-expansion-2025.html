Sports Franchises Expand Sponsorship Rosters With Strategic Tech Partnerships

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NFL team sponsorship revenue reached $2.7 billion in 2025, marking an 8% year-over-year increase
  • 02/MLB sponsorship revenue climbed to $2.05 billion, up 9% from 2024 and 68% since 2022
  • 03/Teams are prioritizing strategic tech partnerships over traditional brand visibility deals

Professional sports franchises are expanding their sponsorship rosters with a sharp focus on strategic technology partnerships. NFL team sponsorship revenue reached $2.7 billion in 2025, up 8% from the prior season.

MLB teams generated $2.05 billion in sponsorship revenue, representing a 9% year-over-year increase. The league has seen sponsorship revenue surge 68% since 2022.

NBA franchises collected $1.62 billion in sponsorship revenue during the 2024/25 season. This marks an 8% increase compared to the previous year.

The global sports sponsorship industry was valued at $52 billion in 2025. North American leagues continue to dominate market share.

Teams are shifting away from traditional brand visibility deals. They now prioritize partnerships that embed technology directly into operations.

Cisco now partners with nine NFL teams including the 49ers and Patriots. The company provides Wi-Fi 7 infrastructure and cybersecurity systems.

AWS expanded its NFL partnership in 2025 to include generative AI tools. The cloud provider also became the official cloud partner for the NBA and WNBA.

Microsoft deepened its NFL integration with expanded Copilot features. The partnership now spans teams, broadcasts, and football operations.

The Nike-NFL partnership remains the largest apparel deal at $200 million annually. The agreement runs through the 2027-28 season.

Private equity is increasingly entering the sports sponsorship ecosystem. Arctos now holds stakes in multiple NFL teams including the Los Angeles Chargers.
