Rupee Hits Record Low of 94.70 Against US Dollar Amid FII Exodus and Market Selloff

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/The Indian rupee hit a fresh all-time low of 94.70 against the US dollar during intra-day trade on March 27, 2026, depreciating 74 paise from its previous close
  • 02/Sustained FII outflows and a sharp decline in domestic equity markets put severe pressure on the local currency, with FIIs selling Rs 1,805.37 crore on Wednesday alone
  • 03/Elevated Brent crude prices at USD 107.4 per barrel and a stronger dollar index at 99.67 are expected to keep the rupee under pressure in the near term

The Indian rupee plunged to a historic low of 94.70 against the US dollar during intra-day trade on Friday, March 27, 2026. The local currency depreciated 74 paise from its previous close of 93.96.

At the interbank foreign exchange, the rupee opened at 94.18 and kept sliding throughout the session. The currency breached the critical 94.50-mark before hitting the record low of 94.70.

Foreign institutional investors have been relentless sellers of Indian equities. FIIs offloaded equities worth Rs 1,805.37 crore on a net basis on Wednesday, according to exchange data. This sustained selling has created significant dollar demand as foreign funds repatriate capital.

The domestic equity markets mirrored the currency weakness. The BSE Sensex was trading 1,190.76 points or 1.58 per cent down at 74,082.69. The Nifty slumped 352.25 points or 1.51 per cent to 22,954.20 during the session.

Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, rose sharply overnight before easing marginally to USD 107.4 per barrel. India imports nearly 85 per cent of its crude oil requirements, making the currency highly sensitive to oil price movements.

The dollar index, which gauges the greenback's strength against a basket of six currencies, was trading 0.08 per cent higher at 99.67. A stronger dollar typically pressures emerging market currencies including the rupee.

"With consistent buying from oil companies, the rupee breached 94.50 with exporters holding their inflows, while importers buy dollars for their payables," said Anil Kumar Bhansali, Head of Treasury and Executive Director at Finrex Treasury Advisors LLP.

Stock, forex, commodity, and bullion markets remained closed on Thursday on account of Ram Navami. The concentrated selling pressure resumed as markets reopened on Friday.

The rupee has been the worst-performing Asian currency in 2026, depreciating more than 4 per cent year-to-date. FII outflows have crossed $23 billion since the beginning of 2025.

Analysts expect the rupee to remain under pressure amid geopolitical tensions and persistent foreign fund outflows. The next psychological support level is seen at 95 per dollar.
