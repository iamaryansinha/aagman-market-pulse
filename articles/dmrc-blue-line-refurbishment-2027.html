DMRC Blue Line Refurbishment: 70 Trains To Get Mid-Life Upgrade By November 2027

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/DMRC is refurbishing 70 trains from Red and Blue Lines in a phased mid-life retrofit programme
  • 02/31 trains already upgraded; Phase 3 will refurbish 22 Blue Line trains by November 2027
  • 03/Trains inducted between 2002-2007 are getting modern safety and passenger comfort features

The Delhi Metro Rail Corporation (DMRC) has launched a comprehensive mid-life refurbishment programme for its Blue Line trains. A total of 70 trains from the Red and Blue Lines will undergo upgrades in multiple phases.

Dr. Vikas Kumar, Managing Director of DMRC, inspected the first refurbished train of Phase 3 before its induction into passenger service. This marked the 31st train to be upgraded under the programme.

In Phase 1, 12 trains were successfully upgraded. Phase 2 has seen 18 trains refurbished, with work on another 9 trains set to begin soon. The remaining 9 trains of this phase will follow thereafter.

Phase 3 will focus exclusively on Blue Line trains. A total of 22 trains will be refurbished by November 2027. These trains were originally inducted between 2002 and 2007.

The trains have completed nearly 19 to 24 years of service. They are being upgraded to align with the latest rolling stock standards and ensure continued reliability.

Major improvements include repainted interiors for both passenger sections and driver cabins. This addresses wear and tear accumulated over two decades of operations.

Electrical panels have been upgraded to enhance performance and reliability. Old relays and Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) have been replaced with advanced units.

State-of-the-art IP-based Passenger Announcement and Passenger Information Systems (PA-PIS) have been installed. These provide real-time route information, current time, and expected station arrival.

CCTV systems and LCD-based Dynamic Route Maps (DRM) with display screens are now standard across refurbished coaches. These enable better passenger communication during emergencies.

A new Fire Detection System with smoke and heat detectors has been installed in all coaches. This significantly enhances passenger safety standards.

Mobile and laptop charging points have been introduced in all coaches to meet passenger needs. New jumper cables and connectors support upgraded systems and future expansion.
