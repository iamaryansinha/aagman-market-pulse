FM Sitharaman Rules Out Lockdown In India Amid West Asia War, Slams Rumour-Mongering

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 2 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman categorically denies any impending lockdown in India amid rising global tensions from the West Asia war
  • 02/Global crude oil prices have surged to $112 per barrel from $70 within one month due to disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz
  • 03/Government commits to fiscal prudence with 4.3% fiscal deficit target and protecting common man from fuel price burden

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman has strongly dismissed speculation about an impending lockdown in India. She made the statement on March 27, 2026, while responding to the Finance Bill debate in the Rajya Sabha.

The FM accused certain political leaders of spreading rumours to create fear among citizens. She urged them to stop the rumour-mongering immediately.

Sitharaman contrasted India's stable situation with neighbouring Pakistan's crisis. Pakistan has imposed a 200% hike on high octane fuel and 20% on petrol and diesel.

Petrol in Pakistan now sells at 321 Pakistani Rupees per litre. The country has announced smart lockdowns in Sindh province to conserve fuel.

Pakistani schools are shut for two weeks. Government offices have moved to a four-day working week, and private offices must shift 50% staff to work from home.

"We're not doing any of it," Sitharaman emphasised. "Still, some leaders are spreading rumours that there will be lockdown."

Global crude oil prices have surged dramatically due to the West Asia conflict. Brent Crude jumped to $112 per barrel from $70 within just one month.

The price spike is driven by disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil shipping route. Brent Crude has not dropped below $100 since March 13, 2026.

Fuel prices have risen 30-50% in Southeast Asian nations and 50% in African countries. North America has seen a 30% increase, while Europe faces a 20% hike.

The government pledged to maintain fiscal prudence despite global headwinds. "We will be able to keep the government fiscal stance carefully managed," Sitharaman assured.

The Union Budget 2026-27 projects total expenditure of ₹53.47 lakh crore, up 7.7% year-on-year. Capital expenditure is set at ₹12.2 lakh crore.

The fiscal deficit target for FY27 is 4.3% of GDP, lower than the current fiscal's 4.4%. The government aims to shield consumers from rising global crude prices.
