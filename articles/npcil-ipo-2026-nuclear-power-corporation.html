NPCIL IPO 2026: India's Nuclear Power Monopoly Eyes Public Markets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/NPCIL operates 24 nuclear reactors with 8,780 MW capacity and is the only company running nuclear plants in India
  • 02/FY2024-25 turnover hit ₹20,350 crore with net profit of ₹4,737 crore at 23.2% margin
  • 03/IPO expected in 2026 as part of government's asset monetisation drive; 8 reactors of 6,600 MW under construction

Nuclear Power Corporation of India Ltd (NPCIL) is preparing for its IPO expected later this year. The company is India's sole operator of nuclear power plants with a monopoly position in the sector.

NPCIL currently operates 24 nuclear reactors with a total installed capacity of 8,780 MW. This excludes the 100 MW Rajasthan Atomic Power Station Unit 1 which remains under long-term maintenance.

The company delivered strong financial performance in FY2024-25. Turnover reached ₹20,350 crore while net profit stood at ₹4,737 crore, reflecting a healthy margin of 23.2%.

NPCIL maintains the highest credit rating of AAA from both CRISIL and CARE. This rating underscores the company's financial stability and low default risk.

The PSU operates under the administrative control of the Department of Atomic Energy. Its revenue comes primarily from selling electricity to state electricity boards under regulated tariffs.

The company's operational track record remains solid. Average plant load factor has exceeded 85% over the past three years, indicating efficient reactor utilisation.

Expansion continues with eight reactors totaling 6,600 MW currently under construction. This includes Kudankulam Units 3 and 4 alongside indigenous 700 MW PHWR projects.

The IPO aligns with the government's National Asset Monetisation Plan targeting ₹10 lakh crore between 2025 and 2030. NPCIL's listing would mark a milestone for India's nuclear sector.

Investors should note that nuclear tariffs are regulated by the Central Electricity Regulatory Commission based on normative costs from the Department of Atomic Energy.

The counterparty risk from financially weak state discoms remains a concern. However, the strategic importance of nuclear power provides operational backing.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS NPCIL AND WHAT DOES IT DO?
NUCLEAR POWER CORPORATION OF INDIA LTD (NPCIL) IS A PUBLIC SECTOR UNDERTAKING UNDER THE DEPARTMENT OF ATOMIC ENERGY. IT IS THE ONLY COMPANY IN INDIA AUTHORISED TO OPERATE NUCLEAR POWER PLANTS, HANDLING DESIGN, CONSTRUCTION, COMMISSIONING AND OPERATION OF NUCLEAR REACTORS.
HOW MANY NUCLEAR PLANTS DOES NPCIL OPERATE AND WHAT IS THE TOTAL CAPACITY?
NPCIL CURRENTLY OPERATES 24 NUCLEAR POWER REACTORS WITH A TOTAL INSTALLED CAPACITY OF 8,780 MW. THIS EXCLUDES THE 100 MW RAJASTHAN ATOMIC POWER STATION UNIT 1 WHICH IS UNDER LONG-TERM MAINTENANCE.
WHAT WERE NPCIL'S FINANCIAL RESULTS FOR FY2024-25?
FOR THE FINANCIAL YEAR 2024-25, NPCIL REPORTED A TURNOVER OF ₹20,350 CRORE AND A NET PROFIT OF ₹4,737 CRORE. THIS REPRESENTS A NET PROFIT MARGIN OF 23.2%, DEMONSTRATING STRONG PROFITABILITY.
WHEN IS THE NPCIL IPO EXPECTED AND WHAT IS THE ISSUE SIZE?
THE NPCIL IPO IS EXPECTED IN 2026 AS PART OF THE GOVERNMENT'S ASSET MONETISATION DRIVE. THE EXACT ISSUE SIZE AND PRICE BAND HAVE NOT BEEN FINALISED YET. THE LISTING WOULD BE THROUGH AN OFFER FOR SALE (OFS) STRUCTURE WITH ZERO FRESH EQUITY DILUTION.
WHAT ARE THE KEY RISKS FOR INVESTORS IN NPCIL?
KEY RISKS INCLUDE COUNTERPARTY EXPOSURE TO FINANCIALLY WEAK STATE DISCOMS, REGULATORY DEPENDENCE ON THE DEPARTMENT OF ATOMIC ENERGY FOR TARIFFS, LONG GESTATION PERIODS FOR NEW PROJECTS, AND POTENTIAL SAFETY CONCERNS INHERENT TO NUCLEAR OPERATIONS. THE COMPANY ALSO FACES FUEL SUPPLY RISKS DESPITE THE IAEA SAFEGUARDS AGREEMENT.
