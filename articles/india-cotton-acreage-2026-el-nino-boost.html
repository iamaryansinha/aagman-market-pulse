India Cotton Acreage 2026: El Nino Outlook May Boost Sowing by 20%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's cotton acreage could increase by up to 20% in 2026 as prices rebound from recent lows
  • 02/El Nino outlook signals deficit monsoon rains, which may favor cotton as a drought-tolerant crop
  • 03/Stakeholders expect farmers to shift to cotton as price recovery incentivizes higher sowing area

India's cotton acreage could rise by up to 20% in 2026. The increase is driven by rebounding prices and the El Nino outlook.

Stakeholders anticipate higher sowing as cotton prices recover from recent lows. The price rebound is encouraging farmers to allocate more land to cotton.

El Nino conditions are expected to bring deficit monsoon rains during the 2026 season. Cotton is relatively drought-tolerant compared to other kharif crops.

Farmers may shift acreage from water-intensive crops like paddy and soybean. Cotton's resilience to lower rainfall makes it an attractive alternative.

India is the world's largest cotton producer. Higher domestic acreage could impact global cotton supply dynamics.

The 20% acreage increase projection reflects improved farmer confidence. This comes after a challenging period for cotton growers.

Commodity traders are monitoring price trends closely. Any supply increase could influence domestic and international cotton prices.

STAKEHOLDERS PROJECT THAT INDIA'S COTTON ACREAGE COULD RISE BY UP TO 20% IN 2026 COMPARED TO PREVIOUS SEASONS.
WHAT IS DRIVING THE EXPECTED INCREASE IN COTTON SOWING?
A COMBINATION OF REBOUNDING COTTON PRICES AND THE EL NINO OUTLOOK SIGNALING DEFICIT MONSOON RAINS IS EXPECTED TO DRIVE HIGHER SOWING.
WHY DOES EL NINO FAVOR COTTON CULTIVATION?
EL NINO CONDITIONS TYPICALLY BRING BELOW-NORMAL MONSOON RAINS. COTTON IS A DROUGHT-TOLERANT CROP, MAKING IT MORE SUITABLE THAN WATER-INTENSIVE ALTERNATIVES LIKE PADDY.
WHAT CROPS MIGHT FARMERS SHIFT FROM TO PLANT MORE COTTON?
FARMERS MAY REDUCE SOWING OF WATER-INTENSIVE CROPS SUCH AS PADDY (RICE) AND SOYBEAN IN FAVOR OF COTTON.
WHAT IS INDIA'S POSITION IN GLOBAL COTTON PRODUCTION?
INDIA IS THE WORLD'S LARGEST COTTON PRODUCER. CHANGES IN INDIAN COTTON ACREAGE CAN IMPACT GLOBAL SUPPLY AND PRICES.
