Tata Trust Row: Mehli Mistry Challenges Venu Srinivasan, Vijay Singh Eligibility

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Mehli Mistry files challenge against Venu Srinivasan and Vijay Singh's eligibility as Bai Hirabai Trust trustees
  • 02/Claims the individuals fail to meet religious and residency criteria required under trust deed
  • 03/Seeks suo motu inquiry by Maharashtra Charity Commissioner into trustee appointments

Mehli Mistry has challenged the eligibility of Venu Srinivasan and Vijay Singh as trustees of the Bai Hirabai Trust. The challenge alleges both individuals do not meet the required religious and residency criteria.

The Bai Hirabai Trust is part of the larger Tata Trusts structure. The dispute adds another layer to ongoing governance tensions within the Tata Group.

Mistry has sought a suo motu inquiry by the Maharashtra Charity Commissioner. The commissioner oversees charitable trusts registered in the state and has powers to investigate appointments.

The challenge questions whether the trustees meet the specific religious qualifications outlined in the trust deed. It also raises concerns about residency requirements.

Venu Srinivasan is the former chairman of TVS Motor Company. Vijay Singh is a former Union Home Secretary and has served as a Tata Trusts nominee on several boards.

The Tata Trusts have been under scrutiny following the 2016 removal of Cyrus Mistry as Tata Sons chairman. Noshir Mistry and Mehli Mistry have previously raised governance concerns.

The Maharashtra Charity Commissioner has not yet responded to the request for an inquiry. The outcome could impact trustee composition across multiple Tata Trusts.
