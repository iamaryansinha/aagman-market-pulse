Fuel Excise Cut by ₹10/litre to Shield OMCs as Crude Surges 50%

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Finance Ministry cuts special additional excise duty on petrol to ₹3/litre from ₹13, and diesel to zero from ₹10
  • 02/Crude prices have surged nearly 50% since US-Israel strikes on Iran on February 28, escalating West Asia tensions
  • 03/OMCs could face losses of ₹11/litre on petrol and ₹14/litre on diesel if crude averages $100-105/barrel

The Centre cut special additional excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre late on March 26. The move shields oil marketing companies from rising crude prices without lowering retail fuel costs.

Special additional excise duty on petrol was reduced to ₹3 per litre from ₹13. Diesel duty was cut to zero from ₹10 per litre with immediate effect.

Aviation turbine fuel for domestic consumption received full exemption. Export duties were raised to prioritise local fuel availability.

Global crude prices have surged nearly 50% since February 28. The US and Israel launched strikes on Iran that day, escalating West Asia tensions.

According to ICRA, OMCs face under-recoveries at current prices. If crude averages $100-105 per barrel, losses could reach ₹11 per litre on petrol and ₹14 per litre on diesel.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman confirmed the government would absorb part of the burden. "...the government will help them with the things they are buying at higher prices, but the rate should not be increased for the public..."

The annualised fiscal impact is estimated at ₹1.55 lakh crore. Emkay Global said the government would absorb 30-40% of OMC losses at current price levels.

Market reaction was mixed. Shares of Bharat Petroleum and Indian Oil traded lower intraday, while Hindustan Petroleum saw marginal gains.

Private retailer Nayara Energy had raised petrol and diesel prices by ₹5 and ₹3 per litre a day earlier. This reflected underlying pricing pressures in the market.

ICRA warned that elevated energy prices could complicate India's fiscal position in FY27. "...risks remain skewed to the upside if elevated energy prices persist due to a prolonged conflict..."

Iran has granted India safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz. However, uncertainty persists with the US extending the Iran ceasefire deadline to April 6.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE EXCISE DUTY CUT ON PETROL AND DIESEL?
THE SPECIAL ADDITIONAL EXCISE DUTY ON PETROL HAS BEEN REDUCED TO ₹3 PER LITRE FROM ₹13. ON DIESEL, IT HAS BEEN CUT TO ZERO FROM ₹10 PER LITRE. THE NOTIFICATION WAS ISSUED ON MARCH 26, 2026 WITH IMMEDIATE EFFECT.
WHY DID THE GOVERNMENT CUT EXCISE DUTIES ON FUEL?
THE CUTS ARE INTENDED TO PROTECT OIL MARKETING COMPANY MARGINS AMID A NEARLY 50% SURGE IN GLOBAL CRUDE PRICES SINCE FEBRUARY 28. THE GOVERNMENT IS ABSORBING PART OF THE COST BURDEN TO PREVENT RETAIL PRICE INCREASES FOR CONSUMERS.
HOW MUCH COULD OMCs LOSE AT CURRENT CRUDE PRICES?
ACCORDING TO ICRA, IF CRUDE PRICES AVERAGE $100-105 PER BARREL, OMCs COULD FACE LOSSES OF ₹11 PER LITRE ON PETROL AND ₹14 PER LITRE ON DIESEL DUE TO UNDER-RECOVERIES.
WHAT IS THE FISCAL IMPACT OF THESE EXCISE CUTS?
EMKAY GLOBAL ESTIMATES THE ANNUALISED FISCAL IMPACT AT ₹1.55 LAKH CRORE. THE GOVERNMENT IS EXPECTED TO ABSORB 30-40% OF OMC LOSSES AT CURRENT PRICE LEVELS.
WILL RETAIL FUEL PRICES COME DOWN FOR CONSUMERS?
NO. FINANCE MINISTER NIRMALA SITHARAMAN CONFIRMED THAT RETAIL FUEL PRICES WILL REMAIN UNCHANGED. THE EXCISE CUTS ARE DESIGNED TO SHIELD OMCs WITHOUT PASSING SAVINGS TO CONSUMERS AT THE PUMP.
