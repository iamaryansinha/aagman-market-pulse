Gold Price Falls 15% This Month as Fed Rate Cut Hopes Fade, Oil Prices Surge

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Gold prices have declined more than 15% so far in March 2026, marking one of the worst monthly performances
  • 02/Houthi attacks in the Red Sea are driving oil prices higher while reducing expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts
  • 03/Investors should watch Fed Chair Powell's statements and oil price movements for gold direction

Gold prices have fallen more than 15% in March 2026. The decline comes as geopolitical tensions shift investor focus toward oil.

Houthi rebel attacks on shipping in the Red Sea have escalated. These attacks are pushing crude oil prices higher as supply risks mount.

Rising oil prices are complicating the inflation outlook. Higher energy costs could keep US inflation elevated longer than expected.

The Federal Reserve's rate-cut hopes have dimmed significantly. Markets are now pricing in fewer rate cuts for 2026.

Higher interest rates reduce gold's appeal. The precious metal yields no interest, making it less attractive when rates stay elevated.

Gold typically benefits from rate cuts and geopolitical uncertainty. The current environment presents a rare disconnect between these drivers.

Oil prices have become the preferred safe-haven asset. Energy market volatility is capturing risk-off flows that typically went to gold.

Analysts expect continued pressure on gold if oil remains elevated. The Fed may delay cuts until inflation shows clearer signs of cooling.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
HOW MUCH HAS GOLD FALLEN IN MARCH 2026?
GOLD PRICES HAVE DECLINED MORE THAN 15% SO FAR THIS MONTH, MAKING IT ONE OF THE WORST MONTHLY PERFORMANCES FOR THE PRECIOUS METAL IN RECENT YEARS.
WHY ARE HOUTHI ATTACKS AFFECTING GOLD PRICES?
HOUTHI ATTACKS ON RED SEA SHIPPING ARE DRIVING OIL PRICES HIGHER. THIS CREATES INFLATION PRESSURE THAT REDUCES EXPECTATIONS FOR FEDERAL RESERVE RATE CUTS, WHICH NEGATIVELY IMPACTS GOLD.
HOW DO FED RATE CUTS IMPACT GOLD PRICES?
GOLD BECOMES MORE ATTRACTIVE WHEN INTEREST RATES FALL BECAUSE IT YIELDS NO INTEREST. WHEN RATE-CUT EXPECTATIONS DIMINISH, GOLD LOSES APPEAL RELATIVE TO INTEREST-BEARING ASSETS.
WHAT IS THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN OIL AND GOLD CURRENTLY?
TYPICALLY BOTH ARE SAFE-HAVEN ASSETS DURING GEOPOLITICAL TENSIONS. CURRENTLY, OIL IS BENEFITTING FROM SUPPLY DISRUPTIONS WHILE GOLD IS SUFFERING FROM HIGHER INFLATION EXPECTATIONS THAT DELAY RATE CUTS.
WHAT SHOULD GOLD INVESTORS WATCH NEXT?
INVESTORS SHOULD MONITOR FEDERAL RESERVE OFFICIAL STATEMENTS, US INFLATION DATA, AND OIL PRICE MOVEMENTS. ANY DE-ESCALATION IN THE RED SEA COULD REDUCE OIL PRICES AND IMPROVE THE RATE-CUT OUTLOOK FOR GOLD.
