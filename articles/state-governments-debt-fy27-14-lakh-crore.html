State Governments To Raise ₹13.4-14 Lakh Crore Debt In FY27, Up 5-9%

April 6, 2026 | 3 min read

TL;DR

  • • State borrowing projected at ₹13.4-14 lakh crore in FY27, up 5-9% YoY from ₹12.8 lakh crore
  • • RBI launches Benchmark Issuance Strategy pilot for 9 states to borrow ₹1.5 trillion
  • • SDL yields seen at 6.65-6.75%; 15 of 17 major states exceed 20% debt-to-GSDP ceiling

Indian state governments are projected to raise ₹13.4-14 lakh crore through debt issuance in FY2027, representing a 5-9% year-on-year increase from ₹12.8 lakh crore in FY26, according to ICRA.

This marks the third consecutive year of elevated borrowing levels as states continue funding infrastructure projects and managing revenue deficits amid evolving fiscal dynamics.

RBI's New Benchmark Strategy

The Reserve Bank of India has introduced the Benchmark Issuance Strategy (BIS) on a pilot basis. Nine states will collectively borrow ₹1.5 trillion through standardized maturity buckets.

This initiative aims to develop a more structured State Government Securities (SGS) yield curve, improving liquidity and transparency in the SDL market.

Market Dynamics

Net state borrowings after accounting for redemptions of ₹4.2 lakh crore are estimated at ₹9.2-9.7 lakh crore. Interest rates for state development loans are expected to remain elevated.

The 10-year SDL yields are projected in the 6.65-6.75% range. The spread between SDLs and central government securities has widened to 25-75 basis points.

Fiscal Concerns

Aggregate state debt-to-GSDP ratios remain above pre-pandemic levels at around 29.2% in FY26. States' fiscal deficits have stabilized around 3.3% of GSDP.

Fifteen of seventeen major states currently exceed the 20% debt-to-GSDP ceiling. Punjab (46.9%) and West Bengal (40%) carry the heaviest burdens.

Quick Takes

WHAT IS THE BENCHMARK ISSUANCE STRATEGY?
RBI'S PILOT PROGRAM WHERE 9 STATES WILL BORROW ₹1.5 TRILLION THROUGH STANDARDIZED MATURITY BUCKETS TO CREATE A MORE LIQUID AND TRANSPARENT SDL YIELD CURVE.
WHY ARE STATES BORROWING MORE?
INFRASTRUCTURE SPENDING, DEBT REFINANCING, AND REVENUE DEFICITS. STATES RELY ON MARKET FINANCING AS OWN REVENUE GENERATION FACES STRUCTURAL CHALLENGES.
WHAT ARE SDL YIELDS?
10-YEAR STATE DEVELOPMENT LOAN YIELDS PROJECTED AT 6.65-6.75%, WITH 25-75 BPS SPREAD OVER CENTRAL G-SECS.
WHICH STATES HAVE HIGHEST DEBT?
PUNJAB (46.9% DEBT-TO-GSDP) AND WEST BENGAL (40%). 15 OF 17 MAJOR STATES EXCEED THE 20% FRBM CEILING.
WHAT IS THE OUTLOOK?
ELEVATED BORROWING COSTS THROUGH FY27 DUE TO RBI RATE PAUSE. RISK OF CROWDING OUT PRIVATE CREDIT AND INCREASING FUTURE DEBT SERVICING.
