India Aviation Sector Faces Aircraft Supply Crunch: Minister Naidu

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Civil Aviation Minister K Rammohan Naidu identifies aircraft supply crunch as major bottleneck hampering India's aviation sector growth post-COVID
  • 02/1,700 aircraft orders are pending with deliveries stretched up to 15 years due to global supply chain disruptions affecting manufacturing speed
  • 03/Government pushing domestic manufacturing via Embraer and HAL partnerships to address shortage and meet rising demand

Civil Aviation Minister K Rammohan Naidu has flagged aircraft supply crunch as a major bottleneck for India's aviation sector growth. The minister made the remarks while flagging off new flights at Bhavnagar airport in Gujarat on Sunday.

Naidu stated that building airports is manageable once land is secured. However, securing aircraft deliveries has become extremely difficult after the COVID-19 pandemic disrupted global manufacturing.

Indian airlines have placed orders for 1,700 new aircraft. These deliveries could stretch up to 15 years due to worldwide supply chain constraints affecting production speed.

"After COVID-19, there were many difficulties in aircraft manufacturing. There was some disruption in the supply chain," Naidu said. "The speed and scale at which aircraft were supposed to be built have decreased."

The government is working to speed up aircraft imports and boost domestic manufacturing. Partnerships with Embraer and Hindustan Aeronautics Limited for Sukhoi Superjet 100 production are underway.

Around 30,000 pilots will be needed in the coming years to operate the new aircraft. The government plans to develop 50 airports in the next five years and aims for over 350 airports by 2047.

Since 2014, the number of airports, aircraft, and passengers in India has doubled. The country's 165th airport was recently inaugurated in Jewar, Noida.
