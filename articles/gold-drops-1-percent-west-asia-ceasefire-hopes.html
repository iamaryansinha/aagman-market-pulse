Gold Drops 1.2% to $4,451 as West Asia Ceasefire Hopes Ease Safe-Haven Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Spot gold fell 1.2% to $4,451.47 per ounce by 0811 GMT on March 26, 2026 as ceasefire hopes emerged in the Middle East
  • 02/US gold futures for April delivery dropped 2.3% to $4,448 as Fed rate hike expectations climbed to 37% probability by December
  • 03/Brent crude remained above $100 per barrel while silver plunged 2.7% to $69.36 per ounce

Gold prices declined sharply on Thursday as diplomatic efforts to end the Middle East conflict gained momentum. Spot gold fell 1.2% to $4,451.47 per ounce by 0811 GMT.

US gold futures for April delivery lost 2.3% to $4,448. The decline reflected reduced safe-haven demand as ceasefire negotiations progressed.

President Donald Trump indicated Iran was eager to reach a deal to end nearly four weeks of fighting. However, Iranian officials stated they were reviewing a US proposal without committing to formal talks.

Markets now see a 37% chance of a US Federal Reserve rate hike by December 2026. Before the conflict, traders expected at least two rate cuts.

Higher interest rates reduce gold's appeal as a non-yielding asset. Elevated oil prices above $100 per barrel have stoked inflation concerns.

"You're seeing an acceleration of the idea that this war will mean inflation and inflation will mean higher interest rates," said Ilya Spivak, head of global macro at Tastylive.

Spot silver fell 2.7% to $69.36 per ounce. Spot platinum declined 2.3% to $1,874.90, while palladium dropped 2.5% to $1,387.53.

"In the next 24 to 48 hours, gold prices will just be about reacting to headlines about negotiations," said Kyle Rodda, senior analyst at Capital.com.
