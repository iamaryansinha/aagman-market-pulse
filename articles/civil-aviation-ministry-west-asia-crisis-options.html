Civil Aviation Ministry Mulls Options To Cut West Asia Crisis Impact On Airlines

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Civil aviation ministry is exploring multiple options including state-level jet fuel tax cuts to reduce West Asia crisis impact on airlines
  • 02/Airspace restrictions since February 28 conflict are forcing longer routes to Europe and North America, raising fuel burn and costs
  • 03/DGCA is coordinating with international counterparts as airlines face operational challenges from surging oil prices and airspace curbs

The civil aviation ministry is actively exploring multiple measures to minimise the impact of the West Asia conflict on Indian airlines. The crisis involving the US, Israel and Iran began on February 28, 2026.

Minister K Rammohan Naidu has initiated discussions with chief ministers and state governments. The focus is on reducing taxes on aviation turbine fuel (ATF) to lower operational costs.

Jet fuel accounts for approximately 40 per cent of an airline's total operational costs. Value Added Tax (VAT) on ATF varies significantly across states.

VAT on jet fuel is 25 per cent in Delhi. Neighbouring Uttar Pradesh charges just 1 per cent.

Airspace restrictions in the wake of the West Asia turmoil are forcing carriers to take longer routes. European and North American destinations now require extended flight paths.

The longer routes have resulted in increased fuel burn for airlines. Carriers have also curtailed scheduled services, especially to the West Asia region.

The central government has already cut excise duty on petrol and diesel this week. It has also hiked the special additional excise duty on export of diesel and ATF.

This move aims to prioritise domestic availability of fuel. It may provide some relief to airlines facing surging oil prices.

Aviation watchdog DGCA is in touch with counterparts in other countries. DGCA chief Fiaz Ahmed Kidwai said airlines are facing big challenges and their operating costs are going up.

"... we hope we see better days," Kidwai said on March 26. He emphasised that DGCA was promoting ease of doing business for airlines.

The regulator is also taking steps for passenger rights protection. Airlines continue to navigate a challenging operational environment amid ongoing geopolitical tensions.
