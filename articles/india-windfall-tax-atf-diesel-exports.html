India Reimposes Windfall Tax on ATF, Diesel Exports at ₹29.5/litre

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Government reimposes windfall tax on ATF exports at ₹29.50 per litre and diesel exports at ₹21.50 per litre
  • 02/Effective levy is lower than notified ₹50/litre due to exemptions; crude prices recently hit $119/barrel post Iran strikes
  • 03/Move aims to ensure domestic fuel availability as Indian refineries divert exports to local market

The Finance Ministry has reinstated windfall taxes on petroleum product exports. The levy is set at ₹29.50 per litre for Aviation Turbine Fuel (ATF) and ₹21.50 per litre for diesel.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced the measure on March 27, 2026. She stated the tax aims to prioritize fuel for India's domestic market rather than exports.

"ATF is very important. It is necessary for India's aircraft and our companies to get ATF," Sitharaman said. "We have increased the rate on export so that instead of exporting, they will sell it in India itself."

The official notification prescribes a special additional excise duty of ₹50 per litre on ATF. However, specified exemptions bring the effective levy down to ₹29.50 per litre.

Global crude prices recently spiked to nearly $119 per barrel. The surge followed US and Israeli military strikes on Iran before easing to around $100 per barrel.

Indian refineries possess substantial processing capacity and have been actively exporting petroleum products. The new export duties incentivize these facilities to redirect output toward the domestic market.

"This reflects a calibrated approach to balance domestic supply priorities with external market dynamics," said Manoj Mishra, Partner at Grant Thornton Bharat.
