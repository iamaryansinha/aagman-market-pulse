India May Restrict Sugar Exports 2025-26 To Boost Ethanol Blending Policy

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India is considering restricting sugar exports for the 2025-26 season starting October to prioritize ethanol production
  • 02/Government may allow higher ethanol blending percentage beyond the current 15% target to reduce oil imports
  • 03/Sugar mills profitability and cane farmer pricing face uncertainty amid policy shift toward biofuel diversion

India is likely to impose restrictions on sugar exports for the 2025-26 season starting October 2025. The move aims to divert sugarcane towards ethanol production.

The government is considering allowing a higher ethanol blending percentage with petrol. This would exceed the current 15% target set for the ongoing season.

The policy shift signals New Delhi's priority to reduce crude oil imports. India currently imports over 85% of its petroleum requirements.

Sugar mills stand at the center of this transition. Higher ethanol offtake could improve their cash flows but may squeeze sugar processing margins.

Cane farmers face mixed implications. Diversion to ethanol may stabilize prices, but reduced sugar output could pressure procurement rates.

India restricted sugar exports in 2023-24 and 2024-25 to ensure domestic availability. This marked a departure from being the world's second-largest exporter.

The ethanol program has gained momentum under the government's biofuel policy. Blending rates rose from 1.5% in 2014 to approximately 15% currently.

Global sugar prices have been volatile following India's export curbs. Benchmark ICE sugar futures remain sensitive to subcontinent supply updates.

Analysts expect the official announcement before the new crushing season begins. Mills typically start operations in October-November depending on monsoon patterns.

The policy decision will balance food security, farmer welfare, and energy independence. All three remain sensitive political priorities for the administration.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT EXPORT RESTRICTIONS IS INDIA CONSIDERING FOR SUGAR IN 2025-26?
INDIA IS LIKELY TO RESTRICT OR COMPLETELY HALT SUGAR EXPORTS FOR THE 2025-26 SEASON STARTING OCTOBER 2025. THIS WOULD BE THE THIRD CONSECUTIVE YEAR OF EXPORT CURBS TO ENSURE DOMESTIC SUPPLY AND DIVERT CANE TO ETHANOL PRODUCTION.
WHAT IS THE HIGHER ETHANOL BLENDING PERCENTAGE POLICY?
THE GOVERNMENT IS EXPECTED TO ALLOW A HIGHER ETHANOL BLENDING PERCENTAGE WITH PETROL BEYOND THE CURRENT 15% TARGET. THE ULTIMATE GOAL IS TO REACH 20% ETHANOL BLENDING BY 2025-26 UNDER THE NATIONAL BIOFUEL POLICY.
HOW WILL THIS IMPACT SUGAR MILLS PROFITABILITY?
SUGAR MILLS MAY SEE IMPROVED CASH FLOWS FROM ETHANOL OFTAKE CONTRACTS BUT COULD FACE SQUEEZED MARGINS ON SUGAR PROCESSING. DIVERSION TO ETHANOL PROVIDES PRICE STABILITY, THOUGH GLOBAL SUGAR PRICES HAVE BEEN ELEVATED DUE TO SUPPLY CONSTRAINTS.
WHAT IS THE IMPACT ON CANE FARMERS AND PRICING?
CANE FARMERS FACE MIXED IMPLICATIONS. ETHANOL DIVERSION MAY STABILIZE PROCUREMENT PRICES THROUGH GUARANTEED OFTAKE, BUT REDUCED SUGAR PRODUCTION COULD PRESSURE FAIR AND REMUNERATIVE PRICE (FRP) REALIZATIONS. THE GOVERNMENT SETS FRP AT ₹340 PER QUINTAL FOR 2025-26.
WHY IS INDIA PRIORITIZING ETHANOL OVER SUGAR EXPORTS?
INDIA IMPORTS OVER 85% OF ITS CRUDE OIL REQUIREMENTS, COSTING APPROXIMATELY $100 BILLION ANNUALLY. INCREASING ETHANOL BLENDING REDUCES FOREIGN EXCHANGE OUTFLOW, SUPPORTS RURAL ECONOMY THROUGH CANE PROCUREMENT, AND LOWERS CARBON EMISSIONS FROM TRANSPORT FUELS.
