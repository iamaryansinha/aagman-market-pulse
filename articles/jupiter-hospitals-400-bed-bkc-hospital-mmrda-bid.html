Jupiter Hospitals Wins MMRDA Bid for 400-Bed BKC Hospital at ₹354 Crore

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Jupiter Life Line Hospitals wins MMRDA bid for 10,026 sqm land in Mumbai's Bandra Kurla Complex for ₹354 crore on 80-year lease
  • 02/Proposed 400-bed hospital at BKC will be company's seventh facility, taking total bed capacity visibility to 3,000 beds
  • 03/Project subject to regulatory approvals; funding through internal accruals and phased capex over 4-5 years

Jupiter Life Line Hospitals has won the MMRDA bid for land in Mumbai's Bandra Kurla Complex (BKC). The company will pay ₹354 crore for the 10,026 sqm plot on an 80-year lease.

The proposed facility will be a 400-bed multi-specialty hospital. The project is subject to regulatory approvals from relevant authorities.

This marks Jupiter's seventh hospital in its expanding network. The company currently operates four hospitals and has three under construction.

"BKC is amongst the most important locations in the country," said Ankit Thakker, Joint MD and CEO. "This gives us visibility to a 3,000-bed strength."

The company ended FY2025 with revenues of ₹1,060 crore. It maintains a net cash position of ₹300-400 crore to fund expansion projects.

Jupiter follows a greenfield strategy, funding projects through internal accruals over 4-5 years. Last phase completion may require limited debt funding.

The 500-bed Dombivli hospital launched in February 2026 at ₹425 crore. That facility was completed in just 24 months with Phase 1 operational.

BKC joins Dombivli, Mira-Bhayandar, and Pune as expansion locations. Jupiter is targeting 2,500-3,000 total beds over the next four years.
