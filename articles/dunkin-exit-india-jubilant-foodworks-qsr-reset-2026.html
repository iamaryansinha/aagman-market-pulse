Dunkin Exit India: Jubilant FoodWorks Shuts Loss-Making Chain as QSR Market Resets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Jubilant FoodWorks to exit Dunkin after franchise expiry in December 2026; brand posted ₹19.1 crore loss in FY25
  • 02/Dunkin' contributed only 0.61% to JFL revenue; ₹300 price point too expensive for habit, too cheap for premium
  • 03/JFL doubles down on Domino's with 15-year renewal; scales Popeyes as market shifts to ₹99-₹199 value meals

Jubilant FoodWorks Ltd has announced an orderly exit from Dunkin'. The franchise agreement expires in December 2026.

Dunkin' contributed just 0.61 per cent to JFL's FY25 revenue. It posted a loss of ₹19.1 crore for the fiscal year.

The move comes within 48 hours of JFL renewing its Domino's franchise for 15 years. This signals a clear strategic pivot.

At roughly ₹300 for a coffee-and-doughnut pairing, Dunkin' occupied a difficult middle ground. It was too expensive for daily consumption but lacked the experiential premium of Starbucks.

"You are either a 'habit' priced for the common pocket or an 'experience' priced for the elite pedestal," said Harish Bijoor, Brand Strategy Expert at Harish Bijoor Consults Inc. "Dunkin' fell into the crack in between."

The ₹99-₹199 price band has become the centre of gravity for Indian QSR. McDonald's anchors demand with its ₹99 McAloo Tikki. Domino's has built its 2,100-store network around this segment.

Pret A Manger, operated by Reliance Retail, entered with ₹450-₹500 sandwiches. It has since introduced a ₹149-₹249 range and a locally-priced "Bombay Toastie" at ₹295.

Other brands are recalibrating too. Wendy's India has shifted to a cloud-first model with 90% delivery-only kitchens. Café Coffee Day has shrunk from 1,700 cafés to about 450-470 outlets.

JFL is scaling Popeyes while exiting Dunkin'. The company's focus is now on brands that can compete in the value-meal economy.

The message is clear for global QSR brands entering India. This is not a ₹500-sandwich market. Scale comes from the ₹199 habit economy.
