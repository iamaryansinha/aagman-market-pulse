IBC Amendments Pass Parliament: Creditors Must Record Resolution Reasons

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Parliament passes IBC amendment bill requiring Committee of Creditors to document rationale for selecting successful resolution applicants
  • 02/Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman states amendments strengthen insolvency framework and enhance transparency in the process
  • 03/The provision aims to bring more accountability to corporate insolvency resolution proceedings under the IBC Code

Parliament has passed amendments to the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). The new provisions mandate greater transparency in the resolution process.

The key amendment requires the Committee of Creditors to record reasons. They must document why a particular resolution applicant was selected.

Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced the passage. She stated the reforms strengthen India's insolvency framework significantly.

The amendment targets the corporate insolvency resolution process. It addresses concerns about opacity in creditor decision-making.

Currently, over 1,200 companies are undergoing insolvency proceedings. The IBC has resolved more than 1,100 cases since its inception in 2016.

The new rule aims to reduce litigation. Courts previously struggled with challenges due to lack of documented reasoning from creditors.

The IBC amendments align with global best practices. Similar transparency requirements exist in US and UK insolvency frameworks.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT DOES THE NEW IBC AMENDMENT REQUIRE?
THE AMENDMENT MANDATES THAT THE COMMITTEE OF CREDITORS MUST RECORD AND DOCUMENT THE REASONS FOR SELECTING A PARTICULAR SUCCESSFUL RESOLUTION APPLICANT DURING THE INSOLVENCY PROCESS.
WHO ANNOUNCED THE PASSAGE OF THE IBC AMENDMENTS?
FINANCE MINISTER NIRMALA SITHARAMAN ANNOUNCED THE PASSAGE OF THE BILL. SHE STATED THAT THE AMENDMENTS BOOST TRANSPARENCY AND STRENGTHEN THE INSOLVENCY FRAMEWORK.
WHAT IS THE COMMITTEE OF CREDITORS?
THE COMMITTEE OF CREDITORS IS A BODY COMPRISING FINANCIAL CREDITORS OF THE CORPORATE DEBTOR. THEY VOTE ON RESOLUTION PLANS AND SELECT THE SUCCESSFUL APPLICANT DURING INSOLVENCY PROCEEDINGS.
WHY IS THIS AMENDMENT SIGNIFICANT?
THE AMENDMENT BRINGS GREATER ACCOUNTABILITY TO THE RESOLUTION PROCESS. IT HELPS REDUCE LITIGATION BY PROVIDING DOCUMENTED RATIONALE THAT COURTS CAN REVIEW WHEN RESOLUTION DECISIONS ARE CHALLENGED.
WHEN WAS THE IBC ORIGINALLY ENACTED?
THE INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY CODE WAS ENACTED IN 2016. IT HAS RESOLVED MORE THAN 1,100 CASES SINCE INCEPTION, WITH OVER 1,200 COMPANIES CURRENTLY UNDERGOING INSOLVENCY PROCEEDINGS.
