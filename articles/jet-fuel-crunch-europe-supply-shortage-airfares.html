Jet Fuel Crunch Looms: Europe Has 6 Weeks Supply, Airfares Set to Spike

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IEA warns Europe has only 6 weeks of jet fuel remaining as Strait of Hormuz closure cuts 40% of imports
  • 02/Jet fuel prices have doubled since war began, now accounting for 30% of airline operating costs
  • 03/KLM already cutting 160 flights; United warns $11 billion annual cost hit if prices stay elevated

Europe is facing a critical jet fuel shortage that could disrupt global air travel within weeks. The International Energy Agency warns the continent has only six weeks of supply remaining.

The crisis stems from the Iran war and effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz. The strait accounts for about 40% of Europe's jet fuel imports, but no fuel has passed through since the conflict began.

Jet fuel prices have roughly doubled since the war started. Fuel now makes up approximately 30% of airline operating expenses, according to the International Air Transport Association.

The world is losing 10 million to 15 million barrels of oil daily due to the strait closure. Several European countries now hold less than 20 days of fuel coverage, the lowest since 2020.

If inventory falls below 23 days, physical shortages may emerge at airports. This could trigger flight cancellations and reduced demand during the peak summer travel season.

Airlines are already taking action. KLM announced it will cut 160 flights next month, citing rising kerosene costs. The cuts represent about 1% of its total European routes.

EasyJet expects a pretax loss of £540-560 million ($731-758 million) for the first half of fiscal 2026. Despite losses, the carrier reported record Easter travel demand.

United Airlines CEO Scott Kirby warned that sustained high fuel prices could add $11 billion in annual costs. The airline made less than $5 billion in its best year ever.

US carriers are passing costs to passengers. Delta, United, American, Southwest, and JetBlue have all increased checked baggage fees in recent weeks.

Asia-Pacific nations face the greatest supply risk after Europe. The US has boosted jet fuel exports to Europe to 150,000 barrels per day, six times the normal level.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
Q: HOW MUCH JET FUEL DOES EUROPE HAVE LEFT?
THE INTERNATIONAL ENERGY AGENCY ESTIMATES EUROPE HAS APPROXIMATELY SIX WEEKS OF JET FUEL SUPPLY REMAINING. SOME EUROPEAN COUNTRIES CURRENTLY HOLD LESS THAN 20 DAYS OF COVERAGE, THE LOWEST LEVEL SINCE 2020.
Q: WHY ARE JET FUEL SUPPLIES TIGHTENING?
THE STRAIT OF HORMUZ, WHICH ACCOUNTS FOR ABOUT 40% OF EUROPE'S JET FUEL IMPORTS, HAS BEEN EFFECTIVELY CLOSED SINCE THE IRAN WAR BEGAN. NO JET FUEL HAS PASSED THROUGH THE STRAIT SINCE THE CONFLICT STARTED.
Q: HOW MUCH HAVE JET FUEL PRICES INCREASED?
JET FUEL PRICES HAVE ROUGHLY DOUBLED SINCE THE WAR BEGAN. FUEL NOW REPRESENTS APPROXIMATELY 30% OF TOTAL AIRLINE OPERATING EXPENSES, UP FROM HISTORICAL LEVELS.
Q: WHICH AIRLINES ARE CUTTING FLIGHTS?
KLM WILL CUT 160 FLIGHTS NEXT MONTH, ABOUT 1% OF ITS EUROPEAN ROUTES. LUFTHANSA IS SHUTTING DOWN FEEDER AIRLINE CITYLINE IMMEDIATELY AND REMOVING 27 OLDER PLANES FROM SERVICE.
Q: HOW WILL TRAVELLERS BE AFFECTED?
TRAVELLERS SHOULD EXPECT HIGHER AIRFARES, INCREASED BAGGAGE FEES, FLIGHT CANCELLATIONS, AND REDUCED SCHEDULING FLEXIBILITY. US AIRLINES HAVE ALREADY RAISED CHECKED BAGGAGE FEES, AND CATHAY PACIFIC INCREASED FUEL SURCHARGES BY 34% ACROSS ALL ROUTES.
