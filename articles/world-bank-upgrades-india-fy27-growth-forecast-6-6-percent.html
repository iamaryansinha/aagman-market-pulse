World Bank Upgrades India's FY27 Growth Forecast To 6.6% On Strong Domestic Demand

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/World Bank upgrades India's FY27 GDP growth forecast by 30 bps to 6.6% from its earlier projection of 6.3%
  • 02/FY26 growth estimated at 7.6%, one percentage point higher than FY27, driven by strong domestic demand and export resilience
  • 03/Watch for West Asia conflict impact on energy prices and the extent of government subsidy outlays on fiscal deficit

The World Bank has upgraded India's GDP growth forecast for fiscal year 2026-27 to 6.6%. This marks a 30 basis points increase from its earlier projection of 6.3%.

The revision comes despite headwinds from the West Asia conflict and elevated global energy prices. Strong domestic demand and resilient exports prompted the upgrade.

For the current fiscal FY26, the World Bank pegs growth at 7.6%. This is higher than the 7.1% recorded in FY25 and reflects robust private consumption supported by low inflation and GST rationalisation.

India's economy grew 7.8% in the October-December quarter of FY26. Updated GDP data revealed the economy was slightly smaller than previously thought, but recent growth has been faster.

Domestic demand remains strong with consumer confidence reaching its highest post-pandemic level in November 2025. Retail sales data supports this momentum.

Services exports grew about 16% from December to February. Foreign portfolio investors and strong remittances helped contain the current account deficit despite weak goods exports.

The World Bank's FY27 forecast of 6.6% is below RBI's projection of 6.9%. The IMF forecasts 6.4%, while Moody's has cut its estimate to 6.0% citing West Asia conflict exposure.

Risks remain elevated. Energy price pressures could push inflation higher and constrain household disposable income. Investment growth may moderate amid rising input costs and uncertainty.

Government consumption growth is expected to soften as higher subsidy outlays for cooking fuel and fertilisers offset fiscal space. Slower growth in major trading partners could undermine export gains from recent FTAs.

"Growth is projected to decelerate to 6.6% in FY27, reflecting headwinds from the West Asia conflict," the World Bank said in its South Asia Economic Update.
