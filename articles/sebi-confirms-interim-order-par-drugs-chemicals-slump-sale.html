SEBI Confirms Interim Order Against Par Drugs Chemicals Over Slump Sale Violations

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI confirms interim order dated March 25, 2026, barring Par Drugs from Rs 95 crore slump sale to promoter-related entity
  • 02/The business unit generates 99.44% of company revenue; market cap crashed 70% from Rs 428 crore to Rs 139 crore since announcement
  • 03/SEBI found valuation lapses and voting irregularities; independent valuation and fairness opinion ordered

SEBI has confirmed its interim directions against Par Drugs and Chemicals Ltd (NSE: PAR_DRUGS). The confirmatory order was passed on March 25, 2026, by Whole Time Member Kamlesh Chandra Varshney.

The regulator barred the company from proceeding with a Rs 95 crore slump sale. The transaction involved transfer of its core business to Phal-Jig Fine Chemicals Pvt Ltd, a promoter-related entity.

The business unit being transferred accounts for 99.44% of PDCL's total revenue. The sale was approved through a Business Transfer Agreement dated February 14, 2025.

SEBI's initial interim order was passed on September 15, 2025. The regulator cited prima facie irregularities in valuation, shareholder communication, and the voting process.

The company failed to rebut the prima facie findings recorded in the interim order. SEBI noted that observations remain tentative pending detailed investigation.

Valuation reports submitted by PDCL focused only on six physical assets. These included industrial land, office premises, and plant machinery.

No value was attributed to intangibles like employees, goodwill, brand equity, or technical knowledge. These are core elements of a profitable going concern.

The market capitalization plummeted from Rs 428.76 crore in December 2024 to Rs 139.74 crore by September 2025. This represents a decline of nearly 70 percent.

SEBI also identified voting irregularities in the shareholder approval process. Common IP addresses were found linking promoter accounts and shareholder accounts.

The regulator has directed NSE to appoint a registered valuer for independent assessment. A fairness opinion from a merchant banker is also required.

"If the proposed slump sale transaction is allowed to proceed, it could cause grave injustice to the public shareholders since the transaction would become irreversible upon execution," Varshney stated in the order.

The confirmatory order is effective immediately. It will remain operational until further directions from SEBI.

SEBI FOUND PRIMA FACIE IRREGULARITIES INCLUDING VALUATION LAPSES WHERE ONLY PHYSICAL ASSETS WERE VALUED WHILE INTANGIBLES LIKE GOODWILL, BRAND EQUITY, AND TECHNICAL KNOWLEDGE WERE EXCLUDED, AND VOTING IRREGULARITIES WITH COMMON IP ADDRESSES LINKING PROMOTER AND SHAREHOLDER ACCOUNTS.

THE COMPANY'S MARKET CAPITALIZATION HAS CRASHED NEARLY 70% FROM RS 428.76 CRORE IN DECEMBER 2024 TO RS 139.74 CRORE BY SEPTEMBER 2025, WITH SHARP FALLS CORRELATING TO SLUMP SALE ANNOUNCEMENTS AND SHAREHOLDER MEETINGS.

THE BUSINESS UNIT BEING TRANSFERRED ACCOUNTS FOR 99.44% OF PDCL'S TOTAL REVENUE, MAKING THE SLUMP SALE EFFECTIVELY A TRANSFER OF THE ENTIRE CORE OPERATIONS TO A PROMOTER-RELATED ENTITY.
WHAT ARE THE NEXT STEPS AS PER SEBI'S ORDER?
NSE MUST APPOINT A REGISTERED VALUER TO INDEPENDENTLY ASSESS FAIR MARKET VALUE AND OBTAIN A FAIRNESS OPINION FROM A MERCHANT BANKER; PDCL MUST MAINTAIN STATUS QUO ON ALL ASSETS AND COOPERATE WITH THE INVESTIGATION UNTIL FURTHER DIRECTIONS.
