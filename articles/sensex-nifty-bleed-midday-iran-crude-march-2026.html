Sensex, Nifty Bleed Through Midday: Iran War Fears, Crude Grip Markets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Sensex falls 1,191.76 points (1.58%) to 74,081.69, Nifty drops 346.65 points (1.49%) to 22,959.80 at 12:50 pm on March 27, 2026
  • 02/Market breadth reflects severe weakness: only 847 stocks advance while 3,342 decline out of 4,349 BSE stocks traded
  • 03/Brent crude holds at $108.01/barrel; Iran-US tensions over Strait of Hormuz keep investors in risk-off mode

Indian equity markets remained deep in the red through midday trade on Friday, March 27, 2026. Benchmark indices showed no signs of recovery as geopolitical tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict, elevated crude oil prices, and broad-based institutional selling weighed on investor sentiment.

At 12:50 pm, the BSE Sensex was trading at 74,081.69, down 1,191.76 points or 1.58%. The NSE Nifty 50 was at 22,959.80, lower by 346.65 points or 1.49%.

The Nifty has slipped below the critical 23,000 mark. Analysts had flagged this level as key near-term support, and its breach signals potential for further downside.

Market breadth reflected the broad selloff. Of 4,349 BSE stocks traded, only 847 advanced while 3,342 declined, with 160 unchanged.

A total of 575 stocks hit fresh 52-week lows. This compares with just 55 stocks that reached 52-week highs, indicating severe risk aversion among market participants.

Stocks in the lower circuit numbered 210, against 95 in the upper circuit. This lopsided ratio underscores the dominance of selling pressure across the board.

Brent crude was trading at $108.01 per barrel. Elevated oil prices keep inflation and current account deficit concerns alive for the import-dependent Indian economy.

India VIX, a measure of market volatility, stood at 24.64. This indicates elevated anxiety among traders and approaches levels seen during previous major market stress periods.

The Put-Call Ratio was at 0.95. This points to broadly neutral-to-cautious positioning in the derivatives segment.

Aviation, energy, and financial stocks bore the sharpest losses. These sectors are most sensitive to crude oil price movements and interest rate expectations.

IndiGo fell 4.14% to ₹4,116.80 from a previous close of ₹4,294.70. The decline directly reflects fuel cost pressure from elevated crude prices on airline margins.

Reliance Industries dropped 4.11% to ₹1,355.00 against ₹1,413.10. The energy-to-telecom conglomerate faced selling pressure despite its diversified business model.

TVS Motor Company declined 4.62% to ₹303.25 from ₹317.95. The auto sector remains vulnerable to input cost pressures and demand concerns.

Shriram Finance shed 4.38% to ₹914.10 from ₹956.00. Bajaj Finance fell 3.85% to ₹848.80 from ₹882.75. Financial stocks faced pressure amid rising yields and potential RBI policy uncertainty.

On the gainers' side, energy major ONGC bucked the trend. It rose 4.24% to ₹281.65 from ₹270.20, benefiting directly from higher crude oil realizations.

TCS added 1.84% to ₹2,421.20 against ₹2,377.40. Wipro gained 1.31% to ₹191.52 from ₹189.05. IT stocks drew selective buying on rupee depreciation tailwinds.

Nestle India edged up 0.78% to ₹1,213.50 from ₹1,204.10. Power Grid Corporation rose 0.64% to ₹296.90 from ₹295.00. Defensive names provided relative stability.

Institutional flow data from the previous session showed continued divergence. FIIs were net sellers to the tune of ₹1,805 crore, while DIIs provided partial support with net purchases of ₹5,430 crore.

Analysts tracking institutional positioning noted conviction building in financials, real estate, and domestic capital goods names. These sectors are seen as more insulated from global risk factors, provided crude does not breach $110 per barrel.

A crude price above $110 could pressure rate-sensitive sectors by potentially forcing the Reserve Bank of India to delay any rate cuts. This would compound challenges for banking and consumption-oriented businesses.

The Iran-US conflict has escalated significantly through March 2026. President Donald Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Iran over the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint through which approximately 20% of global oil passes.

Iran warned it could fully close the straits if its energy assets were attacked. Reports of missile strikes on a joint US-British base at Diego Garcia confirmed the severity of the situation.

The Indian rupee trades near a record low of ₹93-94 per US dollar. It is down roughly 3% since the conflict began, adding to imported inflation pressures.

With roughly two-and-a-half hours left in the session, the market's direction will likely hinge on any fresh headlines from West Asia and the movement of crude oil prices through the afternoon.
