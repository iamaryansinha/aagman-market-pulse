Iranian Crude Returns To India After Seven Years As Tankers Dock At Key Ports

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Two supertankers carrying Iranian crude arrive at Indian ports on east and west coasts, first such deliveries in nearly seven years
  • 02/Felicity VLCC anchors off Sikka with 2 million barrels; Jaya tanker moors near Paradip with similar volume loaded in February
  • 03/US sanctions waiver expires April 19; 95 million barrels of Iranian oil at sea with around 51 million potentially sellable to India

Two supertankers carrying Iranian crude have arrived at Indian ports. The deliveries mark the first Iranian oil cargoes to reach Indian shores in nearly seven years.

Felicity, a very large crude carrier operated by National Iranian Tanker Company, anchored off Sikka on Gujarat's coast late Sunday. The vessel carries about 2 million barrels loaded at Kharg Island in mid-March.

A second tanker named Jaya moored near Paradip on Odisha's east coast around the same time. It is transporting a similar volume of crude lifted from Kharg Island in late February.

The shipments follow a sanctions waiver issued by the United States last month. The one-month exemption permitted the sale of Iranian oil already in transit.

However, the waiver window expires on April 19. Washington announced a blockade of Iranian ports after peace talks collapsed over the weekend.

Indian Oil Corporation has confirmed purchasing at least one Iranian shipment under the waiver. Sikka serves as a key crude handling hub for Reliance Industries and Bharat Petroleum Corporation.

India was historically a major buyer of Iranian crude. At peak, Iranian oil accounted for 11.5% of India's total imports.

The country imported 518,000 barrels per day of Iranian oil in 2018. Imports slowed to 268,000 bpd between January and May 2019 before ceasing entirely.

An estimated 95 million barrels of Iranian oil is currently on vessels at sea. Around 51 million barrels could potentially be sold to India.
