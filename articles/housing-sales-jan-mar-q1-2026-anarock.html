India Housing Sales Q1 2026: 1.01 Lakh Units, Down 7% On West Asia Conflict

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Housing sales across India's top 7 cities reached 1,01,675 units in Q1 2026, up 9% from 93,280 units in the year-ago period
  • 02/Sales declined 7% quarter-on-quarter from 1,08,970 units in Oct-Dec 2025, impacted by West Asia conflict and rising oil prices
  • 03/Value of sales grew 6% annually to ₹1.51 lakh crore; Anarock expects recovery as Middle Eastern buyer sentiment stabilizes

Housing sales in India's top seven cities reached 1,01,675 units during January-March 2026. This marks a 9% increase from 93,280 units sold in the same period last year.

The quarterly performance, however, showed a 7% decline from the previous quarter. Sales stood at 1,08,970 units in October-December 2025.

The dip is attributed to global economic uncertainties stemming from the West Asia conflict. Surging oil and construction material prices particularly impacted March sales.

Sales value rose 6% annually to ₹1.51 lakh crore. This compares to ₹1.42 lakh crore in the year-ago quarter.

Quarterly value declined 6% from ₹1.60 lakh crore in Q4 2025. The value dip was less severe than the volume decline.

"While India's residential segment's long-term fundamentals remain strong, the short-term tremors of the Iran War were clearly visible," said Anuj Puri, Chairman of Anarock Group.

Middle Eastern homebuyers, significant investors in Indian real estate, paused purchases due to war-induced uncertainty. This segment typically drives demand in premium housing categories.

Anarock expects sales to recover as geopolitical tensions ease. The consultancy maintains a positive outlook for India's residential market fundamentals.
