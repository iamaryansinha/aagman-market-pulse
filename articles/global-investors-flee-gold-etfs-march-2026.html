Global Investors Flee Gold ETFs in March as $2.34 Exits for Every $1 Entered

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Global investors pulled $21.35 billion from gold ETFs while adding just $9.12 billion in March
  • 02/For every $1 flowing in, $2.34 rushed out — a staggering outflow ratio
  • 03/Gold has dropped 15% over the past month to $4,551 per ounce

Gold has lost its shine. March 2026 will go down as a brutal month for the yellow metal, with global investors yanking capital from exchange-traded funds at a pace that surprised even seasoned commodity watchers.

For every dollar that flowed into physically-backed gold ETFs, $2.34 rushed out the door. That is the stark math from World Gold Council data: $9.12 billion in fresh investments against $21.35 billion in withdrawals.

The catalyst traces back to February 27, when the US and Israel launched military operations against Iran. What followed was a textbook commodities storm. Crude oil prices soared. The dollar strengthened. Inflation fears mounted, raising the specter of interest rate hikes.

The price action tells the story. Gold has dropped 15% over the past month. From its peak of $5,608 an ounce on January 30, the metal has shed 19% and now trades at $4,551.08.

BMI, the research arm of Fitch Solutions, sees no quick recovery. The agency expects gold to remain under pressure through the second quarter.

The year-to-date picture offers some nuance. Gold opened January 2 at $4,331 and averaged $4,899 through March 25. Net flows into ETFs have actually been positive at $11.96 billion. But geography tells the real tale.

Asia is hoarding. The region has recorded net inflows of $13.29 billion. China leads with $7.93 billion. India follows at $3.13 billion.

On the selling side, Turkey's central bank offloaded 58-60 tonnes of gold — roughly $8 billion — over two weeks in March. Russia sold 15-15.5 tonnes across January and February.
