JSW MG Motor EV Sales Jump 66% to 62,591 Units in FY26, Windsor Leads

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/JSW MG Motor India reported EV sales of 62,591 units in FY26, marking a 66% jump from 37,730 units sold in FY25
  • 02/The company holds 27.3% market share in India's electric car segment, ranking second behind Tata Motors at 39%
  • 03/MG plans six new model launches in 2026-27, targeting 75% of sales from new energy vehicles

JSW MG Motor India sold 62,591 electric vehicles in FY26, registering a robust 66% year-on-year growth. The company's EV volumes rose from 37,730 units in FY25, cementing its position as India's second-largest EV player.

The Windsor EV emerged as the brand's primary growth driver. Launched in October 2024, the compact electric SUV crossed 65,000 cumulative sales by March 2026. Approximately 70% of Windsor demand came from non-metro cities, indicating broader geographic adoption.

The MG Comet EV also posted strong performance with 140% YoY growth in monthly sales during February 2026. However, the ZS EV witnessed a 13.8% YoY decline as buyers gravitated toward the newer Windsor model.

MG's overall EV market share stood at 27.3% in FY26, down slightly from 30.6% in FY25. The decline reflects intensifying competition rather than slowing sales. Mahindra & Mahindra emerged as the biggest gainer, jumping from 10.5% to 23.9% market share.

India's electric car segment grew 85.6% YoY to approximately 2,29,000 units in FY26. EV penetration in passenger vehicles doubled to 4.9%, though it remains below the 5% threshold.

The company plans six new model launches between 2026 and 2027. At least one plug-in hybrid will join the lineup alongside new EVs. MG expects 75% of its total sales to come from new energy vehicles.

Managing Director Anurag Mehrotra stated that EV penetration could reach 6-7% in 2026 as more products enter the market. The Majestor SUV will serve as the new flagship ICE model, replacing the discontinued Gloster.

Battery-as-a-Service plans and expanding charging infrastructure support MG's growth strategy. The company operates 541 sales and service outlets nationwide, with expansion planned for smaller cities.
