SEBI Working Group Clears Revised CAF Ahead of June PAN Regime Shift

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI working group clears revised Client Acquisition Form (CAF) with enhanced PAN verification ahead of June 1, 2026 regime shift
  • 02/New amendments to ICDR Regulations 2018 streamline KYC compliance for 80 million investors across securities markets
  • 03/Market intermediaries must update systems by June 1, 2026 deadline to ensure seamless transition

SEBI's working group has cleared the revised Client Acquisition Form (CAF) ahead of the upcoming PAN regime shift. The amendments were approved at the December 17, 2025 board meeting and notified effective March 21, 2026.

The revised CAF introduces stricter PAN verification requirements for all market participants. PAN remains the unique identification number for all securities market transactions irrespective of amount.

The amendments come into full effect on June 1, 2026. This gives intermediaries a 180-day window to implement system changes. The deadline applies uniformly across stock brokers, depository participants, and mutual fund distributors.

SEBI has mandated digital certification by practicing Chartered Accountants or Company Secretaries for CAF submissions. This strengthens the verification framework and reduces documentation fraud risks.

The PAN regime shift requires all existing investors to complete re-KYC if their PAN is not linked with Aadhaar. SEBI has flagged that PAN becomes inoperative if not Aadhaar-linked under Income Tax Act provisions.

Market intermediaries must update their systems to validate PAN status in real-time. The new framework integrates with Income Tax Department databases for instant verification.

The revised CAF eliminates duplicate KYC filings across multiple intermediaries. Investors can now use a single KYC form for all securities market activities.

Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) get enhanced Digital Signature Certificate functionality within the CAF portal. This allows simultaneous DSC application and registration submission.

Retail investor participation is expected to improve with simplified documentation. The streamlined process reduces onboarding time from 7 days to 48 hours for new accounts.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT IS THE REVISED CAF CLEARED BY THE SEBI WORKING GROUP?
THE REVISED CLIENT ACQUISITION FORM (CAF) IS AN UPDATED KYC DOCUMENT THAT STRENGTHENS PAN VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS AND STREAMLINES INVESTOR ONBOARDING ACROSS ALL SECURITIES MARKET INTERMEDIARIES. IT WAS APPROVED BY SEBI'S BOARD ON DECEMBER 17, 2025 AND NOTIFIED EFFECTIVE MARCH 21, 2026.
WHEN DOES THE PAN REGIME SHIFT TAKE EFFECT?
THE PAN REGIME SHIFT AND REVISED CAF REQUIREMENTS COME INTO FULL EFFECT ON JUNE 1, 2026. INTERMEDIARIES HAVE A 180-DAY WINDOW FROM THE NOTIFICATION DATE TO UPDATE THEIR SYSTEMS AND PROCESSES.
HOW DOES THE REVISED CAF IMPACT EXISTING INVESTORS?
EXISTING INVESTORS MUST ENSURE THEIR PAN IS LINKED WITH AADHAAR AND VALID AS PER INCOME TAX ACT PROVISIONS. INVESTORS WITH INOPERATIVE PAN MUST COMPLETE RE-KYC TO CONTINUE TRADING OR INVESTING IN SECURITIES.
WHAT CHANGES FOR FOREIGN PORTFOLIO INVESTORS UNDER THE REVISED CAF?
FPIS NOW HAVE ENHANCED DIGITAL SIGNATURE CERTIFICATE (DSC) FUNCTIONALITY INTEGRATED INTO THE CAF PORTAL. THEY CAN APPLY FOR DSC AND SUBMIT REGISTRATION APPLICATIONS SIMULTANEOUSLY, REDUCING ONBOARDING TIMELINES.
WHAT ARE THE COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS FOR MARKET INTERMEDIARIES?
INTERMEDIARIES MUST UPDATE SYSTEMS TO VALIDATE PAN STATUS IN REAL-TIME THROUGH INCOME TAX DATABASE INTEGRATION. THEY MUST ALSO ENSURE DIGITAL CERTIFICATION BY QUALIFIED PROFESSIONALS (CA/CS/CMA) FOR ALL CAF SUBMISSIONS BY THE JUNE 1, 2026 DEADLINE.
