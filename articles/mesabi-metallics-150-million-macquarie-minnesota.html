Mesabi Metallics Secures $150 Million From Macquarie For Minnesota Iron Ore Project

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Mesabi Metallics secures $150 million financing from Macquarie Group to support Q3 2026 startup of Minnesota iron ore mine
  • 02/Project represents $2.5 billion total investment on 16,000+ acres; Essar Group has already invested over $2 billion in equity
  • 03/First new mine and pellet plant in Minnesota in nearly 50 years; aims to reduce US dependence on imported DR-grade iron ore

Mesabi Metallics Company LLC has secured $150 million in financing from Macquarie Group. The Essar Group-backed company announced the funding on April 6, 2026.

The investment supports the planned third-quarter 2026 startup of its Direct Reduction grade iron ore mine and pellet plant in Minnesota.

The project spans more than 16,000 acres in northern Minnesota. It represents a total investment of approximately $2.5 billion.

This Macquarie financing follows a $520 million senior secured credit facility from Breakwall Capital announced recently. The project has also received backing from the US Export-Import Bank.

Essar Group has invested over $2 billion of equity into the project so far. More than 800 construction workers are currently on site.

Once operational, the facility will be the first new mine and pellet plant in Minnesota in nearly 50 years. It will supply DR-grade pellets to electric arc furnace steelmakers in the United States.

The project addresses a critical gap in US steel supply chains. America currently relies heavily on imported DR-grade iron ore for its electric arc furnace steel production.

"This financing from Macquarie marks another major step forward for Mesabi Metallics," said Joe Broking, President and CEO. "These transactions reflect growing confidence in the quality, scale and strategic importance of our project."

"Mesabi is developing a high-quality and strategically important project for the US steel sector," said Mike Burns, Senior Managing Director at Macquarie Group's Commodities and Global Markets business.

The facility will create hundreds of long-term jobs in northern Minnesota. It supports the broader push to reshore critical mineral supply chains and strengthen domestic manufacturing.
