India Energy Supply Secure: 4 Months Crude Cover, LPG Output Hits Record 50,000 TPD

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India has 60 days of crude oil stocks and secured cargoes for next 2 months, ensuring 4 months of supply security amid West Asia crisis
  • 02/Domestic LPG production increased 40% to record 50,000 tonnes per day, meeting over 60% of national requirement
  • 03/Net LPG import shortfall reduced to 30,000 tonnes per day with 800,000 tonnes of cargoes en route from US, Russia, Australia

The Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas has assured that India's energy supply is fully secure with four months of crude oil coverage. The country currently holds 60 days of physical stocks with an additional two months of cargoes already secured.

All Indian refineries are operating at over 100 per cent utilisation. The government emphasized that retail fuel outlets have adequate supplies of petrol, diesel, and LPG across the country.

Domestic LPG production has been ramped up to a record 50,000 tonnes per day under the LPG Control Order. This marks a 40 per cent increase from previous output levels.

The production surge meets over 60 per cent of India's daily LPG requirement of approximately 80,000 tonnes. Consequently, the net daily import requirement has dropped to just 30,000 tonnes.

India has 332 million active domestic LPG connections. The country normally imports over 60 per cent of its cooking gas requirements.

An additional 800,000 tonnes of assured LPG cargoes are currently en route from the US, Russia, Australia, and other suppliers. These will arrive at India's 22 LPG import terminals.

Total national reserve capacity stands at 74 days including strategic cavern storage. Current actual stock cover is around 60 days even as the West Asia crisis enters its 27th day.

The Ministry dismissed reports of fuel shortages as a deliberately mischievous campaign of misinformation. Citizens have been urged not to panic or be misled by false claims.

Strategic petroleum reserves currently hold 3.37 million tonnes of crude oil. This represents approximately 64 per cent of total SPR capacity at Visakhapatnam, Mangaluru, and Padur facilities.
