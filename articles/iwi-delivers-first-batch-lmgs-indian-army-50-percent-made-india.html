IWI Delivers First Batch of 41,000 LMGs to Indian Army; Over 50% Made in India

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/IWI delivers first batch of 2,000 Negev 7.62X51 mm LMGs to Indian Army under 41,000-unit contract
  • 02/PLR Systems JV between Adani Defence and IWI produces over 50% of components locally in India
  • 03/IWI aims to clinch additional bid for 4,500 sniper rifles with ammunition to Indian Army

Israel Weapon Industries (IWI) has delivered the first batch of 2,000 Negev 7.62X51 mm light machine guns (LMGs) to the Indian Army. The delivery took place on March 28, 2026, marking a milestone in the company's Indian defence contracts.

The total contract covers 41,000 LMG units planned over five years. IWI CEO Shuki Schwartz stated the company aims to deliver faster than scheduled.

PLR Systems, a joint venture between Adani Defence & Aerospace and IWI, is manufacturing the weapons locally. Over 50% of production including barrels, assembly, testing, and surface treatment occurs in India.

The Make in India initiative involves co-production between the OEM and the Indian entity. Some components are still produced in Israel while the majority are manufactured domestically.

IWI plans to deliver an additional 4,000 LMG units in 2026. Similar quantities will follow in subsequent years as PLR builds capacity for larger orders.

PLR Systems is also supplying 7.62X51 ammunition for the LMGs. The JV has secured additional contracts including Close Quarter Battle carbines for 58,000 units over four years.

IWI is bidding to supply 4,500 sniper rifles with ammunition to the Indian Army. The company has passed initial trials for its .338 sniper rifle with final tests expected within three months.
