Netafim Launches 5th Gen AI-Powered Dosing 5G Range for Precision Agriculture

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Netafim has launched its 5th generation AI-enabled Dosing 5G product range, bringing self-learning artificial intelligence to fertigation management
  • 02/The portfolio includes four products—FertiKit 5G, FertiOne 5G, NetaJet 5G, and NetaFlex 5G—designed to optimize nutrient delivery, cut input costs, and reduce water waste
  • 03/The system integrates with Netafim's GrowSphere platform for remote monitoring and control

Netafim, the precision agriculture arm of Orbia, has rolled out its fifth-generation automated dosing technology. The Dosing 5G range represents a significant upgrade in fertigation management, combining AI-driven automation with farmer-friendly design.

The product suite comprises four distinct systems: FertiKit 5G, FertiOne 5G, NetaJet 5G, and NetaFlex 5G. Each is engineered to handle both open-field crops and protected cultivation environments.

At the core of the Dosing 5G range is self-learning AI that calibrates nutrient delivery based on real-time crop requirements. This precision approach aims to balance application rates to boost yields and crop quality while preventing the overuse of fertilizers.

The economic case is straightforward—by optimizing consumption of fertilizer, water, and energy, the system cuts input costs and reduces manual labor. Environmental benefits include minimized nutrient run-off and reduced groundwater contamination, supporting long-term soil and water sustainability.

The complete Dosing 5G lineup syncs with GrowSphere, Netafim's unified digital platform. Farmers can plan, monitor, and manage irrigation and fertigation operations through a single interface.

Vikas Sonawane, Chief Operating Officer at Netafim Irrigation India Pvt Ltd, framed the launch as a response to specific pain points facing Indian agriculture. "Farmers in India face rising input costs, water scarcity, inconsistent nutrient dosing and limited real-time visibility, often leading to wastage, uneven crop growth and lower profitability," Sonawane said. He added that the AI-powered solution "brings precision, automation and remote monitoring to fertigation management, ensuring optimal nutrient delivery with minimal manual effort."

The rollout signals continued investment in precision agriculture technologies targeting emerging markets, where resource efficiency and cost control remain critical to farm profitability.
