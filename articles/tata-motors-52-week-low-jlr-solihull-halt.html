Tata Motors Hits 52-Week Low as JLR Solihull Plant Halts Production

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Tata Motors shares plunged 5.6% to hit a 52-week low of ₹301.10 on March 27, 2026
  • 02/JLR halted production at Solihull plant for under two weeks due to supplier parts shortage affecting Range Rover models
  • 03/This follows August 2025 cyberattack that cost £50 million weekly; analysts have price targets of ₹311-₹519

Tata Motors Ltd shares crashed 5.6% on Friday to hit a 52-week low of ₹301.10. The sharp decline came after subsidiary Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) halted production at its Solihull plant.

The temporary shutdown, lasting less than two weeks including the Easter holiday, was triggered by a supplier parts shortage. Production of the high-margin Range Rover and Range Rover Sport models has been affected.

This disruption comes just months after a devastating cyberattack in August 2025. That incident halted JLR production for nearly six weeks, costing an estimated £50 million per week with total liabilities exceeding £2 billion.

Normal operations only resumed by mid-November 2025. The latest pause renews concerns about JLR's operational resilience and supply chain vulnerabilities.

JLR contributes significantly to Tata Motors' consolidated revenue and profitability. The Solihull plant manufactures the company's most profitable vehicles, making any disruption material to financial performance.

Tata Motors shares had closed at ₹317.95 on March 26, up 2.2% ahead of the news. The stock has been under pressure amid global luxury vehicle demand concerns.

JLR is executing a £18 billion five-year investment plan to transition its lineup to electric vehicles. Analysts maintain mixed ratings with price targets ranging from ₹311.25 to ₹519.00.

The production halt raises questions about JLR's ability to execute its turnaround strategy. Investors will watch for management commentary on the financial impact of this latest disruption.
