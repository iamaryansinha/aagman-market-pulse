Coforge Secures RBI Approval for $1 Billion-Plus Overseas Investment to Acquire Encora

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Coforge Ltd has secured Reserve Bank of India approval for overseas direct investment exceeding $1 billion to fund its acquisition of Encora
  • 02/The approval, granted under the Foreign Exchange Management (Overseas Investment) Rules, removes a critical regulatory obstacle for the transaction involving Encora US Holdco Inc. and Encora Holdings Ltd
  • 03/The company stated that remaining approvals are now in advanced stages

Coforge Ltd has received regulatory clearance from the Reserve Bank of India for overseas direct investment exceeding $1 billion. The approval marks a significant milestone in the IT services company's proposed acquisition of Encora.

The RBI approval was granted under the Foreign Exchange Management (Overseas Investment) Rules. This regulatory green light clears a key hurdle for the transaction involving Encora US Holdco Inc. and Encora Holdings Ltd.

The company disclosed the development in a stock exchange filing on March 31, 2026.

Coforge noted that this approval builds on earlier regulatory progress. The company had previously secured Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvement Act approval in the United States. Shareholder approval was also obtained via postal ballot for preferential allotment and debt funding arrangements.

Competition-related clearances have been secured in multiple jurisdictions including Australia.

With the RBI approval now in place, Coforge stated that "the remaining approvals are in advanced stages." The company did not specify which approvals are still pending or provide a revised timeline for closing the transaction.

The acquisition agreement was initially announced earlier, involving share subscription and share purchase agreements with Encora US Holdco Inc., Encora Holdings Ltd, Encora Holdco Ltd, and AI Altius Parent (Cayman) Ltd.

The Encora deal represents one of the largest acquisitions in the Indian IT services sector. The transaction would significantly expand Coforge's presence in the digital engineering and product development space.
