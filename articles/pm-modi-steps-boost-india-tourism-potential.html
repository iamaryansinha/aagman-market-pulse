PM Modi Calls For Urgent Steps To Boost India's Tourism Potential, Sets Ambitious Growth Targets

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/PM Modi calls for enhanced tourism infrastructure to unlock sector's potential, citing it as a tool for prosperity and employment generation
  • 02/Tourism contributes 5.22% to India's GDP and supports 13.34% of total employment with 48 million jobs in 2025
  • 03/Budget 2026-27 allocates Rs 2,541 crore for tourism; targets 100 million inbound tourists by 2047 under Vision@2047

Prime Minister Narendra Modi has called for urgent steps to boost India's tourism potential. Speaking on World Tourism Day 2025, he stated, "Tourism has the potential to bring prosperity to the lives of many."

The Prime Minister emphasized continued government focus on enhancing tourism infrastructure. His vision aims to ensure more people can experience what he called "the wonders of Incredible India."

The tourism sector currently accounts for 5.22% of India's GDP on a total impact basis. The direct contribution stands at 2.72%, according to the India Tourism Data Compendium 2025.

Employment figures are equally significant. The sector supports 13.34% of India's total employment. Approximately 48 million jobs were supported by tourism in 2025.

Foreign tourist arrivals showed strong recovery with 56 lakh visitors recorded till August 2025. Domestic tourism remains robust with 303.59 crore domestic tourist visits in the same period.

The Union Budget 2026-27 has allocated Rs 2,541.06 crore to the tourism sector. Key initiatives include developing 50 top tourist destinations and providing MUDRA loans for homestays.

The government has launched several flagship schemes to boost tourism infrastructure. Swadesh Darshan and Swadesh Darshan 2.0 have developed 110 projects across thematic circuits.

These circuits include the Ramayana Circuit, Buddhist Circuit, Coastal Circuit, and Tribal Circuit. The PRASHAD scheme focuses specifically on pilgrimage tourism infrastructure development.

Budget 2026 introduced several new initiatives to diversify tourism offerings. These include Buddhist circuits development, sustainable Himalayan hiking trails, and medical tourism hubs.

The government plans to establish a National Institute of Hospitality for skilling. It also aims to train 10,000 tourist guides in collaboration with IIMs.

Foreign exchange earnings from tourism reached Rs 78,715 crore between January and March 2025. Medical tourism alone attracted 1,31,856 foreign tourist arrivals from January to April 2025.

The Vision@2047 initiative has set an ambitious target of 100 million inbound tourists by 2047. This aligns with India's goal of becoming a developed nation by its 100th independence anniversary.

"Every rupee invested in tourism generates 3.5 times more jobs than the economy-wide average," noted industry analysts. This makes the sector critical for employment-led growth.

The Ministry of Tourism is also reviving the Incredible India campaign with a digital overhaul. The new focus includes digital storytelling and immersive content for global outreach.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS //
WHAT DID PM MODI SAY ABOUT TOURISM POTENTIAL?
ON WORLD TOURISM DAY 2025, PM MODI STATED: "TOURISM HAS THE POTENTIAL TO BRING PROSPERITY TO THE LIVES OF MANY. OUR GOVERNMENT WILL KEEP FOCUSING ON ENHANCING INDIA'S TOURISM INFRASTRUCTURE TO ENSURE MORE PEOPLE CAN EXPERIENCE THE WONDERS OF INCREDIBLE INDIA."
WHAT IS THE CURRENT CONTRIBUTION OF TOURISM TO INDIA'S GDP?
THE TOURISM SECTOR ACCOUNTS FOR 5.22% OF INDIA'S GDP ON A TOTAL IMPACT BASIS, WITH A DIRECT CONTRIBUTION OF 2.72%, ACCORDING TO THE INDIA TOURISM DATA COMPENDIUM 2025 BY THE MINISTRY OF TOURISM.
HOW MANY JOBS DOES THE TOURISM SECTOR SUPPORT IN INDIA?
THE TOURISM SECTOR SUPPORTS APPROXIMATELY 48 MILLION JOBS IN 2025, REPRESENTING 13.34% OF INDIA'S TOTAL EMPLOYMENT. EVERY RUPEE INVESTED IN TOURISM GENERATES 3.5 TIMES MORE JOBS THAN THE ECONOMY-WIDE AVERAGE.
WHAT ARE THE KEY GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES FOR TOURISM DEVELOPMENT?
KEY INITIATIVES INCLUDE SWADESH DARSHAN AND SWADESH DARSHAN 2.0 (110 PROJECTS ACROSS THEMATIC CIRCUITS), PRASHAD FOR PILGRIMAGE TOURISM, RS 2,541.06 CRORE ALLOCATION IN BUDGET 2026-27, MUDRA LOANS FOR HOMESTAYS, AND DEVELOPMENT OF 50 TOP TOURIST DESTINATIONS.
WHAT ARE INDIA'S TOURISM TARGETS FOR 2047?
UNDER THE VISION@2047 INITIATIVE, INDIA TARGETS 100 MILLION INBOUND TOURISTS BY 2047. THE GOVERNMENT ALSO AIMS TO EXPAND THE SECTOR'S GDP CONTRIBUTION TO RS 43.25 LAKH CRORE BY 2034 AND SUPPORT NEARLY 64 MILLION JOBS BY 2035.
