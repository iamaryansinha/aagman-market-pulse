Namma Yatri Parent Acquires Dutch Firm Automicle, Enters European Market

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Moving Tech Innovations acquires Netherlands-based Automicle Holding BV, marking its first international expansion into Europe
  • 02/Deal terms undisclosed; MTI has completed 150 million trips with ₹2,500 crore in zero-commission driver earnings
  • 03/Acquisition enables MTI to deploy its open mobility model in European cities while bringing Automicle's parking expertise to India

Moving Tech Innovations (MTI), parent company of ride-hailing platform Namma Yatri, has acquired Netherlands-based Automicle Holding BV. The deal marks MTI's formal entry into the European mobility market.

Financial terms of the acquisition were not disclosed. This represents MTI's first international expansion beyond India.

MTI operates multiple open mobility platforms including Namma Yatri, Yatri Sathi, Bharat Taxi, and Chennai One. The company has facilitated over 150 million trips while enabling more than ₹2,500 crore in driver earnings without charging commissions.

Automicle, founded in 2022 by Mohit Mishra, Jef Heyse, and Amit Pal, develops digital solutions for urban mobility. The company specializes in parking systems and integrated transport platforms for European municipalities.

Both companies operate on open protocols including Beckn and TOMP APIs. These standards enable interoperability across mobility services without vendor lock-in.

"With Automicle, we are taking those learnings beyond India," said Magizhan Selvan, co-founder of MTI. "These are not local solutions; they are universal principles."

The acquisition comes as cities worldwide seek alternatives to high-commission ride-hailing platforms. MTI's model emphasizes open digital infrastructure and greater control for city authorities.

"This is a pivotal moment for Automicle," said co-founders Jef Heyse and Mohit Mishra. "What MTI has built at scale proves that city-first models work."

The deal aligns with growing India-EU collaboration on digital public infrastructure. Advanced deployment discussions are reportedly underway across multiple European countries.
