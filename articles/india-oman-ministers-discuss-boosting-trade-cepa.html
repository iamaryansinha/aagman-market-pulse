India, Oman Ministers Discuss Boosting Trade Under CEPA Pact

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal held a telecall with Oman's Minister Anwar bin Hilal bin Hamdoun Al Jabri on April 13, 2026
  • 02/Both sides discussed unlocking opportunities under the India-Oman CEPA signed in December 2025
  • 03/The pact offers duty-free access to 98% of Indian exports but has not yet been implemented

Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal held talks with Oman's Minister of Commerce, Industry and Investment Promotion on Monday. The ministers discussed ways to boost bilateral trade and investment ties.

The call with Anwar bin Hilal bin Hamdoun Al Jabri focused on unlocking opportunities under the India-Oman CEPA. The pact was signed in Muscat in December 2025 but remains unimplemented.

CEPA will provide duty-free access to 98% of India's exports to Oman. Key beneficiary sectors include textiles, agricultural products, and leather goods.

India will reduce tariffs on select Omani imports in return. These include dates, marbles, and petrochemical items.

Bilateral trade between India and Oman stood at $10.5 billion in 2024-25. Indian exports accounted for $4 billion while imports totaled $6.54 billion.

The discussions come amid heightened geopolitical tensions in West Asia. The US-Israel joint attack on Iran has disrupted shipping routes to Gulf nations.

India's total bilateral trade with the Gulf region reached $178 billion in 2024-25. Exports to the region were $56.87 billion against imports of $121.67 billion.

Goyal has held similar trade discussions with ministers from Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, and Kuwait in recent days. The outreach signals India's push to deepen economic ties with West Asia.
