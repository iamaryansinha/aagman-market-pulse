Sustainable Sourcing Can Unlock $15-20B Premium Export Markets For Indian Agriculture

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 3 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/India's agricultural exports have plateaued at $48 billion, remaining below $50B for three consecutive years
  • 02/European buyers now demand traceability and deforestation-free certification, creating compliance barriers for Indian exporters
  • 03/Sustainable sourcing could unlock $15-20 billion in premium export markets across EU, Japan, and South Korea

India's agricultural exports have stagnated at $48 billion. The figure has remained below $50 billion for three straight years.

European importers are tightening sourcing requirements. They now demand full traceability and deforestation-free certification from suppliers.

This shift creates both risk and opportunity. Farmers and exporters who adapt can access premium price markets.

The investment gap stands at ₹1.6 trillion. Cold chains, food processing, and logistics infrastructure need urgent upgrades.

The potential reward is substantial. Sustainable sourcing could open $15-20 billion in premium markets across the EU, Japan, and South Korea.

Policy support is already in place. PLI schemes and 100% FDI in food processing encourage private investment.

Focus crops include coffee, spices, fruits, vegetables, and organic products. These categories command higher margins in developed markets.

The transition requires coordinated action. Exporters, processors, and farmers must align on certification and quality standards.

THE GOVERNMENT OFFERS PRODUCTION-LINKED INCENTIVE (PLI) SCHEMES AND ALLOWS 100% FOREIGN DIRECT INVESTMENT IN FOOD PROCESSING TO ENCOURAGE PRIVATE SECTOR PARTICIPATION.
