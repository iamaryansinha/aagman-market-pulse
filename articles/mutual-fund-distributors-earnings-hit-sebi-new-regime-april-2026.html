Mutual Fund Distributors Brace For 15% Earnings Hit As New SEBI Regime Kicks In April 1

BY AAGMAN DESK | EST. READ: 1 MIN
EXECUTIVE TLDR //
  • 01/SEBI's new TER structure effective April 1, 2026 will deduct 18% GST from distributor commissions, hitting unregistered MFDs with 15% income cut
  • 02/An unregistered MFD earning ₹1 lakh monthly will receive ₹82,000 after GST deduction under the new regime
  • 03/85-90% of India's 2 lakh ARN-registered distributors are not GST registered, facing significant earnings pressure

Mutual fund distributors are staring at a sharp earnings hit from April 1 as SEBI's new Total Expense Ratio regime takes effect. The restructuring will slash take-home commissions by factoring in GST deductions.

Under the new framework, mutual funds will pay commissions to distributors after deducting 18% GST. An unregistered distributor earning ₹1 lakh monthly will now receive ₹82,000 after the deduction.

The changes stem from SEBI's December 2025 overhaul of the TER framework. Statutory levies including GST, Securities Transaction Tax, and stamp duty will now be excluded from the base TER and disclosed separately.

For investors, this brings improved transparency on charges levied by mutual funds. Fund management costs will form the Base Expense Ratio while statutory costs are charged separately.

The MF industry has approximately 2 lakh distributors with registered ARN codes. Industry estimates suggest 85-90% of these distributors are not registered for GST payment.

The new structure replaces the old bundled TER system where expenses were combined. Under the previous system, unregistered distributors could retain the full commission amount.

Aditya Agarwal, co-founder of Wealthy.in, noted there will be a definite impact of about 15% on income for distributors below the ₹20 lakh threshold. His platform works with over 6,000 MF distributors.

However, equity market growth may offset some losses. MFDs manage about 50% of equity AUM, and mark-to-market growth could erase the income drop within a year, Agarwal added.

The changes also include a 10-15 basis point reduction in base expense ratio caps across fund categories. Index funds and ETFs now face a 0.90% cap, down from 1%.

Brokerage caps have been tightened to 6 basis points for equity cash trades and 2 basis points for derivatives. The additional 5 basis points allowance on schemes with exit loads has been removed.
